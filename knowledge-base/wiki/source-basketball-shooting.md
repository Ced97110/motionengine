# Basketball Shooting, Enhanced Edition — Summary

## Overview
*Basketball Shooting, Enhanced Edition* is written by the shooting specialist, widely considered one of the greatest shooters and shooting coaches in the world. Hopla served as shooting coach for the Toronto pro team and Washington pro team, working with players including an elite scoring wing, Ray Allen, Kevin Love, Chris Bosh, and Diana Taurasi. After joining the pro team in 2006, the team's shooting percentage improved every month of the season, and their three-point percentage rose from 30% to 40% between November 2006 and January 2007. [S5, p.14]

The book provides a complete, systematic framework for developing shooting skill — from the most fundamental mechanical elements (grip, balance hand, elbow position, wrist action) through advanced game-shot application (shooting off screens, shooting off the dribble, bank shots, shot fakes) and finally to the evaluation and documentation systems required to turn practice into measurable improvement. It is written in second person, directly addressing the player or coach, and is characterized by relentless emphasis on technique discipline, game-speed practice, and self-accountability.

Every concept is grounded in the principle that **game shots must be practiced from game spots at game speed**, and that footwork is the non-negotiable foundation of every shooting situation — especially on the move.

## Key Themes
1. **Inside-foot plant as the universal footwork rule**: Regardless of direction of movement or screen angle, the inside pivot foot is always planted first on the catch. This is the foundational rule for all moving shots.
2. **Shooting mechanics as a checklist**: Grip, balance hand, shooting hand, shooting arm, body position, and finish are each treated as discrete, evaluable components — not vague "feel."
3. **Game speed practice**: Spot shooting alone is insufficient. Players must practice shooting on the move at game speed, from game spots, simulating real game situations.
4. **Documentation and accountability**: Shot charts and shooting evaluation forms are presented as essential practice tools, not optional extras.
5. **Off-season vs. in-season development**: Mechanical changes require sustained off-season work — in-season changes rarely stick.

## Unique Contributions
Hopla's book is unique in the source library for its **singular focus on shooting mechanics and shooting skill development**. No other source in this collection provides:
- A complete, step-by-step shooting evaluation checklist covering every mechanical component
- A structured shot chart documentation system with a reproducible form
- The "inside-foot plant" rule articulated clearly across all moving-shot scenarios (off screens, off dribble, both directions)
- Drills specifically designed for shooting **while coming off screens** at game speed
- The "stub your foot" technique for maintaining shot alignment when the shooting hand is farther from the basket

This source is the primary reference for all shooting-mechanics and shooting-drill content in the wiki.

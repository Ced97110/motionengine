# Contralateral Kick (Unilateral Ankle Balance)

## Objective
Train unilateral ankle proprioception and stabilization strength — essential for driving layups, shooting off-balance, landing from rebounds, and chasing loose balls.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space
- Equipment: resistance band attached at floor level (anchor to wall, rack, or door)

## Execution
1. Stand on the affected (injured or weaker) lower extremity. Attach a resistance band around the ankle of the non-injured (non-standing) leg at floor level.
2. Maintaining balance on the standing leg, kick the free leg against the resistance band:
   - Forward (sagittal plane)
   - Backward (sagittal plane)
   - Side to side (horizontal plane)
3. The standing ankle must stabilize dynamically against each kick perturbation.
4. To advance, increase resistance band tension or increase the speed of the kicks.

## Coaching Points
- "Stay tall on the standing leg — no trunk leaning"
- Watch for the standing knee collapsing inward — cue a slight outward push at the knee
- The kicking leg should move freely and fully; the standing leg does all the stabilization work
- Progression of kicking speed mimics the unpredictable forces a player faces when running and changing direction
- This exercise is especially important for guards who must drive and finish on one leg against contact

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow, predictable kick directions (forward/back only) with light resistance
2. **Intermediate**: All planes of motion at moderate speed
3. **Advanced**: Rapid unpredictable kicks with heavier resistance band

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Gastrocnemius, soleus, tibialis anterior, extensor hallucis longus, extensor digitorum longus, flexor hallucis longus, flexor digitorum longus, tibialis posterior, peroneals, interossei, lumbricals
- **Secondary**: Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, hamstrings, quadriceps, rectus abdominis

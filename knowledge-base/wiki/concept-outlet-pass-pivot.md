# Outlet Pass Pivot

## Summary
Securing a defensive rebound is only half the battle — the rebounder must then make a safe, effective outlet pass to initiate transition offense. Poor outlet passing technique can negate all the effort spent on the defensive box-out by immediately returning possession to the opposition. The outlet pass sequence involves two critical pivots: one by the rebounder (to turn away from defensive pressure) and one by the outlet receiver (to protect the ball and survey the frontcourt). Both players use the **front pivot**, turning away from the middle of the court toward the sideline. [S6, pp.212-215]

## When to Use
- Immediately after any defensive rebound, regardless of whether the team plays fast break or controlled offense
- The outlet pass is always required to get the ball into a capable ball handler's hands, even if the team does not run a fast break
- Both the rebounder and receiver must execute the pivot correctly to protect the ball from initial defensive pressure

## Key Principles
1. **Chin the ball immediately after securing the rebound** — pull the ball under the chin with both hands to protect it, just as post players are taught when catching an inside pass [S6, p.213]
2. **Front pivot away from the middle** — the rebounder turns away from where most opposing defenders are located (the middle of the court); the pivot is away from defensive pressure [S6, p.213]
3. **Pivot off the outside foot** — the pivot foot is the foot closer to the sideline (outside foot); the inside foot swings toward the sideline [S6, pp.213-214]
4. **The front pivot gives full vision of outlet receivers AND the basket** — the rebounder can see all outlet targets and the entire frontcourt before passing [S6, p.213]
5. **The outlet receiver must meet the pass** — don't stand still and wait; sprint to the ball and catch it actively [S6, p.213]
6. **The outlet receiver also front pivots after catching** — pivoting off the outside foot, swinging the inside foot toward the sideline, away from all defenders in the middle [S6, pp.213-214]
7. **After the pivot, chin the ball and read defensive pressure** before looking downcourt to advance via dribble or pass [S6, p.214]
8. **Safety over speed** — the front pivot is preferred over the reverse pivot for the outlet receiver because it is safer; some coaches prefer the reverse pivot for speed, but the front pivot's security advantage outweighs any minor speed gain [S6, p.214]

## Execution: Rebounder's Pivot

### Left-Side Rebound (Diagram 5-7)
- Rebounder (X5) secures the ball on the left side of the court
- **Chin the ball**
- **Front pivot off the right foot** (the outside/right foot is the pivot foot when rebounding on the left side)
- **Swing the left (inside) foot** toward the sideline — turning away from the middle of the court where most defenders are
- Now facing the sideline and outlet receiver (01); full view of the frontcourt
- Make the outlet pass to 01 [S6, p.213]

### Right-Side Rebound (Diagram 5-8)
- Rebounder (04) secures the ball on the right side of the court
- **Chin the ball**
- **Front pivot off the left foot** (the outside/left foot is the pivot foot when rebounding on the right side)
- **Swing the right (inside) foot** toward the sideline — turning away from the middle of the court
- Now facing the sideline and outlet receiver (02); full view of the frontcourt
- Make the outlet pass to 02 [S6, p.214]

## Execution: Outlet Receiver's Pivot

### Left-Side Outlet (01 receiving)
- 01 uses V-cuts or other footwork to get open from a defender
- Meets the pass actively — does not stand still
- Upon catching: **front pivot off the right (outside) foot**, swinging the left (inside) foot toward the sideline
- **Chin the ball**, survey defensive pressure
- Once pressure is gauged: look downcourt, advance via dribble or ahead pass [S6, pp.213-214]

### Right-Side Outlet (02 receiving)
- Same technique mirrored: **front pivot off the left (outside) foot**, swinging the right (inside) foot toward the sideline [S6, p.214]

## Coaching Cues
- *"Chin it first"*
- *"Pivot away from the middle"*
- *"Outside foot is the pivot foot"*
- *"See your basket, see your teammates"*
- *"Meet the pass — don't wait for it"*
- *"Protect first, then look downcourt"*

## Common Mistakes
1. **Not chinning the ball immediately** → Ball is vulnerable to strip after the rebound; chin it first before pivoting [S6, p.213]
2. **Pivoting toward the middle** → Turns the rebounder directly into defensive traffic; always pivot toward the sideline, away from pressure [S6, p.213]
3. **Using the inside foot as the pivot foot** → Results in turning into the middle of the court instead of away from it; the outside foot is always the pivot foot [S6, pp.213-214]
4. **Outlet receiver standing still** → Pass is easier to intercept, and the receiver doesn't get a running start; must actively meet the pass [S6, p.213]
5. **Looking downcourt before gauging defensive pressure** → Ball gets stolen by a surprise defender; chin the ball, gauge pressure first, then attack [S6, p.214]
6. **Using reverse pivot at the outlet receiver position** → Some coaches advocate it for speed, but the front pivot is safer and recommended [S6, p.214]

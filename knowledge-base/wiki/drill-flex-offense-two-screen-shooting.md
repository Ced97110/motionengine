# Flex Offense — Shooting With Two Screens (4-on-4)

## Objective
Train the cutter to read two available screens and choose the correct reaction — fade, curl, or screener roll — against live defenders in a half-court 4-on-4 setting.

## Setup
- 4 offensive players (1, 2, 4, 5) vs. 4 defenders
- Half court
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. 1 has the ball at the top of the key.
2. 4 and 5 set simultaneous screens for 2 on the wing/block area.
3. 2 reads his defender and chooses which screen to exploit: 4's screen or 5's screen.
4. **Fade read** (defender slides through the screen): 5 changes the angle of the screen (re-screen); 2 fades away from the screen and receives for a jump shot.
5. **Curl read** (defender follows behind 2): 2 curls around, receives the ball, and either shoots or passes to 5/4 who have rolled to the basket.
6. 1 can also pass directly to 5 or 4 on their roll if either defender is caught ball-watching.
7. Play continues until no effective shooting opportunity exists.

## Coaching Points
- "Read the defender's hips — if they go over, you fade; if they trail, you curl"
- 2 must commit to a decision early — hesitation destroys the timing advantage
- Screeners (4 and 5) must roll hard to the basket the instant 2 clears — the roll is always a live scoring threat
- 1 must be patient with the ball but decisive — if the roll is open, throw it immediately
- Defenders guarding the screeners must not cheat to help on 2 or they surrender the roll

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Walk through the fade and curl reads separately before going live
2. **Advanced**: Full 4-on-4 with all flex continuity — after each pass, continue the screen-the-screener pattern rather than resetting

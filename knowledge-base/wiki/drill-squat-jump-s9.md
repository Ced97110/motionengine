# Squat Jump (Power Jump Chain Step 2)

## Objective
Build hip, knee, and leg power by integrating the full lower-body into the jump pattern — the cornerstone of all jumping techniques.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface (no equipment needed)
- Slightly wider than shoulder-width stance

## Execution
1. Stand with feet a little wider than shoulder-width, body flexed for action
2. Dip down into a squat — for maximum height, approximately one-third of the way down (not quite a half-squat); stronger athletes can drop to thighs parallel
3. Explode upward using the full power of the lower body and total-body spring
4. Keep arms reasonably neutral at first (no arm swing)
5. After take-off, let the legs move naturally — do NOT actively pull them up or thrust them down
6. Land softly, catching most weight on the balls of the feet
7. Use the elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- **"Ask a novice to jump and this is what they will do"** — The squat jump is the instinctive jump; train it to make it optimal [S9, p.14]
- **Hip-led descent** — Coach athletes to sit into the squat, not bow forward at the waist
- **One-third depth for maximum power** — Going too deep (below parallel) reduces explosive potential; find the sweet spot
- **Neutral arms to start** — Adding arm swing comes at Step 3; for now, isolate lower-body power
- **Soft landing** — Greater height means greater impact; reinforce balls-of-feet landing and hip-led squat absorption
- Watch for: excessive forward lean on descent → cue upright torso, hips back
- Watch for: landing on heels → cue "land like a cat, soft and quiet"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (regression)**: Quarter-squat jumps — less acute knee bend as a transition between straight hops and full squat jumps
2. **Intermediate**: Standard squat jump with one-third depth, neutral arms
3. **Advanced**: Drop to below-parallel (thighs parallel to floor) to strengthen knees and glutes more — BUT note this reduces explosive potential; also try closer stance for added difficulty

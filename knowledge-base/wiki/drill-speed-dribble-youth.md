# Speed Dribble (Drill #23)

## Objective
Develop proper speed-dribbling position, full-court conditioning, and seamless transition from dribble to pivot to pass — integrating multiple fundamental skills in one continuous drill.

## Setup
- **Players:** Full team in 4 lines at baseline
- **Court area:** Full court
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per line (or 2 balls per player in advanced variation)
- **Duration:** 1 minute per repetition; run multiple sets

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up in 4 lines at the baseline.
2. In speed-dribbling position, dribble the full length of the court as quickly as possible.
3. Execute a **reverse (or front) pivot** at the far end before returning.
4. Dribble down with the right hand; return with the left hand.
5. **Jump-stop** ~15 feet (4.5 m) from the teammate next in line.
6. Execute a **chest pass (or bounce pass)** to the next player in line.
7. All players repeat the sequence.

### Options
- Divide into teams for a dribbling race: guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team
- Two trips down and back: right hand on the first trip, left hand on the second

### Advanced (Two-Ball)
1. **Alternating variation:** Hold one ball in each hand; alternate dribbles — bounce left-hand ball while holding right, then bounce right-hand ball when the other bounces back up
2. **Simultaneous variation:** Bounce both balls at the same height — both touch hands at the same time and bounce at the same time

## Coaching Points
- **Speed-dribbling position:** slightly flexed at the knee and the torso — not fully upright, not deeply crouched
- Ball bounced **2–3 feet (.6–.9 m) in front of the right knee** — carom should reach waist height
- **Push the dribble out in front** — keep it away from the foot and knee to avoid turnovers
- **Eyes straight ahead** — never looking at the basketball
- Dribble with the **first pads of the fingers**
- Reward players who complete the full sequence (dribble → pivot → jump stop → pass) with the most control and speed

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** One ball, right hand down, left hand back, pivot and jump-stop finish
2. **Intermediate:** Two trips — different hand each way; competitive team format
3. **Advanced:** Two-ball alternating, then two-ball simultaneous — highest coordination demand

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-pivot-footwork]] — pivot at the far end and jump-stop before the pass are embedded in every repetition
- [[drill-control-dribble-youth]] — contrasting the speed dribble position with the control dribble position teaches players to shift gears
- [[concept-dribbling-driving-techniques]] — proper hand contact and push-ahead mechanics for open-court attacking

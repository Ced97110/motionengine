# Back Flip (Master Step)

## Objective
Execute a standing no-handed backward flip using a vertical jump and tuck, demonstrating integrated mastery of explosive jump power, hip snap, reactive core strength, and kinesthetic body control.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Open flat floor space with minimum 10 feet clearance behind and overhead
- Soft surface (grass, gymnastics mat) strongly recommended for early attempts
- **Prerequisites**: Four-point back flip (Step 9); comfortable back handspring (Step 7); one-arm back handspring (Step 8) recommended

## Execution
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hands held high.
2. Dip down at knees and hips, swinging arms down by your sides. Keep looking forward as you descend.
3. **Jump straight up hard** — swing hands above you as you go. You can look up as you jump. **Do NOT jump backward** — jump vertically. "Too many athletes fail because they jump back. Don't!" [S9, p.200]
4. When your body is fully extended at maximum height, **explode both knees up into your chest as hard as you can** (gymnasts call this a tuck). The momentum of this tuck creates the rotation.
5. Bring arms in to your sides or to your thighs as you spin over.
6. When you begin to see the ground below you, **whip your legs out straight** to prepare for landing.
7. When your feet are planted, catch your balance and extend your body upright again.

## Coaching Points
- **The most critical cue**: "Jump UP, not back — the tuck creates the rotation, not backward lean." [S9, p.200]
- "Tuck as hard as you can at peak height — the more explosive the tuck, the faster you rotate."
- "Keep arms tight to the body during rotation — wide arms slow the spin."
- "When you see the ground, whip the legs straight — don't keep the tuck through landing."
- Watch for athletes initiating the tuck before maximum height — this sacrifices height for rotation and produces a flat, risky landing
- Common landing error: crouched deep-squat landing → cue athletes to extend on landing

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: A solid four-point back flip (Step 9) naturally evolves into this as leg-whip timing improves; keep drilling Step 9 until feet consistently land before hands.
2. **Intermediate**: True back flip with feet landing first but in a deep crouch; arms may still brush the ground for safety.
3. **Advanced**: Full back flip with extended body landing, feet together — no arm contact with the ground.

## Variations
- **Flashkick**: One leg extended, one tucked during the flip [S9, p.199]
- **Gainer**: Land slightly forward of take-off point using forward run-up momentum [S9, p.199]
- **Loser**: Land rear of take-off point [S9, p.199]
- **Switchflip**: Flip off one leg and land on the opposite leg [S9, p.199]
- **Partner flip / Pitch tuck**: Partner holds foot to assist rotation; or self-replicate with one foot on a knee-height object [S9, p.198]
- **Wall back flip**: Kick off a low wall for a basic parkour back flip [S9, p.198]

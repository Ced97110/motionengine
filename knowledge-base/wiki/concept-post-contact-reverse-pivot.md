# Post Player: Contact and Reverse Pivot Move

## Summary
The contact and reverse pivot move is the offensive counter when a post defender physically jams a duck-in cut by playing on the high side. Instead of trying to rip or swim through the defender, the post player steps INTO the defender and uses a reverse pivot to get around and below the defender, creating a low-side seal for a direct entry pass. This is a high-leverage post-entry move that exploits defenders who are overly aggressive on the high side. [S6, pp.179-180]

## When to Use
- Defender (X4) is on the high side of the post player, actively jamming the duck-in cut
- Ball is on the perimeter, swung to the opposite side — triggering a duck-in attempt
- Post player is at or near the first notch above the block [S6, pp.179-180]

## Key Principles
1. **Step the contact foot BETWEEN the defender's feet** — not outside; the contact foot (lower/baseline foot) goes directly between the defender's two feet. [S6, p.179]
2. **Reverse pivot swings the upper foot toward the baseline** — a strong, quick reverse pivot swings the non-contact foot (left, in most cases) toward the baseline while the contact foot is the pivot. [S6, p.179]
3. **Arm bar up throughout** — the arm/elbow on the high side goes up immediately as an arm bar to seal the defender off and maintain separation. [S6, p.179]
4. **Sit down on the defender's low leg** — lower the center of gravity and press into the defender's lower leg to create and maintain the seal. [S6, pp.179-180]
5. **Stay at or above the first notch above the block** — this position gives the best angle for drop steps in either direction after receiving the entry pass. [S6, p.180]

## Execution

### Scenario: 04 on the right side of the lane, defender X4 on the high side
1. Ball swings from 02 → 01 → across to 03 on the wing.
2. X4 is on the high side of 04, jamming the duck-in.
3. 04 steps his **right foot** (contact/baseline foot) **between** the two feet of X4.
4. 04 quickly executes a **reverse pivot** — swings his left foot toward the baseline.
5. Simultaneously raises left arm and elbow as an **arm bar**.
6. 04 is now on the **low (baseline) side** of X4.
7. 04 **seals X4** with the high foot (left) and arm/elbow combination.
8. 04 **sits down** on X4's low (right) leg to hold the seal.
9. 04 calls for the entry pass from 03, now at the notch above the block with the best drop-step angles. [S6, pp.179-180]

## Coaching Points
- **The contact foot must go between the defender's feet** — placing it outside the stance gives the defender recovery room.
- "Strong and quick reverse pivot" — a slow pivot allows the defender to recover.
- The **arm bar is non-negotiable** — it creates the physical space to seal and prevents the defender from reaching around.
- Perimeter passer (03) may need to **step toward the basket** before passing — getting proper passing angle to feed the sealed post. [S6, p.180]
- After sealing, the post player should **call for the ball** loudly and show a target hand.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Contact foot placed outside the defender's stance** → gives defender recovery space; step between their feet.
2. **Slow or soft reverse pivot** → defender re-establishes position; the pivot must be sharp and decisive.
3. **Arm bar drops after the seal** → defender squeezes through; keep the arm bar elevated throughout.
4. **Posting below the block** → no angle for drop steps; stay at the notch above the block.
5. **Waiting too long after sealing** → the seal must be caught when the ball is in position for entry; communicate immediately with the passer.

# Power Clean From Hang Position

## Objective
Develop explosive total-body power, quadriceps/hamstring strength, and abdominal stability through the front-rack catch — directly improving jumping height, acceleration, and the ability to withstand aerial contact.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Barbell (begin with empty bar or light weight)
- Must have mastered the clean pull from hang before attempting
- **Safety**: Advanced exercise — use light weight initially to master technique and generate a fast bar path [S2, p.103]

## Execution
1. Approach the barbell and grasp it with hands **just outside the knees** (clean grip).
2. Pick up the barbell and slightly flex the knees.
3. Move the hips backward (maintaining knee angle) to lower the bar to a position **slightly above the knees** (hang position).
4. The chest is positioned over and in front of the barbell. Upper back is straight, lower back slightly arched. Arms are relaxed and completely extended.
5. **Extend the hips upward and forward** while maintaining a slightly flexed knee angle — the barbell slides up the front of the thighs.
6. Fully extend the knees and ankles as you **quickly shrug the shoulders**, causing the barbell to rise vertically.
7. Once the barbell reaches its highest point, **rotate elbows up and in** to receive the bar. The barbell rests along both the shoulders and clavicles (front-rack position).
8. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.102–103]

## Coaching Points
- The key distinction from the snatch is the **catch position**: elbows rotate forward and up; the bar sits on the front of the shoulders, not overhead
- "Drive the hips through" — hip extension initiates everything
- Keep the bar as close to the body as possible — contact with the thighs is acceptable and desirable
- On the catch: elbows must be high to keep the bar from rolling off the shoulders
- Core must brace hard at the catch to stabilize the bar atop the shoulders

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Clean pull from hang (no catch) — groove the hip-drive pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Power clean from hang with empty bar or light load, focusing on the catch
3. **Advanced (Box)**: Power Clean From Box — bar elevated 3–12 inches to train dead-stop explosiveness at varied knee angles
4. **Advanced (Floor)**: Power Clean From Floor — most demanding variation; requires greatest joint mobility and technical proficiency [S2, p.103]

## Muscles Involved
**Primary**: Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, semitendinosus, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius, trapezius, lateral deltoid, anterior deltoid

**Secondary**: Gastrocnemius, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, rectus femoris, rectus abdominis, internal oblique, external oblique [S2, p.103]

## Basketball Focus
The power clean is a **total-body exercise** that works the quadriceps, hamstrings, and abdominal muscles simultaneously. Greater floor force production = higher jumping and faster acceleration. The front-rack stabilization phase specifically builds the trunk strength needed to **withstand physical contact when airborne** — such as during a contested layup or a physical rebounding battle. [S2, p.103]

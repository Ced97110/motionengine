# One-Arm Cable Chest Press

## Objective
Develop unilateral pressing strength in the pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, and triceps, while simultaneously challenging the anatomical core as a stabilizer — mimicking the single-arm force transfer demands of passing and ball protection in basketball.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Low-pulley cable machine with a single handle attachment set at chest height
- Split stance position in front of the cable machine

## Execution
1. Stand in front of the cable machine in a split stance (one foot forward, one foot back, knees flexed).
2. Grasp the handle with a neutral grip using the hand **on the same side as the rear foot**. Cable runs underneath the armpit with the hand at chest level.
3. Elbow is behind the wrist; wrist is stopped before reaching the chest line (to protect the shoulder) — this is the beginning position.
4. Maintaining a **rigid, upright upper body**, push the handle forward until the elbow is fully extended (but not locked).
5. Keeping the rest of the body still, slowly reverse and return the handle to the beginning position, keeping the elbow behind the wrist.

**Breathing:** Exhale during the pressing phase; inhale during the return.

## Coaching Points
- "Athletic stance throughout" — maintain the split stance, upright torso, and neutral spine at all times
- "Handle in front of the elbow" — do not let the elbow get ahead of the wrist
- "Locked wrist" — do not allow wrist flexion during the press; keep it straight and rigid
- The body must stay completely still — no twisting, tilting, or hip movement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Kneel on one knee (other leg 90° in front) with cable slightly above the shoulder (over the shoulder, not under armpit) — reduces balance demand
2. **Intermediate**: Standing split stance as described — standard form
3. **Advanced**: Add a rotational component when pressing forward (like a boxing punch) — actively involves the anatomical core muscles for a total-body pressing pattern

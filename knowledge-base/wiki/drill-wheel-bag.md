# Wheel (Bag Drill)

## Objective
Improve balance and quickness of foot movements by rotating around four bags in an X formation while keeping both hands touching the center, then sprinting forward to finish. [S8, p.118]

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4 agility bags arranged in an X (diamond) pattern, approximately 2-3 feet apart
- 5 yards of open space on one end for the sprint finish

## Execution
1. Arrange four bags in an X format.
2. Stand between two of the bags with both hands reaching toward the middle of the X.
3. Sidestep over each bag, rotating around all four bags while keeping your hands in contact with the center of the X.
4. Complete a full rotation and return to the starting position.
5. Quickly **reverse direction** and sidestep back around all four bags in the opposite direction.
6. Finish the drill by immediately sprinting 5 yards (4.6 meters) straight ahead out of the bags. [S8, p.118]

## Coaching Points
- Keeping hands at the center simulates a low body position — do not let the hips rise
- Do not cross feet during the sidestep rotation
- The sprint at the end must be explosive — use the energy built up during the rotations
- Head up — simulate being aware of the court while in defensive stance
- "Stay connected to the center" — this is the coaching cue for the hand contact requirement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow rotation around bags learning the footwork pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed with hands in center, both directions
3. **Advanced**: Reduce the X dimensions for tighter, faster footwork

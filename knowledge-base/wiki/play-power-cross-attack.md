# Power Cross Attack

## Overview
An advanced low-post offensive set designed to deliver the ball to a dominant center via an indirect route using cross screens, screen-the-screener actions, and ball reversal. Used when defenses recognize the Power Down Attack pattern and adapt. Requires precision in screening, passing angles, and decoy movements [S13, pp.116-118].

## Formation
- O4 (left block) and O3 (right block) on opposite blocks
- O5 just above the free-throw line (between the blocks and the elbow)
- O2 at left-side free-throw line extended
- O1 with the ball, above the top of the key in the middle of the court

## Phases

### Phase 1: Cross Screen Entry
- O1 dribbles to the free-throw line extended on the right side
- O2 holds position at free-throw line extended on the left side
- O3 sets a **cross screen** for O4, who moves to the low post on the right side
- As soon as O4 uses O3's screen, O5 **screens the screener** — screens O3
- O3 pops to the top of the key (close to the free-throw line)
- O5 **immediately posts up strong** on the left block

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.13 — O3 cross-screens for O4 (right block), O5 screens the screener (O3 pops top), O5 posts left block, O1 at right wing with ball -->

### Phase 2: First Decision — O1 Reads Two Options
- **Option A**: Pass to O4 on the right block (direct post-up)
- **Option B**: Pass to O3 at the top of the key (setup for indirect feed)
- If O1 passes to O3: O3 quickly passes to O2 on the weak-side left wing
- O2 executes a **good pass into the low post** where O5 is in position

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.14 — O1 passes to O3 (top of key), O3 passes to O2 (left wing), O2 feeds O5 at left block -->

### Phase 3: Post-Up and Screen-the-Screener Action
- After O2 inserts the ball to O5, O2 runs a **split-action screen with O3**
- Simultaneously, O4 moves in behind O2 and sets a **back screen on O2's defender**
- This is a second screen-the-screener action: O4 screens O2's defender as O2 dives to the basket
- O5 keeps an eye on O2 cutting to the basket

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.15 — O2 passes to O5 (left block), O2 runs split action with O3, O4 back-screens O2's defender, O2 dives, O3 rotates to free-throw line extended -->

### Phase 4: O5 Decision — Three Options
1. **Pass to O2 on the dive** (layup/foul)
2. **One-on-one low-post move** (if defender is caught in the screen action)
3. **Pass to O3**, who has rotated to the free-throw line extended for a jump shot
- Peripheral players must keep defenders occupied and away from O5

## Key Coaching Points
- Defenses will recognize patterns over time — the cross screen + screen-the-screener gives a different look than the Power Down
- Precision required: "screens, passes, and decoy moves must be made with precision" [S13, p.116]
- O5 must have proper skill to read the post and distribute, not just score
- Ball may take 3-4 passes to reach O5 — teammates must stay active, read double-teams, and space the floor
- Floor balance and passing angles are critical for the kick-out game when doubles come
- O5 is the decision maker in Phase 4 — teammates must trust the post and cut hard on time

## Counters
- If the cross screen is scouted: O1 can call the Power Down attack instead, alternating looks
- If O4 is denied on the right block: O1 goes immediately to Option B (O3 at top of key)
- If O5 is double-teamed before catching: O3 at the free-throw line extended is the designed kick-out
- If O2's dive is covered: O5 takes the one-on-one opportunity

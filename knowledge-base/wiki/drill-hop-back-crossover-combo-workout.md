# Hop-Back to Crossover Dribble Combination Workout

## Objective
Build mastery of chaining a live-ball hop-back move into a crossover dribble pull-up shot — both left- and right-handed — through progressively larger rep blocks with free throws interspersed.

## Setup
- Number of players: 1 (solo, no passer required — live-ball self-initiated)
- Court area: half court, anywhere on the floor the player initiates a live-ball move
- Equipment: basketball, hoop
- Three scalable workout volumes are defined (40, 80, or 120 combo reps + matching FTs)

## Execution

### Workout #4-19 / #4-20 (40 reps) [S3]
1. **1-Dribble Hop Back to Crossover (L):** Take one dribble, execute a hop-back footwork stop, then immediately initiate a crossover dribble to the right for a pull-up shot — 5 reps (or 10 for the 80-rep version).
2. Shoot 5 free throws (FTs).
3. **1-Dribble Hop Back to Crossover (R):** Same sequence going the other direction — 5 reps.
4. Shoot 5 FTs.
5. **2-Dribble Hop Back to Crossover (L):** Two dribbles before the hop-back, then crossover pull-up — 5 reps.
6. Shoot 5 FTs.
7. **2-Dribble Hop Back to Crossover (R):** Same to the other side — 5 reps.
8. Shoot 5 FTs.
- **Total: 40 shots (20 FG + 20 FT)**; target 60% FG (12-20), 85% FT (17-20).

### Workout #4-20 (80 reps) [S3]
- Double all rep counts above (10 per set instead of 5).
- **Total: 80 shots (40 FG + 40 FT)**; target 60% FG (24-40), 85% FT (34-40).

### Workout #4-21 (120 reps) [S3]
- Set sizes increase to 20 combo reps and 10 FTs per block.
- **Total: 120 shots (80 FG + 40 FT)**; target 60% FG (48-80), 85% FT (34-40).

## Coaching Points
- **Load with purpose:** Each dribble before the hop-back should be an aggressive, directional dribble — not a wasted bounce.
- **Crisp hop-back:** Land in a balanced two-foot athletic stance on the hop-back before initiating the crossover — rushing the landing leads to off-balance shots.
- **Tight crossover:** The crossover after the hop-back must be low and quick, not looping — "protect the ball, not the air."
- **Free throws as reset:** FTs between sets are not rest — they train mental discipline and shooting consistency under mild fatigue.
- **Chart results:** Players record FGA, FGM, FTA, FTM, and percentage every session. Target benchmarks: 60% FG, 85% FT.
- Alternate directions each set to develop equal skill with both hands.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (40 reps):** 5 reps per set, learn the footwork pattern and hop-back landing.
2. **Intermediate (80 reps):** 10 reps per set, increase game-speed execution.
3. **Advanced (120 reps):** 20 reps per set, full conditioning load with maintained technique.

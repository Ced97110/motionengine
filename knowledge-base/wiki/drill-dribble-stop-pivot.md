# Dribble, Stop, Pivot

## Objective
Integrate dribbling, stopping, and pivoting into one sequential skill, building the foundational ball-handling chain that underlies all half-court offense.

## Setup
- Players line up at different spots on the floor (scattered across the half or full court)
- Each player has a basketball
- Coach observes from a central position
- No cones or additional equipment needed

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up at different spots on the floor
2. On coach's signal, all players begin to dribble (speed dribble or control dribble as designated by coach)
3. Players may dribble in any direction
4. After three dribbles, each player stops (jump stop or stride stop as designated by the coach)
5. After the stop, players pivot (front or reverse, as designated by the coach)
6. Coach checks whether all three techniques are correctly executed; correct immediately

### Intermediate
- Non-stop 1-minute drill: players dribble, stop, and pivot, then pause a few seconds
- Players then begin dribbling in the direction they face, stop again, then pivot again
- Continuous repetition for the full minute

## Coaching Points
- Enforce proper fundamentals on all three components: dribble, stop, AND pivot
- "Players tend to take short cuts if you allow it" — do not accept lazy footwork
- Break the drill into parts if needed: drill only the dribble and stop without the pivot, or only the stop and pivot without the dribble, to address weak spots
- The stop type (jump vs. stride) changes the pivot foot options — emphasize this distinction

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach-designated stop type, pivot type; 3 dribbles then stop
2. **Intermediate**: Player self-directed continuous dribble/stop/pivot for 1 full minute
3. **Advanced**: Add a pass and cut after the pivot (see [[drill-dribble-stop-pivot-pass-cut]])

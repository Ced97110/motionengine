# Basketball Sports Medicine and Science — Summary

## Overview
*Basketball Sports Medicine and Science* (edited by Laver et al., ESSKA 2020) is the definitive sports medicine reference for basketball. The book brings together contributions from team physicians, physical therapists, orthopedic surgeons, and performance scientists from NBA, WNBA, EuroLeague, and national team programs worldwide. It covers the full spectrum from epidemiology and biomechanics through surgical management, non-operative rehabilitation, and return-to-performance frameworks.

The book is organized into sections covering: the demands of basketball, injury epidemiology, lower and upper extremity injuries, spine injuries, special medical topics (concussion, cardiac, dermatology), and performance/return-to-sport topics. Chapters ingested here focus on two areas: (1) tendinopathy rehabilitation (patellar and Achilles) and (2) on-court rehabilitation from treatment table to return to performance.

The clinical frameworks are NBA/WNBA caliber — written by practitioners from the Brooklyn Nets, LA Clippers, and Philadelphia 76ers — providing practical, court-side applicable protocols that bridge sports medicine and strength and conditioning.

## Key Themes
1. **Progressive tendon loading** as first-line, evidence-based treatment for tendinopathy — with pain-monitoring models (NPRS) guiding intensity and recovery days
2. **Return to performance vs. return to play** — the distinction that rehabilitation is not complete until pre-injury KPIs are reached
3. **Early basketball integration** in all rehabilitation phases — even non-weight-bearing athletes can use a ball for form shooting, ball-handling, and core work
4. **Motor learning principles** (external focus, implicit learning, differential learning, contextual interference) applied to secondary injury prevention on court
5. **Interdepartmental collaboration** — medicine, S&C, sports science, and coaching working together using shared KPIs and objective milestones

## Unique Contributions
This source is the only one in the wiki that provides:
- Clinical tendinopathy loading classifications with recovery day guidelines (Table 59.5)
- Sport-specific tendon loading exercise tables for patellar and Achilles tendons (Table 59.6)
- NBA/WNBA practitioner-authored return-to-performance KPI framework
- The "working backwards" method for deconstructing basketball skills into rehabilitation components
- Scripted return-to-practice progression (jogging halfcourt → live 5v5 full court)
- Motor learning principles (Gokeler et al.) applied specifically to basketball rehabilitation
- Cognitive load and perturbation training applied to basketball-specific exercises (Table 60.5)

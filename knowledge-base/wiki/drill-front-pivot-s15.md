# Front Pivot (Drill #35)

## Objective
Develop the front pivot as a foundational stopping and pivoting skill — keeping the player closer to the basket and protecting the ball before passing.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- 4 lines at one baseline
- 1 basketball per player
- Duration: 2 minutes

## Execution
### Beginner
1. Players line up in four columns at the baseline (figure 1).
2. The first player in each line **dribbles** (control or speed) to the far endline.
3. At the endline, execute a **jump stop** (or stride stop) and a **front pivot**.
4. Pass back to the second player in line — choice of **chest pass, bounce pass, or overhead pass**.
5. All players repeat the sequence.

### Front Pivot Mechanics (Teaching Points — figure 2)
1. Come to a complete stop and **establish the left foot as the pivot foot**.
2. Just before pivoting, bring the ball to **chin height**, holding with both hands using fingerpads (not palms) — this is the **triple-threat position**.
3. From the left pivot foot, **step (swing) the right (free) foot forward** in the intended direction to face a new target — this is the front pivot.
4. The pivot foot (left) stays planted; only the free foot moves.

### Intermediate
- Each player executes a **controlled dribble anywhere on the court** (free dribbling). When the coach calls **"Front pivot!"** every player immediately jump-stops and executes the front pivot. On **"Go!"** players resume dribbling.
- Variation: Players execute dribbling moves (e.g., spin dribble) while waiting for the command.

## Coaching Points
- **Front pivot keeps you closer to the basket**: Unlike the reverse pivot, the front pivot pivots the free foot forward — keeping the player facing the basket and maintaining scoring threat.
- **Triple-threat position at the stop**: Ball must come to **chin height** before the pivot — this is the starting position for the shot, drive, or pass.
- **Establish pivot foot before swinging**: Common error is moving the pivot foot — emphasize that only the free foot swings.
- **Control the stop**: Players must stop **completely under control** before pivoting — a stumbling stop leads to a travel.
- **Eyes on the target**: After pivoting, find the next pass receiver or the basket immediately.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk-through — dribble 3 steps, stop, front pivot to face the coach. No passing yet.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-court dribble, jump-stop, front pivot, pass back. Both feet as the pivot foot (alternate trips).
3. **Advanced**: Intermediate variation with dribble moves en route; add a live passer/defender who closes out on the pivot to stress the triple-threat hold.

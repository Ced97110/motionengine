# Flex Elbow Shooting Drill

## Objective
Build game-realistic elbow jump shots off a fake cut, developing footwork precision and timing on the catch-and-shoot from both elbows.

## Setup
- Half court
- One coach with two balls at the middle of the half-court
- One player at the low-post spot
- No rebounder required (coach can retrieve, or add one)

## Execution
1. Player fakes a cut into the lane.
2. Player cuts high to the right elbow.
3. Coach delivers the ball; player receives, squares up, and shoots.
4. Player then cuts back into the lane and repeats to the left elbow.
5. Continue for a set number of shots or a set time.
6. Focus on precision of all details: the quality of the fake, the cut angle, and the jump shot.

## Coaching Points
- The fake must be convincing — sell it before cutting up
- Feet must beat the ball to the elbow: land with balance, not scrambling
- "Point 10 toes at the rim" on landing
- Stay low through the cut; rise into the shot — "play low to high" [S7, p.307]
- Wrists bent back at catch; no fumbling — be shot-ready before the ball arrives

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Walk-through of fake and cut; no defender
2. **Intermediate:** Game-speed cuts; coach adds early/late passes to force adjustment
3. **Advanced:** Add a chasing defender behind the cutter to practice shooting with defensive pressure

# Single-Leg Hurdle Jump

## Objective
Develop single-leg explosive takeoff power to improve layup push-off speed and first-jump quickness for rebounds.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A row of low hurdles (or cones/markers if hurdles unavailable)
- Open floor space
- Hurdles set at a height appropriate for player's strength and ability

## Execution
1. Stand erect on one leg with the opposite leg slightly elevated and the standing knee slightly flexed.
2. Flex the knee, hip, and trunk of the standing leg, descending slowly toward the floor.
3. Immediately jump vertically off one leg, quickly extending the body while simultaneously throwing the arms upward to propel the body over the hurdle.
4. Land softly on the **same leg**, slightly flexing at the knee, hip, and trunk to absorb landing forces.
5. Upon landing, immediately jump over the next hurdle with the same leg.
6. Execute the prescribed number of hurdles or repetitions.
7. Repeat the entire set with the opposite leg.

> **No hurdles available?** Jump as high as possible in a forward direction for the same training effect. [S2, p.111]

## Coaching Points
- **"Land soft, spring fast"** — the goal is minimal ground contact time
- Arm throw is critical: synchronized upward arm drive adds height
- Absorb landing through the full kinetic chain — ankle, knee, and hip all flex
- Keep the trunk stable; avoid excessive forward lean on landing
- Watch for collapsing knee on landing (valgus collapse) — correct immediately

## Primary Muscles
- **At takeoff:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medius)
- **At landing:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus [S2, p.111]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Perform without hurdles — single-leg vertical jumps in place, focusing on soft landing mechanics
2. **Intermediate:** Add low hurdles (12 in./30 cm), 3–4 hurdles per set
3. **Advanced:** Increase hurdle height (up to 30 in./75 cm), reduce rest, increase consecutive reps

## Variations
### Double-Leg Hurdle Jump
Perform the same exercise using both legs simultaneously to propel over each hurdle. Good starting point before progressing to single-leg. [S2, p.111]

# Pistol Squat

## Objective
Develop unilateral lower-body strength, balance, and proprioception through a single-leg squat to parallel depth, directly translating to one-leg landing stability, off-balance shot-making, and contact resistance in basketball.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- 12- to 24-inch (30-61 cm) plyometric box
- No additional equipment needed (bodyweight version)
- **Variation**: hold one or two dumbbells/kettlebells as counterweight or to increase intensity

## Execution
1. Stand on the **edge of a plyometric box** on one foot, with the opposite leg held suspended off the side (a).
2. Squat down by **pushing the hips back** and flexing the supporting knee.
3. **Extend the arms forward** to maintain balance.
4. Lower the hips until the top of the **supporting thigh is parallel to the floor** (b).
5. Extend the supporting knee and hip to **return to the beginning position**.
6. Complete all reps on one side, then repeat with the other leg.

## Coaching Points
- Supporting knee must **not move beyond the toes** of the supporting foot
- **Minimize ankle movement** at the supporting ankle — ankle stability is tested, not ankle flexion
- Do **not let the knee of the supporting leg cave inward** — it should track over the 2nd and 3rd toes
- Extending arms forward serves as a counterbalance — this is correct technique, not a cheat
- **Inhale** during the lowering phase; **exhale** during the upward phase

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight single-leg squat on the floor (no box) — use a chair for safety
2. **Intermediate**: Pistol squat on a plyometric box at controlled tempo
3. **Advanced**: Pistol squat holding a dumbbell or kettlebell as added load (counterweight or offset)

## Common Mistakes
1. **Knee caving inward (valgus)** → ACL risk → cue "knee over second toe" → add band squats for hip abductor activation
2. **Knee traveling past toes** → cue "push hips back first" → work on ankle mobility
3. **Not reaching parallel** → insufficient quad strength or hip mobility → use a box with a target for depth accountability
4. **Rapid or uncontrolled descent** → cue "control the down" → 3-second eccentric target

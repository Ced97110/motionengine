# Eight-Point Drill (#151)

## Objective
Challenge an individual defender through all 8 fundamental defensive scenarios — 4 strong-side and 4 weak-side — in one comprehensive drill covering the full defensive sequence from initial pass to post coverage.

## Setup
- Half court
- 1 defender (X1), 1 offensive player (1), 1 coach
- Coach at the elbow/wing area with the ball
- Lines of waiting players
- Rotation: 1 → X1 → end of line; next player becomes new 1
- Can drill all 8 points in one session, or strong side one day / weak side the next

## Execution

### Strong-Side Four
**Part 1 — Jump to the Ball:**
Player 1 passes to a coach. X1 must immediately **jump to the ball**. This forces player 1 to go backdoor.

**Part 2 — Post Defense:**
Player 1 stops in the **low post**. X1 plays post defense. If the coach can pass to player 1, both play **1-on-1 inside**; then return to the low post to continue.

**Part 3 — Corner Denial:**
Player 1 breaks out to the **corner**. X1 **denies the corner**. Player 1 can go backdoor if X1 overplays and makes it available.

**Part 4 — One-on-One:**
Wherever player 1 receives the ball, player 1 **squares up in triple-threat position**. The two players go **1-on-1**. [S15, p.314]

### Weak-Side Four
(After the strong-side series ends at the low post, player 1 breaks to the weak side.)

**Part 1 — Interception Stance:**
X1 must get into **interception stance** — two-thirds the distance from the coach, one-third the distance from player 1, one step off the line between them. The coach rolls the ball hard toward player 1; X1 must **pivot and intercept the pass**. [S15, p.315]

**Part 2 — Dribble Closeout:**
X1 gives the ball back to the coach. Coach begins a **drive baseline or middle**. X1 **closes out on the breakaway dribbler**.

**Part 3 — Pass Receiver Closeout:**
Coach tosses a **lob pass** to player 1. X1 **closes out on the pass receiver**. Player 1 squares up in triple-threat; both play **1-on-1**.

**Part 4 — Flash Pivot Denial:**
Ball is tossed back to the coach. Player 1 **flash pivots**. X1 must **deny the flash pivot cut**. Player 1 can go backdoor if X1 makes it available. When player 1 gets the ball, X1 and 1 play 1-on-1. [S15, p.315]

## Coaching Points
- This drill is advanced — execute all fundamentals correctly before the rotation phase.
- Coach controls the pace and which phase receives the most attention.
- Can be split: strong side in one practice, weak side in another.
- Each of the 8 parts is a complete defensive skill; the drill chains them all together as game situations would.
- Perfect for **late-season check-ups** on individual defenders. [S15, p.303]

## Progressions
1. **Walk-through**: Run each section without live defense first; X1 executes the footwork pattern without competition.
2. **Live**: Run the full 8 parts continuously with competitive offense.

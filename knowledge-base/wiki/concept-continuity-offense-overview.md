# Continuity Offense — Overview

## Summary
The Continuity (high-post) offense is a continuity motion system built around a 2-3 formation with two guards, two forwards near the free-throw line extended, and one post (5) who flashes to the high-post area at the elbows or free-throw line. All four perimeter players are interchangeable — each must be able to play both guard and wing positions. The offense operates through three foundational set options: **pass and screen away**, **pass and cut away**, and **pass and follow the ball (high split)**.

Unlike isolation-heavy pro offenses, the Continuity system keeps all five defenders busy at all times, never lets the ball remain in one player's hands for more than a couple of seconds, and typically creates multiple good scoring opportunities per possession. Its trademark action is the backdoor cut. [S7, pp.117-133]

## When to Use
- Against aggressive overplaying man-to-man defenses that try to deny entry passes
- When your personnel are skilled passers and cutters rather than elite one-on-one creators
- When building a fundamentals-based team culture where all five players contribute offensively
- Against any defense — the system generates answers to all defensive adjustments through its read-based structure

## Key Principles
1. **Offense starts with defense** — aggressive defense creates fast-break opportunities that feed directly into the flex/Continuity transition
2. **All players are interchangeable** — perimeter players must master both guard and wing duties; don't create a "strong side" and "weak side"
3. **The post must be a skilled passer** — the high-post hub (5) reads all defensive situations and distributes; if 5 can't pass, the offense breaks down
4. **Never banana cut** — backdoor cuts must be at a sharp angle, with a clear hand-target to the passer
5. **Constant movement** — players without the ball are always cutting or screening; the ball never stalls
6. **Players flow to new solutions without resetting** — no need to call plays or wait; the system is self-correcting
7. **Winning is in the details** — quality of execution matters more than quantity of repetitions; teach and correct rigorously

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (1)**: Primary ball-handler and initiator; can enter via pass or dribble; executes screen-away, reverse dribble, or pass-and-follow actions
- **SG (2)**: Perimeter player interchangeable with wing; reads backdoor opportunities when overplayed; sets screens and cuts
- **SF (3)**: Interchangeable wing; sets down screens, rolls to basket after screening, reads all cut options
- **PF (4)**: Interchangeable wing/forward; executes backdoor cuts, screens, pops out; most versatile position in the system
- **C (5)**: High-post hub — flashes to elbow or free-throw area to receive ball, reads defense, makes layup/back-screen/pass-and-roll decisions; can post low when needed

## Variations
### Pass and Screen Away
After a pass to the high post, the passer screens away for an off-ball player. Multiple options: backdoor cut off the screen, roller to basket, pop-out. See [[play-pass-and-screen-away]] for full option tree.

### Pass and Cut Away
After passing to the high post, both perimeter players cut away from the ball toward the opposite side, keeping four perimeter spots filled and eliminating weak-side/strong-side differentiation. See [[play-pass-and-cut-away]].

### Pass and Follow (High Split)
After passing to the high post, the passer follows the ball toward the post and executes a split action — pick-and-roll, backdoor, or pop-out — while the weak-side wing responds. See [[play-high-split-action]].

### Reverse Dribble
When no entry pass is available, the ball-handler initiates via a reverse dribble, triggering all other players to adjust positions and cut. Multiple sub-options exist. See [[play-reverse-dribble-options]].

### Chin Action
A specialized back-pick by the high post on the weak side of the half court, creating a variety of scoring opportunities through elbow-post screening and pass-and-screen sequences. See [[play-chin-series]].

### Sets Out of the High Pick
The post flashes to the elbow and immediately sets a high on-ball screen for the PG, generating PnR opportunities, double screens, and backdoor reads from a high-pick entry.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Banana-cutting on backdoor** → correction: cut at a sharp angle, give the passer a clear hand target, call for the ball only when open
2. **Ball stalling in post's hands** → correction: 5 must be a quick decision-maker; if no option is there in 2 seconds, dribble to generate a new angle
3. **Perimeter players standing still** → correction: every pass triggers a cut or screen; no stationary players
4. **Overcomplicating the offense** → correction: teach fundamentals first — cutting, screening, spacing — the system flows from mastery of basics
5. **Forcing the first option** → correction: multiple options exist on every possession; read and react to defense

# Paye Drill (Drill 17)

## Objective
Teach offensive players to **read the defender and select the appropriate move** — developing basketball IQ through systematic 1-on-1 reads before, during, and after the dribble across all phases of the game.

## Setup
- Individual or team drill
- Half court, perimeter (within shooting range of basket)
- **Teaching format**: one offensive player at a time in front of a coach-as-defender
- **Team format**: put a defensive player on an offensive player at each basket in the gym
- One ball per pair

## Execution

The Paye Drill is divided into **three parts**, each covering a phase of offensive ball-handling:

---

### Part I: Before the Dribble (Beginner & Intermediate)

**Three defensive reads the attacker must identify:**

**a. Stance** (first read):
- Front foot to **pivot foot**
- Front foot to **free foot**
- **Parallel** (even)

**b. Coverage** (second read):
- **Tight**: defender within one-half step of the attacker
- **Loose**: defender more than one-half step away

**c. Overplay** (third read):
- **Overplay right**: defender positioned right of the attacker
- **Overplay left**: defender positioned left
- **Straight up**: no overplay

**Drilling sequence:**
1. Player lines up in triple-threat stance.
2. Drill each phase for **10-second series** before progressing:
   - a. Front foot to pivot foot (10 sec)
   - b. Front foot to free foot (10 sec)
   - c. Parallel stance (10 sec)
   - d. Coverage: tight first, then loose (10 sec)
   - e. Overplay: 10 sec per stance × overplay left and right
3. Coach then begins **any combination** of stance, coverage, and overplay.
4. Attacker reads the coach's defensive mode → calls out the read exactly → tells the coach what move would best counter this defensive mode.

**Offensive responses (Part I)**:
- Beginners: use rocker step principles only
- Intermediates: all principles except after-the-dribble techniques
- Advanced: all principles

---

### Part II: During the Dribble (Advanced)

**Moves covered**: 
- **In-and-out** and its counter, the **crossover**
- **Spin** and its counter, the **half-spin**

**Defensive reads**: defensive maneuvers including the advance step, retreat step, swing step, slide step, and overplay.

---

### Part III: After the Dribble (Advanced)

**Offensive techniques covered**:
- **Pump fake**
- **Pump-fake crossover**
- **Pump-fake up-and-under**
- **Step-back move**

---

## Coaching Points
- The Paye Drill is the **master integration drill** — it connects all the stance-and-footwork skills into a unified decision-making framework.
- Players must verbalize the read AND explain the best counter before executing — this builds *basketball IQ*, not just muscle memory.
- Coaching cue: "Read first, then attack" — players who attack without reading are playing on instinct only; players who read and attack are playing with intelligence.
- The 10-second micro-series format prevents cognitive overload while building complete literacy of all defensive positions.
- Rotate through all positions (tight/loose, all stances, all overplay directions) so players do not develop blind spots.
- Part III (after-the-dribble) requires mastery of Parts I and II first — do not rush to pump-fake counters until pre-dribble and during-dribble reads are automatic.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Part I only — triple threat, rocker step reads with a passive coach-defender; player calls out the read verbally
2. **Intermediate**: Parts I and II — add dribble-phase reads (in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin)
3. **Advanced**: All three parts — live defender with full after-the-dribble pump fake, step-back, and up-and-under options

# Power Shot Drills — Post with Bubble Defense

## Objective
Develop post players' power moves (drop step, show-and-go, up-and-under) against token physical defense, building the ability to score in the paint under contact.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1–3 (Post player S, Passer P, Defender X)
- **Court area**: Paint / low block — first notch above the block, both sides of the lane
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball; optional: football bubble pads for the defender
- **Zones**: Left and right blocks worked equally

## Execution — 3-Player Version
1. Passer (P) starts with the ball outside the lane.
2. Post player (S) establishes position at the first notch above the block.
3. P passes into S; S catches and **chins the ball** (ball under chin, elbows out).
4. Defender (X) wears football bubble pads and applies **controlled physical pressure** (bumping, crowding) on S throughout the move.
5. S makes the appropriate power move based on imaginary or real defensive position:
   - **Drop step** (baseline or middle)
   - **Show-and-go opposite** (show one direction, drop step the other)
6. S finishes with a power shot (layup or short-range jump shot).
7. Rotate after 2 minutes: S → X → P → S.
8. Both sides of the lane are worked — 1 minute per side within the 2-minute window.

## Execution — Solo (Self-Toss) Version
1. Post player tosses the ball out in front (simulating a bounce pass).
2. Catches ball, **chins it**, and imagines where the defender is.
3. Makes the appropriate power move in that direction.
4. As ball is caught: take a **small hop** and land on **both feet simultaneously** — allowing either foot to become the pivot foot.
5. Two post players can work simultaneously (one on each side of the lane), staggering catches and shots so paths don't intersect.
6. Each shooter works for a **2-minute window**.

## Show-and-Go-Opposite Drop-Step Power Move
- Start on the first notch above the block.
- Show (pump fake) toward the middle → drop-step toward the baseline, or vice versa.
- Moves should go **both toward the middle AND toward the baseline**.
- Work from **both blocks** — post players must be unpredictable and ambidextrous.
- Coaching emphasis: **"Successful post players are unpredictable — they can go in either direction from both blocks."** [S6, p.63]

## Coaching Points
- Chin the ball immediately on every catch — protect it from the defender
- The small hop landing on both feet preserves **pivot foot options** — critical post footwork
- Bubble pads allow realistic physical contact without injury — contact is encouraged (within reason)
- Alternate the direction of power moves every rep — don't let the drill become predictable
- Defender should bump the shooter through the gather and finish — not just stand still
- Coach watches for: lost dribble, traveling on the pivot, off-balance finish, missing the second step after the drop

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Solo self-toss, no defender, one move per side
2. **Intermediate**: Live passer, token passive defender (no pads)
3. **Advanced**: Full bubble defender, both directions, both blocks, under 2-minute competitive pressure

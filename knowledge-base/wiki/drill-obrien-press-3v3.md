# O'Brien Press 3-on-3 Drill

## Objective
Develop the full press concept on an open court — no boundaries — while teaching defenders to recognize which press to use based on how the offense scored.

## Setup
- Full court, no out-of-bounds restrictions on inbound location
- 3 offensive players, 3 defenders
- Defenders can line up in white press (inbounder covered) or black press (inbounder not covered)
- Can make competitive by keeping score

## Execution
1. Designate which press (white or black) before the drill begins.
2. Defenders deny the inbounds pass per their press alignment.
3. Play live until the ball is dead (score, turnover, or out of bounds).
4. After each possession, defenders must **recognize which press to use next** based on how the three offensive players scored:
   - Near the basket → White press
   - From the perimeter → Black press
5. Defenders quickly learn the necessity of keeping the ball in their vision and how to read and react to various situations.

## Coaching Points
- "This is a key drill in helping your defense develop new concepts." [S7, p.251]
- Defenders must make the white/black decision on their own — no prompting from the coach.
- Emphasize aggressiveness in denying the inbounds pass, especially in the black press when temptation is lower since the inbounder isn't directly covered.
- Teach defenders to see both the ball AND their offensive player simultaneously at all times.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach calls the press; focus is on proper denial positioning.
2. **Intermediate**: Players decide which press based on how the offense scored.
3. **Advanced**: Both presses live with competitive scoring; losers run.

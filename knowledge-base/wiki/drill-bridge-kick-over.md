# Bridge Kick-Over

## Objective
Condition the shoulders, elbows, wrists, and vestibular system for full 360° backward rotation in preparation for the back handspring and back flip.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Flat floor surface next to a sturdy vertical object: wall, column, pole
- No equipment required
- Soft surface (grass, mat) recommended for beginners

## Execution
1. Lie flat on your back with your toes touching the base of a wall or sturdy vertical object; knees well bent.
2. Place your palms beside your ears, fingers pointing toward your toes, elbows angled up toward the ceiling.
3. Press up into a full **bridge hold** using both arms and legs (hips fully extended, back arched).
4. Lift one leg and press your foot firmly against the wall.
5. Under control, press through that foot against the wall until your second foot leaves the floor and extends over your hips.
6. Keep arms braced and rigid; continue pressing until your legs flip all the way over you.
7. Land on one foot first, with hands still in contact with the floor. Do not kick the ground with your toes on landing.

## Coaching Points
- "Keep your arms locked — this is a leg-drive drill, not a shoulder press."
- "Control the flip — don't let gravity take you; maintain tension throughout."
- Watch for athletes bending their elbows under load — this indicates insufficient shoulder/tricep strength; regress before continuing
- Do not attempt this drill without a solid bridge hold; the shoulder and wrist stress is significant [S9, p.182]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Push foot off the **top** of a high object (bed, bench) — the greater the height of the object, the less force is needed. Progress to lower surfaces, then stairs, then a vertical wall.
2. **Intermediate**: Begin with "walking" up the wall using 3–4 steps before the final press-through.
3. **Advanced**: A single forceful foot plant on the vertical wall executes a clean flip-over in one motion.
4. **Elite (optional)**: Kick off the floor rather than a wall — this is advanced and not required to progress to back handsprings [S9, p.182]

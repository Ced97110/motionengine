# Suicide Jump (Master Step)

## Objective
Master all fundamental components of explosive jumping — powerful lift-off, hip/waist integration, maximum tuck, foot speed faster than gravity, and expert force absorption on landing — by passing a bar under the feet at peak jump height.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A length of dowel (broomstick) held with a wide grip in front of the hips; or a lightly-gripped rope or belt for beginners [S9]
- Any open space

## Execution
1. Stand shoulder-width apart, body flexed for action. Hold the dowel with a wide grip in front of the hips (or behind for the reverse variation). Stand up straight — do NOT squat to lower the bar. [S9]
2. Dip only *slightly* (less than a quarter-squat) — not a deep squat.
3. Without pausing, explode upward, tucking the knees as close to the chest as possible.
4. At the peak of leg movement, whip the bar under the jumping feet so it ends up behind you.
5. After the thread, throw the legs back down for landing.
6. Upon landing, catch most weight on the balls of the feet.
7. Reposition and repeat, or immediately perform a *reverse suicide jump* (bar held behind, pulled forward under feet). [S9]

## Coaching Points
- "Stand up straight" — beginners instinctively want to squat lower to hold the bar lower, but this is wrong; the height must come from the jump [S9]
- The dip is minimal — less than a quarter squat — to allow maximum elastic rebound
- If you cannot bring knees very high *and* quickly, it is impossible to pass the bar — this is the self-diagnostic built into the exercise [S9]
- Requires five mastered skills: powerful lift-off, hip and waist integration, high tuck, ability to throw feet down faster than gravity, expert landing force absorption [S9]
- Once suicide jumps are mastered, kip-ups and front/back flips become substantially more accessible [S9]
- Reverse suicide jump (bar behind, pulled forward) is significantly harder and should only be attempted after the standard version is fully mastered [S9]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use a rope or belt (more flexible, more forgiving) in place of a rigid dowel [S9]
2. **Intermediate**: Standard dowel, forward pass
3. **Advanced**: Reverse suicide jump (bar behind hips, pulled forward under feet); angled pike jump (legs at an angle, nearly extended); full pike jump (straight legs parallel to floor at peak) [S9]

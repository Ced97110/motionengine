# Fronting Drill (Drill 98)

## Objective
Teach post players how to receive the ball against a fronting defender using an overhead lob pass — including proper arm placement, target-hand technique, step, catch, and finish without bringing the ball down.

## Setup
- Individual or team — intermediate level
- Low block area (right side of lane in the diagram)
- **3 roles**: Player 5 (post attacker), X5 (fronting defender), Player 3 (passer on the wing/corner)
- **Rotation**: offense 5 → defense X5 → end of 3's line → 3 → end of 5's line

## Execution
1. **Player 5 flash pivots** to the big block or slightly above. **X5 fronts** Player 5.
2. Player 5 uses the **body to push X5 a step or so farther outside** — not so far that X5 moves behind Player 5.
3. Player 5 **turns to face the baseline**, placing the **right arm against X5's lower back** — upper arm at 90° with lower arm (seals X5 in front).
4. Player 5 **raises the left arm and hand high overhead** — gives Player 3 a target for the overhead pass.
5. Player 5 **waits until the pass is directly overhead**, then **steps forward once** toward the basket with the lead foot (left foot).
6. Player 5 **catches the ball** and — **without bringing the ball down** — **leaps toward the basket** and lays it in.
7. **Player 3 throws an overhead flip pass** to Player 5 — pass should be thrown about a **step farther** than where Player 5's target hand is held.
8. **If Player 5 needs to reestablish footwork**: bring the ball down, pump fake, then lay it in.
9. **X5 jumps back toward the basket** as the ball is being thrown overhead. X5 is behind the attacker and must time the jump to try to block the layup without fouling.

## Coaching Points
- **Pass placement**: semi-lob, just slightly out of the reach of a jumping defender.
- Player 5 must **face out of bounds** — the step is taken with the **lead foot first** (left foot per the diagram).
- "Do not bring the ball down" — catch, keep ball up high, lay it in.
- If the catch is off-balance, use pump fake as a reset before the layup.
- The lob pass must arc enough to clear X5 but not so high it allows recovery.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Remove X5 — Player 5 practices the step, catch, and finish without a defender.
2. **Intermediate**: Add X5 as described — live fronting.
3. **Advanced**: Add an on-ball defender guarding Player 3 to make the entry pass harder.

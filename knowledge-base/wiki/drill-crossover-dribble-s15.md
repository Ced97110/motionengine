# Crossover Dribble Maneuver (Drill #29)

## Objective
Develop the crossover dribble — transferring the ball quickly and low from one hand to the other to change direction and attack past a defender.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- 4 lines at one baseline
- **Tape 2×2 foot (.6×.6 m) boxes** at midcourt and ~20 feet (6 m) from each baseline
- 1 basketball per player
- Duration: 1 minute

<!-- DIAGRAM: Four columns of players at baseline. Three taped boxes per column. Players cross over at each box and attack in the new direction with a speed dribble. [S15, p.65] -->

## Execution
1. Players line up in four columns at the baseline.
2. Dribble to each box and execute the **crossover dribble**:
   - Keep the crossover dribble **low** (no higher than mid-lower leg) and **quick**.
   - Once the ball crosses to the opposite (left) hand, bring the **right foot forward and beside the defender's right foot**.
   - **Speed dribble straight** (or slightly veering right) and explode toward the basket.
3. Continue down the floor using the **right hand**, and return using the **right hand** (this develops the move going both toward the center and toward the sideline). Then go down and back **left-handed**.
4. At the far end, execute a **reverse (or front) pivot** and return.
5. **Jump-stop** about 10 feet (3 m) from the next teammate in line and deliver a **chest or bounce pass**.
6. All players repeat the sequence.

## Options
- **Race format**: Guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Advanced Progression
1. **Alternating dual-ball**: Alternate bouncing each ball while driving.
2. **Simultaneous dual-ball**: Both balls touch hands and bounce at exactly the same time.

## Coaching Points
- **Low and quick**: During the crossover, the ball is in its **least protected position** — keep the dribble **no higher than the middle of the lower legs** and get it across fast.
- **Eyes downcourt**: Eyes on the downcourt area — **never** on the ball during execution.
- **Protect after crossing**: After the ball lands in the left hand, the **right side should be close to the ball** to shield it from the defender.
- **Head and shoulder fakes**: Use fakes before every crossover to keep the defender off-balance.
- **Straight-line explosion**: The attack after the crossover should be in an **almost straight line** down the floor.
- **Fingerpads**: Always dribble with the first pads of the fingers.
- **Defenders on their heels**: Use excessive head/shoulder fakes before every move.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary crossover — practice the hand transfer motion before adding movement.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-court bilateral drill with box stops and pivot/pass at end.
3. **Advanced**: Dual-ball; add a live defender at each box.

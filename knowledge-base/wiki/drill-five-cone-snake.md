# Five-Cone Snake Drill

## Objective
Improve cutting ability, change of direction, and body position control by sprinting in a weaving snake pattern around 5 cones.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 5 cones in a 10-yard square with center cone:
  - Cone 1: bottom right (start area: midway between cone 1 and cone 2)
  - Cone 2: top right
  - Cone 3: top left
  - Cone 4: bottom left
  - Cone 5: center
- Player starts midway between cone 1 and cone 2 on the right side

## Execution
1. Start in a ready position midway between cone 1 and cone 2.
2. **Sprint forward** around cone 2.
3. **Sprint diagonally** around cone 5 (center).
4. **Sprint diagonally** around cone 3.
5. **Sprint forward** around cone 4.
6. **Sprint diagonally** around cone 5 (center).
7. **Sprint diagonally** around cone 1.
8. **Sprint** back to the starting position (midway between cone 1 and cone 2).

## Coaching Points
- Attack each cone — do not slow down, redirect at the last moment
- Cut as tightly as possible around each cone
- Sprint at maximum effort between every cone
- "Chase the cone" — lean into each curve with the inside shoulder

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk the pattern first; then jog.
2. **Intermediate**: Full sprint at game speed.
3. **Advanced**: Time each repetition; compete against teammates or personal best.

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — repeated acceleration-to-cut pattern mirrors off-ball movement
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — tight cone turns load the SSC for explosive redirections

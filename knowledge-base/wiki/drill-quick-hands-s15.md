# Quick Hands

## Objective
Develop hand quickness, defensive stance, the ability to deflect a live dribble, and the transition from defense to offense in a competitive setting.

## Setup
- **Players**: Full team in two lines; one player steps out at a time (or two for advanced)
- **Court area**: Half court, players arranged in lines as shown in diagram — coach at the top of the key
- **Equipment**: One basketball per player in line; coach also has a ball
- **Duration**: 2 minutes total

## Execution

**Beginner:**
1. Player 1 steps out directly in front of the coach and hands the coach their basketball.
2. Player 1 assumes the defensive stance requested by the coach.
3. On the coach's signal, player 1 begins rapid-fire foot movement — tapping toes on the floor.
4. Player 1 keeps hands in proper defensive position, constantly jabbing hands forward and pulling them back "like the strikes of a snake," alternating right and left hands.
5. After 5 seconds of proper stance and footwork display, the coach dribbles one dribble (alternating fast/slow) until player 1 deflects the ball.
6. After deflecting, player 1 retrieves the basketball and moves to the end of the opposite line. Player 2 steps out.

**Intermediate:**
1. Instead of deflecting, player 1 tries to catch the basketball.
2. When catching cleanly, player 1 pivots to face the basket, executes a rocker step or other perimeter move, then uses a dribbling move to drive to the basket.

**Advanced:**
1. Both players 1 and 2 step out in front of the coach simultaneously and face each other in defensive positioning, using rapid-fire foot movement and defensive jabs at each other for 5 seconds.
2. Coach then dribbles one dribble (alternating hard/quick and slow/low).
3. Players 1 and 2 battle to deflect the dribble; both race to recover any slapped ball.
4. Whichever player recovers the ball is on offense; the other is on defense — play 1-on-1 to the basket.
5. Both players go to the opposite end of their starting lines; players 3 and 4 step out.

**Options (all levels):**
1. Perimeter vs. post, one-guards vs. two-guards — play a game to 10 (basket = 2 pts, offensive rebound = 1 pt)
2. Lay the ball on the floor instead of dribbling — players race to retrieve
3. Toss the ball directly in front of the coach instead of dribbling — players race to retrieve

## Coaching Points
- Players use proper defensive stances and slides throughout
- Cue: "Jab like the strike of a snake" — quick forward strike, quick retraction
- Players make fundamentally sound offensive moves when they gain possession
- Players learn to read the defense and use the proper attacking move — this is the "savvy" the book references [S15, p.xx]
- The competitive recovery element makes this one of the most game-realistic early drills

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Single player, deflect the dribble, return to line
2. **Intermediate**: Catch instead of deflect, then attack with a live-ball move
3. **Advanced**: Two-player competition for the loose ball → immediate 1-on-1

# 40-Yard Square Drill — Sprint, Bear Crawl, Backpedal

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction, body position, and transition between movement skills (sprint → bear crawl → backpedal → bear crawl) in a continuous pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4 cones positioned in a 10-yard (9-meter) square:
  - Cone 1: start line
  - Cone 2: 10 yards straight ahead
  - Cone 3: 10 yards to the left of cone 2
  - Cone 4: 10 yards to the left of cone 1

## Execution
1. Start in a ready position at cone 1.
2. **Sprint** forward from cone 1 to cone 2.
3. **Bear crawl** left (facing out) from cone 2 to cone 3.
4. **Backpedal** from cone 3 to cone 4.
5. **Bear crawl** right from cone 4 back to cone 1.
6. Upon reaching the start, immediately repeat the drill going in the **opposite direction** (clockwise).
7. Two consecutive trips around the square (one counterclockwise, one clockwise) = one full set.

## Coaching Points
- Keep hips low during bear crawl — do not bounce or raise the hips
- Maintain athletic backpedal posture: lean forward slightly, weight on balls of feet
- Transition quickly at each cone — no pausing at corners
- "Attack each corner" — accelerate into every change of direction

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: One direction only (counterclockwise); rest between each trip.
2. **Intermediate**: Both directions back-to-back as described.
3. **Advanced**: Reduce rest between sets; add a ball-handling component during the sprint phase.

# Down Screen Reads — the source coach

## Summary
The down screen is set near the baseline. The screen receiver (cutter) waits until near the screen to set up the defender, then runs into it. The cutter reads the defender's reaction to determine whether to curl, cut back, or bump-and-pop, while the screener reads the same cues to determine whether to pop, come high, or cut to the lane. [S7, p.56]

## When to Use
- When a shooter needs to get free from a defender on the weak side or from the baseline
- As a primary action in continuity offenses (flex, Continuity, motion)
- In ATO (after timeout) plays to free the best shooter for a catch-and-shoot

## Formation
Three players involved: ball handler at the top or wing, screener (5) coming from the lane to set a screen near the baseline, screened player/shooter (2) starting near the baseline and running off the screen.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Setting the Screen
- Screener (5) moves to a position near the baseline, establishes wide low stance.
- Cutter (2) waits until close to the screen, sets up the defender, then runs into the screen.

### Phase 2: The Three Reads

**Read 1 — Defender Trails the Screened Player** [S7, p.56]
- Defender is caught behind the cutter coming off the screen.
- **Cutter (2)**: Curl around the screen, receive the ball on the curl toward the basket.
- **Screener (5)**: Pop out to the corner as a safety valve.

**Read 2 — Defender Anticipates the Cut** [S7, p.56]
- Defender jumps to the cutter's anticipated path before the screen.
- **Cutter (2)**: Cut back (backdoor or back-cut), going opposite the anticipated direction.
- **Screener (5)**: Come high as a safety valve for the ball handler.

**Read 3 — Defender Slides Under the Screen** [S7, p.56]
- Defender takes an under path on the screen, positioning low to cut off the curl.
- **Cutter (2)**: Bump and pop out to the corner (defender went under, so the top/outside is open).
- **Screener (5)**: Cut into the lane (opposite of the cutter's new direction).

## Key Coaching Points
- The cutter's setup is crucial — walk the defender toward the baseline first, then change direction into the screen. A premature or obvious cut gives the defender time to navigate around.
- The screener must stay wide and low; a narrow or high stance allows defenders to squeeze through.
- Screen angle should be 90° to the defender's path.
- Screener always goes **opposite** the cutter's final direction.
- On the curl (Read 1): cutter must brush the screener's shoulder as tightly as possible to prevent the defender from recovering.

## Counters
- If defense consistently switches (Read 2 variation): screener slips to the basket before contact for a mismatch.
- If defense fights through every screen: set a flare screen instead to change the angle and force a different defensive decision.

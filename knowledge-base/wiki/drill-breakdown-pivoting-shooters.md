# Breakdown Pivoting Drills for Shooters

## Objective
Isolate and ingrain the inside-heel pivot and free-foot square-up for both cutters/shooters and dribblers/shooters before incorporating live shooting, maximizing repetitions and coaching feedback. [S6, pp.164-168]

## Setup
- **Players:** 3–4 players at each circle; up to 7 simultaneous circles (six foul-line circles + center jump circle) = up to 28 players working at once
- **Court area:** All available foul-line circles and center jump circle in the gymnasium
- **Equipment:** One basketball per group of 3–4 players
- **Passer position:** Coach, manager, or a ball placed in the **center of each circle** to represent a hypothetical passer; this keeps the passer in the same position regardless of where cutters are on the circle
- Coach in center can also see the footwork of all players in their group simultaneously

## Execution

### Version 1 — Cutter/Shooter Drill (No Live Shot)
1. 3–4 players jog **clockwise** slightly outside the circle line, controlling their pace.
2. On a whistle, each player stops jogging and **tosses the ball slightly ahead** of themselves (self-pass).
3. As they catch the self-pass, each player **drives the inside (right) heel straight down**, pivots off it, and **swings the free (left) foot** around to square up to the imaginary basket at the center.
4. Pull the ball into the shooting pocket and simulate the shot (do NOT shoot at an actual basket — eliminates distraction and saves time).
5. On the next whistle, players begin jogging clockwise again.
6. Repeat for several reps, then **switch to counterclockwise** jogging — this makes the **left foot** the new inside pivot foot.
7. Drill both directions equally; players who avoid their weak side must be motivated to address it.

### Version 2 — Dribbler/Shooter Drill (No Live Shot)
1. Same circle setup. Players **dribble with the outside hand** (left hand going clockwise; right hand going counterclockwise).
2. On the whistle, all four dribblers **kill the dribble** by driving the inside heel into the floor simultaneously with the last dribble.
3. Make a **front pivot** off the inside heel to square up to the imaginary basket.
4. Swing the free foot around; simulate the shot.
5. On next whistle, resume dribbling.
6. Repeat; switch directions to practice both pivot feet.

**Counterclockwise group (Diagram 3-19 / 3-20):** Inside foot = left foot; outside dribble hand = right hand.

## Coaching Points
- **"Drive the heel straight down"** — not a slide or a shuffle, a downward stomp into the floor that kills lateral momentum.
- **Watch for drift** — if a player's jump travels sideways, their heel is not planting early enough or hard enough.
- **Hands must be ready before the catch** — both hands should arrive in the shooting pocket simultaneously with the heel plant.
- **Free foot must complete its arc** — it should swing all the way around so shoulders are fully squared before the simulated jump.
- **Motivate players toward their weak direction** — players always prefer going the direction where they are strongest; force counterclockwise reps for right-handed shooters.
- Set individual, group, or team goals (e.g., consecutive perfect pivots) to add competition and game realism. [S6, p.169]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Version 1 (cut/catch, no dribble, no actual shot) — focus purely on heel plant and square-up.
2. **Intermediate:** Version 2 (dribble, no actual shot) — same footwork demand but with active dribble termination.
3. **Advanced:** Add live shooting to the basket OR incorporate a shot fake before 1–2 dribbles before shooting. "Catcher catches the ball, shot fakes, and then dribbles one or two dribbles before actually shooting off the dribble." [S6, p.169]

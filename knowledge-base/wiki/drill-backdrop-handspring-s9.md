# Back-Drop Handspring (Front Flip Chain — Step 5)

## Objective
Learn the take-off and rotation mechanics of a front handspring by first mastering an incomplete rotation — landing on feet then falling back to glutes — building confidence and physical preparation for the full handspring.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- **Heavy padding required** — mattress, gymnastics crash mat, or stacked floor pads
- Minimum 15–20 feet of clear space

## Execution
1. Take **one step** to build momentum. Raise hands overhead. Kick off with strongest leg.
2. Allow the torso to fall as hands drop to the floor. As hands approach the ground, **jump up hard**, swinging the highest leg.
3. Keep arms fairly straight as feet leave the floor — "catching yourself with palms."
4. Let momentum from the jump carry legs above the torso.
5. Allow forward momentum to turn you over. As you spin: **tuck the head, curl the trunk, whip hands up off the floor.**
6. **Feet contact the floor first** (shock absorbers), then glutes, arms, and back also contact the ground.

## Coaching Points
- **"Jump hard as hands approach the ground"** — The explosive jump is the engine of rotation; do not passively fall into the handspring
- **Arms whip up off the floor** — The arm push-off action creates rotation; arms must actively press and release, not stay passive
- **Feet first, then butt** — The landing sequence (feet → glutes → back) protects the spine; "never land flat on your back if you can avoid it" [S9, p.20]
- **Padding is non-negotiable** — Beginners WILL land on their glutes or back initially; thick padding (mattress) is essential for spine safety [S9, p.14]
- **Very weak athletes can do this** — "Very little upper-body explosiveness is required to master this" [S9, p.14]
- **"Fall-back handspring" hidden step** — If the athlete begins landing on feet then falling back onto glutes, treat this as a valid sub-step between back-drop and running front handspring [S9, p.15]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Land on glutes after feet; use maximum padding; practice consistently until feet-first landing is automatic
2. **Intermediate**: "Fall-back handspring" — lands on feet and falls back; weight is over the feet, not in front of them
3. **Advanced**: Rotation is sufficient to stay upright after foot contact → advance to full front handspring (Step 6)

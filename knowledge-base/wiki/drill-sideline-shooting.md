# Sideline Shooting Drill

## Objective
Develop catch-and-shoot footwork from the wing areas off a crossing V-cut, reinforcing the inside-foot pivot and "shoot and stay" habits.

## Setup
- **Players**: Minimum 6 (can add a rebounder for advanced version)
- **Court area**: Half court; two lines at each **free-shot-line extended**, outside the three-point line on each wing
- **Equipment**: Multiple balls (one per player in the passing line)
- **Positions**:
  - **S** (Shooter): First player in one line, no ball
  - **P** (Passer): Players in the opposite line, each with a ball
  - Optional: **R** (Rebounder) near the basket

## Execution
1. The first shooter (S) stands at the front of one line without a ball.
2. Shooter V-cuts **across toward the opposite line** and receives a pass from the passer (P).
3. Shooter pivots **heel-to-toe off the inside foot** and rises into the jump shot.
4. After shooting, **freeze the follow-through** — **shoot and stay** (do not follow the shot).
5. The **passer (P)** then becomes the next shooter: runs **outside** of the player they just passed to, moving in the opposite direction, and pivots off the inside foot (the **opposite foot** from the first shooter).
6. **Optional rebounder**: Retrieves the shot and dribbles (preferably with the weak hand) to the line the ball was passed from.
7. Continue in continuous flow.

**Key coaching note:** "The only time players should follow their shots is when they shoot inside the paint." — for all perimeter shots, shoot and stay. [S5, p.4]

## Coaching Points
- This drill mirrors the [[drill-baseline-shooting]] but from the **wings** — the footwork and movement principle is identical.
- The rebounder addition is **recommended** because it reinforces the shoot-and-stay habit and teaches the rebounder to pursue aggressively with weak-hand dribbling.
- Both shooters in the cross pattern use **opposite inside feet** — the first shooter uses one foot; the returning passer/shooter uses the other.
- Watch for overstride on the V-cut — players should cut with purpose but land in a balanced, compact position.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the V-cut and footwork; no shot — focus on inside-foot identification.
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill at moderate speed; add a rebounder.
3. **Advanced**: Game speed; instruct the passer to delay or vary timing to force the shooter to "wait" in athletic position before the catch.

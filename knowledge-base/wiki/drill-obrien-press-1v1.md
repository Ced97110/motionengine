# O'Brien Press 1-on-1 Drill

## Objective
Build the two core full-court press skills simultaneously: (1) denying the inbounds pass and (2) forcing the ball handler into a speed dribble while maintaining contact — then back-tipping if beaten.

## Setup
- Court split down the middle from basket to basket — only one side used
- Can run simultaneously at both ends
- Coach inbounds the ball into the near half of the court (the shaded area)
- 1 offensive player, 1 defensive player per pair
- Offensive player starts directly above the coach inbounding
- Defender lines up on the sideline side, in physical contact with the offensive player (hip and elbow)

## Execution
1. Coach attempts to inbound to the offensive player.
2. Defender is in physical contact (hip and elbow) on the sideline side, facing the coach, to deny the inbounds pass.
3. Defender's goal: prevent the ball from entering the court in his area.
4. If the ball is inbounded successfully, the defender immediately pressures the ball handler, forcing a **speed dribble** while staying in contact with the dribbler.
5. If the dribbler blows by the defender, the drill's focus switches to the defender **sprinting after the dribbler to attempt a back-tip**.
6. The ball can only be passed to the offensive player on this side of the court, from the top of the key extended to the baseline.

## Coaching Points
- "This drill works on disallowing the ball to be inbounded in front of the defender." [S7, p.249]
- Denial technique: hand/arm no lower than shoulder height; elbows bent close to body like a boxer, thumbs pointing at shoulders.
- Defender maintains vision of the inbounder AND his offensive player simultaneously — see both at all times.
- At the beginning of the season, emphasize denial **without fouling**.
- Back-tip cue: "When a player who is dribbling fast must suddenly slow down as he confronts a defender upcourt, he's often susceptible to the back-tip." [S7, p.248]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Denial only; coach tosses easy inbounds passes, defender works on body positioning.
2. **Intermediate**: Full denial + force speed dribble as described.
3. **Advanced**: Add a second offensive player — if the ball is inbounded, it must stay on one side of the court and play continues live 1v1 with back-tip pursuit if beaten.

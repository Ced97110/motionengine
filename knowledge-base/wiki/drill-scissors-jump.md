# Scissors Jump

## Objective
Build explosive hip movement speed, vertical jumping power, and trunk rotational strength through alternating split-position lunge jumps with mid-air leg switches.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat open space (no equipment needed)
- Starting position: split/lunge stance with one foot forward and one behind

## Execution
1. Start in a **split position** — one leg forward, one behind, in a partial lunge.
2. **Dip down** into the lunge, lowering the back knee toward the floor.
3. From the bottom of the dip, **explode upward** off both feet.
4. In mid-air, **switch the legs** — front leg goes back, back leg comes forward.
5. Land in the split position on the opposite side, absorb the landing, dip, and repeat.
6. Continue alternating for desired reps [S9, p.2].

## Coaching Points
- "Switch fully in the air" — incomplete leg switches reduce the hip drive demand and the benefit of the drill
- This drill develops **super-quick hip movement** — an athletic quality essential for many explosive actions [S9, p.2]
- The rotational trunk component ("twist") is a secondary benefit that keeps trunk muscles explosive
- Watch for: athletes who barely get off the ground — cue a more aggressive arm swing upward to maximize air time for the switch
- This is NOT a static split squat — the explosive jump and mid-air switch are the entire point
- Land with soft knees to protect the joints

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Step-switch (no jump) to learn the alternating split pattern.
2. **Intermediate**: Low jump with full leg switch — focus on clean landing mechanics.
3. **Advanced**: Maximum height, fast switch, minimal ground contact time — emphasize elasticity [S9, p.2].

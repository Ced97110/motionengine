# Standing Heel Raise

## Objective
Build plantar flexion strength in the gastrocnemius and soleus muscles to increase push-off power for running faster and jumping higher, while also supporting ankle stability.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor or edge of a step (for greater range of motion)
- Optional: dumbbells

## Execution
1. Stand with the knees fully extended. Optionally hold a dumbbell in each hand for added resistance.
2. Rise up onto the balls of both feet (lift heels off the floor as high as possible).
3. Slowly lower back to the starting position under control.
4. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.
5. **Progression**: Add more weight (dumbbells or weight vest); for greater range, stand on a step and lower the heel below the step level [S2, p.138].

## Coaching Points
- "Slow on the way down" — the eccentric (lowering) phase builds the elastic ability needed for explosive movements
- Keep the knees fully extended throughout to isolate the gastrocnemius
- Rise as high as possible on each rep — maximum plantar flexion
- "Calves give you the ability to push off the floor to run faster and jump higher" [S2, p.138]
- The goal is strength AND elastic ability in the muscle — not just raw strength

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bilateral, body weight, flat floor
2. **Intermediate**: Add dumbbells; or perform on a step for greater range
3. **Advanced**: Single-leg heel raise (unilateral); heavier load [S2, p.138]

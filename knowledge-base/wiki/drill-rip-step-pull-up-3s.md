# Rip Step Pull-Up 3s Drill Series

## Objective
Build the ability to pull up for a three-point shot directly off a rip-step live-ball move, training the footwork, balance, and shooting rhythm required to hit off-dribble threes at game speed.

## Setup
- 1 player, half court
- Three-point arc or perimeter area
- No additional equipment required; rebounder optional
- Free throws taken between sets at the free-throw line

## Execution

### Workout 2-22 (25-Shot Version — Introductory)
1. **Rip Step Pull-Up 3s** — 5 sets of 5 = 25 total three-point shots: From the perimeter, execute a rip-step live-ball move, take the dribble(s) needed to create, and pull up for a three-pointer.
2. **Free Throws between each set** — 5 sets of 5 = 25 total FTs
- **Target**: 60% from three, 85% from the free-throw line [S3, p.6]

### Workout 2-23 (50-Shot Version — Intermediate)
1. **Rip Step Pull-Up 3s** — 5 sets of 10 = 50 total three-point shots
2. **Free Throws between each set** — 5 sets of 10 = 50 total FTs
- **Target**: 3PT 15-25 (60%); FT 21-25 (85%) [S3, pp.7-8]

### Workout 2-24 (100-Shot Version — Advanced)
1. **Rip Step Pull-Up 3s** — 10 sets of 10 = 100 total three-point shots
2. **Free Throws between each set** — 10 sets of 10 = 100 total FTs
- **Target**: 3PT 30-50 (60%); FT 43-50 (85%) — Total shots: 200 [S3, pp.9-10]

## Coaching Points
- **Establish game-speed rip before pulling up** — the rip step must be aggressive and realistic; if the move isn't convincing, the pull-up loses its value against a defender.
- **Land in balance on the pull-up** — two-foot landing on the hop back; do not fade, lean sideways, or rush the shot before balance is established.
- **Hold the follow-through** — freeze the shooting hand in the follow-through position until the ball hits the floor to build consistent muscle memory.
- **Chart every session** — use 3PTA/3PTM/% and FTA/FTM/% columns; track progress over time to measure improvement.
- **Free throws are not optional** — the free throw sets between shooting sets build mental focus under accumulated fatigue, simulating late-game free-throw situations.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout 2-22, 25 shots)**: 5-shot sets allow correction between sets; focus on footwork and balance before adding volume.
2. **Intermediate (Workout 2-23, 50 shots)**: 10-shot sets demand sustained concentration; add a passive/token defender to simulate shot creation.
3. **Advanced (Workout 2-24, 100 shots)**: Full game-speed execution at high volume; player must maintain technique and percentage standards throughout all 10 sets.

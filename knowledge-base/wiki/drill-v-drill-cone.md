# V-Drill Cone Drill

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction between forward sprint and backpedal, building the plant-and-go explosive transition used constantly in basketball defense and transition.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 3 cones in a V-shape (90-degree angle):
  - Cone 1: starting point (bottom of V)
  - Cone 2: 10 yards diagonally forward and to the left
  - Cone 3: 10 yards diagonally forward and to the right

<!-- DIAGRAM: Cone 1 at bottom of V. Cones 2 and 3 are each 10 yards up-and-diagonal from cone 1, forming two arms of the V. Player sprints left up to cone 2, backpedals to cone 1, sprints right to cone 3, backpedals to cone 1. See [S8, p.104] -->

## Execution
1. Start at cone 1.
2. **Sprint diagonally** forward and to the left to cone 2.
3. **Plant left foot** at cone 2.
4. **Backpedal** diagonally back to cone 1.
5. Without pausing, **sprint diagonally** forward and to the right to cone 3.
6. **Plant right foot** at cone 3.
7. **Backpedal** diagonally back to cone 1.
8. One sprint + backpedal in both directions = one repetition.

## Coaching Points
- Plant foot hard at each outer cone — weight low, drive the hips back into the backpedal immediately
- Do not slow down approaching the cone — "attack and redirect"
- Backpedal with forward lean and quick, short steps — not a lazy backward walk
- Keep eyes up and forward at all times

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reduce distance to 5 yards per arm; focus on clean foot plant.
2. **Intermediate**: 10-yard spacing at full speed.
3. **Advanced**: Add a reaction component — coach points left or right to indicate which cone to sprint to first.

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — the forward-backward plant-and-go pattern is fundamental to transition defense
- [[defensive-transition-principles]] — backpedal mechanics transfer directly to getting back on defense

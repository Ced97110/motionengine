# Shell Drill (Youth — 10 Parts)

## Objective
Build complete team man-to-man defense by drilling all six off-ball defensive rules plus help rotations, post coverage, and screen defense in a flexible 4-on-4 half-court framework.

## Setup
- **Players**: 4 attackers + 4 defenders (rotate after 5 minutes or 2 consecutive stops)
- **Court area**: Half court
- **Equipment**: None
- **Formation**: Change daily — 2-2, 1-3, 1-2-1, or mirror your next opponent
- After ~2 minutes, rotate guards (1/2 and X1/X2) to forward positions (3/4 and X3/X4) so all players practice guard and wing defense

## Execution

The 10 parts are run progressively or combined:

1. **Positioning** — 1, 2, 3, 4 pass (including skip passes) and hold 3 seconds. X1–X4 jump to the ball while the pass is in flight. Help-side defenders maintain ball-you-man and a flat triangle position.

2. **Rotation** — Coach activates a baseline or middle drive. Weak-side deep defender (X4) calls "rotate" and picks up the breakaway dribbler. Weak-side outside defender (X2) drops to defend the basket. Original ball defender rotates to cover the open attacker.

3. **Close the gap** — 1–4 take turns dribbling toward the basket. Nearest defender on the drive side steps in to stop penetration. All defenders help then recover to their assignments.

4. *(Implicit)* — Interception stance (before gap closes) transitions to close-the-gap stance.

5. **Pass penetration** — Whenever a pass enters a side, high, or low post, the nearest perimeter defender dives toward the ball to force it back to the perimeter.

6. **Flash pivot defense** — Player 4 flash pivots after 1 passes to 3. X3 should deny the pass to 3; X4 must then deny the flash to 4. X1 and X2 sag to help X4. Play 1-on-1 until the ball returns to the perimeter.

7. **Cutter drill** — Whenever a pass is made, the passer cuts. Defenders jump to the ball; middle cuts are never allowed. Other attackers V-cut to maintain 15-foot (4.5 m) spacing.

8. **Screen away** — Passer screens away from the ball. The two players in the screen-and-roll/fade/replace read the defense's tactics (switch or stay) to determine primary and secondary targets.

9. **Screen on ball** — Passer screens the ball handler. Help-side defenders sag to help on the ball screen. On-ball screen defenders use jump-switching maneuvers.

10. **Shell drill with a post** — Activate post defense coverage choice (three-quarter, front, or play behind). When the ball enters the post, all perimeter defenders dive toward it to help.

11. **Two or more tactics combined** — Run any combination of parts from the same possession to address what created problems in the last game.

## Coaching Points
- "Jump to the ball while the pass is in flight" — never move after the catch
- Help-side defenders always maintain **ball-you-man** and stay in a **flat triangle**
- Interception stance transitions to close-the-gap stance as the ball enters your area
- Middle cuts must **never** be allowed — jump to the ball immediately
- Rotation (Part 2) is the defense's last chance to correct a mistake — it must be automatic
- Change formations daily to prevent stagnation and to prep for opponents

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Parts 1–3 only (positioning, rotation, close the gap)
2. **Intermediate**: Add Parts 4–7 (pass penetration, flash pivot, cutter, screen away)
3. **Advanced**: Add Parts 8–11, including random movement by all four attackers and post activation

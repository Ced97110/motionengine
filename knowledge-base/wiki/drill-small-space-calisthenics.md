# Small Space Calisthenics Drills

## Objective
Develop total-body explosive power, abdominal strength, lateral push-up power, and unilateral hip power using three solo, no-equipment drills that can be performed in any small space as warm-ups, finishers, or supplementary work.

## Setup
- 1 player (all drills are solo)
- Any flat surface — no equipment required
- Minimal space needed (approximately 2m × 2m)
- Can be inserted into any explosive training session as warm-up, variety work, or finisher [S9, p.4]

## Execution

### Drill 1: Sideways Pop-Ups
1. Begin in a push-up position, arms extended.
2. Bend the arms into the bottom of a push-up position.
3. Explosively pop the *entire body* (not just hands) approximately 6 inches to one side, landing in the same push-up position.
4. Repeat to the other side: left, right, left, back, forward — using all directional variations.
5. Can be combined with clap push-ups in the same set for variety. [S9, p.4]

### Drill 2: Pike Slaps
1. Lie flat on the ground. Balance on your butt (hips are the pivot point).
2. Simultaneously raise the torso *and* legs explosively into a pike position (body forms a V).
3. At the peak of the movement, slap both insteps with both hands.
4. Lower back down and immediately bounce back up — keep the rhythm fast and continuous.
5. Do not sacrifice speed for height. [S9, pp.4-5]

### Drill 3: Jump Kick
1. Assume a staggered fighting stance — one leg forward, one leg back.
2. Quickly snap the *back leg* forward and upward.
3. Use that leg's momentum to whip up the *other* (originally front) knee.
4. At the peak of the double-knee-drive, extend the lead knee into a kick.
5. Land under control and repeat or switch legs. [S9, p.5]

## Coaching Points
- **Sideways pop-ups**: The whole body moves — not just the hands. If only the hands shift, it's a lateral hand walk, not a pop-up. Think of it as a full-body lateral plyometric push-up. [S9, p.4]
- **Pike slaps**: "All explosive pulling work requires steely abs" — this drill specifically targets abdominal power under speed. If you can't keep the bounce rhythmic, slow down slightly but never reduce speed for more reps. [S9, p.5]
- **Jump kick**: This is a *low-stress* exercise for the hips — it's one of the rare small-space drills that loads the legs *unilaterally*. Focus on the snap of the back leg driving the front knee upward. [S9, p.5]
- These drills can be performed *rhythmically for higher reps* unlike the step-chain progressions — they work well as conditioning finishers. [S9, p.4]
- All three work with any of the main explosive chains as supplementary or warm-up work.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Each drill performed slowly with full control; focus on body position
2. **Intermediate**: Continuous rhythmic performance at moderate speed; 3-5 reps per direction (pop-ups), 5-8 reps (pike slaps), 5-10 reps per side (jump kicks)
3. **Advanced**: Maximum-speed rhythmic performance; combine all three in a circuit with minimal rest; integrate clap push-ups into sideways pop-up sets

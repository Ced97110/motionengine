# Super Transition Drill

## Objective
Develop elite offense-to-defense and defense-to-offense transition under overload conditions — one team (Squad A) continuously competes against two alternating squads (B and C) that always have a positional head start, making the drill harder than any real game situation.

## Setup
- **Players:** Minimum 15 (three squads of 5); rotate players onto Squad A throughout.
- **Court area:** Full court.
- **Equipment:** Multiple basketballs; at least one assistant coach stationed near each frontcourt hash mark.
- **Three squads:**
  - **Squad A:** The "overloaded" team; no rest, no positional advantage.
  - **Squad B:** Half-court defensive team; repositions toward the 10-second line after each possession.
  - **Squad C:** Fast-break team; pre-positioned near the 10-second line and frontcourt hash marks for a permanent head start.
- Three coaches may run the drill simultaneously, one with each squad.

## Execution

### Phase 1 — Half-Court Offense (Squad A vs. Squad B)
1. Squad A runs their half-court offense or offensive entries against Squad B's defense.
2. Possession ends via: **turnover**, **missed shot**, or **made shot**.

### Phase 2A — Transition After a Turnover (Diagram 1-58)
1. On coach's whistle, Squad A drops the ball (simulated turnover).
2. Squad B recovers the loose ball and immediately outlets to an **assistant coach** near the hash mark.
3. Assistant coach hands off to **C1** who dribbles the full length of the court.
4. C1's teammates (Squad C) have already sprinted ahead and are running the primary and secondary fast break at the opposite end.
5. **Squad A** must sprint back and prevent any uncontested shots.

### Phase 2B — Transition After a Missed Shot (Diagram 1-59)
1. Squad A shoots; Squad B secures the defensive rebound.
2. Squad B outlets to the assistant coach → assistant hands to C1.
3. Same Squad C fast break as above, with the same head-start advantage over Squad A.
4. If Squad A **makes** the shot, the assistant coach uses a **new ball** and outlets to C1 immediately to start Squad C's break.

### Phase 3 — Squad A Converts to Offense (Diagram 1-60)
1. When Squad A regains the ball on defense against Squad C, they immediately reverse direction and sprint back to their original offensive end.
2. Squad A runs their own primary and secondary fast break against Squad B (who has repositioned to the 10-second line for a head start back on defense).
3. Squad A has NO positional help — they run the full length of the floor.
4. Squad A then attacks Squad B in their half-court offense, and the cycle repeats.
5. As Squad A attacks Squad B, Squad C repositions at the hash marks, ready for the next fast break.

### Transition Assignments Within Squad A
- **Tailback (TB) = Safety:** No offensive rebounding responsibility; gets back on defense immediately after every shot.
- **Halfback (HB):** Half defensive transition, half offensive rebound responsibility; most flexible assignment.
- **Three Fullbacks (FBs):** Full offensive rebounding responsibility — chase every missed shot.
- On transition to defense: all five sprint in defensive fast-break lanes, looking over the inside shoulder to intercept outlet passes.
- **Basket Man** (first defender back): Runs to the middle of the free throw lane and calls "Basket!"
- **Ball Man** (second defender back): Positions at the top of the key and calls "Ball!"

### Squad C Options Each Possession
- Force a shot out of the primary or secondary break.
- Force a pass likely to become a turnover.
- Remain patient and look to score in a controlled manner.

## Coaching Points
- Squad A never gets a rest or a positional advantage — substitute players in continuously.
- Watch Tailback discipline: "Get your tail back — zero offensive rebounding."
- "Look over your inside shoulder while sprinting back — intercept the outlet."
- Basket Man and Ball Man must communicate loudly immediately: "Basket!" / "Ball!"
- Ball Man stops penetration; Basket Man protects both blocks and initially covers wing passes.
- When Ball Man's wing is passed to, Basket Man rotates out to guard that ball; Ball Man drops to protect the paint.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Walk through each phase with no live defense from Squad C; emphasize tailback/halfback/fullback assignments.
2. **Intermediate:** Full speed but Squad C only runs primary break; no secondary break.
3. **Advanced:** Squad C instructed to run primary AND secondary break; Squad A works into half-court offense after stopping the break.

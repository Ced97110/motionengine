# Uphill Acceleration Run

## Objective
Enhance starting power and improve stride length during acceleration by running short explosive efforts up a hill.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A hill with an incline of 20 to 35 degrees
- No equipment required beyond a marked starting point
- Distance/time is self-regulated (4–8 seconds of effort)

## Execution
1. Position yourself at the base of the hill in your starting stance.
2. Explode up the hill for a 4- to 8-second effort, driving powerfully with each stride.
3. Count your strides and mark or note your position at the end of the timed effort.
4. Rest fully, then repeat the run.
5. In each subsequent run, attempt to cover the same distance with **fewer strides** — the goal is longer, more powerful strides.

## Coaching Points
- The incline forces a forward lean that mimics proper acceleration mechanics — use this to reinforce natural acceleration posture.
- Drive the arms hard to complement leg drive.
- Focus on each foot pushing powerfully into the hill, not just spinning the legs fast.
- Count strides each rep; progress = same distance, fewer strides = greater stride length.
- Keep efforts short (4–8 seconds) to maintain maximum intensity and avoid lactic acid buildup that undermines neuromuscular quality.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 4-second efforts, moderate hill (20°), 3–4 reps
2. **Intermediate**: 6-second efforts, steeper hill (25–30°), 4–6 reps
3. **Advanced**: 8-second efforts on 30–35° incline, 6–8 reps, challenge stride-count PR each set

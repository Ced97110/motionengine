# Flex Offense Shooting Drill — Two Players

## Objective
Practice getting open off a vertical screen and an overload cut in the corner, replicating the two primary scoring positions generated by the flex offense.

## Setup
- 2 players per group; 2 coaches (or managers) act as passers at the elbows/wings
- 2 lines of players outside the baseline, each line with a ball
- Half court, around the low-post area on each side
- Players 2 and 3 start at the low-post positions on opposite sides

## Execution
1. The first two players (2 and 3) enter the court from the baseline lines, each with a ball
2. Both players pass their balls to the coaches (positioned as passers)
3. **2** cuts along the baseline and comes off the screen set by **3** (simulating the flex back screen)
4. **3** comes high after setting the screen (simulating the screen-the-screener pop)
5. Both players receive passes from the coaches at their respective spots and shoot
6. Each player grabs their own rebound
7. Players go to the **opposite line** from where they started
8. **Next repetition inverts the roles**: 3 comes off 2's screen (baseline cut), and 2 comes high

## Coaching Points
- Screener must set a legal, physical screen — no moving
- Cutter should read the defender: "Come off tight shoulder-to-hip — don't give daylight"
- Cutter must be ready to shoot immediately on the catch — "catch and fire"
- Player coming high should stop at the correct receiving spot before catching
- Self-rebound and quickly exit to the correct line — keep pace high
- Coaches vary pass timing to simulate real flex reads

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Both players are stationary screener/cutter at half-speed; no defender
2. **Intermediate**: Add live defenders on both cutters; read over/behind the screen
3. **Advanced**: Add a third player (1) at the top who initiates the action with a pass, making it a full 3-player flex rep

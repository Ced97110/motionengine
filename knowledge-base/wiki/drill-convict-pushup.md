# Convict Pushup / Behind-the-Back Clap Pushup (Power Pushup Step 7)

## Objective
Develop elite-level upper-body explosive pushing power by generating sufficient airtime and shoulder speed to clap both hands behind the back before returning to catch the floor.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor
- No equipment; padded surface recommended given the fall risk for beginners

## Execution
1. Squat down, place palms on floor, stretch legs out behind
2. Palms below shoulders at shoulder-width or wider
3. Feet at comfortable width
4. Keep legs, hips, torso aligned and horizontal
5. Dip ~1/3 depth
6. Immediately push explosively off floor
7. In mid-air, whip hands **back** and **clap them together behind the back** (hands briefly in handcuff position)
8. Return hands forward to catch floor
9. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- This is a significant step up from hip-strikes — do not attempt without mastering Steps 4-6 first [S9, p.96]
- **Regression**: practice the kneeling version first (Step 2 style) to get the feel of the rear clap before doing it from full plank [S9, p.96]
- The behind-the-back clap places hands briefly in a "handcuff position" — hence the name convict pushup [S9, p.96]
- "Don't fall flat on your face" — start kneeling to learn the mechanics safely [S9, p.96]
- Progression: front clap + rear clap (×2) in same airtime is achievable for advanced athletes; quintuple clap pushups (3 front + 2 rear or similar) are the extreme progression [S9, pp.96-97]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Kneeling convict push-off to learn rear-clap mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Full flat floor convict pushup; consistent clean rear clap
3. **Advanced**: Front clap + double rear clap in same airtime; combine with half-super and full body pop-up training before advancing to Superman

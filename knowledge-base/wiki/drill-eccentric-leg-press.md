# Eccentric Leg Press

## Objective
Restore eccentric quadriceps strength in the injured leg to resolve jumper's knee, improve landing stability, and establish the strength baseline needed for return to plyometric activity.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Leg press machine (seated or incline)
- Weight: start below body weight

## Execution
1. Set up in the leg press machine with feet approximately shoulder-width apart and toes turned out slightly. Select a weight less than body weight.
2. Push both legs until they are fully extended (concentric phase uses both legs).
3. Remove one foot from the platform. Over a slow count of eight, lower the weight using only the injured leg to the starting position (eccentric phase is single-leg).
4. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions and switch legs [S2, p.144].

## Coaching Points
- The key is the slow eccentric (8-count lowering) on the injured leg — this is the therapeutic component [S2, p.144]
- Start with sub-body-weight resistance; once the player can control body weight, progress to body-weight activities on the floor
- "Not only does eccentric strength enhancement help resolve jumper's knee, but it also provides enhanced strength and stability during jump landings" [S2, p.144]
- Do not rush the tempo — a shorter count defeats the purpose
- Monitor for knee pain during the eccentric phase; adjust load if pain occurs

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light load (50-70% body weight), 8-count eccentric, bilateral concentric
2. **Intermediate**: Body weight equivalent, 8-count eccentric
3. **Advanced**: Once body weight is mastered, transition to body-weight floor activities (squats, step-downs) [S2, p.144]

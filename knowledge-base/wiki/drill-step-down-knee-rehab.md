# Step-Down (Knee Rehabilitation)

## Objective
Enhance eccentric quadriceps strength and single-leg control by stepping down from a box; also used as a functional readiness test to determine if a player is ready to begin running after jumper's knee.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Plyometric box or step
- Start height: 4 inches (10 cm); progress to 8 inches (20 cm)

## Execution
1. Stand on a plyometric box. Begin with a 4-inch (10 cm) box and progress in height until 3 sets of 10 reps can be performed from an 8-inch (20 cm) box.
2. Step down slowly with the healthy leg while dorsiflexing the ankle. Make floor contact with the heel. Maintain control of the stepping-down knee — do not allow it to turn inward (valgus) or outward (varus). The injured leg stays on the box throughout.
3. Return the healthy leg to the box.
4. Perform the prescribed number of reps and switch legs [S2, p.146].

## Coaching Points
- The injured leg is the working leg — it is eccentrically loading the quad to control the step-down
- "Do not allow the knee to turn inward (valgus) or outward (varus)" [S2, p.146] — knee alignment is critical
- Contact with the heel first on the way down; this cues proper dorsiflexion and eccentric control
- "This exercise enhances eccentric strength and emphasizes the quadriceps. It also is used sometimes to determine readiness to initiate running activities" [S2, p.146]
- Progress box height only when valgus/varus is fully controlled

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 4-inch (10 cm) box, 1-2 sets of 8-10 reps
2. **Intermediate**: 6-inch (15 cm) box, 3 sets of 10 reps
3. **Advanced**: 8-inch (20 cm) box, 3 sets of 10 reps — readiness test for running [S2, p.146]

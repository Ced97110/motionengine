# On-the-Ball Pressure (Fratello)

## Summary
Mike Fratello's on-the-ball pressure system teaches defenders to pressure the ball handler at all times, force a speed dribble, jam the ball on the pick-up, and rotate quickly in help-and-recover or full-rotation situations. Fratello argues that pressuring the ball is the single most important aspect of defense regardless of scheme — if the ball moves freely around the perimeter or gets easily into the low post, no defense will be effective. [S7, pp.237-240]

## When to Use
- Every possession in man-to-man defense
- When the ball handler is isolated on the wing or has two dribbles or fewer remaining
- When the defense wants to force tempo changes or turnovers
- As the foundation before adding any zone or press principles

## Key Principles
1. **Pressure the ball first** — "Pressuring the ball is the key to any type of defensive set and is by far the most important aspect of this phase of the game." [S7, p.240]
2. **Step and slide, not reach** — The defender steps and slides to cut off the dribbler's path to the lane and to the sideline; never reaches with the hands.
3. **Jam the ball on the pick-up** — When the dribbler picks up the ball, the defender raises hands with wrists crossed to jam and mirror the ball, preventing any pass.
4. **Use chest, not hands, on closeouts** — On closeout drills the defender hits the dribbler with his chest to cut off the baseline path, not with a push of the hands.
5. **Communicate constantly** — X1 and X2 must call out "baseline!" and "middle!" to prevent the ball from going to the baseline or splitting the defense.

## Player Responsibilities
- **On-ball defender**: Step and slide to cut off lane and sideline; when dribbler picks up ball, raise crossed wrists to jam; prevent all passes; chase with back-tip if beaten.
- **Nearest help defender**: Stunt toward middle penetration to help on-ball (help-and-recover); communicate with on-ball defender; rotate if full rotation is called.
- **Weak-side defenders**: Slide to corner or basket-side to take away skip passes; be ready to rotate on full-rotation.

## Variations

### Help-and-Recover
When the ball is driven toward the middle, the nearest defender (X2) steps in to help stop penetration and then immediately recovers to his own offensive player. X1 stays with the ball; X3 is positioned to help X2 if 2 penetrates, then recovers to 3. [S7, p.239]

### Full Rotation
If penetration cannot be stopped by a stunt, the next-closest defender fully rotates off his offensive player and traps the ball. The next defender rotates to cover the open man. For example: 1 penetrates to middle → X2 traps, X3 rotates to 2, X1 rotates to 3. [S7, p.239]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Reaching with hands** → Correction: use body position, step-and-slide to cut off the dribbler.
2. **Failing to jam on the pick-up** → Correction: the instant the dribbler picks up the ball, raise crossed wrists immediately to mirror and jam.
3. **Poor communication on help-and-recover** → Correction: defenders must verbally call out before rotating so teammates know their assignment.
4. **Not closing out with the chest** → Correction: accelerate into the dribbler's path and absorb contact with the chest, not a hand-push.

# Power Clean (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop total-body explosive power through a coordinated lower-body drive and upper-body pull that closely mimics the vertical jump pattern essential for basketball.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Barbell with appropriate load
- Olympic weightlifting platform preferred
- No spotter needed (athletes can drop weight safely if required)

## Primary Muscles Trained
Gluteus maximus, biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, soleus, gastrocnemius, trapezius [S11, p.8]

## Execution
1. **Beginning position**: Stand with feet slightly turned out, hip-width apart, holding the bar near the shins with a closed pronated grip (palms facing body), elbows fully extended. Hands shoulder-width apart or slightly wider.
2. **First pull**: Under control, lift the bar from the floor keeping shoulders over the bar and back flat (neutral spine). Keep the bar close to the body and hips down. As the bar rises, raise the hips slightly to prepare for the second pull.
3. **Second pull**: Explosively extend the hips, legs, and ankles while keeping elbows fully extended. Thrust shoulders up and slightly back; drive hips slightly forward and up. Rise onto the toes before the arms begin pulling. Keep the bar close to the body.
4. **Rack**: As the lower body becomes fully extended, rapidly shrug the shoulders and begin the arm pull. Drop to a quarter squat and rack the bar on the front of the shoulders. Rotate the wrists under the bar while raising the elbows through in front so the upper arm is parallel with the floor. Stand up straight.
5. **Lowering**: Under control, lower the bar to the thighs, keeping it close to the body. Flex the hips and knees to lower the bar to the floor with back flat.

## Breathing Guidelines
- Take a deep breath before initiating the first pull off the floor
- Exhale during the aggressive, explosive jumping action of the second pull [S11, p.9]

## Coaching Points
- **"Do not yank the bar from the floor"** — controlled initial pull is critical
- **"After the initial pull, be explosive"** — first pull is controlled, second pull is maximum aggression
- Keep the bar close to the body throughout the entire lift
- No spotter; athletes can drop the weight if needed [S11, p.6]
- Maintain neutral spine throughout the first pull
- "Stay focused, stay balanced, and maintain proper posture" — general Olympic lift cue [S11, p.6]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hang clean (bar starts above knees) — reduces technical demand of the first pull and directly simulates the vertical jump position
2. **Intermediate**: Power clean from floor with submaximal loads — focus on technique and explosiveness
3. **Advanced**: Full power clean with heavier loads; add lateral clean variation for basketball-specific lateral power

## Variations
### Lateral Clean
Hold a dumbbell in the right hand with a pronated grip. Assume the same stance as the power clean/hang clean with the dumbbell between the legs. Perform the explosive jumping action of the power or hang clean, but instead of jumping straight up, **laterally drive to the right with both legs** while pulling the dumbbell to the shoulders. When feet land, drop the hips and catch the dumbbell in an underhand (supinated) position at shoulder level. Return by jumping to the left and lowering the dumbbell across the body toward the floor (power version) or knees (hang version). Complete all reps on one side, then switch.

> **Why the lateral clean matters**: Basketball's movement demands are predominantly lateral and unilateral. The lateral clean directly trains explosive lateral power production — the physical quality required for defensive slides, offensive cuts, and attacking a driving lane [S11, p.10].

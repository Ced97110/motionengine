# The Racetrack (Workout #7-4)

## Objective
Integrate box ball-handling under pressure, open-court dribble moves, and game-speed scoring finishes into a single continuous full-court conditioning drill that mimics real fast-break and half-court scoring situations. [S3, p.175]

## Setup
- 1-2 players (1 offensive player; optional coach or partner to call "Go")
- Full court
- No cones required (imaginary box at top of key)
- Reps determined by athlete's conditioning level

## Execution
1. **Offensive player throws the ball off the backboard** and gets the rebound.
2. Immediately attacks the full length of the court at **full speed in a fast-break situation** — this is the "racetrack."
3. As the player approaches the top of the key box, he **slows down and handles the ball in the box** using:
   - Strong dribble
   - Rocker dribble
   - Rocker-to-crossover dribble
   - Switch dribble
4. When the coach (or partner) calls **"Go"**, the offensive player immediately **attacks and completes a scoring move** using:
   - Roll Out
   - Roll Out Crossover
   - Roll Out Behind the Back
   - Roll Out Between the Legs
5. Executes **any available scoring opportunity** after the move (layup, pull-up, three-pointer, etc.).
6. Gets the rebound and **immediately goes the length of the court again**.
7. Repeat for the predetermined number of repetitions based on the athlete's conditioning.

## Coaching Points
- **Tempo change is the key skill**: the whole point of the racetrack is learning to shift from full-speed fast-break pace (racetrack) to controlled half-court handle (box) to explosive live-ball attack (roll out). This mirrors real in-game pace changes.
- **Throw-off the backboard**: this simulates a missed shot rebound before the outlet, requiring the player to secure the ball with two hands before pushing the floor.
- **"Go" is a game read**: the coach's call simulates a defender getting set — the offensive player must react instantly, not plan ahead.
- **Full effort on every trip**: reduce reps before reducing effort; this drill only works at game speed.
- **Scoring variety**: do not take the same scoring action on each trip — vary between pull-ups, floaters, three-pointers, and drives.
- Coaching cue: *"Sprint the track, control the box, attack on the call — no wasted steps."*

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the sequence without defense or conditioning demand; 2 trips.
2. **Intermediate**: Full sequence at 75% sprint effort, 4-5 trips.
3. **Advanced**: 100% effort; coach delays the "Go" call randomly to increase decision pressure; add a live defender in the box (see [[drill-1-on-1-box-dribbling]]).

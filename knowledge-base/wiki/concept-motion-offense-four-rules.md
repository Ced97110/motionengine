# Motion Offense Four Rules (Youth Framework)

## Summary
The motion offense, as taught in youth basketball, is governed by four universal rules that every player must internalize before 2-on-2 or team play can be effective. These rules replace rigid set plays with a read-and-react framework that scales from 2-player to 5-player situations. When all five players follow these four rules simultaneously, the offense creates constant movement and consistent scoring opportunities without needing called plays.

The four rules are introduced progressively — two rules in the 2-player cutting drill and two additional rules in the 3-player cutting drill — so players assimilate each rule before the next is layered on. [S15, pp.118-121]

## When to Use
- Every half-court offensive possession in a motion-based system
- Teaching read-and-react basketball at the youth and intermediate levels
- As the foundation for all 2-on-2 and 3-on-3 drill work
- When the goal is developing decision-making, not just memorized play execution

## Key Principles

### Rule 1: Use the Cut Your Defender Gives You
1. If the defender drops away (sags) when you pass, execute a **middle cut** — cut between the passer and the nearest cone/defender
2. If the defender overplays the passing lane (front knee past toes), execute a **backdoor cut** — cut behind the defender toward the basket
3. If neither cut is available, execute a **V-cut** to replace yourself and maintain floor spacing
4. "Never, ever allow a backdoor cut when a middle cut should have been used" [S15, p.127] — the read must be correct before the cut is made

### Rule 2: Maintain 15-Foot (4.5 m) Spacing
1. All five players must stay at least 15 feet from one another at all times
2. If a teammate moves closer than 15 feet, the player must **dribble away** to re-establish spacing
3. A player may V-cut to any spot on the floor to restore proper spacing — this is always a legal motion offense action
4. Proper spacing creates driving lanes and prevents defensive help from being in position

### Rule 3: Every Player Must Move on Every Pass
1. When the ball is passed, **all players without the ball must make a cut** — V-cut, middle cut, backdoor cut, or flash pivot cut
2. Standing still after a pass is a violation of motion offense principles
3. This rule ensures the defense cannot rest and keeps all help defenders occupied
4. Players verbalize "V-cut," "middle cut," or "backdoor" with the reason: "Every player must move on every pass"

### Rule 4: When a Dribbler Moves Toward You, Cut or Fade
1. If a teammate with the ball dribbles **toward** a properly spaced player, that player must react immediately
2. **Cut** — go backdoor past the dribbler toward the basket
3. **Fade** — move away from the dribbler toward the perimeter
4. Staying put when a dribbler approaches collapses the spacing and clogs the driving lane
5. A **dribbling entry** into the motion offense is initiated when the ball handler dribbles toward an overplayed teammate, who then backdoor cuts [S15, p.136]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: All four rules apply to every player regardless of position. In the motion offense, roles are interchangeable — any player can be the passer, cutter, or dribbler.

## Progressions
### Teaching Sequence
1. **Introduce Rules 1 & 2** (2-player cutting, Drill 58): Players make cuts and verbalize name + reason. Six cones define middle cut zones and backdoor zones.
2. **Introduce Rules 3 & 4** (3-player cutting, Drill 59): Add a third player. All three must move on every pass. Introduce the dribbling entry and cut-or-fade rule.
3. **2-on-2 with defenders** (Drill 62–63): Players apply all four rules with live defenders without verbalization requirements. Read and react in real time.
4. **3-on-3 without dribbling** (Drill 64): Force sharp cuts and accurate passes — no dribbling allowed.
5. **3-on-3 with dribbling** (Drill 65): Full integration: pass, cut, dribble, drive, rocker step.

## Cone Teaching Method
Place 6 cones at different spots on the court (change locations daily). The cones serve as virtual defenders:
- Cutting **between** the passer and a cone = **middle cut**
- Cutting **behind** a cone = **backdoor cut**
- Replacing yourself to any open spot = **V-cut** [S15, p.118]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Backdoor cut when a middle cut was available** → Coach must stop play immediately and correct the read — "turnovers don't need correction; bad decisions do" [S15, p.123]
2. **Standing still after a pass** → Every player on the court must move when the ball moves — no exceptions
3. **Ignoring the dribbler approaching** → The player nearest the dribbler must immediately cut or fade, not wait to see what happens
4. **Spacing collapse** → Players bunch up; correct by requiring any player who is too close to immediately dribble or V-cut away to restore 15-foot distance
5. **Verbalization without understanding** → Players can name the cut but not explain the reason — push for the reasoning, not just the label

# Lateral Skaters

## Objective
Improve cutting ability and first-step lateral quickness through single-leg lateral bounding. [S8, p.169]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor or court space
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Begin with both feet together.
2. Push off laterally with one leg, bounding across to land on the opposite leg.
3. Upon landing, immediately push off in the opposite direction without pausing.
4. Continue for prescribed reps or time.
5. To develop quickness, perform as many reps as possible in 10 seconds or less.

## Coaching Points
- "Land and push" — there should be no hesitation or settling on the landing leg.
- Soft, controlled landings reduce injury risk; landing with a stiff leg invites ankle/knee stress.
- This drill closely mimics the lateral defensive slides and cuts used in basketball.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Shorter lateral distance; focus on controlled landing mechanics.
2. **Intermediate**: Increase lateral distance and speed.
3. **Advanced (Diagonal)**: Jump diagonally so as to move laterally and forward simultaneously — mimics defensive sliding angles.

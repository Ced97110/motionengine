# Daily Pre-Practice Routine (42-Minute Scoreboard Clock System)

## Objective
Systematically develop all six individual offensive skill categories (stretching, shooting, dribbling, pivoting/passing, rebounding, screening, transition) in concentrated 1-minute intervals for up to 24 players simultaneously before regular team practice begins.

## Setup
- **Players**: 12–24 (two 12-man squads: Varsity and Junior Varsity)
- **Court**: Full gymnasium with 2 main baskets + 4 side baskets (6 total), plus 2 dribbling lines (Lines 8 & 9), and 7 designated floor areas
- **Equipment**: One basketball per player; scoreboard clock set to 42:00; air horn or whistle for drill transitions
- **Coaches**: Minimum 2 — mobile, observing, correcting, motivating at all times
- **Groups**: Players organized in 2-man and 3-man groups; positions denoted as 1A (starting PG), 1B (backup PG), 11 and 12 (11th and 12th players)

## Execution

### Clock 42:00–38:00 (Minutes 0–4): Stretching Phase
- **Varsity**: Areas 1, 2, 3, 4 — ankle stretch, yogi/butterfly stretch, hurdle stretch (L & R), quad stretch, fingertip stretch, back stretch, lie-on-back shooting technique
- **JV**: Dribbling Lines 8 & 9 + all 6 baskets — hesitation dribble, front crossover, through-legs, behind-back dribbles
- Each activity lasts 1 minute; air horn signals each transition

### Clock 38:00–34:00 (Minutes 4–8): Post Moves & More Stretching
- **Varsity**: 6 baskets + Areas near baskets + Area 7 — show-and-go power moves (L/R), up-and-under power moves (L/R), whirl power moves (L/R), V-cuts, high-leg easy jog, basket education (L/R hands), rebound snatch off wall, Mikan shooting drill (L/R hand)
- **JV**: Areas 3, 4, 5, 6 — offensive rebounding technique drill, Pride drill

### Clock 34:00–33:00 (Minute 8): Wolf Defensive Drill / Baby Hook Shots
- **Varsity Group A (6 players)**: Area 7 — Wolf defensive drill (1 min)
- **Varsity Group B (6 players)**: Assigned basket — baby hook shots, left/right hand (baskets 1-6)
- **JV**: Areas 3 & 4 — run-and-jump defensive and offensive drill

### Clock 33:00–30:00 (Minutes 9–12): Pivoting and Passing
- **Varsity**: Areas 3, 4, 5, 6 — pivot-and-pass: passer (1 min), defender (1 min), pass receiver (1 min)
- **JV**: All 6 baskets — dot shots (left/right side, no fake → 1 fake → 2 fakes) in 1-minute intervals

### Clock 30:00–28:00 (Minutes 12–14): Dribbling
- **Varsity**: Dribbling Lines 8 & 9 — hesitation dribble, front crossover, through-legs, behind-back (2 minutes)
- **JV**: Areas 1, 2, 3, 4 — ankle stretch, yogi/butterfly, hurdle, quad, fingertip stretches (begin JV stretch cycle)

### Clock 28:00–24:00 (Minutes 14–18): Power Moves & JV Stretch
- **Varsity**: 6 baskets — show-and-go power moves, up-and-under, whirl power moves (L/R, both sides, baskets 1-3 and 4-6)
- **JV**: Areas 1, 2, 3, 4 — completing stretching routine

### Clock 24:00–19:00 (Minutes 18–23): Rebounding & Defensive Drills
- **Varsity**: Areas 3, 4, 5, 6 — offensive rebounding technique drill (1 min), Pride drill (2 min), run-and-jump defensive & offensive drill (2 min)
- **JV**: Area 7 + 6 baskets + wall areas — baby hook shots, Wolf defensive drill, Mikan drill, rebound snatch, basket education

### Clock 19:00–16:00 (Minutes 23–26): Dot Shots & Pivoting
- **Varsity**: All 6 baskets — dot shots (no fake → 1 fake → 2 fakes, L/R, 1 min each)
- **JV**: Areas 3, 4, 5, 6 — pivot-and-pass: passer, defender, pass receiver (1 min each)

### Clock 16:00–13:00 (Minutes 26–29): Interception Drill
- **Both Varsity and JV**: Respective half-courts — interception drill (3 min)

### Clock 13:00–11:00 (Minutes 29–32): Screen Setting & Receiving
- **Varsity**: Areas 3 & 4 — setting/receiving screens (2 min)
- **JV**: Areas 5 & 6 — setting/receiving screens (2 min)

### Clock 11:00–07:00 (Minutes 32–36): Box-Out & Offensive Rebounding
- **Both teams**: Respective half-courts — circle box-out drill on shooter (2 min), circle box-out drill on non-shooter (2 min)

### Clock 07:00–00:00 (Minutes 35–42): Full-Court Crosscourt Drills
- **Both teams**: Respective half-court areas → crosscourt
  - Three-man weave drill (1 min)
  - Long throws for lay-ups drill (3 min)
  - 3-on-2 to 2-on-1 drill (3 min)
- Clock hits 0:00: Pre-practice routine complete; regular team practice begins

## Coaching Points
- Transition between activities must take **no more than 5 seconds** — the next drill starts immediately on the air horn
- Players work in 2-man AND 3-man groups; groups can be reshuffled daily for variety and competition
- Two mobile coaches observe all areas simultaneously, correcting and motivating
- During the first week, the routine will look like "organized chaos" — persist through it; players learn the circuit quickly [S6, p.13]
- Post statistics from each segment where applicable (make counts, attempts)
- This routine sets the **tone and intensity level** for the entire practice that follows

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (first week)**: Walk players through area assignments; reduce time pressure; coach leads transitions verbally
2. **Intermediate (weeks 2-3)**: Full speed with scoreboard clock; players self-manage transitions; introduce make-count competition
3. **Advanced (regular season)**: Players know their assignments without instruction; groups reshuffled daily; add penalty consequences for missing standards

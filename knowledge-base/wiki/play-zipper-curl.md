# Zipper Curl

## Overview
Designed to get the team's best shooter an open jump shot on the wing from midrange or the three-point line. The play runs the shooter off a single screen, then off a double screen, with a post flash as a secondary option. [S4, pp.78-79]

## Formation
1-4 high: 1 at top with the ball, 2 and 3 at wings, 4 and 5 at the elbows. [S4, p.78]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Zipper Action — Getting the Ball In
- 1 begins by dribbling to the wing.
- 3 cuts baseline to the weak-side low block.
- 2 cuts baseline and then runs a **zipper cut** off 4's down screen, receiving the basketball in the slot. [S4, p.78]

### Phase 2: Down Screen for the Shooter
- On 2's catch in the slot, 5 sets a down screen for 3.
- 3 must **read their defender** off 5's screen:
  - **Option A (Pop):** If the defender goes under the screen, 3 pops out to the wing for a catch-and-shoot.
  - **Option B (Curl):** If the defender fights over the screen, 3 curls off 5's screen and continues into the double screen from 1 and 4. [S4, p.78]

### Phase 3: Double Screen Finish
- If 3 curls, they come off the double screen set by 1 and 4, arriving at the ball-side wing for an open catch-and-shoot. [S4, p.78]
- 5, after setting the down screen, quickly flashes to the ball to see if there's a quick pass inside for an easy score. [S4, p.78]

## Key Coaching Points
- "3 must do a good job of reading the defense on the down screen. Often whether the play is successful or not will rely on their decision." [S4, p.79]
- "1 must time their cut to set the double screen so that 3's running off the screen as 1 arrives. Don't want to give the action away early." [S4, p.79]
- If 3 pops out and receives the pass but the immediate shot isn't open, this can trigger 5 to step up and set an on-ball screen. [S4, p.79]
- Pass to 2 in the slot is the key pivot point — 2 must be ready to initiate the next phase quickly.

## Counters
- If defender goes under the down screen → 3 pops out immediately for catch-and-shoot off 5's screen (skip the double screen).
- If defense switches the double screen → 5 flashing to the ball may be open inside.
- If no shot is available after 3 receives → 5 steps up for on-ball PnR with 3 as the dribbler.

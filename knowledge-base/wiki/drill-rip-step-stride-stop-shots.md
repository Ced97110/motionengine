# Rip Step Stride Stop Shots

## Objective
Build the ability to execute a rip step move into a pull-up jump shot off a stride stop — both left and right, using 1 or 2 dribbles — while embedding free-throw practice throughout the workout.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court; work from the wing, elbow, or short corner
- Ball only; no additional equipment required
- Choose one of three volume levels (see Progressions)

## Execution
1. Start in triple-threat position on the wing or at the elbow.
2. Execute the rip step: swing the ball low from one side of the body to the other, protecting it from a closeout defender.
3. Take **1 dribble** (or **2 dribbles** for the advanced set) while driving laterally.
4. Perform a **stride stop**: lead foot (inside foot relative to the basket) hits first, then shooting foot lands as you square up.
5. Rise into a pull-up jump shot, maintaining the shot line from the stride stop landing.
6. After completing the prescribed reps going **left (L)**, shoot the prescribed **free throws** before switching to **right (R)**.
7. Alternate L and R sets with free throws between each set throughout the workout.

**Rep/Set Structure by Volume Level:**

| Volume | Total Shots | FG Sets × Reps | FTs Between Sets | FT Goal |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Workout #2-5 (80 shots) | 80 | 8 × 5 (4 sets ea. direction) | 5 FTs per set | 34-40 (85%) |
| Workout #2-6 (120 shots) | 120 | 8 × 10 (4 sets ea. direction) | 5 FTs per set | 34-40 (80%) |
| Workout #2-6 (240 shots) | 240 | 8 × 20 (4 sets ea. direction) | 10 FTs per set | 68-80 (85%) |

**FG Target:** 60% from the field (e.g., 48/80 on the 120-shot workout)

**Set Order (all volume levels follow same pattern):**
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Stride Stop (L)
- Free Throws
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Stride Stop (R)
- Free Throws
- [Repeat 1-dribble L/R cycle once more]
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Stride Stop (L)
- Free Throws
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Stride Stop (R)
- Free Throws
- [Repeat 2-dribble L/R cycle once more]

## Coaching Points
- "Rip the ball LOW — keep it below your waist so the defender can't strip it"
- Watch for the stride stop footwork: inside foot must land first, then the shooting foot squares up
- The shot should come up on the same line as the dribble direction — no re-gathering or re-planting
- Free throws between sets simulate game pressure and train mental reset between action sequences
- Track FGA, FGM, %, FTA, and FTM in a shot chart log for each workout session [S3]
- "Don't rush the stride stop — get your base under you before rising"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (80 shots — Workout #2-5)**: 5 reps per set, 5 FTs between sets. Focus on footwork quality over speed.
2. **Intermediate (120 shots — Workout #2-6)**: 10 reps per set, 5 FTs between sets. Increase pace to game speed.
3. **Advanced (240 shots — Workout #2-6)**: 20 reps per set, 10 FTs between sets. Full-volume game-conditioning workout.

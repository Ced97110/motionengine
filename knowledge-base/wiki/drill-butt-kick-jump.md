# Butt-Kick Jump (Power Jump Chain — Step 5)

## Objective
Develop hip flexor activation and leg speed in the air — the first step introducing tucking mechanics — while creating objective performance criteria that require both sufficient jump height and hamstring contraction speed.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface — no equipment required
- Minimal space needed

## Execution
1. Stand feet shoulder-width, body flexed for action
2. Dip into a squat (same as Step 2 squat jump)
3. Explode upward using full lower-body power
4. At the peak of the jump, explosively bend the legs — drive heels up and back to kick the glutes
5. After the kick, throw legs back down for landing
6. Land softly on balls of feet
7. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- This is the "first drill in the chain which requires you to lift your knees during airtime" [S9, p.20] — hips pull knees slightly forward as heels drive back
- Two demands must be met simultaneously: sufficient jump height AND sufficient leg speed — "if you don't jump high enough, or move with enough leg speed, you will not be able to kick your butt" [S9, p.20]
- This dual demand makes it the first step with objective performance criteria — athletes cannot cheat their way through
- This drill conditions the hips for the harder tucks in Steps 6-8
- Arms-behind-head progression removes the arm-momentum advantage, increasing difficulty significantly

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Unilateral butt-kick — kick with only one heel at a time; allows athletes to learn the hamstring-contraction pattern at reduced intensity
2. **Intermediate**: Bilateral heel-to-glute kick; elastic rebound between reps; 15-20 consecutive reps
3. **Advanced**: Hands clasped behind the head (fingers loose, do NOT pull on neck) — removes arm momentum and demands greater leg power and speed

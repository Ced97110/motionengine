# The 55-Second Shooting Drill Format

## Objective
Develop passing, catching, pivoting, shooting, and rebounding simultaneously in a rotating three-player format under time pressure that simulates game speed — each player receiving 55 concentrated seconds per role.

## Setup
- **Players**: Groups of 3
- **Court area**: Any basket location (diagrams show wing shooting with passer near elbow, shooter on wing, rebounder under basket)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball per group; scoreboard clock or stopwatch
- **Roles**: Designated Passer (P), Designated Shooter (S), Designated Rebounder (R)

## Execution
1. **Set** the three players in their starting roles: Passer (foul-line area), Shooter (wing/perimeter), Rebounder (under basket)
2. **Passer** cuts without the ball first, receives a pass from the rebounder's outlet, pivots properly with correct footwork, then delivers a sharp, game-realistic pass to the shooter
3. **Shooter** reads and breaks to his shooting location (simulating cutting free from a hypothetical defender), catches the ball already in shooting position using proper pivot footwork, and fires at game speed
4. **Rebounder** reads the trajectory of the shot, pursues aggressively, gains possession, pivots away from a hypothetical defender, and makes a crisp outlet pass back to the passer
5. The drill runs for **55 seconds** continuously; all three players repeat the sequence as many times as possible
6. At 55 seconds, the **air horn sounds**: all three players rotate one position (P → S → R → P) in **5 seconds or less**
7. The drill repeats for another 55 seconds in the new positions
8. After three rotations (55 sec × 3 + transitions = approximately 3 minutes), each player has performed all three roles
9. The group then **moves to a different shooting location** and repeats the entire 3-minute cycle

## Coaching Points
- **Shooter**: Catch already in shooting position — the ball should not need to be re-set after the catch; this allows a quicker release [S6, p.18]
- **Passer**: Cut and move without the ball before receiving — do not stand and wait; pivot correctly before passing [S6, p.18-19]
- **Rebounder**: Read the shot off the rim, not just stand under the basket; pivot away from pressure before outlet passing [S6, p.19]
- **Transition**: The 5-second role transition is part of the drill — slow transitions are penalized by fewer total reps [S6, p.19]
- **Total attempts**: Track how many shots the group attempts in 55 seconds; post a pre-set standard (e.g., 8-10 attempts per 55 seconds) and make a competition of it [S6, p.18]
- Coach cue: "Every rep is a game rep — no walking to your spot, no drifting."
- Vary shooting locations across the multiple 3-minute cycles to match game-specific shot locations from your offense [S6, p.18, 20]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary passer (no cutting); shooter starts at spot, no cutting; rebounder stays under basket
2. **Intermediate**: Passer cuts to receive before passing; shooter makes a V-cut before catching; rebounder pivots before outlet passing
3. **Advanced**: Shooter comes off an imaginary screen before catching; passer must use game-specific pass type (chest, bounce, skip); rebounder goes to the glass aggressively; add a manager as a dummy defender near the shooter

## Drill Variations
- **Diagrams 1-5 to 1-9** (referenced [S6, p.20]): Multiple passer/shooter location combinations to match different offensive sets — elbow-to-wing, top-of-key-to-corner, baseline-to-elbow, etc.
- Combine multiple 3-minute cycles at different locations to create a 12–15 minute shooting practice segment

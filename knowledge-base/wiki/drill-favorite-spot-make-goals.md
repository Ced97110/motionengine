# Favorite Spot Make-Goal Workouts (Workouts #11-10, #11-11, #11-12)

## Objective
Build shooting confidence and mastery at a player's three best spots by requiring a fixed number of makes — regardless of attempts — developing mental toughness, consistency, and zone awareness.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court
- Player self-selects 3 spots on the court where they have the most success
- Throw ball to self (spinout), sprint after rebound

## Execution

### Workout #11-10: Make 10 at Favorite Spots
1. Player identifies **3 spots** on the floor where they shoot best (e.g., right elbow, left wing, top of key).
2. At Spot 1: throw ball out, sprint to it, square up, and shoot — **must make 10 shots** from this spot.
3. Number of attempts is irrelevant — the only requirement is 10 makes.
4. Hustle after every rebound; do not walk.
5. Once 10 makes are recorded at Spot 1, move to Spot 2 and repeat.
6. Once 10 makes at Spot 2, move to Spot 3.
- **Total makes required:** 30

### Workout #11-11: Make 20 at Favorite Spots
- Same structure as Workout #11-10, but **make 20 shots** from each spot before advancing.
- **Total makes required:** 60

### Workout #11-12: Make 30 at Favorite Spots
- Same structure, but **make 30 shots** from each spot before advancing.
- **Total makes required:** 90

## Coaching Points
- "Pick spots where you can make shots — confidence-building workouts require success, not struggle."
- Hustle after every rebound — this is a conditioning drill as much as a skill drill.
- Track FGA and FGM per spot on the shot chart to calculate percentage over time.
- These workouts teach players to know their hot zones and trust their best spots in games.
- Progress from Make 10 → 20 → 30 over weeks or months as mechanics improve.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #11-10):** Make 10 per spot — quick reps, high success rate, confidence building.
2. **Intermediate (Workout #11-11):** Make 20 per spot — requires sustained focus across the session.
3. **Advanced (Workout #11-12):** Make 30 per spot — high-volume mastery drill for elite shooters.

# 1-4 Quick Floppy

## Overview
A man-to-man set play from the 1-4 high formation that gets open perimeter looks early in an offensive possession. Both guards (2 and 3) simultaneously cut off post screens using 'floppy' action — each guard can choose to use either the staggered screen or single screen — creating immediate perimeter shots and post duck-in opportunities. [S4, pp.52-53]

## Formation
1-4 high. 1 at top of key, 2 on the right wing, 3 on the left wing, 4 at the right elbow, 5 at the left elbow.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Guard Baseline Cuts
- 1 dribbles down the floor and calls the play.
- 2 and 3 simultaneously cut along the baseline (both moving toward the paint), with **2 stopping close to under the rim**.
- 4 and 5 turn and trail (pivot to face the basket) once 2 and 3 have cut past them. [S4, p.52]

### Phase 2: Screen Setup
- 3 sets a screen just inside the paint (staggered with 4).
- 4 and 5 each set screens a foot outside the low blocks on their respective sides.
- This creates **two screen options** for each guard: a staggered screen (using two screeners in sequence) or a single screen (one screener). [S4, p.52]

### Phase 3: Floppy Cut and Read
- **2 chooses** to use either the staggered screen (3+4 or 3+5) or the single screen, cutting to the perimeter.
- **3 cuts off the opposite screen** from whatever 2 chose — so if 2 uses the staggered screen, 3 uses the single screen, and vice versa. [S4, pp.52-53]
- 1 reads the open player and makes the pass to whichever guard is open on the perimeter for the shot.

### Phase 4: Post Duck-In
- After setting screens, **4 and 5 immediately duck in** to the paint, looking for a quick interior pass for the layup.
- Often, 4 or 5's defenders are pulled to help on the perimeter cutters, leaving the post players on smaller defenders. [S4, p.53]
- The point guard now has **4 passing options**: 2 perimeter, 3 perimeter, 4 duck-in, 5 duck-in.

### Phase 5: Post Catch Response
- If 2 or 3 catch on the perimeter, the **nearest post player immediately seals low** while the other post clears to the high post to create space. [S4, p.53]

## Key Coaching Points
- Players must pre-determine "who the initial cutter is" so the guards know who sets the screen first and coordination is maintained. [S4, p.53]
- The point guard must be a great decision-maker: "There will be open looks from this play — your point guard must be able to make the pass on-time and on-target." [S4, p.53]
- This is a versatile play — every player can potentially score out of it. [S4, p.52]
- 2 and 3 should ideally be good shooters to maximize the perimeter threat.

## Counters
- If a defender goes under screens, the guard should pop out expecting to shoot immediately.
- If defenders switch, a post player will be on a smaller defender in the duck-in — exploit immediately.

# Follow the Rules — Motion Offense Drill

## Objective
Force players to truly internalize the rules of the motion offense by requiring them to execute the rules in a specific sequential order during live team play.

## Setup
- Half court, 4 offensive players vs. 4 defenders (see diagram description below)
- Chalkboard listing the six motion offense rules in any coach-chosen order
- Rotation: offense → defense → end of line
- Duration: 9 minutes

## Execution
1. Write the six rules of the motion offense on a chalkboard in any order before practice.
2. Players begin with **no defense** and run the offense freely for several repetitions to understand the movement patterns.
3. Players must practice the listed rules **in order** — e.g., they may execute rule 3 multiple times before calling out rule 4, but they cannot call rule 5 before completing rule 4.
4. After players understand the rules, **add defense** (token first, then live).
5. Rotation is from offense to defense to the end of the line.

**Advanced variation:** Instead of listing the six standard motion rules, list several different tactical options. Offense must execute them in order *and* tell the coach where each tactic fits within the broader motion rules.

## Coaching Points
- The purpose is genuine rule internalization, not just execution — players must **name** the rule as they perform it
- Watch for players skipping rules or performing them out of sequence
- Start with token (passive) defense; move to live 5v5 only after the sequential rule execution is solid
- "You must not call out rule 5 before you call out rule 4" [S15, p.257]
- Use this drill to assess which rules players struggle with conceptually, then revisit those individually

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No defense — free motion for 1 full minute, finish with a score
2. **Intermediate**: Token defense — defense allows reads but challenges decisions
3. **Advanced**: Live 5v5 — defense must stop offense twice consecutively before rotating to offense

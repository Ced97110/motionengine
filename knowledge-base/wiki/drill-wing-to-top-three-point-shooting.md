# Wing to Top Three-Point Shooting (Workout #11-16)

## Objective
Build game-speed three-point shooting rhythm and consistency by shooting catch-and-shoot threes while moving laterally from the wing to the top of the key and back.

## Setup
- 1 shooter, 1 rebounder/passer (or solo with self-pass)
- Half court; three-point arc
- Shot chart tracking sheet (FGA, FGM, FT columns)
- 3 spots tracked per session

## Execution
1. Shooter starts on the wing (right or left side).
2. Player receives a pass (or uses a self-pass spinout) and shoots a three-pointer from the wing — **Spot 1**.
3. After the shot, player moves laterally toward the top of the key and receives the next pass — **Spot 2** (top of key).
4. Player shoots a three-pointer from the top of the key.
5. Player moves back to the wing and receives the next pass — **Spot 3** (wing).
6. Repeat the cycle until the player has taken **10 three-point shots** from that side.
7. Switch to the **opposite side** and repeat for another 10 shots.
8. Between sets of 10, shoot **5 free throws**.

**Full workout structure (each side):**
- Wing to Top 3PT: 10 shots
- Free throws: 5
- Wing to Top 3PT: 10 shots
- Free throws: 5
- Wing to Top 3PT: 10 shots

**Total shots per workout: 80 field-goal attempts + 20 free throws** (both sides combined).

**Performance targets:**
- FG: 36-60 made (60%)
- FT: 17-20 made (85%)

## Coaching Points
- **All shots at game speed** — no standing-still catches; simulate game movement before each catch
- Feet should be already moving so the catch leads directly into a one-two step or hop into the shot
- Track every attempt on a shot chart (date, FGA, FGM, %)
- "Move before you catch, not after"
- Freeze the follow-through on each shot for feedback

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the movement pattern without a defender; focus on footwork and shot mechanics before adding speed
2. **Intermediate**: Game-speed movement; self-pass spinouts to simulate real catches
3. **Advanced**: Add a live passer who varies the timing and location of the pass, forcing the shooter to adjust footwork on the fly

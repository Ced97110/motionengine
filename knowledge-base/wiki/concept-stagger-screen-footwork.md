# Stagger Screen Footwork

## Summary
Stagger screens involve two sequential off-ball screens set by different players for a single cutter (03). The S6 source focuses on the footwork of the first screener (05), who must reverse-pivot after the cutter scrapes off his shoulder — turning himself into an immediate secondary scoring threat in the free-throw lane. The slip option (05 breaking off 01's second screen) is particularly effective when the defense sags to help on the cutter. All three players involved (passer 02, primary cutter 03, first screener 05) must execute individual pivots and footwork to maximize the multiple scoring opportunities created by the action. [S6, pp.267-270]

## When to Use
- In half-court man offense to free a perimeter shooter (03) from a weakside position
- When the defense is concerned primarily with the cutter and neglects the screeners
- When 05 is a legitimate secondary scoring threat in the lane
- As a constraint against aggressive defensive hedging on the cutter

## Key Principles
1. **First screener (05) is farthest from the ball**: The stagger screener farthest from the ball sets the first screen; the closer screener (01) sets the second. [S6, p.267]
2. **Reverse pivot off inside foot**: After 03 scrapes off 05's outside shoulder, 05 immediately reverse-pivots off the inside pivot foot — the exact shoulder of 05 that 03 scrapes off determines which foot is the pivot foot. [S6, p.267]
3. **Timing of pivot**: 05 must time the pivot to coincide with the moment 03 scrapes off — too early disrupts 03's cut; too late means 05 is not open when 02 looks for him. [S6, p.267]
4. **05 as secondary scoring threat**: After the reverse pivot, 05 is an immediate viable pass receiver/potential scorer in the free-throw lane for 02. Primary cutter (03) is the first look; 05 is the second. [S6, pp.267-268]
5. **05 uses 01 as a brush screener**: After reverse pivoting, 05 can use the second screener (01) as a brush screener to find an open spot in the free-throw lane, also sealing his defender (X5). [S6, p.270]
6. **Slip option**: If the defense sags off to help on 03, 05 can slip his part of the stagger screen (break off 01's screen) in a screen-the-screener action — cutting directly to the open space before 02's defender can react. [S6, p.267]
7. **All three must pivot individually**: 02 (passer), 03 (primary cutter), and 05 (first screener) each have specific pivots that must be executed to convert this into a scoring play. [S6, p.270]

## Player Responsibilities
- **C (05 — First Screener)**: Set first screen for 03 (farthest from the ball); reverse-pivot off the inside foot after 03 scrapes off; use 01 as a brush screener to get open in the lane; seal X5 for a potential pass from 02.
- **SF (03 — Primary Cutter)**: Use 05's screen on the outside shoulder, then 01's screen to cut to the perimeter; be shot-ready on the catch from 02.
- **PG (01 — Second Screener)**: Set the second (closer-to-ball) screen for 03; after the cut, drift to a spacing spot.
- **SG (02 — Passer)**: Primary look is 03 coming off the second screen; secondary look is 05 after his reverse pivot in the lane.

## Variations
### Standard Stagger
05 sets first screen → 01 sets second screen → 03 uses both screens for a perimeter catch-and-shoot; 05 reverse-pivots as secondary option. [S6, pp.267-270]

### Slip Option
When the defense hedges or sags aggressively on 03, 05 reads the hedge and slips his screen, breaking directly into the lane before 01's screen is even needed — exploiting the defensive overplay. [S6, p.267]

## Common Mistakes
1. **05 pivots before 03 scrapes off** → Premature pivot leaves 05 out of position and prevents the proper screening angle; must wait for contact with 03's shoulder before pivoting.
2. **Wrong pivot foot** → The shoulder that 03 scrapes off determines the pivot foot; using the wrong foot rotates 05 in the wrong direction and away from the ball.
3. **05 jogs after setting the screen** → Must immediately reverse-pivot at game speed to threaten the defense; slow movement gives X5 time to recover.
4. **02 only looks at 03** → Passer must be trained to immediately look for 05 after 03 receives the screen; if 02 only stares at 03, 05's pivot is wasted.
5. **01 sets a weak second screen** → The second screen must be physical and stationary; a half-hearted screen gives 03's defender a clear path over the top.

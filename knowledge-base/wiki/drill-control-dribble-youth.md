# Control Dribble (Drill #24)

## Objective
Develop the control-dribbling position and ball-protection habits — the low, guarded dribble used in congested half-court situations — while integrating pivots and passes in a full-court drill.

## Setup
- **Players:** Full team in 4 lines at baseline
- **Court area:** Full court
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per line (or 2 balls per player in advanced variation)
- **Duration:** 1 minute per set

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Line up in 4 lines at the baseline.
2. Dribble the length of the court in **control-dribbling position**, **zigzagging** one step off a straight line as if avoiding a defender.
3. Dribble down with the right hand, back with the left hand.
4. **Reverse (or front) pivot** at the far end before returning, again zigzagging.
5. **Jump-stop** ~15 feet (4.5 m) from the next teammate in line.
6. Execute a **chest (or bounce) pass** to the next player in line.
7. All players repeat.

### Options
- Guards vs. post players dribbling race; first team vs. second team
- Two trips: right hand first, left hand second

### Advanced (Two-Ball)
1. **Alternating:** One ball in each hand; alternate dribbles while zigzagging down the floor
2. **Simultaneous:** Both balls bounce at the same height at the same time

## Coaching Points
- **Control-dribbling position:** significantly flexed at the knee AND torso — more crouched than speed dribble; speed is not the issue, quickness and ball protection are
- **Off arm:** bent 90° at elbow, parallel to the floor — used to shield the ball from the defender
- Ball should be **2–3 feet (.6–.9 m) to the side of and beside the knee** — keep the body between the ball and the defender
- Ball to the **side and under control** — away from the foot and knee to avoid turnovers
- **Eyes straight ahead** — never looking at the basketball
- Dribble with the **first pads of the fingers**
- Differentiate clearly from speed dribble: control dribble = protection and quickness; speed dribble = maximum advancement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** One ball, zigzag, right hand down/left hand back, pivot and jump-stop finish
2. **Intermediate:** Team race format with position groupings
3. **Advanced:** Two-ball alternating then simultaneous

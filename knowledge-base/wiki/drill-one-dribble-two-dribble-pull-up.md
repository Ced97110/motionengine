# One-Dribble and Two-Dribble Pull-Up Drill

## Objective
Build the fundamental pull-up jump shot mechanics — one-two step off a live dribble — going both directions with both feet as the pivot, creating true two-way pull-up competency.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court, top of the key or wing area
- 1 basketball

## Execution
### One-Dribble Pull-Up
1. Start with the **right foot as the pivot foot** in a balanced stance.
2. Take one hard dribble to the **right**; use the one-two step (left foot lands first, right foot follows) and pull up for the jump shot.
3. Return to the starting spot.
4. Take one hard dribble to the **left**, still keeping the right foot as the pivot, and pull up into the jump shot.
5. Perform **20 total reps (10 each direction).**
6. Repeat the entire set with the **left foot as the pivot foot.**

### Two-Dribble Pull-Up
1. Same concept and rep count as the one-dribble drill.
2. Use two dribbles to drive slightly closer to the basket before pulling up — generates a better look and more momentum.
3. Again, work going both ways with each foot as the pivot.

## Coaching Points
- **Pick up the ball correctly going to the weak side.** Going right with a right-handed shooter: two-hand pickup into shot pocket. Going left: let the ball come up into the left hand, then bring across to the shooting hand — "load the gun." This keeps the shooting elbow on the shot line.
- **Dribble out in front, not outside the body.** The ball leads you into the shot like a good pass would. Dribbling to the side causes the ball to hit the foot.
- **Long stride with the live (non-pivot) foot.** Gets the player low and past the defender while setting up the one-two step.
- **20 reps = 10 each direction per pivot foot.** Insist on equal work in both directions.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (One-Dribble)**: Focus entirely on the correct foot order and ball pickup. Speed is secondary.
2. **Intermediate (Two-Dribble)**: Full speed, emphasis on the "load the gun" technique going weak-side.
3. **Advanced**: Add a ball-handling move (crossover, inside-out) between the start and the pull-up; extend to three-point range.

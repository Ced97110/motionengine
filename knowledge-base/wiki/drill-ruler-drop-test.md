# Ruler Drop Test

## Objective
Improve upper-body quickness and reaction to a visual stimulus, while also providing measurable feedback on hand reaction time. [S8, p.185]

## Setup
- 2 players (partner + athlete)
- 1 standard ruler (12 inches / 30 cm)
- Open space

## Execution
1. The partner holds the ruler **vertically at shoulder height**, with the zero end pointing down.
2. The athlete extends their arm with **hand open**, thumb and index finger aligned with the **"0" measurement** at the bottom of the ruler (without gripping it).
3. Without warning, the partner **releases the ruler**.
4. The athlete must **catch the ruler as quickly as possible** before it hits the ground.
5. The goal is to catch the ruler without letting it fall to the floor.
6. Record the distance the ruler falls before being caught — a shorter distance = faster reaction time. [S8, p.185]

## Coaching Points
- The athlete should be in an **athletic stance** — slight knee bend, leaning slightly forward — not standing upright
- Teach athletes to focus on the partner's hand/wrist, not the ruler itself, for a faster reaction cue
- Multiple trials should be averaged for a reliable measure
- Use as both a training tool (improving over time) and a pre/post assessment

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Dominant hand only
2. **Intermediate**: Non-dominant hand
3. **Advanced**: Reaction from different athletic positions (defensive stance, post-up position, sprint start); two rulers dropped simultaneously for bilateral reaction

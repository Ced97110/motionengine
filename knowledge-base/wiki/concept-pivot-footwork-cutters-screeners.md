# Pivot Footwork for Cutters and Screeners Off Ball Screens

## Summary
Every offensive player must know how to pivot correctly at the moment of catching a pass or executing a screen roll. The goal is to arrive in triple-threat position — ready to shoot, drive, or pass — as instantly as possible. The inside foot (the foot closer to the basket relative to the cutter's path or the ball screen location) is always the pivot foot. This applies to cutters, pass receivers, ball-screen dribblers, and screen-rollers alike. [S6, pp.121–123]

## When to Use
- Any time a cutter receives a pass off a back screen, flex cut, or curl cut
- When a ball-screen dribbler pulls up for a jump shot after scraping off the screen
- When a screener initiates the roll to the basket after contact is made by the dribbler

## Key Principles

### Pass Receivers / Cutters
1. **Inside foot = pivot foot.** The inside foot is the foot closer to the basket on the side the cutter is attacking. For a cutter moving from the right side, the inside foot is typically the right foot. [S6, p.121]
2. **Pivot at the moment of the catch.** The cutter/pass receiver should front pivot off the inside heel at the exact instant the pass hits his hands — not before, not after. This allows immediate squaring to the basket. [S6, p.121]
3. **The free foot swings around.** After planting the inside (pivot) heel, the outside (free) foot swings forward and around to square up to the basket, creating instant triple-threat position. [S6, p.122]
4. Coaching cue: *"Pivot on the catch — not after."*

### Ball-Screen Dribblers (Pull-Up Jump Shot)
1. **Front pivot off the inside heel.** When scraping off a ball screen to the right, the left foot is the inside foot and is the pivot foot. The right (free) foot swings around to square to the basket. [S6, p.122]
2. **Mirror for left-side screens.** Scraping off a screen to the left makes the right foot the inside/pivot foot; the left (free) foot swings around. [S6, p.122]
3. Once squared, the dribbler can shoot quickly or pass to a teammate. Coaching cue: *"Scrape the screen — pull up squared."*

### Screen Rollers (Screen Roll)
1. **Trigger: dribbler's shoulder contact.** The moment 01's body makes contact with 05's outside shoulder, the screener begins the roll. [S6, p.123]
2. **Reverse pivot off the inside foot.** 05 reverse pivots off his inside foot (right foot when screening right) while swinging the free (left) foot toward the basket. [S6, p.123]
3. **Maintain ball and dribbler vision.** This technique allows the roller to constantly face both the ball handler and the basketball as he rolls, maximizing catching readiness. [S6, p.123]
4. Coaching cue: *"Reverse off the inside foot — keep your eyes on the ball."*

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: As primary dribbler in ball-screen actions, scrapes the screen and front pivots off the inside heel to pull up or pass.
- **SG/SF**: As cutters or off-ball receivers, front pivot off the inside heel at the catch to immediately square up.
- **PF/C**: As screeners/rollers, reverse pivot off the inside foot at dribbler contact and roll with eyes on the ball.

## Variations

### Back Screen Shuffle Cut (Right Side)
Cutter (02) receives a back screen near the elbow while shuffle cutting from the right side. The inside foot is the right foot. The cutter is already halfway facing the basket. Front pivot off the right heel at the catch to complete the squaring action. [S6, p.121]

### Ball Screen Pull-Up Left Side
Dribbler scraping off a screen on the left side uses the right foot as the inside/pivot foot. Front pivot right heel, swing left foot around to square up. [S6, p.122]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Pivoting before the catch** → results in off-balance reception; always pivot on contact of the ball.
2. **Using the outside foot as pivot** → player faces away from the basket or takes extra steps to square up; always identify the inside foot first.
3. **Roller loses sight of the ball** → using a front pivot (instead of reverse pivot) on the roll; screener should reverse pivot to keep the ball in view.
4. **Dribbler steps laterally instead of squaring up** → creates a poor shooting angle; free foot must swing all the way around to face the basket.

# Chest-Strike Pushup (Power Pushup Step 5)

## Objective
Develop greater upper-body explosive power than the clap pushup requires by extending the hand travel distance — whipping hands upward to slap the chest — activating the biceps as fast-twitch fast-snap muscles.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor
- No equipment

## Execution
1. Squat down, place palms on floor, stretch legs out behind
2. Palms below shoulders at shoulder-width or wider
3. Feet at comfortable width
4. Keep legs, hips, torso aligned and horizontal
5. Dip ~1/3 depth
6. Immediately push explosively off floor
7. In mid-air, whip hands **vertically upward** to slap the chest with palms
8. Return palms downward to catch floor
9. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- The degree of hand movement during airtime is a direct measure of explosive pushing power — chest-strikes require significantly more airtime than clap pushups [S9, p.92]
- The biceps are involved here as fast-twitch snappers to whip hands upward — a unique recruitment demand not present in clap pushups [S9, p.92]
- Regression: begin by just lifting hands **toward** the chest before catching the floor; in time, the full pec slap becomes achievable [S9, p.92]
- Progression path: chest → ribs → obliques → hips (each smaller body target moves the goal downward toward the hip-strike)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Lift hands partway toward chest before catching floor
2. **Intermediate**: Fully slap the chest on each rep
3. **Advanced**: Progress from chest to ribs to obliques to hips — bridging into Step 6 (hip-strike pushup)

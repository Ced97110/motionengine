# Full-Court Press 1-on-1 Inbounds Drill (O'Brien)

## Objective
Practice denying the inbounds pass on one side of the court and then forcing the ball handler into a speed dribble — with the back-tip as a recovery tool if beaten.

## Setup
- Court split down the middle from basket to basket — only one half-side used
- Run simultaneously at both ends of the court
- Coach inbounds the ball into the shaded half-side
- Offensive player starts directly above the coach; defender lines up on the sideline side in **physical contact** with the offensive player (hip and elbow)

## Execution
1. Coach attempts to inbound the ball to the offensive player on the half-side of the court
2. Defender uses the limited space aggressively to **deny the inbounds pass** — vision on the inbounder AND the offensive player simultaneously
3. If the ball is successfully inbounded, drill shifts to **ball pressure**: defender forces the dribbler into a speed dribble while remaining in hip contact
4. If the dribbler beats the defender, **defender sprints after and tries to back-tip the ball** from behind
5. Continue until the ball reaches the far end; then exchange roles

## Coaching Points
- Defender in contact with offensive player: hip and elbow — but NO fouling
- "Keep vision on the passer AND your man — like a DB reading the QB"
- The narrow half-side space makes inbound denial much easier — use it to build confidence
- Ball pressure goal: force a speed dribble, NOT a steal (yet)
- Back-tip: sprint to the dribbler's hip, reach inside to tip the ball forward — not a slap from behind
- "At the beginning of the season, we make the defender aware that denial must happen without fouling"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Denial only — no live dribble; coach passes, defender deflects; repeat 10 times
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill as described
3. **Advanced**: Offense plays freely; defender must maintain hip contact for at least 3 dribbles before the speed dribble is triggered

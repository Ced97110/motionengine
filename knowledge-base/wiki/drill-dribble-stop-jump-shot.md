# Dribble, Stop, Jump Shot

## Objective
Develop the pull-up jump shot by combining dribble-drive with either a jump stop or stride stop before shooting — the foundational off-the-dribble scoring move.

## Setup
- Players line up as shown in the figure (line on the wing/elbow area)
- Each player has a basketball
- Coach can choose 1–5 different spots on the floor for shot locations
- Option: If gym has six goals, divide into groups of 2 at each basket; coaches observe from midcourt
- Duration: 5 minutes

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up as shown
2. Player 1 dribble-drives to a predetermined spot on the floor
3. Player 1 either jump-stops or stride-stops and shoots a jump shot
4. Player 1 rebounds the shot and dribbles back to the end of the line
5. Coach can choose up to five different floor spots for each series; one spot can also be used for the entire 5 minutes

### Intermediate
1. A rocker step move is executed at the beginning of the drill; players may shoot after the rocker step (coach's call) or proceed to step 2
2. After the rocker step, a dribbling move is executed before the stop-and-shoot action
3. A dribbling move can also be made on the way back to the end of the line after the rebound

### Advanced
1. Split the squad into teams of 2; a live defender is placed on the attacker
2. The attacker must read the defense and use savvy to choose the correct move and shot
3. The defender and attacker exchange places after the shot

## Coaching Points
- Player should know their stop spot before the drive; the defender will not know it
- Jump stop allows pivoting in any direction before the shot; stride stop enables a quicker shot release
- Knees must be bent and ready to spring into the jump shot immediately at the stop
- The pull-up is a primary weapon: "If you can stop and shoot, you can score from anywhere on the floor"
- In the advanced level, the attacker must read the defender — going to a live 1-on-1 requires decision-making, not just mechanical repetition

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Dribble to spot → stop → shoot → rebound → return; no defender
2. **Intermediate**: Rocker step → dribbling move → stop → shoot; add moves on the return trip
3. **Advanced**: Live 1-on-1 with defender; read defense; rotate roles after the shot

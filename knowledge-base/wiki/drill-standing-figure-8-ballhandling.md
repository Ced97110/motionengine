# Standing Figure 8 (Drill #19)

## Objective
Develop touch, hand-eye coordination, and ambidextrous ball control by passing and dribbling the basketball in a figure-8 pattern around and through both legs.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1 per ball (or full team in lines ~15 feet/4.5 m apart)
- **Court area:** Any stationary position; option to use full-court lines
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per player
- **Duration:** 2 minutes

## Execution

### Beginner (Stationary)
1. Pass the ball from right to left hand and back, circling it around the right leg.
2. Repeat, circling the basketball around the left leg.
3. If drilling as a team, players stand ~15 feet apart.

**Team option (beginner):**
1. Players line up in four lines (see the court diagram — 4 columns, players at baseline).
2. First line walks down the court and back using the figure-8 movement around both legs.
3. First line passes to the second line and goes to the back of the line.
4. Second line repeats.

### Intermediate (Through the Legs)
1. Begin with ball held in right hand in front of right leg.
2. Take ball between spread legs — pick up behind the left leg with the left hand.
3. Bring ball around the front of the left leg with the left hand, through the legs again to behind the right leg — right hand picks up.
4. Bring ball from behind the right leg to the front — continue the figure-8 pattern for 5–15 seconds.
5. **Reverse** the direction of the figure-8 for another 5–15 seconds.

**Intermediate team option:**
1. First line races down the floor and back using the through-the-legs figure-8.
2. Players jump-stop ~10 feet (3 m) from the second line and execute a pass (coach's call) to the second line.
3. Second line repeats.
4. Divide into teams for a dribbling race — guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Coaching Points
- Watch for players handling the ball **low** by bending at the knees and trunk — correct body mechanics are essential
- Players should use the **pads of the hands, not the palms** (see the hand-contact drill): spread hands until they hurt, then relax — this forms a perfect cup
- Keep the head up — do not watch the ball
- Dribble rapidly and keep the ball low during intermediate variations

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Single-leg circles standing in place — develops the wrist-flip and ambidextrous touch
2. **Intermediate:** Through-the-legs figure-8 stationary — adds bilateral coordination
3. **Advanced:** Figure-8 while walking, then racing at full speed down the floor — requires maintaining technique under movement stress

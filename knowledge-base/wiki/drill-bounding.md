# Bounding

## Objective
Increase hip extension and flexion strength, improve ankle-muscle stiffness, and enhance leg power and stride length through exaggerated running mechanics with a slight jump on each step.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open flat surface — field, track, or court
- Distance: typically 30–40 meters per rep

## Execution
1. Run forward, but drive the **free knee so the thigh reaches a parallel position with the ground** on each step.
2. Jump slightly on each step — creating a bouncy, exaggerated running pattern.
3. The movement should resemble a **bouncy run with longer-than-normal strides**.
4. Maintain aggressive arm action to coordinate with each bounding step.
5. Be careful **not to reach forward** with the foot at ground contact — this creates a braking force.

## Coaching Points
- The visual cue: "like a slow-motion sprint" — each stride is powerful and exaggerated.
- "Thigh to parallel" on every step — if the knee doesn't rise enough, the hip extensors don't get the full loading benefit.
- Do not reach (overstride) with the lead foot at contact — this slows you down and increases injury risk.
- Ankle remains stiff at ground contact — the spring-loaded calf and Achilles tendon should absorb and return energy.
- Use bounding as a progression from skipping drills toward sprint mechanics.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow rhythm bounding, 20m, focus on knee height and not overstriding
2. **Intermediate**: Speed-up bounding, 30m, emphasize arm coordination
3. **Advanced**: Max-effort bounding for distance, 40m, competition format (cover distance in fewest bounds)

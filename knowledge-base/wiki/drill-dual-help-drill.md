# Dual-Help Drill

## Objective
Train help-side defenders to provide multiple sequential help actions in a single possession — deflecting a lob pass to the high post AND drawing a charge on a baseline drive — reinforcing that help-side defense means helping on anything that could lead to a score.

## Setup
- Half court
- 3 participants: coach (with 2 basketballs), player 1 (X1's assignment on the weak side), player 2 (baseline attacker)
- X1 is a help-side defender
- Duration: 3 minutes

<!-- DIAGRAM: Coach at the top with two balls. X1 is on the help side (right lane area). ① is weak-side wing. ② is at the baseline left side. Coach throws lob to high post, then passes baseline to ②. -->

## Execution
**Beginner:**
1. Coach starts with two basketballs.
2. Coach's **first action**: lob pass into the high-post area. X1 must **deflect this pass** (simulating help on the imaginary high-post defender's assignment).
3. Coach's **second action**: pass to player 2 along the baseline. Player 2 immediately begins a drive toward the basket (as if having beaten an imaginary defender).
4. X1 must **sprint down**, get in position, and **draw the charge** on player 2's baseline drive.
5. "Help-side defense means just that: Players must help on anything that might be happening on the ball side that could lead to a score" [S15, p.276].

**Intermediate Options:**
1. Once defenders know how to rotate, add a weak-side attacker along the free-throw line extended and run the full rotation drill
2. Require player 1 to move up and down the sideline — X1 must maintain flat triangle while still being ready to help
3. Allow player 1 to perform any offensive cutting technique — X1 must defend a cutter from weak side to ball side

## Coaching Points
1. X1 must read both help situations **simultaneously** — one eye on the high-post lob, one eye on the baseline drive cue
2. After deflecting the lob, X1 immediately sprints baseline — no hesitation or celebration
3. Charge position must be **established before the driver arrives** — X1 cannot step in front of a moving player
4. "Here X1 helped twice (dual help): on a lob pass to the high post and by drawing a charge on a breakaway dribbler" [S15, p.276]
5. The drill teaches defenders that help-side positioning is not passive — it requires active reading and multiple physical actions per possession

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach telegraphs both passes; X1 practices each help action separately first
2. **Intermediate**: Add player 1 movement to maintain flat triangle between help actions
3. **Advanced**: Full rotation — add weak-side attacker, run live 3-player rotation after the baseline drive

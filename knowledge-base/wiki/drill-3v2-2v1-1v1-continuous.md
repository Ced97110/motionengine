# Three-Against-Two, Two-Against-One, One-Against-One Continuous Fast-Break Drill

## Objective
Develop transition defense under different numerical matchups — 3v2, 2v1, and 1v1 — in a seamless, high-intensity cycling drill. [S1, p.212]

## Setup
- All players start at one end of the court
- Three offensive players attack two defenders at the far basket (3-on-2)
- After the break concludes, the drill immediately cycles into a 2-on-1 and then a 1-on-1 going the opposite direction

## Execution
### Cycle 1: 3-on-2
1. Three O players attack two X defenders toward the far basket.
2. Once the offense scores or the defense gets the ball:

### Cycle 2: 2-on-1
3. The two X defenders immediately become offensive players and attack the **offensive player farthest from the ball** — who has transitioned to become the lone defender.
4. This creates a 2-on-1 full-court situation going the other direction.
5. Once the offense scores or the defense gets the ball:

### Cycle 3: 1-on-1
6. The lone defender becomes the new offensive player and attacks the **offensive player farthest from the goal** (from the 2-on-1 group), who is now the 1-on-1 defender.
7. Full-court 1-on-1 ensues.
8. Repeat the cycle with the next groups from the original line.

## Coaching Points
- **Defensive objectives in all outnumbered scenarios:** guard the goal, stop the ball, force the dribbler to pick up by faking or feinting, cause a turnover, take a bad shot, or slow the ball until teammates recover [S1, p.212]
- "We practice transition defense as much as possible — it wins games."
- 2-on-1 defender: protect the rim first, do not commit to the ball handler until the pass is made
- 1-on-1 defender: contain, do not reach — stay between the ball and the basket
- Transition from defense to offense must be **immediate** — no walking

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Run 3v2 only until defensive tandem principles are understood.
2. **Intermediate:** Add the 2v1 phase.
3. **Advanced:** Full cycle 3v2 → 2v1 → 1v1 at game speed with no rest.

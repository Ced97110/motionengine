# Transition Jump Shot Drill

## Objective
Teach players to decelerate smoothly from a sprint and time a one-two step into a balanced jump shot — simulating catch-and-shoot opportunities in transition.

## Setup
- Minimum 7 players: 1 rebounder, 3+ passers with balls, 3+ shooters
- Full court or half court
- Shooters start at midcourt on either sideline
- Passers line up in the corner on the **same side of the court** as the shooters

## Execution
1. First shooter sprints from midcourt inbounds toward the basket, taking long strides that transition to shorter steps to time the deceleration.
2. Shooter **calls for the ball** — critical at high speed.
3. Passer (in the corner) delivers the ball to time the pass with the shooter's one-two step stride.
4. Shooter receives the ball, completes the one-two step, and shoots the jump shot; freezes the follow-through.
5. Rebounder secures the ball (not letting it hit the floor) and dribbles to the corner passer line.
6. First shooter becomes the new rebounder; passer moves to the end of the shooting line.
7. Repeat continuously.

## Coaching Points
- **"Call for the ball — especially when moving fast."** The call cues the passer to deliver at the right moment. A late pass or an early pass disrupts the one-two step timing entirely.
- **Long strides to shorter steps** is the key deceleration pattern. Players should not slam on the brakes; they should gradually shorten stride length to maintain balance through the catch.
- **Timing is everything.** Passer must watch the shooter's approach and deliver the ball so it arrives as the shooter's non-shooting foot is hitting the floor ("one" of the one-two step).
- **Competition options:** Total makes per team; individual make tracking; time the drill.
- **Freeze the follow-through** — moving feet after the shot indicates balance was not established.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Jog rather than sprint; focus on deceleration pattern and timing.
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed; use competitive tracking (total makes in a set time).
3. **Advanced**: Start at full court; vary the pass angle from corner to elbow; shooter makes the shot fake or jab before shooting.

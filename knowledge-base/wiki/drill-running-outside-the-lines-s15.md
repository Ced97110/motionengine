# Running Outside the Lines

## Objective
Develop quickness, balance, agility, and the ability to execute basketball footwork (stops, pivots, dribble moves, cuts, defensive slides) on verbal command while in motion.

## Setup
- **Players**: Individual or full team
- **Court area**: Full court, players jog along the out-of-bounds boundary lines
- **Equipment**: Basketballs (optional — for dribbling variation)
- **Duration**: 3 minutes
- Line players up at one baseline facing right; all jog around the out-of-bounds perimeter

## Execution
1. Players begin jogging around the out-of-bounds lines of the court on the coach's command "jog."
2. The coach calls verbal commands and players react immediately while continuing to move. Commands vary by skill level (see Progressions below).
3. Between commands, players always return to jogging.

**Beginner Commands:**
1. "Jump stop" — players stop using a two-foot jump stop; "Go" resumes jogging
2. "Stride stop" — players stop using a stride stop; "Go" resumes jogging
3. "Step in" — players step once into the court then back on the line while jogging; "Step out" — step outside the boundary
4. "In and out" — players execute the in-and-out offensive dribble move, then resume jogging

**Intermediate Commands (add to beginner):**
1. "Front pivot" — jump/stride stop, execute a front pivot, resume jogging in opposite direction
2. "Reverse pivot" — jump/stride stop, execute a reverse pivot, resume jogging in opposite direction
3. "Jab step" — hard jab step with either foot, resume jogging
4. "Crossover step" — crossover step with either foot, resume jogging
5. "Spin step" — full spin with either foot as pivot foot, resume jogging
6. "Half-spin step" — half-spin with either foot as pivot foot, resume jogging

**Advanced Commands (add to all):**
1. "Sprint" — sprint for five steps, resume jogging
2. "V-cut" — execute a V-cut, resume jogging
3. "Break down" — drop into defensive stance, hold until "jog" called
4. "Pat the floor" — defensive stance, tap toes rapidly on floor, resume jogging
5. "Triple threat" — jump/stride stop into offensive triple-threat position, resume jogging
6. "Fence slide" — defensive fence slide for five steps, resume jogging
7. "Advance step" — jump stop, one advance step, resume jogging
8. "Retreat step" — jump stop, one retreat step, resume jogging
9. "Swing step" — jump stop, one swing step, resume jogging

## Coaching Points
- Watch for precision in movement execution — quickness without correct technique is counterproductive
- Cue: "Toes grab the floor" for all slides and stops
- Cue: "Stay low" during defensive stance commands
- Players should look up, not at the floor, during dribbling option
- Observe how quickly players react — reaction time is a training goal

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Jump stop, stride stop, step-in/out, in-and-out move only
2. **Intermediate**: Add all pivot types, jab step, crossover, spin, half-spin
3. **Advanced**: Add sprint bursts, V-cuts, full defensive stance series (break down, pat floor, fence slide, advance/retreat/swing steps)

**Options (all levels):**
- Players flip a basketball from hand to hand while jogging instead of empty-handed
- Players dribble a basketball while jogging and execute all commands with ball in hand

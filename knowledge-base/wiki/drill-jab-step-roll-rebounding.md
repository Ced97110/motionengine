# Jab Step and Roll (Rebounding)

## Objective
Train offensive rebounders to use the jab-step-and-roll and jab-step-and-go to beat defensive boxout techniques, while training defenders to execute proper reverse pivot or slide-and-pivot boxouts.

## Setup
- 2 players (1 offense, 1 defense) near the basket
- Coach lays the ball in the middle of the free-throw circle (beginner) or shoots at the goal (advanced)
- After 1 minute, offense and defense switch roles
- Can be run at both ends of the court simultaneously

## Execution

### Intermediate
1. Coach lays the ball in the middle of the free-throw circle. On coach's signal, the defender (X) tries to prevent the attacker (O) from touching the ball.
2. **Defense** uses one of two boxout techniques:
   - **Immediate boxout**: On the shot, immediately reverse pivot to make contact with the assignment. Hold until the ball is retrieved.
   - **Slide and boxout**: Slide-step with the attacker for two steps (watching their movement), then front pivot to make contact and hold position.
3. **Offense** uses the jab-step-and-roll or jab-step-and-go, combined with the swim move.
4. Defense must hold the attacker off the ball for a count of **5 seconds**.

### Advanced
1. Instead of using the circle, the **coach shoots the ball at the goal**.
2. Defenders must hold boxouts until the ball hits the floor.
3. If offense gets the rebound: use pump fakes or pump fake crossovers to put it back.
4. If defense gets the rebound: **outlet pass to coach** or **blast out with the dribble**.
5. Defense may play it live after the rebound.

## Coaching Points

### Pivot Selection for Defenders
- **Reverse pivot** (near the basket): use when the defender is close to the basket — allows the defender to see the ball more quickly. The attacker cannot move far because the ball arrives quickly.
- **Front pivot** (on the perimeter): use when the defender is farther from the basket — allows the defender to watch the assignment longer before the ball arrives.
- **Slide one step, then pivot**: use against exceptional offensive rebounders who anticipate the boxout — forces the attacker to use 2+ moves to get open.

### Jab Step and Roll (Offense)
- Execute the jab step. When the defender completes the reverse pivot blockout, **begin the roll** alongside the defender.
- Use the **swim move** when getting alongside the defender to establish inside position.

### Jab Step and Go (Offense)
- Jab step (left foot). If the defender does not react/block out, bring the right foot up even with the jab foot.
- Use the **swim move** as the right foot comes forward — attack the open gap.

### Rebound Angle Rules (Savvy)
- Shots rebound **away from the shooting angle**: a shot from the left corner caroms toward the right corner.
- A shot at a **45-degree angle** rebounds at approximately a 45-degree angle on the opposite side of the basket.
- These angles allow offensive rebounders to predict and move to the carom spot before others.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Ball on the circle floor — read and react; no shot arc to anticipate.
2. **Intermediate**: Coach shoots — add reading shot arc and rebound angles.
3. **Advanced**: Live possession after the rebound; pump fakes and blast-out dribble required.

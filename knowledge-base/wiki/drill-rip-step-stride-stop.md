# Rip Step Stride Stop (Pull-Up Jump Shot)

## Objective
Develop the rip step initiation into a balanced stride-stop pull-up jump shot off 1 or 2 dribbles, both left and right, building ambidextrous scoring ability and composure under pressure.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court, wing or elbow area
- Ball only; no equipment required
- Optional: cone to simulate a catch spot

## Execution
1. Start in triple-threat position on the wing or elbow
2. Execute the **rip step**: swing the ball in a low arc beneath an imaginary defender's hands while simultaneously stepping into the drive with the lead foot
3. Take **1 dribble** (left-hand version: drive left; right-hand version: drive right), then plant with a **stride stop** (1-2 step footwork: lead foot lands first, then shooting foot lands and stops momentum)
4. Rise immediately into a pull-up jump shot — do not dip the ball; go straight up from the stride stop landing
5. Complete the set (5, 10, or 20 reps depending on workout volume), then shoot the prescribed **free throws** before switching directions
6. Progress to **2-dribble** version after mastering the 1-dribble: same rip, same stride stop, just one additional dribble to push farther into the lane

**Workout Volumes:**
- Workout #2-5 (Introductory): 5 reps per set × 8 sets = 40 FG attempts; 5 FTs between each set; Total: 40 FG + 20 FT
- Workout #2-5 (Standard): 10 reps per set × 8 sets = 80 FG; 5 FTs between; Total: 80 FG + 40 FT (120 total shots)
- Workout #2-6 (Extended): 20 reps per set × 8 sets = 160 FG; 10 FTs between; Total: 160 FG + 80 FT (240 total shots)

**Target Performance:** 60% FG, 80–85% FT [S3]

## Coaching Points
- **"Rip low, step through"**: the ball and foot move simultaneously — no hesitation between rip and step
- Watch for the stride stop landing: lead foot first, shooting foot second — players who jump-stop instead will have slower rise times
- The pull-up should feel identical to a stationary catch-and-shoot — same elbow alignment, same follow-through freeze
- **Do not dip**: the momentum from the stride stop creates natural load; no extra ball dip needed
- Free throws between sets are mandatory — they train the mental shift from physical drive to composed shooting, simulating a foul-shot situation

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #2-5)**: 5-rep sets, 1-dribble only, slow tempo to establish footwork pattern; confirm stride stop is L-R (or R-L) not a jump stop
2. **Intermediate (Workout #2-5/2-6)**: 10-rep sets, introduce 2-dribble version after 1-dribble is clean; alternate directions within session
3. **Advanced (Workout #2-6)**: 20-rep sets at game speed, mix 1 and 2 dribbles randomly, add a live defender or chair to simulate pressure; emphasize 60% FG standard

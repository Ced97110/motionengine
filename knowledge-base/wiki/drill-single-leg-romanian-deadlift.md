# Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

## Objective
Develop unilateral posterior chain strength (hamstrings, glutes, spinal erectors) and balance through a single-leg hip hinge, building the stability and strength needed for basketball deceleration, cutting, and injury prevention.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Kettlebell (or dumbbell) held in the **hand opposite** the supporting leg (contralateral grip) — or the same-side hand (ipsilateral) as a variation
- Flat, open floor space

## Execution
1. Stand with the kettlebell held in the hand opposite the supporting leg, in front of the hip on that side (a).
2. Hold the chest up and shoulders back; slightly flex the knee of the supporting leg.
3. **Lower the kettlebell by hinging** at the hip of the supporting leg — this is a **hip hinge**, not simply bending forward at the spine.
4. Keep the knee and hip of the other (non-supporting) leg **extended** — as the torso flexes, that leg lifts behind as a counterbalance.
5. Maintain a **rigid torso and neutral spine** throughout the movement.
6. Allow the supporting knee to flex slightly during the descent.
7. Continue until the kettlebell is at approximately **midshin height** (b).
8. Reverse the movement to return to the starting position.
9. Complete all reps on one side, then repeat with the other leg.

## Coaching Points
- **Feel as if being pulled backward by a rope around the waist** — this cue promotes the correct hip hinge, not a spine-forward bend
- Keep the **spine neutrally aligned** — no rounding of the lower back
- Rear leg stays straight with hip and knee extended — acts as a counterbalance to maintain balance
- The movement is a **hip hinge of the supporting leg**, not a forward torso lean
- **Inhale** during the lowering phase; **exhale** during the upward phase
- Dumbbell/kettlebell can be held ipsilateral (same side as supporting leg) or contralateral (opposite side) — each variation changes the balance challenge

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight single-leg RDL (touch the floor with a single hand for balance) — focus on hip hinge pattern and neutral spine
2. **Intermediate**: Kettlebell or dumbbell single-leg RDL, contralateral grip
3. **Advanced**: Heavier load, ipsilateral grip (harder for balance), or double-kettlebell variation

## Common Mistakes
1. **Spine rounding at the bottom** → cue "flat back, chest proud" → reduce range of motion until flexibility improves
2. **Supporting knee flexing excessively** → converts to a lunge → cue "slight bend only, feel the hamstrings load"
3. **Rear leg bending** → reduces balance challenge → cue "back leg straight, toes pointing down"
4. **Not hinging — just bending the torso** → cue "push the hip back, not the chest down"

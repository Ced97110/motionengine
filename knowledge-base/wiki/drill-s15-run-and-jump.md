# Run and Jump (Defensive Stunt)

## Objective
Force ball-handler turnovers or disrupt offensive rhythm by using a rotating help-defender (X2) to jump the dribbler at half-court, with two-player and three-player variations plus a hedge-and-recover fake.

## Setup
- **Players**: 5 offense (1 with ball at half-court, 2 and 4 at wing/corner, 3 and 5 at opposite wing/corner), 5 defenders (X1–X5)
- **Court area**: Full court, front-court initiation at the half-court line
- **Formation**: 2-1-2 (even front) as shown in figures; alternately use 1-3-1 (odd front)
- Rotate defenders: X1 → X2 → X3 → X4 → X5 → X1; then rotate offense to defense

## Execution

### Setup
1. Player 1 dribbles into the front court. X1 keeps 1 under control — playing a half-step off to force a direction change.
2. X3 denies player 3. X5 denies player 5.

### Two-Player Run-and-Jump (Figure 1)
1. As player 1 crosses the half-court line, **X1 races to get in front of 1**, forcing 1 to change direction (ideally spin dribble). X1 can draw the charge if 1 keeps going.
2. **X2 runs and jumps player 1** just as 1 comes out of the spin move (or crossover). X2 runs directly into the passing lane between 1 and 2 with arms up and active. X2 now assumes control of player 1.
3. X1 immediately **rotates to cover player 2**.
4. X4 (in proper help-side flat triangle) covers **both players 2 and 4**.
5. The "habit" pass is to player 2 (who appears briefly open). X4 intercepts or deflects.

### Three-Player Run-and-Jump (Figure 2)
1. X2 runs and jumps just as 1 comes out of the spin move.
2. **X4 leaves at the exact same moment X2 leaves** and shoots the gap for the steal of the "habit" pass to player 2.
3. X1 knows the three-player run-and-jump is on and rotates to **cover player 4**.
4. X3 and X5 still deny their assignments.

### Hedge-and-Recover (Figure 3)
1. X2 begins the run-and-jump movement toward player 1 but **stops short** and hurries back to assignment — staying in the passing lane to player 2.
2. Player 1 frequently interprets this as a full run-and-jump or run-and-trap and **picks up the dribble** (especially after facing multiple true traps).
3. If player 1 picks up the basketball: all five defenders get in the passing lanes to deny all passes. This is called **"dribble used"** — a 5-second count is a turnover.

### Rotation
- Rotate X1 → X2 → X3 → X4 → X5 → X1 until all defenders have played all positions.
- Then rotate offense to defense and continue.

## Coaching Points
- X1 must keep 1 **under control** before jumping — playing too loose allows 1 to drive straight to the basket
- X2's arms must be **up and active** when jumping the dribbler
- X1 must **time the departure** to coincide exactly with X2's arrival on player 1
- All three maneuvers (run-and-jump, three-player, hedge-and-recover) are used in the **same session** to keep player 1 guessing
- "That is the purpose — you have either forced a turnover or at least disrupted an efficient offense" [S15, p.325]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two-player run-and-jump only (X1 forces direction change, X2 jumps)
2. **Intermediate**: Add three-player run-and-jump (X4 steals the habit pass)
3. **Advanced**: Mix all three maneuvers randomly — run-and-jump, three-player, hedge-and-recover — so player 1 cannot predict the defensive action

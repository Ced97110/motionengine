# Goalie Drill (Sports-Ball Reaction)

## Objective
Improve quickness in the upper body — specifically the reactive lateral movement and hand-eye coordination that underlies deflections, steals, and ball-tracing defense. [S8, p.151]

## Setup
- 2 players
- A line and 2 cones define a "goal" on the floor
- 1 ball (any sport; basketball works well)
- Player A = goalie (defender); Player B = shooter (roller)

## Execution
1. Player A assumes a **defensive athletic stance** in front of the goal (centered on the line between the cones).
2. Player B stands several feet away and **rolls the ball** toward the goal along the ground.
3. Player A attempts to **stop the ball from crossing the goal line** using any combination of hands, feet, or body.
4. Players switch roles after a set number of attempts or time period.

## Coaching Points
- Goalie stays in an athletic base — do not reach prematurely; react to the ball's path
- "Read, react, react" — track the ball before committing
- The shooter should vary pace, angle, and the moment of release to challenge the goalie's perception stage
- Works best with a shooter who intentionally looks to fake with body language (spatial anticipation challenge)
- Hands stay active and low — ready to deflect at ground level

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow rolls from directly in front; small goal width
2. **Intermediate**: Faster rolls; wider goal; angled approach
3. **Advanced**: Shooter can use either hand to roll (unpredictable); goal is widened further; shooter adds a pump-fake before rolling to challenge temporal and spatial anticipation [S8, p.151]

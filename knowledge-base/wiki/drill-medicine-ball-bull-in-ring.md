# Medicine-Ball Bull in a Ring

## Objective
Improve quickness and elastic (reactive) upper-body strength through continuous chest passing while moving laterally in a circle — simulating the physical demands of passing and defending at game speed. [S8, p.146]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Medicine ball (weight appropriate to ability level)
- Open gym floor
- Imaginary or marked circle (~5–8 ft diameter)

## Execution
1. Two players face each other on opposite sides of the circle, each in an athletic stance.
2. Both players begin moving laterally — circling around the perimeter of the ring.
3. While continuously moving in a circle, the players chest-pass the medicine ball back and forth.
4. Both players maintain eye contact and readiness throughout — the receiver must catch and immediately return the ball.
5. Continue for time (e.g., 20–30 seconds), then reverse direction.

## Coaching Points
- Hands ready before the ball arrives — "catch and fire, not catch and hold."
- Keep athletic stance: knees bent, core braced, weight on balls of feet.
- The circular movement adds a lateral coordination demand on top of the passing task — this is the reactive challenge.
- Medicine ball weight must allow quick, explosive passes; too heavy = slow, strength-only movement. [S8, p.146]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary chest passing; focus on quick release.
2. **Intermediate**: Add the circular movement; maintain pass tempo.
3. **Advanced**: One player leads the direction change at will; the other reacts and follows — transitions into a recognition-reaction training scenario.

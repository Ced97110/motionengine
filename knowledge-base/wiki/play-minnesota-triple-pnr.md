# Minnesota Timberwolves Triple Pick-and-Roll

## Overview
Flip Saunders expanded the Detroit stacked double PnR by adding a third screen, creating a triple pick-and-roll action. The structure is a double stack leading to a single PnR, with a cleared weak side giving the ball handler maximum room after clearing the final screen. Terrell Brandon (1) took the ball off Kevin Garnett (3) and Joe Smith (4), then off Elden Campbell's equivalent role (5). The play naturally converts into a powerful two-man game between Brandon and Garnett in the post. [S14, pp.37]

## Formation
- **1** handles the ball on the sideline
- **3** and **4** form a double stack (stacked screens)
- **5** is positioned near the opposite elbow
- **2** spots up in the ball-side corner
- Weak side is **cleared** once 1 clears the final screen

## Phases

### Phase 1: Drive Off Double Stack
- **1** handles the ball and drives off the **3-4 double stack**
- As **1** dribbles off the stack:
  - **3** rolls to the basket, continuing to post up on the right block
  - **4** pops toward the top of the key

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.10 — 1 dribbles sideline off 3-4 double stack. 3 rolls to right block. 4 pops to key. 2 is in ball-side corner. 5 is near opposite elbow. -->

### Phase 2: Single Screen by 5
- **1** continues the dribble off a **single screen set by 5**
- **5** rolls to the **opposite (left) block** after setting the screen
- **1** now has a cleared weak side
- **1** looks for:
  1. Own shot
  2. **Post-feed to 3** (primary option — the Brandon-to-Garnett two-man action)

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.10 continued — 1 dribbles off 5's single screen. 5 rolls to left block. Weak side cleared. 3 is posted right block, 4 is at top. -->

### Phase 3: Reversal Options (When Post Feed Is Denied)
- **1** swings the ball back to **4** at the top
- **4** has:
  1. Jump shot
  2. Reversal to **2**, who cuts toward the wing
- If **2** gets the ball on the wing:
  - **5** is available on the left block for a **post-feed**

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.11 — Ball reversed to 4 at top. 2 cuts from corner to wing. 5 on left block available for interior feed. -->

## Key Coaching Points
- The double stack-to-single sequence forces the defense to make three sequential decisions — it is very hard to hedge or trap all three screens
- Clearing the weak side after the final screen gives the ball handler a true one-on-one look with only the roll man's defender as potential help
- The primary value is setting up the **post two-man game** between the ball handler and the first big who rolled (Garnett)
- **4**'s pop to the top after the stack serves as both a safety valve and a reversal mechanism for the third option

## Counters
- If the defense loads up to deny the post feed to **3**, **1** immediately swings to **4** at the top
- If **4**'s defender drops to double **3** in the post, **4** is open for a perimeter jumper
- **5** on the left block is a second post-up option if the reversal reaches **2** on the wing

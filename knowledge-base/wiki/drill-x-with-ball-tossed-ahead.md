# X With the Ball Tossed Ahead

## Objective
Warm up the body with basketball-specific movements — defensive closeout, diagonal slides, sprint, and transition layup — in one continuous, game-realistic sequence.

## Setup
- 1 player working, 1 coach or next player in line at mid-court with a ball
- Half-court to full-court
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Player starts in a corner of the court.
2. **Sprint** to the free-throw line extended — **close out** on arrival (choppy steps, high hand).
3. **Diagonal defensive slide** from free-throw line extended to the pro-lane line / baseline corner.
4. **Sprint** from that baseline corner to the elbow.
5. **Diagonal defensive slide** from the elbow back to the original start corner.
6. At this moment, the coach (or next player in line) **tosses the ball ahead** down the court.
7. Player **chases the ball down**, picks it up, and **dribbles in transition** to score a layup at the other end.
8. From the far end, player **repeats the routine back** to the original side of the court.

## Coaching Points
- Sprint segments must be at full speed — this is a warm-up but also a neuromuscular activation drill.
- Defensive slides should be proper: stay low, don't cross feet, maintain wide base.
- Closeout footwork (step 2): sprint then chop steps into a balanced defensive stance.
- On the ball toss, immediate pursuit — no hesitation; trains transition mindset.
- "Close out with high hands — chop your feet — pick up the ball and attack!"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk or jog the slides; toss ball gently ahead for an easy chase.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed slides and sprint; toss ball to wing for a pull-up.
3. **Advanced**: Add a shot fake or dribble move before the layup at the far end; player must also return to the original side without rest.

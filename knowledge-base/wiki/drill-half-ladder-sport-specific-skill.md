# Half Ladder Skill to Sport-Specific Skill

## Objective
Improve lower-body quickness while simultaneously developing reaction to a sport-specific stimulus — bridging pure agility training with game-speed ball-handling and reaction. [S8, p.184]

## Setup
- 2 players (athlete + coach/partner)
- Half agility ladder
- Basketball (or sport-specific ball)
- Coach positioned on the opposite side of the ladder from the athlete

## Execution
1. Athlete performs **any agility ladder drill** (quick feet, bunny jumps, hop-scotch, one-leg hop, etc.) while looking at the coach positioned on the opposite side of the ladder.
2. Mid-run, the coach throws a **basketball** (or sport-specific ball) to either side of the ladder.
3. The athlete reacts to the ball mid-drill and moves to the side the ball is thrown, catching or hitting the ball.
4. The drill continues after the reaction; the athlete resumes the ladder pattern. [S8, p.184]

## Coaching Points
- The athlete must maintain good ladder footwork while tracking the coach — don't sacrifice movement quality for the reaction
- The coach should vary timing and direction of the throw unpredictably
- "Keep your head up and react — feet and hands at the same time"
- This is the highest-level agility ladder variation because it adds a cognitive and sport-specific demand to the physical footwork

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Ladder drill only (no ball) — establish the footwork pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Add a visual reaction command (coach points to a cone) at exit
3. **Advanced**: Coach throws basketball mid-drill; athlete must react, catch/tip, and continue the ladder sequence

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — reacting to a ball mid-movement is a core basketball quickness demand
- [[concept-lower-body-plyometrics]] — integrating a ball adds the cognitive layer that makes plyometrics sport-specific
- [[rate-of-force-development-basketball]] — responding to a stimulus while in motion demands maximum RFD from the first reaction step

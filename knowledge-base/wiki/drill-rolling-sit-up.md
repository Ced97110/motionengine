# Rolling Sit-Up (Kip-Up Step 1)

## Objective
Condition the midsection, hips, and back for the full kip-up while ingraining the critical roll pattern, bridge hand position, and leg-swing mechanics.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat surface (mat recommended for beginners)
- No equipment

## Execution
1. Sit on floor with knees bent, heels on the ground; palms on floor or lower legs.
2. Roll onto back, bringing legs above the body; maintain a bend at the knees.
3. As you roll, place hands palms-down alongside ears, fingers pointing toward feet (bridge hand position).
4. Take some body weight through palms.
5. Continue swinging legs over and back, rolling onto upper-back and shoulders.
6. Bring knees close to head (careful not to hit face).
7. Roll back into starting position.
8. Repeat rhythmically.

## Coaching Points
- The "fingers-facing-feet" hand position is identical to bridging — athletes familiar with bridge push-ups will find this natural
- This drill prepares the vestibular system for the rapid directional reversal of the full kip-up
- Low-impact: can be practiced daily with high frequency
- Focus on full range of motion in the roll — knees genuinely close to the head
- Do NOT use crunches or ab-machine work as a substitute — this requires hip flexors, not isolated abs

## Progressions
1. **Regression**: Swing legs up above head only — smaller range, less demand on midsection
2. **Standard**: Full roll — knees to head, roll back to seated
3. **Progression**: At top position, touch toes to ground behind head

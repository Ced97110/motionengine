# Split-Squat Jumps

## Objective
Increase hip power and stride length by executing repeated explosive vertical jumps from a lunge (split-squat) position without pausing.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Small floor area (no movement required)
- Hands placed on sides of head near ears OR used in unison to drive upward

## Execution
1. Start in a lunge position — one foot forward, one foot back, back knee close to but not touching the ground.
2. Jump straight upward explosively, driving off both legs.
3. Return to the **original position** (same leg forward) on landing.
4. Repeat without pausing — continuous reactive jumps.
5. Keep the back knee from touching the ground throughout the set.
6. Complete the full set, then repeat with the other leg forward.

## Coaching Points
- "Back knee never touches" — this maintains tension and prevents a passive reset between jumps.
- Drive the arms either upward in unison or keep hands at the ears to isolate leg power.
- Stay tall through the torso — do not collapse forward at the hips.
- Land softly but immediately re-drive — minimize ground contact time.
- Start with fewer reps (6–8 per leg) and build volume as leg strength develops.

## Variations
### Alternating Split-Squat Jumps (Complex Variation)
- On each jump, switch leg positions in the air so the **opposite leg is forward** at landing.
- This introduces a bilateral alternating coordination challenge and increases the cardiovascular demand significantly.
- Recommended for intermediate-advanced athletes only.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 6 jumps per leg, same leg throughout, hands on ears
2. **Intermediate**: 10 jumps per leg, arms driving upward, same-leg then alternate
3. **Advanced**: Alternating split-squat jumps, 15–20 total contacts, minimum ground contact time

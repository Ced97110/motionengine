# Medicine-Ball Upper-Body Shuffles

## Objective
Improve power and quickness in the upper-body pushing musculature through lateral explosive shuffles with one hand on a medicine ball. [S8, p.164]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor
- Equipment: 1 medicine ball

## Execution
1. Begin in a push-up position with one hand on the medicine ball, shoulders parallel to the floor, body straight.
2. Drop down into a push-up.
3. Explosively push off laterally with both hands so the body passes over the ball.
4. The opposite hand lands on the ball while the starting hand lands on the floor on the other side.
5. Feet remain approximately hip-width apart; core stays tight throughout.
6. Immediately repeat the drill in the other direction upon landing.

## Coaching Points
- Shoulder parallelism is the starting checkpoint — if shoulders tilt, reduce ball height or slow down.
- Lateral push must be explosive — "fire sideways, not just up."
- Core rigidity is non-negotiable; a sagging core is a safety risk and negates the training effect.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow, controlled lateral shift over the ball (not explosive).
2. **Intermediate**: Add explosive push with brief air time.
3. **Advanced**: Increase speed to maximum rep-rate for time (10-second sets).

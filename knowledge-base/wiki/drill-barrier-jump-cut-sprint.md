# Barrier Jump With Cut and Sprint

## Objective
Improve lower-body power and quickness, with an added reactive component: the athlete must read a coach's signal in mid-air and sprint in the directed direction immediately upon landing. [S8, p.176]

## Setup
- 2 players (athlete + coach/partner)
- Open space
- Equipment: 1 hurdle, cone, or bag as the barrier; 2 additional cones placed at 45-degree angles from the barrier, approximately 10 yards (9 meters) forward
- Coach stands between the two cones, facing the athlete

## Execution
1. Athlete faces the barrier and jumps over it using full hip, knee, and ankle extension (triple extension).
2. While the athlete is in the air, the coach gives a **visual or auditory signal** indicating left or right.
3. As soon as feet hit the ground, the athlete opens their hip position and drives the first step toward the signaled cone.
4. Sprint to the designated cone at full speed. [S8, p.176]

## Coaching Points
- "Eyes on the coach as soon as you're in the air"
- The hip opening happens instantaneously — no hesitation or extra shuffle step
- First step must be explosive and in the correct direction
- Use triple extension (hip-knee-ankle) to clear the barrier efficiently
- Landing foot should be flat and balanced to enable immediate direction change

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Jump the barrier and sprint straight ahead (no command) — build triple extension habit
2. **Intermediate**: React to a visual signal (coach points left/right)
3. **Advanced (Complex Variation)**: After landing, use a **crossover step** and sprint in the intended direction — adds hip rotation demand and mimics a basketball cut off a screen or defensive rotation

# Side-to-Side Skiers With Front Rotation (Slide-Board Drill)

## Objective
Improve lateral agility, rotational transition, and balance using a slide board with a counterclockwise (front) or clockwise (back) rotation during the lateral glide. [S8, p.120]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Slide board with side supports
- Athletic footwear or slide-board booties

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance with a slight bend in the knees.
2. Drop into a slight squat.
3. Push off the slide board's side support and glide to the opposite side.
4. **While in transition, rotate counterclockwise** so as to land on the opposite support with the push-off foot leading.
5. When you make contact, push off again and repeat.
6. Perform the drill to both the right and left sides. [S8, p.120]

## Coaching Points
- The rotation happens **during the glide** — commit to the turn before landing
- Maintain the slight squat throughout — do not stand up between reps
- Use the core to initiate and control the rotation, not just the legs
- "Push, turn, land" — the three-beat coaching cue for this drill

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Side-to-side skiers without rotation — just lateral slide to build comfort on the board
2. **Intermediate**: Front rotation as described
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - *Back Rotation*: Perform the drill starting by facing the opposite direction to achieve a backrotation on each glide.
   - *Front + Back Combination*: Do the front rotation version, then immediately at the opposite support push off toward the other side with a back rotation — alternating front/back on every rep. [S8, p.120]

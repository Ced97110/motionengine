# Clean Pull From Hang Position

## Objective
Develop explosive force production into the ground through triple extension using a shoulder-width grip, building the pulling mechanics necessary for the power clean.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Barbell with appropriate weight
- Should be able to perform the kettlebell swing before attempting

## Execution
1. Grip the barbell with hands **just outside the knees** (clean grip — narrower than the snatch grip). Position the bar against the midthighs, just above the knees.
2. Shoulders slightly over (in front of) the barbell. Knees slightly bent, back flat, head facing straight ahead.
3. **Raise the barbell** by extending the hips and legs, keeping the bar close to the body while bending the elbows as the bar rises.
4. The bar should rise to **chest level or slightly below** as the ankles, knees, and hips reach full extension.
5. Once maximum bar height is achieved, slowly decelerate and lower the bar to starting position by flexing the knees and sitting back with the hips.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.96–97]

## Coaching Points
- The difference between the clean pull and snatch pull is **grip width and bar height** — clean grip is narrower, bar rises to chest or slightly below (not above chest)
- Both lifts use the same triple extension mechanics — the variation in grip and load creates different training adaptations
- "Drive the floor away" — cue players to think about pushing down through the feet, not pulling up with the arms
- Bar should stay in contact with the thighs throughout the pull phase

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Kettlebell swing → snatch pull (to learn pull mechanics) → clean pull
2. **Intermediate**: Clean pull from hang with moderate load at controlled tempo
3. **Advanced**: Power clean from hang position (adding the front-rack catch)

## Muscles Involved
**Primary**: Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, semitendinosus, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius, trapezius, lateral deltoid, anterior deltoid

**Secondary**: Gastrocnemius, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, rectus femoris [S2, p.97]

## Basketball Focus
The clean pull specifically develops the ability to **drive force into the floor** — the same physical quality needed to accelerate past a defender and to jump explosively. The variation in grip and load from the snatch pull provides a different training stimulus for positive adaptation. [S2, p.97]

# 2-on-1 Trapping Drill

## Objective
Teach two defenders to trap and contain a live ball handler — preventing him from splitting the trap, beating them on either side, or throwing a gut pass — for a sustained 8-10 seconds.

## Setup
- **Players:** 2 defenders, 1 ball handler, 1 coach with the ball
- **Court area:** Backcourt (near a sideline) OR front-court corner
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Coach passes the ball to the offensive player (O1) in the backcourt near a sideline, or in the front-court corner.
2. Two defenders (X1 and X4) immediately close in on O1 from a right angle — one fronts, one comes from the blind side.
3. Defenders attempt to contain and trap O1 without fouling for 8-10 seconds (backcourt version) or until O1 passes, picks up his dribble, or a turnover is forced.
4. Defenders keep their inside legs crossed to seal the gap and prevent O1 from splitting through the middle.
5. Hands are up and active — trace, deflect, attempt to steal.
6. Defenders communicate: one calls "ball!" the other shouts "trap!"
7. O1 attempts to beat the trap with live dribbling. If he passes, the drill ends; reset and repeat.
8. Rotate offense and defense after each rep.

## Coaching Points
- *"Come at a right angle — attack from the blind side, not from behind."* [S1, p.152]
- Hands up to trace and deflect, not to foul — *"deflections lead to steals and easy baskets."* [S1, p.162]
- Inside legs must be crossed: if the gap exists, the offensive player will find it.
- Force the dribbler toward the sideline or baseline — never let the ball go middle.
- The trapper who doesn't have the ball-side angle must bump with his chest, not his hands.
- After the ball is passed out: trappers sprint out immediately — move on the pass, not the catch.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Stationary — coach places ball in the corner, offense starts at rest; defenders practice closing out and forming the trap legally.
2. **Intermediate:** Live dribble in the backcourt for 8-10 second containment; offense can use crossovers and direction changes.
3. **Advanced:** Front-court corner, live, with a third player positioned as an outlet receiver — interceptor must be added; now 2-on-1 trap + 1 interceptor vs. 1 dribbler + 1 outlet.

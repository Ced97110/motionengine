# Fast Break Off a Free Throw Drill

## Objective
Practice transition defense immediately after a made or missed free throw — training proper defensive alignment at the line and rapid retreat against a team that runs after free throws.

## Setup
- Full court
- Full 5-on-5 lineup
- One team at the free-throw line shooting; the other team set up in their free-throw offensive alignment (lane spots)
- Coach designates whether to defend a make, a miss, or both

## Execution
1. The shooting team shoots the free throw.
2. **On a make**: The opponent's team inbounds and immediately sprints for a fast break. The shooting team must execute their pre-set transition defense alignment and stop the break before a score.
3. **On a miss**: Rebounding and outlet pass trigger the fast break. Transition defense must sprint back immediately.
4. The defensive team must:
   - Sprint to get level with or below the line of the ball
   - First man back covers the goal
   - Second man back stops/contains the ball
   - All five defenders contest the shot and block out
5. Optional extension: immediately practice **full- and half-court pressing defenses** against the many different offensive free-throw lane alignments the opponent may use. [S1, p.173]

## Coaching Points
- Remind players that many teams run both after makes AND misses — never assume the opponent won't push
- The free-throw shooter is almost always the last one back — his teammates must communicate and fill roles without him initially
- Vary the offensive free-throw alignments each rep to simulate different opponents
- Use this drill to also install your pressing defense post-free-throw

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the alignment after the free throw without running — diagram who goes where.
2. **Intermediate**: Half-speed live, defense focuses on communication and matching up.
3. **Advanced**: Full speed, competitive; press the offense back up the court after securing the stop.

# Drive-and-Kick Series (Three Out, Two In)

## Objective
Train the ball handler to attack the paint and read the correct kick-out pass while training off-ball players to move to the proper floor spots, creating open passing lanes for the driving teammate.

## Setup
- 5 players: 3 out (perimeter), 2 in (posts/elbows)
- Half court
- No defense initially; add defense for live variations
- Players must take at least 2 dribbles and get both feet in the three-second lane on every drive

## Execution

### Variation 1: Middle Drive
1. 1 (PG) attacks the three-second lane to either side, kicks to 3, and continues to the ball-side corner.
2. 3 drives to the middle and hits 2 sliding up. 3 follows his pass to fill that corner.
3. 2 drives to the middle and makes a play.
4. 4 and 5 flatten to the baseline.
5. Finish with a shot from anyone.
6. Practice on both sides and with guards starting in different spots.

### Variation 2: Baseline Drive — Peel
1. 1 passes to 3, who drives hard to the baseline.
2. 4 "peels up" to the elbow of the free-throw line, always seeing the ball.
3. 5 cuts to "find the dots" (center of the three-second lane).
4. 2 drifts to the baseline; 1 drifts to the weak-side elbow.
5. 3 passes to 4 at the elbow.

### Variation 3: Baseline Drive — Find Big at the Dots
1. Same setup as peel. 3 drives baseline.
2. 4 peels up to the corner of the free-throw lane.
3. 5 finds the dots in the middle of the three-second lane.
4. 3 passes to 5 at the dots (high-percentage interior shot).

### Variation 4: Baseline Drive — Baseline Drift
1. Same setup. 3 drives baseline.
2. 4 peels up to the elbow; 5 finds the dots.
3. 2 drifts to the baseline; 1 drifts to the weak-side elbow.
4. 3 passes to 2 for a corner/baseline shot.

### Variation 5: Baseline Drive — Skip It, Second Drive, and Kick
1. Same setup. 3 drives baseline.
2. 4 peels up to elbow; 5 finds dots; 2 drifts baseline; 1 drifts to weak-side elbow.
3. 3 finds 1 on the **skip pass** to the weak side.
4. 1 drives hard away from where the defense is loaded, draws 2's defender, then hits 2 for an open jump shot.

## Coaching Points
- **"At least two dribbles, two feet in the lane"** — ball handlers must commit to the paint, not pull up short.
- **"Always seeing the ball"** — the peeling big (4) must maintain ball vision at all times on the peel-up.
- **"Find the dots"** — 5 locates the center of the lane for the highest-percentage post catch.
- **Read the kick**: the drive key is reading WHICH defender collapses, then passing to THAT defender's man.
- **Second drive** in Variation 5 is critical — after a skip, the defense must shift, creating a new driving lane.
- Practice on both sides; guards start from different spots to develop two-way reads.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through each variation 5-on-0, then 5-on-0 game speed.
2. **Intermediate**: Add a single token defender on the ball to simulate collapse.
3. **Advanced**: Live 5-on-5 — run all variations within the same possession; defense must guard all reads.

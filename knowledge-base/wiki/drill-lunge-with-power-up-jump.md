# Lunge With Power-Up Jump

## Objective
Improve lower-body power and quickness by driving explosively out of a lunge position into a forward jump, alternating legs to build unilateral push-off power. [S8, p.175]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open space (20–30 yards)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Step out into a deep lunge position and shift your weight toward the front leg.
2. Explosively drive off the front leg into a forward motion, extending through the hip, knee, and ankle.
3. Leave the ground and land on two feet simultaneously.
4. Immediately step out with the other leg into a lunge and repeat.
5. Continue alternating legs for the designated distance. [S8, p.175]

## Coaching Points
- "Drive forward and up — not just up"
- The power comes from the front leg; the back leg assists with push-off rhythm
- Land softly on two feet to absorb force before stepping into the next lunge
- Maintain an upright torso throughout the jump phase
- Arms should drive up and forward to assist lift

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walking lunge to controlled step-off (no jump)
2. **Intermediate**: Lunge with full power-up jump, moderate distance per rep
3. **Advanced**: Continuous lunge-jump at maximum effort, minimal pause between steps

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-lower-body-plyometrics]] — this exercise develops the unilateral leg drive used in first-step acceleration and layup take-offs
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — the weight shift into the front leg pre-loads the quad and glute for explosive concentric output
- [[leg-strength-basketball]] — alternating single-leg drive builds asymmetrical strength critical for basketball cutting

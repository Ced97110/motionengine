# T-Test (Change-of-Direction Assessment for Basketball)

## Summary
The T-test is one of the most commonly used change-of-direction tests for basketball athletes. It assesses how quickly an athlete can sprint forward, side-shuffle left and right, and backpedal — movements that are fundamental throughout a basketball game. The T-shaped cone layout (10 yd forward, 5 yd each side) replicates the multi-directional demands of basketball defense and transition.

The test is valuable for baseline fitness assessment, program evaluation, talent scouting, and injury rehabilitation benchmarking [S11].

## When to Use
- Pre-season agility baseline testing
- Evaluating effectiveness of agility training programs
- Identifying position-related athleticism differences (guards vs. centers)
- Return-to-play benchmarking post-injury
- Roster management and scouting

## Equipment
- Tape measure
- Four cones (A, B, C, D)
- Stopwatch or timing gates (timing gates preferred for accuracy)
- Position timing gates at the start/finish line

## Setup
<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 3.5 — T-shaped cone layout: Cone A at start/finish baseline, Cone B 10 yards forward, Cone C 5 yards left of B, Cone D 5 yards right of B -->
- **Cone A**: Starting point on the baseline
- **Cone B**: 10 yards (9.1 m) straight ahead from A
- **Cone C**: 5 yards (4.6 m) to the left of B (90°)
- **Cone D**: 5 yards (4.6 m) to the right of B (90°)

## Testing Protocol
1. Athlete starts with the front foot behind cone A
2. Sprint forward toward cone B as fast as possible
3. Touch the bottom of cone B with the right hand; side-shuffle LEFT 5 yards to cone C
4. Touch the bottom of cone C with the left hand; change direction and shuffle RIGHT 10 yards past B to cone D
5. Touch the bottom of cone D with the right hand; change direction and shuffle LEFT back to cone B
6. Touch the bottom of cone B with the left hand; backpedal 10 yards past cone A to finish
7. Total time = time to complete the full T formation
8. Perform **three trials**; record the **lowest (fastest) score**

## Coaching Points
- "Touch the base of each cone" — athletes must make clear contact to ensure they reach each mark
- Feet must not cross during shuffle portions — penalize crossing
- Athlete must face forward throughout (do not turn and sprint during shuffle segments)
- Backpedal must be true backpedal, not a turn-and-sprint

### Key Interpretation Points
- **Guards are fastest** at every level (NCAA DI guards avg 8.74 sec vs. centers at 9.28 sec)
- Professional men's data (9.49-9.7 sec) being slower than NCAA DI (8.95 sec) likely reflects testing methodology differences across studies
- Use position-matched norms for the most accurate athlete evaluation

## Common Mistakes
1. Athlete turns body during shuffle → correction: feet must stay facing forward throughout shuffle segments
2. Athlete does not touch cone bases → correction: disqualify the trial and retest
3. Only one trial administered → correction: always use best of three trials
4. Comparing raw times without considering position → correction: use position-specific norms

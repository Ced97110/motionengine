# Squirm

## Objective
Develop footwork coordination and reaction time by combining forward sprints with full 360-degree rotations.

## Setup
- 1+ players
- Open space 15+ yards long
- No equipment needed (or mark 5-yard and 10-yard intervals)

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Sprint forward 5 yards (4.6 m).
3. Rotate 360 degrees (full spin) and sprint another 5 yards (4.6 m).
4. Rotate 360 degrees again and sprint another 5 yards (4.6 m).
5. Sprint right or left for 10 yards (9 m) to the finish.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Vertical path with two 360-degree rotation points at 5-yard intervals, ending in a lateral 10-yard sprint to either side. [S8, p.81] -->

## Coaching Points
- Complete a true 360-degree rotation — do not cut it short
- Accelerate immediately out of each rotation — use short choppy steps to re-accelerate
- Head should snap forward quickly after the spin to re-establish visual focus
- Stay balanced through the rotation — do not lean or stumble
- Time the drill and track for improvement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 180-degree rotation instead of 360; slow pace
2. **Intermediate**: Full 360 rotations at game tempo
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Right hand down on ground during first (right) 360-degree rotation; left hand down during second (left) [S8, p.81]
   - Vary the distance between sprint segments
   - Make turns on coach's command rather than at set intervals
   - Use various biomotor skill combinations (carioca, crossover skip) in each segment

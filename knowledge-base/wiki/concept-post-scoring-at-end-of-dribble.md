# Scoring at the End of the Dribble (Post Finishing)

## Summary
When a perimeter player dribbling to the basket is cut off by a defender just shy of scoring and picks up the dribble, they are in the same position as a **post player who has used the dribble inside**. This situation — *scoring at the end of the dribble* — requires specific techniques: pump fakes, pump fake crossovers (up-and-under), and the jump stop to preserve pivot options.

This concept is central to the continuity between perimeter and post play in motion offenses. Great one-on-one players must be able to operate both on the perimeter and at the post [S15, p.201].

## When to Use
- When a dribble penetration attack is cut off at the rim or near the paint
- When a post player completes a move inside and picks up the dribble
- After an offensive rebound where the player is near the basket
- Any time a ball handler picks up their dribble inside 10 feet

## Key Principles
1. **Jump stop to preserve options**: Always end a post move or dribble with a jump stop — this creates two pivot feet, enabling a crossover in either direction [S15, p.203].
2. **Pump fake must look real**: Shoulders square to basket; both hands violently throw arms upward. An unconvincing fake won't move the defender [S15, p.202].
3. **Maximum three pump fakes**: More than three risks a 3-second violation. Shoot anytime the defender leaves the floor [S15, p.202].
4. **Choose your counter based on the defender's reaction**: If they rise up on toes → crossover. If they don't bite at all → look for the lob or kick-out pass.
5. **Slide-step dribble only**: In the paint, the only safe dribble is directly between the legs, no higher than the knees, one step at a time [S15, p.204].

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF/C**: Primary user — develop all four finishing options (pump fake, crossover, up-and-under, baby hook).
- **SF/SG**: Secondary user — when dribble penetration is cut off before the layup.
- **PG**: Tertiary — reading when to kick out vs. attempting to finish.

## Variations

### Pump Fake → Crossover (Standard)
Pump fake forces defender to rise → step past the defender's now-flat feet with a crossover step for the layup.

### Pump Fake → Direct Step (Up and Under)
Instead of crossing over, step **directly through** the defender's body with the non-pivot foot → tuck the ball through under the outstretched arm → finish on the other side.

### Pump Fake → Jump Shot
If the defender does not bite, the pump fake creates separation for a clean mid-range shot.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Telegraphing the pump fake** → Consequence: defender doesn't bite. Correction: make the arm extension explosive and convincing — it must look like a real shot.
2. **Not jump stopping first** → Only one pivot foot available, limiting crossover options. Correction: always end the dribble with a jump stop inside the paint.
3. **Dribbling in front of the body** → Sagging perimeter defenders will tip the ball. Correction: slide-step dribble stays between the legs at all times [S15, p.204].
4. **Too many pump fakes** → 3-second violation. Correction: use 1–2 fakes max; shoot as soon as the defender is airborne.

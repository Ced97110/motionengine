# Back Macaco

## Objective
Transition from lateral rotational mechanics (side macaco) to a fully backward leg swing over the head, building the strength and neurological pattern for the back handspring.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Open flat floor space (minimum 6 feet clearance behind)
- No equipment required
- **Prerequisite**: Comfortable side macaco (Step 4)

## Execution
1. Feet fairly close together; squat down and lean back.
2. Place one palm on the floor behind you, thumb pointing away, inner-elbow facing **out** with a locked arm.
3. Thrust up through your hips, swinging your free arm up and over your head.
4. Push off the floor with your legs (main pushing leg on same side as arm swinging overhead).
5. Swing legs **straight up and over your head** — NOT around the side as in the side macaco. Lead with the swinging arm.
6. For a split second, your legs will be directly over your head with your **entire body supported on one arm**. As soon as possible, set the swinging arm down to share the load.
7. Flip fully over and touch down with your foot behind where your back was.
8. Initially the locked-arm-side leg lands first; symmetry improves with practice.
9. Push through your arms and use momentum to stand up.

## Coaching Points
- "Don't attempt this until you're comfortable with the side macaco — this is an evolution, not a separate skill." [S9, p.186]
- Watch the swinging arm's path: it should swing close to the side of the head, not wide — this keeps the legs tracking directly overhead [S9, p.187]
- "Straighter legs = harder exercise" — beginners should allow a slight knee bend; advanced athletes aim for straight legs overhead
- The brief single-arm inversion is safe only if the pivot arm is locked; watch carefully

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Veer slightly toward the loaded arm side (a blend of side macaco and back macaco) to reduce the backward component.
2. **Intermediate**: Legs go directly overhead; swinging arm close to the head.
3. **Advanced**: Fully straight legs overhead with the non-loaded arm swinging tightly beside the head — ready for the monkey flip (Step 6).

# In-Place Ankle Jumps

## Objective
Improve elastic strength and quickness in the lower body by isolating the ankle in fast in-place jumps. [S8, p.167]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface
- No equipment required (optional: a line on the floor)

## Execution
1. Stand in an upright position.
2. Perform in-place jumps using only the ankle joint (no knee bend; no hip drive).
3. Spend a minimum amount of time on the ground between jumps.

## Coaching Points
- "Only the ankle" — the knees and hips should remain relatively still; all propulsion comes from the calf/ankle complex.
- Tight, rapid contacts with the floor — if the athlete is spending more than a fraction of a second on the ground, they are not isolating the ankle.
- This drill directly trains the stretch-shortening cycle in the Achilles tendon.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: In-place bilateral ankle jumps.
2. **Intermediate**: Jump back and forth over a line on the ground for front-to-back reactive challenge.
3. **Advanced**: Jump sideways over the line (lateral ankle jumps); single-leg version.

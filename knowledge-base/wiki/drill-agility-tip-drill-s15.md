# Agility Tip Drill

## Objective
Build jumping conditioning, multi-directional agility, and the constant-effort habit required for rebounding — players jump continuously for 30–60 seconds while executing 90-degree turns between each jump.

## Setup
- **Players**: Individual or full team (each player at their own wall space)
- **Court area**: Any wall, players stand about 1 foot (.3 m) from the wall
- **Equipment**: Optional — tennis ball or softball (for grip-strength variation)
- **Duration**: 30 seconds early season → 45 seconds mid-season → 60 seconds end of season

## Execution

**Intermediate (main version):**
1. Players face the wall, standing about 1 foot away.
2. Jump as high as possible and touch the wall with both hands.
3. On the way down, execute a 90-degree turn to the right. Right shoulder now faces the wall.
4. Immediately jump and touch the wall with the right hand. On the way down, execute another 90-degree turn. Now facing the wall again.
5. Immediately jump and touch the wall with both hands. On the way down, execute a 90-degree turn. Left shoulder now faces the wall.
6. Immediately jump and touch the wall with the left hand. On the way down, execute a 90-degree turn back to facing the wall.
7. Continue the cycle for the full timed duration without rest.

**Advanced:**
- Execute the same sequence but on each landing, complete one of the following before jumping again: advance step, retreat step, swing step, slide step, or fence slide.

**Options (all levels):**
1. Give each player a tennis ball — player must touch the tennis ball to the wall each jump
2. Use a softball instead of a tennis ball to develop grip strength

## Coaching Points
- Players must maintain the jump the entire timed period — the purpose is to build constant effort on the boards [S15, p.16]
- Developing well-conditioned legs that can jump repeatedly is the primary physical adaptation
- The 90-degree turns force players to quickly reorient their body — a direct agility demand
- Track duration progress through the season: 30s → 45s → 60s
- Advanced variation simultaneously trains defensive footwork in a fatigued state — high game-transfer value

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Jump-touch-90° turn cycle, both hands and single hands alternating, 30 seconds
2. **Advanced**: Add a defensive footwork step (advance/retreat/swing/slide/fence) between each jump-and-land
3. **Optional loading**: Tennis ball → softball for grip and hand-strength development

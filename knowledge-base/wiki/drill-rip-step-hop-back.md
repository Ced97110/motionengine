# Rip Step Hop Backs

## Objective
Build the "hop back" shooting move off the rip step — a backward two-foot hop that creates separation from the defender for an open three-pointer or mid-range jumper.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court; three-point line range on either wing
- Ball only
- Choose volume level (40, 80, or 120 total shots)

## Execution
1. Start in triple threat on the wing or elbow.
2. Execute the **rip step**: rip the ball low across the body and drive one dribble toward the defender to draw their momentum forward.
3. After **1 dribble** (or **2 dribbles**) the player gathers and **hops backward** — jumping off one foot and landing simultaneously on **both feet** behind the three-point arc (or at mid-range).
4. Land in a balanced shooting stance — feet under hips, weight centered — and immediately rise into the jump shot.
5. Alternate **left (L)** and **right (R)** with free throws between each set.

**Note:** "Hop Back 3s" (used in 1-dribble sets) indicates the finish is a three-point attempt; 2-dribble "Hop Back" sets may finish at any range.

- FTs × 5

## Coaching Points
- The one-dribble drive before the hop must be decisive — attack the defender to draw their weight forward before hopping back
- "Land soft and balanced — both feet hit simultaneously, knees bent, ready to shoot"
- The hop back should be backward along the shooting line — not sideways — to maintain alignment to the basket
- This move is a direct counter to an aggressive, overplaying closeout defender
- Track FGA, FGM, 3PTA, 3PTM, FTA, and FTM separately in the shot chart [S3]
- "Don't fade sideways — hop straight back on your shot line"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #2-13, 40 shots)**: 5 reps per set — focus on the backward hop landing mechanics; shoot from comfortable 3PT range
2. **Intermediate (Workout #2-14, 80 shots)**: 10 reps per set — add game-speed drive before the hop; hold follow-through
3. **Advanced (Workout #2-15, 120 shots)**: 20 reps per set — full volume, simulate defender pressure on every rep

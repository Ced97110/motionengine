# Rocker Step (Drill 11)

## Objective
Teach all four stages of the rocker step — jab, jab-step pullback, jab-step direct drive, and jab-step crossover — so players know both *how* to execute each move and *when* to use each one based on the defender's reaction.

## Setup
- Individual drill; one coach acts as defender, one player at a time (teaching phase)
- After teaching: players line up at the free-throw line; coach calls out which stage to execute
- Half court, free-throw line area
- No equipment needed

## Execution

### Beginner (Teaching Phase)
1. One player at the free-throw line with a coach acting as the defender.
2. Coach explains why each move is made and when, and judges the player's quickness.
3. Player must give the defender time to react to each move before beginning the next stage.
4. Teach the four stages in order:
   - **Stage 1**: Jab step
   - **Stage 2**: Jab-step pullback
   - **Stage 3**: Jab-step direct drive
   - **Stage 4**: Jab-step crossover

### Drilling Phase
5. Coach lines players up and **calls out** which stage to execute; checks for proper execution.
6. After each call, coach reviews when each portion is used.

## Coaching Points — The Four Stages

### Stage 1: The Jab Step
- A **short, hard-thrust step**, no more than **18 inches (.45 m)**.
- Land on the **toes** of the nonpivot foot — should make a screeching sound.
- From triple-threat position, lower the ball slightly and lean forward slightly — create the impression of driving.
- Player must **check the defense** while executing the jab step.
- **Use when**: Setting up all other rocker-step reads.

### Stage 2: Jab-Step Pullback
- Because the player landed on the toes of the nonpivot foot, they are ready to move quickly.
- If the defender **gives ground** on the jab step, pull back quickly to release a **jump shot**.
- Pullback should be not quite 18 inches. Free foot must be **in front of the pivot foot** for perfect shooting balance.
- Can also be used to get the defender rocking in motion for an ensuing drive.
- **Use when**: Defender retreats after the jab step.

### Stage 3: Jab-Step Direct Drive
- From the pullback or the jab step, the attacker **checks the defender's front foot** to see how far away it is.
- If the defender keeps the front foot near the original jab OR races forward to stop the pullback:
  - Pick up the nonpivot (right) foot and put it **at least beside the defender's front foot** (farther if possible).
  - Push the ball out of the hand to the right side of the body.
  - Bring the left (pivot) foot **forward explosively**, several feet in front of the right foot.
  - Drive directly to the basket.
- **Use when**: Defender is within or at the jab step.

### Stage 4: Jab-Step Crossover
- If the defender brings their near foot forward to challenge the jab-step pullback or original jab:
  - Place the jab foot **beside the defender's same foot** (or farther).
  - Release the ball out of the dribbling hand to the **opposite side of the body** before picking up the pivot foot.
  - Explode past the defender in a **direct line to the basket**.
- **Use when**: Defender steps toward the jab foot.

## Common Mistakes
1. Jab step too long (>18 inches) → loses quickness advantage; keep it short and sharp
2. Flat-footed jab → land on toes only, ready to spring in any direction
3. Not reading the defender before acting → always check defender's foot and body position first
4. Driving straight through the defender (not past) → get your foot beyond their front foot before exploding

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Learn each of the four stages individually with coach as passive defender
2. **Intermediate**: Coach calls a stage at random; player executes instantly
3. **Advanced**: Coach plays live defense; player must read and respond with the correct stage

# Press Break: Inbounds After a Made Basket

## Summary
the head coach's system for getting the ball inbounds and advancing it against full-court pressure after the opponent scores. The key weapon is a brush pick by the center (5) to free the receiver (1) on a banana cut. If the inbounder or ball handler is trapped in transition, the team immediately squares the floor. [S7, pp.79-80]

## When to Use
- Immediately after the opponent scores a basket
- When the opponent applies full-court or half-court trap pressure
- Any time the ball handler is trapped while advancing the ball

## Key Principles
1. **Brush pick for the receiver.** After the opponent scores, 5 positions himself to set a pick (brush pick) for 1 (the receiver of the inbounds pass). The defender of 5 (X5) will typically go down to cover the defensive basket as a safety, making the brush pick highly effective.
2. **Banana cut.** 1 uses the brush pick to cut on a banana (curved) path to receive the inbounds pass with space.
3. **Square the floor if trapped.** If 1 is trapped after receiving the inbounds pass, the other three teammates (4, 3, and 2) immediately form a square around 1 to provide multiple passing outlets.
4. **Pressure-release player is higher.** The nearest teammate (2) must be positioned higher than 1 (not at the same level) to offer a better passing angle and stretch the floor as much as possible.
5. **Ball handler stays strong.** If trapped after picking up the dribble, step between the two defenders or pivot away from the trap to pass. Never assume a surrender position.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (1):** Primary receiver of the inbounds pass; uses 5's brush pick; if trapped, looks for outlets in the square.
- **SG (2):** Positions higher than 1 as the pressure-release player; provides the most accessible passing angle.
- **SF (3):** Fills one corner of the square; can receive the swing pass and continue the advance.
- **PF (4):** Fills another corner of the square.
- **C (5):** Sets the brush pick for 1 immediately after the opponent's basket; then heads downcourt.

## Common Mistakes
1. **2 sets himself at the same level as 1** → Must position higher to stretch the floor and provide a better passing angle.
2. **Ball handler holds the ball after the outlet pass** → Receiver must catch and act immediately.
3. **Surrender position** → Ball handler must never hold the ball high overhead in a trap; maintain strong body position.

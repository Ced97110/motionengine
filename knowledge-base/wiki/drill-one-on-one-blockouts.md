# One-on-One Blockouts

## Objective
Train defensive boxout techniques (slide-and-box and immediate boxout) against offensive jab-step-and-roll / jab-step-and-go moves in a focused 1-on-1 format.

## Setup
- 2 players per group (1 offense, 1 defense)
- Basketball placed in the middle of the free-throw circle
- Multiple groups can work simultaneously at different baskets
- Duration: 1 minute per round; roles switch each round

## Execution
1. Line up offense (O) and defense (X) as shown near the basket.
2. Coach's signal starts the drill.
3. **Defense** uses one of two boxout techniques to keep the attacker from touching the ball:
   - **Slide and Box**: slide-step for the first two steps tracking the attacker's direction, then front pivot to make contact and box out.
   - **Immediate Boxout**: on the signal, immediately reverse pivot and make body contact with the assignment. Hold the position.
4. **Offense** uses jab-step-and-go or jab-step-and-roll (with swim move) to get to the ball.
5. Defender must keep the attacker from touching the ball for **3 seconds**.

## Coaching Points
- Constantly check **body positioning** for both offensive and defensive rebounding (weight on toes, hips low, elbows out, fingers spread).
- **Slide-and-Box** technique: watch the assignment for two steps; do not look at the basket when the shot is taken. After two slides, front pivot into the attacker.
- **Immediate Boxout**: the quicker response — use when the attacker is already close and the ball will arrive fast. Reverse pivot on signal; hold until ball is secure.
- The **swim move** is the key offensive counter: when the attacker gets alongside the defender, sweep the lead arm over the defender's arm to establish inside position.
- Remind defenders: **legal body contact** is the goal. Slapping, pushing, and illegal holds will turn potential rebounds into fouls.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Ball on the circle; 3-second hold requirement; coach watches footwork.
2. **Intermediate**: Coach shoots instead of placing the ball; boxouts must be held until ball hits floor.
3. **Advanced**: After the rebound, live play continues — pump fakes for offense, blast-out or outlet pass for defense.

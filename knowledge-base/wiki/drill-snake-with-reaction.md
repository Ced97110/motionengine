# Snake With Reaction (Quick Feet)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, court sense, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, total-body agility, general athleticism, and elastic strength in the ankle complex. [S8, p.221]

## Setup
- 1 player, agility ladder
- Start in a two-point stance **straddling one side** of the ladder
- Coach positioned at the side or end of the ladder

## Execution
1. Start straddling one side of the ladder (both feet outside the ladder on the same side).
2. Keeping both feet together, perform a series of **quarter-turn jumps** through the ladder.
3. The direction the feet point for each successive jump: **straight ahead → right → straight ahead → left → straight ahead**, and so on.
4. **Keep the shoulders relatively square** while rotating the hips during each quarter turn.
5. Advance down the ladder with each jump sequence.
6. **Simultaneously**, react to the coach's visual commands throughout the drill.

## Coaching Points
- Shoulders stay square — only the hips and feet rotate
- Land softly on the balls of both feet simultaneously
- Quick ground contact — the ankle elastic recoil drives each jump
- "Feet together, hips snap, shoulders still — quick!"
- Coordinate foot focus with peripheral coach awareness

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice quarter-turn jumps in place without ladder
2. **Intermediate**: Add ladder and advance through squares without reaction cues
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Odd/even number filtering
   - Coach semicircle for peripheral vision training
   - Multiple coaches at periphery
   - 2–3 step run-in before the ladder [S8, p.221]
   - Sport-specific skill at end of ladder

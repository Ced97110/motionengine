# Basketball Wall Reaction Drills

## Objective
Develop reflexes, reaction time, and explosive body control using a basketball and a wall as solo training tools — no teammates or gym required.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 basketball
- A solid wall (any outdoor or indoor wall)
- Approximately 6–8 feet of clear space in front of the wall
- No other equipment needed

## Execution

### Drill 1: JUMP THROW
1. Stand approximately two arm-lengths from the wall, holding the basketball to your chest.
2. Jump as high as you can.
3. The instant your feet leave the ground, throw the ball at the wall.
4. You must catch the basketball on the rebound **before your feet hit the ground again**.
5. The challenge is synchronizing the throw and catch within the airborne window.

### Drill 2: 360 THROW
1. Hold the basketball down near your belly in an athletic ready position, knees slightly bent.
2. Throw the basketball against the wall (or straight up if no wall is available).
3. The moment the ball leaves your hands, immediately spin/hop 360 degrees in place.
4. Complete the full rotation and catch the ball on the rebound.
5. Progress: increase the spin speed so you have more time to track the catch.

### Drill 3: DIP THROW
1. Hold the basketball at belt height. Bend the knees slightly but keep an upright torso.
2. Release (do **not** throw) the ball downward toward the floor — let gravity take it.
3. The moment the ball touches the floor (not before!), dip down and catch it before the **second** bounce.
4. Reaction window is from the first bounce to the second bounce — typically less than 0.5 seconds.
5. Progress: drop from standing vs. crouching; increase height dropped from.

## Coaching Points
- Keep the body in an athletic ready position (slight knee bend, weight on balls of feet) throughout all drills
- In the Jump Throw, the ball should be released at maximum jump height — practice the coordination of the release timing
- In the 360 Throw, do not track the ball through the spin — locate it quickly upon completing the rotation
- In the Dip Throw, the cue is "react to the sound of the bounce" — auditory trigger trains faster reaction than visual tracking alone
- "These are time-tested b-ball drills" for honing reactions when team practice is unavailable [S9]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Dip Throw with a slow underhand release; 360 Throw against a wall (slower rotation); Jump Throw at a low jump height.
2. **Intermediate**: Full height Jump Throw; 360 Throw thrown hard (shorter reaction window); Dip Throw from standing full height.
3. **Advanced**: Two-ball variations (one each hand); blindfolded start on 360 Throw (only begin locating after rotation completes); Dip Throw — catch on first bounce only.

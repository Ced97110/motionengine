# Four-Against-Four Half-Court Game of Stops

## Objective
Reward and reinforce defensive stops (turnovers, steals, defensive rebounds) in a competitive half-court setting where staying on defense is the goal. [S1, pp.212-213]

## Setup
- 4-on-4 on the half-court
- Ball starts just inside the mid-court line
- A **coach stands on the sideline** as the passer — keeps pace of play and eliminates dead time
- Win condition: first team to **7 stops** (coach may adjust)
- Multiple groups of 4 wait to rotate in as offensive challengers

## Execution
1. Coach passes to one of the four offensive players to begin play.
2. Offense attacks; defense defends.
3. **On a stop** (turnover, steal, or defensive rebound): the defensive team throws an **outlet pass to the sideline coach** — this counts as the stop and earns 1 point. The defensive team sprints to mid-court and sets up to defend the next four offensive players.
4. The new offensive group receives a pass from the coach to begin play.

### Stop Rules (important nuances)
- If the defense **gives up an offensive rebound** before getting the stop, they earn **no point** — but they stay on defense.
- If the **offense scores**, the scoring team switches to defense (earning no point). The defensive team goes to the end of the offensive line.
- Teams move from **offense to defense only by scoring**. If they fail to score, they go to the end of the offensive line.
- First team to **7 stops** wins.

## Coaching Points
- "A stop without a defensive rebound is not a stop — finish the play!"
- Defenders must box out every shot — offensive rebounds cost you the stop point
- Contest every shot, don't foul — clean stops only
- Outlet pass to the coach must be crisp — reinforce outlet-pass habits
- Use this drill to install any half-court defense: zone, man, double-team, run-and-jump

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Play to 3 stops, no boxing-out rule — just learn the rotation format.
2. **Intermediate:** Add the no-point-if-offensive-rebound rule to emphasize finishing possessions.
3. **Advanced:** Coach dictates specific defensive schemes mid-drill; offense runs specific sets.

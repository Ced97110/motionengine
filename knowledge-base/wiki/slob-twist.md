# SLOB Twist

## Overview
A sideline out-of-bounds play from a modified box formation that creates multiple scoring windows: an opening lob pass, and if unavailable, a ram screen followed by the point guard attacking the paint off an on-ball screen. [S4, pp.97-98]

## Formation
Box formation, but the two high players (1 and 3) start in the slots rather than on the elbows of the high post. 2 is the inbounder on the sideline. 4 and 5 are the low players. [S4, p.97]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Simultaneous Screens + Lob Read
- At the start, **5** sets a down-screen at approximately the elbow area while **3** sets an up-screen simultaneously.
- **2** (the inbounder) takes a quick look at the lob opportunity created by these simultaneous screens.
- If the lob is open, 2 delivers the pass to the cutting player at the rim.
- If the lob is not open, 2 inbounds the basketball to **1**. [S4, p.97]

### Phase 2: Ram Screen + On-Ball Screen for PG
- After inbounding, **2** steps onto the court and moves to the wing.
- **5** now hunts down **4's defender**, who should be positioned under the basket, and sets a solid screen (ram screen) on that defender before clearing out of the key.
- **4** sprints out to exploit the trailing defender, then sets an **on-ball screen** for **1**.
- With help defense arriving late, **1** quickly attacks the paint and looks to create a shot for themselves or a teammate. [S4, pp.97-98]

## Key Coaching Points
- **4 must sprint** out immediately after 5's ram screen to take full advantage of the defender being screened. Any delay allows the defense to recover. [S4, p.98]
- **1 must be patient** — do not use the on-ball screen too quickly or 4 may be called for an illegal screen. [S4, p.98]
- **1 must attack the rim aggressively** looking to score, not just probe. [S4, p.98]
- If the lob option is not installed (personnel don't support it), skip Phase 1 and go directly to Phase 2.

## Key Personnel
- **1 (PG)**: Must be the team's best creator with the ability to finish at the rim.
- **4 (PF)**: If using the lob option, must be able to catch and finish at the rim. Must sprint to set the on-ball screen.
- **3 (SF)**: Should be the best shooter out of 2 and 3 — placed here to maximize secondary options.
- **2 (SG)**: The inbounder; reads the lob, then enters the court to the wing.
- **5 (C)**: Executes both the initial down-screen and the ram screen on 4's defender.

## Counters
- If the defense switches all screens, **1** can reject the on-ball screen and attack the opposite direction, using **5** as a secondary screener.
- If **1** is denied on the inbound, **2** can look to skip to **3** who may have flared open off the up-screen.

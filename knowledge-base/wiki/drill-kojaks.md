# Kojaks

## Objective
Build explosive pressing power in the shoulders and triceps needed for the front handspring, using a small-space pike push-up with an airborne hand-slap.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Minimal space required (any flat surface)
- No equipment
- Can be performed as a warm-up or finishing exercise in an explosives session

## Execution
1. Bend over and place your palms on the floor in a **pike push-up position** (hips high, legs straight).
2. Bend the arms, lowering your skull until it **gently touches the floor**.
3. Explosively straighten the arms, pushing yourself **off the ground** so hands leave the floor.
4. While airborne, **quickly slap the top of your head** with both hands.
5. Catch yourself back on the palms before falling, and repeat rhythmically.

## Coaching Points
- The head touch is just a light kiss — do not slam the skull down
- The explosive extension phase is the training stimulus — press as hard as possible
- Rhythm is key: this drill can be performed for higher repetitions once the pattern is learned
- "This is a great preliminary to build the kind of explosive pressing power needed in the front handspring." [S9, p.168]
- Can be used as a warm-up or finisher in an explosives session

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard pike push-up with slow lowering; practice the press-up without the airborne phase
2. **Intermediate**: Explosive press-up just enough for hands to leave the floor momentarily; clap under the chest
3. **Advanced (Kojaks)**: Full hand-slap to top of head while airborne in pike position

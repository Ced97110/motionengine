# Wing to Top Combination Live-Ball Move Shooting (Workout #11-17)

## Objective
Develop the ability to execute combination offensive live-ball moves at the three-point line off game-speed movement from wing to top of the key, creating a shot off the dribble rather than a straight catch-and-shoot.

## Setup
- 1 shooter, 1 rebounder/passer (or solo with self-pass spinout)
- Half court; three-point arc
- Shot chart tracking sheet (3PTA, 3PTM, FTA, FTM columns)
- Both sides of the floor used

## Execution
1. Shooter starts on the wing.
2. Player moves from the wing toward the top of the key (or back to the wing), catching the ball on a **two-foot stop** beyond the three-point line.
3. After the catch, the player executes a **combination live-ball move** (e.g., jab-step into a shot, jab-jab-jump, shot-fake-into-pull-up) out past the arc.
4. The move results in a shot — either a pull-up three or a short off-the-dribble jumper depending on the move used.
5. Repeat until **10 shots** are taken from each side.
6. Intersperse **5 free throws** between each set of 10 field-goal attempts.

**Full workout structure (each side):**
- Wing to Top Combo Shots: 10
- FTs: 5
- Wing to Top Combo Shots: 10
- FTs: 5
- Wing to Top Combo Shots: 10

**Total shots per workout: 80 field-goal attempts + 20 free throws** (both sides combined).

**Performance targets:**
- FG: 36-60 made (60%)
- FT: 17-20 made (85%)

## Coaching Points
- The **two-foot stop** is critical — it gives the player a balanced, live-ball pivot position with both pivot feet available
- The combination move should be decisive; don't telegraph the move with slow footwork
- "Catch balanced, move quickly" — hesitation after the catch gives defenders time to recover
- Combination moves that work here: jab-rise-and-shoot, jab-jab-jumper, shot-fake-into-pull-up (see [[concept-combination-scoring-moves]])
- Track all attempts on the shot chart

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Designate one specific combination move per set; practice the move at half-speed to master footwork before adding pace
2. **Intermediate**: Full game-speed execution with a predetermined move; passer delivers on time
3. **Advanced**: Passer calls out or signals which live-ball move to use mid-catch, requiring the shooter to read and react in real time

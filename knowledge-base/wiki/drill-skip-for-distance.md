# Skip for Distance

## Objective
Increase hip power and stride length by maximizing horizontal distance on each skip cycle through aggressive forward knee drive and arm action.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open flat surface — court, field, or track
- Distance: typically 20–30 meters per rep

## Execution
1. Begin skipping forward.
2. Drive the free knee **upward and forward** as aggressively as possible.
3. Make arm action equally aggressive — opposite arm drives forward-up with each knee drive.
4. Try to skip **as far forward as possible** on each bound.
5. Land firmly but with minimal ground contact, immediately driving into the next skip cycle.

## Coaching Points
- Contrast with [[drill-skip-for-height]]: height = hip extension emphasis, distance = hip projection emphasis — pair both drills for complete hip development.
- "Reach forward with the knee" — the drive angle is more horizontal than vertical.
- Maintain a slight forward lean to project the center of mass forward.
- Avoid reaching forward with the foot at ground contact — this creates a braking force.
- Strong arm action is non-negotiable — "the arms set the tempo for the legs."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow technique-focus skip, 20m, count the number of ground contacts (fewer = greater distance)
2. **Intermediate**: Aggressive skip for distance, 25–30m, 3–4 reps
3. **Advanced**: Competition format — cover a set distance in the fewest skips possible

# Butt-Kickers

## Objective
Increase foot speed by training the rapid heel recovery phase of the running stride cycle.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Any open flat area, typically 20-40 yards
- Performed during warm-up or mechanics circuit

## Execution
1. Begin from a jog
2. Pull the heel of the lower leg up quickly to **contact the gluteus**
3. As the leg bends and heel rises, the knee should come **forward and up** simultaneously
4. Alternate legs rapidly — maintain jogging pace throughout
5. Do not allow the knee to drive excessively backward; emphasis is on quick heel recovery [S8, p.26]

## Coaching Points
- "Quick heel to the butt" — the faster the heel recovers, the more time the leg has to be repositioned for the next ground contact
- The knee must come forward, not just straight down — the forward knee position sets up proper foot placement
- This drill directly trains the high heel kick at speed noted in sprint mechanics: "The greater the running speed, the higher the heel should kick up" [S8, p.20]
- Maintain upright posture and proper arm swing throughout

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow walk with exaggerated heel pull-up to gluteus
2. **Intermediate**: Jogging pace as described — rapid, rhythmic contacts
3. **Advanced**: Increase speed progressively; add Wall Slides variation (see [[drill-wall-slides]]) where heel does not travel behind the body [S8, p.26]

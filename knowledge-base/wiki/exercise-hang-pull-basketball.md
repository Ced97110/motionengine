# Hang Pull (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop total-body explosive power (triple extension — hips, knees, ankles) from an above-knee position without the technical demand of catching the bar on the shoulders or overhead, making it an ideal teaching tool and prerequisite for hang cleans and hang snatches.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Barbell with appropriate load
- No spotter required

## Primary Muscles Trained
Gluteus maximus, biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, soleus, gastrocnemius, deltoids, trapezius [S11, p.20]

## Execution
1. **Beginning position**: Stand with feet hip- or shoulder-width apart, toes turned out slightly. Grip bar with a closed pronated grip, elbows fully extended. Grip should be **slightly wider than for the power clean**. Under control, lift the bar from the floor keeping back flat and bar close to body. From standing, lower the bar along the thighs to **mid-thigh, at the knees, or just above the knees** (hang position).
2. **Explosive pull**: Explosively extend the hips, legs, and ankles while keeping elbows fully extended. Thrust shoulders up and slightly back; drive hips forward and up. Rise onto toes before arms begin pulling. Keep bar close to body.
3. **High pull**: As the lower body becomes fully extended, rapidly shrug the shoulders and **pull the bar up to chest height**, keeping it close to the body with **elbows above the bar**.
4. **Return**: Under control, lower bar to the thighs, then to the hang position for the next rep. After the last rep, lower the bar to the floor.

## Breathing Guidelines
- Inhale before initiating the pull from the hang position
- Exhale during the explosive jumping action

## Coaching Points
- Elbows must stay **above the bar** at the top of the pull — this cue prevents an upright row motion and trains the correct catch-preparation mechanics
- The hang pull is the ideal **first Olympic-family exercise** to teach because there is no catch — the athlete can focus entirely on the explosive pull pattern
- Three acceptable hang heights provide flexibility based on athlete proportions and mobility
- The explosive shrug at the top is the critical power-transfer moment — cue: **"shrug and jump"**

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Kettlebell swing — teaches the hip hinge and explosive hip extension pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Hang pull with barbell — adds the pulling chain
3. **Advanced**: Progress to hang clean (add the rack catch) or hang snatch (add the overhead catch)

# Guard-Oriented Attack

## Overview
A balanced half-court offensive set designed for teams whose best ball handlers, shooters, and decision makers are guards. Built-in flexibility for catch-and-shoot jumpers, three-pointers, dribble-drive attacks, pick-and-rolls, draw-and-kick plays, and two-player games with a post-up forward [S13, pp.108-111].

## Formation
- O1 and O2 above the three-point line, ~20 feet apart
- O3 and O4 at free-throw line extended, ~3 feet inside the sideline
- O5 at high post, just above the free-throw line

## Phases

### Phase 1: Guard Cut and High Post Relay
- O1 passes to strong-side forward O3, steps away to the middle, and when even with the defender, pivots hard off the inside foot — making a strong inside-shoulder V-cut to the basket
- **If O1 is open**: O3 hits O1 with an inside return pass for a layup or foul
- **If O1 is not open**: O1 stops at the ball-side block
- Simultaneously: O2 dives straight to the weak-side block
- O3 "looks O1 through" (watches O1 cut) while O5 fakes a basket cut, reverse pivots, and sprints to the top of the key to receive O3's pass

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.1 — O1 passes to O3 on the right wing, O1 V-cuts to the basket, O2 dives to weak block, O5 at high post -->

### Phase 2: Simultaneous Down Screens
- After O5 catches at the top of the key, immediate down screens are set on both sides:
  - O3 screens down for O1 (ball-side)
  - O4 screens down for O2 (weak side)
- Guards must let screeners come to a complete, legal stop before working off the screens
- **Screen depth determines shot type**: Deep screens → medium jump shot; Screens farther out → three-point shot

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.2 — O5 at top of key, O3 and O4 setting simultaneous down screens for O1 and O2 coming off the blocks -->

### Phase 3: O5 DHO or PnR Read
- O5 has the option to dribble-hand-off (DHO) or pass to either O1 or O2
- After DHO/pass, O5 screens away or initiates a pick-and-roll
- **PnR location determines O5's pop**:
  - Sideline screen for middle drive → O5 pops to the corner
  - Baseline-side screen → O5 pops toward the free-throw line same side

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.3 — O5 DHO to guard, O5 sets PnR, guards reading middle or baseline drive -->

### Phase 4: Multiple Screens Variation (Cross Screen Entry)
- Guards O1 and O2 start on opposite blocks
- **If cross screen is called**: Ball-side guard O1 takes two steps into the lane and sets a cross screen
- O2 works off the cross screen first, then O3's down screen
- O1 sets the screen, then works off O4's down screen
- O3 and O4 (after screening down) can post up or screen across and post up
- O2, receiving a pass from O5, reads shot, drive, or post feed

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.4 — Cross screen by O1 inside the lane, simultaneous down screens from O3/O4, O2 catches from O5 and reads -->

## Key Coaching Points
- "Look O1 through" — O3 must watch the cutter before looking for the relay pass to O5
- Guards must time their screen cuts — wait for screeners to reach a legal stop
- Screen depth is a coaching decision based on desired shot type (mid-range vs. three)
- O5 is the hub: must read the defense and choose between DHO, PnR, or screen-away options
- Ball handling, shooting, and decision making stays in the guards' hands throughout

## Counters
- If defenses deny the initial guard-to-forward pass: O1 initiates with a dribble entry or attacks the basket directly
- If the down screen is switched: O3/O4 immediately post up after screening
- If O5's PnR is hedged: O5 pops to open space; guard continues to drive

# Hang Clean (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop total-body explosive power from an above-knee "hang" starting position that closely simulates the athletic vertical jump stance — making it more immediately applicable to basketball performance than the full power clean.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Barbell with appropriate load
- No spotter required

## Primary Muscles Trained
Gluteus maximus, biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, soleus, gastrocnemius, trapezius [S11, p.11]

## Execution
1. **Beginning position**: Stand with feet hip- or shoulder-width apart, toes turned out slightly. Grip bar with closed pronated grip, elbows fully extended, hands shoulder-width apart or slightly wider. Under control, lift bar from floor keeping back flat, shoulders over bar. From standing, lower bar along the thighs to just above the knees — this is the hang position.
2. **Explosive pull**: Explosively extend the hips, legs, and ankles while keeping elbows fully extended. Thrust shoulders up and slightly back; drive hips forward and up. Rise onto the toes before the arms begin pulling. Keep the bar close to the body.
3. **Rack**: As the lower body becomes fully extended, rapidly shrug the shoulders and begin the arm pull. Drop to a quarter squat and rack the bar on the front of the shoulders. Rotate wrists under the bar while raising elbows through in front so upper arms are parallel with the floor. Stand up straight.
4. **Return to hang**: Under control, lower bar to the thighs and then to just above the knees for the next repetition. After the last rep, slowly lower the bar to the floor.

## Breathing Guidelines
- Take a deep breath before initiating the pull from the hang position
- Exhale during the aggressive, explosive jumping action [S11, p.11]

## Coaching Points
- **"Be explosive"** — the single key cue for the hang clean [S11, p.12]
- The hang position (bar just above knees) closely simulates an athletic, vertical jump position — this is why it is directly applicable to basketball performance [S11, p.5]
- Keep the bar close to the body throughout the lift
- Can be performed catching in a deep squat position or a taller (quarter-squat) position
- Can start the beginning position from blocks or a rack instead of the floor

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hang pull (no rack/catch) — develops the explosive extension pattern without the technical demand of the rack
2. **Intermediate**: Hang clean with a tall/quarter-squat catch
3. **Advanced**: Hang clean with a deep squat catch; increase load

# Closeout Shooting Drill — Off the Pass

## Objective
Train catch-and-shoot mechanics under live defensive closeout pressure from three zones (inside, outside, three-point), while simultaneously developing outlet passing and offensive rebounding habits.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2 (standard) or 3 (Rapid Fire variation)
- **Court area**: Half court — passer inside the free throw lane; shooter in designated zone
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball (2 for Rapid Fire)
- **Zones**: Inside lane, outside lane (midrange), behind three-point line

## Execution — 2-Player Version
1. Passer (P) starts inside the free throw lane with the ball.
2. P passes to Shooter (S) in the designated zone, then immediately sprints to close out on S with hands up (token defensive pressure).
3. P remains in S's area and becomes the **next shooter**.
4. S catches with feet and hands ready, shoots as quickly as possible.
5. S follows shot → rebounds → makes outlet pass back (now acting as passer) → closes out on new shooter.
6. Players alternate passer/shooter roles continuously for 3 minutes.
7. Record attempts and makes for each player from each zone.

## Zone Standards (Both Shooters Combined, 3 Minutes)

### Inside Shots
- Expected attempts: **37–39**
- 70% accuracy = ~26–27 made
- 60% accuracy = ~22–23 made
- 50% accuracy = ~18–20 made

### Outside Shots (Midrange)
- Expected attempts: **35–37**
- 60% accuracy = ~21–22 made
- 50% accuracy = ~18 made

### Three-Point Shots
- Expected attempts: **28–32**
- 50% accuracy = ~14–16 made per shooter

## Rapid Fire Variation — 3-Player Version
Adds a **third player and a second basketball** to maximize intensity:
1. Passer 1 (O1) passes to Shooter (O3) and closes out → O3 shoots, follows shot.
2. Simultaneously, Passer 2 (O2) passes the second ball to O1 (now the second shooter).
3. O1 catches, shoots, follows shot → becomes third passer.
4. All three players rotate continuously (pass → close out → shoot → rebound → pass).
5. Rotation: 1→3, 2→1, 3→2.
6. 3 minutes should yield **26–28 shots per shooter**; 50% accuracy = ~13–14 made.

## Coaching Points
- Shooter must have **feet and hands ready before the pass arrives** — "Be ready, not getting ready"
- Closeout must be at game speed with hands up — not a jog
- Passer follows the pass immediately; no resting after passing
- Track both attempts and makes — use data to benchmark individuals and groups
- Closeout pressure should be **token** (no reaching, no block attempts) — enough to create timing pressure

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow closeout, shooter has full time to set
2. **Intermediate**: Fast closeout, shooter must be pre-set
3. **Advanced**: Rapid Fire (3 players, 2 balls) — maximum tempo

# Backward Roll to Reaction (Whole-Body Reaction)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, and total-body agility. [S8, p.215]

## Setup
- 1 player, open floor space
- No equipment required
- Coach/partner provides audible or visual cues

## Execution
1. Start in a ready position (athletic stance).
2. Squat low and bend forward, beginning to fall backward in a controlled manner.
3. Roll over the left or right shoulder backward (alternate shoulders each repetition).
4. Complete the roll and return immediately to a ready position.
5. React instantly to the coach's audible or visual cue — execute the commanded action as fast as possible.

## Coaching Points
- Tuck the chin to protect the neck during the backward roll
- Roll over the shoulder, not the back of the head
- The pop-up to ready position should be explosive and immediate
- "Chin in, shoulder roll, pop up — react!"
- Eyes open immediately upon standing

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice backward roll alone to build confidence
2. **Intermediate**: Add reaction cues immediately upon standing
3. **Advanced (Complex Variation)**: Add a sport-specific skill after the roll — volleyball dig, sprawl, lateral shuffle, or vertical jump [S8, p.215]

# Shooting Technique Fundamentals

## Summary
Before a player can shoot from the three-point line on the move, they must be able to make a stationary 2-foot shot with perfect form. Shooting development is a process — crawl, walk, then run — that requires patience and discipline. The shot is broken down into sequential components built from the ground up: stance (foundation), shooting hand grip, elbow alignment, balance hand, target focus, and follow-through. Each layer depends on the one below it. The greatest shooters never stop critiquing their own form. [S5, pp.9-20]

## When to Use
- As the master framework for all shooting instruction
- When evaluating a player's shot using the end-of-chapter checklist approach
- When introducing shooting to beginners
- When retraining ingrained bad habits in more advanced players

## Key Principles
1. **Build from the ground up** — every element of the shot flows from proper foot position upward [S5, p.10]
2. **Process before results** — master short-range stationary form before attempting long-range or moving shots [S5, p.9]
3. **Legs/core = power; arms/wrists/fingers = finesse** — the lower body generates the power source; the upper body controls touch and direction [S5, p.12]
4. **Consistency is the goal** — the stance, grip, elbow position, balance hand, and target should be identical on every shot [S5, p.10]
5. **Self-evaluation is essential** — great shooters constantly critique their own form; players should apply this discipline daily [S5, p.9]

## The Shot Components (In Order)

### 1. Stance
- Feet shoulder-width apart, shooting foot slightly forward (toes to arch of non-shooting foot)
- Lead foot pointed at the target — establishes the **shot line** (toe → knee → hip → elbow → shoulder → wrist → ball → target)
- Bend at knees AND hips; shoulders in front of feet, head in front of shoulders
- See: [[shooting-stance]]

### 2. Shooting Hand Grip
- Ball on fingertips/fingerpads only — palm off the ball
- Shooting hand centered on ball, valve between index and middle finger (seaming)
- Arm in L-position; daylight between heel of hand and ball
- Wrist cocked (wrinkled)
- See: [[shooting-hand-grip]]

### 3. Elbow Alignment
- Elbow directly under ball and above the shooting foot
- L-shape maintained — lifter, not slinger or pusher
- Elbow faces the target
- Pre-set elbow in L before catching (shot-ready)
- See: [[shooting-elbow-alignment]], [[shot-ready-position]]

### 4. Balance Hand
- On the side of the ball — fingertips only, T-thumb check
- Does NOT shoot or guide — only balances and lifts
- Shooting hand must remain centered when balance hand is added
- See: [[shooting-balance-hand]]

### 5. Target Focus
- Center of the basket — always, from every location
- Eyes on target through and after the release — never follow the ball's flight
- Bank shot exception: top corner of backboard square
- See: [[shooting-target-focus]]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Rushing to long-range shots before mastering short-range form** → Mechanics break down; bad habits ingrained → Start at 2 feet, progress only when form is solid [S5, p.9]
2. **Any single component out of alignment** → Each flaw cascades; an off-stance breaks the shot line; an out elbow breaks accuracy → Check and correct each layer in order [S5, pp.10-19]

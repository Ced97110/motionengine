# Backward Roll to Hand Push-Off (Tumbling Drill)

## Objective
Develop total-body agility and kinesthetic awareness through a backward roll that uses a hand push-off to return to feet — demanding greater upper-body contribution than a standard backward roll. [S8, p.123]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Soft grass or gymnastics mat
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Bend the legs and start to sit on the ground behind you.
3. As you are about to make contact with the ground, roll straight back.
4. Continue to roll, and as your feet come over your head, **push off using your hands** and land on your feet. [S8, p.123]

## Coaching Points
- This differs from the shoulder roll: the push-off is bilateral and from overhead, not rotational
- Time the hand push-off precisely as the hips travel overhead — too early or too late and you miss the pop
- Wrists must be strong and prepared — taper into this drill from the shoulder roll progressions
- Finish in a balanced two-point stance immediately ready to react
- "Pop off your hands" is the coaching cue for the explosive extension at the top of the roll

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Backward roll over shoulder (no push-off required) to build comfort with backward movement
2. **Intermediate**: Hand push-off roll as described
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Add a sprint in any direction before or after
   - React to a visual or auditory cue after landing
   - Add a basketball-specific skill (catch, pass, shoot) immediately after standing [S8, p.123]

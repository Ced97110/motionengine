# Quadruped Arm and Leg Lift (Bird Dog) — Core Extension

## Objective
Develop core and spinal stability by resisting rotational forces during simultaneous opposite-arm and opposite-leg lifts, building the trunk strength needed to maintain ground position against contact on the court.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (gym or weight room)
- Optional: cuff weights for wrists/ankles, or rubber tubing for advanced variation

## Execution
1. Assume an all-fours position on the floor with hips directly aligned over knees and shoulders directly over hands. Maintain a neutral spinal position. Draw in the abdominal muscles.
2. Position the head and neck naturally with eyes focused on the floor.
3. Lift one arm and the **opposite leg** straight out into full extension, achieving complete alignment with the torso. **Do not allow either the hip or shoulder girdle to rotate.**
4. Maintain this extended position for the prescribed hold time — this builds endurance in the muscles involved.
5. Return to starting position, then lift the other arm and opposite leg.
6. Alternate for the prescribed number of repetitions and sets. [S2, pp.40-41]

## Coaching Points
- **"Carpenter's level"** cue: imagine a level running left-to-right across the back of the shoulders and across the hips — they must stay perfectly flat; no tilting
- Spinal neutral must be maintained throughout — no sagging or arching in the lumbar spine
- This exercise resists the rotatory moment created by lifting opposite supports — the challenge is maintaining stability, not speed [S2, p.41]
- Watch for hip or shoulder rotation — this is the most common error; reduce hold time before adding reps
- "You dominate the boards by being unmovable — this exercise builds that"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Short hold time (3-5 seconds); focus on not rotating hips or shoulders
2. **Intermediate**: Longer holds (10-15 seconds); multiple sets with deliberate alternating pattern
3. **Advanced — Weighted Bird Dog**: Add cuff weights on wrists and ankles, or attach rubber tubing to opposing arm and leg pairs for added resistance [S2, p.41]

### Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Multifidus, transversus abdominis, gluteus maximus, hamstrings (biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus), posterior deltoid, trapezius
- **Secondary**: Erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), transversus abdominis [S2, p.41]

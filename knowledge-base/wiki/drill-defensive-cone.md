# Defensive Cone

## Objective
Train defenders to control an attacker for 3 seconds using all on-ball defensive footwork and tactics — while the attacker employs all one-on-one dribbling moves — in a high-pressure competitive environment.

## Setup
- Player 1 at the wing, Player 2 in the corner, each with a basketball (dribble still alive)
- Defender X1 guards player 1; Defender X2 guards player 2
- One cone is placed between the ball handler and the basket
- Remaining players line up in the middle of the free-throw lane as a count crew
- Half court

<!-- DIAGRAM: Player 1 on wing, X1 guarding; Player 2 in corner, X2 guarding; cone between each player and basket; remaining players in paint counting. p.281 -->

## Execution
1. Player 1 and X1 go first; Player 2 and X2 wait until 1 and X1 finish.
2. Player 1 wants to dribble past the cone for an easy shot. X1 wants to prevent this and belly up to draw the charge when possible.
3. Players in the free-throw lane count aloud: "one-thousand-one, one-thousand-two, one-thousand-three." This adds competitive pressure.
4. The other players cheer on the defense loudly to build team defensive spirit.
5. Players have exactly 3 seconds to accomplish their purpose.
6. Rotate: offense → defense → middle of the free-throw lane (count crew).
7. Don't let players 1 and 2 go simultaneously; stagger them so rotation is smooth.
8. Continue until all players have played both offense and defense.
9. This is a great drill to end practice.

## Coaching Points
- "All you have to do is control the attacker for 3 seconds — that's team defense"
- The count crew creates game-like pressure without a shot clock
- Defender should use belly-up technique and be ready to draw the charge
- Use all defensive stance fundamentals: low base, eyes on belt buckle, active hands
- The drill forces defenders to concentrate and intensify on every possession

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Allow dribbler to use only one dribble move (e.g., crossover only)
2. **Intermediate**: Allow all four dribbling moves (in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin)
3. **Advanced**: Permit dribblers to try to fully drive past the defender at full speed

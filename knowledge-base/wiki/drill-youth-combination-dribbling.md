# Combination Dribbling Moves (Drill 16)

## Objective
Chain the rocker step (before-dribble move) with one of the four dribbling moves (in-and-out, crossover, spin, or half-spin) in one continuous sequence, integrating all stance-and-footwork skills into a realistic offensive attack.

## Setup
- Individual drill (advanced)
- Half court; choose one of four court locations rotationally:
  - **Day 1**: Left side of court, drive baseline
  - **Day 2**: Middle of court, drive left
  - **Days 3-4**: Right side of court
- Mark a **2-foot × 2-foot (.6m × .6m) box** on the floor at the dribbling-move execution point
- Players line up at the sideline/wing; coach at the elbow/free-throw line area
- One ball per station

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure shows line of players on the left wing. Player V-cuts to receive from coach at the elbow. Player dribbles into the taped box near the baseline and executes the coach-designated move, driving to the basket. -->

## Execution

1. Players line up as shown.
2. A player **V-cuts** to receive the ball from the coach.
3. Player receives the ball and assumes **triple-threat position**.
4. Player performs the part of the **rocker step** designated by the coach (coach explains when that portion would be used in a game).
5. Player begins to **dribble under control** in the designated direction, moving to the box.
6. At the box, player executes the **dribbling move** designated by the coach (in-and-out, crossover, spin, or half-spin).
7. Player drives to the basket and scores.

## Coaching Points
- The V-cut into triple threat into rocker step into dribble move is the **complete offensive sequence** — teach players to see it as one fluid action.
- The coach designates both the rocker step stage and the dribbling move — players do not choose yet. The Paye Drill (Drill 17) adds the read component.
- Rotate court locations **daily** to develop bilateral competency and versatility.
- Emphasize that the transition from rocker step to the first dribble should be **seamless and explosive** — no hesitation.
- Coaching cue: "Rocker, then attack" — the rocker creates the window, the dribble move exploits it.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Work each move in isolation (Drills 11-15) before combining
2. **Intermediate**: Coach designates rocker step stage and dribble move — player executes on command
3. **Advanced**: Player reads a live defender and self-selects both the rocker stage and dribble move (transitions into Drill 17 — Paye Drill)

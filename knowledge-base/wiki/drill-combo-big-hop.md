# Combo Big Hop Drill

## Objective
Train the big hop gather — a wide, athletic two-foot landing — as a pull-up jump shot finish off 1 and 2 live dribbles in both directions.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court — elbow area, wing, or short corner
- Shot tracking table (FGA, FGM %, FTA, FTM %)

## Execution
1. Player starts at the perimeter in triple-threat or off a live dribble.
2. **1-Dribble Left**: take 1 hard left-hand dribble, then gather into a big hop — both feet land wide and simultaneously, creating a wide, low, stable base.
3. Rise into a balanced jump shot from the big hop landing position.
4. Shoot 5 (Beginner), 10 (Intermediate), or 20 (Advanced) reps going left.
5. Step to the free-throw line; shoot 5–10 FTs with full routine.
6. **1-Dribble Right**: mirror the sequence going right.
7. Follow the L/R/FT cycle a second time for 1-dribble hops.
8. Repeat the entire structure for **2-dribble** big hops (L and R, twice each).
9. Total: 8 combo sets + 8 FT clusters.

**Volume Progressions:**
- **Workout #4-10 (80 shots)**: 8 sets × 5 combos + 8 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 24–40 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (85%)
- **Workout #4-11 (120 shots)**: 8 sets × 10 combos + 8 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 48–80 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (80%)
- **Workout #4-12 (240 shots)**: 8 sets × 20 combos + 8 × 10 FTs | FG goal: 97–160 (60%) | FT goal: 68–80 (85%)

## Coaching Points
- **Big hop = wide stance**: the landing is wider than a stride stop — feet land outside shoulder width to create a powerful, grounded shooting base
- **Simultaneous landing**: both feet must hit the floor at the same time; sequential landing converts this to a one-two step
- **Low center of gravity**: hips should be below the knees at landing; cue: **"Sit into the hop"**
- **Immediate lift**: as soon as the landing is absorbed, the shooting motion begins — no pause at the bottom
- **Direction of hop**: hop toward the basket angle, not straight up; the body should be square to the basket on landing
- Compare left vs. right percentages — if one side lags more than 10%, devote extra sets to that side

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (80 shots)**: 5 reps per set; practice the hop without a dribble first, focusing on landing mechanics and posture
2. **Intermediate (120 shots)**: 10 reps per set; add the dribble and execute at 75% speed
3. **Advanced (240 shots)**: 20 reps per set at full game speed; vary shot distance from 8–18 ft within the same set

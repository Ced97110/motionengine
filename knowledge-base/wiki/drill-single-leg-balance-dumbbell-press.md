# Single-Leg Balance Dumbbell Presses

## Objective
Increase single-leg balance under resistance by performing overhead dumbbell presses through a variety of tri-plane patterns while standing on one leg. [S8, p.133]

## Setup
- 1 player
- A pair of dumbbells (weight appropriate for overhead press; start light to master balance demand)
- Open floor area

## Execution
1. Stand balanced on a single leg (slight knee bend, not locked).
2. Hold dumbbells at shoulder height.
3. Perform overhead dumbbell presses through a **variety of tri-plane patterns** — pressing straight overhead, pressing at an angle (medial, lateral), alternating single-arm presses, etc.
4. Maintain balance throughout each pressing pattern without the elevated foot touching the ground.
5. Repeat on the opposite leg. [S8, p.133]

## Coaching Points
- Start with a light enough weight that balance — not pressing strength — is the limiting factor
- Keep the stance knee over the toes, not caving inward (dynamic valgus)
- Core must be braced — the pressing motion creates a destabilizing force that the stance leg and trunk must resist
- Vary the press direction to challenge balance in different planes
- "Press and stabilize" — the two concurrent demands must happen simultaneously

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Single-leg stance without dumbbells (see [[drill-single-leg-balance-opposite-arm-reach]])
2. **Intermediate**: Bilateral overhead press with light dumbbells
3. **Advanced**: Unilateral (single-arm) press on single leg in varied planes — maximally destabilizing

# Three-Point X-Out (SLOB)

## Overview
A side out-of-bounds play from the frontcourt designed to get an open three-point attempt when down 3 points. Uses a sequential pick-the-picker action to free two perimeter shooters (O2 and O3) in succession.

## Formation
- **O4**: Inbounder on the sideline (frontcourt SLOB)
- **O2 and O3**: Dive to their respective low blocks instead of cutting wide to corners (the key misdirection)
- **O5**: Sets up near the free-throw line extended to screen for O1
- **O1**: Ball handler target at the inbounds catch

## Phases

### Phase 1: Inbounds Entry
- **O2 and O3** dive to low blocks on their respective sides (not wide to corners — the misdirection element)
- **O5** sets a screen for **O1**, who receives the inbounds pass from O4
- **O1** clears O5's screen and catches the inbounds pass

### Phase 2: Sequential Screening for O2 (Primary)
- Simultaneously with the inbounds pass, **O3** sets a cross screen for **O2** as O2 sprints toward the corner
- After inbounding, **O4 immediately sets a second screen** for O2
- **O2** runs a curl off O4, looking for a three-point catch-and-shoot from the corner

### Phase 3: Pick-the-Picker for O3 (Secondary)
- After O1 clears O5's screen, **O5** continues to the right-side low block
- O5 sets the **final screen** in the pick-the-picker sequence for **O3**
- **O3** sprints to the three-point line, looking for a catch-and-shoot

### Phase 4: O1 Reads
1. **Shoot** if open after the catch
2. **Pass to O2** in the corner for a three-point shot
3. **Pass to O3** at the three-point line for a catch-and-shoot

## Key Coaching Points
- The misdirection of O2 and O3 diving to the blocks (not going wide) is essential — it draws defenders inside before the curl
- O4's immediate second screen after inbounding is the key to freeing O2
- O5 and O3 can exchange roles if O5 is the better perimeter shooter (modern positionless consideration)
- All three options (O1 shoot, pass to O2, pass to O3) are legitimate — O1 must read the defense immediately
- Timing is critical: O4's second screen and O5's pick-the-picker screen must be sequential, not simultaneous

## Counters
- If defense anticipates the curl for O2, O3's cut to the three-point line off O5's screen becomes the primary
- If the defense overloads the ball side, O1 can attack off the dribble
- Exchange O5 and O3 responsibilities if your center is a three-point shooter

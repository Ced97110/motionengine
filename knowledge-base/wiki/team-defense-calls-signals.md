# Team Defense Calls and Signals

## Summary
Organized teams use a numbering system (or keyword system) to communicate defensive type and pressure depth quickly and simply. Herb Brown describes one practical example that coaches at every level can adapt. The key principle: **make it simple and easy for your players to remember.** [S1, pp.179-180]

## When to Use
- After made baskets, free throws, dead balls, timeouts, end of quarters/halves
- Any time you want to change defensive tempo or surprise the offense
- Whenever a scouted situation triggers a specific defensive call based on which player scored

## Key Principles
1. **Use a two-digit number system**: the tens digit describes the defensive type; the units digit describes the court depth/location of pressure initiation.
2. **Make it simple**: Players must recall and execute the correct defense under pressure in real time.
3. **Key on who scores**: Some coaches call different defenses based on which offensive player scores — a powerful element of surprise. [S1, p.182]
4. **Change defenses after timeouts**: Switch the half-court defense after **every** timeout regardless of which team called it. [S1, p.184]

## Numbering System — Defense Type (Tens Digit)
| Code | Defense Type |
|------|--------------|
| 10 | Combination/gimmick defense — takes opponent out of set offense |
| 20 | Normal man-to-man (basic defense) |
| 30 | Run-and-jump or run-and-switching defense with ball in front |
| 40 | Trapping/double-teaming defense from behind the ball |
| 50 | Zone defenses |

## Numbering System — Court Depth (Units Digit)
| Code | Pressure Location |
|------|-------------------|
| 4 | Full-court pressure |
| 3 | Three-quarter court pressure |
| 2 | Half-court pressure |
| 1 | Perimeter pressure only |

**Example**: `34` = Run-and-jump, full-court. `52` = Zone, half-court pressure.

## Keyword Variants
- **"Hard"**: Really pressure the player taking the ball out of bounds
- **"Soft"**: Defender drops back into passing lanes, looks to trap or steal the first pass
- **"Marry" / "Deny"**: Full denial of the opposing point guard or best ball handler — prevent him from getting the ball back after a pass [S1, pp.179-180]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Overly complex signal systems** → players cannot recall them in pressure situations; simplicity wins
2. **Never changing defense** → predictability is a defense killer; changing after every timeout is a powerful tactic
3. **Not practicing the changes** → practice changing defenses after timeouts, dead balls, and free throws regularly [S1, p.176]

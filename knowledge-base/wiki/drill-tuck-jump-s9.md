# Tuck Jump (Step 7)

## Objective
Build explosive lower-body power (calves, ankles, glutes, quads, hip flexors, abdomen) and develop the full tuck — knees drawn to chest at peak height — as an essential prerequisite for advanced explosive and acrobatic movements.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any open space (no equipment required)

## Execution
1. Stand shoulder-width apart, body flexed and ready for action. [S9]
2. Dip down into a squat (same depth as a squat jump).
3. Explode upward using the power of the lower body plus all total-body spring available.
4. At the peak of the jump, explosively draw the knees as close to the chest as possible (the *tuck*). The trunk will naturally lean slightly forward — do not bend over excessively.
5. After the tuck, throw the legs back down for landing.
6. Land softly, catching most weight on the balls of the feet.
7. Use the elastic rebound immediately to repeat.

## Coaching Points
- "If you can't tuck properly, you can forget becoming really fast" — [S9, p.64]
- Form is everything: if knees cannot reach the chest, first target hip level; height increases with practice [S9]
- Hips and abdomen must fire explosively to pull the knees up horizontally — this is not a passive movement
- Calves, ankles, glutes, thighs, and lower back all contribute; this is a total lower-body demand [S9]
- Keep trunk lean minimal; avoid excessive forward bend
- Land on balls of feet; absorb force through the entire kinetic chain

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bring knees to hip level minimum; progress toward chest height over time [S9]
2. **Intermediate**: Standard — knees to chest in a full tuck
3. **Advanced**: Add a hand clap under the hamstrings at peak of movement — this tests height, accentuates midsection involvement, and builds hand speed [S9]

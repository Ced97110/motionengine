# Rip Step Big Hop

## Objective
Develop the rip step into a "big hop" two-foot gather and finish at the rim — building a balanced, contact-resistant finishing move for attacking the paint off the dribble.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court; wing or elbow to paint
- Ball only
- Choose volume level (80, 120, or 240 total shots)

## Execution
1. Start in triple threat on the wing or elbow.
2. Execute the **rip step**: rip the ball low across the body to protect it and begin the drive.
3. Take **1 dribble** (or **2 dribbles**) attacking the lane.
4. Perform a **big hop**: gather the ball off the dribble and hop forward (toward the basket) off one foot, landing simultaneously on **both feet** — creating a wide, stable base inside or near the paint.
5. From the two-foot landing, finish with a layup, power lay-in, short floater, or draw contact for foul shots.
6. Alternate **left (L)** and **right (R)** sides with free throws between each set.

**Set Order (all volume levels):**
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Big Hop (L)
- Free Throws
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Big Hop (R)
- Free Throws
- [Repeat 1-dribble cycle once more]
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Big Hop (L)
- Free Throws
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Big Hop (R)
- Free Throws
- [Repeat 2-dribble cycle once more]

## Coaching Points
- "Land on TWO feet at the same time — not one foot, then the other — to stay balanced through contact"
- The hop is forward (toward the basket), not upward — this gives horizontal momentum to finish through defenders
- Keep the ball high and two-handed on the gather to protect it from shot-blockers
- Landing with two feet gives the player a live pivot — they can pump-fake, spin, or shoot after the gather
- Practice finishing with both hands — go left and finish left-handed, go right and finish right-handed
- Track FGA, FGM, FTA, FTM in shot chart journal [S3]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #2-10, 80 shots)**: 5 reps per set — focus on landing mechanics and two-foot simultaneity
2. **Intermediate (Workout #2-11, 120 shots)**: 10 reps per set — game speed with varied finish types (power lay-in, floater)
3. **Advanced (Workout #2-12, 240 shots)**: 20 reps per set — full volume; add imagined contact resistance on each hop

# Jump Chain Progression (Explosive Calisthenics)

## Summary
The jump chain from *Explosive Calisthenics* is a 10-step progressive bodyweight plyometric system designed to build maximum lower-body speed-power. The chain progresses from basic squat jumps through butt-kick jumps, slap tuck jumps, tuck jumps, catch tuck jumps, thread jumps, and culminates in the Master Step: the suicide jump. Each step systematically develops the five foundational skills of explosive jumping: powerful lift-off, hip/waist integration, full tuck mechanics, the ability to throw the feet down faster than gravity, and expert force-absorption on landing [S9].

The chain is designed not only to build power in isolation but to serve as a prerequisite ladder for advanced acrobatic techniques (front flips, back flips, kip-ups). "If you can't tuck properly, you can forget becoming really fast" [S9].

## When to Use
- As the foundational lower-body explosive training block for all basketball athletes
- During the off-season power development phase before shifting to sport-specific plyometrics
- As a maintenance sequence during the in-season for athletes who have mastered baseline levels
- As a warm-up or conditioning block when gym equipment is unavailable (no equipment needed)

## Key Principles
1. **Step-by-step progressions are always the way to go** — do not skip steps; each builds on the previous [S9]
2. **Five basic jumping skills** must be mastered: lift-off power, hip/waist integration, tuck mechanics, foot-throw speed (faster than gravity), and landing force absorption [S9]
3. **Jumps are a power movement, not a skill movement** — although technique matters, the primary adaptation is speed-power (strength × speed), not complex skill [S9]
4. **Elastic rebound** — always use the elastic rebound at landing to power the next rep; do not pause between jumps
5. **Maintain jumps even after advancing** — even at a high level on flips or box jumps, basic power jumps remain valuable because they allow full energy focus on power rather than coordination [S9]
6. **Land softly** — on the balls of the feet; force absorption is itself a trainable skill
7. **Strict form over impressive appearance** — "worming" (butt-up alignment break) during plyometric push-ups applies equally here: form builds real power [S9]

## The 10-Step Chain
- **Steps 1-3**: Basic squat and spring drills (foundation power)
- **Step 4**: Butt-kick jumps (slight knee lift during jump)
- **Steps 5**: Intermediate tuck development
- **Step 6**: [[drill-slap-tuck-jump]] — knees to hip level, slap hands
- **Step 7**: [[drill-tuck-jump-s9]] — knees to chest, full tuck
- **Step 8**: [[drill-catch-tuck-jump]] — pull shins into body with hands, full compression
- **Step 9**: [[drill-thread-jump]] — single-leg thread jump; unilateral preparation for Master Step
- **Step 10 (Master)**: [[drill-suicide-jump]] — pass a bar under feet at peak tuck height

## Going Beyond the Chain
Once the suicide jump is mastered:
- **Reverse suicide jump**: bar held behind, whipped forward under feet — significantly harder due to awkward position and unnatural backward jump over an object [S9]
- **Angled pike jump**: legs kept straight at an angle (less than parallel) at peak — increases hip flexor and midsection demand vs. bent-knee tuck [S9]
- **Full pike jump**: straight legs reach parallel to the floor at peak — extreme hip flexor and core strength demand [S9]
- **Box jumps**: add height measurement and require a different mindset — best explored after basic jump proficiency [S9]
- **Front/back flip chains**: the logical continuation; flips are advanced jump patterns (launch → tuck → land) [S9]

## Small Space Ancillary Drills
Three equipment-free drills can supplement any step in the chain as warm-ups, finishers, or variety work:
- **Cossacks**: Low stance lateral leg-stretch drill — powers knees/hips/ankles through a full stretch range; builds tension-flexibility (supple strength) and balance [S9]
- **Wide-to-Close Pop-Ups**: From close push-up position, explode up, spread hands to wide push-up landing — pairs fast arms with fast legs for total-body power [S9]
- **Wall Threads**: One foot on a sturdy vertical surface, jump over it keeping foot in contact as long as possible — "awesome for fast feet" [S9]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Skipping steps** → Use every step in order; each builds specific qualities the next step demands [S9]
2. **Deep squat before the jump** — In suicide jumps, athletes squat to lower the bar; this is wrong → Stand tall; the height comes from the jump, not a lower start position [S9]
3. **Trying to jump the foot through the hole (thread jump)** → Lift the jumping leg up, then pull the hole back — the foot does not move through the loop [S9]
4. **Pausing at landing** → Use elastic rebound immediately; pausing loses the SSC benefit
5. **Excessive forward trunk lean during tuck** → A slight natural lean is acceptable, but do not bend over excessively

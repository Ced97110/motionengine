# Wheelbarrow Drill

## Objective
Improve power in the upper body and core through partner-assisted hand-walking and jumping patterns. [S8, p.155]

## Setup
- 2 players (working athlete + partner)
- Open floor or field space
- Equipment: optional — ladders, minihurdles, dot patterns for visual cue progressions

## Execution
1. One partner holds the working athlete's feet (as in a classic wheelbarrow).
2. The working athlete is on hands only, body straight and rigid.
3. The athlete performs a predetermined pattern or task with the hands as quickly as possible — hand runs, lateral shuffles, or circular walks.
4. The body must remain straight throughout; do not allow hips to pike or sag.
5. The drill can be timed to judge improvement, or the coach can use visual cues (ladders, dots) for the athlete to navigate.

## Coaching Points
- "Straight body from head to toe" — partner should be able to check this visually.
- Speed of hand movement is the training variable — the faster the hands move, the greater the neural demand.
- Visual cues (agility ladders, minihurdles, dot patterns) add a cognitive quickness layer.
- Can be initiated via a stimulus (e.g., whistle) and preceded or followed by any sport-specific skill (catching a ball, sprinting, tumbling). [S8, p.155]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow controlled hand walk forward in a straight line.
2. **Intermediate**: Time the athlete; add lateral shuffles with the hands.
3. **Advanced**: Perform jumps (instead of hand runs); circular walks; navigate an agility ladder.

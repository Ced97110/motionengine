# Two-Player Cutting (Drill 58)

## Objective
Teach the first two rules of the motion offense (use the cut your defender gives you; maintain 15-foot spacing) by having two players cut, pass, and verbalize their decisions.

## Setup
- 2 players per group; multiple groups rotate (1 minute active, then switch)
- Half court
- 6 cones placed at various spots around the half court (change locations daily)
- No defenders in the beginner version
- Equipment: 1 basketball, 6 cones

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Place 6 cones at different spots on the half court. Cones act as virtual defenders.
2. Player 1 and Player 2 begin in open positions approximately 15 feet apart.
3. Player 1 passes to Player 2 and immediately makes a cut — calling out the cut name and reason:
   - **Middle cut** if the cone/defender did not jump to the ball: "Middle cut — defender didn't jump to the ball"
   - **Backdoor cut** if the cone/defender overplays the passing lane: "Backdoor cut — defender overplayed"
   - **V-cut** to replace themselves if no advantage exists or spacing requires adjustment: "V-cut — maintaining spacing"
4. Player 2, after receiving, checks spacing. If Player 1 is more than 15 feet away, Player 2 may **dribble toward 1** to close the gap before passing.
5. Player 2 passes back to Player 1 and makes their own cut with verbalization.
6. The drill continues for **1 minute** with both players freely choosing V-cuts, middle cuts, and backdoor cuts — and dribbling as needed to maintain 15-foot spacing.
7. Players rotate to the end of the line; two new players begin.

### Intermediate
- Add a live defender on one of the two players (token defense only — not full pressure).
- The offensive player with a defender must now read a real defender instead of a cone.

### Advanced
- Both players have live defenders.
- Player 1 must use a jump stop at the end of any driving dribble near the basket.
- Player 1 must use an end-of-dribble move: pump fake, pump fake crossover, spin, or half-spin before finishing.

## Coaching Points
- **Verbalization is mandatory in the beginner level**: players must name the cut AND give the reason. If they can't explain the reason, the cut was wrong.
- The most common error is making a backdoor cut when a middle cut was available. Stop play and correct immediately.
- "Never, ever allow a backdoor cut when a middle cut should have been used" [S15, p.127]
- Watch for spacing — players who drift closer than 15 feet should immediately dribble away or V-cut to restore distance.
- Players must maintain 15-foot spacing **even if they have to dribble** to do so.
- Let players decide their own cuts — do not prescribe the action. The drill teaches reading, not choreography.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No defenders; cones as virtual defenders; verbalization required
2. **Intermediate**: Add one live defender; reading becomes real; remove verbalization requirement
3. **Advanced**: Both players have live defenders; jump stop + end-of-dribble move required near the basket

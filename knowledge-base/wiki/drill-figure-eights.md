# Figure Eights

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction and reaction time by running a continuous figure-eight pattern around two cones.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 2 flat cones placed 5 to 10 yards (4.6 to 9 m) apart
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Position two flat cones 5–10 yards apart.
2. Start in a two-point stance.
3. Run a figure-eight pattern between and around the two cones continuously, placing the inside hand on each cone as you make the turn.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Infinity/figure-eight shape; athlete loops around left cone (inside hand touching cone), crosses center, loops around right cone (inside hand touching cone), repeats. [S8, p.92] -->

## Coaching Points
- Touch the inside cone with the inside hand on each loop — this ensures proper body lean and tight turn radius
- Stay on the balls of the feet throughout
- Look ahead through the turn — do not look at the cone
- Arm drive through the crossover section accelerates the body into the next loop
- Vary the speed from controlled (learning the pattern) to maximum effort

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow jog, focus on touching each cone and maintaining body lean
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed continuous loops for a set time (10–20 seconds)
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Change the distance between cones [S8, p.92]
   - Change the radius of the turns (smaller = more difficult)
   - Start from various positions (lying, sitting, four-point stance)

# Barrier Jumps

## Objective
Improve power and quickness in the lower body through bilateral jumps over obstacles (hurdles, cones, boxes), using triple extension and knee tuck. [S8, p.173]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor or field
- Equipment: hurdles, cones, or boxes at appropriate height

## Execution
1. Stand in front of a barrier (hurdle, cone, or box).
2. Propel the body over the barrier by jumping forward using an ankle-knee-hip extension sequence.
3. Maintain a vertical body posture and tuck the knees to the chest while clearing the obstacle.
4. Use a double-arm swing to maintain balance and assist in achieving vertical height.
5. Land, immediately load again, and jump the next barrier.

## Coaching Points
- "Up first, then over" — clearing the barrier requires adequate vertical height before horizontal displacement.
- Double-arm swing is not optional — it adds height and stabilizes the trunk.
- Land softly in an athletic position and immediately reload for the next jump.
- Start with lower barriers (6–8 inches); progress height as proficiency develops.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Low barriers; slow reload between jumps.
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate barrier height; continuous without stopping.
3. **Advanced — Lateral Barrier Jumps**: Stand parallel to the barrier; load and propel laterally over it; land and immediately jump back. This version trains the lateral cuts and defensive movements in basketball.
4. **Advanced — Single-Leg Barrier Jumps**: Perform with one leg over shorter obstacles; gradually increase height.

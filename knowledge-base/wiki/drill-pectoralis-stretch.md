# Pectoralis Stretch

## Objective
Restore flexibility in the chest and anterior shoulder to increase shoulder range of motion during shooting and defending.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: a corner or doorway

## Execution
1. Stand in a corner or doorway with elbows at shoulder height. Place forearms flat on the walls.
2. Lean into the corner or doorway until you feel a stretch in the upper chest.
3. Hold this position for 30 seconds.
4. Repeat 3 to 5 times per day.

## Coaching Points
- Keep elbows exactly at shoulder height — raising or lowering changes which portion of the pec is stretched
- Do not shrug the shoulders; keep them relaxed and down
- Feel the stretch across the front of the chest, not in the shoulders or elbows

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Light lean, 30-second hold × 3 reps
2. **Intermediate:** Deeper lean with elbows at varied heights (high/low) to target different portions of the pectoralis

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Pectoralis major, pectoralis minor

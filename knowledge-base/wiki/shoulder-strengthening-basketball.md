# Shoulder Strengthening for Basketball

## Summary
Strengthening the shoulder — specifically the rotator cuff and scapular stabilizers — improves shooting, defending, and blocking while dramatically reducing overuse injury risk. Basketball players perform frequent overhead motions that stress these structures; targeted isotonic and plyometric exercises build the strength and neuromuscular control to protect the joint and optimize on-court performance. [S2, pp.156-161]

## When to Use
- Year-round maintenance program: 3 sets of 10 repetitions, performed 3 days per week
- Off-season: heavier emphasis on progressive strengthening
- In-season: maintenance volume (same exercises, controlled fatigue)
- After shoulder stretching is completed (flexibility before strength)
- For players recovering from minor shoulder soreness (with clearance)

## Key Principles
1. Pinch the shoulder blades together before and during all scapular exercises — this activates the lower and middle trapezius, the foundation of proper shoulder mechanics [S2, p.157]
2. Strengthen the scapular stabilizers (trapezius, rhomboids) to build the platform from which the rotator cuff fires [S2, pp.156-159]
3. Progress from bodyweight to resistance band to weighted exercises — never advance before control is mastered at the current level [S2, pp.156-161]
4. Plyometric shoulder training (medicine ball) converts strength gains into explosive power for shooting and rebounding [S2, p.161]
5. Never arch the back during overhead pressing movements; arms should finish in line with the ears [S2, p.160]

## Exercise Inventory

### YTW (Physioball)
- **Category**: Isotonic strengthening
- **Target muscles (primary)**: Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Target muscles (secondary)**: Biceps brachii, posterior/lateral deltoid, multifidus, erector spinae, transversus abdominis
- **Execution**: Lie prone on a Physioball, arms at sides. Pinch shoulder blades. **Y**: lift arms straight up toward head. **T**: lift arms straight to sides. **W**: lift arms to sides with elbows bent and rotate upward. Return between each position. 3 × 10 alternating Y, T, W.
- **Advancement**: Add hand weights.
- **Basketball focus**: Strengthens scapular stabilizers to facilitate proper muscle firing patterns for shooting, defending, and blocking. [S2, pp.156-157]

### Side-Lying Shoulder External Rotation
- **Category**: Isotonic strengthening
- **Target muscles (primary)**: Rotator cuff (infraspinatus, teres minor)
- **Target muscles (secondary)**: Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids
- **Execution**: Lie on your side, rolled towel under the arm, elbow bent to 90°. Pinch shoulder blades together. Lift the forearm toward the ceiling by rotating through the shoulder; keep elbow bent. Return to start. 3 × 10.
- **Advancement**: Add hand weights.
- **Basketball focus**: Strengthening the rotator cuff assists with shooting, defending, and blocking. [S2, p.158]

### Cheerleader (Resistance Band)
- **Category**: Isotonic strengthening
- **Target muscles (primary)**: Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Target muscles (secondary)**: Biceps brachii, anterior/posterior/lateral deltoid
- **Execution**: Stand, hold ends of resistance band in each hand in front of body. Pinch shoulder blades. Keep arms straight. Sequence: (1) stretch arms to sides; (2) stretch arms up toward right shoulder; (3) stretch arms to sides; (4) stretch arms up toward left shoulder. 3 sets × 5 repetitions.
- **Advancement**: Use a stronger resistance band.
- **Basketball focus**: Strengthens scapular stabilizers to facilitate proper muscle firing patterns for shooting, defending, and blocking. [S2, p.159]

### Overhead Press (Barbell or Dumbbell)
- **Category**: Isotonic strengthening
- **Target muscles (primary)**: Rotator cuff (supraspinatus)
- **Target muscles (secondary)**: Anterior/lateral deltoid, middle/lower trapezius, rhomboids, rectus abdominis
- **Execution**: Stand holding barbell or dumbbells, elbows bent at 30°, weights at shoulder level. Lift weights overhead to full extension; arms should finish in line with the ears. Do not arch back or look up. Slowly lower to start. 3 × 10.
- **Advancement**: Increase weight.
- **Basketball focus**: Increases general shoulder strength, improving shooting, defending, and blocking. [S2, p.160]

### Overhead Medicine Ball Toss (Plyometric)
- **Category**: Plyometric strengthening
- **Target muscles (primary)**: Biceps brachii, triceps brachii, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Target muscles (secondary)**: Anterior deltoid, middle/lower trapezius, rhomboids, multifidus, erector spinae, transversus abdominis, rectus abdominis
- **Execution**: Stand a few inches from a wall. Hold medicine ball overhead, arms bent to 90°. Repetitively toss the ball against the wall, mimicking a soccer throw-in action, for 10-second intervals. Perform prescribed number of throws.
- **Advancement**: Use a heavier medicine ball or increase timed intervals.
- **Basketball focus**: Plyometric strengthening increases power for shooting and rebounding. [S2, p.161]

## Common Mistakes
1. Skipping the shoulder blade pinch cue → scapular stabilizers won't fire correctly; always set the scapula before moving the arm
2. Using too much weight too soon in overhead press → the rotator cuff is small; start light and master form before adding load
3. Letting the elbow drift out in side-lying external rotation → keep elbow pinned to the rolled towel throughout the movement
4. Arching the back during overhead press → core must remain neutral; arching shifts load to the lumbar spine
5. Skipping the plyometric phase → power for shooting and rebounding requires explosive shoulder strength, not just static strength

# Five-Player Passing Game Fast Break Drill

## Objective
Build full-court passing game execution and conditioning simultaneously by running five players through unrestricted, continuous fast-break actions at full speed until physical exhaustion forces a stoppage.

## Setup
- Full court
- 5 players, no defense
- Each group has a ball
- No time limit — drill ends when a player calls time-out due to fatigue

## Execution
1. Five players distribute across the court at the baseline
2. Initiate the fast break (toss off backboard or coach passes to start)
3. Players run any passing game options they choose — cuts, give-and-go, backdoor, screens on/off ball
4. **Everything must go at full speed** — no walking, no half-speed execution [S7, p.159]
5. Continue running both directions, full court, until one player requests a time-out because they are out of gas
6. Rotate groups; run for 15 minutes total

## Coaching Points
- There is no play called — players react to the defense (or each other) with the best read
- Any player who slows down or coasts is immediately corrected
- The drill's self-regulating nature (runs until exhaustion) ensures honest conditioning work
- Quality of decisions at fatigue is as important as fresh — game situations come in the fourth quarter
- "Players can run any options they want on the passing game, but everything must go at full speed." [S7, p.159]

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: 5-on-0 only, free-flowing options
2. **Advanced**: Add 1–2 defenders who do not contest shots but simulate pursuit angles
3. **Game-speed**: Full 5-on-5 continuous, defense transitions to offense on every score/stop

# Mentally Correcting a Miss

## Summary
Hal Wissel introduces a specific mental protocol for handling missed shots — one of the most undercoached aspects of basketball. The core idea: every time a player reacts negatively to a miss (negative word, negative body language, negative thought), the brain is conditioned to repeat that missed shot. Players must instead "act like a shooter, feel like a shooter, be a shooter" by immediately correcting the miss mentally and visualizing a good shot [S7, p.10].

The computer analogy: when you type a mistake, you don't retype it — you delete it and correct it. The same process applies to shooting errors. Identify the cause, correct it with a trigger word, visualize the corrected shot with an anchor word, and move on [S7, pp.10-11].

## When to Use
- After any missed shot in practice or games
- During shooting slumps — when consecutive misses threaten confidence
- As part of a pre-shot routine to prevent error repetition
- When teaching young players how to handle adversity on the court

## Key Principles
1. **Identify the cause of the miss**: Use rim reaction (sidespin, long, short), feel during the shot, or video to diagnose the specific error [S7, p.9].
2. **Never dwell on the miss**: Negative body language or words re-program the brain for failure. Act like a shooter regardless of outcome [S7, p.10].
3. **Correct immediately with a trigger word**: If the shot was short due to lack of leg use, say "Legs!" out loud while mentally visualizing proper leg action [S7, p.10].
4. **Reinforce with an anchor word**: Immediately after the trigger correction, say an anchor word ("Yes!", "Net!", "Through!") while visualizing the ball going in [S7, p.11].
5. **Rim reaction as feedback**: Ball circles out = sidespin (hand started on side of ball); miss right = arm/elbow pointed right or body not square; miss left = ball positioned too far right, shoving motion [S7, pp.9-10].
6. **Shooting with eyes closed**: An excellent drill for developing feel — partner rebounds and reports direction/reaction of miss; reveals side-spin and other errors through kinesthetic feedback [S7, p.10].

## Three Sources of Feedback After a Miss
1. **Rim reaction**: Observe where the ball hits and how it bounces — circles out = sidespin, front-to-back skim = sidespin, hard hit = palm contact
2. **Internal feel**: Feel for hand rotation, ball sliding off ring finger instead of index finger, body position
3. **Video review**: Watch shooting form on tape to confirm visual and kinesthetic observations [S7, p.9]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Thinking too much after a miss** → Replace overthinking with a one-syllable trigger word; slow rhythm is the symptom
2. **Negative body language** → Conditions failure; immediately adopt a positive physical posture
3. **Not diagnosing the miss** → Without knowing the cause, the next shot uses the same error; always analyze rim reaction or feel
4. **Using an anchor word without first correcting** → The anchor without the trigger can reinforce unconscious errors; always pair them in order: correct first, then reinforce

# Side-to-Side Skiers (Angle-Board Drill)

## Objective
Develop agility and rotational change of direction by jumping and rotating in mid-air to land on alternating angle-board positions. [S8, p.119]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Angle board (a board with angled surfaces on each side of a center run)
- Open area with angle board set flat

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance standing perpendicular to the angles in the middle of the run.
2. **Jump and rotate in mid-air** so as to land with both feet on the right angle, facing the direction from which you have run.
3. Immediately jump back, rotating in mid-air, to land in the original center position.
4. **Jump and rotate in mid-air** so as to land with both feet on the left angle, again facing the direction from which you have run.
5. Immediately jump back, rotating in mid-air, to land in the original center position.
6. Repeat the pattern continuously. [S8, p.119]

## Coaching Points
- All rotation happens **in the air** — the landing should be balanced and quiet
- Land on the balls of both feet simultaneously with slight knee bend to absorb impact
- Minimize ground contact time between hops — reactive power is the goal
- Core must be braced through every rotation to prevent loss of balance
- This drill can also be done without an angle board using painted lines or tape

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice bilateral rotational jumps in place (no equipment) to build pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill as described
3. **Advanced**: Increase speed of repetitions; add visual cue from a coach to direct which side to jump to

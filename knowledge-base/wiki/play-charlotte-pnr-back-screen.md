# Charlotte Hornets Pick-and-Roll with Back Screen

## Overview
Coach Paul Silas used a wing pick-and-roll with David Wesley (1) and Elden Campbell (5) as the surface action, but the real goal was getting a post-up for Campbell. Eddie Jones (2) plays the key role by back-screening for the rolling 5, who crosses the lane to establish a post-up. A secondary variation posts up the 2 instead. [S14, pp.33]

## Formation
- **1** handles the ball on the left wing/extended area
- **5** is the ball-screen setter (near high post or midpost area)
- **2** aligns on the left block
- **4** on the right wing
- **3** in the right corner

## Phases

### Option A: Post-Up for 5 (Primary)

#### Phase 1: PnR Initiation
- **1** runs a wing pick-and-roll with **5**
- **5** rolls after screening

#### Phase 2: Back Screen Cross
- As the PnR unfolds, **2** (from the left block) sets a **back screen for 5**
- **5** continues his roll off **2**'s back screen and crosses the lane to **post up on the right block**

#### Phase 3: Entry and Reads
- **1** swings the ball to **4**
- **4** reads:
  1. Feed **5** in the post (primary)
  2. Swing to **3** in the right corner for the **interior pass to 5** (if 4 can't feed directly)

### Option B: Post-Up for 2 (Variation)

#### Phase 1: Same PnR Initiation
- **1** runs the same 1-5 wing PnR

#### Phase 2: Role Reversal
- **2** begins a back-screen move for **5** — but instead cuts **back across the lane** to post up on the **right block** himself
- **5** rolls to the **baseline** (not across the lane)
- **1** dribbles off **5**, who rolls to the baseline

#### Phase 3: Entry and Reads
- **1** passes to **4**
- **4** reads:
  1. Feed **2** directly in the post
  2. Swing to **3** in the corner for the **interior pass to 2**

## Key Coaching Points
- The back-screen by the wing man (2) disguises the real intent — the PnR looks like a standard drive but the primary goal is the cross-lane post entry
- **Timing**: 2's back screen must occur as 5 is rolling — the cross happens naturally off the momentum of the screen
- 4's ability to read whether to feed directly or swing to the corner is critical
- The two options (posting 5 vs. posting 2) can be called to exploit whichever big/wing has the better matchup

## Counters
- If the defense hedges hard on the PnR, **1** has room to attack the basket before the back-screen reads develop
- If X4 sags to protect the post, **4** is open for a mid-range catch
- If X2 sags off to help, **2** can pop to the perimeter instead of posting

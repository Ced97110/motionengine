# 10-Cone Snake Drill

## Objective
Improve cutting ability, lateral shuffle, and change of direction by combining shuffles and diagonal sprints through a 10-cone snake pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 10 cones total:
  - 7 cones in a straight horizontal line, 5 yards (4.6 m) apart (cones 1–7, left to right)
  - 3 additional cones placed 5 yards in front of cones 2, 4, and 6

## Execution
1. Start at cone 1 (far left of the bottom row).
2. **Shuffle laterally** (no foot crossover) from cone 1 to cone 2.
3. **Plant right foot** and drive forward to the cone 5 yards in front of cone 2.
4. **Sprint diagonally** around that lead cone to cone 3.
5. **Shuffle** from cone 3 to cone 4.
6. **Plant right foot** and drive forward to the cone in front of cone 4.
7. **Sprint diagonally** around it to cone 5.
8. **Shuffle** from cone 5 to cone 6.
9. **Plant right foot** and drive forward to the cone in front of cone 6.
10. **Sprint diagonally** around it to cone 7 to finish.
11. **Perform the drill in both directions** for one full repetition.

## Coaching Points
- During shuffles: feet never cross — maintain a legal defensive slide
- Plant the right foot decisively at the base cone before driving forward
- Sprint diagonally around lead cones with maximum acceleration
- "Shuffle sharp, sprint hard" — no deceleration between the two movement modes

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through to learn the complex pattern first.
2. **Intermediate**: Game speed, both directions.
3. **Advanced**: Time each repetition; add a defensive stance arm position during shuffles.

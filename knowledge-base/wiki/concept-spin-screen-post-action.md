# Spin Screen Post Action

## Summary
The spin screen is a two-man inside game between two post players that combines a duck-in cut with a blind back screen set from the weak side. When the ball is reversed to the top of the key, the ballside post (05) attacks with a duck-in cut. If the duck-in is denied (jammed), the weak-side post (02) cuts across the lane and sets a baseline-side back screen on 05's defender, allowing 05 to spin off the screen 180 degrees and post up on the opposite block. The action is difficult to defend without a switch, and a switch creates a second, equally dangerous scoring threat.

The spin screen is named for the reverse pivot "spin" the cutter makes off the screener's body. Coaches should note that the screener (02) is typically smaller than the cutter (05), creating a small-on-big back screen — a deliberate design choice that discourages defensive switching. [S6, pp.181-182]

## When to Use
- After a ball reversal from wing to the top of the key, eliminating a true ballside and helpside
- When the post defender (X5) is aggressively fronting or three-quartering the duck-in cut
- When the offense wants to attack a size or quickness mismatch in the post
- As a repeatable action — the primary receiver can vary each time it is run, making it unpredictable [S6, p.184]

## Key Principles
1. **Weak-side post starts lower than the cutter** — 02 must be below 05's vertical level, placing him out of X5's line of sight for a true blindside screen angle [S6, p.182]
2. **05 commits to the duck-in cut first** — the spin move is only triggered when X5 denies. A half-hearted duck-in telegraphs the action
3. **Spin off the baseline (low) shoulder of the screener** — 05 uses a reverse pivot off 02's low shoulder and cuts across the lane to the opposite block [S6, p.182]
4. **Contact foot into the defender's stance** — 05 places his contact foot (the foot on the side closest to X5) between X5's feet and uses it as the pivot foot for the 180-degree reverse pivot [S6, p.183]
5. **Free elbow locks high** — the elbow on the free-foot side is held high and locked during the reverse pivot, using the backside and free elbow to seal off the defender [S6, p.183]
6. **"Battle of the feet"** — the post player must win the footwork battle to win the scoring opportunity: *"To win the war (catch and score), the battle (of the feet) must be won."* [S6, p.183]
7. **Ideal location: notch above the block** — 05 must establish position at the notch above the new ballside block, not allowing the defender to push him below [S6, p.183]
8. **Ball reversal to the top neutralizes helpside** — with the ball centered in 03's hands, no true helpside or ballside is designated, isolating both X5 and X2 [S6, p.185]

## Player Responsibilities
- **C (05, cutter):** Commit aggressively to the duck-in cut first. If jammed, execute the reverse pivot off 02's baseline shoulder. Step contact foot between defender's feet, swing free foot 180°, lock free elbow high, use backside to maintain seal. Post up on the notch above the opposite block. [S6, pp.182-183]
- **PF (02, screener):** Start below 05's vertical level on the weak side. Cut across the lane when X5 denies 05. Set a hard blindside back screen on X5's baseline side. If the defense switches, execute a reverse pivot (Diagram 4-9) or a front crossover pivot with swim move (Diagram 4-10) to seal off the new defender in the middle of the lane. [S6, pp.182, 185]
- **PG (03):** Receive the ball reversal at the top of the key. Read the spin screen action. Deliver the pass to whichever post player is more open — either the cutter (05) or the screener (02) after a switch. [S6, p.184]
- **Perimeter players (01, 04):** Maintain spacing on the wings. Their distance from the action keeps the lane clear and prevents the defense from collapsing to help. [S6, p.181]

## Variations
### Switch Counter — Screener (02) Reverse Pivot Seal
If X2 switches onto 05, 02 executes a reverse pivot off his top (left) foot, swinging the lower foot around to box-out X5 in the middle of the lane. 02 seals X5 and becomes the primary scoring threat from a high-percentage area. [S6, p.185]

### Switch Counter — Screener (02) Front Crossover Pivot / Swim Move
Instead of a reverse pivot, 02 sets the spin screen and then makes a front crossover pivot off his top foot, using the swim technique with his contact arm to seal X5 in the same high-scoring location. Achieves the same result as the reverse-pivot seal with different footwork. [S6, p.186]

### Mirror Action (Opposite Side)
The same action can be initiated from the opposite side of the court with 05 on the offense's right block. All footwork descriptions are mirrored — pivot and free feet switch. [S6, p.184]

## Common Mistakes
1. **02 starts too high (level with 05)** → X5 sees the screen coming. Fix: 02 must be on a lower vertical level than 05 to be out of X5's line of sight.
2. **05 does not fully commit to the duck-in** → the action is telegraphed. Fix: 05 must take at least one aggressive step on the duck-in cut before spinning.
3. **Spin is off the wrong shoulder of the screener** → illegal screen or ineffective seal. Fix: always spin off the low (baseline) shoulder.
4. **Free elbow not locked high during reverse pivot** → defender can fight through the screen. Fix: hold free elbow high and locked like a box-out pivot.
5. **05 allows himself to be pushed below the block** → loses the scoring angle. Fix: 05 must establish and fight to maintain position at the notch above the block.
6. **Passer (03) predetermines the receiver** → defense can cheat. Fix: the primary receiver should vary; 03 must read who is more open.

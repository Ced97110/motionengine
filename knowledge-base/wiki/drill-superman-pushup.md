# Superman Push-Up

## Objective
Build total-body explosive power by simultaneously launching all four limbs off the floor in a horizontal push-up position, developing anterior and posterior chain strength in conjunction with upper-body pushing muscles.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface with adequate clearance overhead and in front
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Squat down, place palms on the floor below your shoulders (shoulder-width or wider), and stretch legs behind you.
2. Place feet at a comfortable width — beginners use a wider base; advanced athletes go narrower.
3. Keep legs, hips, and torso aligned and horizontal (perfect plank position).
4. Bend arms and shoulders, dipping torso toward the floor (descend into the push-up).
5. Immediately push explosively off the floor, straightening arms fully.
6. Explode upward as hard as possible; while airborne, thrust hands directly out in front of the body.
7. Simultaneously kick legs out and up behind you, striving to keep the body horizontal throughout the flight phase.
8. Generate enough force to allow hands AND feet to leave the floor at the same time.
9. As gravity reverses movement, "catch" the floor with hands and feet simultaneously.
10. Use the elastic rebound to immediately repeat the technique — do not pause between reps.

## Coaching Points
- "Body stays a plank — hips don't pike or sag"
- The goal is all four limbs airborne simultaneously — if feet leave before hands or vice versa, focus on synchronizing the push and the kick
- Push higher and higher as technique improves — hip height is the progression marker
- Arms thrust forward and legs thrust back — the body should look horizontal at peak airtime
- Catch the floor softly to use elastic rebound energy for the next rep
- Beginners: focus on getting any air at all; chest may still touch floor lightly in early stages

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Regular clapping push-ups (hands only leave the floor) to build explosive pushing power
2. **Intermediate**: Feet begin to leave the floor; hands thrust forward a short distance
3. **Advanced**: Full Superman — all four limbs fully airborne, body horizontal
4. **Elite**: X-Jump Push-Up — spread-eagle arms and legs at peak (requires more airtime and hip activation); chest-strike mid-air; clap behind the back; hands behind head

## Variations
### X-Jump Push-Up
At peak airtime, spread-eagle arms and legs rather than thrusting straight out. Requires greater limb movement and more hip activation. [S9, p.4]

### Aztec Push-Up
Propel hips so high that a toe-touch pike can be performed at the top position. More of a waist-power exercise than a pure push-up. [S9, p.4]

### Crossing Aztec Push-Up
Aztec variation where the opposite hand and foot are touched at the top, increasing coordination demands. [S9, p.5]

### One-Arm Clap Push-Up
Unilateral version — one hand missing means the free hand strikes the chest mid-air. Maximum upper-body power demand. [S9, p.6]

## Exercise X-Ray (Why It Works)
Regular push-ups strongly work the **anterior chain** (abs, hips, quads) to prevent hip sinking. The Superman also demands the **posterior chain** (spinal muscles, glutes, hamstrings) to blast the feet off the ground. Both chains fire explosively, in conjunction with upper-body pushing muscles (chest, triceps, shoulders). This makes it arguably the most complete explosive upper-body exercise available [S9, p.2].

## Common Mistakes
1. Hips piking upward → correction: maintain plank alignment throughout
2. Hands and feet not leaving simultaneously → correction: synchronize explosive leg kick with arm push
3. Landing stiff → correction: "catch" the floor with bent elbows and soft knees; use rebound
4. Pausing between reps → correction: use elastic energy immediately on contact

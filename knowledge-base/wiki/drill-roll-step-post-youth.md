# Roll Step Drill (Drill 100)

## Objective
Teach the **offensive roll step** — the "unstoppable" counter to any defensive two-step or one-step post coverage — where the post player allows the defender to commit to the movement, then uses a 180° reverse pivot to seal and receive an open pass.

## Setup
- Same setup as Drill 99 (Two-Step Drill)
- **4 roles**: Player 5 (post attacker), X5 (post defender), Players 1 and 3 (perimeter passers)
- **Rotation**: same as Drill 99
- Advanced level

## Execution
1. **Line up players exactly as in Drill 99** (figure 1).
2. Instead of fighting the defensive two-step (or one-step), **Player 5 allows X5 to complete the movement without interference**.
3. As X5 completes the two-step, **Player 5 turns to face the defender** (figure 2).
4. **As Player 3 catches the ball**, Player 5 executes a **180° reverse pivot**:
   - **First step**: foot away from the defender (right foot in the diagram).
   - **Second step**: the reverse pivot itself.
5. Now the defender **cannot possibly recover** — Player 5 has sealed X5 on their back.
6. **Player 3 quickly passes back to Player 1**, giving Player 1 a perfect passing angle into the post.
7. The post defender **cannot prevent** the pass from going into the post — Player 5 is prepared to execute offensive moves.

**Option:** Make it a live one-on-one drill — defender and post attacker read and react to developing situations.

## Coaching Points
- Key insight: **fighting the two-step is counterproductive** — the roll step is easier and more effective.
- "Allow the move, then own the position" — patience is the key.
- The reverse pivot must be **explosive** — slow pivots allow the defender to recover.
- First step must be **away from the defender** to create the pivot leverage.
- After sealing, hands are out as targets — prepare to catch and immediately attack.

## Progressions
1. **Advanced (non-live)**: Walk through the sequence — X5 executes two-step, Player 5 performs roll step without resistance.
2. **Live**: Full live one-on-one with perimeter passers reading and making the entry pass.

# S-Drill Cone Drill

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction, body position, and cutting ability by weaving through 4 cones arranged in an S-shape.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4 cones in an S-shape:
  - Cone 1: start line
  - Cone 2: 10 yards forward and 5 yards to the right
  - Cone 3: 10 yards further forward and directly in line with cone 1
  - Cone 4: 10 yards further up and in line with cone 2
- Total distance: approximately 30 yards

<!-- DIAGRAM: S-curve path — cone 1 at bottom left, cone 2 upper right, cone 3 upper left, cone 4 top right. Player loops around outside of each cone alternating sides. See [S8, p.102] -->

## Execution
1. Start at cone 1.
2. **Sprint diagonally** forward and around the **outside** of cone 2.
3. **Sprint** from cone 2 diagonally and around the **outside** of cone 3.
4. **Sprint** from cone 3 diagonally and around the **outside** of cone 4.
5. **Loop around** cone 4 and **return** in the opposite S-pattern back to cone 1.

## Coaching Points
- Stay as close as possible to each cone on each loop — tight curvilinear running
- Drive with inside arm during each curve to generate momentum
- Lean into the curve; do not run upright
- "Make the S tight" — sloppy loops waste time and reduce training effect

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reduce distances to 5 yards between cones for tighter patterns.
2. **Intermediate**: Full 10-yard spacing at game speed.
3. **Advanced**: Time each run; compete against teammates or personal record.

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — curvilinear speed and continuous direction changes
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — centripetal force loading at each cone turn

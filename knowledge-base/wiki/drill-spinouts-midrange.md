# Spinouts (Midrange)

## Objective
Simulate a catch-and-shoot midrange opportunity when practicing alone, reinforcing the one-two step, shot pocket catch, and frozen follow-through.

## Setup
- 1 player, no partner needed
- Half court, 7 spots around the basket (midrange distance)
- 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Start in a balanced athletic stance at your chosen spot.
2. Spin the ball toward yourself so it bounces off the floor and rises directly into your shot pocket. **The spin should never come above your waist.**
3. Time the spin and step into the ball with a one-two step: non-shooting foot first, shooting foot second. Do not catch and pause — the second step should already be in motion.
4. Catch the ball in the shot pocket and rise directly into the jump shot with no dip.
5. Release just before the top of the jump.
6. Freeze your follow-through (elbow above eyebrow, hand in hoop, balance hand fingertips pointing up) **until the ball hits the floor.**
7. Perform 10 repetitions from one spot. Record your makes.
8. Rotate through 7 spots circling the basket.

## Coaching Points
- **"Start low, stay low."** If the spinout rises above the waist, the player must bring it down first — creating a high-low-high motion that allows defenders more time to contest.
- **Freeze the follow-through until the ball hits the floor.** Dropping hands in practice = dropping hands in games.
- **Go for makes, not takes.** Great shooters practice to make shots. Once comfortable, progress from shooting 10 reps to making 10 reps. If making 10 is a struggle, start by making 5.
- **Strings style option:** Shoot until you miss; track your personal best streak from each spot.
- **Add faking elements progressively:** Monday = straight shot; Tuesday = add shot fake; Wednesday = add jab step; Thursday = jab → shot fake → shoot.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Shoot 10 from each spot, record makes. Focus on mechanics — frozen follow-through and low catch.
2. **Intermediate**: Progress to making (not just shooting) 10 from each spot. Use strings style from all 7 spots.
3. **Advanced**: Add faking variations (shot fake, jab step, jab-then-fake), timed sets, and extend range to the three-point arc.

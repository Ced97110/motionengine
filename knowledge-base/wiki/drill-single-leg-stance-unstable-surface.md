# Single-Leg Stance on Unstable Surface

## Objective
Restore single-leg balance, quadriceps strength, and joint stability in the knee and ankle using an unstable surface — simulating the single-leg demands of driving to the basket and jumping off one leg.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Unstable surface: BOSU ball, Dynadisc, or foam pad
- Optional: partner for tap variation

## Execution
1. Stand erect on an unstable surface (BOSU, Dynadisc, or foam pad) with all body weight distributed through the affected lower extremity. Lift the other leg.
2. Slightly bend the standing knee and lower into a minisquat. Balance and maintain fixed posture.
3. If the bent-knee position is painful, begin with a straight leg and progress to a pain-free slightly bent position.
4. Perform the prescribed number of timed bouts [S2, pp.142-143].

## Coaching Points
- "Find your balance first, then introduce the knee bend"
- Monitor for valgus (inward) knee collapse during the minisquat — cue the knee to stay over the second toe
- "The ability to stand on one leg strengthens the quadriceps and aids in developing stability in the knee and ankle" [S2, p.143]
- Begin on the floor before the unstable surface if needed
- Progress only when pain-free and controlled

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Single-Leg Stance)**: Flat floor, straight or slightly bent knee, hold timed bout
2. **Intermediate**: BOSU or foam pad, slight knee bend, timed bouts
3. **Advanced (Single-Leg Stance With Tap)**: Partner taps player from side to side, forcing reactive balance control [S2, p.143]

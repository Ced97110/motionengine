# Continuity Offense — Entry Options

## Summary
The Continuity Offense (high-post offense) can be started in many different ways, giving it unpredictability against aggressive defenses. The three categories of entries are pass entries, dribble entries, and screen entries. Each entry is a read-based response to what the defense gives, not a scripted call. [S7, pp.114-116]

## When to Use
- **Pass to wing**: When the wing is available (not the most common — wings are often overplayed)
- **Pass to high post + guard cuts**: When both wings AND the off-guard are overplayed
- **Dribble clear**: When all four teammates are covered and no pass is available
- **Dribble weave**: When defense plays loose and will allow a direct dribble approach
- **Reverse dribble**: When the wing toward whom 1 dribbles is overplayed but the opposite wing is free
- **Midscreen entries**: When 5 can set a ball screen and 1 needs to get around a ball defender

## Key Principles
1. No entry is "the entry" — the right entry is the one the defense allows.
2. Every dribble toward a wing signals that wing to clear and cut backdoor or post low.
3. The high-post player (5) must be able to pass, dribble, and shoot — he is "like a big point guard." [S7, p.114]
4. Wings should be above the free-throw line extended at the start so they can cut in any direction.
5. Guard-wing exchanges and position swaps keep the defense from settling.

## Pass Entries

### Pass to Wing
- 1 passes to 3 (wing on his side) — the easiest, least common entry because wings are often denied.
- After passing, 1 can cut to the same side as the ball OR to the other side of the court.
- Read: if defender denies 3, skip to a dribble or high-post entry. [S7, p.115]

### Pass to High Post + Guard Cut
- Triggered when both wings (3 and 4) AND off-guard (2) are overplayed.
- 1 passes to 5, who has flashed to the free-throw area.
- 1 and 2 simultaneously cut to the **low-post positions** on the same side as their cuts.
- Wings 3 and 4 **replace the guards** at the guard spots.
- 1 and 2 can exchange positions with 3 and 4 after the entry. [S7, p.115]
- Result: fresh positions, new 1-on-1 matchups, defense must recover.

## Dribble Entries

### Dribble Clear
- When no teammate is free, 1 begins the play by **dribbling directly toward** one of the wings.
- This dribble toward the wing is a **non-verbal signal**: that wing clears out and either cuts backdoor to the basket or posts down low.
- Example: 1 dribbles toward 3; 3 clears out and cuts to the basket. [S7, p.115]
- Coaching cue: "When 1 comes at you, go away — either backdoor or low."

### Dribble Weave
- Used to beat an aggressive defense that prevents a direct pass.
- 1 dribbles toward 3 and makes a **short kickoff pass** (or handoff) to 3 if the defense plays loose.
- Simultaneously, 2 and 4 **exchange positions**.
- If executed at full speed, this creates confusion for help defenders. [S7, p.116]

### Reverse Dribble
- 1 dribbles in one direction, then **executes a reverse dribble** and passes to the wing (4) who has exchanged positions with 2.
- 1 can also pass to 5 who has **popped out to the free-throw lane** to get free.
- Useful when the wing toward whom 1 originally dribbled is overplayed. [S7, p.116]

## Screen Entries

### Midscreen on the Ball
- 5 sets a **midscreen on 1** to create an entry angle.
- 2 (overplayed by his defender) **cuts into the lane** as soon as he sees 5's screen on 1 and goes to the **opposite corner**.
- 3 and 4 come high.
- 1 uses the screen and **passes to 3**.
- Coaching cue: 2 must read 5's screen immediately — the cut happens simultaneously with the screen. [S7, p.116]

### Midscreen on Ball + Reverse Dribble
- 1 receives 5's midscreen but **cannot pass to 3** (overplayed).
- 1 makes a **reverse dribble** (direction change).
- Passes to 2, who has come off a **down screen by 4**.
- Requires 4 to set the down screen on the weak side while 5 is screening on ball — two screens simultaneously. [S7, p.116]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Wing not reacting to the dribble-clear signal** → When 1 dribbles toward you, move immediately — hesitation kills the timing.
2. **Guards not cutting on the high-post entry** → On a pass to 5, 1 and 2 must cut immediately to low-post spots while 3 and 4 fill guard spots.
3. **Midscreen called too late** → 5's screen on 1 must happen before 1 is out of dribbles — set the screen early.
4. **2 not reading 5's screen on the midscreen entry** → 2 must cut the moment he sees 5 screening 1, not after the pass is made.
5. **Over-dribbling on dribble entries** → Dribble entries are designed to create ONE specific pass; excessive dribbling kills offensive spacing.

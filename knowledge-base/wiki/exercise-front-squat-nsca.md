# Front Squat (NSCA Basketball)

## Objective
Build quadriceps-dominant bilateral lower-body strength with an upright torso — improving jumping, deceleration, and the vertical power required for layups, dunks, and post play.

## Setup
- Power rack with barbell at **shoulder height**
- Two spotters for heavy loads
- Optional: one or two kettlebells (goblet squat variation — no spotter needed)
- Primary muscles: gluteus maximus, semimembranosus, semitendinosus, biceps femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris [S11, Ch.6]

## Execution

### Beginning Position — Bar Rack Options
**Option 1: Crossed-Arm Position**
- Cross the arms in front, step under the bar to get in position
- Place hands on top of the barbell at the opposite shoulders
- Drive elbows up so the upper arms are **parallel to the floor**

**Option 2: Parallel-Arm Position**
- Grasp bar with closed pronated grip just outside the shoulders
- Step under the bar with arms parallel to each other
- Drive elbows up so upper arms are **parallel to the floor**

- Lift bar off rack by extending hips and knees
- Take a few steps back; feet **shoulder-width apart**, toes slightly pointed out

### Movement (Athlete)
1. Flex hips and knees in unison so the **torso-to-floor angle remains constant**
2. Maintain **neutral spine, elbows up, chest up**
3. Continue descending until **top of thighs is approximately parallel to the floor**
4. Reverse: press through feet, extend hips and knees at the same rate to maintain torso angle
5. Return to beginning position

### Two-Spotter Protocol
- Stand at bar ends with underhand grip
- Help lift bar from rack, stand ready with hands under bar ends
- Mirror the athlete's squat; do not touch unless needed
- After the set: help return bar to rack

**Breathing:** Inhale on the way down; exhale after the sticking point on the way up [S11, Ch.6]

## Coaching Points
- "Pick a point on the wall before descending — keep eyes on it throughout"
- "Keep the chest up — elbows must stay high to prevent the bar from rolling forward"
- "Keep the weight over the heels and midfoot"
- The front squat demands more ankle and thoracic mobility than the back squat — address restrictions before loading

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Goblet Squat)**: Hold one or two kettlebells at the front of the chest — no spotter needed; teaches upright torso and depth without bar balance demands
2. **Intermediate**: Standard front squat with crossed-arm or parallel-arm grip to parallel depth
3. **Advanced**: Increase load; add pause at the bottom; progress to clean-grip parallel-arm position for Olympic lift carryover

# Detroit Pistons Step-Up Flare Action

## Overview
Former Detroit Pistons coach Alvin Gentry's step-up pick-and-roll with secondary flare action. The step-up PnR brings the ball to the sideline side of the screen rather than the middle, making it difficult to trap. The play features Grant Hill (3) as the ball handler driving off Jerry Stackhouse (2), with secondary back-screen and flare options when the drive is stopped. [S14, pp.27-29]

## Formation
- **3** (point forward, Grant Hill) handles the ball on the left sideline near free-throw line extended
- **2** (Stackhouse) sets the step-up screen — positioned at free-throw line extended
- **4** aligned inside the left elbow
- **5** lifted on the weak side
- **1** in the right corner

## Phases

### Phase 1: Step-Up Pick-and-Roll
- **3** dribbles off **2**'s step-up screen set from baseline up toward the ball
- The screen brings the ball to the sideline side (not the middle)
- **Option 1**: If **3** can turn the corner and attack the basket off the screen — take it. This is the primary action and sets up the secondary phase when used.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.1 — 3 dribbling left sideline toward FT line extended, 2 setting upward screen. 4 inside left elbow, 5 on weak-side elbow, 1 in right corner. -->

### Phase 2: Back Screen Flare (When Drive Is Stopped)
- If **3** cannot take the ball to the basket:
  - **4** steps over and sets a **back screen** for **2**
  - **2** flares off **4** toward the top of the key looking for a **skip pass from 3**
  - **4** slides to the basket after setting the screen (possible post-feed)
  - **5** sets a **down screen for 1** simultaneously with **2**'s catch
- **2** catches at the top: looks for shot, or passes to **1** cutting off **5**'s down screen, or passes to **4** in the post

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.2 — 3 has ball left wing. 4 back-screens 2, who flares top. 4 slides to basket. 5 down-screens for 1 in right corner. Skip pass arc from 3 to 2. -->

### Phase 3: Tertiary Options (From Stackhouse Catch)
- **2** can flare for jump shot
- **2** can swing to **1** (Lindsey Hunter) who has cut off **4**'s (Christian Laettner's) down screen
- **1** can feed **4** (Laettner) in the post if jump shot is unavailable

## Key Coaching Points
- The step-up screen (baseline-to-ball direction) creates a sideline-side angle that is harder to trap than a standard side PnR
- **3** must be able to turn the corner — a legitimate drive threat creates the secondary action
- **4**'s back screen and basket cut are simultaneous — he screens, then immediately slips, making him a two-read threat
- **5**'s down screen timing must coincide with **2**'s catch at the top
- Using a point forward (Hill) who can drive creates help-defense decisions that open the back-screen flare

## Counters
- If the defense overhelps on **3**'s drive, **4** will be open on the slip or **2** will be free on the flare
- If **4**'s defender steps out to help on the flare, **4** slips to the rim
- If **1**'s defender helps down on the PnR, **1** will be open off **5**'s down screen

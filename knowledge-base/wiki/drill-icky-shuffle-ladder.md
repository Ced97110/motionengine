# Icky Shuffle Agility Ladder Drill

## Objective
Enhance lower-body coordination and quickness through a lateral 2-in-1-out footwork pattern on an agility ladder.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder laid flat on the ground
- Player starts on the left side of the ladder

## Execution
1. Start on the left side of the ladder at square 1.
2. Step laterally with the **right foot** inside the first square, then **left foot** inside the first square.
3. Step laterally with the **right foot** to the right side (outside) of the ladder.
4. Advance the **left foot** to the next square inside the ladder.
5. Bring the **right foot** up to the same square (inside).
6. Step laterally with the **left foot** to the left side (outside) of the ladder.
7. Advance the **right foot** to the next square.
8. Repeat this alternating in-in-out pattern the full length of the ladder.

**Key**: The pattern is: right-in, left-in, right-out, advance left-in, right-in, left-out, advance right-in...

## Coaching Points
- Look **up** during the drill — do not look at your feet [S8, p.108]
- Perform all drills both **forward and backward** to train symmetric footwork
- Stay on the balls of the feet throughout — no heel striking
- Quick, light contacts — "hot coals" on every step
- Aim to minimize ground contact time between steps

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the pattern slowly to learn the sequence.
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed; increase step cadence.
3. **Advanced**: Perform backward (reverse direction on the ladder); add arm swings to simulate running form.

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — quick lateral foot contacts develop on-court change-of-direction speed
- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — precise foot placement on the ladder rungs trains proprioception

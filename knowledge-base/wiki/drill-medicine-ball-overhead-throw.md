# Medicine-Ball Overhead Throw

## Objective
Improve explosive power in throwing and overhead activities — directly transfers to rebounding, shot-blocking, and overhead passing in basketball. [S8, p.160]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Solid wall
- Equipment: 1 medicine ball

## Execution
1. Stand in a parallel stance facing the wall with feet flat.
2. Load the ball over and behind the head with both hands, extending the entire body.
3. Throw the ball at the wall with both hands and catch it with both hands on the rebound.
4. Keep feet flat and parallel stance during the loading ("cocking") phase.
5. Repeat for prescribed reps.

## Coaching Points
- Full-body extension during loading is key — engage ankles, knees, hips, trunk, and shoulders.
- "Lock the stance" — feet stay flat during loading to prevent energy leakage.
- The catch is a plyometric loading phase — do not slow down the transition from catch to next throw.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Parallel stance, moderate throw speed, focus on full extension.
2. **Intermediate**: Step forward (alternating legs) while throwing — adds lower-body contribution.
3. **Advanced**: Throw from a kneeling position — isolates upper body and core.

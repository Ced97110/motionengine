# Shooting Error Correction (Wissel)

## Summary
Hal Wissel provides a systematic framework for diagnosing and correcting shooting errors — both in real-time (through rim-reaction reading and internal feel) and retrospectively (through video analysis). The core principle: the ball goes where the shooting arm, hand, and finger direct it. Every error has a mechanical root cause that can be identified and corrected with a specific trigger word or technique adjustment. This self-coaching ability is critical because most practice occurs without a coach present. [S7, pp.9-16]

## When to Use
- During individual shooting workouts when no coach is present
- When a player enters a shooting slump
- During film sessions reviewing poor shooting nights
- As a coach's diagnostic tool during skill development sessions

## Key Principles

### Reading the Rim to Diagnose Errors
1. **Ball misses right** (right-handed shot) → Elbow out, or body not square to basket, or ball set in front of head. Fix: square up, elbow in, set ball between ear and shoulder.
2. **Ball misses left** (right-handed shot) → Ball set on right hip or too far right; not enough leg power; shoving ball right-to-left. Fix: square up, use legs, set ball between ear and shoulder.
3. **Ball circles out off the rim** → Sidespin. Cause: shooting hand starting on side of ball then rotating behind it; ball sliding off ring finger; balance hand thumbing ball. Fix: block-and-tuck starting position; shoot with one hand (shooting hand only controls release). [S7, p.10]
4. **Ball consistently short** → Not enough leg power and/or incomplete follow-through. Fix: use leg motion, keep arm up until ball reaches basket, smooth even rhythm.
5. **Ball consistently long** → Too flat an arc. Fix: raise shooting arm higher for a 45-60° arc; head and shoulders forward and upward on follow-through.
6. **Ball inconsistently short and long** → Leaning shoulders back on release. Fix: extend arm with complete elbow extension on follow-through rather than leaning back.
7. **Shots hit hard on the rim despite good mechanics** → Ball resting on palm. Fix: relax thumb, set ball on finger pads with palm off the ball for backspin and soft touch. [S7, p.15]

### Diagnosing With Feel
- Feel the shooting hand rotate off-center → indicates sidespin source
- Feel ball come off ring finger instead of index finger → sidespin
- Practice shooting with **eyes closed**; partner rebounds and reports miss direction and rim reaction

### Mentally Correcting a Miss
*"Act like a shooter, feel like a shooter, be a shooter."*
1. Identify the cause (rim reaction or feel)
2. Immediately correct using a positive **trigger word** (e.g., "Legs!" if the miss was short from not using legs)
3. Visualize a successful shot
4. Say an **anchor word** ("Through!" "In!" "Yes!" "Net!") to reinforce the visualization
5. Never recite a negative word, show negative body language, or dwell on the miss — the brain conditions itself to repeat what it rehearses [S7, pp.10-11]

> **Computer analogy:** "When people type on a keyboard and make a mistake, do they type the same mistake again? Of course not. They erase the mistake and correct it." — Hal Wissel, p.10

## Complete Error → Correction Reference

| Error | Cause | Correction |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Short | Not enough leg power | More leg drive; complete follow-through arm up; even rhythm |
| Long | Too flat a trajectory | Higher arc (45-60°); head/shoulders forward on follow-through |
| Inconsistent short/long | Leaning shoulders back | Complete elbow extension; don't lean back |
| Hits right rim (RH shot) | Elbow out; not square | Square up; set ball between ear/shoulder; elbow in |
| Hits left rim (RH shot) | Ball too far right; shoving; not enough legs | Square up; use legs; set ball between ear/shoulder; elbow in |
| Circles out (sidespin) | Hand starts on side; thumbing with off-hand | Block-and-tuck; release off index finger; one-hand emphasis |
| Hard rim contact | Ball on palm | Relax thumb; ball on finger pads |
| Lacking range/control | Improper ball position | Set between ear/shoulder; down-and-up legs; arm up on follow-through |
| Eyes moving during shot | Watching ball flight | Lock eyes on front rim until ball reaches basket |
| Slow release (catch) | Lowering ball before shooting | Catch in position; keep ball high; shoot in one motion |
| Slow release (side pass) | Facing passer, reaching | Face basket; turn head to see pass; jump behind ball |
| Sidespin on catch | Hands on sides, rotating ball | Block-and-tuck on catch; never rotate ball from sides |
| Floating off the dribble | Not picking up ball at knee | Pick up at shooting knee, knees flexed, redirect momentum upward |
| Sidespin off the dribble | Picking up with hands on sides | Pick up with shooting hand on top, balance hand under |
| Reaching for dribble | Starting shot from side of body | Dribble to front of shooting knee; don't reach sideways |

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Develop internal awareness of your shot. Know what your common errors feel like and what triggers them. React to every miss with a positive mental correction, not negativity.

## Coaching Tips (Wissel's Direct Recommendations)
- Get to know each player individually before coaching their shot
- **Listen more than talk** — players won't improve while you're talking
- Coach each player on only what *they* need to improve — not everything you know
- Ask: "When you're shooting well, what are you doing?" — reveals confidence level and self-awareness
- Keep confidence levels high; constantly say "You are a shooter!"
- Say: "I'm here to coach and help you, but you're going to learn to coach yourself." [S7, p.16]

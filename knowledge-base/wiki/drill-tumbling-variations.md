# Tumbling Drill Variations

## Objective
Develop total-body agility and kinesthetic awareness by applying progressively more demanding conditions to all foundational tumbling drills. [S8, p.127]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Soft grass or gymnastics mat
- Optional: a low barrier (hurdle, foam roller, or box) for the running-start variation

## Execution
### Running Start and Tumbling Over Barrier
1. Perform any of the previous tumbling drills (forward roll, backward roll, cartwheel, round-off) with a **running start**.
2. Tumble over a low barrier — this increases the amplitude and difficulty of the roll/cartwheel.
3. The running momentum and increased height demand faster body awareness and greater skill. [S8, p.127]

### Tumbling Sequencing
1. **String two or more tumbling drills together** in a continuous sequence without pausing between movements.
2. Example sequences:
   - Forward roll → backward roll (see [[drill-forward-backward-roll-combo]])
   - Cartwheel → forward roll
   - Cartwheel → round-off
3. The sequencing increases kinesthetic demands by requiring additional coordination between movements. [S8, p.127]

## Coaching Points
- All complex variation rules apply: add a sprint before/after, react to a visual cue, or add a sport-specific skill
- Increase amplitude gradually — do not go straight to maximum barrier height
- In sequencing, maintain a rhythm — the transition between tumbling movements is where most athletes falter
- "Never stop moving" — continuous locomotion is the training stimulus

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master each individual tumbling drill before combining
2. **Intermediate**: Running start versions of each individual drill
3. **Advanced**: Full tumbling sequences over barriers with sport-specific skill appended

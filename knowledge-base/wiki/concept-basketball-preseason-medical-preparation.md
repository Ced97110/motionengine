# Basketball Preseason Medical Preparation

## Summary
High-quality medical care begins with thorough preseason preparation. A systematic preseason screening process identifies existing conditions, documents injury history, establishes baselines, and informs the S&C plan for the entire season. There is no universally accepted gold standard protocol, but elite programs combine cardiac evaluation, orthopedic screening, muscle strength testing, and comprehensive blood work to develop individualized reconditioning plans [S10, pp.126-128].

At the NBA level, the pre-participation evaluation (PPE) is formalized into five components: medical evaluation, orthopedic evaluation, concussion screening, cardiac screening, and clinical lab tests [S10, p.146].

## When to Use
- At the start of every preseason, before any player is cleared for contact practice
- When a player joins a team mid-season after being with another organization
- When a player has a significant injury history that may require imaging or specialist clearance

## Key Principles
1. **Medical questionnaire first**: All medical history, injuries, surgeries, prior MRIs, and specific issues collected from players before physical examination begins
2. **Stress test minimum**: Every player must pass a Bruce Protocol stress test before being allowed to compete — but for elite programs, this is the floor, not the ceiling [S10, p.126]
3. **Enhanced cardiac evaluation**: Cardiac echo (ideally stress echo), CBC, full biochemistry, vitamin levels, EBV, CMV, HIV tests, and urine analysis are recommended for elite programs [S10, pp.126-127]
4. **Dual orthopedic screening**: Performed by both the head physiotherapist AND the orthopedic surgeon, checking all joints, stability, flexibility, and muscle strength — including previously injured areas
5. **Biodex muscle strength testing**: Recommended to plan preseason workup and seasonal strengthening/stretching program; detects muscle imbalances before they cause injury [S10, p.127]
6. **Imaging as needed**: MRI for previously operated knees or prior lumbar disc herniation — decided case-by-case after the screening
7. **Medical bag preparation**: The sideline medical bag must be fully stocked and the physician must be familiar with all equipment [S10, pp.127-128]

## NBA Pre-Participation Evaluation (PPE) Components

### Medical Evaluation
- History (family history of sudden death, cardiac disease, musculoskeletal injuries, neurologic/concussion history)
- Physical examination

### Orthopedic Evaluation
- Spine, upper and lower extremities assessed for orthopedic pathology
- Additional evaluation of any known musculoskeletal injury history

### Concussion Screening
- NBA Concussion Assessment Tool (NBA-CAT)
- CogState Computerized Cognitive Assessment Tool (baseline)

### Cardiac Screening (performed by cardiologist)
- Standard vital signs
- Auscultation of heart sounds
- Resting electrocardiogram (ECG)
- Treadmill stress transthoracic echocardiogram

### Clinical Lab Tests (annual)
- Lipid panel
- Comprehensive metabolic panel
- Hemoglobin A1c
- Thyroid stimulating hormone (TSH)
- Ferritin
- 25-OH Vitamin D
- Complete blood count (CBC)
- Sickle cell status (one-time, on entry to the league)
- QuantiFERON-TB gold testing (tuberculosis screening)

### Recommended Vaccines
- Varicella, measles, mumps, rubella, tetanus/pertussis, hepatitis B, influenza
- Antibody titers checked to confirm immunity [S10, p.146]

## Medical Bag Contents
The sideline medical bag for elite basketball should include:

**CPR/Cervical Spine**: AED, rigid cervical collar, bolt cutters

**Airway/Ventilation**: Oral/nasal airways, pocket mask, cricothyroidotomy kit, bag-valve mask, intubation equipment, portable suction, oxygen tank

**Circulation**: IV catheters, IV tubing, normal saline/Ringer's lactate, tourniquet rubber tubing

**Miscellaneous**: Exam gloves, stethoscope, blood pressure cuff, 4×4 gauze, sterile dressings, thermometer, reflex hammer, athletic tape, alcohol swabs, fluorescein eye drops, antiseptic, tooth transport medium, suture kit, crutches/wheelchair, sharps box, spineboard/stretcher

**Medications**: Epinephrine (1:1000 prefilled), antibiotics, anti-inflammatory medication, dextrose (50%), local anesthetic, nitroglycerin, oral glucose, aspirin (chewable), beta agonist inhaler, topical medications, list of banned substances [S10, pp.127-128]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Bruce Protocol only** → This is the minimum standard; elite programs should add cardiac echo, blood work, and orthopedic Biodex testing
2. **Single-examiner orthopedic screening** → Have both the head PT and orthopedic surgeon independently screen each player
3. **Not individualizing the plan** → Each player's screening must produce a personalized S&C plan addressing their specific imbalances
4. **Unprepared medical bag** → Physician must train with all equipment — the sideline is not the time to learn

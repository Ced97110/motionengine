# LA Offense

## Summary
The LA Offense ("all-purpose offense") is a continuity half-court system built around a simple governing principle: **each of three possible first passes triggers a completely different set of offensive actions**. The guard entering the frontcourt can pass to the forward on his side (guard-to-forward), to the opposite guard (guard-to-guard), or to the high-post center (guard-to-center). Every pass demands a different spontaneous and coordinated response from all five players.

Developed over a 50-year coaching career by synthesizing ideas from Adolph Rupp (guard play, fast-break principles), John Wooden (pass-dictates-action continuity, UCLA low-post entry), and Dean Smith (floor balance, finishing, inside play), the LA Offense is designed to get a disciplined good shot quickly — not a 30-second dribble-and-pass pattern. All five players have scoring opportunities on every possession [S13, pp.127-131].

## When to Use
- Primary half-court offense against both man-to-man and zone defenses (no separate zone attack needed)
- When facing pressure defense: the guard-to-center pass gives a natural pressure release
- Against switching defenses: the multiple screen options create confusion
- When the team needs tempo control: the continuity pattern regulates pace
- When the coach wants all five players involved in scoring opportunities

## Key Principles
1. **Each pass dictates a different play** — guard-to-forward, guard-to-guard, and guard-to-center all trigger distinct actions; players must read which pass was made and react immediately.
2. **Floor balance and spacing** — always maintain three players on one side, two on the other; clear to the three-point line when cutting or screening; never dribble into a congested "dead baseline."
3. **Take the first good shot available** — disciplined shot selection means shooting when the shot comes out of the pattern, not after 30 seconds of dribbling.
4. **Always finish at the basket** — when offensive rebounds occur, finish strong; don't settle for missed putbacks.
5. **Take the ball inside** — low-block opportunities produce three benefits: score, foul earned, and opponent foul trouble.
6. **Designate one safety** — one player always rotates back to protect the basket; another contains the ball on transition.
7. **Constant ball and player movement** — stationary players lose their defenders; moving players create loose-ball and hustle opportunities [S13, pp.128-131].

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (01)**: Initiates play by making one of three passes; reads defensive pressure to select entry; after guard-to-forward pass, makes a hard jab-step inside cut then clears to strong-side block.
- **SG (02)**: Mirror of the PG; in guard-to-guard entry, exchanges position with the wing; after plays develop, often receives ball reversal for pick-and-roll or catch-and-shoot.
- **SF (03)**: Wing player who receives the guard-to-forward entry pass and establishes right-foot pivot; sets baseline screens and relocates to elbows as the play develops.
- **PF (04)**: Sets multiple screens (baseline, down, staggered); relocates to weak-side low post or pins down; is a post-up and rebound option.
- **C (05)**: Plays the high post facing the basket; pops out to receive guard-to-center pass as a pressure release; passes to backdoor cutters and guards-around; sets sideline pick-and-roll; relocates to weak-side low post.

## Variations

### Guard-to-Forward Pass Pattern
The PG (01) passes to the strong-side forward (04) and makes a hard jab-step inside cut. If open, 01 gets a return pass; if not, 01 clears to the low block. Simultaneously, 05 screens for 03, who relocates to the strong-side elbow. 02 V-cuts to the top of the key and receives from 04. From here, three continuity options emerge:
- **Option 1**: 02 gets the ball at the top; 01 turns out off 05's baseline screen; 04 pins down for 03; 02 reverses to 01 who can shoot, post-pass, or call PnR.
- **Option 2**: 02 reverses dribble toward 03 while 05 steps into the middle for a post-up — effective counter called at a timeout.
- **Option 3**: 02 passes to 01, then reverses and sets a staggered double screen with 04 for 03's catch-and-shoot [S13, pp.133-134].

### Guard-to-Guard Pass Pattern
01 passes to 02 and sets an inside screen for 03. 03 exchanges with 01 and receives directly from 02. 03 passes to 01; using a jab step, 03 dives to the strong-side block. 02 dives to the weak-side block. 05 watches both post areas clear, then pops out to receive 01's pass. As 01 passes to 05, 03 steps up and sets a back pick on X1 for 01's inside cut. 04 pins down on 02 weak side. Multiple catch-and-shoot, curl-to-basket, and PnR options emerge [S13, pp.135-136].

### Guard-to-Center Pass Pattern
01 passes to high-post 05 and sets a screen for 02. Strong-side 03 makes an automatic backdoor cut (first option — extremely effective against pressure). 02 uses 01's screen for a jump shot or drive (second option). If both are covered, 03 uses 04 and 01's double-stack screen for a catch-and-shoot (third option). **Releases**: dribble handoff to 03, 04 dives middle off 01, or 05 passes to 02 at the extended free-throw line for a PnR [S13, pp.136-137].

## Strengths and Weaknesses

### Strengths
1. Great floor balance
2. Built-in scoring opportunities for all five players
3. Constant ball and player movement
4. Excellent inside-out possibilities
5. Great counter to switching defenses
6. Controls game tempo within the offensive set

### Weaknesses
1. Requires unselfish players who share the ball and make good decisions
2. Requires disciplined passing skills and decision-making capability [S13, p.138]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Dead baseline** — ball handler attacks the basket while a teammate occupies the low block on the same side → correction: always check the block before driving; if it's occupied, attack the other side or dribble to improve the passing angle.
2. **Forcing the guard-to-center pass into traffic** → correction: 05 must step out to meet the pass and be available as a legitimate outlet.
3. **Not making five passes before shooting** (drill standard) → correction: practice the five-pass rule before a shot is allowed, except on layups.
4. **Guards standing still** → correction: every player must read each pass and immediately execute the prescribed cut or screen.

# Help the Helper

## Objective
Train defenders to execute the dual mandates of the weak-side helper — proper flat-triangle/ball-you-man positioning AND active help-and-recover or rotation — while simultaneously managing on-ball denial and pressure.

## Setup
- 3 offensive players (1 at point, 2 at wing, 3 at opposite wing) and 3 defenders (X1, X2, X3)
- Half court
- 1 basketball
- Player 1 begins by bringing the ball into the frontcourt under pressure from X1

<!-- DIAGRAM: Player 1 at top of key, X1 guarding 1. Player 2 on strong-side wing, X2 denying. Player 3 on weak side, X3 in flat triangle help position. p.278 -->

## Execution

### Phase 1 — Positioning
1. Players 1, 2, and 3 pass the ball to each other; each player holds the ball for 2 seconds before passing.
2. X1, X2, and X3 constantly change positions to maintain proper defensive stances: denial, ball-you-man, and flat triangle.
3. Teaching point: all defenders jump to the ball and end two-thirds the distance from the ball, one-third from their assignment.

### Phase 2 — Denial and Fence Slide
1. X2 and X3 use denial stances and fence slides to prevent players 2 and 3 from receiving the pass.
2. X1 helps with denial by pressuring player 1 after 1 picks up the dribble.

### Phase 3 — Drive and Help Activation
1. Whenever player 2 or 3 receives a pass, that player must drive to the middle of the court.
2. The nearest help defender (X1 if 2 has ball) must **close the gap** — this is the first line of defense.
3. If the penetrator splits X1 and X2, X3 **rotates** to stop the ball; if penetration continues, X3 draws the charge. X1 then drops to cover player 3 cutting baseline.
4. When player 2 passes back to player 1, X3 must **help the helper**: hedge toward player 1, then retreat (help-and-recover) back to player 3.

### Rotation
- Rotate 1→2→3→1 (offense) and X1→X2→X3→X1 (defense) so all players defend both the point and wing positions.

## Coaching Points
- "Jump to the ball — two-thirds to the ball, one-third to your man"
- Defenders must be in flat triangle and interception stance — these are the heart of man-to-man help defense
- X2 and X3: use fence-sliding techniques on denial; never lose sight of the ball
- Third phase: X1 must close the gap with proper weight transfer (close-the-gap stance); X3 hedges and recovers precisely
- Anytime player 2 has the ball, X3 is in help-side defense; anytime player 3 has the ball, X2 is in help-side defense
- This is a hustle drill — defenders must constantly adjust

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Phase 1 only — stationary ball movement, defenders adjust positions
2. **Intermediate**: Phases 1 and 2 — add fence slides and denial stances
3. **Advanced**: Full three-phase drill; allow player 2 to drive the baseline (activates full rotation drill); allow shooting (triggers offensive and defensive rebounding)

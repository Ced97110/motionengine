# Barbell Jump Squat

## Objective
Develop explosive lower-body power and rate of force development (RFD) through a loaded countermovement jump, directly transferring to vertical leap, first-step quickness, and acceleration on the court.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Olympic platform or adequate overhead clearance
- Barbell positioned across the upper back (high bar back squat position)
- **Do NOT use a spotter** for safety reasons (athlete must be able to drop the bar if needed)
- Light to moderate load — if the load is too heavy, movement speed slows and RFD drops

## Execution
1. Begin in a standing high-bar back squat position (a).
2. Using a **countermovement**, partially squat to approximately **quarter-squat depth** (b).
3. Immediately reverse direction and **explode off the floor** — extend hips, knees, and ankles fully (triple extension) (c).
4. Maintain good posture and an upright torso throughout the jump.
5. Upon landing, **absorb impact** through the legs by slightly flexing the hips and knees — do not land stiff-legged.
6. Reset and repeat for prescribed repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- **Load selection is critical** — too heavy = slow speed = reduced RFD; too light = insufficient stimulus
- "Explode off the floor" — the intent to move fast is as important as actual speed
- **Do not let the knees cave inward** (valgus) on the way up or on landing
- Exhale during the upward drive out of the squat into the air; inhale right before the descent
- Perform on an Olympic platform for safety — adequate space is essential
- No spotter: athlete can drop the bar in a controlled manner if needed

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight or very light bar jump squat to learn mechanics; focus on triple extension
2. **Intermediate**: Add load (20-30% of 1RM back squat); aim for maximal height
3. **Advanced**: Optimal load (typically 30-45% of 1RM) for peak power output; track bar velocity

## Common Mistakes
1. **Too much load** → speed too slow, RFD reduced → reduce weight, prioritize bar speed
2. **Knee valgus on ascent or landing** → cue "knees out" and add band squats and clam exercises for hip abductor activation
3. **Landing stiff-legged** → cue "soft landing, absorb through the legs" → may indicate patellar tendon issues if repeated
4. **Quarter-squat too deep** → partial squat only — do not go below quarter depth

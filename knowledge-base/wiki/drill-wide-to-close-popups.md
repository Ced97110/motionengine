# Wide-to-Close Pop-Ups (Small Space Drill)

## Objective
Develop upper-body explosive power by training the arms and chest to launch and reposition rapidly during an airborne push-up — establishing the fast-arm/fast-hand speed required to complement lower-body power training.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment
- Any flat, clean floor surface (mat preferred for beginners)
- Small space only — can be done in a 3-foot-wide area

## Execution
1. Start in a close-grip push-up position (hands closer than shoulder-width).
2. Dip down slightly and explosively push up so that both hands leave the floor.
3. While airborne, quickly spread both hands outward so you "catch" yourself landing in a wide push-up position.
4. Without pausing on landing, dip down immediately and repeat for reps — this time the hands will come together (wide-to-close) on the next rep.
5. Continue alternating close→wide→close→wide rhythmically.

## Coaching Points
- "Fast legs must be paired with fast arms to unlock total-body power" [S9, p.75]
- Maintain aligned body throughout — hips must not shoot up (no worming)
- The transition happens in the air — both hands must leave the floor on every rep
- Start slow if needed; as arm speed develops, increase rhythm and reduce airtime
- Can be used as a warm-up, finisher, or ancillary drill alongside any jump chain session
- Pairs naturally with the jump chain to create a balanced total-body explosive training session

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform the wide-to-close spread slowly, focusing on alignment; allow feet to touch between reps
2. **Intermediate**: Standard rhythmic wide-to-close at moderate speed
3. **Advanced**: Maximize speed and minimize ground contact time on each transition

# Heel and Toe Walks

## Objective
Develop anterior tibialis strength (heel walk) for proper foot dorsiflexion and shin splint prevention; develop calf strength (toe walk) for push-off power and shin splint prevention.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open flat surface — court, hallway, field
- Distance: 15–30 meters per variation

## Execution
### Heel Walk
1. Walk forward balanced entirely on the **heels**, keeping the toes lifted completely off the ground.
2. Maintain upright posture — do not lean forward.
3. Cover 15–30 meters.

### Toe Walk
1. Walk forward balanced entirely on the **toes**, keeping the heels completely off the ground.
2. Maintain upright posture and take small deliberate steps.
3. Cover 15–30 meters.

## Coaching Points
- Both variations should be done slowly and deliberately — this is a strengthening, not a conditioning, drill.
- Heel walks are often neglected but are critical for basketballers — the anterior tibialis contributes to the dorsiflexed foot position needed during sprint mechanics (high knees, bounding).
- Toe walks are essentially slow-motion calf raises in motion — "every step is a calf raise."
- This drill pair works well as part of a pre-practice warm-up or post-practice recovery.
- Shin splint prevention: by strengthening both sides of the lower leg, athletes reduce the muscular imbalance that causes shin splints during repeated jumping and sprinting.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 15m heel walk + 15m toe walk, once through
2. **Intermediate**: 30m heel walk + 30m toe walk, 2–3 rounds
3. **Advanced**: Weighted vest version or on a slight incline for increased resistance

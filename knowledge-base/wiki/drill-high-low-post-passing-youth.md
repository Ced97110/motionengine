# High-Low Post Passing (Drill 102)

## Objective
Teach high-post and low-post players to read defensive coverage and make the correct pass (bounce, lob, or step-and-bounce) to create a high-percentage scoring opportunity.

## Setup
- Team — intermediate to advanced
- Half-court, paint and elbows
- **4 roles**: Player 4 (high post at free-throw line), X4 (defends 4), Player 5 (low post at big block), X5 (defends 5), plus a coach as initial passer
- **Rotation**: 4 → X4 → 5 → X5 → end of line
- Run without defense first; make it live after several reps

## Execution
1. **Player 4 flash pivots** to the free-throw line area. **X4 defends** this move.
   - If X4 **denies**: Player 4 cuts **backdoor** for a lob pass from the coach.
   - If Player 4 is **successful with the flash pivot cut**: coach passes Player 4 the ball — high-low drill is activated.
2. When Player 4 receives the pass at the **high post**, Player 4 executes a **front pivot into triple-threat position** (facing basket).
3. **Player 5 ducks into the lane** and tries to post X5.
4. **Players 4 and 5 read X4 and X5's coverage** to determine the next pass:
   - **X5 is behind Player 5**: Player 4 **bounce passes** to Player 5 → Player 5 uses post moves or dribble moves.
   - **X5 fronts Player 5**: Player 4 **tosses the overhead lob pass** to Player 5 → layup.
   - **X5 is sealed properly** (Player 5 has position): Player 4 **steps right** and **bounce passes** to Player 5.

## Progressions / Advanced Options
- Add a **perimeter player in the corner (or wing)** on the same side as Player 5.
- When Player 5 seals X5, Player 4 could pass to the **corner player** — who has the perfect entry angle to Player 5 at the low post.

## Coaching Points
- High-post player must **read the defense BEFORE passing** — don't force a predetermined pass.
- Front pivot to triple threat is essential — it creates a true passing threat to all options.
- Lob pass must be a **semi-lob** just out of reach of a jumping defender (see Drill 98).
- The bounce pass to a sealed post player must be **on the open side** of the defender.
- "Step and bounce" keeps the passer from telegraphing the pass to the sealed side.

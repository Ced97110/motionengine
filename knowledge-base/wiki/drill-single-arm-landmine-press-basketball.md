# Single-Arm Landmine Press (Basketball Strength Exercise)

## Objective
Develop unilateral overhead shoulder strength (anterior and lateral deltoid, supraspinatus) with trunk stability that directly mimics the overhead arm extension position of a layup or dunk.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Landmine apparatus anchoring one end of a barbell (or barbell end placed securely in the corner of a wall)
- Weight plate loaded on the free end of the barbell
- Starting position: defensive stance (hips and knees flexed)

## Execution
1. Place one end of the barbell in the landmine apparatus or secure it in the bottom corner of a wall.
2. Load an appropriate weight plate on the free end of the barbell.
3. Stand in a defensive stance (hips and knees flexed). Grab the weighted end of the bar with the left hand. Lift the bar and rotate the elbow forward so the bar rests in the palm at shoulder height, elbow close to the body.
4. Maintaining the solid defensive stance, extend the arm upward and forward until fully extended — pressing the bar up in an arcing path.
5. Slowly lower the barbell back to shoulder-height starting position under control.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions, then repeat with the right arm.

## Coaching Points
- Start in a true defensive stance — hips and knees bent; this activates the lower body stabilizers during the press
- "Elbow close to the body" before extending — prevents shoulder impingement
- The arcing path of the landmine bar naturally mimics reaching overhead to finish a layup or slam a dunk
- Control the descent — the weight wants to pull the arm down fast; resist it
- Single-arm action forces trunk stability; "brace the core throughout"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reduce weight; focus on smooth arc and controlled return
2. **Intermediate**: Standard defensive-stance single-arm press with moderate load
3. **Advanced**:
   - **Double-Arm Landmine Press**: Both hands on the bar simultaneously — increases total load
   - **Staggered-Stance Landmine Press**: One foot forward for added core stability challenge

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid, supraspinatus
- **Secondary**: Pectoralis major, posterior deltoid, trapezius, triceps brachii

## Basketball Transfer
The landmine press mimics the overhead arm position used in a layup or dunk — the arc of the bar closely replicates the path the arm takes when extending overhead to finish at the rim. Single-arm action also develops shoulder and trunk stability critical for fighting through contact. Stronger shoulders support rebounding battles. [S2, pp.84-85]

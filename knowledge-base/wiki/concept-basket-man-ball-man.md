# Basket Man / Ball Man — Fast Break Defensive Tandem

## Summary
When two defenders must stop a 3-on-2 fast break, their first priority is immediate verbal communication to assign roles. The first defender back becomes the **Basket Man** (protecting the rim); the second becomes the **Ball Man** (stopping penetration). This tandem must buy time until the remaining three defenders sprint back. Clear role assignment prevents both players from chasing the ball and leaving the basket unprotected.

This concept also applies whenever any two defenders are back first after any transition trigger (missed shot, made shot, or turnover). In a **turnover** situation, no pre-assigned roles exist — whoever gets back first takes the basket, regardless of position.

## When to Use
- Any 3-on-2 or 2-on-1 fast break defensive scenario.
- After any change of possession (turnover, rebound, made shot) before additional defenders recover.
- In the Super Transition Drill and all transition drills as the foundational two-player structure.

## Key Principles
1. **Communication first:** Both defenders must call their role immediately and loudly — "Basket!" or "Ball!" — the moment they arrive.
2. **Basket Man position:** Sprint to the **middle of the free throw lane**. Protect both low blocks. Take responsibility for both the first AND second perimeter passes from the ball handler.
3. **Ball Man position:** Position in the lane up to the **top of the key**. Stop the dribbler's penetration — do not fully commit; force the pass.
4. **Automatic rotation on wing passes:** When ball is passed to either wing — Basket Man rotates OUT to defend the wing; Ball Man drops IN to protect the basket. No hesitation, no talking needed.
5. **Buy time:** The tandem's goal is to slow the offense and prevent uncontested shots until the three trailing defenders arrive.
6. **Turnover situation:** No predetermined roles — any of the five players can become basket man or ball man; protect the basket first.

## Player Responsibilities
- **First Defender Back (Basket Man):** Rim protection — middle of the lane, both blocks covered, absorb the first two perimeter passes.
- **Second Defender Back (Ball Man):** Penetration stopper — top of the key, force ball handler to pick up or pass, then drop to basket on any wing pass.
- **Tailback:** The designated offensive-to-defensive specialist (the "safety") who gets back first; usually becomes Ball Man or Basket Man depending on timing.
- **Halfback:** Divided responsibility — half offensive rebound, half defensive transition; likely becomes the third defender back.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Both defenders chasing the ball** → Correct: One always protects the basket while the other pressures the ball.
2. **No verbal communication** → Correct: Call your role the instant you arrive — confusion leads to uncontested layups.
3. **Ball Man fully committing to the dribbler** → Correct: Force the pass, don't lunge — stay between the dribbler and the rim.
4. **Basket Man failing to rotate on the wing pass** → Correct: When the pass goes to the wing, Basket Man immediately rotates out; Ball Man drops in — automatic.
5. **Defenders not building defense from the inside out** → Correct: Once tailback and halfback get to the defensive top of the key, set up in the lane first, then build outward.

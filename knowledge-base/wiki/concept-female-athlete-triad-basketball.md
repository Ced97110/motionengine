# Female Athlete Triad and RED-S in Basketball

## Summary
The Female Athlete Triad is a syndrome of three interrelated conditions: low energy availability (EA), menstrual dysfunction, and low bone mineral density. Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S) is a broader term introduced to emphasize that sustained low EA can affect many physiological systems beyond bone and menstrual function — and also applies to male athletes. Female basketball players are at risk due to the high training demands of the sport combined with the pressures of weight and body composition.

Early detection and intervention are critical. A multidisciplinary team including a physician, dietitian, and behavioral health expert is the optimal management approach [S10, Ch.66].

## When to Use
- Screening all female basketball players at pre-participation physicals
- When a player presents with stress fractures, missed menstrual periods, or unexplained fatigue
- When a player demonstrates disordered eating behaviors or excessive weight loss
- When bone mineral density is low on imaging

## Key Principles
1. **Energy availability is the root cause**: Low EA (caloric intake minus exercise energy expenditure relative to lean body mass) is the driver of both menstrual disruption and bone loss.
2. **RED-S broadens the concept**: RED-S emphasizes that low EA affects performance, immunity, cardiovascular health, and psychological well-being — not just the classic triad of bone/menses/EA.
3. **Multidisciplinary management is optimal**: Physician, dietitian, and behavioral health expert should be involved simultaneously [S10, Ch.66].
4. **Screening should be proactive**: Athletes should be screened to aid in early detection and intervention before the triad fully manifests.
5. **Both energy deficiency and estrogen deficiency exacerbate bone metabolism alterations**: The combination is worse than either alone [S10, Ch.66].

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Monitor for symptoms — irregular or absent periods, bone pain, fatigue, or restrictive eating patterns — and report them to medical staff promptly.

## Variations
### RED-S (Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport)
Broader than the classic Female Athlete Triad; applies to male athletes as well. Encompasses impaired physiological function caused by relative energy deficiency including metabolic rate, menstrual function, bone health, immunity, protein synthesis, and cardiovascular health [S10, Ch.66].

## Common Mistakes
1. **Treating only the symptoms** → Address the root cause: inadequate energy intake relative to training load.
2. **Ignoring male athletes** → RED-S affects males as well; screening should not be limited to females.
3. **Using a single-provider approach** → Optimal management requires a physician, dietitian, and behavioral health expert working together.
4. **Delaying return to play without a plan** → Return-to-play decisions should be individualized and guided by the multidisciplinary team.

# Mat Walk

## Objective
Develop dynamic balance during forward movement on a narrow surface to restore lower-extremity neuromuscular control during the early phase of knee rehabilitation.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Thin mat, approximately 5-10 yards (meters) long, placed on the floor lengthwise

## Execution
1. Stand erect on the lateral edge of the mat.
2. Walk the length of the mat with one foot placed directly in front of the other in tightrope fashion. One half of each foot (divided by the midline) is on the mat; the other half is off the mat.
3. Do not allow either foot to touch the floor throughout the walk.
4. Perform the prescribed number of mat walks back and forth [S2, pp.140-141].

## Coaching Points
- "Eyes forward, core engaged — this is controlled walking, not shuffling"
- Monitor the player for excessive trunk sway or hip drop, which indicates weakness
- "Maintaining balance during movement is essential for changing direction or for recovering in specific situations such as cutting quickly to avoid a defender" [S2, p.141]
- The drill is simple but reveals deficits quickly — players who struggle indicate a need for more static balance work first
- This is the first exercise in the jumper's knee rehabilitation sequence; it should be manageable but challenging

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Full mat walk, slow pace, arms out for balance
2. **Intermediate**: Arms crossed on chest, faster pace
3. **Advanced**: Carry a ball or dribble during the walk [S2, pp.140-141]

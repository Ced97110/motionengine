# Mirroring Two-Box Drill

## Objective
Improve the ability to change direction and react to a partner's movements by mirroring unpredictable sprints within adjacent cone boxes. [S8, p.190]

## Setup
- 2 players
- 2 boxes marked with 4 cones each; each cone 5 yards (4.6 meters) apart
- The two boxes are set side by side
- Each player starts at the center of their own box

## Execution
1. Both athletes begin standing in the center of their respective boxes.
2. Athlete 1 (commander) sprints to any cone in their box, touches it, and returns to center.
3. Athlete 2 (reactor) must mirror the same movement in their box — sprinting to the corresponding cone and returning to center.
4. Athlete 1 continues to choose different cones in random order.
5. Athlete 2 must react and mirror every movement as quickly as possible.

## Coaching Points
- Cue (reactor): "Watch their first step — react before they get there."
- The commander should vary direction, pace, and timing to challenge the reactor.
- Both athletes must return to center before the next movement.
- Low athletic stance maintained at center — weight on the balls of the feet, hips down.
- Watch for: reactor anticipating instead of reacting, commander telegraphing direction with body language.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow version — commander walks to cones, reactor mirrors.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed as described.
3. **Advanced**: Commander can touch 2 cones before returning to center, increasing cognitive and physical demand.

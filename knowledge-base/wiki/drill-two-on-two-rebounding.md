# Two-on-Two Rebounding

## Objective
Integrate low-post offense, defensive boxout techniques, offensive rebound escape moves, and blast-out transitions in a game-realistic 5-on-5 structure.

## Setup
- 10 players split into two groups of 5
- **Group 1 (inside)**: Players 1, 2 (offensive post), X1, X2 (defensive post), and 1 rebounder under the goal
- **Group 2 (outside)**: Players A, B, C, D, E — perimeter passers
- 1 basketball
- Half court
- Groups exchange after 2 minutes; inside players rotate roles after each shot

**Inside rotation order** (after each shot): 1→X1, 2→X2, X1→1, X2→rebounder under goal, rebounder under goal→2.

## Execution

### Intermediate
1. The 5 outside players (A–E) pass the ball around the perimeter — including **skip passes** — until one of them passes inside to a post player (1 or 2) or takes a perimeter shot.
2. When the ball goes inside, the post player executes a **low-post move** and shoots.
3. Whether the shot is from the perimeter or the post, **X1 and X2 must use defensive boxout techniques** (immediate boxout or slide-and-box).
4. If X1 or X2 gets the defensive rebound: **blast out** before passing to a perimeter player; drill resets.
5. If Players 1 or 2 get an offensive rebound: use the **pump fake series** to go back up for a power layup.
6. The rebounder under the goal retrieves makes and passes back out to restart.

### Advanced Options
1. On a defensive rebound, the rebounder throws an **overhead outlet pass** instead of blasting out.
2. Once the ball is passed inside, the two post players can **pass between themselves** until one shoots or passes back to the perimeter.
3. Post players may **screen for each other** inside (if post screening is part of your motion offense system).

## Coaching Points
- Defensive boxout technique must remain fundamentally sound under fatigue — check body position every shot.
- Post players must know both primary **and secondary** rebounding positions — if the primary spot is taken, move to the secondary angle.
- **Blast-out technique**: when securing the defensive rebound, quickly dribble out of traffic before making the outlet pass (avoids a jump ball or strip in the congested lane area).
- Pump fakes are essential for the offensive rebounder — use them every time to get the defender off their feet before going back up.
- Perimeter players: skip passes and ball reversal help probe the defense and set up better post entries.
- Post players can use any low-post move from their repertoire: drop step, spin, jump hook, up-and-under, etc.

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Structured rotation; no live defense on perimeter passes.
2. **Advanced**: Live outlet pass → transition; post players can screen each other; two post players may pass between each other before shooting.

# YTW (Physioball Shoulder Strengthening)

## Objective
Strengthen the scapular stabilizer muscles (trapezius, rhomboids, rotator cuff) to establish proper muscle firing patterns that support shooting, defending, and blocking.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Physioball (stability ball)
- Open floor space
- Equipment (advanced): light hand weights

## Execution
1. Lie prone (face down) on a Physioball with arms at your sides.
2. Pinch the shoulder blades together (this is the constant activation cue for all three positions).
3. **Y position**: Lift both arms straight up toward your head, forming a Y shape. Return to start.
4. **T position**: Lift both arms straight out to your sides, forming a T shape. Return to start.
5. **W position**: Lift both arms to your sides with elbows bent, then rotate the hands upward (thumbs toward ceiling), forming a W shape. Return to start.
6. Alternate Y, T, and W positions for **3 sets of 10 repetitions**.
7. **Advancement**: Hold light hand weights during each position.

## Coaching Points
- "Pinch the shoulder blades before you lift" — scapular retraction must come first
- The Physioball adds core stability challenge; the player must maintain body position throughout
- Small range of motion is acceptable initially; quality of scapular control matters more than height of arm lift
- The W position specifically targets the rotator cuff's external rotation function — don't rush it
- This exercise fires muscles in the same pattern used during a jump shot follow-through [S2, p.157]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight only, lying flat on the floor instead of a Physioball
2. **Intermediate**: Physioball without weights, 3 × 10
3. **Advanced**: Add light hand weights (2-5 lbs); increase to 3 × 15

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Secondary**: Biceps brachii, posterior deltoid, lateral deltoid, multifidus, erector spinae, transversus abdominis

# Lunge Patterns

## Objective
Increase lower-extremity strength in three planes of motion, increase range of motion of the hips, and improve dynamic balance — foundational for basketball cutting, defensive slides, and drive-and-kick footwork. [S8, p.135]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface (court, weight room)
- Optional: dumbbells, medicine ball for progressions

## Execution
1. Start in a tall, upright posture — chest up, shoulders back, core engaged.
2. Step into one of the following lunge variations:
   - **Anterior lunge**: step directly forward, lower rear knee toward floor, return
   - **Lateral lunge**: step directly to the side, lower into a squat on the stepping leg, return
   - **Transverse lunge**: rotate the body as you step to create a rotational lunge pattern
3. Push off strongly from the stepped foot to return to the start position.
4. **Do not drag the trailing foot** when returning — lift it cleanly.
5. Alternate legs or complete a full set on one side before switching.

## Coaching Points
- "Tall posture throughout — don't let the chest drop forward"
- Knee stays directly over the second toe — no inward collapse
- Push-off must be explosive — this is a strength AND power exercise
- Full hip extension at the top of each rep — don't shortchange the return
- Watch for the heel leaving the floor on the stepping foot — maintain full contact

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight anterior lunge only; focus on posture and knee tracking
2. **Intermediate**: Add lateral and transverse lunge patterns; hold a dumbbell in each hand and reach toward the foot
3. **Advanced**: Throw arms overhead with a medicine ball while lunging (challenges balance and core); close eyes; shake head "no" to increase vestibular demand [S8, p.135]

## Concepts Taught
- [[lower-extremity-strength-basketball]] — multi-plane lunge builds the quad, glute, and hip strength needed for acceleration and deceleration
- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — single-leg loading in the lunge positions develops proprioceptive control
- [[quickness-and-reaction-time-training]] — dynamic balance in unpredictable positions transfers to on-court reactivity
- [[acl-injury-prevention]] — multi-plane knee loading with correct alignment is a key neuromuscular prehabilitation pattern

# Press Break — Banana Cut and Square the Floor

## Summary
When the opponent scores and the defense applies full-court pressure, the head coach's press-break scheme uses the center (5) to free the primary ball receiver (1) with a brush pick. Because the defensive center (X5) typically drops back to protect the defensive basket as a safety, 5 is available to set this pick. The receiver makes a banana cut to receive the inbounds pass and push the ball upcourt.

If the ball handler is trapped in transition, the team immediately squares the floor — three teammates (4, 3, 2) form a square around the ball handler to give four passing angles and stretch the defensive trap. [S7, pp.79-80]

## When to Use
- After the opponent scores (full-court pressure likely)
- When the defense is set up in a run-and-jump or zone press
- Anytime the inbound receiver is denied by a face-guard defender

## Key Principles
1. **X5 drops as safety** — This predictable defensive positioning frees 5 to set a brush pick for 1.
2. **Brush pick** — 5 sets a stationary screen for 1, who makes a banana cut (arcing cut) to get to the ball side and receive the inbounds pass.
3. **Square the floor immediately if trapped** — As soon as the ball handler is trapped, 4, 3, and 2 form a square around him at equal distances, each offering a clear passing lane.
4. **Pressure-release player higher** — One corner of the square (typically 2) must position himself higher than the ball handler to stretch the floor vertically and give the passer a better angle.
5. **Impossible trap = corner collapses** — If all four corners are covered and it is impossible to pass out, one corner player sprints to the center of the square to offer a safe, short outlet.

## Execution
1. Opponent scores. 5 (center) identifies that X5 has gone back as safety.
2. 5 immediately sets a brush pick for 1 near the free-throw line or mid-court area.
3. 1 makes a banana cut — arcing toward the sideline — to receive the inbounds pass.
4. If 1 receives and is immediately trapped, 4, 3, and 2 snap into the square formation.
5. 2 positions higher than 1 to serve as the pressure release and best passing angle.
6. 1 identifies the open corner and passes; receiver immediately attacks.

## Common Mistakes
1. **5 not reading X5 drop** → If X5 does not drop, 5 must adjust the screen or the play-call must change.
2. **1 running straight** → The banana cut must arc to avoid the denying defender; straight cuts are easily picked off.
3. **Teammates bunching** → When forming the square, players must be spread wide — not clustered near the ball.
4. **Receiver holding the ball** → On receiving the outlet, the player must attack; the defense is still rotating.

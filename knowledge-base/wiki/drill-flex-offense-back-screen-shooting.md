# Flex Offense — Shooting After a Back Screen

## Objective
Practice the flex back-screen option in which 3 sets a back screen for 5, creating three simultaneous scoring opportunities from one ball movement sequence.

## Setup
- 3 players: 3 (back screener), 5 (cutter off back screen), 2 (weak-side corner cutter)
- 2 balls, 2 coach/passers at low-post areas
- Half court

## Execution
1. 5 and 2 pass to the coaches.
2. 3 goes around 2 and sets a back screen for 5.
3. Simultaneously, 2 cuts across the baseline to the opposite corner.
4. The two coaches now choose from three options:
   - **Option A**: Pass to 5, who uses 3's back screen to cut to the block or lane
   - **Option B**: Pass to 2 in the corner, who has an advantage over his defender after the baseline cut
   - **Option C**: Pass to 3, who pops out after setting the back screen
5. Players shoot, grab their rebounds, rotate to the opposite line.

## Coaching Points
- 3 must set a firm back screen (legal contact) — the screener's body should be solid so 5's defender cannot easily get through
- 5 reads the defender: if the defender goes over the screen → 5 cuts baseline; if under → 5 cuts high
- 2's baseline cut to the corner must be wide and decisive to actually pull the help defender
- 3 pops — not stands still; after setting the screen, 3 reads which direction to pop based on where the defense is
- Coaches should not be predictable; vary the pass target

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach always passes to the back-screen cutter (5); build timing of the back screen
2. **Intermediate**: Random read; all three options live
3. **Advanced**: Add a defender on the back screener to practice popping against contact

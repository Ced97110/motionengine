# One-Leg Hop (Agility Ladder)

## Objective
Improve lower-body quickness by performing single-leg hops through every square of a half agility ladder, emphasizing minimal ground contact time. [S8, p.183]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half agility ladder
- Open space

## Execution
1. Stand at the start of the ladder on **one leg**.
2. Hop into every square of the ladder using **only that one leg**.
3. **Minimize ground contact time** — think quick and reactive off the floor.
4. Look straight ahead, not at the ground.
5. Repeat on the opposite leg.
6. To add difficulty, a **sport-specific skill** (e.g., dribbling or catching a ball) can be added while performing the drill. [S8, p.183]

## Coaching Points
- "Quick off the floor — don't sink into the ground"
- The ankle and calf do the work — not a deep knee bend
- Use arm swing for balance and rhythm
- Eyes forward at all times — simulate game-like visual demands
- Both legs must be trained equally — always alternate which leg leads

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two-leg hops (bunny jumps) through the ladder first, then progress to single-leg
2. **Intermediate**: Single-leg hops at moderate speed with full control
3. **Advanced**: Maximum speed single-leg hops + reaction command sprint at exit; or add ball-handling skill overlay

## Concepts Taught
- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — single-leg hopping at speed maximizes proprioceptive demand on the ankle
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — rapid single-leg contacts exploit ankle elastic energy
- [[ankle-strengthening]] — repeated unilateral push-off builds plantar flexion and inversion/eversion control

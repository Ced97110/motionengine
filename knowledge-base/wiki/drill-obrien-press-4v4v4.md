# O'Brien 4-4-4 Full-Court Press Drill

## Objective
Simultaneously train the full-court press (white and black), fast-break offense, tandem defense, and transition defense in one live, competitive full-court drill.

## Setup
- 12 players split into three teams of four: Team A, Team B, Team C
- **Initial alignment** (see Figure 15.20 [S7, p.251]):
  - Team A: backcourt, top of the key area (on offense with the ball)
  - Team B: two defenders in tandem at the far basket; two players at the intersection of the center line and each sideline
  - Team C: two defenders in tandem at Team A's basket; two players at half-court sidelines
- Run live to a set score; winner gets a water break, losers run

## Execution
1. **Team A attacks Team B** in a 4-on-2 fast break (Team B's two half-court players cannot enter until the ball crosses the center line; when it does, they must touch the center circle before defending).
2. **If Team A misses**: Team B rebounds and fast-breaks against Team C at the other end. Team C has two in tandem at their basket; two at half-court sidelines (same rules apply for trailers).
3. **If Team A scores**: Team A immediately presses Team B with the white press (if scored in the lane) or black press (if scored on a jump shot). Team A can only press to half-court — Team C takes over at mid-court.
4. **If Team B beats the press**: Team C defends as Team B attacks. Team C's two trailers touch the center line when Team B crosses. Team A puts two defenders at half-court out of bounds and two others in a tandem at the basket it just attacked.
5. Cycle continues live until the set score.

## Coaching Points
- "This can be a wild drill at times, and the coaching staff must be diligent in striking a balance of nonstop aggressiveness and teaching the rules of the press." [S7, p.252]
- The self-imposed half-court press boundary for Team A allows extreme aggressiveness — they have nothing to lose.
- Trailers **must touch the center circle** before they can defend — this ensures the drill teaches proper fast-break defense.
- Communicate which press (white or black) clearly on each possession.
- Run to a set score; competitive incentives maintain intensity.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Run with coach calling each press and all rotations; teams walk through transitions.
2. **Intermediate**: As described with live play; coach calls which press.
3. **Advanced**: Teams self-select white or black press; coach only intervenes for rule violations.

# Muscle-Up Variations

## Objective
Build total-body explosive power, grip strength, bicep strength, and asymmetric pulling ability through advanced muscle-up progressions beyond the standard overhand technique.

## Setup
- 1 player (solo drills)
- Pull-up / muscle-up bar at appropriate height (athlete can hang with feet off ground)
- No additional equipment required
- Outdoor calisthenics bar or gym pull-up rig

## Execution

### Swing Dips
1. Begin at the top of a dip position (arms locked out, body above the bar).
2. Swing legs forward at the bottom of the dip movement.
3. As arms straighten, swing legs and entire body *back* simultaneously.
4. By the time arms are locked out, trunk and legs should be virtually horizontal behind you.
5. This peak position lasts a split-second before swinging back down.
6. The total-body swing generates and absorbs momentum, demanding full-body coordination and power. [S9, p.1]

### Underhand Muscle-Up
1. Hang from the bar with an *underhand* grip (palms facing toward you, supinated).
2. Execute the muscle-up pull-push sequence as normal — swing/kip, rotate arms, push up over the bar.
3. Note: the underhand position places new power demands on grip and biceps, and makes the press at the top significantly harder than the standard overhand version. [S9, p.2]

### Archer Muscle-Up
1. Prerequisites: proficient in the underhand muscle-up.
2. Hang from the bar with an asymmetric grip: one arm in normal overhand position, one arm extended out to the side so it provides less pulling/pushing assistance.
3. Execute the muscle-up: the "bent" (working) arm must compensate by amplifying its output while the extended arm acts as a guide/balance only.
4. Switch sides each set to train both arms.
5. This variation exposes and corrects weak links in unilateral pulling strength. [S9, p.2]

### Full Kip
1. Hang from the bar with straight arms, overhand grip.
2. Build momentum through a body swing (swing kip).
3. Using maximum body power — not arm strength — launch the entire body *up and over* the bar in a single explosive movement.
4. Arms remain *fairly straight* throughout (unlike a muscle-up, where arms bend significantly to pull).
5. Body momentum powers the athlete over the bar. [S9, p.3]

## Coaching Points
- **Swing dips**: "Swing and extend — your body is horizontal at the top." The movement requires total-body timing, not just arm strength. Don't rush the swing. [S9, p.1]
- **Underhand muscle-up**: The grip shift creates a different pulling groove — expect the transition over the bar to feel awkward at first. Stay patient.
- **Archer muscle-up**: The extended arm should not assist much — resist the temptation to bend it. "The bent arm compensates by amplifying its output." [S9, p.2]
- **Full kip**: Arms stay straight — if they bend significantly, it has become a muscle-up, not a kip. The power must come from body momentum, not arm pulling.
- Apply [[concept-parc-progression-principle]] before attempting each variation: proficiency → adaptation → regularity → confidence.
- Never rush to add reps. Speed and precision matter more than volume. [S9, p.11]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard overhand muscle-up (prerequisite for all variations)
2. **Intermediate**: Underhand muscle-up — same movement, harder grip/bicep demand
3. **Advanced**: Archer muscle-up (after underhand is mastered); Full kip (requires maximum body-power development)
4. **Master**: Swing dips combined with archer or underhand grip for total-body power chains

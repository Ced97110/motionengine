# Perimeter One-on-One Moves

## Summary
Once a perimeter player has the ball, their offensive arsenal must be so ingrained that they can select any move in a split-second based on the defender's reaction. Stan Van Gundy's system — built around pro players like Dwyane Wade, Rashard Lewis, an elite scoring wing, and Allen Iverson — organizes perimeter 1-on-1 moves into three tiers: direct moves (no fakes needed), combined moves (jab-step-based fakes), and advanced dribble combinations [S7, pp.21-33].

The cardinal principle: **never decide your move in advance**. Read and react to the defender on every possession.

## When to Use
- After receiving the ball on the perimeter in triple-threat position
- When one-on-one opportunities are created by ball movement or screens
- When defenders overcommit in any direction, creating a specific attack lane

## One-on-One Essentials
1. **Read the defender** — react to what they give you; deciding in advance is a mistake
2. **Start low, finish high** — begin with knees bent, explode high for the jump shot or layup
3. **Three dribbles maximum** — shoot after no more than three dribbles (two is better); gives the defense time to recover
4. **Drive straight lines** — on all drives to the basket, go in a straight line, not a curved line
5. **Use body contact on contested layups** — lean inside shoulder and forearm into the defender's chest (without pushing) while leaning with inside hip and leg; this prevents the defender from recovering to block the shot

## Direct Moves (No Fakes)

### Jump Shot
If in shooting range, the defender isn't tight, and their hands are down — shoot immediately. No fakes needed.

### Direct Drive
If the defender leaves a straight driving lane, attack it directly.

**Footwork:** First stride is long but not so long balance and power are lost; inside shoulder at the level of the defender's hip; turn nondribbling shoulder inside to protect the ball and slim profile; foot touches floor simultaneously with the dribble (avoid walking). Second stride: quick and strong; stay low on the knees; do not decelerate.

**Dribble:** Push the ball hard to the ground; dribbling hand follows ball with fingers pointing toward/as close as possible to the floor (ball bounces back quickly and stays in control longer). Ball must bounce over the hip of the defender, slightly on the side of the right foot, in front of the left foot. Head stays up — watch basket and floor to see defenders and teammates. Protect ball with non-dribbling arm bent about 45°.

### Sweep (Rip) of the Ball
Required technique before any crossover: quickly move the ball from one side of the body to the other. Stay low; bring the ball from chest/hip level on one side, swinging it below the knees (high-to-low arc) to the other side. Simultaneously rotate the dribbling hand laterally and place the other hand on the back of the ball, wrist bent backward.

> pro variation: Tim Duncan — shoulder to shoulder; Allen Iverson — ankle to ankle; an elite scoring wing — shoulder to hip. [S7, p.23]

### Crossover Step
Used when the defender blocks the straight line.
1. Sweep the ball from right to left (below knee level)
2. Put weight on the ball of the left foot
3. Make a long crossover step with the right foot over the defender's forward foot; push body forward with the left foot
4. Simultaneously make a long dribble with the left hand
5. After the crossover: right shoulder brushes defender's right hip and comes to the level of the defender's right hip (to prevent recovery)
6. Toes of right foot must be turned inside and toward the basket (not laterally) — body moves straight to the rim, not laterally
7. Right arm bent 45° to protect the ball

### Reverse (or Spin) Drive
When the defender overplays the high side upon receiving the ball on the wing.

**Footwork:** Use the inside foot (nearest the baseline) as the pivot foot; rotate on the ball of the foot while making a long step toward the new direction with the nonpivot foot; turn toes of nonpivot foot as far toward the basket as possible to avoid moving away from the rim.

**Dribble:** On the last side dribble, move the dribbling hand laterally on the ball with forearm bent ~45° (ball pushed to floor near body, not away from it). This brings the ball around the body, ending with a hard push to the floor in the new direction.

**Key:** Turn head in new direction before making the move — allows last-second changes based on defender reaction and avoids charging calls.

## Combined Moves (Jab Step Based)

### Jab Step Mechanics
The jab step is the foundation of all combined moves. It is NOT just a foot move — it involves both the foot AND the ball simultaneously.

**Footwork:** From triple-threat position (ball on right side), make a forward, strong, aggressive **short** step with the right foot (if right-handed); land on the forefoot (heel of left foot stays up for quicker pace); right foot lands ~6–7" ahead of the left foot; turn right shoulder slightly inside to protect the ball and give the impression of a drive; head and trunk straight or slightly forward; eyes over the defender (not at feet), watching the whole court.

**Ball movement:** Simultaneously bring the ball aggressively to the side of the right knee to fake a dribble with the right hand. Do NOT overextend the arm (maintain balance) and do NOT expose the ball too much in front of the defender.

**Purpose:** The jab step forces the defender to react — crossing their feet, leaning, or straightening their body. **Read the reaction, then decide your move.**

### Fake Principles
A fake must be realistic — you must truly *start* the move you are faking to sell it. The fake must not be too quick; give the defender time to commit to a mistake. Always basing the next move on the defender's reaction.

### Jab Step and Drive (Same Hand)
If the defender doesn't react quickly enough to the jab:
- Take a long step with the right foot (not losing balance)
- Make a dribble over the defender's hip with the right hand; bring left shoulder to hip level of defender; left arm bent 45° at hip level protects ball
- Wrist must be bent backward and shifted behind ball with elbow bent (pushes ball forward)
- Second step after jab is quick and strong; stay low; don't decelerate

### Jab Step and Crossover
If the defender slides in the direction of the jab step:
- Use a crossover step and dribble in the opposite direction

### Jab Step and Jump Shot
If the defender retreats after the jab step (if in shooting range):
- Immediately bring ball into the "shooting pocket" and shoot
- Jab step must be aggressive and short; knees flexed; palm of shooting hand behind ball with wrist cocked toward chest to speed up release

### Shot Fake and Drive (Same Hand or Crossover)
- Bring ball over the forehead with hands on it as if to shoot; simultaneously flex legs more
- When defender straightens their legs reacting to the fake, explode to the basket (same hand or crossover depending on defender's position)

### Jab Step, Shot Fake, and Drive
- Three-count move (jab step → shot fake → drive); don't rush any count
- Take enough time on each fake to force the defender to commit; be realistic
- Do NOT decide in advance — react to the defender

### Jab Step, Shot Fake, Drive Fake, and Jump Shot
- Opposite of above: fake the drive, pull up for the jumper
- Stay low on knees for all fakes; then explode vertically for the jump shot
- All combined moves should take ≤3 seconds maximum

### Pass Fake and Drive or Jump Shot
- Fake a lateral pass with two hands (don't overextend arms)
- Take a short step in the direction of the pass fake and shift weight to ball of forward foot
- Quickly push body in the opposite direction; drive or pull up for jump shot

## Dribble Moves (Live Dribble)

### Hockey Dribble
**Footwork:** Short, choppy skating steps back and forth with feet nearly parallel; keep defender clueless with head and shoulder fakes; then drive hard to the basket in a straight line (knees bent).
**Dribble:** Hand over the ball, pushing very hard to the floor for control; on last dribble, hand brought behind to push ball hard and low over the defender; body weight shifts to the foot on same side as dribbling hand for the drive.

### Change-of-Pace Dribble
- Approach at moderate controlled speed; get defender to relax
- When defender's knees straighten, quickly drop knees for a sudden sprint
- Avoid short, nervous steps on approach (telegraphs the move)
- Head and shoulder fake in opposite direction can help

### Speed Dribble: Stop and Go
- Dribble quickly toward defender; stop suddenly (keep dribble alive)
- Raise head and legs slightly — looks like slowing down
- When defender straightens or relaxes: explode into speed dribble
- Sequence: speed dribble → controlled dribble → brief pause → speed dribble again

### Step-Back Dribble
- When defender covers aggressively with no room to drive
- **Footwork:** Dribbling right hand — make a strong step with left foot toward defender (full body weight on ball of forward foot; lift right foot). Then step back, pushing off left foot, lifting it, to create space.
- **Dribble:** As left foot steps forward, shift right hand fingers from back to front of ball, pulling it back. When defender closes, explode with a power dribble
- Stay low throughout; fake with whole body, not just ball

### In-'n-Out
Crossover dribble fake:
1. Dribbling right hand — step outside with left foot; shift right hand top/laterally on ball bringing it momentarily toward middle
2. Defender anticipates crossover and leans that direction
3. At last second, bring ball back to starting position and dribble again on the right side
4. Simultaneously take a long stride right to get past the defender
5. Weight must be on ball of left foot to push body right (straight line, not lateral)

### Crossover Dribble
**Footwork:** Bring body weight to inside of right foot; push body in new direction before taking a long step with left foot; left foot points toward basket (not laterally).
**Dribble:** On last lateral dribble, pump the side of the ball very hard below knee level and as low to floor as possible. Ball should bounce on the side, over the toes of the left forward foot. Left hand positioned laterally to briefly stop the ball, then immediately shift palm backward for speed dribble. Turn shoulders in new direction.

### Behind the Back
*Safer than crossover — ball not in front of the defender.*

**One foot forward:** Step forward with left foot on last right-hand dribble; shift right hand in front of ball; bring ball behind back; control until near opposite hip; push ball hard to floor. Ball bounces left side, over toes of left foot. Left hand controls for speed dribble.

**Two feet even:** Bend knees on last dribble; back straight, feet parallel; right hand on side of ball, forcefully push ball behind back and below buttocks. Ball bounces over left hip. Left hand stops ball for a split second, then immediately shifts palm backward for speed dribble.

### Between the Legs
Step forward with left foot on last right-hand dribble; right hand shifts laterally on ball pushing it between the legs to the left side; ball bounces over toes of left foot; left hand stops then shifts back for speed dribble.

### Reverse (or Spin) Dribble
Step forward with left foot; right hand shifts to side of ball, forearm bent ~45°; pivot on ball of left foot while rotating body in new direction; bounce ball with hard, quick movement (fingers throw/slap it hard to floor). Ball comes back quickly for better control. Protect ball between body and defender. Head anticipates the rotation in new direction (watching defender reaction over right shoulder).

Key coaching details:
- Stay low during AND after the move
- Spread legs more than shoulder-width; bend knees for quicker explosive move
- Keep arm near body, ~45° angle (wider = ball lands away from body, loses control)
- The carrying hand leaves ball only after body reverse is nearly complete
- Begin dribbling with new hand only AFTER the reverse is complete — any earlier exposes ball to a defensive slap from behind
- Toes of forward foot after reverse must point straight forward (not lateral) to avoid moving away from basket

### Half-Reverse (or Half-Spin)
Same as reverse, but once the defender has recovered and is in your path, dribble back to where the reverse started and go in the same direction you began. Maintain the same dribbling hand throughout. Turn head in intended direction in advance.

## Advanced Combined Dribble Moves

All are combinations of two consecutive dribble types with changes of speed and direction. These are not showboating — they are effective pro moves for countering defensive reactions [S7, p.30].

### Dribble, Step Back, and Jump Shot
- Dribble once toward defender; step with left foot (defender expects drive)
- Put full weight on left foot; push back with small hop; land right foot first, then left
- Pull ball back and shoot jumper over defender; stay low throughout

### Dribble, Step Back, Turn, and Jump Shot *(an elite scoring wing specialty)*
- Same step back, but use left foot as pivot foot
- Pull ball back and make a rear turn to face basket; shoot jumper

### Dribble, Step Back, and Crossover
- Use right foot as pivot foot on step back; pull ball back; perform crossover dribble; drive or jump shot
- Head fake (stay low) a split second before the crossover to force defender to straighten legs

### Dribble, Step Back, and Reverse
- Step back; pull ball back; pivot on right foot; front the basket; perform reverse dribble; drive or jump shot

### Dribble, Step Back, Crossover, and Reverse
- After step back, combine crossover + reverse in the same action; decision based on defender's reaction

### Dribble, Step Back, Hesitation, and Speed Dribble
- After step back, hesitate briefly (slightly raise head and knees — looks like slowing down)
- When defender stands up or relaxes: hard, quick dribble to beat them; direct drive, crossover, or reverse

### Other Advanced Combinations
- In-and-out → crossover
- Crossover → between-the-legs
- Crossover → reverse
- Stop-and-go → crossover
- Between-the-legs → reverse → crossover
- Between-the-legs → behind-the-back
- Double between-the-legs
- Lateral step and dribble → speed direct drive (same hand)
- Lateral step and dribble → crossover or in-and-out
- Hockey dribble → crossover
- Hockey dribble → lateral step and dribble → speed forward dribble or crossover

## Shooting in Traffic (Two-Defender Scenarios)

### Reverse Dribble vs. Inside-Foot-Up Defender
If the free-throw-line defender has inside foot up: go toward them, perform a reverse dribble, approach the basket defender, lean inside shoulder and hip into them to freeze them, shoot a layup.

### Hesitation and Change of Pace vs. Parallel-Feet Defender
If the free-throw-line defender has feet parallel: use a hesitation dribble with head/shoulder fake, then explode to the basket on the other side (straight line), lean inside shoulder and hip into the second defender, shoot a layup.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Deciding your move in advance** → good defenders will exploit this; always read and react
2. **Curved drives to the basket** → drive in straight lines; curved drives give help defenders time to recover
3. **Short, nervous steps on approach** → telegraphs the intended direction to defenders
4. **Speed dribble not low enough** → speed dribble must be lower than stationary dribble; higher dribbles are slower and easier to steal
5. **Performing the reverse before it's complete** → dribbling with new hand too early exposes ball to a defensive slap from behind
6. **Toes pointed laterally on crossover/drive** → toes must point toward the basket to drive straight to the rim
7. **Fakes that are too quick** → give the defender time to react and commit a mistake; slow, realistic fakes are more effective

## Progressions (Teaching Sequence)
1. **Beginner**: Basic moves without a defender — direct drive, crossover, jab step and jump shot
2. **Intermediate**: Combined moves at moderate speed without a defender; add defender at 50% effort
3. **Advanced**: Full game-speed 1-on-1 with defender at 100% effort; incorporate advanced dribble combinations

## Teaching Points
- Extensive ball-handling work is required to master 1-on-1 moves: stationary and on-the-move dribbling, one and two balls, basic → advanced dribbles
- Learn to "pump" the ball very hard and low to the floor (not just push it)
- Teach gradually: basic → combined → advanced; don't rush
- Always emphasize reading and reacting over pre-planned moves

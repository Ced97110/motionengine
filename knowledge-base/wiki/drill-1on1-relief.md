# 1-on-1 Relief Drill (#105)

## Objective
Develop all perimeter live-ball moves and off-ball cutting reads within a 15-foot lane against live defense, with a safety-valve passer to keep the drill continuous.

## Setup
- **Players**: 3 per group (attacker 3, defender X3, relief passer 4); run multiple groups simultaneously
- **Court area**: Half court; offensive player stays inside a designated 15-foot (4.5 m) lane; change lane location daily
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball per group
- **Rotation**: 3 → X3 → 4 → 3

<!-- DIAGRAM: Player 3 dribble-drives against X3 inside the lane; Player 4 stands at the top of the key as a relief passer. Arrow shows 3 passing to 4, then V-cutting or backdoor cutting to receive the return pass. -->

## Execution
1. Player 3 begins with the ball inside the 15-foot lane in triple-threat position.
2. 3 uses a rocker step and any dribbling move to attempt to create a quality shot.
3. If 3 cannot create separation, **3 does NOT force a bad shot** — instead, 3 passes to relief passer 4.
4. After passing to 4, player 3 immediately makes a V-cut, middle cut, or backdoor cut — whichever the defense gives.
5. Player 4 returns the pass to 3 who again operates 1-on-1 against X3.
6. X3 plays solid defense from stance to denying the shot throughout.
7. Change the 15-foot lane location from day to day to simulate different game positions.

## Coaching Points
- "Don't take a bad shot — use the passer and cut" — the safety valve is integral, not optional
- Defender must play from stance to denying the shot; no passive defense
- Watch for players forcing moves when the defense is set — reinforce reading the defense first
- Cuts after the pass (V-cut, middle cut, backdoor) must be decisive based on X3's positioning
- Reward smart shot selection over volume of attempts

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Limit attacker to one or two predetermined moves only
2. **Intermediate**: Open to any move, attacker reads the defense freely
3. **Advanced**: Defender must stop attacker two consecutive times before rotating (raises defensive stakes)

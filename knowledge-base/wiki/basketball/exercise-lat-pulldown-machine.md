# Lat Pulldown (Machine)

## Objective
Develop bilateral latissimus dorsi, teres major, trapezius, rhomboid, and posterior deltoid strength to improve rebounding pull-downs, overhead reaching, athletic posture, and upper-body balance relative to pressing exercises.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Lat pulldown machine with a thigh pad
- Standard wide-grip bar (can substitute handle, rope, towel, or other attachment)
- No spotter required for machine version

## Execution
1. Sit facing the machine, thighs secured under the pad, feet flat on the floor.
2. Grab the bar **wider than shoulder-width apart** with a closed, pronated grip.
3. Fully extend the arms, draw the shoulders back and down slightly, lean the torso back slightly.
4. Look slightly upward and maintain a fixed eye position to maintain posture and keep the neck relaxed — this is the beginning position.
5. **Pull:** Pull the bar down toward the upper chest in a smooth, controlled manner while maintaining posture. Touch the bar to the **upper portion of the chest**, keeping wrists stiff and shoulders back and down.
6. **Return:** Slowly allow the elbows to extend, returning to the beginning position while maintaining torso angle.

**Breathing:** Exhale during the pull; inhale during the return.

## Coaching Points
- "Shoulders back and down" — initiate the pull by depressing and retracting the scapulae before pulling with the arms
- "Stiff wrists" — wrists must remain rigid throughout; no flexion or deviation
- "Chest to the bar" — the bar comes to the chest, not the chest jutting forward to the bar
- Maintain a neutral-to-slightly-reclined torso position — no excessive swinging or momentum
- "Fixed eyes" — keeping a fixed gaze slightly upward helps maintain cervical spine position and proper posture

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Machine lat pulldown with standard wide bar — light to moderate load; focus on scapular depression/retraction at the start of the pull
2. **Intermediate**: Change attachment to a handle, rope, or V-bar for varied hand positions and grip demands; add pauses at peak contraction
3. **Advanced**: Tempo variations (slow eccentric 3–4 seconds); towel around the handle for grip-strength training; progress toward [[drill-pull-up-basketball]] (bodyweight pulling)

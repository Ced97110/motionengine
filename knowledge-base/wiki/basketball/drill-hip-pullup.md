# Hip Pull-Up (Muscle-Up Chain Step 7)

## Objective
Develop the explosive hip-thrust mechanics that power the bar pullover — this step achieves the height required for the muscle-up's pulling portion using a posterior-chain hip drive rather than arm strength.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Overhead horizontal bar
- No additional equipment required

## Execution
1. Grab and hang from the overhead horizontal bar with shoulder-width grip.
2. Shoulders pulled down tight into the armpits.
3. Build a **powerful swing kip** — backswing should carry you high.
4. As soon as you are close to the peak of the backswing, **snap backwards with your arms** — this is a *short tug*, not the full-range pull used in chest pull-ups.
5. Simultaneously **clench your glutes and posterior chain** to thrust your hips upward toward the bar.
6. Thrust hard enough so that your **lower stomach (lower abs) kisses the bar**.
7. Re-grip the bar and lower yourself quickly, back into the swing kip.

## Coaching Points
- **Arms vs. hips distinction**: Chest pull-ups use full arm range at the top; hip pull-ups use only a short backward arm snap — the majority of the force comes from the hip thrust [S9, p.225].
- **Glute squeeze is everything**: Actively clenching the glutes and posterior chain is what drives the hips upward. This is not a passive movement.
- **Short backward arm snap**: Do NOT attempt the full elbow-behind-body pull from Step 6. A short tug to redirect + the hip thrust creates the required height.
- **"Believe it or not, once you can achieve the hip pullup, you have pretty much achieved the muscle-up — the pulling portion, anyways."** [S9, p.225]
- **Height target**: Start with upper abs touching if lower abs contact is not yet achievable.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner regression**: Aim for upper abs contact with the bar if lower abs is too difficult.
2. **Intermediate**: Lower abs contact with the bar (standard hip pull-up).
3. **Advanced**: Explode the upper thighs into the bar — building extra height reserve [S9, p.225].

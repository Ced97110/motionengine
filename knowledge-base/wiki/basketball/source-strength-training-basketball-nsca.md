# Strength Training for Basketball (NSCA) — Summary

## Overview
*Strength Training for Basketball* is an NSCA-edited multi-author volume bringing together some of the leading strength and conditioning coaches and sport scientists working in professional and collegiate basketball. Contributors include practitioners from NBA franchises (Washington Wizards), major universities (Villanova, UC Berkeley, Washington State), and research institutions, as well as experts in biomechanics, physical therapy, and sports medicine.

The book is designed as a comprehensive, evidence-based resource for basketball S&C professionals, covering the full spectrum of physical preparation from needs analysis and program design through strength, power, plyometrics, speed, agility, injury prevention, and rehabilitation.

## Key Themes
1. **Basketball-specific strength and power development** — periodized weight training, Olympic lifts, and plyometrics tailored to the demands of basketball.
2. **Injury prevention and rehabilitation** — ACL, ankle, knee, and shoulder protocols by credentialed physical therapists and sports medicine physicians.
3. **Evidence-based practice** — contributions from PhDs in biomechanics, exercise science, and sports medicine alongside practitioners.
4. **Multi-author breadth** — each chapter authored by a specialist (S&C coaches, physical therapists, MDs, PhDs) reflecting diverse expertise.

- **Tania Spiteri** (PhD) — Head of Sport Science, World Basketball Academy; PhD biomechanics, Edith Cowan University; ASCA level 2 S&C coach
- **Scott Thom** (MA, CSCS, USAW) — Head S&C at Cal (men's basketball) and Washington State; 2016 Pac-12 All-Star Olympic preparation; author of *Season of Strength*

# Snake Jump Agility Ladder Drill

## Objective
Improve agility, balance, coordination, hip flexibility, and quickness by performing a series of quarter-turn two-foot jumps that rotate the hips with each landing.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder laid flat
- Player starts straddling one side of the ladder (feet outside the first rung)

## Execution
1. Start straddling one side of the ladder in a two-point stance, feet together.
2. Keep **both feet together** for all jumps.
3. Jump sequence — the direction feet should point at landing:
   - Jump 1: **straight ahead** (face forward into the ladder square)
   - Jump 2: **right** (quarter-turn right)
   - Jump 3: **straight ahead** (quarter-turn back left)
   - Jump 4: **left** (quarter-turn left)
   - Jump 5: **straight ahead** (quarter-turn back right)
   - Continue alternating right-straight-left-straight the full length of the ladder
4. Each jump advances one rung forward in the ladder.
5. Each jump forces a quarter-turn rotation of the hips.

## Coaching Points
- Land softly on the balls of both feet — absorb through ankle, knee, and hip
- Hips rotate fully with each quarter-turn — shoulders follow the hips
- Minimal air time — "quick jumps" not max-height jumps; this is a quickness drill
- Look forward, not at the ladder
- "Rotate the hips, not just the feet" — the training effect comes from the hip turn

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice stationary quarter-turn jumps in place before adding ladder advancement.
2. **Intermediate**: Full ladder length at training speed.
3. **Advanced**: Increase jump speed; compete for time; add a full-sprint finish after the last rung.

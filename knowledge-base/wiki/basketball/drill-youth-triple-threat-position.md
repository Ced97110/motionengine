# Triple-Threat Position (Drill 9)

## Objective
Teach players to establish and maintain the triple-threat position after receiving a pass off a V-cut, and progressively add fakes, drives, and jump shots.

## Setup
- Individual or team drill
- Half court, at the wing/perimeter (shooting range from basket)
- Players line up on the sideline/wing; a coach stands at the top of the key or elbow area
- One ball per station

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure shows single line of players on the left wing. Coach is at the top of the key. Player V-cuts to the wing, receives pass from coach, reverse pivots into triple threat, passes back and goes to end of line. -->

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up as shown.
2. A player V-cuts to an open spot on the perimeter; the coach passes to the player.
3. The player **reverse pivots** and puts the ball in triple-threat position.
4. The player passes the ball back to the coach and goes to the end of the line.

### Intermediate
- After pivoting into triple-threat position, the player executes a **jab-step fake**, **crossover fake**, or **rocker-step fake** (coach's choice).

### Advanced
1. After executing one of the fakes, the player begins a **dribble-drive to the basket** for a layup, OR stops short with a **jump stop** and takes a jump shot.
2. Instead of driving all the way, the player stops with a jump stop and executes a **post-dribble fake** (spin move, half-spin, or up-and-under) to get the jump shot off.
3. Instead of driving all the way, the player executes a **dribbling move** (in-and-out, spin, or half-spin) after two dribbles before driving for the layup.
4. **Solo variation**: Player passes the ball a few feet away with backspin, goes to catch it, reverse pivots, and completes the drill as described.

## Coaching Points
- **Stance**: Players crouch with a **slight bend at the knees and trunk**. They bring the ball up **under the chin**, with one hand on each side of the ball.
- **Ball position**: The ball can be slightly to the **side of the torso opposite the pivot foot**.
- **Three options**: From this position, players can **pass**, **begin a dribble**, or **shoot** — hence the name triple threat.
- Emphasize that the reverse pivot must be crisp and quick — do not let the pivot drag.
- The ball should be high enough to protect against steals but ready to shoot without any extra lifting motion.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: V-cut → receive → reverse pivot → triple threat → pass back
2. **Intermediate**: Add jab step / crossover fake / rocker step fake from triple threat
3. **Advanced**: Complete attacking sequences (drive, pull-up, dribble moves)

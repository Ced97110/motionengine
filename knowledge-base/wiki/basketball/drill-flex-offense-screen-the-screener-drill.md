# Flex Offense — Shooting on Screen-the-Screener Action (4-on-4)

## Objective
Practice the continuous screen-the-screener rhythm of the flex offense against live defenders on both sides of the floor until a shooting opportunity is created.

## Setup
- 4 offensive players (1, 2, 3, 4) vs. 4 defenders
- Half court
- Coach or player initiates from the top

## Execution
1. 1 passes to 3. When the ball reaches 3, 4 sets a back screen for 2.
2. Immediately after 4 screens for 2, 1 sets a high screen for 4 (screen-the-screener).
3. 4 pops high and receives from 3.
4. 4 makes a skip pass to 1. When the ball is in 1's hands, 2 screens for 3 and 4 screens for 2 (the action repeats on the other side).
5. Continue until an effective shooting opportunity is found — either a cutter catching in the lane off the back screen or a player coming off the high screen for a perimeter shot.
6. If no shot is available, continue the pattern — do not force a bad shot.

## Coaching Points
- The screen-the-screener must happen **immediately** — there should be no pause between the back screen and the high screen
- "If you screen, you're getting screened next" — this is the core rhythm players must internalize
- The back-screen cutter (2 or 3) must read over or under based on the defender
- Players must communicate verbally — "screen left," "coming high" — to maintain the flow
- Defenders: do not give up — the drill should be competitive, stopping when an actual shot is earned

## Progressions
1. **Walk-through**: No defense; coach narrates each action step by step
2. **Passive defense**: Defenders shade but do not contest; cutter reads the shade
3. **Live 4-on-4**: Full competition; play until a shot, a turnover, or the coach whistles

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-flex-offense]] — the screen-the-screener is the defining action of the flex
- [[concept-screen-the-screener-footwork]] — the player setting a pick for a teammate who just screened is often the most open player
- [[concept-setting-screens]] — screeners must set and hold; the screener who releases early kills the action

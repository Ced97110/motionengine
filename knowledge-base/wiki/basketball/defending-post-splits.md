# Defending Post Splits

## Summary
Post splits occur when two perimeter players cut off a post player holding the ball — typically one cuts over the top and one cuts underneath — attempting to free one cutter on a quick pass. The key defensive weapon against post splits is communication and switching: defenders must talk to each other, get level with the ball, and the back defender must loudly call both the screen and the switch. [S1, p.149]

## When to Use
- Whenever two cutters attempt to split off a post player with the ball (high post or low post)
- On designed post-split actions after a ball entry to the block or elbow
- When the offense has isolated the post with two perimeter cutters clearing to the basket

## Key Principles
1. **Communication is the most important thing** — The defenders involved must talk to each other without hesitation. [S1, p.149]
2. **Get level with the offensive post man** — Defenders must get level with the post player who has the ball to combat quick slips and cuts. Being caught behind means they cannot recover to contest the cutter. [S1, p.149]
3. **Get level with the ball to switch** — Defenders must also be level with the ball if they want to execute a clean switch on the splitting cutters. [S1, p.149]
4. **Don't let cutters split the switch** — Defensive indecision is the offense's goal. The switch must be decisive — if the switch is called, both defenders commit immediately. [S1, p.149]
5. **Back man calls everything** — On high splits or any split requiring a switch, the *back defender* is responsible for calling out both the pick AND the switch. He has the best view of what the offense is running. [S1, p.149]

## Player Responsibilities
- **Defender guarding the over-the-top cutter (X1)**: Hear the call; commit to the switch; stop the cutter from splitting. [S1, p.149]
- **Defender guarding the underneath cutter (X2)**: Read the play from behind; call the pick AND the switch; communicate early. [S1, p.149]
- **Post defender**: Get to ball level; be ready to hedge or show on cutters if needed to help teammates recover. [S1, p.149]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Late or no communication** → One cutter goes free; the switch is split. Correction: Back defender must call it early and loudly — "screen, switch!" [S1, p.149]
2. **Defenders not level with the ball** → Quick passes exploit depth disadvantage. Correction: Both defenders must adjust upward on the pass to the post so they are already in position when the split begins. [S1, p.149]
3. **Soft switch** → Offensive player dribbles through the middle of the switch. Correction: Both defenders must come together aggressively and not allow any splitting of the seam. [S1, p.149]

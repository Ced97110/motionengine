# Shooting Hand Grip

## Summary
The shooting hand controls touch and directs where the ball goes. While the legs and core provide power, the shooting hand, wrist, and fingers provide the finesse. Proper grip — ball resting on fingertips and fingerpads, palm off the ball, L-shaped arm, wrist cocked — ensures correct backspin, alignment, and a repeatable release. Seaming the basketball (aligning the valve between index and middle finger) builds consistent rotation on every shot. [S5, pp.12-14]

## When to Use
- Applied on every shot — catch-and-shoot, off-the-dribble, free throws
- Taught as Step 2 after the shooting stance is established
- Evaluated and corrected during all form shooting drills

## Key Principles
1. **Palm does NOT touch the ball** — the ball rests entirely on the fingertips and fingerpads; palm contact forces the ball to roll off the hand, killing proper backspin [S5, p.13]
2. **Shooting hand in the center of the ball** — position the palm facing the ceiling, then place the ball into the hand; center positioning ensures control regardless of hand size [S5, p.13]
3. **Form an L with the shooting arm** — bring the ball back toward the head so the forearm is vertical and the upper arm is horizontal, creating an L-shape; this is the launch position [S5, p.13]
4. **Daylight check** — there should be visible space between the heel of the shooting hand and the ball; test by placing two fingers from the non-shooting hand between the heel of the shooting hand and the ball [S5, p.13]
5. **Seam the basketball** — find the valve (air hole) and place it between the index finger and middle finger; this ensures beautiful rotation on every shot; great shooters always seam the ball [S5, p.14]
6. **Wrist cocked and locked** — bend the wrist back until the skin wrinkles before initiating the shot [S5, p.15]
7. **"Be a lifter, not a slinger or a pusher"** — the L-shape lifts the ball properly; too much elbow bend = slinging; not enough = pushing [S5, p.15]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Practice seaming the ball every time you pick it up in a workout until you can feel the seams without looking at them.

## Variations
### One-Handed Grip Check
With no balance hand, hold the ball in the shooting hand only in the L-position. If the elbow is correctly tucked and the hand is under the center of the ball, the ball will balance naturally. If it falls, the hand is off-center or the elbow is out. [S5, p.16]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Palm on the ball** → Ball rolls off the hand, no backspin, poor touch → Keep the palm off; ball rests on fingertips only [S5, p.13]
2. **Hand not centered on ball** → Uneven control, ball rolls sideways → Place hand with palm to ceiling, then set ball directly into center [S5, p.13]
3. **No daylight between heel and ball** → Shooting hand too close, reduces wrist snap → Use the two-finger check to verify proper spacing [S5, p.13]
4. **Not seaming the ball** → Inconsistent rotation, erratic shot flight → Practice finding the valve every rep until tactile feel is automatic [S5, p.14]
5. **Wrist not fully cocked** → Reduced backspin and arc → "Bend the wrist back until the skin wrinkles" [S5, p.15]

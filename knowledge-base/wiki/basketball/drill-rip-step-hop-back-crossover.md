# Rip Step Hop Back / Roll Out / Crossover Shooting Drill Series

## Objective
Develop the ability to use a rip step to create separation, hop back for a three-pointer, and transition into a roll-out or crossover dribble to attack from a different angle — all from the same offensive read.

## Setup
- 1 player, half court
- Ball required; rebounder optional (player retrieves own shots)
- Shooting starts from three-point or mid-range zone on the wing or top of key
- Free throws taken between every segment at the free-throw line

## Execution

### Workout 2-19 (40-Shot Version)
1. **Rip Step 1 Dribble Hop Back to Crossover Dribble (Left)** — 5 reps: Catch (or self-initiate), rip through the ball, take 1 dribble, hop back to the 3-point line for the shot; if no shot, crossover and attack left.
2. **Free Throws** — 5 reps
3. **Rip Step 1 Dribble Hop Back to Crossover Dribble (Right)** — 5 reps: Same action going right.
4. **Free Throws** — 5 reps
5. **Rip Step 2 Dribble Hop Back to Crossover Dribble (Left)** — 5 reps: Take 2 dribbles before hopping back and crossing over.
6. **Free Throws** — 5 reps
7. **Rip Step 2 Dribble Hop Back to Crossover Dribble (Right)** — 5 reps
8. **Free Throws** — 5 reps
- **Total shots: 40** (FG target: 60%; FT target: 85%) [S3, pp.1-2]

### Workout 2-20 (80-Shot Version)
Double each segment to 10 reps; all other structure identical to 2-19.
- **Total shots: 80** (FG: 24-40 at 60%; FT: 34-40 at 85%) [S3, p.3]

### Workout 2-21 (120-Shot Version)
Double segment reps to 20 per action (FT segments stay at 10).
- **Total shots: 120** (FG: 48-80 at 60%; FT: 34-40 at 85%) [S3, pp.4-5]

## Coaching Points
- **Rip through aggressively** — the rip step must be explosive through the defender's outstretched arm, not a soft transfer.
- **Hop back is a full two-foot landing** — land balanced and in shooting position; do not fade or lean.
- **Read before the crossover** — the hop back is the primary option; only crossover if the defense takes away the hop-back shot.
- **Shoot at game speed** — these workouts have no value if performed at half-speed; every rep should simulate a live-game action.
- **Track your makes and misses** — use the workout log table to chart FGA, FGM, FTA, and FTM for every session.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout 2-19, 40 shots)**: Low reps, focus on footwork — ensure the rip, hop, and landing are technically sound before volume increases.
2. **Intermediate (Workout 2-20, 80 shots)**: Double the reps at the same technical standard; add a live passer to simulate game rhythm.
3. **Advanced (Workout 2-21, 120 shots)**: High-volume game-speed execution; add a passive defender on the initial rip to force a realistic reaction.

# Donkey Kick

## Objective
Explosively fire the spine and posterior chain by jumping the legs upward and fully extending them in mid-air from a quadruped (hands and feet) starting position — a useful warm-up or ancillary drill for movements requiring quick, powerful spinal muscles.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Small flat space (no equipment needed)
- Starting position: supported on palms and feet, arms locked straight, legs bent

## Execution
1. Start in a quadruped position: palms flat on the floor, arms locked, legs bent, hips up.
2. **Jump the legs upward explosively**, kicking them up and back like a donkey kicking.
3. In mid-air, **straighten the legs fully** at the peak of the kick.
4. Land softly with bent legs back in the starting position.
5. Repeat for desired reps [S9, p.1].

## Coaching Points
- This drill is specifically valuable because the **spine and posterior chain** do most of the work — not the abs/waist [S9, p.1]
- "Lock the arms" — the arms serve as a fixed base; the power comes entirely from the hip/spine extension
- "Fully extend in the air" — a partial kick does not activate the posterior chain maximally
- Useful as a **warm-up or supplemental drill** for athletes training back handsprings or any movement requiring explosive spinal extensors
- Watch for: athletes who only use hip flexion — the key is spinal extension driving the legs up

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Small kick, focus on locking arms and getting a feel for posterior chain firing.
2. **Intermediate**: Build height of the kick, achieve full leg extension in mid-air.
3. **Advanced**: Maximize kick height and speed; use as a warm-up for advanced explosive calisthenics [S9, p.1].

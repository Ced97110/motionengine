# Five Essential Jumping Skills

## Summary
Effective jump training must develop five distinct skills, not just jump height. These five skills — launching, tucking, landing, myotatic rebound, and blocking — are identified in *Explosive Calisthenics* as the complete framework for athletic jump ability. Athletes who only train jump height via box jumps will develop launching and landing but miss tucking, myotatic rebound, and blocking, which are critical for advanced athletic movements including basketball-specific actions.

For basketball players specifically, all five skills transfer directly to game performance: launching for rebounds and vertical elevation, landing safely for injury prevention, myotatic rebound for second-jump ability, tucking for contested shot clearance, and blocking for converting fast-break momentum into vertical jumping power.

## When to Use
- When designing jump training programs for basketball athletes
- When diagnosing why an athlete's vertical jump or landing mechanics are deficient
- As a teaching framework when coaching plyometric progressions

## Key Principles
1. **All five skills must be trained** — No single drill develops all five; a progressive chain is required [S9, p.7]
2. **Tucking is the most commonly neglected** — Even coaches who program launching and landing often skip tuck training [S9, p.9]
3. **Landing is as important as launching** — Faulty landing mechanics cause the majority of jump-related injuries [S9, p.8]
4. **Myotatic rebound is trainable** — Don't pause between reps; the stretch reflex is a skill that improves with practice [S9, p.8]
5. **Blocking is essential for momentum conversion** — Any athlete who accelerates horizontally and must jump vertically (fast break to layup, attacking the rim off a drive) uses blocking whether they know it or not

## The Five Skills in Detail

### Launching
- The ability to squat down and explode upward from the ground
- Keys: body symmetry, hips lead the descent ("sit down, not bow down"), total leg and hip power on extension
- Developed through high-volume thoughtful reps on basic squat jumps and vertical leaps
- Coaching cue: *"Lead with your hips, explode through the floor"* [S9, p.7]

### Tucking
- Pulling the knees up toward (and ultimately into) the chest during airtime
- Requires strong hip flexors
- Progresses from minimal knee lift (butt-kick jump) → waist-level (slap tuck) → chest-level (tuck jump) → full grip (catch tuck)
- So fundamental that gymnasts name the front and back flip the front and back *tuck* [S9, p.9]
- Coaching cue: *"Drive your knees to your chest, not your chest to your knees"*

### Landing
- Absorbing force symmetrically through the entire leg and body
- Keys: hip-led squat bend on impact, balls-of-feet first, NOT heels
- Beginners, overweight athletes, and older athletes often find landing the most difficult aspect
- Begin with small hops to condition soft tissues before progressing to high jumps [S9, p.8]
- Coaching cue: *"Catch on the balls of your feet, sit into the landing"*

### Myotatic Rebound
- The stretch reflex (myotatic reflex) causes stretched muscles to contract at precisely the right moment to support elastic tendon rebound
- On landing, if the athlete does NOT pause, the tendon elastic energy + myotatic reflex powers the next jump automatically
- This is the physiological basis of plyometric training
- Coaching cue: *"Don't pause — the moment you land, bounce right back up"* [S9, p.8]
- Also applies to small space drills and rhythmic repetition exercises

### Blocking
- The transformation of horizontal momentum into vertical momentum
- Executed by: taking a run-up → planting both feet together → "punching" down through the floor
- The foot-plant punches momentum into the floor, which redirects it upward
- Also exploits the myotatic rebound on the bilateral foot plant
- Essential prerequisite for front flips and for any sport action combining horizontal speed with vertical jumping [S9, p.9]
- Basketball application: fast break drive converting to a jump stop and vertical finish at the rim
- Coaching cue: *"Run, skip-plant both feet, punch the floor and fly up"*

## Common Mistakes
1. **Only training jump height** — Missing tucking, myotatic rebound, and blocking entirely
2. **Landing on stiff legs** — Correct: hip-led squat absorb; incorrect: straight-leg impact on heels
3. **Pausing between reps** — Eliminates the myotatic rebound benefit
4. **Neglecting hip flexors** — Weak hip flexors prevent proper tuck development
5. **Skipping blocking** — Athletes who never take a run-up before jumping miss this skill entirely

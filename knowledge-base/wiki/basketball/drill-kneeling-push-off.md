# Kneeling Push-Off (Power Pushup Step 2)

## Objective
Develop explosive upper-body pushing power from a kneeling position, bridging between incline pop-ups and full flat-floor explosive pushups.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Padded floor or mat (knees will contact floor)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Kneel with thighs and trunk upright; arms braced out in front
2. Lean forward until torso begins to fall — keep thighs, hips, and trunk aligned
3. Touch down with palms at approximately shoulder-width or slightly wider
4. Bend arms and shoulders, dipping torso closer to the floor (~1/3 depth; stronger athletes can go to the floor)
5. Immediately push explosively off the floor, straightening arms
6. Explode upward as hard as possible, maintaining thigh/hip/trunk alignment
7. Stop (absorb) when back at initial kneeling position
8. Repeat

## Coaching Points
- This is a **significant power jump** from Step 1 — strong athletes may fail on first attempt not from lack of strength but because they haven't learned to convert strength into speed [S9, p.86]
- "Don't bend at the hips" — hip bend makes it easier but eliminates the training effect; avoid it when possible [S9, p.87]
- Keep the body as one aligned unit throughout the fall and push
- The negative (falling) phase can be practiced alone first to get the feel of the movement
- TIP: bending at the hip will make getting vertical much easier — avoid it if you can [S9, p.87]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice negative phase only — fall forward onto hands; build feel for the technique
2. **Intermediate**: Full bilateral kneeling push-off; allow hip bend only as last resort
3. **Advanced**: Unilateral (one-arm) kneeling push-off — exceptional for building punching/passing force; foot-pivot version using toes instead of knees

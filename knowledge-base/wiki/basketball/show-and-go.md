# Show-and-Go

## Summary
The show-and-go is an offensive move that combines a shot fake ("show") with an immediate dribble drive ("go") in the direction the defender leaves open. It is the primary tool for creating a pull-up jump shot or drive to the basket after the defender has been taken out of position by the shot fake.

The move has two versions — strong-side and weak-side — defined by which hand is used for the dribble drive. The offensive player cannot predetermine direction; they must read the defender's closeout and react accordingly. Both versions must be mastered to be an effective scorer. [S5, pp.99-100]

## When to Use
- When a defender closes out aggressively and takes away the direct shot
- When attacking a set half-court defense after the shot fake triggers the defender's jump
- As the primary late-clock shot-creation tool when the defender is in good position
- When the pull-up bank shot or pull-up jumper is the target finish

## Key Principles
1. **Do not predetermine direction** — read the defender's closeout and go where they give you space; going the wrong direction leads to offensive fouls [S5, p.99]
2. **Stay low and in your shooting stance** — knees must remain bent after the shot fake so you can explode without reloading [S5, p.99]
3. **Push the dribble out in front, not to the side** — the drive must go north-to-south; pushing to the side allows the defense to recover [S5, p.99]
4. **Use the arm bar immediately** — the beaten defender will try to scramble back and poke at the ball; protect the dribble throughout the drive [S5, p.99]
5. **Jump stop to finish** — because you are attacking a set defense, help defenders will be ready; the jump stop prevents overpenetration and offensive fouls [S5, pp.99-100]
6. **On all weak-side drives, load the gun** — when driving with the weak hand, the shooter must bring the ball across the body and load the gun before elevating to maintain shot alignment [S5, p.100]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF**: Must master both strong-side and weak-side show-and-go from straight-on catches and on-the-move catches
- **PF/C**: Primarily use the show-and-go from mid-post catches where defenders may jump on a post shot fake

## Variations

### Strong-Side Show-and-Go
The player drives using the strong (shooting) hand. In a straight-on catch with a right-handed shooter:
- Receive with a one-two step (right foot = live foot, left foot = pivot)
- Show the ball (shot fake)
- Step with the right foot (live foot) **past** the defender
- Push the dribble out in front with the right hand
- Jump stop, then rise into the pull-up jump shot
- Always "go by" the defender, not "around" — stay tight to the defender's body [S5, p.99]

On-the-move catch variation (moving right to left, pivoting inside on the shooting foot):
- Catch and stub the toe to square up
- Shot fake, then use a crossover step with the left foot to seal the defender
- Dribble with the right hand, jump stop, shoot [S5, p.100]

### Weak-Side Show-and-Go
The player drives using the weak (non-shooting) hand when the defender closes out on the shooting side. Execution:
- After the shot fake, **rip the ball down and across to the opposite hip** (do not bring it down on the same side — too slow and exposes the ball)
- Use a crossover step with the live (shooting) foot across the body
- Dribble with the weak hand, push the ball out in front
- Jump stop, load the gun, rise into the pull-up jump shot
- "On all weak-side drives, you will have to load the gun" to maintain alignment [S5, p.100]

## Skill Progression
1. Develop strong-side show-and-go from a straight-on catch (one-two step)
2. Develop weak-side show-and-go from a straight-on catch
3. Progress both to on-the-move catches with inside pivot foot
4. Combine with [[bank-shot]] finish for compound skill

## Common Mistakes
1. **Predetermining direction** → Read the defender first; committing early results in offensive fouls [S5, p.99]
2. **Locking the knees after the shot fake** → Defender recovers while the player reloads; stay bent and explosive [S5, p.99]
3. **Bringing the ball down on the same side on a weak-side drive** → Exposes the ball; defender can knock it free → rip across to the opposite hip [S5, p.100]
4. **Dribbling to the side, not in front** → Lateral dribble slows the drive; always push the ball out in front of the body [S5, p.99]
5. **Overpenetrating without a jump stop** → Results in offensive fouls against set help defense → use the jump stop consistently [S5, p.99]

# Basic Three-Step Read Drill (#112)

## Objective
Teach offensive players to read help-side defensive spacing and alignment errors and execute the correct cut (V-cut, middle cut, or backdoor cut) to exploit those errors — the foundational weak-side reading drill for the motion offense.

## Setup
- **Players**: 3 (Player 1 with ball, Player 2 off-ball weak side, Defender X2 guarding 2)
- **Court area**: Half court; 1 on one perimeter spot, 2 on weak-side wing
- **Rotation**: 1 → 2 → X2 → 1
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Player 1 holds the ball. X2 guards Player 2 as a help-side defender. Player 2 moves up and down the weak side until X2 makes a coverage mistake.
2. **Proper X2 positioning**: Two-thirds of the distance from the ball to Player 2, one step off the straight line from ball to Player 2 in an interception stance.
3. **Three reads for Player 2**:
   - **Fig 1 — Proper spacing and alignment**: No attacking cut available. Player 2 uses a **V-cut to replace** themselves. This keeps X2 occupied and prevents strong weak-side help.
   - **Fig 2 — X2 too deep** (not on the straight line, below it): Player 2 **dips and middle cuts** for a layup. The dip compels X2 to drop deeper, opening the lane further.
   - **Fig 3 — X2 too high** (above the straight line): Player 2 **dips toward the high side** to encourage X2 higher, then plants the right foot and **sprints backdoor** for the pass and layup. Player 2 can signal a backdoor cut to the passer with a closed fist.
4. **Beginner phase**: Walk players through each of the three situations before adding live movement.
5. Players always rotate 1 → 2 → X2 → 1.

## Coaching Points
- Constantly watch defenders for stance, spacing, and alignment errors — correct immediately
- Attackers must take advantage of defensive mistakes immediately; hesitation kills the read
- The pass must lead the cutting receiver — do not throw into the cutter's body
- Ensure proper rebounding technique after every shot
- Any time a defender takes an eye off the attacker to look at the ball, the attacker must middle cut or backdoor cut
- "This drill is great for two-on-two play inside the rules of the motion offense" — easy to see and correct mistakes with few players

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through each of three defensive scenarios; 1 is stationary
2. **Intermediate**: Allow 1 to dribble up and down the frontcourt; 2 moves on the weak side; X2 must constantly reposition — once X2 makes a mistake, 2 immediately executes the appropriate cut. If X2 holds position, 1 can drive, triggering rotation rules.
3. **Advanced**: Add defender X1 on Player 1. Now strong-side (X1) and weak-side (X2) reads both apply. Players 1 and 2 must recognize when either defender is out of position and exploit it. Includes closeout reads if a defender is too close to the ball (two-thirds rule broken).

# Lying-Down Flip-Ball Drill

## Objective
Eliminate all lower-body and stance variables so players experience pure wrist/hand/arm alignment — the only way the ball can go straight up is if the shooting arm is perfectly aligned.

## Setup
- Players: individual or full team
- Court area: open floor — no basket needed
- Equipment: one basketball per player
- Players lie on the floor about 15 feet (4.5 m) apart

## Execution
1. Players lie on their backs on the floor
2. Each player holds a basketball in the shooting hand only
3. On coach's command, every player shoots the basketball **8 to 10 feet (2.4-3 m) straight up into the air** using the lift-extend-flip technique
4. The ball should be held directly in front of the face, with **elbows IN** — not out to the side
5. Players hold the ball only in the shooting hand; may catch the ball from its downward flight with both hands
6. Continue for 1 minute

## Coaching Points
- **"Elbow in"** — in the lying position it is easy to see whether the elbow flares out; the arm should be vertical, not angled sideways
- **"Straight up"** — the ball should go perfectly straight up; any sideways drift reveals alignment error on the side the ball drifts toward
- **Ball on the pads** — spread-and-relax to find the cup before each rep; if the ball is in the palm it will not go straight
- **"Wrinkle behind the wrist"** — check the wrist cock before every rep
- Use the tape test (tape around the center of the ball) to verify pure backspin vs. side spin

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: shoot up 8-10 feet and catch with both hands
2. **Intermediate**: shoot up and catch the return with only the shooting hand — requires perfect balance to keep the ball in the cup on the catch
3. **Advanced**: increase height to maximum; pair with the partner flip drill (Lift, Extend, Flip) immediately after to transfer the lying-down alignment feel to a standing position

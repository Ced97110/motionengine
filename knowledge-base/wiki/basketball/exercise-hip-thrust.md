# Hip Thrust

## Objective
Maximally recruit the gluteal muscles to build horizontal and vertical propulsion power for acceleration, jumping, cutting, deceleration, and safe landing mechanics.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1
- **Location:** weight room
- **Equipment:** secure padded bench or box, well-padded barbell
- **Safety:** The back of the shoulder should have firm support on the padded bench. The cervical spine (neck) should NOT be the main support during exercise. [S2, p.18]

## Execution
1. Sit with hips on the floor, both legs extended. Align the upper back across a secure padded bench or box. Position a well-padded barbell over the lower legs.
2. Lean forward to grab the barbell and, if plate size allows, roll the barbell over the thighs toward the hips. Position the bar symmetrically at the crease of the hips slightly above the pelvis.
3. Lean back to place the upper back and shoulders on the padded bench. Slide feet toward the buttocks, shoulder-width apart, knees flexed to 90°, tibia vertically positioned.
4. Lift the barbell off the floor by **extending the hips using the gluteal muscles** while simultaneously keeping the spine and hips in a neutral position (no excessive arching). The extension must come from the hips — NOT the low back.
5. Lower under control and repeat. [S2, pp.18-19]

## Coaching Points
- **"Drive through the hips, not the lower back"** — the back should remain neutral at the top of the movement.
- Shoulder blades stay on the bench; do not let the upper body slide.
- Knee angle of 90° and vertical tibia are critical for maximal glute activation.
- Start with bodyweight or light load to learn the position before adding barbell weight.
- Bar padding is essential to prevent hip-bone discomfort.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Bodyweight glute bridge on the floor (no bench, no bar).
2. **Intermediate:** Barbell hip thrust on bench at moderate load.
3. **Advanced:** Increase load; single-leg hip thrust for unilateral strength and balance.

## Basketball Application
The gluteal muscles significantly contribute to improving horizontal and vertical body propulsion, necessary for accelerating past and outjumping an opponent. These muscles are also crucial for safe and efficient landings, and contribute to breaking away from an opponent via enhanced deceleration and cutting. [S2, pp.18-19]

**Primary muscles:** gluteus maximus, hamstrings (semitendinosus, semimembranosus, biceps femoris), adductor magnus

**Secondary muscles:** gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, erector spinae, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius)

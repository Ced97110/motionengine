# Spin Screen Footwork

## Summary
The spin screen is an interior back-screen action triggered when the ball is re-reversed to the top of the key. The post player (04) makes a duck-in cut into the free-throw lane; if the defender denies it aggressively, the weak-side post (05) crosses the lane to set a blind headhunting back screen on the preoccupied defender. The cutter (04) fakes high, then spins low by reverse pivot off 05's shoulder and cuts to the opposite mid-post — creating a high-percentage catch-and-shoot or post-up opportunity with no true defensive helpside (ball at the top of the key). [S6, pp.241-244]

## When to Use
- Ball is re-reversed to the top of the key (03 → 02 re-reversal)
- The ballside post defender (X4) is overplaying or denying the duck-in cut
- The offense wants to exploit a post defender who is mentally locked onto one man
- When the ball sits at the top of the key and there is no true helpside

## Key Principles
1. **Duck-in read first**: 04 makes a 3-step duck-in cut (inside contact foot, outside foot, contact foot again) to probe the middle of the lane — if X4 allows it, 02 feeds 04 for a high-percentage shot. [S6, p.243]
2. **Blind headhunting screen**: If X4 denies the duck-in, 05 steps diagonally across the lane from a lower starting position to set a back screen on the unsuspecting X4 in the middle of the lane. [S6, p.241]
3. **Cutter fakes high, cuts low**: 04 always fakes a high cut before reverse pivoting using the contact foot as the pivot foot, spinning 180° off the low shoulder of 05, and cutting across the lane. [S6, pp.242-244]
4. **Spin cutter catch**: 04 should cut off the lower shoulder of 05, stay above the notch/block, and upon receiving the pass, take a small hop and land with both feet to read the defender and use either foot as the pivot foot. [S6, p.242]
5. **Screener becomes a threat**: After setting the screen, 05 front pivots off the top (left) foot, steps toward the ball at the top of the key, and is a second scoring option — especially if defenders switch. [S6, pp.242-243]
6. **Switch read**: If X5 switches to take 04, X4 must take 05. 05 uses a front crossover pivot off the top pivot foot to seal the new defender (X4) in the free-throw lane. [S6, p.243-244]
7. **No true helpside**: Ball at the top of the key means no definitive helpside defense for X4 — 04 is isolated in the middle of the lane. [S6, p.244]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF (04 — Spin Cutter)**: 3-step duck-in cut; if denied, fake high and reverse pivot spinning 180° off 05's low shoulder; cut across to the opposite mid-post; land with both feet on catch; use preferred post moves.
- **C (05 — Spin Screener)**: Start lower in the lane than 04; step diagonally across to set a blind back screen on X4; after the screen, front pivot off the top foot toward the ball; if defenders switch, seal the new defender with a front crossover pivot.
- **PG (01), SG (02), SF (03)**: All three perimeter players must maintain proper footwork as potential shooters and interior passers; ball at the top of the key provides the passing angle to 04 on the opposite side.

## Variations
### Spin Screen — No Switch (Standard)
04 cuts off the low left shoulder of 05, receives the pass from 01 at the opposite mid-post, small hop landing on both feet, then attacks with preferred post moves. [S6, p.242]

### Spin Screen — Switch
X5 follows 04 cutting across; X4 picks up 05. 05 immediately executes a front crossover pivot (off the lower/right foot as free foot) to seal the new defender and post up in the free-throw lane. [S6, pp.243-244]

### Headhunting Lane Exchange Variation
Instead of the spin screen, 05 sets a lane-exchange back screen on X4 as 04 makes his duck-in cut. Combined with 05's proper angle, this screen frees both 04 and 05 in the lane simultaneously. [S6, p.241]

## Common Mistakes
1. **04 cuts straight without faking** → Must fake high first so X4 cannot anticipate the low spin direction; without the fake, the cut is predictable and X4 can fight over.
2. **05 starts too high** → 05 must start lower than 04 before crossing; if he starts too high, the screen angle is poor and X4 sees it coming.
3. **04 catches below the block** → Must stay above the notch/block on the catch to maximize the attack angle to the basket.
4. **04 catches with only one foot planted** → Should take a small hop and land with both feet to read the defender and preserve either pivot foot as an option.
5. **Perimeter players relax** → All three perimeter players must be shot-ready with proper footwork on every pass; they are not just spectators.

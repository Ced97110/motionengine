# Back Extension (Core Extension)

## Objective
Strengthen the posterior chain (erector spinae, glutes, hamstrings) to support jumping, defensive positioning, and back injury prevention in basketball.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Hyperextension machine (Roman chair) — or Physioball/pillows for machine-free variation
- Optional: weight plate, dumbbells, or elastic bands for advanced loading

## Execution
1. Position yourself prone on the hyperextension machine, feet secured under the foot pads, fronts of thighs resting on the main support pad.
2. Hips should be flexed forward at a 90-degree angle with the upper body hanging straight down. Hands behind the head or crossed in front of the body.
3. **Safety Tip: Do not go beyond parallel to the floor or arch the spine.**
4. Slowly raise the trunk while maintaining a straight spine. All motion occurs at the hip — rise until the torso is parallel to the floor (hip angle = 180°).
5. Pause for 1-2 seconds at the top position for added difficulty.
6. Lower the torso in a controlled manner back to the starting position.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.38-39]

## Coaching Points
- **"Hip hinge only"** — all motion comes from the hip joint; the upper torso stays stable and rigid
- Do not go beyond parallel — hyperextension of the spine places dangerous stress on the vertebrae; the exercise name is a misnomer
- Keep the motion fluid, slow, and controlled — no rocking or swinging
- This is properly called "back extension" not "hyperextension" because movement goes from flexion to neutral lumbar, not beyond [S2, p.39]
- Watch for players using momentum — if they swing up, reduce the range of motion

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Body weight only, partial range; pause at top for 1 second
2. **Intermediate**: Full range of motion; 3 sets of 10-12 reps
3. **Advanced — Weighted Back Extension**: Hold a weight plate or dumbbells against the chest; alternatively, loop elastic bands from the machine around neck and shoulders for progressive resistance [S2, p.39]
4. **Machine-Free**: Perform prone over a Physioball with feet secured against a wall, or lying on pillows — valid variation but may not allow full range of motion compared to a Roman chair [S2, p.39]

### Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), gluteus maximus, hamstrings (biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus)
- **Secondary**: Transversospinalis, transversus abdominis, trapezius, rhomboids, gastrocnemius, soleus [S2, p.39]

# Pull-Up (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop upper-back strength and power — particularly the latissimus dorsi — to improve rebounding, defensive positioning, running momentum, and overall court physicality.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Pull-up bar (squat rack cross bar, doorframe bar, or dedicated pull-up station)
- Optional: heavy elastic resistance band for assisted variation

## Execution
1. Grip the pull-up bar in an **overhand grip**, palms facing forward, arms fully extended — body hanging.
2. Slightly bend the knees and cross the ankles to stabilize the lower body.
3. Pull the body upward, keeping elbows in line with the body, until the **chest reaches bar level**.
4. Slowly lower back to the full hanging (arms fully extended) starting position — controlled descent.
5. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Keep your elbows tracking in line with your body — no flaring out."
- Control the descent — the lowering phase builds as much strength as the pull.
- Do not swing or kip — maintain a stable, straight body position.
- Beginners: start with a **narrow grip** to recruit secondary muscles for assistance; wide grip is the most advanced and targets the broadest range of back muscles.
- "Squeeze the shoulder blades together at the top of the pull."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Band-Assisted Pull-Up — loop a heavy elastic band around the bar, place one or both knees in the band loop to reduce bodyweight load; perform prescribed reps.
2. **Intermediate**: Standard overhand (pronated) pull-up with shoulder-width grip.
3. **Advanced**: Wide-grip pull-up (targets majority of back muscles); or chin-up (palms facing you — supinated grip) for increased biceps brachii involvement; or alternating grip (one palm forward, one palm toward you).

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Latissimus dorsi
- **Secondary**: Lower trapezius, rhomboid major, rhomboid minor, teres major, biceps brachii, brachialis

## Basketball Focus
The pull-up is one of the most demanding bodyweight exercises for basketball players, especially at the middle- and high-school levels. A strong back generated by pull-up training directly assists with:
- Going up for **rebounds** against physical opponents
- **Defending** opponents in contested positions under the basket
- Generating **running momentum** down the court

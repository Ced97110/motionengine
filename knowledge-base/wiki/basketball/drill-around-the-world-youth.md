# Around the World

## Objective
Build shooting accuracy from multiple court locations using a sequential make-to-advance format that creates competitive pressure similar to game situations.

## Setup
- Players: 1 or multiple; team competition variants available
- Court area: half court — paint area through three-point line
- Equipment: one basketball per group
- Duration: 10 minutes

## Execution
**Little Around the World (Beginner) — 7 spots:**
1. Big block, left side of basket
2. Second lane line, left side of basket
3. Corner of free-throw line and lane line, left side
4. Middle of free-throw line
5. Corner of free-throw line and lane line, right side
6. Second lane line, right side of basket
7. Big block, right side of basket

Player must MAKE the shot at Spot 1 before advancing to Spot 2, and so on all the way around. When reaching Spot 7, coach may stop or require the player to go back in reverse order (7 → 1). First player to complete all spots wins.

**Big Around the World (Advanced) — 5 additional three-point spots:**
8. Right corner (3-point line)
9. 45° angle right (3-point line)
10. Top of key (3-point line)
11. 45° angle left (3-point line)
12. Left corner (3-point line)

## Coaching Points
- Enforce straight-stick alignment on every shot — this drill is not about speed; it is about applying correct mechanics from multiple angles
- Watch for players rushing and breaking mechanics to advance; emphasize accuracy over speed
- The two baseline spots (1 and 7 in the intermediate competition variant) must ALWAYS be made on the first try — no "chance" available at those spots
- Use this drill as a diagnostic: which spots does each player struggle with? Those are the spots to train

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Little Around the World, 7 spots — must make each shot before advancing
2. **Intermediate — Chance Rule**: players may call "chance" on any spot except 1 and 7; if the chance shot is made, advance; if missed, return to Spot 1
3. **Intermediate — Team Competition**: Team A at one end, Team B at the other; team players rotate shooting spots; first team through wins
4. **Intermediate — Player Race**: one player per team races to go around the world; first player to finish wins for their team
5. **Intermediate — Assigned Spots**: each of the five players on a team gets one spot; shoot in order; first team with all five players making their spot wins
6. **Advanced**: combine Little and Big Around the World — all 12 spots; bank shots required on all angle shots except the straight-on middle (spot 4 and spot 10)

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-straight-stick-shooting]] — all shots should apply the straight-stick technique; this drill reinforces it from multiple locations
- [[concept-shot-charting-and-evaluation]] — coaches can use results from Around the World to identify each player's weak spots

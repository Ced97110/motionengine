# Hop-Scotch With Reaction (Whole-Body Reaction)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, court sense, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, total-body agility, general athleticism, and elastic strength in the ankle complex. [S8, p.217]

## Setup
- 1 player, agility ladder
- Coach positioned at the side or end of the ladder
- No additional equipment required

## Execution
1. Start with **both feet inside the first square** of the ladder.
2. Jump and land with each foot **outside** the ladder (straddle position).
3. Immediately upon landing outside, jump again and land with **both feet inside the next square** of the ladder.
4. Repeat this in-out (hop-scotch) pattern through the entire ladder.
5. **Simultaneously**, react to the coach's visual commands (e.g., number of fingers) throughout the drill.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Hop-scotch pattern shown on p.217 — both feet in square, then both feet straddle outside, alternating through ladder -->

## Coaching Points
- Minimal ground contact time — "light as a feather" landings
- Keep body upright and core engaged throughout
- The ankle complex does the work on each jump — avoid excessive knee bend
- Divide attention: footwork is automatic, eyes are on the coach
- "Quick feet, soft landings, eyes on me!"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master hop-scotch footwork without reaction cues
2. **Intermediate**: Add coach finger-count visual reaction
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Odd/even number filtering
   - Coach semicircle for peripheral vision development
   - Two or three coaches flashing numbers around the periphery
   - Add 2–3 step "run in" before the ladder [S8, p.217]
   - Add a sport-specific skill at the end of the ladder

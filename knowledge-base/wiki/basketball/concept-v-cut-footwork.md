# V-Cut Footwork

## Summary
The V-cut is the fundamental off-ball movement used to set up a defender before using a screen. Critically, a V-cut is *not* simply a single jab step — it is a multi-step sequence (ideally 2-3 steps) designed to convincingly sell the defender that the offensive player is going one direction, before abruptly changing direction to come off a screen and receive a pass.

The V-cut is the essential prerequisite to all off-ball screen cuts. Without a convincing V-cut, defenders can anticipate the cutter's direction and fight through or around screens, eliminating the shot opportunity. [S6, p.146]

## When to Use
- Before using any type of off-ball screen: down screen, flare screen, back screen, stagger screen, double screen
- Any time an off-ball player wants to create separation from their defender before receiving a pass
- As a baseline move to get open in any half-court offense

## Key Principles
1. **Multiple steps, not a single jab**: Three steps constitute a proper V-cut — enough to sell the defender that the cutter is going the wrong way.
2. **Inside foot plants at the direction change**: The inside foot (foot closest to the center/basket in relation to the direction of travel) is planted to stop momentum and initiate the new direction.
3. **Sell the initial direction**: The first 1-2 steps of the V-cut must look exactly like the player is going away from where they actually want to go. The defender must commit.
4. **Explosion off the planted inside foot**: After planting, the cutter pushes explosively off the inside foot and takes the first step in the new direction with the outside foot, scraping off the screen.
5. **Get feet and hands ready during the cut**: As the cutter transitions from the fake direction to the receiving direction, they begin positioning their feet for the catch pivot and raising their hands into the shooting pocket.

## Execution by Body Orientation

### Cutter with Back to Basket (e.g., coming off a down screen)
- Initial inside foot is the foot closer to the basket (relative to initial position)
- Example — left side of court, back to basket: **left foot is inside foot**
- V-cut: step toward lane (2-3 steps), plant **inside (left) foot**, step with outside (right) foot to scrape off screen
- On catch: pivot off **inside (left) heel** to square up facing basket

### Cutter Facing Basket (e.g., coming off a flare screen)
- Initial inside foot is the foot closer to the lane/center of court
- Example — left side of court, facing basket: **right foot is inside foot**
- V-cut: step toward lane, plant **inside (right) foot**, step with outside (left) foot to scrape off flare screen toward perimeter
- On catch: pivot off **inside (right) heel**, swing free foot to square up

## Common Mistakes
1. **V-cut is only one step (single jab)** → Defender doesn't commit; fights through screen → Teach 3-step V-cuts at game speed
2. **Outside foot plants instead of inside foot** → Rotation after the plant is slow and awkward → Drill the inside-foot plant until it is automatic
3. **Not going all the way in the fake direction** → Defender can tell it's fake → "Go far enough that your defender has to turn their hips"
4. **Cutting slowly off the screen after the V-cut** → Defender recovers → "Explode off the plant — your first step out of the V should be your fastest"
5. **Hands still at the side when the cut completes** → Must reach up and meet the pass in the shooting pocket → Start raising hands as the last step of the V-cut is planted

# Balance-Hand Form Shooting Drill

## Objective
Maintain proper shooting arm alignment while integrating the balance hand, ensuring it supports without interfering with the shot.

## Setup
- 1 shooter; 1 partner or coach (optional but recommended to check positioning)
- Any basket, starting directly in front of the rim
- 1 basketball
- Begin 2 feet (0.6 m) from the basket

## Execution
1. Assume proper shooting stance
2. Grip the ball in the shooting hand — fingertips, palm off, seamed correctly, L-shaped elbow
3. Add the balance hand to the **side** of the ball; fingertips/fingerpads only, palm off the ball; thumbs form a letter T
4. **Before shooting**, have a partner or coach check that:
   - Shooting elbow is in and above the shooting foot
   - Shooting hand remains centered under the ball (hasn't drifted to the side)
   - Balance hand is on the side, not in front of or on top of the ball
5. Shoot until you make **5 shots** from 2 feet
6. Take a half-step back and make 5 more
7. Continue stepping back until form breaks down
8. Goal: **10 spots**, **50 total makes**

## Coaching Points
- This drill is a direct continuation of [[shooting-hand-form-shooting-drill]] — master one-handed form first [S5, p.19]
- The most common error when adding the balance hand: the shooter unconsciously shifts the **shooting hand to the side** of the ball, ending up with a two-handed shot; the shooting hand must stay centered under the ball [S5, p.18]
- Watch that the balance hand does not push or guide the ball — it should fall away cleanly at release
- "The balance hand **balances and lifts** — it does NOT shoot or guide the ball" [S5, p.18]
- Avoid the terms 'off-hand' or 'nonshooting hand' — use "balance hand" to reinforce its positive, supportive role
- Same progressions as shooting-hand form shooting: straight-on, angled, around the world, or strings style [S5, p.19]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Straight-on, make 5 at each half-step spot with coach checking hand position before each shot
2. **Intermediate**: Work different angles — wing, baseline — make 5 per spot without partner check
3. **Advanced — Strings**: Shoot consecutively until a miss; demands focus and reveals mechanical inconsistencies under concentration pressure

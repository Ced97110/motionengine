# Dribble Curl Shot

## Summary
The dribble curl is the live-ball extension of the [[concept-curl-shot-off-screen]]. When the defender trails the curl cut and is positioned directly behind the cutter (rather than to one side), the shot at the elbow is contested — so the cutter does not pull up but instead continues the curl momentum with a dribble into the middle of the paint and performs any available scoring opportunity. [S3, pp.171-179]

It is similar to the [[concept-dribble-fill-shot]], except the cutter is already in a curl-cut trajectory attacking the basket, giving it more momentum and a tighter scoring angle.

## When to Use
- Defender trails the screen and is directly behind the cutter (not to the side)
- Elbow shot is contested by a trailing defender
- Curl cut has built enough momentum to attack the paint directly
- Anytime a trailing defender cannot recover in time to prevent a paint attack

## Key Principles
1. **Read defender position on the curl** — if defender is directly behind, don't stop; continue the attack
2. **Transition curl momentum into dribble attack** — the cutting movement converts directly to a dribble without a hesitation or reset
3. **Attack the middle of the paint** — target the paint, not the baseline or the wing
4. **Use any scoring opportunity** — floater, layup, pull-up, or draw the foul at the rim
5. **Do not reset or stop at the elbow** — stopping gives the trailing defender time to recover
6. **The dribble curl is more momentum-forward than the dribble fill** — the cutter is already attacking the basket, not reacting from a catch-and-pause position [S3, p.171]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF (cutter)**: Curl tightly off screen, read defender trailing directly behind, continue curl with a dribble into the paint, finish or create
- **Screener**: Set a screen that forces the defender into a direct-trail position
- **Passer**: Read that the cutter is continuing the curl (not stopping) and delay the pass — or the cutter may receive after the first dribble inside the paint

## Variations
### Standard Dribble Curl (Elbow to Paint)
Curl past the elbow, dribble once or twice to the rim, and finish. [S3, p.171]

### Dribble Curl to Pull-Up
Curl momentum leads to a pull-up mid-range shot at the block or inside the paint if help defense cuts off the rim.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Stopping at the elbow to shoot when defender is behind** → contested shot; if they're trailing, keep going
2. **Dribbling too many times** → allows help defense to collapse; attack decisively in 1–2 dribbles
3. **Going wide around the defender** → losing the momentum advantage of the curl; stay on the direct attack line to the basket
4. **Not reading passer timing** → trying to catch the ball and then dribble is slower; ideally catch just inside the paint or attack with live dribble before catching
5. **Confusing dribble curl with dribble fill** → dribble fill starts from a caught-ball-on-wing position; dribble curl starts from within the curl cut trajectory — more momentum, tighter angle

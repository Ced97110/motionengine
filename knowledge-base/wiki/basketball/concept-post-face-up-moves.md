# Post Face-Up Moves and Shots — an iconic post player

## Summary
an iconic post player's complete system for post players who face the basket off the low or high post, covering both the front pivot (baseline or middle) and the reverse pivot (away from the basket toward space). These moves require decent ball-handling skills and complete body control. The post must master three dribble types when facing the basket: crossover, spin (reverse dribble), and step-back. Do not dribble more than twice before shooting — the defense will collapse. [S7, pp.47-50]

## When to Use
- When no drop step or spin is available from back-to-basket position
- When the defender is completely behind the post and cannot be read for a drop step
- When facing a defender who backs off (jump shot opportunity) or stays close (drive/step-back)

## Key Principles
1. **Use a front or reverse pivot to face the basket** — these are the initiating actions for all face-up moves.
2. **Stay low on the knees throughout the entire pivot** — standing up destroys balance and power.
3. **Head near perpendicular to the floor** — head forward = body forward = imbalance.
4. **Ball at side at chest level, wrist bent behind** — ready position for any action.
5. **Eyes on the rim** — always focus on the target.
6. **No more than two dribbles** before shooting — more invites the defense to collapse.
7. **Master the three face-up dribbles:** crossover, spin (reverse), and step-back.

## Player Responsibilities
- **C/PF**: Develop ball-handling skills specifically for face-up situations; read whether the defender backs off (shoot) or stays close (drive/step-back/up-and-under).

## Variations

### Low-Post Front Pivot
Used when the defender is completely behind the post with no opportunity for a drop step or spin. Two directions: toward the middle or toward the baseline.

**Setup:** Fake the ball slightly over the shoulder nearest midcourt (to go baseline) or the opposite shoulder (to go middle). Turn head to watch defender's feet to sell the fake. Use the foot nearest the baseline as the pivot foot; touch heel first and roll on it to quickly face the basket. Start the pivot while making the ball fake — faster and stays closer to the basket.

**Shots off front pivot:**
- **Jump shot:** If defender falls for the fake and gives space. Legs bent throughout. Ball at forehead level, shooting hand behind ball, both feet toward basket. Release immediately. Use backboard if front-pivoting to the baseline.
- **Up and under:** If defender recovers and is between post and basket. Step into lane long enough to beat the defender, finish with the opposite hand (left hand if on the right side, right hand if on the left side).
- **Reverse and shoot:** Start with ball-and-head fake, front pivot — when defender recovers and closes that direction, reverse pivot to the opposite direction and shoot a hook.

### Low-Post Reverse Pivot
pro defenders commonly stay between the basket and the post, pushing with a forearm against the post's back and hip.

**Setup:** Back parallel to baseline, inside forearm (nearest the lane) bent 45° against defender's chest. Ask for ball with other hand. After receiving: face the basket and pivot AWAY from it on the foot furthest from the defender to create space. Heel touches floor first, roll to forefoot for drive or jump shot.

**Shots off reverse pivot:**
- **Jump shot:** If defender backs off to prevent the drive. Keep ball high. Shot fake is advantageous here.
- **Up and under:** If defender stays between post and basket and tries to block — fake, step under.
- **Rip the ball and drive:** If space exists to the rim (baseline or middle): rip ball from one side to the other, below knee level; long step with the leg opposite to the ball over the defender's hip. Dribble to avoid traveling, protect ball with nondribbling forearm at hip level. Finish with dunk or power shot.
- **Crossover step:** If defender has a leg forward and closes space for straight drive: crossover step + simultaneous dribble. Rip ball from one side to other, below knee level. Step and dribble over defender's forward leg. Toes of nonpivot foot must point toward basket. Bounce ball over defender's hip. Protect with nondribbling hand bent at defender's hip.
- **Drive and spin dribble:** If defender recovers after first drive and cuts off the path: spin dribble (reverse dribble) and go the opposite direction. Velocity of the spin dribble must be very fast so ball comes up quickly for a catch-and-shoot.
- **Drive, step-back dribble, and shot or crossover:** Master step-back first. If defender recovers after straight drive: step-back dribble to create space (hop backward, push off nonpivot foot, bring ball back with same hand) — then shoot or crossover dribble to basket.
- **reverse-pivot post move:** Named for an elite post player (7-time All-Star, a pro team/a pro team). After receiving pass with back to basket: keep ball at forehead level. Using either foot as pivot, take a step forward, turn to face basket, simultaneously bring ball overhead and shoot a jump shot. Nearly impossible to block — the ball appears to go behind the head before release. [S7, p.48]

### High-Post Front and Reverse Pivot
Same shots as low post, with these notes:
- **Front pivot + jump shot:** If space is immediately available or after fake + pivot.
- **Front pivot + up-and-under:** A dribble may be necessary to get to the rim.
- **Sweep the ball and drive:** Same technique as low post, but dribble and step must be longer due to distance.
- **Crossover dribble:** High post must dribble only ONCE to reach the basket — avoid getting caught by rotating defenders.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Head forward during pivot** → Body tilts forward, causes imbalance. Keep head perpendicular.
2. **Dribbling more than twice before shooting** → Defense collapses. One or two dribbles maximum.
3. **Not staying low on knees throughout the pivot** → Loses power, defender can push you off.
4. **Crossover step with nonpivot foot toes pointing away from basket** → Body goes the wrong direction; toes must point toward the basket.
5. **Spin dribble too slow** → Ball comes up slowly, defender recovers. Spin dribble velocity must be maximum.

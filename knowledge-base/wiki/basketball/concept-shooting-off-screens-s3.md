# Shooting Off a Screen — Cutter & Screener Principles

## Summary
Shooting off a screen is described as "probably one of the most difficult things to learn and develop in basketball." It requires communication between teammates, elusiveness from the cutter, and precise tempo. When executed correctly, screens force defenders to fight through stationary players, allowing the cutter to receive a clean catch for a shot or create a closeout at a bad angle. [S3]

The key insight is that success depends as much on what happens **before** the screen as after it. The cutter must set the defender up, wait for the screen, and read the defensive response to make the correct cut decision (curl, fill, or fade). The screener must verbalize, hold position, and react to the cutter's call.

## When to Use
- Any time a set play or motion offense incorporates a down screen, flare screen, or cross screen
- When a shooter needs to create separation from a ball-denying or trail defender
- In late-game ATO (after timeout) situations where a shot for the best shooter must be created
- In motion offense when the ball handler is scanning for a catch-and-shoot opportunity for a teammate

## Key Principles

### Cutter Principles [S3]
1. **Set the defender up**: Make the defender think you are going one direction before cutting the other
2. **Watch the defender, not the ball**: "The ball will find you" — reading the defender's position determines the cut
3. **Take your time and wait for the screen**: Don't rush into the screen; let it be set first
4. **Come off the screen shoulder-to-shoulder**: Tight cuts eliminate space for the defender to recover
5. **Curl, fill, or fade based on how the defense played the screen**: The cut is a read, not predetermined
6. **Verbalize the cut**: Say "curl," "fill," or "fade" so the screener and ball handler both know where you're going
7. **Catch ready to shoot**: If no shot is available on the catch, immediately establish a dominant pivot foot and be ready to play

### Screener Principles [S3]
1. **Verbalize to the cutter that a screen is coming**: Call "Use me" to alert the cutter
2. **Get a wide base and set a stationary screen**: The cutter comes off the screener — the screener cannot move into the defender
3. **React to what the cutter verbalizes**: After the screen, the screener reads the cutter's call and prepares to re-engage (duck-in, pop, or roll based on the defense)

## Cut Decisions
### Curl
If the defender trails the cutter (chases from behind), the cutter curls tight around the screen and attacks the basket for a layup or close shot.

### Fill
If the defender goes under the screen, the cutter fills straight to the open space (perimeter) for a catch-and-shoot jumper.

### Fade
If the defender tries to go over the screen or shows early, the cutter fades away from the screen to create space for a longer catch-and-shoot shot (15–20 feet). [S3]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Primary passer — reads the cutter's verbal call and delivers the pass at the right moment; timing the pass is critical
- **SG/SF**: Primary cutter — executes the setup, cut, and catch-ready position
- **PF/C**: Primary screener — sets wide, stationary screens and reacts after the screen is used

## Common Mistakes
1. **Cutter rushing the screen** → Take your time; a late, properly timed cut is more effective than an early, crowded one
2. **Watching the ball instead of the defender** → Eyes on the defender determine your cut; the passer will find you
3. **Loose cut off the screen** → Come shoulder-to-shoulder with the screener; any gap lets the defender recover
4. **Screener moving before the catch** → Hold the screen until the cutter uses it; early movement is a foul
5. **No verbal communication** → If the cutter doesn't call curl/fill/fade, the screener can't react and the ball handler can't time the pass
6. **Catching with no pivot foot established** → Catch ready to shoot; if no shot, immediately establish a dominant pivot foot [S3]

## Variations
### Down Screen
Screener comes from the baseline to screen for a player above — cutter reads defender and curls/fills/fades.

### Flare Screen
Screener sets a screen away from the ball on the perimeter — cutter fades to the three-point line.

### Cross Screen
Screener moves across the lane to free a post player — more applicable to inside scoring.

# Wave Good-Bye Drill

## Objective
Isolate correct wrist mechanics and hand position to build the foundational shooting cup and follow-through motion before any ball work.

## Setup
- Players: entire team or any number
- Court area: open floor — no basket needed
- Equipment: none (ball added as optional progression)
- Spread players 15 feet (4.5 m) apart facing the coach

## Execution
1. Players hold their shooting hand up and spread their fingers as wide as possible until the hand hurts, then relax — the position the hand naturally falls into IS the proper shooting cup
2. Players hold the arm in front of the face, upper arm parallel to the floor, lower arm perpendicular to the upper arm (L-shape)
3. Coach gives command: **"Cock the wrist"** — players cock the wrist backward until a visible wrinkle appears behind the wrist; the wrist should be parallel to the ground; hold until coach confirms all players have the wrinkle
4. Coach gives command: **"Wave good-bye"** — players wave their shooting hand goodbye and HOLD the position
5. Check the finished position: wrist should be straight in line with the lower and upper arm; arm extended toward the coach; fingers pointing DOWN toward the floor
6. Repeat the two-step sequence (cock → wave) until all players execute it correctly and consistently

## Coaching Points
- "Spread and relax to find your cup" — the natural relaxed position after spreading is exactly the shooting cup; don't overthink it
- "I want to see a wrinkle" — use this cue to check wrist cocking; no wrinkle = no cock = ball rolls out on release
- After waving goodbye, fingers should point to the floor, not up or sideways
- Upper arm parallel to the floor; lower arm perpendicular — check the L-shape on every rep
- Do NOT move on until ALL players have correct positioning

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: no ball — pure wrist and arm position work as described above
2. **Intermediate**: add a ball; players hold ball in the shooting hand only on the pads/fingertips and perform the full wave sequence, checking that the ball stays balanced
3. **Advanced**: combine with the Flip-Ball Drill — cock, wave, and lightly flip the ball 2-3 feet into the air using only the wrist snap

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-straight-stick-shooting]] — the wrist cock and wave-goodbye are the foundation of straight-stick alignment and the lift-extend-flip sequence
- [[shooting-follow-through]] — the frozen follow-through position (fingers down, palm facing floor) is what players hold at the end of this drill

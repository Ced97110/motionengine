# YTW Shoulder Strengthening

## Objective
Strengthen scapular stabilizer muscles to facilitate proper muscle firing patterns for shooting, defending, and blocking.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: Physioball (stability ball); optional hand weights for advanced

## Execution
1. Lie face-down (prone) on a Physioball with your arms at your sides and shoulder blades pinched together.
2. **Y position:** Lift arms straight up and diagonally toward your head, forming a Y shape. Return to starting position.
3. **T position:** Lift arms straight out to the sides, forming a T shape. Return to starting position.
4. **W position:** Lift arms out to the sides with elbows bent, then rotate arms upward (thumbs up) to form a W shape. Return to starting position.
5. Alternate Y, T, and W positions for 3 sets of 10 repetitions each.
6. **Advanced:** Add hand weights to increase resistance.

## Coaching Points
- "Pinch the shoulder blades before you lift" — scapular retraction must precede arm elevation
- Keep the core braced on the Physioball to stabilize the trunk during arm movements
- Move slowly and with control — this is not a speed exercise
- The W position specifically trains external rotation — key for rotator cuff health in shooters

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Bodyweight only, 3 × 10 each position
2. **Intermediate:** Add 2–5 lb hand weights
3. **Advanced:** Increase weight progressively

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Secondary:** Biceps brachii, posterior deltoid, lateral deltoid, multifidus, erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), transversus abdominis

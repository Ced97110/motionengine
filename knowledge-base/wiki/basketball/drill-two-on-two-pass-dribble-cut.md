# Two-on-Two Pass, Dribble, and Cut (Drill 62)

## Objective
Teach offensive players to recognize **when** to make each type of cut (middle, backdoor, V-cut) by having the defense simulate the specific coverage that triggers each cut, then combining all reads in a live 2-on-2 setting.

## Setup
- 2 offensive players, 2 defenders (X1, X2)
- Lines at top of key / wing area
- Half court
- Rotate: offense → defense → end of line after 1 minute
- Equipment: 1 basketball

## Execution

### Beginner (each cut run separately)

**Phase 1 — Middle Cut:**
1. Defenders drop away from their assignment rather than jumping toward the ball (signals the middle cut).
2. Player 2 dribbles into the frontcourt. Player 1 sees the defender sagging — **middle-cuts** between the passer and the nearest cone/defender.
3. Player 2 can hit Player 1 for a driving layup. Play restarts.
4. Player 2 passes to Player 1 on the wing; Player 2 middle-cuts; Player 1 attempts to hit Player 2 for a layup.
5. Continue for 1 minute — run only middle cuts in this phase.

**Phase 2 — Backdoor Cut:**
1. Defenders overplay between the ball and their assignment — exaggerate with front knee past the toes.
2. Player 2 dribbles into frontcourt. Player 1 reads the overplay and **backdoor-cuts**.
3. Triggers: defender overplays the passing lane; defender's front knee goes in front of the toes; defender takes eyes off the cutter to watch the ball handler.
4. Player 2 can hit Player 1 for a driving layup. Play restarts.
5. Player 2 passes to Player 1; Player 2 backdoor-cuts; Player 1 attempts to hit Player 2 for a layup.
6. If Player 2's cut is not rewarded, Player 2 cuts to short corner and X2 follows.

**Phase 3 — V-Cut:**
1. Defenders play proper denial defense — neither middle nor backdoor cuts are available.
2. Players must V-cut to reverse the ball and maintain spacing ("every player must move on every pass").
3. Player 2 dribbles into frontcourt. Player 1 V-cuts to replace herself; Player 2 hits Player 1 after she's back out to guard position.
4. Player 2 V-cuts toward the corner; Player 1 could hit Player 2 for a layup if X2 doesn't cover properly.

### Intermediate / Advanced
- Players create their own cuts based on how the defense plays — no assigned cut type
- Players do **not** need to verbalize their moves
- Player 1 can use fakes, rocker steps, and dribbling moves to drive to the basket, then kick back out
- Players may cut any time the ball moves by pass **or** dribble
- No structure imposed on either player's movement

## Coaching Points
- Run each cut type separately before combining them — players must recognize the cue, not guess
- Defenders in early phases should **exaggerate** the cue (sag deeper, overplay further) to make reads obvious
- "Players may cut any time the ball moves by pass or dribble" [S15, p.129] — cuts don't require a specific pass/receive event
- Reinforce all four motion offense rules through the teaching points after each rotation
- Watch that Player 1 doesn't backdoor cut when a middle cut was available — this is the most correctable error

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Each cut type in a separate 1-minute phase; defense scripted to trigger one cut
2. **Intermediate**: All cuts available; defense plays live; players read without verbalizing
3. **Advanced**: Full live 2-on-2; players use all dribbling moves, rocker steps, and pump fakes; no structure

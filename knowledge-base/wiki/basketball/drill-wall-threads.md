# Wall Threads (Small Space Drill)

## Objective
Develop rapid foot speed and threading coordination by keeping one foot elevated on a vertical surface while jumping over it repeatedly — a no-equipment bridge between thread jump training and the full suicide jump.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Equipment: any sturdy vertical base or object (wall, pillar, step edge, low railing) — must be stable and non-slip
- Small space — only needs a foot-width of lateral clearance

## Execution
1. Place one foot on a sturdy vertical base at a comfortable height.
2. With the standing leg, explosively jump laterally over the fixed foot, keeping the elevated foot on the base as long as possible.
3. Land on the opposite side of the base on the standing leg.
4. Immediately repeat in the opposite direction — jump back over the elevated foot.
5. Continue rhythmically for reps or time.

## Coaching Points
- "Awesome for fast feet" [S9, p.76] — the combination of a stable fixed foot and a rapidly moving jumping leg demands very rapid foot repositioning
- Keep the elevated foot in contact with the base for as long as possible during each jump — this increases the speed demand on the jumping foot
- Works well paired with Thread Jumps (Step 9) and Suicide Jumps (Master Step) — same movement vocabulary
- Can be used as a warm-up or finishing drill in any explosives session
- Zero equipment; can be done against any wall or sturdy object

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Low base height, small jump, focus on keeping foot on base
2. **Intermediate**: Standard height, full lateral jump clearance
3. **Advanced**: Increase height of the base; add a speed challenge (maximum reps in 30 seconds)

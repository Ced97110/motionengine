# Pick-and-Roll Defensive Coverages (Del Harris)

## Summary
Del Harris lays out a comprehensive menu of pick-and-roll (PnR) defensive coverage options used at the pro level. Rather than a single answer, the best coverage depends on personnel, score, game situation, and scouting. Harris identifies four primary coverages—trap, switch, overplay-down-to-baseline, and rotation adjustments—each with specific rotation rules for all five defenders. A team must commit to one system, drill it relentlessly, and communicate consistently to eliminate doubt during live game situations. [S7, pp.257-260]

## When to Use
- **Trap**: Against an elite ball-handler who can create off the dribble; most aggressive, highest risk/reward
- **Switch**: When equal-sized players are involved; critical late in game to prevent quick three-pointer; use with 4 or even all 5 defenders when protecting a lead with the shot clock under 7 seconds
- **Overplay-down-to-baseline**: Against wing and corner PnR; safer on the wing than at the top of the key
- Coverage choice also informed by the picker's shooting ability — if the picker is a strong shooter from 15-17 feet, a rotation is required to cover him on a pass

## Key Principles

### Trap Coverage
1. X5 (picker's defender) jumps out to trap the ball handler (1) as he comes over the pick
2. X4 rotates to cover the rolling 5 as he sees the ball released from 1 toward 5
3. X5 rotates opposite the pass into the paint and picks up the open man (normally 4)
4. X3 provides help for X5 until X5 can get there
5. If X5 is late, he covers 3 on the weak side
6. **Most aggressive version**: picker's defender traps BEFORE the pick is set — highest risk, forces early decisions

### Switch Coverage
1. Easiest form of help-and-recover; best when equal-sized players are in the PnR
2. Teach players to pass off mismatches quickly after a switch
3. When a big is stuck on the perimeter defending a quick-penetrating small (mismatch): a small player (X3) rotates over to replace the bigger teammate (X5)
4. The mismatched big rotates off to the weak side and picks up an open man
5. X2 rotates to 3, X1 picks up 2 on the mismatch coverage
6. **Big player rule**: a big must automatically trap DOWN for a small teammate being posted by an opposing big — "X4 traps down for X3 being posted"

### Overplay-Down-to-Baseline Coverage
1. Picker's defender (X5) calls the coverage as soon as he sees the pick being set: signal color or simply "Down!"
2. X5 positions 2-3 steps below the picker in the direction the ball must be pushed
3. Ball defender (X1) overplays severely, pushing the ball handler DOWN toward the baseline and into X5
4. Most effective on wing and corner PnR
5. Can be used at the top with less safety: X5 calls "Down right!" or "Down left!" and X1 gets on the HIGH side
6. **Critical rule**: X1 must maintain contact with the ball handler to prevent splitting the trap between X1 and X5
7. On a sideline PnR, decide in advance whether X5 recovers back to 5 on a pass to him, OR whether a different player rotates to 5 based on scouting (e.g., if 5 is a strong midrange shooter, X2 rotates to 5 instead)

## Player Responsibilities

### Ball Defender (X1)
- In trap: funnel ball handler into the trapping X5
- In switch: switch assignment cleanly; pass off mismatch quickly
- In overplay-down: overplay severely on high side; maintain contact to prevent splitting

### Off-Ball Defenders (X2, X3)
- X2: rotates to cover 3 when X5 is occupied with mismatch; or rotates to 5 if he's a strong shooter
- X3: provides interim help until X5 rotates to his assignment; covers weak-side open man

### Picker's Defender (X5)
- In trap: jumps to trap ball handler; rotates opposite the pass into the paint
- In switch: communicates "switch!"; big man traps DOWN if posted by bigger player
- In overplay-down: positions 2-3 steps below in desired direction; calls "Down!"

### X4
- In trap: rotates to cover the rolling post player (5) as he sees ball in the air
- In switch: if X5 doesn't recover, X4 rotates to cover 5 on the roll

## Variations

### Pre-Screen Trap
The most aggressive trap variation: picker's defender traps the ball handler BEFORE the pick is actually set. Forces the offense to react early but requires faster rotation coverage.

### Scouting-Informed Rotation
If scouting reveals the rolling big (5) is a strong shooter (15-17 ft), X2 is pre-assigned to rotate to 5 rather than X5 recovering. X5 then takes the weak-side open man. [S7, p.258]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Allowing the split** → X1 loses contact with the ball handler; maintain body contact to prevent splitting X1 and X5
2. **Late rotation on the roll** → X5 must read the pass in the air and rotate simultaneously, not after the catch
3. **No mismatch trap** → After a switch, big players must immediately trap down if a smaller teammate is being posted by a bigger opponent
4. **Inconsistent communication** → Coverages only work if the picker's defender CALLS the coverage FIRST; the ball defender reacts to the call

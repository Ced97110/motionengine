# Vertical Leap (Power Jump Chain Step 3)

## Objective
Coordinate lower and upper body for maximum jump height by adding aggressive arm swing to the squat jump — the foundation for measuring and tracking jump progress.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface (no equipment needed; optional: chalk mark on a wall for measuring reach height)
- Slightly wider than shoulder-width stance

## Execution
1. Stand with feet a little wider than shoulder-width, arms at sides, body flexed for action
2. Dip down into a squat — approximately one-third depth for maximum height; stronger athletes can go deeper
3. Explode upward using full lower-body power and total-body spring
4. As you lift off, swing your arms explosively upward above you — reach for maximum height
5. After take-off, let legs move naturally; do NOT pull them up or thrust them down
6. Land softly, catching most weight on the balls of the feet; allow arms to fall back to sides
7. Use the elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- **Arms are now part of the jump** — "The arms are the accelerator; throw them up as you leave the ground" [S9, p.16]
- **Progressive arm development** — Begin with moderate arm swings; over time achieve a fully overhead stretch position
- **Limited ceiling? Bend the arms** — The vertical leap can still be performed with elbows bent overhead in low-clearance spaces [S9, p.17]
- **Progress measurement** — Reach for a chalk mark on a wall; use the Sargent Jump Test protocol to track improvement over time [S9, p.16]
- Watch for: arms thrown back behind the body instead of forward/up — cue "reach for the ceiling"
- Watch for: leg tuck at peak height — legs should move naturally at this step; active tuck comes at Steps 5-8

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Moderate arm swing — arms swing forward and up to roughly shoulder height
2. **Intermediate**: Full arm swing — arms reach full overhead extension at peak of jump
3. **Advanced**: Track reach height against a wall mark; set measurable height goals over a training block

# Swish Free-Shot Drill

## Objective
Create competition and accountability at the free-throw line by rewarding swishes and penalizing misses, building pride and focus in free-shot shooting.

## Setup
- 2 players per basket
- Free-throw line
- 1 basketball
- Keep score (mental or on a whiteboard)

## Execution
1. Players pair up at a basket.
2. Player A shoots **two free shots**.
3. Scoring:
   - **Swish (no rim)** = **+2 points**
   - **Make (hits rim, goes in)** = **+1 point**
   - **Miss (no make)** = **−1 point**
4. Player B shoots two free shots (same scoring).
5. Alternate until one player reaches **11 points**.
6. **Must win by two** — if tied at 10-10, play continues until one player leads by 2.

## Coaching Points
- This drill rewards **quality** over quantity — players are incentivized to swish, which reinforces proper arch and follow-through [S5, p.54]
- The miss penalty (-1) creates real pressure, simulating game consequences
- Watch for players rushing to avoid the penalty — reinforce the **routine first, always**
- Pair players of similar shooting ability to maximize competition
- *"When you are an excellent shooter, everyone is shocked when you miss."* [S5, p.47]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Play to 7 instead of 11; remove the -1 penalty for misses.
2. **Intermediate**: Standard scoring to 11 win-by-2.
3. **Advanced**: Add a conditioning element — the loser runs a sprint before the next game.

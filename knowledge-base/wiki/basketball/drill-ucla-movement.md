# UCLA Movement Drill

## Objective
Teach floor balance, spacing, screening, and timing by requiring players to make five passes before attempting a shot (except layups), with no dribbling or on-ball screening allowed.

## Setup
- 6 players split into two groups of 3; run simultaneously at both ends of the court
- Half-court format
- No dribbling permitted; no offensive screening on the ball
- Players rotate from offense to defense based on the outcome of each possession

## Execution
1. **O1 passes to O2 on the wing.** O1 immediately rubs off O3's stationary screen, looking to get a layup or quick post-up.
2. **After setting the screen, O3 takes two steps toward midcourt** and receives O2's pass.
3. **O2 sets a down screen for O1**, who curls middle or wide, looking for a pass from O3.
4. **Players have now made three passes** — the teaching moment begins. When O1 receives O3's pass:
   - O2 must either **up-screen for O3**, or
   - O3 must **down-screen for O2**.
5. This decision creates continuity, player movement, and a learning moment about spacing and timing.
6. **The offense must make five passes before taking a shot** (unless a layup opportunity arises earlier).

## Coaching Points
- "Earn the shot — make all five passes first."
- Watch for players drifting to congested areas; demand three-on-one-side, two-on-the-other balance.
- Emphasize the O2/O3 decision after the third pass — this is where basketball IQ is developed.
- Players who can't execute the decision ("sight and insight") should first be given simpler roles, then progressively added to the decision point.
- Look for proper screen angles — the 90-degree rule: screener sets the angle that forces the cutter's defender to go the long way.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the three-pass sequence with no defense; name each movement before executing it.
2. **Intermediate**: Add token defense (arms only, no full denial); introduce the O2/O3 choice after the third pass.
3. **Advanced**: Full defense with rotation; loser goes to defense; defense earns a turn by getting a stop without allowing a layup.

# Baseline Shooting Drill

## Objective
Develop catch-and-shoot footwork off angle cuts at the elbows, reinforcing heel-to-toe planting, shot-pocket receiving, and "shoot and stay" habits in a continuous team flow.

## Setup
- **Players**: Minimum 6 (at least 3 per line)
- **Court area**: Half court, under the basket extending to both elbows
- **Equipment**: 1 ball per player except the first shooter
- **Positions**:
  - Two lines under the basket at the free-shot lane, one on each side
  - One player (S, shooter) starts at the front of one line **without a ball**
  - All other players have balls and will serve as passers (P)

## Execution
1. The shooter (S) starts at the front of one line and runs **up the lane line to the near elbow**.
2. At the near elbow, the shooter plants the **inside pivot foot** and points the toe of that foot in the direction of travel (toward the far elbow).
3. The shooter continues across to the **opposite elbow**, planting the **same inside foot heel-to-toe**, angling toward the basket, and preparing to step into the shot.
4. The passer (P) — standing at the baseline across from the shooter — delivers the ball to the **shot pocket** as the shooter arrives at the far elbow; shooter calls for the ball.
5. Shooter catches in rhythm, squares to the basket, shoots on balance, and **lands on balance**. Freeze the follow-through. **Shoot and stay.**
6. The passer now becomes the **next shooter**: runs **outside** of the player they just passed to, plants the inside foot at the near elbow, cuts to the far elbow (moving in the opposite direction), and prepares to receive the next pass from the opposite line.
7. Continue the drill in continuous flow. Coaches can set a **make goal** (e.g., 20 makes) or **time the drill** competitively.

## Coaching Points
- **Make angle cuts** — approach each elbow at an angle that allows the shooter to face the basket on the catch.
- **Step heel to toe** — the inside foot plants heel first, then toe, to generate upward momentum into the shot.
- **Stay low** throughout the cut; do not stand up early.
- Pass to the **shot pocket** — passers must deliver the ball where the shooter can catch in rhythm without repositioning.
- **Call for the ball** — verbal communication is a game habit.
- **Shoot and stay** — "If you follow your shots, you will often see the ball miss and go right back to where you just shot from." [S5, p.3]
- Keep the drill competitive: track makes over a set time to create an incentive to improve.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the cut and footwork without a pass; player mimes the catch-and-shoot at the elbow.
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill at moderate pace; track the number of makes in 2 minutes.
3. **Advanced**: Game speed; add a second shooter in the opposite line simultaneously to increase pace and decision-making.

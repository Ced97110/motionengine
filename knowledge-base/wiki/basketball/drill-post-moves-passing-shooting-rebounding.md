# Post Moves: Passing-Shooting-Rebounding

## Objective
Integrate all low-post offensive skills (positioning, moves, shots), flash pivot cuts, help-side defense, and both offensive and defensive rebounding techniques into one continuous team drill.

## Setup
- Players: 5 (2 low-post players, 2 low-post defenders, 1 perimeter player)
- Court area: half court — low-post and paint area
- Equipment: one basketball (coach begins with ball)
- Duration: 4 minutes
- **Rotation** after score or defensive rebound: Player 3 → Player 4 → Player 5 → X4 → X5 → Player 3

<!-- DIAGRAM: Coach on the left wing with the ball. Player 3 at the right wing perimeter. Players 4 (low post left) and 5 (flash position / right low post) guarded by X4 and X5 respectively inside the paint. -->

## Execution
**Intermediate:**
1. Line players up in positions: 4 and 5 are low-post players; X4 and X5 are low-post defenders; Player 3 is a perimeter player; Coach is at the wing with the ball
2. Coach may immediately **shoot** OR **pass to Player 3** OR **pass to the flash pivot cutter (5)**
3. If X4 is NOT in proper help-side defensive position, coach passes to Player 4 — this keeps weak-side defenders alert
4. Player 3 and coach pass the ball until they can enter it to Player 5; when 5 receives the ball, 5 makes a **low-post move and shoots**
5. X4 and X5 must box out; Players 4 and 5 use offensive rebounding techniques
6. **Three-second rule**: if 5 has not received the pass after 3 seconds, 5 rolls down the lane and through the basket area; Player 4 then begins a flash pivot cut to get open — drill continues until a shot is taken and rebound secured
7. Coach (or preferably Player 3) can shoot an outside shot at any time; inside four players fight for the rebound using correct offensive and defensive techniques
8. When a pass goes inside, the post receiver MUST make a move to score; if unable to get the shot off, pass back outside — do NOT take a bad shot

**Advanced:**
1. The two post players may screen for each other if post-to-post screening is part of your motion offense
2. Limit post-to-post screening to before the inside pass OR allow it to occur after a post player has received the ball

## Coaching Points
- **"Two to primary, two to secondary"**: make sure two players attack the primary rebounding area (paint) and two cover the secondary area (back toward shooter) on every shot [S15, p.179]
- **Flash pivot cut execution**: watch for proper footwork — front pivot on the catch, establish position, and receive the pass at the proper angle; the flash cut must also be properly defended
- **Immediate post move**: the moment a post player receives the ball, they must make a decisive move; no holding and looking; post players who hesitate lose the advantage of position
- **Help-side defense**: X4 must demonstrate proper help position when the ball is on the opposite side; use the coach's pass to Player 4 as the teaching moment
- **Drill tougher than games**: "You want to drill these fundamentals tougher than anything your team will face in a game" [S15, p.179]

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: standard drill — coach + 3, 4, 5, X4, X5
2. **Advanced**: post-to-post screening allowed; post player with ball can pass to the other post player if they cannot score
3. **Advanced variation**: Player 3 is replaced by a live defender; now the perimeter pass must be made against pressure before entering the ball inside

# Prone Y's, T's, I's (Basketball Shoulder Strengthening)

## Objective
Strengthen the rotator cuff, scapular stabilizers, and posterior shoulder complex (infraspinatus, teres minor and major, trapezius, rhomboids, posterior deltoids) to prevent shooting-arm injuries and maintain shoulder health throughout a basketball season.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: Incline bench (20–35°) or stability ball; light dumbbells
- **Players**: 1 athlete
- **Location**: Weight room or rehabilitation area

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Lie prone on a stability ball or 20–35° inclined bench. Head face-down in a neutral position. Arms hanging straight down, holding dumbbells with a **closed, neutral grip**.
2. **Rotate hands** so palms face each other with thumbs pointing up.
3. **Y Position**: Squeeze shoulder blades together and raise arms at a **45-degree angle** from the body to create the letter Y. Return to beginning position.
4. **T Position**: Raise arms at **90 degrees** from the body to create the letter T. Return to beginning position.
5. **I Position**: Raise arms **directly above the shoulders** (in line with the torso) to create the letter I. Return to beginning position.

## Coaching Points
- Do **not let the chest lift** — keep it stationary on the bench/ball throughout
- Do **not squeeze the neck and shoulders forward** — draw shoulder blades **back and down**
- **Keep the thumbs pointing up** throughout all three positions
- Use very light weights — this is a shoulder health exercise, not a strength exercise
- "Pinch a pencil between your shoulder blades"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform the exercise **on the floor** — reduces range of motion but is accessible; or use a cable machine standing
2. **Intermediate**: On incline bench at 20–35° with light dumbbells
3. **Advanced**: One arm at a time on a flat table — maximizes range and neuromuscular challenge per arm

## Variations
- **Floor version**: Reduced range of motion but requires no equipment
- **Cable machine (standing)**: Provides variable resistance through the range
- **Single-arm table**: One arm at a time for greater isolation

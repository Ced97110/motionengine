# Small Space Power Drills (Sideways Pop-Ups, Pike Slaps, Jump Kick)

## Objective
Develop explosive total-body power, core strength, and unilateral hip power using solo, equipment-free drills that can be performed in any small space as warm-ups, finishers, or ancillary explosive work.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (solo drills)
- **Space**: Any small flat area — no court required
- **Equipment**: None
- **When to use**: As warm-up, finishing exercise, or ancillary work within any explosive training session; performable rhythmically for higher reps or combined with chain exercises [S9, p.4]

## Execution

### Drill 1: Sideways Pop-Ups
1. Get into a push-up position (arms extended, core tight)
2. Bend the arms slightly, then explosively pop the entire body approximately 6 inches to one side
3. Land in push-up position and immediately pop back in the other direction
4. Vary directions: left, right, forward, backward
5. **Combination option**: Mix in clap push-ups between lateral pops for increased upper-body explosive demand
- **Purpose**: Lateral explosive push-up power; demonstrates how different power push-up patterns can be combined in a single set [S9, p.4]

### Drill 2: Pike Slaps
1. Lie on the ground on your back, balancing on your butt (hips as fulcrum)
2. Explosively raise both torso and legs simultaneously into a pike position
3. Slap the insteps (top of feet) with your hands at the peak of the movement
4. Lower back down and immediately repeat — bouncing back up rhythmically
5. Do not sacrifice speed for height — maintain explosiveness throughout
- **Purpose**: Develops powerful abdominals essential for all explosive pulling work; "all explosive pulling work requires steely abs" [S9, p.5]
- **Note**: More painful than it looks at high reps — excellent core conditioning despite the simple appearance

### Drill 3: Jump Kick
1. Assume a fighting stance with one leg in front of the other
2. Quickly snap the back leg forward (front kick motion)
3. Use that momentum to whip the knee of the opposite leg upward
4. At the peak, extend the lead knee into a full kick position
5. Return to stance and repeat; alternate legs each set
- **Purpose**: Develops unilateral hip power; a low-stress exercise for hip health that trains leg-raising rarely addressed in standard small-space drills [S9, p.5]
- **Basketball application**: Hip flexor quickness directly transfers to first-step speed and lateral defensive athleticism

## Coaching Points
- **Sideways Pop-Ups**: Land softly — don't crash down. Pop-up should be light and quick, not heavy and slow
- **Pike Slaps**: The bounce rhythm is key — don't pause at the bottom. Keep the torso and legs both active throughout
- **Jump Kick**: Don't lean backward; stay upright. Use the snap of the back leg to generate momentum — don't muscle the kick with the standing leg alone
- All three drills should be performed explosively — "don't sacrifice speed!" [S9, p.5]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform each drill for 5-8 reps at moderate pace, focusing on form
2. **Intermediate**: Perform for 10-15 reps rhythmically; combine sideways pop-ups in alternating directions without pausing
3. **Advanced**: Combine all three drills back-to-back as a circuit; add clap push-ups to the sideways pop-up set; perform jump kicks with maximum snap and height

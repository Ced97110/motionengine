# Curl Shot Off Screen

## Summary
The curl shot is executed when a cutter reads that the defender is chasing them over the top of a screen or taking a high path. Instead of filling to the wing, the cutter curls tightly around the screen toward the basket and rises for a shot in or around the elbow area (10–15 feet). This is a catch-and-shoot action taken off the curl cut momentum — the player does not take additional dribbles before shooting. [S3, pp.162-170]

The curl cut to the elbow is one of the highest-percentage off-screen actions in basketball because it gets the shooter closer to the basket and closer to the scoring range while maintaining shot-ready momentum.

## When to Use
- Defender takes a high path over the screen, trailing the cutter
- Defender is fighting hard to get through the screen above the ball
- Down screen or lateral screen is set near the elbow or paint area
- Any time the elbow/mid-post area (10–15 ft) is open after the screen

## Key Principles
1. **Read the defender going high/over the screen** — curl cut is the response to a chasing defender
2. **Rub shoulders with the screener** — a tight curl maximizes separation; a wide curl gives the defender recovery room
3. **Target the elbow area** — shots taken 10–15 feet from the basket in the elbow zone [S3, p.162]
4. **Catch in rhythm off the curl momentum** — the cutting movement feeds directly into the shooting motion
5. **Square shoulders to the basket on the catch** — pivot and face on the catch before releasing
6. **Hold the follow-through** — freeze until the ball hits the floor

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF (cutter/shooter)**: V-cut setup, read defender going high, curl tightly off screen to elbow, catch and rise in rhythm
- **Screener**: Set a legal screen near the elbow area so the cutter's curl ends in shooting range
- **Passer**: Read the curl cut and deliver the ball to the elbow on time — ball must arrive as the cutter arrives, not before or after

## Variations
### Left-Side Curl
Cutter curls from the left side to the elbow area. [S3, p.163]

### Right-Side Curl
Cutter curls from the right side to the elbow area. [S3, p.163]

### Dribble Curl (Live-Ball Extension)
When the defender trails directly behind the cutter through the curl, the cutter does not stop to shoot but continues the curl attack into the middle of the paint with a dribble — see [[concept-dribble-curl-shot]]. [S3, pp.171-179]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Wide curl** → gives defender recovery space; "rub shoulders with the screener" — make it tight
2. **Curling past the elbow shooting zone** → ending up under the basket with no shot angle; identify the elbow as the target
3. **Arriving before the pass** → stopping at the elbow without ball, losing momentum; timing must be synchronized with the passer
4. **Catching with no pivot/squaring** → shooting off-balance or turning away from basket; square on the catch in one motion
5. **Not reading the defender** → curling when the defender is low (fill shot is better); always check defender position first

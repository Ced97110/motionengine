# Quick Foot Movement

## Objective
Develop rapid foot coordination, pivoting quickness, and the mental focus required to execute a specific multi-step foot pattern at maximum speed.

## Setup
- **Players**: Individual or groups of 3 (one group works at a time, or multiple groups simultaneously)
- **Court area**: Foul-lane area; five foot-contact positions labeled A-E on the floor (tape or imagination)
- **Equipment**: None (floor positions only — see diagram for layout: A and B are the starting positions, C is the center contact, D and E are the jump-out positions)
- **Duration**: 25 seconds per group (record full revolutions as objective measure)

**Floor Layout (from diagram):**
- B and A = starting positions (left foot B, right foot A, facing coach)
- C = single-foot center contact
- D and E = jump-out positions (right foot D, left foot E)

<!-- DIAGRAM: Three groups shown side by side in the foul lane area, each with A-B-C-D-E positions marked. Coach is above at the center. [S15, p.15] -->

## Execution

**Beginner:**
1. Players place right feet on A, left feet on B, facing the coach.
2. Players jump in with right feet touching C.
3. Then jump out with right feet hitting D and left feet hitting E.
4. Immediately execute a 180-degree turn, placing left feet on D and right feet on E.
5. Place right feet on C, then jump out with left feet hitting A and right feet hitting B.
6. Execute another 180-degree turn. Drill continues for 25 seconds.
7. Beginners learn only this one foot pattern — the simplest version.

**Intermediate:**
1. On the initial jump, put the left foot on C instead of the right foot — same pattern continues.
2. Alternatively, jump with both feet on C on the initial jump — same pattern continues.

**Advanced:**
1. Begin with left foot on A and right foot on B (back now facing the coach).
2. Jump into the C block backward, landing on both feet, right foot only, or left foot only (coach's choice).
3. Jump out with left foot on D, right foot on E.
4. Execute a 180-degree turn (now facing the coach). Drill continues.

**Option (all levels):** Substitute a front or reverse pivot for the 180-degree jumping turn.

## Coaching Points
- The goal is quickness, not height — players should not raise feet high off the floor, drag feet, or hop
- "Glide quickly" — feet lift ever so slightly; toes grab the floor as they slide through the drill [S15, p.15]
- Mental cue: "Think quickness, quickness, quickness"
- Record the number of full revolutions per 25 seconds and track improvement over time
- The 180-degree turn/pivot option is an excellent way to reinforce pivoting within a conditioning context

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Fixed pattern (right foot to C, jump out, 180 turn) — 25 seconds
2. **Intermediate**: Alternate starting foot; add both-feet option on C
3. **Advanced**: Begin backward, add variability on landing foot, pivot option replaces jump

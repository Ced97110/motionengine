# Show-and-Go (Shot Fake Drive)

## Summary
The show-and-go is a shot fake followed immediately by an attacking dribble drive. The offensive player catches the ball, raises it to the head as if shooting ("showing" the ball), then rips it down to the hip and explodes past the defender. It is one of the foundational one-on-one scoring moves because it exploits defenders who are susceptible to shot fakes, especially during closeout situations.

The move has two primary variations: a **strong-side show-and-go** (driving to the shooting-hand side) and a **weak-side show-and-go** (driving opposite the shooting hand). Each requires specific footwork depending on the pivot foot used on the catch.

## When to Use
- When a defender closes out aggressively and is susceptible to shot fakes
- After receiving a pass in a closeout drill or game situation
- When the help defense is slow to rotate, creating driving lanes
- Both from a stationary catch and on-the-move catches at wing areas

## Key Principles
1. **Sell the fake first** — raise the ball convincingly to head height; the defender must believe you are shooting
2. **Rip to the hip** — once the defender bites, rip the ball down to the dribble hip quickly to protect it
3. **Read the closeout direction** — if the defender closes out left, show and go right; if they close out right, show and go left
4. **Stay low** — do not come up out of your stance on the fake; stay athletic and coiled to explode
5. **Crossover vs. open step** — the correct footwork depends on which way you are driving relative to your pivot foot

## Footwork Details

### Strong-Side Show-and-Go
The offensive player drives to the shooting-hand side. After shot-faking, use a **crossover step** with the non-pivot foot to initiate the drive.

### Weak-Side Show-and-Go (Straight-on Catch)
For a right-handed player: pivot foot is the left foot. After shot-faking, do a **crossover step with the right foot** and drive left. [S5, p.101]

### Weak-Side Show-and-Go (Moving Right to Left)
For a right-handed player moving right to left on the catch: **stub the toe on the catch**, show the ball, rip it to the hip, and dribble by with the **left foot** — producing an **open step** rather than a crossover step. [S5, p.101]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF**: Primary users; should master both strong-side and weak-side variations from all seven shooting spots and on the move
- **PF**: Can use the move on mid-post catches, especially when defenders close out on face-up opportunities

## Variations
### Straight-On Catch Show-and-Go
From a stationary catch at one of the seven shooting spots. Begin here before progressing to on-the-move.

### On-the-Move Show-and-Go
Catch while moving from the wing. Spin the ball out toward the baseline or toward the middle and catch on the inside pivot foot. Work from both sides of the floor. [S5, p.102]

### Closeout Read Show-and-Go (Competitive)
- Have a teammate throw a pass and close out either left or right
- Offensive player reads the direction of the closeout and attacks the opposite side
- Progress to a live 1-on-1 game: scorer stays on offense, defender who gets a stop earns the ball
- Team version: groups of 3-4 per basket, 1-on-1-on-1 for 5 minutes; most points wins [S5, p.102]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Fake too short** — a half-hearted show does not freeze the defender → raise the ball fully to the head, make it convincing
2. **Rising up before driving** — standing tall on the fake kills your explosive first step → stay low and athletic throughout the move
3. **Wrong foot step** — using a crossover when an open step is needed (or vice versa) based on direction of travel → learn footwork for each catching direction separately
4. **Driving same direction as the closeout** — going the same way the defender closes out leads to a charge or blocked driving lane → always read and go opposite

# Continuity Basic Action (Three Core Sets)

## Summary
The Continuity offense "basic action" begins from a 2-3 formation: two guards (1 and 2), two forwards (3 and 4) near the free-throw line extended, and one center/post (5) who starts low and flashes to the high-post elbows or free-throw area. All four perimeter players are interchangeable. From this formation, every possession flows through one of three foundational sets — Pass and Screen Away, Pass and Cut Away, or Pass and Follow the Ball (High Split). Each set includes multiple options based on defender reactions. [S7, pp.119-124]

## When to Use
- Pass and Screen Away: primary set; triggers multiple backdoor cuts, down screens, and roll reads simultaneously
- Pass and Cut Away: when the defense is playing help-and-recover — cutting keeps the four perimeter spots occupied and eliminates weak/strong sides
- High Split (Pass and Follow): when the initial pass goes to the high post and the passer wants to use their own cut as a decoy while triggering the split
- All three can flow into each other within the same possession without resetting

## Key Principles
1. **Always occupy all four perimeter spots** — every cut must be timed so a replacement fills the vacated spot
2. **High post (5) is the hub** — all sets route through a pass to 5 at the elbow or free-throw area; 5 must be a skilled reader and passer
3. **Screen away from the ball** — the passer immediately screens for someone other than the pass receiver, maximizing defensive confusion
4. **Cut timing matters** — in the Pass and Cut Away set, the second cutter times their cut after the first cutter's cut to preserve spacing
5. **Multiple options cascade** — if Option 1 is denied, the action flows directly into Option 2, then 3, etc., without the need for a new call
6. **Screeners read the roll** — after every screen, the screener rolls to the basket, pops out, or posts down based on how the defense plays the screen

## The Three Core Sets

### Set 1: Pass and Screen Away From the Ball
The passer passes to the high post (5) and immediately screens away from the ball for a teammate, who can cut backdoor or come off the screen. Multiple cascading options follow based on defender reactions.

**Option 1** (Figures 7.18):
- 5 flashes to high post, receives from 1
- 1 screens away for 2; 2 cuts backdoor or comes off a down screen from 3
- 5 can pass to: 2 (backdoor or off 3's screen), 3 (rolls to basket after screening), 3 (posts low), or 1 (pops out after screening)

**Option 2** (Figure 7.19):
- 2 passes to 5; 2 screens for 4, who goes backdoor; 2 pops out
- 5 can pass to: 4 (backdoor or off 3's screen), 2 (pops out), 3 (rolls after screen), or 3/4 (posts low)

**Option 3** (Figures 7.20-7.22):
- 1 cannot make entry pass; 1 drives toward 3 for a dribble handoff while 2 and 4 exchange spots
- 3 passes to 4, 4 passes to 5 (flashed to elbow); 3 cuts to opposite side
- 5 can pass to 3, or to 4 (after back screen by 4 for 5), or to multiple roll/pop options
- Secondary: 4 screens for 2, then 1 screens for 2; 5 has multiple options

**Option 4** (Figures 7.24-7.27):
- 2 reverses to 1; 1 passes to 3; 2 cuts away; 1 screens down for 2; 5 pops
- 3 passes to 5; 3 and 4 each screen down for 2 and 1 respectively
- 5 distributes to the open cutter, roller, or guard
- Late option: 5 screens away for 1 (backdoor), or 5 posts low and flashes to elbow for a secondary action

**Option 5** (Figure 7.28):
- 1 passes to 2, cuts and screens for 5, then goes to opposite wing
- 5 receives from 2; 2 screens for 4 (backdoor)
- 5 distributes: 4 backdoor, 1 pops, 2 rolls after screen

### Set 2: Pass and Cut Away
Every player passes and cuts to the opposite side of the court, keeping all four perimeter spots occupied and eliminating any weak side. [S7, pp.122-123]

**Basic Action** (Figures 7.29-7.30):
- 2 passes to 1; 5 flashes to high post opposite the ball; 1 passes to 5
- 2 cuts to opposite side of court; 1 times cut after 2's cut
- 5 can pass to 2 or 1 on the cut
- While 1 comes out of the lane, 4 sets a down screen for 1; 5 can pass to 1 or to 4 rolling
- If 5 has no pass, 5 dribbles toward overplayed 3 → 3 cuts backdoor or 3 screens for 2

### Set 3: Pass and Follow the Ball (High Split)
Passer follows the pass toward the high post, while the other guard cuts to the opposite side and is screened. The high post reads multiple split options. [S7, pp.123-124]

**Basic Action** (Figure 7.31):
- 2 passes to 1; 5 flashes to high post (elbow opposite ball); 1 passes to 5
- 2 cuts to opposite side and comes off 3's screen
- 5 can pass to 2 on the cut or off the screen

**Alternatives when primary is denied:**
- 1 follows the pass to 5; 4 fakes a split and goes backdoor; 5 passes to 4 or 1 (pops out) [Fig 7.32]
- 1 screens for 4; 4 receives for a jump shot or drive; 5 can pass to 1 rolling [Fig 7.33]
- 1 screens for 4; 4 goes backdoor; 1 pops out for a jump shot [Fig 7.34]
- 1 splits with 4; 4 goes backdoor and posts low; 1 pops to wing [Fig 7.35]
- 4 back-screens for 5 after 1's pass; 5 receives; 4 pops out [Fig 7.36]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (1)**: initiates sets; screens away, cuts, or follows the pass; always replaces or fills a perimeter spot
- **SG (2)**: cuts on the pass, reads screens, executes backdoor when denied; secondary initiator
- **SF (3)**: occupies wing; sets down screens; rolls after screening; can post if needed
- **PF (4)**: cuts backdoor, sets back screens, receives inside after screen action; reads high-split options
- **C (5)**: high-post hub; receives the entry pass; must read all five options simultaneously; rolls or pops after screening

## Common Mistakes
1. **Not screening away immediately** → correction: the passer's next action is always a screen for someone on the other side
2. **Second cutter too early** → correction: in Pass and Cut Away, time your cut after the first cutter has cleared — don't cut simultaneously
3. **Post not reading defenders** → correction: 5 must watch all defenders before passing; the best option often appears one pass cycle late
4. **Screeners standing still after screening** → correction: roll, pop, or post immediately after setting every screen
5. **Ignoring the backdoor when overplayed** → correction: if your defender tries to deny you, cut backdoor every time — this is the automatic read

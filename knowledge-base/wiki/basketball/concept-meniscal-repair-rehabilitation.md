# Meniscal Repair Rehabilitation Protocol (Basketball)

## Summary
Following meniscal repair in adolescent basketball players, a carefully phased rehabilitation program is required to protect the healing meniscus while progressively restoring range of motion, strength, and neuromuscular function. The full protocol spans a minimum of 20 weeks, with return to sport no earlier than 6 months post-operatively after physician clearance. This is in contrast to partial meniscectomy, which allows immediate weight-bearing and return to sport when ROM and strength are full [S10, p.401].

Meniscal repair is strongly preferred over meniscectomy in the pediatric/adolescent population due to 80-87% surgical success rates and the high long-term morbidity associated with meniscectomy in young athletes [S10, p.400].

## When to Use
- Post-operative management after arthroscopic or open meniscal repair
- Distinguish from partial meniscectomy protocol (immediate WB, faster return)
- Applies to red-white and white-white zone meniscal tears repaired surgically

## Key Principles
1. **Protect the repair**: No weight-bearing with flexion >90° during early phases
2. **Brace compliance**: Locked in extension for WB and sleeping; unlocked to 90° for ROM exercises and hygiene only
3. **Progressive loading**: Advance from partial to full weight-bearing over 8 weeks
4. **Neuromuscular focus**: Proprioception and balance training are central to Phases IV-V
5. **No rushing**: Phase V advancement requires physician clearance based on clinical and functional criteria
6. **Eccentric hamstring work**: Critical component introduced in Phase III [S10, p.401]

## 5-Phase Protocol (Table 33.3)

### Phase I: 0-2 Weeks
- **Weight-bearing**: Partial weight-bearing with crutches
- **Brace**: Locked in full extension for sleeping and all activities; off for exercises and hygiene
- **ROM**: 0-90° when non-weight-bearing
- **Exercises**: Heel slides, quad sets, patellar mobilizations, SLR, SAQ
- **Restriction**: No weight-bearing with flexion >90°

### Phase II: 2-8 Weeks
- **Weight-bearing**: 2-4 weeks: 50% WB with crutches; 4-8 weeks: progress to full WB
- **Brace**: 2-6 weeks: unlocked 0-90° off at night; discontinue brace at 6 weeks
- **ROM**: As tolerated
- **Exercises**: Add heel raises, total gym (closed chain), terminal knee extensions
- **Restriction**: Activities with brace until 6 weeks; no WB with flexion >90°

### Phase III: 8-12 Weeks
- **Weight-bearing**: Full
- **Brace**: None
- **ROM**: Full
- **Exercises**: Progress closed chain activities; begin hamstring work, lunges/leg press 0-90°, proprioception, balance/core/hip/glutes; begin stationary bike

### Phase IV: 12-20 Weeks
- **Weight-bearing**: Full
- **Brace**: None
- **ROM**: Full
- **Exercises**: Progress Phase III; single-leg balance, core, glutes, eccentric hamstrings, elliptical, bike
- **Note**: Swimming okay at 16 weeks

### Phase V: >20 Weeks
- **Weight-bearing**: Full
- **Brace**: None
- **ROM**: Full
- **Exercises**: Advance to sport-specific drills and running/jumping once cleared by MD
- **Return to sport**: Minimum 6 months post-operatively

## Post-Meniscectomy (Contrast)
For partial meniscectomy or saucerization WITHOUT repair:
- Immediate weight-bearing
- PT initiation at 2 weeks
- Return to sport when full ROM and strength achieved (much faster timeline)

## Common Mistakes
1. **Early full weight-bearing with flexion >90°** → Can disrupt repair; strictly avoided in Phases I-II
2. **Early brace discontinuation** → Brace continues until 6 weeks minimum
3. **Skipping eccentric hamstring work** → Essential for posterior chain protection of the repaired meniscus
4. **Time-based return to sport** → Must be physician-cleared with functional criteria met

# Rope-Skipping (Lower-Body Plyometric)

## Objective
Improve quickness and elastic strength in the lower body through rhythmic and reactive rope-skipping patterns. [S8, p.166]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space with marked spots or targets
- Equipment: jump rope (standard or weighted)

## Execution
1. Skip rope while jumping to designated spots on the ground.
2. Can be performed with both legs or a single leg.
3. Perform for time or a target number of jumps.

## Coaching Points
- Land on the balls of the feet; do not flat-foot — the goal is elastic ankle strength.
- "Spots" on the floor make the drill directional and game-specific (forward, lateral, diagonal).
- Minimal air time between jumps — tight, quick rotations.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Basic bilateral skipping in place.
2. **Intermediate (Variable Patterns)**: Skip rope while changing jump patterns in response to coach commands (bilateral or single-leg). Develops reactive decision-making.
3. **Advanced (Weighted)**: Perform with a weighted rope to increase resistance.
4. **Advanced (Side-Skipping)**: Skip to the left or right — the arm and knee sequence remains the same; considered the most challenging variation.

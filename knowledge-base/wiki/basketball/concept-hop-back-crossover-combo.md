# Hop-Back to Crossover Dribble Combination

## Summary
The hop-back to crossover dribble is an advanced combination live-ball move that chains two distinct dribble actions together to create scoring separation. The player uses a hop-back (a quick backward hop on one or two dribbles) to freeze the defender, then immediately crosses over to attack the open side. This move is particularly effective against help defenders who try to cut off the initial hop-back direction. [S3]

The combination can be initiated off one dribble or two dribbles before the hop-back, giving the offensive player flexibility based on spacing and defensive position. Both left and right hand variations must be trained equally to make the move a complete weapon.

## When to Use
- When the defender overplays the drive and a step-back creates space
- When the initial hop-back is read by the help defense, crossover into the opening
- In half-court isolation situations where the player needs to create their own shot
- In pull-up 3-point situations after using the crossover to gain step-back angle

## Key Principles
1. The hop-back creates the illusion of a pull-up or step-back shot — sell it with your eyes and body
2. The crossover immediately follows the hop — don't pause between the two actions
3. Stay low on the hop so the crossover drive is explosive off the floor
4. Alternate directions on each repetition in practice to build two-way comfort
5. Establish a dominant pivot foot on the catch before initiating the combo
6. "One dribble" variant: catch → 1 dribble → hop-back → crossover; "Two dribble" variant: catch → 2 dribbles → hop-back → crossover [S3]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Primary user — must be able to read when the crossover is open vs. when the hop-back creates the shot
- **SG/SF**: Wing players use this off the catch from a skip pass or off a DHO entry

## Variations
### 1 Dribble Hop-Back to Crossover
One dribble before executing the hop-back, then a single crossover to attack. More compact and quicker — used when space is limited. [S3]

### 2 Dribble Hop-Back to Crossover
Two dribbles before the hop-back — allows more lateral movement to create the angle, then crossover. More space required but can drag the defender further before the crossover. [S3]

### Pull-Up 3-Point Variant
After the crossover, instead of driving all the way to the rim, the player hops into a pull-up three-pointer. Trained in Workout #4-22 through #4-24 (25/50/100 pull-up 3s). [S3]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Telegraphing the crossover** → Keep the ball low and close to the body; don't wind up
2. **Standing up on the hop-back** → Stay in an athletic crouch; rising makes the next move slow
3. **Only practicing one direction** → Train left and right hand versions equally in every workout session
4. **Losing dominant pivot foot** → Establish pivot foot on catch before beginning the combination

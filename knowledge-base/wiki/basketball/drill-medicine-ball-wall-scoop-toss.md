# Medicine-Ball Wall Scoop Toss

## Objective
Enhance total-body extension, quickness, and power by training the kinetic chain from squat to overhead release. [S8, p.161]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Solid wall
- Equipment: 1 medicine ball

## Execution
1. Face the wall in an upright athletic stance holding the medicine ball.
2. Quickly squat down (tight back, low-squat stance) then explosively extend the entire body.
3. Toss the ball against the wall as fast as possible while maintaining a tight-backed, low-squat stance throughout.
4. Receive the rebound and immediately repeat.
5. Perform for time or for a predetermined number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Tight back, fast hips" — the squat must remain controlled even at high speed.
- The scoop motion (from low to high) trains the upward kinetic chain used in rebounds and post-entry feeds.
- Do not allow the back to round during the squat phase.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow, controlled squat-extend-toss sequence.
2. **Intermediate**: Increase toss speed; add reps for time.
3. **Advanced**: Perform a reverse scoop toss backward; perform a long jump then a scoop toss.

# Mass Footwork Breakdown Drill

## Objective
Efficently develop offensive footwork for all 12 players simultaneously — perimeter passing/feeding, cutting off screens, and post receiving/attacking — by rotating through four roles in timed intervals.

## Setup
- **Players**: 12 (entire squad) split into 3 four-man groups
- **Court area**: Half court or full court; each group works at a different location (top of key, right wing/post, left wing/post)
- **Equipment**: 3 basketballs
- Each group has: a perimeter ball handler (passer), an offensive post player, a perimeter cutter/receiver, and a defender on the perimeter ball handler

## Execution
1. Three groups work simultaneously at different court locations.
2. Each group executes the offensive footwork action being practiced (e.g., pin screen action, stagger screen cut, post entry).
3. **Work interval**: 55 seconds of continuous repetitions at game speed.
4. **Rotation**: 5-second window for all four players to rotate to the next position.
5. Repeat until all four roles have been covered by each player (4 rotations = ~4 minutes per group).
6. Later in the same practice, repeat the mass drill 2+ more times with each group **starting at a different location**.
7. Total time: ~12 minutes covers all players in both perimeter passer/feeder AND interior post receiving/attacking roles.

## Coaching Points
- Limit dribbles available to the perimeter ball handler (e.g., 2-dribble max) to force quick decisions
- Limit what the post player can do after receiving (e.g., only drop step, or only shot fake and score)
- Dummy defenders (on ball handler) start passive; increase resistance as players improve
- Add a defender on the screener in later repetitions to increase realism
- "Players learn best by doing" — maximize active repetitions, minimize standing and waiting
- Rotate players into both offensive AND defensive roles within the drill

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No defenders — all four players walk through their roles at half speed
2. **Intermediate**: Passive dummy defender on the ball handler; game-pace execution
3. **Advanced**: Live defenders on both the ball handler and the screener; game speed throughout

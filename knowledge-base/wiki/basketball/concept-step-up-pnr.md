# Step-Up Pick-and-Roll

## Summary
The step-up pick-and-roll is a variation of the standard side ball screen where the screener sets the screen from the **baseline upward toward the ball**, rather than from the side. This brings the ball to the **sideline side** of the screen rather than the middle of the floor. Developed by coaches Doug Collins and Alvin Gentry with the Detroit Pistons, the step-up specifically defeats defensive schemes that try to force the ball toward the sideline or trap the standard side PnR. [S14, pp.27-29]

The screen angle creates a geometric advantage: if the defense pushes the ball toward the sideline, the ball handler has already established the sideline-side angle and can attack freely. If the defense traps, the ball handler's momentum carries him away from the trap.

## When to Use
- When the defense is trying to force the ball toward the sideline on standard PnRs
- When the defense is trapping the ball screen from the side (the step-up angle makes the trap much harder to execute)
- When the ball handler is a point forward or combo guard who can turn the corner on either side
- As an early-offense or early-set action to establish a tempo
- When you want to generate secondary back-screen or flare actions from the PnR initiation

## Key Principles
1. **Screen angle is everything**: The screener must set the screen from below (baseline) upward toward the ball, not from the side. The ball ends up on the sideline side of the screen — this is what defeats sideline-forcing defenses.
2. **Ball handler starts higher**: The ball handler keeps the ball higher than normal on the dribble approach to establish the proper 45-degree angle with the screener.
3. **Drive first**: The ball handler must be able to turn the corner and attack — a legitimate drive threat makes the secondary actions (flare, back screen, pop) work because defenders must help.
4. **Screener pops or slips**: After setting the step-up screen, the big man should be a perimeter jump-shot threat (pop) or be able to slip to the rim when the defense over-helps on the drive.
5. **Weak-side spot-ups**: The two weakest defenders must guard perimeter players, which removes quick rotations to the roll man.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SF (Ball Handler)**: Dribble approach from the sideline, higher than normal. Attack the basket on Option 1. If stopped, make a quick read to skip passer or find the secondary action.
- **PF (Screener)**: Set the screen from baseline-to-ball direction. After setting, immediately read: roll to basket, pop to perimeter, or slip if defender over-commits.
- **SG/SF (Weak-side shooter)**: Spot up on the weak side. Move to receive a skip pass if your defender drops to help on the drive.
- **C**: Can serve as the second big in double-screen variants; sets down screens for weak-side players after the main PnR resolves.

## Variations
### Basic Step-Up PnR (Detroit)
Single step-up screen; screener pops or rolls; weak-side shooters spot up [S14, pp.27-29].

### Step-Up Flare Action
When the drive is stopped, a third player (4) sets a back screen for the original screener (2), who flares to the top for a skip-pass catch-and-shoot. The 5 simultaneously down-screens for 1 in the corner [S14, pp.27-29].

### Angled Wing PnR (Orlando)
Chuck Daly's version explicitly establishes a 45-degree angle with the screener positioned midway between the elbow and the three-point line — with a weak-side staggered double screen for a shooter [S14, pp.33-35].

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ball handler approaches too low** → The screen angle collapses; becomes a standard side PnR that the defense is prepared for. Correction: keep the ball higher on the approach.
2. **Screener sets the screen from the side** → Negates the step-up angle. Correction: screener must move from below the ball's position upward.
3. **No secondary action ready** → If the drive is stopped and there is no secondary action, the defense stops the play completely. Correction: always have a flare, back screen, or pop option built in.
4. **Weak-side players don't spot up** → Defenders can cheat to help on the drive. Correction: weak-side players must be in position before the screen is set.

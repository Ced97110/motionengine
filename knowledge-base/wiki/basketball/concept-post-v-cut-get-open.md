# Post Player V-Cut: Getting Open to Receive the Ball

## Summary
The V-cut is the foundational post-entry technique for getting open against denial defense. A post player cannot score until he receives the ball; he cannot receive the ball unless he creates a passing window. The V-cut uses a misdirection step (one or more steps in the wrong direction) followed by a change-of-direction cut using a front crossover pivot. The direction of the V-cut is determined entirely by **how the defender is playing**. [S6, pp.173-175]

The V-cut works equally well for perimeter players facing denial defense, not only for post players. [S6, p.179]

## When to Use
- When a post defender is in a stationary position on one side (high or low) denying the pass
- When flashing across the lane to receive an interior pass
- Any time a post player needs to create a passing window against active denial defense

## Key Principles
1. **Read the defender first** — see where the defender is (high side, low side, how tight) before selecting the direction of the V-cut. [S6, p.174]
2. **Take the defender the wrong way** — if the defender is low, go lower first before cutting high; if high, go higher before cutting low. [S6, pp.175-176]
3. **V-cut using a front crossover pivot** — the direction-change step is a front crossover pivot, not a simple drop step. [S6, p.174]
4. **Rip the contact arm** on the front crossover step — rip the arm through the defender's chest or past their shoulder to eliminate pursuit angle. [S6, pp.174-175]
5. **Flash to the notch above the block** — always post up at the first notch above the block, never below it. [S6, p.175]
6. **Get close to the defender before cutting** — proximity reduces the defender's reaction time when the cut is made. [S6, p.178]

## Execution — Fake Low, Cut High (Defender Plays Low)
1. Post player (05) is on the left side of the lane, back to basket.
2. Reads that X5 is on the low side.
3. Takes one or more initial steps **lower** (away from the ball).
4. Plants inside (left) foot in the lane.
5. Steps with right foot.
6. **Front crossover pivot** with left foot — crossing over toward the ball-side.
7. Simultaneously **rips the left (contact) arm** across the defender's chest.
8. Flashes to the ball on the opposite side of the lane.
9. Posts up at notch above the block. [S6, pp.174-175]

## Execution — Fake High, Cut Low (Defender Plays High)
1. Post player (05) reads that X5 is on the high side.
2. Takes one or more initial steps **higher** (toward the elbow/high post).
3. First step: top (right) foot goes higher.
4. Second step: lower (left) foot.
5. **Front crossover step** with top (right) foot going low.
6. Simultaneously **rips right arm** past the defender's **baseline shoulder**.
7. Flashes low across the lane.
8. Posts up at notch above the block on the opposite side. [S6, p.175]

## Teaching Phrases (Coach Cues)
- "If they start low, you start lower. (But never start below the block.)"
- "If they start high, you start higher."
- "If they play you low, take them lower and scrape cut high."
- "If they play you high, take them higher and scrape cut low." [S6, p.176]

## Swim Move Upgrade (vs. Physical Denial)
When the defender physically jams the V-cut (too tight to rip the arm through), the post player **swims** the contact arm **over** the defender instead of ripping through:
- Same front crossover step footwork
- Swing the contact arm over the defender's shoulder/head
- Post player ends up on the high side, closer to the ball than the defender
- Teaching phrase: "Get into the defender's body before you make your swim move." [S6, pp.177-178]

Swim phrases:
- "If they jam you low, take them lower and swim high."
- "If they jam you high, take them higher and swim low." [S6, pp.177-178]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Flashing directly without a misdirection step** → gives the defender time to cut off the flash; always V-cut first.
2. **Cutting away from the defender's position** → the cut direction must counter how the defender is playing — go where they are NOT.
3. **Not ripping or swimming the arm** → leaving the arm passive lets the defender recover to cut off the flash.
4. **Posting below the block** → no angle for drop steps; always at the notch above the block.
5. **Front crossover pivot too far from the defender** → cut from close proximity — maximum 1-2 feet — to minimize the defender's reaction time.

# Reverse Hip Extension Hold

## Objective
Strengthen the gluteal complex, erector spinae, and multifidus through a prone isometric hip extension hold — developing posterior chain strength for jumping, acceleration, and maintaining athletic posture during contact.

## Setup
- 1 player
- High bench or plyometric box
- Athlete lies face down with upper body supported, hips at the edge

## Execution
1. Lie face down on a high bench or box so that only the upper body is supported.
2. Hold the outer edges of the bench for support and stability.
3. Position the hips at the edge of the box and flex them to 90 degrees (beginning position — legs hang toward the floor).
4. Keeping the knees fully extended and toes pointed, push the hips into the bench surface (contract the glutes) while lifting the legs under control until the hips reach a neutral position.
5. Once the legs reach the top and hips are in neutral alignment, hold isometrically for the prescribed time.
6. Lower with control and repeat.

### Bosch Hip Extension Hold (Unilateral Variation)
- Lie face up on the floor with one foot elevated on a bench and the opposite leg extended straight up toward the ceiling.
- Dorsiflexed the ankle of the foot on the bench, then drive through the heel to fully extend the hips and lift them off the floor.
- Hold for the prescribed time, then return to the start and repeat on the other leg.
- Challenges single-leg posterior chain stability directly relevant to one-foot landing and take-off mechanics.

## Coaching Points
- Cue: "Squeeze your glutes hard when lifting the legs into position."
- From a side view at the top position, the athlete's shoulders, hips, and knees should all be in a straight horizontal line.
- Focus on controlled breathing — matched inhales and exhales during the hold.
- Do not use momentum to swing the legs up; the movement should be slow and deliberate.
- **Regression**: Perform with knees held at 90 degrees of flexion — shorter lever arm reduces difficulty.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reverse hip extension hold with knees at 90 degrees (bent knees).
2. **Intermediate**: Full reverse hip extension hold with straight legs as described.
3. **Advanced**: Bosch Hip Extension Hold — unilateral version demanding greater hip stabilizer activation.

# Kipping Pull-Up (Muscle-Up Chain — Step 3)

## Objective
Combine swing kip momentum with an explosive inward arm pull to carry the jaw above the bar — building the explosive pulling power and kinesthetic timing that is the direct prerequisite to the full muscle-up.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Full-height horizontal overhead bar
- No additional equipment needed
- Prerequisites: mastery of [[drill-swing-kip]] (Step 1) and [[drill-jumping-pullup]] (Step 2)

## Execution
1. Grab and hang from an overhead horizontal bar, shoulder-width grip.
2. Shoulders pulled down tight into armpits (protects shoulder socket).
3. Initiate with a **swing kip** (Step 1) to build momentum before the pull.
4. As you reach the **peak of the backswing**, thrust hips forward and simultaneously pull down on the bar by bending the arms and shoulders.
5. Use the combined backswing momentum plus arm pull to carry yourself upward until the jaw is above the bar.
6. **Lower quickly** in a reverse path — this creates a slight forward swing. Use that forward momentum to thrust the chest forward again into the next swing kip.
7. Continue for multiple reps, maintaining rhythm and momentum between reps [S9, p.17].

## Coaching Points
- **"Backswing first, then pull"** — the hip thrust and arm pull must happen simultaneously at the *peak* of the backswing, not before
- "Shoulders down into the pockets" — packed shoulders protect the joint and maximize lat engagement
- At first, goal is jaw over bar. As this becomes easy, progress to getting shoulders — then chest — over the bar at the top
- Don't strip the movement of momentum by trying to make it stricter — more height is the progression, not slower speed [S9, p.17]
- Watch for: athletes who pull too early (before peak backswing) — they waste the momentum and fatigue the arms unnecessarily
- **Regression**: Build more swing kips in a chain before the pull — use 2-3 kips to generate more backswing height if one swing is insufficient [S9, p.17]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use a chain of multiple swing kips before the pull to build maximum momentum.
2. **Intermediate**: Pull from a single swing kip, getting jaw over bar consistently.
3. **Advanced**: Progress to getting head/shoulders higher above the bar at peak — leads directly to [[drill-pullup-hop]] (Step 4) [S9, p.17].

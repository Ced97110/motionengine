# Rip Step Stride Stop / Step-Through

## Objective
Train the two-option read off the rip step drive: stride stop for a pull-up jump shot when the defender recovers, or step-through to a layup when the defender commits to the pull-up.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court, wing or elbow starting position
- Ball; optional cone at drive-lane entry to simulate defender

## Execution
1. Start in triple-threat on the wing; execute the **rip step** — ball rips low while lead foot steps aggressively toward the basket
2. Take **1 dribble** and begin a **stride stop** (1-2 step landing)
3. **Read the defender**: 
   - If defender is late recovering → complete the pull-up jump shot (stride stop finish)
   - If defender closes out hard on the pull-up → continue the stride stop momentum, plant off the lead foot, and **step through** past the defender to a layup at the rim
4. In solo practice without a live defender, alternate: odd reps = stride stop / pull-up; even reps = step-through layup
5. Shoot prescribed **free throws** between each set (5 or 10 FTs depending on volume tier)
6. Repeat with the **2-dribble** version after mastering 1-dribble

**Workout Volumes:**
- Workout #2-7 (Introductory): 5 reps × 8 sets = 40 total; 5 FTs between; Total: 40 FG + 40 FT (80 total shots)
- Workout #2-8 (Standard): 10 reps × 8 sets = 80 FG; 5 FTs between; Total: 80 FG + 40 FT (120 total shots)
- Workout #2-9 (Extended): 20 reps × 8 sets = 160 FG; 10 FTs between; Total: 160 FG + 80 FT (240 total shots)

**Target Performance:** 60% FG, 80–85% FT [S3]

## Coaching Points
- The key teaching point: **same footwork, two reads** — the step-through does not require a different initial action; the defender's movement determines the finish
- "Sell the pull-up first" — the stride stop posture must look like a real shot attempt to hold the defender before the step-through
- On the step-through, the ball is protected on the far hip away from the defender; use the body as a shield
- Watch for traveling on the step-through: the gather foot (lead foot in the stride stop) is the pivot foot; the step-through foot must go first
- Free throws between sets simulate the and-one transition — the step-through often draws contact

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 5-rep sets, 1 dribble, no live defender — alternate pull-up and step-through every rep to build both patterns
2. **Intermediate**: 10-rep sets, introduce 2-dribble version; use a chair or cone as a defender to practice the read
3. **Advanced**: 20-rep sets with a live closeout defender; player must make a real read on each possession; track makes separately for pull-up vs. step-through finishes

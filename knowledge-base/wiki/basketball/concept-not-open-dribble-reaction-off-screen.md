# Not Open? No Problem — Dribble Reaction Off a Screen

## Summary
Setting a good screen and coming off it is only half the equation. Aggressive, well-coached defenders will fight through screens and be on the shooter's heels as they receive the ball. A skilled offensive player must be mentally and physically prepared to put the ball on the floor instead of forcing a contested catch-and-shoot. The key is to immediately protect the ball with an outside-hand dribble while keeping the eyes up and on the target.

This concept bridges the gap between catch-and-shoot off screens and shot creation off the dribble — it is the same footwork foundation applied to a different game scenario.

## When to Use
- Defender fights through the screen and arrives on the shooter as they catch
- The catch-and-shoot opportunity is closed but the defender has momentum in one direction
- Receiving the ball in a live game situation off any screen (down screen, flare screen, inbounds screen)
- Any time a shooter catches and instinctively feels defensive pressure before shooting

## Key Principles
1. **Inside foot plant is still the foundation**: Whether shooting or dribbling, you plant the inside foot on the catch. The footwork does not change.
2. **Immediately dribble with the outside hand**: This protects the ball from the chasing defender and sets you up to attack in the open direction.
   - Moving **left to right**: plant left foot (inside), dribble with the **right hand** (outside)
   - Moving **right to left**: plant right foot (inside), dribble with the **left hand** (outside)
3. **Inside hand goes to armbar position**: When catching right-to-left and dribbling with the left hand, the inside (right) hand moves into armbar position to protect the ball and create space from the defender.
4. **Keep your head up and eyes on the target**: "If your eyes are on your target, you are thinking score." Seeing the full court also lets you find open teammates or identify help defenders.
5. **Dribble with a purpose**: "When you dribble, you want to go somewhere with the dribble." Never dribble aimlessly — attack a gap, draw a foul, or find a better shot.
6. **Be the aggressor**: Once you have protected the ball, you are in control. Make a move and attack.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF**: This is primarily a perimeter skill. All wing players coming off screens must be ready to execute this instantly upon catching — it is not a thinking-time decision, it is a trained reaction.

## Variations
### Moving Left to Right
- Catch the ball while moving left to right
- Plant the **left foot** (inside pivot foot)
- Immediately dribble with the **right (outside) hand**
- Protect the ball and attack the opening

### Moving Right to Left
- Catch the ball while moving right to left
- Plant the **right foot** (inside pivot foot)
- Immediately dribble with the **left (outside) hand**
- Move **inside (right) hand** to armbar position for protection

## Common Mistakes
1. **Catching and gathering before dribbling** → The dribble must be **immediate** on the catch; any hesitation lets the defender recover and contest
2. **Dribbling with the inside hand** → Always dribble with the **outside hand** to protect the ball from the chasing defender
3. **Looking down at the dribble** → Eyes must stay up on the target — looking down kills court vision and telegraphs your next move
4. **Dribbling without a destination** → Every dribble must have a purpose; "go somewhere" with it
5. **Losing the inside foot plant habit** → Players sometimes break their footwork habit under pressure — the inside-foot plant must be automatic regardless of whether shooting or dribbling

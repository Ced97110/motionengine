# Combo Hop Back / Roll Out / Crossover Drill

## Objective
Train the most advanced hop-back counter: after a hop back, cross the ball to the opposite hand mid-roll-out to attack the defender's new position from the opposite angle — bilaterally off 1 dribble.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court — three-point line area transitioning to the paint
- Shot tracking table (FGA, FGM %, FTA, FTM %)

## Execution
1. Player starts at the three-point line with a live dribble.
2. **1-Dribble Hop Back to Crossover Left**: take 1 left-hand dribble, hop backward to gain space.
3. Instead of rolling out with the left hand, cross the ball to the right hand (crossover) and immediately attack to the right side.
4. Finish with a pull-up, layup, or floater on the right side.
5. Shoot 5 reps going left (hop back, crossover to right).
6. Step to the free-throw line; shoot 5 FTs with full routine.
7. **1-Dribble Hop Back to Crossover Right**: take 1 right-hand dribble, hop back, crossover left, attack left side.
8. Shoot 5 reps, then 5 FTs.
9. **2-Dribble versions**: repeat with 2 initiating dribbles before the hop back + crossover for L and R.
10. Total: **Workout #4-19 (40 shots)**: 4 sets × 5 combos + 4 × 5 FTs.

**Volume/Goal Standards:**
- Total shots: 40 | FG goal: standard 60% | FT goal: 85%

## Coaching Points
- **Crossover timing**: the crossover must happen immediately at the moment of the roll-out — not after the player stands up; a delayed crossover gives the defender time to recover
- **Protect the crossover dribble**: the ball must cross at or below knee height — a high crossover during the attack is a turnover
- **Attack the hip**: after the crossover, the player must attack the defender's new near hip, not the space behind them
- **Prerequisite mastery**: this drill should only be introduced after the player is consistent (≥60% FG) in the standalone hop-back and hop-back/roll-out drills
- Cue: **"Hop back. Cross. Explode."** — three words, three beats
- This is the highest-complexity live-ball combo move in the series; prioritize execution quality over volume

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: practice the hop back + crossover without a shot finish; just work the footwork and ball transfer
2. **Intermediate (40 shots)**: add the shot finish at 75% speed; focus on clean crossover mechanics
3. **Advanced**: have a defender shadow-defend the hop back; crossover only when the defender commits; finish vs. live passive defense

# Five-Second On-Ball Pressure Drill (Fratello)

## Objective
Train defenders to guard two hard dribbles and then immediately jam the ball with crossed wrists to prevent a pass, simulating the five-second violation situation.

## Setup
- Pairs of players; defenders inside the court, offensive players outside
- Run on half-court using sidelines, baseline, and half-court line as boundaries
- Can be run on any two lines on the court

## Execution
1. Defender with the ball passes to the offensive player outside the boundary
2. Offensive player makes two hard dribbles, picks up the ball, and raises it over his head
3. Defender steps and slides to guard the two dribbles
4. When the offensive player picks up the ball, defender immediately "jams" the ball: raises hands with **wrists crossed**, mirroring the ball to prevent any pass
5. Ball handler dribbles back and forth from starting position; each time he picks up the ball, defender re-jams
6. After reaching half-court, players exchange roles and return to baseline using the same method

## Coaching Points
- "Jam" = wrists crossed, hands at ball level, elbows bent close to body like a boxer
- Thumbs point toward own shoulders for quickest hand movement
- Never lunge or reach — maintain foot contact with the floor
- Believe you can deflect every pass; the mindset of active hands is the key
- This is a short but intense drill — quality over quantity
- "Don't concede any pass — make it uncomfortable"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Defender walks through jam technique stationary before adding the dribble guard
2. **Intermediate:** Full drill as described
3. **Advanced:** Add a live pass attempt by the offense; defender tries to deflect or intercept

# Post Getting Open — an iconic post player's System

## Summary
an iconic post player's comprehensive system for low-post and high-post players getting open against all types of defensive coverage. The system is organized by ball position and defensive stance, providing specific footwork and arm sequences for each situation. The key insight: "It's not stature or bulk that's most important but rather the size of the post player's will and heart." [S7, pp.39-42]

## When to Use
- When establishing position on the low block, high post, or weak side
- Against three-quarter overplaying, fronting, and body-check defense
- When the ball is on the wing, at the top of the key, or the weak side needs a cross cut

## Key Principles
1. Work with feet and buttocks first, arms and hands second.
2. "Don't release the catch position until the ball is in the air and over the head of the defender" — release too early and the defender recovers.
3. Start the weak-side cut from outside the lane (not near the ball or rim) — you need space to react and create.
4. Signal ball reversal with your hand when you're overplayed and cannot receive directly.
5. On all weak-to-strong side cuts, force a defensive reaction before starting the cut.

## Player Responsibilities
- **C/PF**: Execute the specific move dictated by where the defender is positioned; do not use the same move every time.

## Variations

### Low Post, Ball Side — Defender Three-Quarter Overplaying
**Reverse (facing the defender):**
- Feet: Face defender, head up (or lean head on defender's chest to create space). Put foot nearest midcourt between defender's feet. Make a big, strong reverse step over defender's front foot. "Sit" on defender's front leg and open up to the ball.
- Arms: Raise both arms bent 45°, palms open and up. If defender tries to come over the shoulder — put forearm under defender's elbow and lift it high (without fouling). If defender goes under the armpit — push defender's arm down. Both times, ask for ball with the opposite hand.

**Step-over (facing the passer):**
- Feet: Short but strong fake step toward midcourt with forward foot, followed by a long step over the defender's forward foot, sitting on his leg to get open.
- Arms: Put inside arm over the defender's inside arm, then "swim" (bring arm high then down, as if swimming) over the defender with light contact. Ask for the ball.

### Low Post, Ball Side — Defender in Front (Fronting)
**Keep the position:** Body parallel to baseline, forward leg (nearest sideline) between defender's legs, hip against defender's buttocks, corresponding arm bent 45° over defender's shoulder, opposite arm extended high palm-up asking for the ball. This approach avoids fouling versus using a forearm against the defender's back.

**Ball reversal:** Signal with hand to reverse ball to weak-side wing. While ball is in the air, pin down the defender, then receive from the wing or teammate at the top.

**Step back and turn around:** Short step back, then big step over the defender's front foot, "sit" on his front leg.

**Release contact and come back:** Release contact, step to middle of lane, come back. Then:
- If defender body-checks or is face-to-face: spin around the defender.
- If defender is in help stance with inside foot forward: V-cut (step one way, then opposite).

### Low Post, Ball at Top of Key — Duck In
The duck-in pass from the top is one of the most effective for a high-percentage shot near the basket.

**Defender body check / face-to-face:**
Put forward foot between defender's feet, make a turnaround move, long quick aggressive step over defender's front foot, swim over defender's front arm simultaneously.
- If defender's front arm goes high: push it up with bent forearm at middle of defender's forearm.
- If defender extends arm below armpit: push defender's forearm down.

**Defender three-quarter overplaying:**
Short fake step with forward foot (to close distance). Momentarily grab the wrist of defender's forward arm, push it down and swim over. Long aggressive step over defender's front foot, sit on defender's front leg, ask for the ball.

### High Post, Ball at Top of Lane
Same moves as low post. Use reverse move when facing the defender, step-over when back to the basket.

### Low Post, Weak Side — Baseline Cut
Must start the cut from OUTSIDE the lane (not near the rim) to have enough space to react.

**Defender body check / face-to-face:** Foot nearest midcourt between defender's feet, reverse to seal, ask for ball.

**Defender three-quarter overplaying:** V-cut, push down defender's extended arm, big step over defender's front leg.

**Defender double reaction:** After V-cut, if defender switches to face-to-face, combine both moves: V-cut brings defender high, then reverse with midcourt foot, face baseline, seal defender, receive the ball.

**Ball reversal:** If overplayed, signal ball reversal to other wing/corner. While ball is in air, go deep into lane, step-over or reverse to seal the defender under the basket, ask for ball.

### Using a Screen to Get Open
- **Horizontal screen (baseline):** Right after the screened teammate cuts, step over the defender's feet OR use a reverse to seal and ask for the ball.
- **Vertical screen (screener's back to ball):** Right after screened teammate cuts, reverse to open up to the ball, seal, ask for the ball.
- **Fake a screen:** Instead of actually screening, once near the teammate, immediately open up and ask for the ball.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Starting the weak-side cut too close to the rim** → You'll be forced to receive away from the basket. Start outside the lane.
2. **Releasing position before ball is in the air** → Lets the defender recover and deny the pass. Hold until the ball is over the defender's head.
3. **Both arms extended waiting for the catch** → Invites steals from behind. Use one target hand.

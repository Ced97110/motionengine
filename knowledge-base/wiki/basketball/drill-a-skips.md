# A-Skips

## Objective
Increase hip extension and flexion strength and ankle-muscle stiffness — the key mechanical components of high stride frequency at sprinting speed.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Open area, 20-40 yards
- Performed after mastering A-March Walk; part of the A-B form sequence

## Execution
1. Perform the same mechanics as the [[drill-a-march-walk]]
2. Add a **skip** to create airborne time between contacts
3. While in the air, emphasize the **high recovery posture** practiced in the A-March Walk (knee high, ankle dorsiflexed, ankle close to gluteus)
4. Keep the upper body **upright and steady** at all times
5. Foot strike should be **quiet but explosive** — emphasize muscle stiffness at the ankle
6. **Do not slam the foot onto the ground** [S8, p.29]

## Coaching Points
- "Quiet but explosive" — the foot should create an elastic spring contact, not a heavy stomp
- "Ankle stiffness" — the ankle should act like a rigid lever at ground contact, not a soft absorber
- High recovery position must be maintained in the air — athletes often let the knee drop too soon
- Upper body stays upright and tall; no forward lean in this drill

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Return to [[drill-a-march-walk]] if form breaks down
2. **Intermediate**: A-Skips as described — controlled pace with emphasis on form
3. **Advanced**: A-Form Runs — perform full sprint-mechanics form runs emphasizing the A-skip technique but at running speed. Skipping Paw Drill — A-skip with an aggressive pawing motion (quick leg extension followed by explosive hip extension) to simulate ground-contact mechanics at max speed [S8, p.29]

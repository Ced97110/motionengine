# Circle Box-Out Drill

## Objective
Practice all defensive box-out pivot techniques (front pivot for on-ball defenders, reverse pivot for off-ball defenders) and all three offensive rebounding counter-techniques simultaneously across multiple court circles in a competitive 1-on-1 format. [S6, pp.217-220]

## Setup
- **Players**: 4 minimum (2 pairs); up to 28 players across all circles simultaneously
- **Court**: Use any of the three circles/semicircles on a main court — center jump circle and both free-throw line semicircles; up to 7 circles available including side courts
- **Equipment**: One basketball per circle, placed in the **center** of the circle
- **Pairs**: Each circle holds 2 pairs of players
- **Defensive players**: Line up in various on-the-ball and off-the-ball defensive stances at the **outer edge** of the circle, facing outward toward their offensive opponent
- **Offensive players**: Line up **one full step to the outside** of their defender, facing their defender and the ball in the center of the circle
- Maximum capacity: 7 circles × 2 pairs × 2 players each = **28 players working simultaneously** — 14 practicing defensive boxing out, 14 practicing offensive rebounding [S6, p.217]

## Execution
1. An observing coach yells **"Shot!"** to start each repetition
2. Each **defensive player** initiates contact with the appropriate pivot based on their assigned scenario (see Scenarios below)
3. The defender maintains contact using short, choppy steps, elbows high and locked in a V, hands up
4. The **offensive player** attempts to use one of the three offensive rebounding methods to legally defeat the box-out and touch the basketball in the center of the circle
5. The drill runs for a **predetermined time limit** (3–4 seconds recommended)

**Scoring:**
- Defender WINS if the offensive player cannot touch the basketball for the full time limit
- Defender LOSES if the offensive player legally pushes the defender into the center of the circle (simulates being shoved under the basket in a game)
- If the offensive player can push the defender all the way to the ball, that defender will allow himself to be pushed under the basket in a real game [S6, p.219]

## Three Scenarios
Each player must practice all three scenarios, both offensively and defensively, in every session. **2 reps on defense, then 2 reps on offense** per scenario before rotating to the next:

### Scenario 1: On-the-Ball (Shooter)
- Offensive player pretends to shoot the ball
- Defensive player (X) works on **front (crossover) pivot** technique — reading the direction the "shooter" moves and stepping across into their path
- Coaching focus: hand high during shot, forward momentum, crossover step directly into the rebounder's path [S6, p.219]

### Scenario 2: One Pass Away
- Offensive player is in a one-pass-away denial position
- Defensive player is in a denial stance
- Defender uses **reverse pivot** off the trail (lower) foot, swinging the lead (top) foot into the path the offensive player must take
- The top foot is the free foot; the bottom foot is the pivot foot [S6, pp.207-208]

### Scenario 3: More Than One Pass Away (Helpside)
- Offensive player is a helpside (weakside) player more than one pass from the ball
- Defensive player fans toward the offensive player's high (inside) shoulder to force a predictable baseline path
- Defender initiates contact with a **reverse pivot** off the lower (baseline) foot, ideally making contact outside the imaginary free throw lane
- Coaching focus: fan the opponent, reverse pivot outside the lane, butt-to-gut contact [S6, pp.209-212]

## Coaching Points
- Watch for **wrong pivot type**: on-ball defenders must use front pivot; off-ball defenders must use reverse pivot — mixing these up is the most common error [S6, p.204]
- Remind defenders: *"Keep your elbows high and locked"* — the V formed by the bent elbows controls the offensive rebounder [S6, p.203]
- Watch for defenders **reaching down** to hold the opponent — call it as a foul and correct immediately [S6, p.203]
- Watch for defenders being **pushed into the center of the circle** — if they allow this in the drill, they'll allow it in a game [S6, p.219]
- For helpside defenders: ensure contact is initiated at the outer edge of the circle (representing outside the free throw lane in a game) [S6, p.210]
- Watch offensive players for which of the three counter-methods they use — give feedback on technique for each: reverse spin, swim move, or step-back read
- This drill serves double duty: **offense and defense work simultaneously** — extremely time-efficient for a full squad [S6, p.217]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through each scenario — no competition; coach narrates the correct pivot and body position at each step before adding live reps
2. **Intermediate**: Live 1-on-1 with 3-second time limit; coach calls the scenario before each rep
3. **Advanced**: Live 1-on-1 with 4-second time limit; defender must correctly identify and execute the appropriate pivot type without a coach call; rotate scenarios without a break

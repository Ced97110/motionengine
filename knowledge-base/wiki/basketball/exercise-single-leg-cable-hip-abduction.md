# Single-Leg Cable Hip Abduction

## Objective
Isolate and strengthen the hip abductors (gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, tensor fascia latae) to improve lateral stability, defensive slide mechanics, and reduce knee valgus and ACL injury risk.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Low-pulley cable machine
- Ankle cuff attachment
- Light weight (isolation exercise — form over load)

## Execution
1. Attach an ankle cuff to the low-pulley cable and fasten it to the anklebone of the exercising leg (the leg **farther** from the pulley).
2. Stand perpendicular to the pulley. Place the hand closer to the cable machine on the machine handle for balance.
3. Stand far enough from the machine to ensure tension on the cable before the movement begins.
4. Raise the exercising leg 1–2 inches off the floor — this is the beginning position.
5. Slowly and with control, abduct the hip to move the exercising leg **laterally away** from the body in an arc.
6. Continue until the hip position can no longer be maintained (hip begins to hike or tilt) — do not sacrifice posture for range.
7. Pause at peak abduction, then slowly lower the leg under control.
8. Complete all reps on one leg, then switch.

**Breathing:** Inhale during the outward (abduction) movement; exhale during the return.

## Coaching Points
- "Light weight, quality contraction" — use a light weight and focus on feeling the gluteus medius working, not momentum
- Keep the hips centered over the vertical line of the body — do not allow the stance hip to hike or the trunk to lean away
- The exercising leg moves **directly to the side** in an arc — not forward, not backward
- **Keep feet parallel** during the movement; avoid external rotation of the exercising thigh
- Do not let the hips twist or rotate as the leg lifts — maintain a neutral spine throughout

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Very light cable load; focus entirely on hip position and movement arc
2. **Intermediate**: Gradually increase cable load while maintaining perfect posture; add a 1-second pause at peak abduction
3. **Advanced**: Perform without holding the machine handle (free-standing) for additional balance demand

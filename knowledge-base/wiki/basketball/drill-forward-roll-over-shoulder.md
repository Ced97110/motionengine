# Forward Roll Over Shoulder (Tumbling Drill)

## Objective
Develop total-body agility and kinesthetic awareness by performing a controlled forward roll over the shoulder and returning to a standing position. [S8, p.121]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Soft grass or gymnastics mat (never on hard flooring without adequate padding)
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance with the left foot forward.
2. Bend over and start to fall forward.
3. As you are about to make contact with the ground, roll over the left shoulder.
4. Continue the roll and come back up to your feet.
5. Perform forward rolls over **both shoulders** and with **either foot forward**. [S8, p.121]

## Coaching Points
- The roll should be over the shoulder, not directly on the neck or head — tuck the chin to the chest
- Keep the body curled into a tight ball throughout the roll for smooth execution
- Practice on both sides equally — bilateral training prevents asymmetrical movement habits
- The goal is to return to feet in a balanced, ready athletic position — not just survive the roll
- Kinesthetic awareness means understanding where your body is in space at all times

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Static forward roll from kneeling position on a mat
2. **Intermediate**: Full standing forward roll as described
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Add a sprint in any direction before or after the tumbling drill
   - React to any stimuli after tumbling (e.g., a visual cue to run to a cone)
   - Add a basketball-specific skill (e.g., catch a pass) after tumbling [S8, p.121]

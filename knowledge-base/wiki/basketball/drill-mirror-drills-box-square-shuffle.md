# Mirror Drills in Box With Square Shuffle

## Objective
Improve lower-body quickness in the lateral direction and reactive ability by constraining both athletes to shuffle only on the outside lines of their cone boxes while one leads and the other mirrors. [S8, p.191]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Two boxes each marked with **4 cones**, with **5 yards (4.6 meters) between each cone** (marked lines are preferable for accuracy)
- Athletes start on opposite lines of their respective boxes

<!-- DIAGRAM: p.191 shows two adjacent 5-yd × 5-yd boxes. Commander starts on one side of the left box, mirror starts on the same relative line of the right box. Arrows show lateral shuffling along the outside edges only. -->

## Execution
1. Commander starts on one line of their box; mirror starts on the **same relative line** of their adjacent box.
2. Commander **shuffles only along the outside lines** of the box (no cutting through the middle).
3. Mirror athlete must **react and mirror** the commander by shuffling along the same lines of their own box.
4. The drill stays on the **outside perimeter** of each box — no cutting through the interior.
5. Rotate commander and mirror roles. [S8, p.191]

## Coaching Points
- Constraint (outside lines only) forces pure lateral shuffle mechanics — no shortcutting
- Stay low in an athletic stance throughout — "defensive slide position the whole time"
- Mirror athlete must read the commander's shoulders and hips, not wait for the feet to move
- This drill is directly transferable to defensive containment and lateral closeout movement
- Use marked lines for precision — it prevents cutting corners and ensures proper footwork

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Commander moves at 50–60% speed; mirror focuses on reading cues
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed shuffling; commander changes direction randomly
3. **Advanced**: Add a basketball and defensive voice commands to simulate game-like defensive situations

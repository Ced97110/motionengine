# Shots-a-Day Club

## Summary
The Shots-a-Day Club is a high-volume daily shooting framework designed to develop work ethic, shot consistency, and a full repertoire of game-speed scoring moves. Players commit to completing structured workouts of 300 or 500+ shots every day, incorporating spot shots, shot-fake series, rip-step series, free throws, and game-speed three-point attempts. The program is scalable — workouts can be reduced for younger or less experienced athletes — but is fundamentally designed for the most dedicated players.

The philosophy is simple: repetition at game speed breeds automaticity. By completing hundreds of shots daily from a variety of moves and angles, players internalize footwork and release mechanics so that in-game shooting becomes instinctive. [S3, pp.181-185]

## When to Use
- Daily individual pre-practice or post-practice workout routine
- Off-season skill development phase when practice volume is reduced
- For players who want to elevate their scoring versatility beyond the team setting
- As a team accountability structure — coaches can require club membership as an off-season standard

## Key Principles
1. **Game-speed reps only** — every shot is taken off one or two dribbles, as if a defender is present; no casual shooting allowed
2. **Alternate directions** — all dribble-move shots are taken to both left and right to build two-way scoring ability
3. **Free throws between sets** — FTs serve as active recovery and pressure simulation (2 FTs between each shot group in the 300+ workout, 4 FTs in the 500+ workout)
4. **Track makes and percentages** — players record FGA/FGM, 3PTA/3PTM, FTA/FTM each session to monitor progress over time
5. **Scale to the athlete** — if the full workout cannot be completed, reduce reps proportionally rather than skipping move categories
6. **Consistent shot menu** — both workouts use the same move taxonomy (Shot Fake series + Rip Step series + Game Speed 3PT), ensuring balanced development across all scoring actions

## Shot Move Taxonomy
Both the 300+ and 500+ workouts are built from two primary move families:

### Shot Fake (SF) Series
- **SF Stride Stop (L/R)** — shot fake into a stride stop pull-up
- **SF Stride Stop Step-Through (L/R)** — shot fake, stride stop, then step through the defender
- **SF Big Hop (L/R)** — shot fake into a two-foot big-hop landing pull-up
- **SF Hop Back 3s (L/R)** — shot fake into a backward hop for a three-pointer
- **SF Hop Back (HB) (L/R)** — standard hop back pull-up from midrange
- **SF HB to Roll Out (L/R)** — hop back, then roll-out dribble escape into a pull-up
- **SF HB to Cross (L/R)** — hop back, then crossover dribble into a pull-up

### Rip Step Series
- **Rip Step Stride Stop (L/R)**
- **Rip Step Stride Stop Step-Through (L/R)**
- **Rip Step Big Hop (L/R)**
- **Rip Step Hop Back 3s (L/R)**
- **Rip Step Hop Back (L/R)**
- **Rip Step HB to Roll Out (L/R)**
- **Rip Step HB to Cross (L/R)**

### Volume Comparison
| Move | 300+ Club | 500+ Club |
|---|---|---|
| Spot Shots (per side) | 10 | 20 |
| FTs (between sets) | 2 | 4 |
| Each SF/Rip move (L or R) | 5 | 10 |
| Game Speed 3PT sets | 6×10 = 60 | 12×10 = 120 |
| Total FTs | 60 | 120 |
| **Total Shots** | **308** | **520** |

## Performance Benchmarks
| Category | 300+ Club Target | 500+ Club Target |
|---|---|---|
| FG% | 60% (96-140 FGM) | 60% (120-200 FGM) |
| 3PT% | 60% (48-80 3PTM) | 60% (96-160 3PTM) |
| FT% | 85% (75-88 FTM) | 85% (136-160 FTM) |

## Common Mistakes
1. **Taking lazy spot shots instead of game-speed reps** → Enforce the rule: every shot comes off at least one dribble, simulating a live-ball move
2. **Skipping free throws** → FTs are part of the workout structure; they build mental resilience under fatigue
3. **Only shooting to the dominant side** → Alternate L/R for every move; track each direction separately
4. **Abandoning the workout when results are poor** → Poor shooting days reveal mechanical flaws — push through and correct rather than quit
5. **Starting at 500+ before building base** → Begin with the 300+ club, build consistency, then progress

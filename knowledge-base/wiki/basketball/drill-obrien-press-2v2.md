# O'Brien Press 2-on-2 Drill

## Objective
Practice both White and Black press alignments in a live 2-on-2 setting — deny the inbounds, force the speed dribble, execute the trap, and back-tip out of broken traps.

## Setup
- One side of the court; imaginary out-of-bounds line from basket to basket down the middle
- Inbounder is now a live **player** (not a coach)
- 2 offensive players, 2 defenders
- Two variations:
  - **White press**: Inbounder is covered by a defender
  - **Black press**: Inbounder is not covered (defender in off position)

## Execution
1. Start by designating **white** or **black** press alignment.
2. Defenders deny the inbounds pass (white: defender on inbounder; black: defender supporting off-ball).
3. If the ball is inbounded, play is **live 2-on-2**.
4. Defenders look to force a speed dribble and execute a trap.
5. Do not worry about the open man — defenders try to get a piece of every pass.
6. If the pass is completed after the trap, defenders sprint out of the trap and **back-tip the ball handler** who now has a breakaway opportunity.
7. Because of the limited space on one side of the court, inbounding against either press should be very difficult. [S7, p.250]

## Coaching Points
- Limited court space = maximum denial pressure; defenders should develop confidence in their ability to deny.
- "The emphasis is on denying the inbounds pass. We're always emphasizing trying to deflect a pass." [S7, p.250]
- When the trap is executed and a pass escapes, defenders immediately sprint after the ball handler for a back-tip — never concede the easy basket.
- Use this drill to build confidence in the front line of the press before expanding to 3-on-3 or 4-on-4.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: White press only; offense walks through; focus on denial technique.
2. **Intermediate**: Live 2v2 white then black; one rep each alignment.
3. **Advanced**: Alternate white/black press on each possession; defenders must read which press to use from the inbounder's body language.

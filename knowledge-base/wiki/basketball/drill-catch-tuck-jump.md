# Catch Tuck Jump (Step 8)

## Objective
Develop forward trunk compression power and maximal tuck technique — drawing the thighs fully against the chest using the arms — to build the prerequisite strength for suicide jumps and advanced acrobatic movements.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any open space (no equipment required)

## Execution
1. Stand shoulder-width apart, body flexed and ready for action. [S9]
2. Dip down into a squat (same depth as a squat jump).
3. Explode upward using lower-body power and total-body spring.
4. Explosively draw the knees as close to the chest as possible.
5. At the peak of the jump, pull the shins into the body with the hands — thighs should be completely compressed against the chest at this point. [S9]
6. Release the shins and throw the legs back down for landing.
7. Land softly, catching most weight on the balls of the feet.
8. Use the elastic rebound immediately to repeat.

## Coaching Points
- This step adds *forward trunk power* to the leg-and-hip power of the basic tuck jump — the trunk must actively compress the legs [S9]
- "Pulling in" the legs with the arms during a tight tuck is a key pattern for more complex movements like front and back flips [S9]
- Do not attempt to pull the tuck too tightly at first — get arms past the legs, then wrap, then pull in firmly
- At full mastery: thighs fully against chest, knees under the chin [S9]
- Land softly — absorb through balls of feet and full kinetic chain

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform the best tuck available, and try to get arms to meet beyond the legs (no shin grip yet) [S9]
2. **Intermediate**: Wrap arms around legs during the tuck
3. **Advanced**: Pull legs in tightly with the arms; advance toward knees-under-chin height [S9]

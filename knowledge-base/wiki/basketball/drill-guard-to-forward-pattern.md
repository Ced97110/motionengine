# Guard-to-Forward Pass Pattern (LA Offense)

## Objective
Teach players the three continuity options that result from the guard-to-forward entry pass in the LA Offense, developing simultaneous ball and player movement off a two-guard front.

## Setup
- Half court, 5 players
- Two-guard front: O1 and O2 at guard positions; O3 at strong-side wing (free-throw line extended); O4 at the strong-side forward (free-throw line extended, opposite side); O5 at high post
- Begin with no defense or token defense; progress to live defense

## Execution

### Setup Phase
1. O1 passes to O4 (guard-to-forward entry), takes a hard jab step to the middle, and attempts to get head and shoulder around the defender for an inside cut to the basket.
2. If O1 is open, O4 returns the pass immediately. If not, O1 continues to the strong-side low block.
3. As O1 cuts, O5 turns and screens for O3, who relocates to the strong-side elbow looking for a catch-and-shoot.
4. O2 V-cuts to the top of the key and gets a pass from O4.
5. O5 relocates to the weak-side low post.

### Option 1 (Ball Reversal to Corner)
5. As O2 receives the pass, O1 turns out off O5's baseline screen. O3 continues toward the basket and relocates to the low block opposite O5.
6. After O4 passes to O2, O4 pins down on O3's defender; O3 pops out. This puts O4 at the basket for the rebound.
7. O2 takes one or at most two dribbles to reverse the ball. O2 passes to O1, who can shoot, pass to O5, or call O5 out for a sideline PnR.
8. After passing to O1, O2 turns and screens for O3 coming off O4's down screen.

### Option 2 (Dribble Reversal Counter — Timeout Call)
5. O2 catches at the top of the key. O4 screens down for O3; O1 turns out off O5.
6. O2 starts toward O1, reverses the dribble, and passes to O3.
7. If O2 reverses and dribbles toward O3, O5 steps into the middle for a post-up opportunity on X5.
8. This effective counter play is best called during a timeout.

### Option 3 (Staggered Double Screen for O3)
5. O2 has the ball at the top and rotates it to O1.
6. O2 then reverses and sets a staggered double screen with O4 for O3.
7. O3 works off the screens, looking for a catch-and-shoot.
8. O5 is a post-up option. O1 can also run a pick-and-roll with O5.

## Coaching Points
- O1's jab step inside cut must be aggressive — "get your head and shoulder around the defender."
- O5's screen for O3 should open immediately as O1 cuts, not after the pass is made.
- In Option 1, O2 must take no more than two dribbles before reversing — reinforce ball movement habits.
- Option 2 is a counter, not a default — practice recognizing when to use it.
- "Either guard can start the play to either side" — practice in both directions.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through Setup + Option 1 only with no defense.
2. **Intermediate**: Add token defense; practice all three options in a round-robin sequence.
3. **Advanced**: Live defense; offense must read and select the correct option based on defensive coverage.

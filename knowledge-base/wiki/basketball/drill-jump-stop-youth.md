# Jump Stop Drill (Youth)

## Objective
Teach players to execute a balanced two-foot jump stop at game speed, establishing either foot as the pivot foot to preserve maximum offensive options.

## Setup
- Players line up on the baseline in multiple vertical lines across the court
- No basketballs required for beginner level
- Full court
- Coach positions in front of players and backpedals

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up on the baseline
2. They begin running downcourt at half-speed
3. When the coach says "jump stop," players execute the jump stop
4. On "go," players sprint again
5. Coach may give commands at different court locations from day to day
6. **Key**: Coach gets in front of players and backpedals before turning and sprinting a step toward the racing team — this gives a **visual command** for the jump stop instead of a voice command (develops see-and-react skill)

**Option**: If players have trouble jump-stopping, have them sprint directly toward the coach and jump-stop on arrival. Coach carefully observes and corrects technique.

### Intermediate
- Give each player a ball; tell players which dribbling move to execute
- At the end of the dribbling move, the player jump-stops
- Lines on the court serve as points where the player executes the move followed by the jump stop
- Player dribbles to the next line and repeats

### Advanced
1. Players execute a designated dribbling move then a jump stop, then also pivot (reverse or front)
2. Sequence: dribble back to start, execute dribbling move + jump stop + pivot, moving progressively down the floor: free-throw line extended → half-court → far free-throw line extended → far endline
2. Same as above but alternate dribbling moves (in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin) and alternate front/reverse pivots

## Coaching Points
- **Lower the center of gravity**: The greater the speed, the lower the buttocks must be — "sit in a chair as you stop"
- **Land on both feet simultaneously**: The leap should be as low to the floor as possible, not a high jump; buttocks may touch the back of the legs on landing
- **Back position**: Back straight, may be bent slightly forward; torso bent at the waist; more speed = more bend
- **Knee angle**: Bent between 90 and 180 degrees
- **Two pivot feet**: "When the stop is completed, you may move either foot to establish the pivot foot" — this is the jump stop's greatest advantage over the stride stop
- "The jump stop gives you two pivot feet — you can pivot in any direction without traveling"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Running at half-speed with coach verbal/visual command to stop
2. **Intermediate**: Dribbling move into jump stop on court lines
3. **Advanced**: Dribbling move + jump stop + front or reverse pivot in sequence down the full court

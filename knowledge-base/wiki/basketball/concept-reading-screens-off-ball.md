# Reading Screens Off the Ball

## Summary
The ability of an off-ball player to read their defender while using a screen is one of the most important skills in half-court offense. Whether it's a down screen, double screen, or flare screen, the cutter must make an instant decision that maximizes the advantage created by the screener. A predictable cutter — one who always pops, always curls, or always cuts baseline — allows the defense to cheat and negate the screen action entirely.

Coaches who run screen-heavy offenses (box sets, flex, zipper actions) must teach this skill explicitly, not assume players will develop it naturally.

## When to Use
- Any half-court possession featuring down screens, double screens, or flare screens for perimeter players
- Designed plays (SLOB, ATO) where a shooter must free themselves off one or multiple screens
- When the offense needs a catch-and-shoot opportunity for a specific player

## Key Principles
1. **Read the defender's position before committing to a cut.** Is the defender trailing, on the high side, cheating under? The answer dictates the cut direction.
2. **Pop when the defender trails or goes under the screen.** The cutter has space to receive the ball with their feet set for a catch-and-shoot.
3. **Curl when the defender chases tight and tries to follow through the screen.** Curling forces the defender into the screener's body and creates a shorter path to the basket.
4. **Backdoor when the defender overplays the anticipated cut.** If the defender cheats to deny the pop, a hard backdoor cut is the correct counter.
5. **Sell the fake before the cut.** Take 1-2 steps in the opposite direction of the intended cut to put the defender off-balance. "Don't give the action away early." [S4, p.79]
6. **Sprint off screens.** A slow cutter allows the defender to recover. Shoulders low, change of direction explosive.
7. **Catch in the shot pocket, ready to shoot.** The passer must deliver to the inside shoulder of the cutter so the cutter can receive and shoot in one motion. [S4, p.92]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Often the passer — must read the cutter's direction and deliver to the inside shoulder or shot pocket. Don't throw the pass early or late.
- **SG**: Typically the primary cutter (shooter). Read the defender, sell the fake, commit fully to the chosen direction.
- **SF**: Secondary cutter or screener. Must set a legal, stationary screen and hold position until the cutter clears.
- **PF**: Often the screener. Set the screen, hold it, then read whether to roll, pop, or flash.
- **C**: Heavy-duty screener in box-set plays — must set physical screens that force defenders to make contact decisions.

## Variations
### Pop (Catch-and-Shoot)
Used when the defender goes under the screen. Cutter pops to the perimeter with feet ready to shoot immediately. Requires the passer to recognize the pop and deliver the ball immediately.

### Curl
Used when the defender tries to fight over the top of the screen. Cutter curls tight around the screener's body, using the screener as a barrier between themselves and the defender. Often leads to a midrange shot or drive rather than a three.

### Backdoor
Used when the defender heavily anticipates and cheats to deny the pop. Cutter plants the outside foot and cuts hard to the basket, expecting a lob or bounce pass.

### Flare
Used when a secondary screen is set away from the ball (flare screen). Cutter sprints to the perimeter (often corner or wing) looking for a skip pass. Must not telegraph the flare direction.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Telegraphing the cut direction** with eyes or body posture → correction: keep a neutral stance and look away from the intended cut direction until the last moment.
2. **Cutting too slowly off the screen** → correction: sprint off every screen as if it's the last one; defense recovers on slow cuts.
3. **Choosing the wrong option** (e.g., popping when curling would be open) → correction: coach must drill both options in practice and demand the read, not the pre-programmed action.
4. **Catching outside the shot pocket** (too far from the body) → correction: passer delivers to the inside shoulder; catcher prepares hands early.
5. **Screener moving prematurely** (moving screen violation) → correction: screener holds position until the cutter has completely cleared. [S4, p.77]

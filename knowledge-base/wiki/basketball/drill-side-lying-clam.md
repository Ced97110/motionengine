# Side-Lying Clam (ACL Ligament Dominance)

## Objective
Develop gluteal and hip external rotator strength to prevent the knee from collapsing inward (dynamic valgus) during basketball cuts, landings, and pivots.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Floor or mat
- Equipment: elastic resistance band

## Execution
1. Place an elastic band just above both knees.
2. Lie on your side. Flex knees and hips to approximately 90°. Head, shoulder, knee, and ankle should be in a straight line.
3. While maintaining the side-lying position and keeping the feet together, separate the knees by rotating the top leg outward — creating tension in the band.
4. Return to the starting position (feet remain together throughout; all motion occurs at the hip).
5. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions, then switch sides.

## Coaching Points
- "All the motion is at the hip — the feet stay together"
- Avoid excessive rotation of the upper body — the torso must stay still; only the top leg moves
- Maintain the straight line from head → shoulders → knees → ankles; hips should not roll backward
- The range of motion is smaller than it looks — quality over quantity
- If the upper body is rotating, the player is compensating; reduce band resistance or reps [S2, p.168]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No band — bodyweight only, mastering the alignment and isolated hip rotation
2. **Intermediate**: Light resistance band, 3 × 10 each side
3. **Advanced**: Heavier resistance band; add a brief pause at the top of each rep (1-2 seconds)

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, tensor fasciae latae, piriformis, obturator externus, quadratus femoris
- **Secondary**: Sartorius

# X-Out Into Single-Double (SLOB)

## Overview
A frontcourt side out-of-bounds play (used as a baseline set) designed to free a perimeter shooter off a staggered double screen when down 3 points. Three best perimeter shooters occupy O1, O2, and O3. The single-double sequential screening action produces two read options for O1.

## Formation
- **O1**: Receives the inbounds pass in the middle of the court beyond the three-point line
- **O2**: Realigns directly under the basket in the middle of the free-throw lane
- **O3**: Relocates to the left-side free-throw line (approximately 12 inches/30 cm higher than O2)
- **O4**: Just off the left block — positioned as the second screen in the stagger
- **O5**: Screener at the start of the action

## Phases

### Phase 1: Inbounds Entry
- Players begin in the X-out formation
- **O1** receives the inbounds pass beyond the three-point line in the middle of the court
- As the inbounds pass is made, **O3 relocates** to the left-side free-throw line and **O2 realigns** under the basket in the free-throw lane

### Phase 2: Single Screen for O2 (Cross Screen)
- **O3** sets up ~12 inches higher than O2 in the lane
- **O3 steps into the free-throw lane** for a **cross screen** on O2's defender
- **O2 uses a jab step** to the middle and goes off the **staggered double screen** — O3 is the first screen, O4 is the second screen
- O2 pops to the wing or corner looking to shoot

### Phase 3: Screen for O3 (Off O5's Screen)
- After O2 clears, **O3 uses O5's screen** and cuts to the **top of the key**
- O3 looks for a catch-and-shoot three-pointer from the top

### Phase 4: O1 Reads
1. **Pass to O2** coming off the stagger — O2 can shoot or rotate for a three-point shot
2. **Pass to O3** at the top of the key — catch-and-shoot three-pointer

## Key Coaching Points
- The three best perimeter shooters must occupy O1, O2, and O3
- O3's positioning (~12 inches higher than O2) is critical for proper cross-screen angle
- The stagger sequence (O3 first, O4 second) must be coordinated with the inbounds catch timing
- O2's jab step to the middle sets up the screen — without the setup, the defender can cheat
- O1 is the decision-maker: both O2 and O3 have shooting options

## Counters
- If the defense switches or hedges the stagger, O2 should fade baseline rather than curl
- O3's cut to the top of the key off O5 is automatic after the O2 action — always have a top option

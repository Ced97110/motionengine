# Shot Fake → Big Hop Pull-Up

## Summary
The shot fake into big hop is a variation of the shot fake drive family where, after the attack dribble(s), the offensive player gathers with a wide, explosive two-foot simultaneous jump stop (the "big hop") rather than a stride stop. The big hop allows the player to load both legs simultaneously for a more explosive upward launch — often used for floaters, mid-range jumpers, and short-corner pull-ups where creating maximum elevation quickly is the priority.

The move is practiced in four combinations: 1-dribble left, 1-dribble right, 2-dribble left, and 2-dribble right. [S3]

## When to Use
- Against closing defenders where a quick, high-arc shot is needed
- When operating in tight spaces near the paint or short corner
- When the stride stop doesn't generate enough elevation on the shot
- Against shot-blockers — the big hop generates more vertical explosion than a stride stop
- When pulling up off a speed dribble where the stride stop is difficult to execute cleanly

## Key Principles
1. **Explosive two-foot gather** — both feet land simultaneously, wide apart (approximately shoulder-width or slightly wider), loading both legs equally for the jump.
2. **Stay low during the hop** — the landing needs to be bent-knee to absorb and immediately redirect force upward.
3. **Ball protection on the gather** — bring the ball to the shot pocket during the hop, not after.
4. **Minimal hang time on the ground** — the big hop is a reactive movement; land and fire immediately (stretch-shortening cycle).
5. **Same fake technique** — the shot fake setup is identical to all SF moves; do not telegraph by changing body language.
6. **Alternate L/R and 1/2 dribble** — all four combinations must be equally trained.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF**: Primary skill — especially valuable for guards finishing in traffic.
- **PF**: Useful in face-up elbow situations.

## Variations
### 1-Dribble Big Hop (L/R)
One dribble toward the basket, big hop stop, shoot.

### 2-Dribble Big Hop (L/R)
Two dribbles to cover more ground or shake the defender, big hop stop, shoot.

### Big Hop Floater
Big hop gather but instead of a standard jump shot, finish with a high-arc floater over the rim protector.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Landing on one foot instead of two** → Loses the simultaneous power advantage of the big hop; revert to stride stop drill if this occurs.
2. **Hopping too far** → Lose balance and drift; the hop should be controlled forward movement, not a lunge.
3. **Standing up before the hop** → Stay low through the dribble and into the hop; standing up telegraphs the stop.
4. **Rushing the shot** → Land fully, feel the ground, then explode up — a rushed shot loses accuracy.

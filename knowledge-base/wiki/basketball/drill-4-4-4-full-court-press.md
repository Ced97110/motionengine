# 4-4-4 Full-Court Press Drill (O'Brien)

## Objective
Teach full-court press execution, fast-break defense, and transition simultaneously in a continuous live drill using three rotating teams of four.

## Setup
- 12 players split into three teams: A, B, and C (4 players each)
- **Team A**: ball at backcourt top of key, attacking the far basket
- **Team B**: 2 defenders in tandem protecting the far basket; 2 players at half-court sidelines (out of bounds)
- **Team C**: 2 defenders in tandem at the near basket; 2 players at half-court sidelines (out of bounds)
- Full court

## Execution
1. **Team A attacks Team B** in a 4-on-2 tandem fast break to the far basket
2. Team B's two half-court players cannot enter the action until the ball is dribbled/passed over the center line — they must then **touch the center circle** before they can defend
3. **If Team A scores**: Team A immediately presses Team B using the White Press (scored in the lane) or Black Press (scored from outside)
4. **Team A can press Team B only to half-court** — Team C takes over the defense at half-court
5. **If Team B beats the press**: Team C defends at half-court; two Team C players come onto the floor by touching the center line when Team B crosses midcourt
6. Team A then sets up two tandem defenders at the near basket and two at half-court out of bounds for the next rotation
7. **If Team A misses**: Team B rebounds and fast-breaks against Team C at the far end, with the same tandem/trailer rules
8. **Scoring**: Run live to a set score; winners get a water break, losers run

## Coaching Points
- Coaching staff must balance nonstop aggressiveness with teaching the rules of the press
- Team A must recognize whether they scored in the lane or on a jump shot to call the correct press
- Trailers must sprint to the center circle on every trip — this discipline is enforced strictly
- "If the ball crosses half-court past your press, your press is done — get back"
- Conditioning is the purpose as much as technique: players must run at full speed for an entire game

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk Team A through White/Black press alignment before going live; slow down transitions
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill as described; coach calls "White" or "Black" instead of letting Team A decide
3. **Advanced**: Team A makes the press decision based on how they scored — full game-speed execution with competitive scoring

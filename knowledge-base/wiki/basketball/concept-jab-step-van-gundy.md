# Jab Step (Van Gundy)

## Summary
The jab step is the foundation of all basic combined perimeter moves. Named after a boxer's forward step, it creates space between the offensive player and the defender and forces the defender to react — which then determines the next move. Stan Van Gundy's version covers the mechanics precisely: foot position, ball movement, eye direction, and timing. The key is never pre-deciding; the jab step is designed to reveal the defender's weakness and exploit it. [S7, pp.24-27]

## When to Use
- From triple-threat position when the defender is in a perfect defensive stance and a direct move won't work
- When space must be created before shooting or driving
- As the entry point into all basic combined perimeter moves

## Key Principles
1. **Jab step involves both the foot AND the ball** — the foot steps forward while the ball is simultaneously brought to the side of the knee to fake a dribble. Both must happen together.
2. **Short, aggressive, and forward** — the jab step with the right foot lands about 6-7 inches ahead of the left foot on the forefoot; the heel of the pivot foot does not touch the floor (improves pace of the next move).
3. **Protect the ball; don't overextend** — bringing the ball too far from the body disrupts balance and exposes it to the defender.
4. **Turn the shoulder slightly inside** — gives the impression of wanting to drive while keeping balance.
5. **Eyes look OVER the defender** — not down at the feet; this makes the defender think you're attacking a specific spot and lets you see the entire court.
6. **Give the defender enough time to react** — the fake must not be too quick; you need the defender to commit a mistake (cross feet, lean, straighten up) before reading and reacting.
7. **Read and react; never pre-decide** — the continuation after the jab step (drive same side, crossover, jump shot) is determined entirely by the defender's response.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF**: All perimeter players must master the jab step as the starting point of their one-on-one game.

## Variations

### Jab Step and Drive With the Same Hand
If the defender does not react quickly enough after the jab step:
- Take a long step with the right foot (but not so long that balance is lost) and dribble over the defender's hip with the right hand.
- Bring the left shoulder to hip level of the defender. Left arm bent 45 degrees at hip level protects the ball.
- Push ball hard to floor: wrist bent backward and shifted behind the ball with elbow bent to push ball forward.
- Sequence: short jab step → immediately longer step with the same foot. Stay low; do not decrease speed. [S7, p.26]

### Jab Step and Crossover
If the defender slides over in the direction of the jab step:
- Take a crossover step and a dribble.
- Sequence: hard jab step with the right foot → crossover step with the right leg attacking the opposite side. [S7, p.26]

### Jab Step and Jump Shot
If the defender retreats after the jab step:
- Shoot a jump shot immediately (if in shooting range).
- Jab must be aggressive and short; knees bent.
- Shooting palm must be behind the ball with wrist cocked toward the chest to speed up the shot.
- As soon as the jab step hits the floor and the defender retreats: bring ball into "shooting pocket" and go up for the jumper. [S7, p.26]

### Shot Fake and Drive (Same Hand or Crossover)
Used when the jab step alone is insufficient:
- Bring the ball over the forehead with both hands as if to shoot.
- While raising the ball, flex the legs more.
- When the defender straightens their legs in response to the shot fake, explode to the basket with a drive (same hand or crossover depending on the defender's position). [S7, p.27]

### Jab Step, Shot Fake, and Drive
Combination of two fakes before the drive. A three-count move — each count must be timed properly to force sequential reactions from the defender. Never rushed. [S7, p.27]

### Jab Step, Shot Fake, Drive Fake, and Jump Shot
The opposite: fake the drive after the shot fake, then pull up for the jumper. Stay low throughout all fakes, then explode vertically for the shot. Total sequence: maximum 3 seconds. [S7, p.27]

### Pass Fake and Drive or Jump Shot
Fake a lateral pass with two hands (do not extend arms too much). Simultaneously take a short step in the direction of the pass fake and shift weight to the forward foot. Then push the body in the opposite direction — drive or pull up. [S7, p.27]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Jab step too quick** → gives the defender no time to commit; make it realistic and purposeful — force them to react first
2. **Overextending the ball on the jab** → disrupts balance; keep ball close to the body during the step
3. **Pre-deciding the continuation** → the entire system depends on reading the defender's reaction after the jab
4. **Heel of the pivot foot touching the floor** → slows down the next move; keep weight on the ball of the pivot foot
5. **Eyes down** → miss reading the defender and miss kick-out opportunities; look over the defender

# Midrange Jump Shot

## Summary
The midrange jump shot is any shot taken inside the three-point arc but outside the painted area. It is the foundation of a complete offensive game. Mastering the consistent 12-15 foot (3.6-4.5 m) jump shot makes a player **unguardable** because defenders must play up — creating drives to the basket — while the shooter is also a constant scoring threat. an iconic scorer's ten pro scoring titles were built on this shot more than any other. [S5, pp.57-60]

The mechanics of the jump shot are identical to the fundamentals covered in form shooting, with the key addition of being airborne at the point of release.

## When to Use
- Catch-and-shoot off a pass (inside-out pass from the post or wing)
- Pull-up off the dribble at the mid-range area
- When a defender closes out aggressively on three-point attempts — step inside the arc for a higher-percentage look
- Developing players: always master the open 10-12 foot shot before extending to the three-point line [S5, p.57]

## Key Principles

### Why Start Mid-Range
1. **Progress from close to far**: You must consistently make an open 10-footer before attempting an open 20-footer. The mid-range is the bridge between form shooting and deep shooting. [S5, p.57]
2. **Creates two-way threat**: A reliable mid-range shot forces defenders to close out, opening driving lanes. *"Will he play up on you? Boom — you have a lane to the basket."* [S5, p.57]

### Stance (Pre-Receive)
3. **Athletic stance before the catch**: Feet shoulder-width apart, knees bent, hips bent so shoulders are in front of the feet and head is in front of the shoulders. Heels lift off the floor — be on your toes. [S5, p.59]
4. This is the universal basketball ready position — also used for catching passes, sliding defensively, and boxing out. [S5, p.59]

### Shot Pocket and Target Hand (Receiving the Ball)
5. **Show a target**: Before stepping to receive, the shooting hand's palm faces the passer — wrist wrinkled back, cocked and locked, fingertips to the ceiling, elbow in an L-shape. This is called **"palm to passer."** [S5, p.60]
6. **Balance hand fingertips** (not palm) point toward the passer. Passer should see only the palm of the shooting hand. [S5, p.60]
7. This pre-catch position is the **shot pocket** — it establishes shot line alignment from the moment the ball arrives and prevents the chicken-wing elbow.

### One-Two Step into the Shot
8. **Pivot foot step**: As the ball comes, step aggressively toward it with the **non-shooting foot first** (left foot for right-handed shooters). [S5, pp.59-60]
9. The step should be long and assertive — it gets you to the ball quicker and keeps you low.
10. Heel hits the floor first, then weight transfers to the toes/ball of the foot. That foot is now the pivot foot.
11. **Catch the ball as the pivot foot is established** — the one-two step times the catch and the footwork together.
12. Eyes watch the ball all the way into the shot pocket; then immediately shift focus to the **center of the basket** (the target).

### Jump and Release
13. All standard shooting mechanics apply: bend and extend, shot line (toe-knee-hip-shoulder-elbow-wrist-hand), shooting hand under the ball, balance hand on the side, wrist wrinkled.
14. The critical difference from a free shot: the ball is released **at the peak of the jump**.
15. **Freeze the follow-through** — elbow above eyebrow, hand in the hoop, both hands held high on the descent.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG**: Primary users — must master the straight-on mid-range as the core of their offensive arsenal before extending range.
- **SF/PF**: Crucial for high-low game; mid-range pull-up off a closeout is essential when posting or driving.
- **C**: Mid-range face-up jumper is a key weapon for modern bigs who can step away from the basket.

## Variations
### Straight-On Jump Shot (Catch-and-Shoot)
Ball comes from an inside-out pass; player steps into the shot with the one-two step, catches, and rises. Can be self-practiced with a **spinout** — toss the ball forward with backspin so it bounces back as a simulated pass. [S5, p.58]

### Pull-Up Jump Shot (Off the Dribble)
Covered in greater depth in subsequent chapters; same shooting mechanics but the footwork involves gathering out of the dribble rather than stepping to a pass. [S5, p.58]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Going to the three-point line before mastering 12-15 ft** → Builds bad habits and inconsistency. Correction: earn range — make open 10-footers before extending.
2. **Flat-footed stance before the catch** → Slow reaction, no leg power. Correction: *"be on your toes before the ball gets to you."*
3. **Palm to passer not established** → Elbow flares ("chicken wing"), shot line breaks. Correction: show palm before the ball arrives, every time.
4. **Short, lazy step to the ball** → Late catch, off-balance. Correction: *"long aggressive step — attack the ball, stay low."*
5. **Catching the ball without watching it into the shot pocket** → Ball not seated correctly in the hand. Correction: *"eyes on the ball all the way in, then snap to the target."*

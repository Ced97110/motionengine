# Hesitation Dribble (Drill #26)

## Objective
Develop a deceptive change-of-pace hesitation move that freezes defenders before an explosive attack dribble.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- 4 lines at one baseline (see diagram — players line up in 4 columns)
- 1 basketball per player
- Duration: 1 minute

<!-- DIAGRAM: Four columns of players at baseline, each column dribbling full length of court and back. Arrows show straight-line path down and back. [S15, p.57] -->

## Execution
1. Players line up in four columns at the baseline.
2. On the coach's signal, the first player in each line begins **dribbling slowly** down the floor for a few dribbles.
3. Player **stops abruptly**, then **explodes with a quick, hard dribble** — this is the hesitation move. Throw head and shoulders back when stopping to freeze the defender before the next explosive dribble.
4. After exploding, the player stops again and explodes again — repeating the hesitation pattern every 3–4 dribbles down the floor.
5. At the far end of the court, players execute a **reverse pivot (or front pivot)** and return, using the same hesitation move every third or fourth dribble.
6. Players **dribble down with the right hand** and **back with the left hand**.
7. About 15 feet (4.5 m) from the next player in line, players execute a **jump stop** and deliver a **chest pass (or bounce pass)** to the next teammate.
8. All players repeat the sequence.

## Options
1. **Race format**: Divide squad into guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.
2. **Two-trip bilateral**: Right hand down and back on trip 1; left hand down and back on trip 2.

## Advanced Progression
1. **Alternating dual-ball**: Hold one ball in each hand; bounce the left-hand ball while holding the right, then bounce the right-hand ball when the left bounces back up.
2. **Simultaneous dual-ball**: Hold one ball in each hand and bounce both at the same height simultaneously — both balls touch hands and floor at exactly the same time.

## Coaching Points
- **Ball position**: Keep ball 2–3 feet (.6–.9 m) away from and beside the knee — body between ball and imaginary defender.
- **Eyes up**: Eyes should be straight ahead, **never** looking at the basketball.
- **Change of pace is the key**: Train players to move quickly, stop on a dime, then explode — don't let the pace become predictable.
- **Fingerpads only**: Players dribble with the first pads (fingertips/fingerpads) of the fingers — never the palm.
- **Body position**: Use a control-dribble body position when hesitating; shift to speed-dribble position when exploding away from the defender.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the hesitation concept — take 3 slow dribbles, stop, take 1 hard dribble. Focus on the stop before the explosion.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed hesitation every 3–4 dribbles, bilateral, with pivot and pass at the end.
3. **Advanced**: Dual-ball alternating or simultaneous versions; add time pressure with racing format.

# Rocker Board Balance

## Objective
Build ankle and lower-extremity balance and stability in a minisquat position, simulating the starting position for free throws and the landing position after a jump.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface (can use court floor for beginner variation)
- Equipment: rocker board or BOSU ball

## Execution
1. Stand erect on a rocker board or BOSU with full body weight distributed evenly through both legs. The unstable surface challenges medial/lateral and anterior/posterior stability.
2. Keeping a slight bend in the knees, slowly lower into a minisquat position. During the balance activity, maintain fixed posture and keep the board in a neutral position.
3. Hold the minisquat position for the prescribed timed bout.
4. **Progression**: Introduce external forces — have a partner provide light perturbations or toss a chest pass to catch while maintaining balance [S2, p.131].

## Coaching Points
- "Stay tall through the spine — don't hunch forward"
- "Feel the board and microadjust; don't fight it, control it"
- Knees should stay aligned over toes — prevent valgus (inward) collapse
- Watch for players shifting weight to one side; cue even distribution
- The goal is a quiet, still board — not rocking back and forth

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform on flat floor with both feet, hold minisquat 20-30 seconds
2. **Intermediate**: Rocker board or BOSU bilateral minisquat, holding still for timed bouts
3. **Advanced**: Add chest pass catch or partner perturbations while maintaining balance [S2, p.131]

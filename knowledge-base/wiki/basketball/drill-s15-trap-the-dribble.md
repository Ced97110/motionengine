# Trap the Dribble

## Objective
Force a turnover by channeling the dribbler into a midcourt direction change that signals a teammate to spring a two-man trap, while other defenders cover passing lanes with safety or all-out interception coverage.

## Setup
- **Players**: 5 offense (1 with ball, 2–5 spread around front court), 5 defense
- **Court area**: Full court — trap occurs near the midcourt line
- **Formation**: 1-3-1 (odd front) as shown in the figure
- Rotate defenders: X1 → X2 → X3 → X4 → X5 → X1; then rotate offense to defense

## Execution
1. **X1 channels the dribbler**: X1 forces player 1 toward the middle of the court, then compels 1 to change direction near the midcourt line. This change of direction is the **signal** to teammates to activate the trap.
2. **X3 denies first, then races to trap**: X3 must deny player 3 the ball *before* the trap is activated. Once X3 begins the move to trap, X3 still stays in the passing lane between 1 and 3. A pass to player 3 immediately breaks the trap.
   - X5 and X2 also deny their assignments the ball.
   - X4 covers player 4 and watches for any careless pass by 1.
3. **X3 races toward player 1** but stays in the passing lane to player 3. This forces player 1 to use either a lob or bounce pass — the two slowest pass types, most easily intercepted.
4. **X3 stays with X1 and traps** as long as the dribble is alive.
5. **Interception coverage** (safety technique in the figure against 1-3-1):
   - X2 plays in the gap between players 2 and 5.
   - X5 plays in the gap between players 3 and 5, using interception techniques.
6. **Mirror-image trap**: If player 1 dribbles to the other side of the court, X2 becomes the trapper, X5 plays between 2 and 5, and X3 plays between 3 and 5.

## Coaching Points
- "Make sure the trap is properly set and arms are active" [S15, p.329]
- X3 must stay in the passing lane to player 3 **while racing to the trap** — leaving the lane invites a quick escape pass
- Forcing lob/bounce passes is the goal of staying in the direct passing lane; "these two passes are the slowest of all passes" [S15, p.328]
- The midcourt direction change is the **trigger** — all defenders must recognize and respond instantly

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: X1 channels, X3 traps, safety interception only
2. **Intermediate**: Add all-out interception coverage; coach calls the option pre-possession
3. **Advanced**: Mirror to both sides; vary the trap trigger signal (direction change vs. crossover dribble)

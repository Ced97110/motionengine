# Change of Pace Dribble (Drill #25)

## Objective
Develop the change-of-pace dribbling move — alternating between fast, medium, and slow dribbles to freeze or beat defenders — while building full-court conditioning.

## Setup
- **Players:** Full team in 4 lines at baseline
- **Court area:** Full court
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per line (2 balls per player for advanced variation)
- **Duration:** 1 minute per set

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up in 4 lines at the baseline.
2. Walk (then progress to running) down the court alternating: **fast dribble → slow dribble → fast → slow** — add medium-speed dribbles to vary the pace further.
3. Dribble down with the right hand, back with the left hand.
4. **Reverse (or front) pivot** at the far end before returning, changing pace with every second or third dribble.
5. **Jump-stop** ~15 feet (4.5 m) from the next teammate in line.
6. Execute a **chest (or bounce) pass** to the next player in line.
7. All players repeat.

### Options
- Divide into teams for a dribbling race — guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team
- Two trips: right hand on first trip, left hand on second

### Advanced (Two-Ball)
1. **Alternating:** One ball in each hand; alternate dribbles while applying change-of-pace rhythm down the floor
2. **Simultaneous:** Both balls bounce at the same height at the same time — change of pace applied to both simultaneously

## Coaching Points
- **Body position:** Assume a **control-dribble body position** as the default — protection of the ball is the priority; shift to speed-dribble body position only when trying to clear the backcourt
- Ball **2–3 feet (.6–.9 m) to the side and beside the knee** — body between ball and defender at all times
- **Eyes straight ahead** — never looking at the basketball
- Dribble with the **first pads of the fingers**
- The change of pace is a **fake** — the speed variation is what frees the attacker from a defender, not just raw speed
- Encourage players to add a hesitation or pause to the slow phase — this sells the fake most effectively

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Walk down the court alternating fast/slow dribbles — establish the rhythm first
2. **Intermediate:** Full-speed running with change of pace; competitive team race format
3. **Advanced:** Two-ball alternating and simultaneous variations

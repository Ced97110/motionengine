# One-Arm Cable Row

## Objective
Develop unilateral upper-back pulling strength (latissimus dorsi, teres major, trapezius, rhomboids, posterior deltoids) to improve rebounding pull-downs, defensive physicality, and postural strength in the athletic position.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Cable machine with a single handle attachment (can also use rope, V-bar, towel, or basketball attachment)
- Split stance facing the machine

## Execution
1. Stand facing the cable machine in a split stance — one foot forward, rear foot about 2–3 feet (61–91 cm) back.
2. Slightly flex the knees. Hold the handle in the hand **on the same side as the rear foot**.
3. Maintain an upright, athletic stance with scapulae pulled back slightly; shoulder relaxed and not elevated — this is the beginning position.
4. Maintaining a **rigid, upright upper body** throughout, pull the handle back to the torso. The **elbow drives directly backward**; the wrist falls in line with the elbow and stays close to the body.
5. Reverse direction by allowing the handle to move forward in a controlled manner back to the beginning position.

**Breathing:** Exhale during the pull; inhale during the return.

## Coaching Points
- "Back initiates the pull" — the pulling action must be initiated by the **back muscles**, not the torso, midsection, or momentum
- "Wrist in line with elbow" — the wrist must not be higher or lower than the elbow during the pull
- "Keep the upper body erect" — no leaning forward or twisting
- Control the weight throughout — no jerking or momentum-driven reps

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light cable load; focus on scapular retraction and elbow path
2. **Intermediate**: Increase load; kneel on one knee variation (other leg 90° in front) for reduced base of support
3. **Advanced**: Use a rope, V-bar, or towel around the handle for grip-strength development; vary tempo (pause at peak contraction; slow eccentric); attach a basketball-specific cable attachment for sport-specific pulling patterns

# Stability-Ball Cyclic Impact Lockouts

## Objective
Strengthen the core and improve the body's ability to absorb and redirect impact forces — training the reactive arm and core stiffness needed for boxing out, absorbing contact on drives, and stabilizing the trunk under physical pressure. [S8, p.153]

## Setup
- 1 player
- 1 stability ball (physioball)
- Open floor space

## Execution
1. Start in a **"hand-on-ball" push-up position** — both hands on the stability ball, body in a plank.
2. Keep the core (abs, lower back, and hips) **tight throughout**.
3. Release the hands from the ball, allowing the upper body to **fall forward** and make contact with the ball at the **upper abdominals**.
4. As the upper body bounces off the ball, **reach out and secure the ball with the hands**, then **lockout the arms** to return to the start push-up position.
5. Repeat cyclically without pausing.

## Coaching Points
- Core must be braced before the fall — "abs tight before you release"
- Impact should be absorbed and immediately redirected — this is an upper-body plyometric, not a collapse
- "Catch and lock" — hands find the ball quickly and arms extend fully
- Do not hold the breath during impacts — exhale on contact
- Progress volume slowly; this places high demand on the shoulder girdle

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow, controlled falling contact; focus on core pre-tension and arm lockout mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Increase cyclic speed; maintain a continuous rhythm without pausing
3. **Advanced**:
   - **Reach-and-touch**: as you bounce off the ball, reach out and touch a designated target before catching [S8, p.153]
   - **Clap behind the body**: clap hands behind the back while bouncing off the ball — demands faster arm recovery and greater shoulder elasticity [S8, p.153]

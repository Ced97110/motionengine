# Fill Shot Off Screen

## Summary
The fill shot is the foundational catch-and-shoot option for a player coming off a screen to the wing. After setting up the screen and cutting to the wing, the player receives the pass and shoots a fill (wing) shot at 15–20 feet. This is a stationary catch-and-shoot — there is no live-ball dribble — and it represents the "defender is beaten" read off the screen. [S3, pp.148-154]

The fill shot is the baseline action that all other off-screen reactions (dribble fade, dribble fill, curl) are built upon. Mastering the fill shot first creates the decision-making foundation for live-ball reactions.

## When to Use
- Defender is beaten cleanly off the screen (late getting through or going under)
- Shooter arrives at the wing with separation and is in rhythm
- Passer hits the shooter in the shooting pocket on the wing
- Set plays designed to free a shooter on the wing (e.g., floppy, High-Post, flare)

## Key Principles
1. **Use the screen correctly** — body-to-body contact, V-cut setup to maximize separation
2. **Arrive at the spot in rhythm** — feet should be moving toward the shot, not planted flat
3. **Inside pivot foot plant** — inside foot (toward the basket) lands first to square the body
4. **Catch in the shooting pocket** — ball goes directly from catch to shot, no pump fake unless necessary
5. **Hold the follow-through** — freeze the hand until the ball hits the floor for muscle-memory reinforcement
6. **Distance: 15–20 feet** — wing mid-range to three-point range depending on level and player capability [S3, p.148]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF (shooter)**: Set up the screen with a V-cut, sprint to the wing spot, catch in rhythm, shoot fill shot
- **Screener**: Set a legal stationary screen that forces defender to fight through or be late
- **Passer** (at top of key): Read when the shooter is open, deliver a pass to the shooting pocket — not early, not late

## Variations
### Left-Side Fill
Shooter sets up screen and fills to the left wing. [S3, p.149]

### Right-Side Fill
Shooter sets up screen and fills to the right wing. [S3, p.149]

### Fill Shot with Live-Ball Threat
After catching for the fill shot, if the defender recovers quickly, transition to a jab step, dribble fade, or dribble fill read — see [[concept-dribble-fade-shot]] and [[concept-dribble-fill-shot]].

## Common Mistakes
1. **Arriving flat-footed** → kills momentum and rhythm; practice cutting to the spot with movement
2. **Catching outside the shooting pocket** → forces an extra setup motion before shooting; passer must hit the right target, shooter must call for the ball correctly
3. **Ball dip before shooting** → slows release and makes shot easier to contest; catch and rise in one continuous motion
4. **Shooting too far from the basket** → poor percentage; maintain 15–20 ft range until mechanics are solid
5. **Not holding follow-through** → prevents coach feedback and self-correction; "freeze until the ball hits the floor"

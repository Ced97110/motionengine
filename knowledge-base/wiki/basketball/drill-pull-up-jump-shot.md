# Pull-Up Jump Shot Drills (One-Dribble and Two-Dribble)

## Objective
Develop the foundational pull-up jump shot off the live dribble in both directions with both pivot feet, training the one-two step pickup and shot mechanics off the dribble.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1
- **Court area**: Half court, midrange area (approximately 12–18 ft from the basket)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball

## Execution

### One-Dribble Pull-Up
1. Start at the midrange area with the ball in hand and the right foot as the pivot foot.
2. Take a hard dribble going **right**, using the one-two step to gather (right foot stays as pivot, left (live) foot takes the long stride).
3. Pickup the ball with two hands into the shot pocket and pull up into the jump shot.
4. Return to the starting spot.
5. Take a hard dribble going **left** (still using the right foot as pivot foot), gather with the one-two step, pick up into the shot pocket, and pull up.
6. Repeat 20 times total — 10 right, 10 left.
7. Repeat the entire sequence using the **left foot** as the pivot foot.

### Two-Dribble Pull-Up
- Same concept and repetition structure as the one-dribble drill.
- Use two dribbles to gain a closer position to the basket and higher-percentage shot.
- Work both directions with both pivot feet [S5, p.64].

## Coaching Points
- **Push the ball out in front.** Never dribble straight down — the ball must lead the shooter into the shot like a good pass [S5, p.64].
- **Long stride with the live foot.** The live (non-pivot) foot takes a long aggressive stride, getting the shooter low and allowing them to get past the defender [S5, p.64].
- **Going left (weak side for right-handed shooter): load the gun.** When going left, let the ball rise into the left hand first, then bring it across to the shooting hand. Reaching across with the shooting arm first pulls the shot line out of alignment [S5, p.64].
- Keep the shooting elbow tucked and in a straight line with the target throughout the pickup.
- Watch for players dribbling the ball too close to the body — ball must be pushed out front.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: One-dribble pull-up with strong hand only; focus on pickup mechanics.
2. **Intermediate**: One-dribble pull-up both directions with both pivot feet.
3. **Advanced**: Two-dribble pull-up both directions; then combine into the Stop and Pop full-court drill [S5, p.67].

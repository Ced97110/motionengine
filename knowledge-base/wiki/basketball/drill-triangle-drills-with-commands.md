# Triangle Drills With Commands

## Objective
Improve quickness of the foot plant and change of direction by sprinting around a triangle of three cones and changing sprint direction on the coach's verbal command. [S8, p.192]

## Setup
- 1–2 players (1 athlete + 1 coach, or 2 athletes for complex variation)
- **3 cones** in a triangle with sides **5 yards (4.6 meters)** apart
- Start at the designated cone

## Execution
1. Place three cones in a triangle, 5 yards between each.
2. Athlete starts at the designated cone and **sprints as fast as possible** around the triangle.
3. Stay **close to the cones** (tight turns).
4. After **2 to 4 turns** (or anytime), the coach gives a **verbal command** to change sprint direction.
5. Athlete immediately reverses direction and continues sprinting around the triangle. [S8, p.192]

## Coaching Points
- "Stay tight to the cone" — wide arcing turns waste time and reduce agility demand
- The foot plant at each cone should be a sharp, single-step redirect — not a deceleration zone
- Eyes on the coach between cones to catch the direction-change command
- Vary the timing of the command — don't command on a predictable rhythm
- This drill develops the "split stop" needed when cutting off screens

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Sprint the triangle without a command — establish the pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Add the verbal direction-change command every 2–4 turns
3. **Advanced (Complex Variation — Triangle Chase Drill)**: Stand on the line between two cones. Add a **partner chaser** who starts at the third cone on the coach's verbal command. The original athlete runs; the chaser tries to tag them. Drill ends when tagged.

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — sharp direction changes at sprint speed train deceleration and re-acceleration
- [[rate-of-force-development-basketball]] — foot plant at each cone requires maximal RFD in the change-of-direction step
- [[concept-lower-body-plyometrics]] — the cutting action at each cone apex is a plyometric movement

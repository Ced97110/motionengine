# Front Handspring (Front Flip Chain — Step 6)

## Objective
Execute a complete run-and-block front handspring with a controlled two-foot landing — developing running, blocking, jumping, explosive upper-body pressing, rotational tolerance, and landing skill simultaneously.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (solo)
- **Equipment**: Soft surface or mat for early practice; open hard surface once technique is solid
- **Space**: At least 10-15 feet of open floor for approach run

## Execution
1. Take a run-up to build momentum.
2. *Block* — punch down on the floor with the strongest foot to convert horizontal momentum to vertical. The rear leg swings up simultaneously to assist rotation.
3. Kick up hard through the strongest leg, pushing the foot off the ground but allowing the torso to fall while dropping the hands to the floor. The free foot swings up behind, assisting rotation.
4. Land on the palms with arms slightly bent just as the feet leave the floor — catching your bodyweight on the palms.
5. Let the momentum carry the legs above the torso into a bent-leg handstand position.
6. Do not hold the handstand — allow forward momentum to continue turning the body over.
7. Extend through the arms and hands to gain extra height.
8. Continue pulling the torso forward as both feet land simultaneously. Step forward if needed to dissipate extra momentum. [S9, pp.156-157]

**Key X-ray**: The front handspring is a key development stage for the front flip AND a phenomenal explosive drill in its own right. Mastery demonstrates excellent running, blocking, jumping, explosive pressing, rotational tolerance, and landing. [S9, p.156]

## Coaching Points
- "Block hard — the block converts horizontal speed into upward lift."
- Arms extend to add height out of the handstand — don't just passively fall through.
- Landing should progress from deep squat → lower squat → upright — measure rotation quality by how extended the body can be at landing.
- Avoid over/under-rotation; aim to land without any adjustment steps.
- Step forward only as needed — the goal is a stuck, no-step landing.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Land in a low squat position (requires less rotation); landing on glutes is acceptable early on.
2. **Intermediate**: Land in a squat but absorb cleanly on the feet without falling to glutes.
3. **Advanced**: Land with a more extended body posture; eliminate adjustment steps; achieve a clean, stuck, upright landing consistently before moving to Step 7.

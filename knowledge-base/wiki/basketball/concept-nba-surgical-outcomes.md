# NBA Surgical Outcomes

## Summary
Musculoskeletal surgical procedures are relatively uncommon in the NBA (ranging from 0.8 to 2.2 per team per year), but can be career-altering. Two key outcome metrics are used: Player Efficiency Rating (PER — the NBA's normalized combined statistic metric) and games played upon return. Achilles tendon repair and knee microfracture have the worst outcomes on both metrics. Shoulder surgery and hand/wrist fracture repair have the best outcomes [S10, pp.151-152].

## When to Use
- Counseling athletes and teams on realistic expectations after surgery
- Prioritizing injury prevention resources toward highest-impact procedures
- Interpreting return-to-play timelines for planning purposes

## Key Principles

### Surgical Procedure Outcomes Table (Table 14.4)

| Surgery | Return to Play % | Procedures/Year | PER Seasons 2/3 | Games Played Seasons 2/3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Achilles tendon repair | 70.8% | 0.8 | **Lower** | **Lower** |
| Microfracture | 71.9% | 1.1 | **Lower** | **Lower** |
| Patellar tendon repair | 75.0% | 0.9 | No change | No change |
| Lumbar discectomy | 79.4% | 1.2 | No change | No change |
| Meniscal repair | 81.5% | 1.9 | No change | **Lower** |
| ACL reconstruction | 84.6% | 2.2 | No change | **Lower** |
| Hip arthroscopy | 85.7% | 1.6 | No change | No change |
| Foot fracture repair | 90.2% | 1.4 | No change | No change |
| Shoulder surgery | 93.5% | 1.6 | No change | No change |
| Hand/wrist fracture repair | 98.1% | 1.8 | No change | No change |

All comparisons are to the season prior to injury [S10, pp.151-152]

### Critical Interpretations
1. **Worst outcomes**: Achilles tendon repair and microfracture — both have the lowest return-to-play rates AND significantly lower PER and games played for 2-3 seasons post-return [S10, p.151]
2. **Lumbar discectomy and ACL reconstruction** — reduced PER in the first season of return, but PER normalizes by seasons 2 and 3; however, ACL players still play fewer games in seasons 2/3 [S10, p.151]
3. **Patellar tendon, hip arthroscopy, shoulder, hand/wrist** — do not significantly impact PER in the first season of return [S10, pp.151-152]
4. **ACL reconstruction** is the most common surgical procedure (2.2/year) but also has a meaningful burden — games played remains lower for 2-3 seasons post-return [S10, p.151]
5. **Meniscal repair** — PER unaffected but games played remains lower for 2-3 seasons [S10, p.152]

### Return-to-Play Probability Hierarchy
Best to worst:
1. Hand/wrist fracture repair (98.1%)
2. Shoulder surgery (93.5%)
3. Foot fracture repair (90.2%)
4. Hip arthroscopy (85.7%)
5. ACL reconstruction (84.6%)
6. Meniscal repair (81.5%)
7. Lumbar discectomy (79.4%)
8. Patellar tendon repair (75.0%)
9. Microfracture (71.9%)
10. Achilles tendon repair (70.8%) [S10, pp.151-152]

## Common Mistakes
1. Equating "returned to play" with "returned to pre-injury performance" → Achilles and microfracture players who return still perform at significantly lower levels for 2-3 seasons
2. Expecting full recovery in season 1 after lumbar discectomy or ACL → PER is reduced in season 1; recovery takes to season 2 or 3
3. Underestimating ACL burden → high return rate but persistently fewer games played for multiple seasons

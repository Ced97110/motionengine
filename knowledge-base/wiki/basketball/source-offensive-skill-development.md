# Basketball Coaches' Guide to Advanced Offensive Skill Development — Summary

## Overview
*Basketball Coaches' Guide to Advanced Offensive Skill Development* was written by Shawn Nelson, head women's basketball coach at Carroll College (Helena, Montana). Nelson built one of the most prolific three-point shooting programs in NAIA history — his teams led the nation in three-point percentage for four straight years, and his player Jolene Fuzesy holds the all-time record for career three-point percentage in college basketball at any level (51.7% over four years). The book reflects the specific workout system Nelson used to produce those results.

The book is organized around a systematic, progressively complex set of individual offensive workouts. Each workout is named, numbered, and accompanied by a shot-tracking chart so players can measure their output against specific performance benchmarks (typically 60% from the field and 85% from the free-throw line). This data-driven, accountable approach to individual skill development is the book's defining characteristic.

The system begins with foundational live-ball skills (triple-threat, jab steps, shot fakes, rip steps, combination moves) and shooting mechanics, then layers in movement — catch-and-shoot off cuts, screens, and live-ball moves across the entire perimeter. The final workout series (#11) covers the full wing-to-top-of-key movement pattern, incorporating straight three-point shooting, combination live-ball moves, rip step moves, and shot fake moves off game-speed catches.

## Key Themes
1. **Systematic Progression**: Every skill is introduced stationary, then on the move, then at game speed — never skipping steps in the developmental sequence.
2. **Live-Ball Mastery**: Emphasis on catching on a two-foot stop with maximum pivot-foot options (live-ball position) before making any offensive move.
3. **Shot Accountability**: Each workout uses a standardized shot chart (FGA, FGM, %, FTA, FTM, %) with explicit targets, creating measurable improvement over time.
4. **Both Sides of the Floor**: Every drill is completed on both the right and left side; no dominant-hand shortcuts.
5. **Game-Speed Execution**: All workouts specify that shots must be taken at game speed — not half-speed rehearsal.
6. **Three-Point Emphasis**: The workout system places heavy volume on three-point shooting from all perimeter spots, reflecting Carroll College's identity as a three-point specialist program.

## Unique Contributions
This source is unique in the wiki for three reasons:
1. **Numbered workout system with charting**: Unlike other sources that describe concepts or plays, Nelson provides a complete, numbered practice program with built-in performance targets that can be tracked session-by-session.
2. **Carroll College three-point shooting system**: The three-point shooting volume and specificity (wing→top movement on both sides, 80 attempts per workout) is unmatched in any other source.
3. **Live-ball move integration with shooting**: The combination of live-ball move categories (rip step, shot fake, combination) applied to three-point range is a distinct offensive development approach not covered in the shooting mechanics sources (S5).

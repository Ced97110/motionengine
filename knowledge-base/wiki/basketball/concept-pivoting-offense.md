# Pivoting as an Offensive Foundation

## Summary
Pivoting is the most elementary offensive basketball skill — it actually *precedes* passing and dribbling in the hierarchy of fundamentals. A player who cannot pivot correctly cannot properly position themselves to pass or dribble without committing a traveling violation or turnover. No matter how skilled a team's post players or perimeter shooters are, none of those abilities can be realized if the team cannot move and reposition the ball through correct pivoting.

The complete basketball coaching guide (S6) argues that the art of pivoting is *more important* than ballhandling itself — pivoting is the enabling skill that makes all other offensive skills functional. [S6, pp.141-143]

## When to Use
- Every catch of the basketball — the pivot determines the offensive player's next action
- After receiving a pass under defensive pressure: pivot away from the defense to protect the ball
- When advancing the ball against a press: pivot to find the next open receiver after passing into the middle of a trap
- Any time a player needs to change the direction they're facing without dribbling

## Key Principles
1. **Pivoting is pre-passing and pre-dribbling**: Before a player can pass or dribble, they must establish a pivot foot and legally position their body — the pivot is always step one.
2. **Pivot away from defense**: When receiving a pass under pressure, the first action is to pivot away from the nearest defender to protect the ball with the body.
3. **Pivot enables court vision**: The direction and type of pivot (front vs. reverse) determines how much of the court the player can see after the catch — pivots that open to the ball give more court vision.
4. **Pass into the middle, extend immediately**: When the ball is passed into the middle of a trapping defense, the receiver must pivot to find the next open teammate and immediately extend the pass. [S6, p.142]
5. **Front pivot vs. reverse pivot tradeoff**: Front pivot (toward the basket) squares up for an immediate shot or drive. Reverse/inside pivot (toward the ball) opens up court vision and creates triple-threat position but takes slightly more time to face the basket.
6. **Players who cannot pivot commit turnovers**: Players who skip the pivot and go directly to dribbling or passing from a poor position will be called for traveling or will make low-percentage passes.

## Front Pivot vs. Reverse Pivot

### Front Pivot (Crossover Pivot)
- The non-pivot foot swings forward (toward the basket or direction of attack)
- Used when the player wants to immediately face the basket, shoot, or drive
- Best for: immediate shooting threat off screen cuts, pick-and-pop situations, back screeners slipping to become shooters

### Reverse Pivot (Inside Pivot / Drop Step)
- The non-pivot foot swings backward (away from the initial facing direction)
- Used when the player wants to open up to the ball, survey the court, or seal a defender
- Best for: post players sealing defenders, screeners opening to the ball after setting a screen, players wanting to enter triple-threat while keeping the ball protected

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Must master both front and reverse pivot to navigate trapping defenses and create passing angles against pressure
- **SG**: Must develop a reliable pivot off the inside foot off every type of screen to become a consistent catch-and-shoot threat
- **SF**: Needs pivoting versatility — can receive on the wing (front pivot to attack) or in the post (reverse pivot to seal)
- **PF**: Most often uses reverse pivot (drop step) in the post; must also master front pivot when popping to the perimeter after screening
- **C**: Reverse pivot is essential for post entry and seal; front pivot needed for pick-and-pop and interior face-up game

## Common Mistakes
1. **Skipping the pivot entirely** → Traveling violation or awkward catch-and-drive → "Establish your pivot foot every single catch, no exceptions"
2. **Wrong type of pivot for the situation** → Front pivot when back is to a defender exposes the ball; reverse pivot when an immediate shot was open wastes the opportunity → Teach players to read the situation and choose pivot type before the catch
3. **Pivoting on the wrong foot** → Body rotation is slow and awkward; shot release is late → Drill inside-foot pivot rules by court position until automatic
4. **Pivoting while the ball is in flight but before catching** → Illegal: the pivot foot must be established on the catch → "Wait for the catch, then pivot"

# Shooting-Hand Form Shooting

## Objective
Isolate and perfect shooting-arm alignment (elbow under ball, L-position, proper grip) by removing the balance hand from the equation entirely.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Directly in front of the basket, starting 2 feet (.6 m) from the rim
- 1 basketball
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Assume the proper shooting stance — feet shoulder-width apart, shooting-side foot slightly forward, knees and hips bent.
2. Grip the ball in the shooting hand only (NO balance hand): palm off the ball, fingertips and fingerpads, wrist wrinkled (cocked), elbow in the L-position directly under the ball above the shooting foot.
3. Verify: elbow is tucked, not flared; shooting hand is centered under ball; wrist is fully cocked.
4. Shoot, releasing the ball off the fingertips with a full upward extension. Elbow finishes above the eyebrow.
5. Make 5 shots from this distance.
6. Take a half-step back and make 5 more shots.
7. Continue stepping back a half-step at a time until form breaks down — i.e., the ball is being thrown or slung rather than lifted.
8. That distance marks the player's current shooting range. Do not attempt shots beyond it in a game.
9. Goal: make 5 shots from 10 different spots (totaling 50 makes) before ending the drill. [S5, pp.16-17]

## Coaching Points
- **Watch for the chicken wing** — if the elbow flares, stop and reset before the next rep [S5, p.16]
- **"Be a lifter, not a slinger or a pusher"** — L-shape is the lifting position [S5, p.15]
- The balance hand is intentionally absent; the balance hand is often the culprit that causes the elbow to flare [S5, p.16]
- Watch that the wrist is fully cocked (skin wrinkled) before the player begins the lift
- The ball must stay balanced on the fingertips without the balance hand — if it rolls off, the hand position is incorrect
- Reinforce: "Daylight between heel and ball" — two fingers should fit between the heel of the shooting hand and the ball [S5, p.13]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Straight-on only, 2 feet from the rim, making 5 per spot. Stop when form breaks down.
2. **Intermediate**: Vary angles — work from the side (first lane marker / above the box), other side, baseline angle. Around-the-world pattern starting directly in front and working around the lane. [S5, p.17]
3. **Advanced — Strings**: Instead of "make 5 then move," shoot for a consecutive run (e.g., 10 in a row) or shoot until a miss. Higher concentration requirement. [S5, p.17]

# Defensive Rebound Outlet Pass — Pivot Mechanics

## Summary
Securing a defensive rebound is only half the job. Once the rebound is grabbed, the defensive rebounder must immediately execute a **front pivot** to face the outlet receiver and initiate the transition. The pass receiver must also execute a front pivot upon catching the outlet pass to protect the ball and face up-court. Pivoting is the critical link between the defensive stop and the scoring opportunity in transition. [S6, pp.281-283]

This sequence — box out, rebound, chin ball, front pivot, outlet pass, receiver front pivot — is the complete chain that converts a defensive possession into offensive opportunity.

## When to Use
- Immediately after every defensive rebound, whether running a fast break or playing in a slower-paced half-court offense
- Any time a guard (outlet receiver) catches the outlet pass under pressure
- Anytime a rebounder's back is to the court after securing the ball

## Key Principles

1. **Chin the ball first.** After grabbing the defensive rebound, the rebounder should "chin" the ball — pull it tight under the chin with both hands and elbows wide — to protect it before pivoting. [S6, p.282]
2. **Front pivot off the outside foot.** The rebounder front pivots away from the opposition's ball pressure. The pivot foot is the **outside foot** (the foot closer to the sideline or away from the center of the court). The inside (free) foot swings away from defenders. [S6, p.282]
3. **Sight the outlet receiver before passing.** The front pivot must be completed so the rebounder can see the outlet pass receiver AND the defenders near the receiver. Never throw a blind outlet pass. [S6, p.281]
4. **Outlet receiver must meet the pass.** The receiver (typically X1, the point guard) should move toward the ball rather than waiting for it to arrive. "Meet the pass before catching the ball." [S6, p.282]
5. **Receiver front pivots on the catch.** Upon receiving the outlet pass, the guard should immediately front pivot **toward the sideline** (away from defenders in the middle of the court) off the **outside foot**, swinging the inside foot toward the sideline, while also chinning the ball. [S6, pp.282-283]
6. **Face up-court after the pivot.** After the receiver's front pivot, the player can see the basket at the far end and spot any teammates ahead to initiate the fast break or controlled advance. [S6, p.283]

## Player Responsibilities
- **C / PF (Rebounder):** Box out, secure the rebound, chin the ball, front pivot off the outside foot away from ball pressure, sight the outlet receiver and defenders, make the outlet pass.
- **PG / SG (Outlet Receiver):** Read the rebounder's pivot, move to meet the pass (don't stand still), catch and chin the ball, front pivot off outside foot toward the sideline to protect against middle defenders, face up-court and initiate offense.
- **SF / other guards:** Advance ahead of the ball in transition lanes to become secondary options after the outlet pass.

## Execution Step-by-Step

### Step 1 — Secure the Rebound
- All five defenders box out their assigned opponents.
- The rebounder jumps to the ball at its highest point and grabs it with two hands.

### Step 2 — Chin the Ball
- Pull the ball to chin level with elbows wide immediately upon landing.
- This protects the ball from rip attempts and prepares for the pivot.

### Step 3 — Front Pivot (Rebounder)
- Pivot off the **outside foot** (the foot farther from the center of the court / closer to the sideline on the side of the rebound).
- Swing the inside free foot **away from defenders** (toward the outlet receiver side).
- This turn moves away from the opposition's ball pressure because the rebounder's back was initially to the court.
- Now the rebounder can see the outlet receiver and the defenders near them.

### Step 4 — Make the Outlet Pass
- Once the outlet receiver is sighted and the passing lane is confirmed, deliver the outlet pass.
- Do not rush the pass — complete the pivot and sight first.

### Step 5 — Outlet Receiver Meets the Pass
- The receiver (typically PG) moves toward the ball rather than waiting.
- Catch the ball while moving toward it to reduce interception risk.

### Step 6 — Receiver Front Pivots
- Immediately upon catching the outlet pass, the receiver front pivots **toward the sideline** (away from the defenders in the middle of the court).
- Pivot foot: **outside foot** (right foot if receiving on the right side of the court).
- Swing the inside free foot toward the sideline.
- Chin the ball during the pivot.
- After the pivot, the receiver faces up-court and can see the basket and any trailing/leading teammates.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Not chinning the ball before pivoting** → exposes the ball to defensive rip attempts during the pivot; always chin first.
2. **Back pivoting instead of front pivoting** → turns the rebounder into the defender's pressure rather than away from it; always front pivot off the outside foot.
3. **Throwing the outlet pass before sighting the receiver** → results in turnovers; complete the pivot and confirm the receiver's location before passing.
4. **Receiver standing still and waiting for the pass** → makes it easy for defenders to intercept; always move to meet the outlet pass.
5. **Receiver not pivoting after the catch** → leaves the receiver facing the middle of the court where defenders can strip or steal the ball; always front pivot toward the sideline.
6. **Pivoting off the wrong foot (inside instead of outside)** → swings the body into defenders rather than away; the pivot foot is always the outside foot.

## Coaching Cues
- "Chin it, then spin it" — chin the ball before executing the pivot
- "Outside foot is your pivot foot" — consistent cue for both rebounder and outlet receiver
- "Meet the pass, don't wait for it" — for the outlet receiver
- "Pivot to the sideline" — cue for the receiver's front pivot direction
- "See the basket, see your teammates" — reminder of what a good pivot accomplishes

## Variations
### Fast Break Teams
The outlet pass is made quickly to initiate a full-speed fast break. The receiver may not have time to fully chin and pivot — but the mechanics remain the same in principle; speed is increased. The outlet pass is a critical fast-break trigger.

### Half-Court / Controlled Teams
Even teams that do not run a fast break must still use the outlet pass sequence. The same pivot mechanics apply — the guard brings the ball up the court slowly and carefully after the front pivot.

### Right-Side vs. Left-Side Rebounds
When the rebound is on the **right side**: rebounder's outside (pivot) foot is the right foot; receiver's outside pivot foot is also the right foot (pivoting toward the right sideline).
When on the **left side**: mirror image — left foot is the outside pivot foot for both the rebounder and the receiver.

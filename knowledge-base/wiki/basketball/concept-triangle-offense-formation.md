# Triangle Offense: Forming the Sideline Triangle

## Summary
Unlike most offenses that require a specific entry pass to begin a play, the triangle offense can be initiated from multiple first-pass options depending on defensive adjustments. The key structural elements are: a **sideline triangle** on the strong side (formed by the corner player, wing, and low-post center) and a **two-man game** on the weak side. Court symmetry is achieved through 15-20 ft spacing between all five players. All positions are interchangeable — "there's no need for guards, forwards, and centers to play only in their typical spots." [S7, pp.91-96]

## Formation
**Starting alignment:** Two guards near half-court, two forwards at the wings, center at the low post. All players are 15-20 feet apart (pro spacing). The offense is "symmetrical, forming a triangle on both sides of the half-court." [S7, p.91]

## Phases

### Phase 1: First Pass — Strong-Side Fill Options
All options begin with the point guard (1) at the top:

**Guard to Wing (1 passes to 3), then 1 cuts:**
- **Outside cut:** 1 passes to wing (3), cuts outside him, goes to the corner — forming the sideline triangle with 5 and 3.
- **Slice cut:** 1 passes to 3, moves toward him as if to set a screen, then cuts away to the corner.
- **Blur screen cut:** 1 passes to 3, cuts inside, brushes off the center (5), moves to the corner.
- **Basket cut:** 1 passes to 3, cuts hard to the basket coming off 5, then moves to the corner.

*On all strong-side guard cuts, the weak-side guard (2) moves to the middle of the floor for defensive balance and then plays 2-on-2 on the weak side.*

**Wing Dribble Entry:**
1 dribbles toward 3 (signaling 3 to go to the basket then corner); 5 moves to the high post; 2 goes to the middle; 4 cuts into the lane taking the center position; 5 replaces 4 at the wing on a weak-side cut.

**Center Corner Fill:**
1 passes to 3; 5 goes to the corner; 4 cuts (high or low) into the lane and replaces 5; 2 cuts to the weak-side wing spot replacing 4. 1 moves to the top of the circle.

Alternative: 1 passes to 3, 5 goes to the corner, and 1 (or whichever guard is the better post player) replaces 5 in the post.

### Phase 2: First Pass — Weak-Side Fill Options

**Weak-Side Forward Fill:**
1 passes to 3; 4 makes a baseline or over-the-top cut and fills the corner; 2 replaces 4 in the wing position. Court is balanced.

**Weak-Side Guard Fill:**
1 passes to 3; weak-side guard (2) fills the corner via either:
- A basket speed cut
- After a back-pick off the wing (4)

### Phase 3: Sideline Triangle in Action
Once the triangle is formed (wing + corner + low post on one side; guard + forward on the weak side), the offense reads the defense:
- If the post is open on the line of deployment, the wing enters to the post
- If the wing can drive, he drives and passes to the dunker, corner, or weak-side player
- If nothing develops on the strong side, the ball is reversed to the two-man game on the weak side
- Constant ball and player movement prevents the defense from resting

## Key Coaching Points
- **3 must set himself at a proper distance from the sideline** so 1 can cut behind him on the outside cut (and execute all other cut types).
- **Spacing first.** No cut or pass works without proper 15-20 ft spacing maintained throughout.
- **Defense dictates the action.** Once the triangle is set, no play is called — the offensive options are dictated by the positioning and reactions of the defenders.
- **Interchangeable roles.** Any player can fill any spot. This means the defense cannot key on a single player's position or tendency.

## Counters
- If the defense jumps the outside cut, use the basket cut option
- If the defense denies the wing, use the dribble entry (1 dribbles at 3, who clears)
- If the defense overloads the strong side, reverse quickly to exploit the two-man game on the weak side

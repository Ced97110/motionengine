# Shot Fake → Hop Back → Roll-Out Dribble

## Summary
The shot fake hop back / roll-out is an advanced three-part combination offensive sequence: (1) show a shot fake, (2) drive with 1–2 dribbles to force the defender to commit, (3) hop back to create space — and if the defender over-recovers or charges at you, immediately roll the dribble out (attack laterally or diagonally forward) to find a new driving or shooting lane. This is the counter to the counter — when the defender reads your hop back, the roll-out re-attacks their recovery movement.

This move is distinct from the straight hop back in that there is an additional dribble after the hop, changing direction to exploit the defender's over-pursuit. [S3]

## When to Use
- When the defender reads your hop back and charges forward to close
- Against ball-pressure players and defensive gamers who over-pursue the ball
- In late-shot-clock situations where multiple counters are needed to find a shot
- As part of a full dribble-attack series (show-and-go → stride stop → hop back → roll out) to keep defenders guessing

## Key Principles
1. **Hop back is the setup** — the hop back must look like a genuine shot attempt; the defender must commit to closing before the roll-out matters.
2. **Read the defender on the hop** — if they stay, shoot; if they charge, roll out.
3. **Roll-out direction = away from the defender's charge** — if they come straight at you, roll laterally; if they come from your right, roll left.
4. **Keep the dribble alive through the sequence** — do not gather prematurely on the hop back if the roll-out is needed.
5. **1-dribble and 2-dribble setups** — same structure as the straight hop back; practice both directions.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG**: Primary users — primary ball-handlers who engage in extended dribble-attack sequences.
- **SF**: Secondary users — especially in isolation wing situations.

## Variations
### 1-Dribble Hop Back → Roll-Out (Left and Right)
Fake, 1 drive dribble, hop back, if defender closes → roll-out dribble to a new shooting spot.

### 2-Dribble Hop Back → Roll-Out (Left and Right)
Fake, 2 drive dribbles, hop back, roll-out. [S3, Workouts #3-16, #3-17, #3-18]

### Hop Back → Roll-Out → Crossover
Same sequence but instead of a straight roll-out dribble, a crossover changes hands to further separate from the defender (see [[concept-shot-fake-hop-back-crossover]]). [S3, Workout #3-19]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Pre-deciding the roll-out** → Read the defender; only roll out if they close on the hop back, otherwise shoot.
2. **Traveling on the roll-out** → The roll-out dribble must be initiated before the pivot foot moves; sequence must be legal.
3. **Rolling out without a target** → Know where you are rolling to (specific shooting spot) before initiating.
4. **Losing ball control on the roll** → Keep dribble low and tight; roll-out under defensive pressure invites a strip.

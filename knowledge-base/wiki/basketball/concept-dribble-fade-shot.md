# Dribble Fade Shot

## Summary
The dribble fade is a live-ball scoring move executed after catching the ball off a screen. When the defender slips the screen and closes out on a high angle (leaving the baseline open), the cutter establishes a dominant pivot foot and attacks the baseline with a dribble fade. Any scoring opportunity — pull-up jumper, floater, layup, or draw foul — can be used once the baseline is attacked.

This is one of four core reactions for a player catching off a screen, and it is triggered specifically by a high-angle closeout from the defender. [S3, pp.141-143]

## When to Use
- Defender slips (goes under) the screen and closes out at a high angle
- Baseline is left open after the defender overplays the top/middle
- Any time a high-side closeout creates a baseline gap on a wing catch
- Wing catches and high-post catches after down screens or lateral screens

## Key Principles
1. **Read the defender first** — identify a high-angle closeout before committing to the baseline
2. **Establish the dominant pivot foot immediately** on the catch — do not waste time
3. **Attack the baseline with the dribble** — take only the dribbles necessary to get to the scoring opportunity
4. **Use any scoring opportunity** once the baseline is attacked: pull-up jumper, floater, layup, reverse, or draw the foul
5. **Stay low** — keep your center of gravity down to change direction if the defense recovers

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF (ball receiver/shooter)**: Use the screen properly, read the defender's closeout angle, establish pivot foot on catch, attack baseline with dribble fade
- **Screener**: Set a legal stationary screen to force the defender to choose their recovery path
- **Passer**: Deliver the ball to the wing on time and on target so the receiver can read and react immediately

## Variations
### Wing Dribble Fade
Most common version — catcher receives on the wing, reads the high closeout, and attacks the baseline corner area. [S3, p.141]

### Elbow / Mid-Post Dribble Fade
Applied when the catcher receives at the elbow, attacks baseline off the dribble toward the low block. Less common but effective against aggressive help-side defenders.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Not reading the defender** → committing to a direction before identifying the closeout angle; correct by pausing momentarily on catch to read the coverage
2. **Catching flat-footed without a pivot foot** → losing precious reaction time; correct by establishing dominant pivot foot in the air on the catch
3. **Over-dribbling** → taking unnecessary extra dribbles that allow help defense to recover; attack immediately with purpose
4. **Going baseline when it's not open** → resulting in a charge or contested shot; trust the read and only go baseline when it is truly open
5. **Stopping short of a scoring opportunity** → pulling up too far from the basket; attack all the way to the rim or to a high-percentage zone

# Detroit Pistons Double Pick-and-Roll Series

## Overview
Originally developed by Doug Collins and maintained under Alvin Gentry, this series features two variations of a double big-man pick-and-roll for the ball handler (typically Grant Hill at 3). The key is running the PnR off a big who can pop out and hit the perimeter jump shot (Terry Mills in Detroit). Weak-side shooters spot up to eliminate quick rotations. [S14, pp.29-31]

## Formation
**Stacked Version**: 4 and 5 shoulder-to-shoulder facing the sideline, with 5 outside the left elbow — the double stack. Weak-side shooters (2 and 1) spot up opposite.

**Staggered Version**: 4 on left elbow, 5 on right elbow. High cross screen starts the action.

## Phases

### Phase 1A: Stacked Double Pick-and-Roll
- **3** brings the ball up the sideline
- **3** dribbles off the double stack set by **4** and **5** (shoulder to shoulder)
- **5** rolls off **4** to the basket as **3** drives
- **4** pops out to the wing area
- **3** reads:
  1. Turn the corner into the lane for own play
  2. Pass to **5** on the roll
  3. Pass out to **4** for wing jump shot
  4. Pass to **2** if **2**'s defender drops to help on the drive

### Phase 1B: Staggered Double Pick-and-Roll
- **4** aligns left elbow, **5** right elbow
- **4** sets a high **cross screen** for **5**
- **5** cuts off **4** directly to the wing and sets the **first screen** of the stagger for **3**
- **5** rolls to the basket
- **3** continues off **4**'s second screen (4 returns to the left elbow area for this second ball screen)
- **4** pops to open area for jump shot after setting second screen
- **3** reads:
  1. Own shot
  2. **5** on the roll
  3. **4** on the pop
  4. **2** if his defender helps (2 looks for shot or passes to **1** in corner for post-feed to **5**)

## Key Coaching Points
- The big who pops must be a legitimate shooting threat — if he can't knock down the perimeter jumper, the defense doesn't have to honor the pop and can load up on the roll
- Having players **cut and move into** the screens (rather than standing and waiting) makes it harder for defenders to provide help [S14, p.31]
- Weak-side spot-up alignment is essential — if spot-up defenders drop to help, the kick-out is automatic
- Staggered version is harder to defend because the cross-screen that sets it up forces defensive rotation before the main PnR action

## Counters
- If the defense switches, the big who pops will have a size mismatch on the perimeter
- If the defense traps, the quick pop to **4** or kick to weak-side spot-ups is available
- If **2**'s defender overhelps, **1** in the corner has a post-feed opportunity to **5**

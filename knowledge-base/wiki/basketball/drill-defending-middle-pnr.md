# Defending Middle Pick-and-Rolls Drill

## Objective
Develop execution, communication, and technique for containing the middle pick-and-roll — preventing O1's drive, O5's roll layup, and O4's pop-out jumper simultaneously.

## Setup
- 10 players, 5 vs 5 on the half court
- O1: ball, above the top of the key
- O2: right-side free-throw line extended, above three-point line
- O3: left-side free-throw line extended, above three-point line
- O5: top of the key, ready to screen for O1
- O4: left low post, opposite the direction O1 will drive
- Defense in man-to-man

## Execution
1. O1 runs the play to the right side. O5 sets up above the top of the circle, left shoulder toward midcourt.
2. X1 and X5 must **communicate** before the screen — X5 calls out the screen.
3. **At the point of the screen**: The primary goal is **not letting O1 split X1 and X5**.
4. **Soft coverage option** (recommended when straight man-to-man is not viable):
   - Hearing a signal from X5, X1 **drops back even with O5**, avoiding the screen and playing soft to keep O1 in front.
   - This allows X2 and X3 to **stay home** and reduces three-point shooting and draw-and-kick opportunities.
5. **Alternative**: X1 attacks O1, attempting to double up with X5 (hard hedge).
6. When X5 stays with the dribbler, **O5 rolls immediately to the basket**, forcing defensive rotation.
7. As O5 rolls, **O4 pops up the lane** — creating a dangerous high-low threat that must be covered.
8. To **eliminate rotations**: zone, switch, or attack the ball handler to force him away from the screen.

## Coaching Points
- **"Keeping O1 in front of X1 is the key"** — if X1 plays soft and stays in front, X2/X3 can stay home and reduce three-point and kick-out opportunities.
- **"No splits"** — O1 must never penetrate between X1 and X5; this is the middle PnR's primary threat.
- **Know the screener's identity**: Is O5 a roll man or a pop shooter? The coverage choice depends entirely on this [S13, p.212].
- **High-low awareness**: When O5 rolls and O4 pops, the defense faces a 2-on-1 interior threat that requires predetermined rotation rules.
- **Consider zone**: Dallas's game-7 zone against Miami in the 2011 NBA Finals is the model — zone takes teams out of their PnR patterns, timing, and comfort zone [S13, p.212].

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through coverage assignments before any live action; have O5 and O4 execute roll and pop in slow motion so defenders see both threats.
2. **Intermediate**: Live 5v5 with soft coverage as the only allowed option; focus on X1 staying in front and X2/X3 staying home.
3. **Advanced**: O1 reads the defense and chooses between jump shot, O5 roll, or draw-and-kick to O2/O4; defense must respond to all options.

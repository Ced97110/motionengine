# Overhead Dumbbell Triceps Extension

## Objective
Develop triceps brachii strength for explosive elbow extension in chest passing, shot-blocking position, and overhead finishing and rebounding.

## Setup
- 1 player + optional spotter
- Flat bench or seated on a stability ball
- One dumbbell (or two dumbbells for the bilateral variation)

## Execution
1. Sit on a bench with one dumbbell held in both hands behind the head, feet flat on the floor.
2. Position the upper arms next to the head; allow the elbows to flex to approximately 90 degrees — this is the beginning position.
3. While keeping the upper arms fixed and stationary, extend the elbows to raise the dumbbell overhead until the elbows are fully extended.
4. Lower the dumbbell back to the beginning position with control.
5. Repeat for the prescribed number of repetitions.

**Single-Arm Variation**: Perform with one arm at a time to address side-to-side strength imbalances.

**Cable or Barbell Variation**: Attach a rope to an overhead cable pulley or use a barbell behind the head for the same motion.

## Coaching Points
- "Keep the upper torso still by squeezing the elbows toward the head to prevent back arching" [S11]
- Upper arms must remain fixed and close to the head — if the elbows flare outward, the prime mover shifts away from the triceps
- A spotter stands behind the athlete to prevent dropping the dumbbell and to assist getting the weight into the starting position [S11]
- Watch for excessive lumbar extension — if it occurs, reduce the weight or switch to a cable variation

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Seated on a bench (stable base) with a light dumbbell; focus on elbow isolation
2. **Intermediate**: Seated on a stability ball (adds postural stability demand) or single-arm variation
3. **Advanced**: Cable overhead extension with a rope (constant tension, full ROM) or standing bilateral barbell skull-crusher

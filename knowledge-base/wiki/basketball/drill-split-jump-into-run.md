# Split Jump Into Run

## Objective
Develop a quicker first step and explosive forward acceleration — simulating the rebound-to-fast-break transition by combining a split-squat jump with an immediate sprint.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor or full court
- No equipment required
- A measured sprint distance (e.g., 10–20 yards) for the run phase

## Execution
1. Assume a **split squat stance**: front leg with hip and knee flexed at ~90°, front foot pointing straight ahead; rear leg with knee flexed at ~90° pointing toward the floor — half-kneeling position.
2. Hands on hips (below shoulder height) to emphasize leg drive and reduce arm compensation.
3. Bounce up and down twice **without leaving the floor** to load the muscles.
4. Immediately jump as vertically and as high as possible while maintaining the split squat position.
5. On landing, maintain the split squat position and bend the knees to absorb forces.
6. **Immediately sprint** the prescribed distance upon landing.
7. Alternate the forward leg (right vs. left) each repetition for bilateral development.
8. Continue for the prescribed number of reps and sprint distance.

> **Proper technique is critical for injury prevention** — the split position places significant demand on hip flexors, quadriceps, and hamstrings under load. [S2, p.119]

## Coaching Points
- **"Bounce, bounce, EXPLODE"** — the two floor bounces pre-load the stretch-shortening cycle
- Hands on hips enforces leg-dominant movement; add arms only when technique is solid
- Land in the split position — do not allow the rear knee to slam the floor
- The sprint must be immediate — no hesitation between landing and acceleration
- **"Rebound and go"** — this is a fast-break simulation exercise

## Primary Muscles
- **At takeoff:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medius)
- **At landing:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus [S2, p.119]

## Basketball Application
Simulates landing from a rebound and transitioning to a full-speed fast break. Teaches the body to use both legs for force generation and then immediately react into a forward sprint — the exact motor pattern of outlet-pass fast break situations. [S2, p.119]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Split squat hold and jump only — no sprint; focus on landing mechanics
2. **Intermediate:** Add 10-yard sprint after landing; alternate legs each rep
3. **Advanced:** Full-court sprint, add ball carry or ball-handling after sprint initiation

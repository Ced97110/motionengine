# Zigzag Crossover Shuffle Agility Ladder Drill

## Objective
Improve flexibility in abductors and adductors, footwork, and ability to change direction by performing a complex crossover-and-behind zigzag pattern through the agility ladder.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder laid flat
- Player starts on the left side of the ladder in a two-point stance

## Execution
**Forward Pattern:**
1. Start on the left side, two-point stance.
2. **Crossover step** — left foot crosses in front of right foot into the first square.
3. **Bring right leg behind** the left leg to the right side of the first square.
4. **Laterally step** left leg slightly outside the right side of the first square.
5. **Crossover** right leg in front of left into the second square.
6. **Bring left leg behind** right leg to the left side of the second square.
7. **Laterally step** right leg slightly outside the left side of the second square.
8. Continue this zigzag crossover pattern the full length of the ladder.

**Complex Variation (Backward Pattern):** [S8, p.112]
1. Start on the left side, facing backward, two-point stance.
2. Crossover right foot in front of left into first square.
3. Bring left leg behind right to right side of first square.
4. Laterally step right leg in front of left to outside right of first square.
5. Cross left leg behind right into second square.
6. Bring right leg in front of left to left side of second square.
7. Continue pattern backward down the ladder.

## Coaching Points
- Each step is a crossover or a behind-the-leg — no simple side steps
- "Cross, behind, lateral" — verbalize the three-step sequence to train pattern memory
- Emphasize full hip rotation with each crossover — this is the flexibility training component
- Look up — not at feet
- The forward and backward variations train opposite hip-rotation patterns

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the forward pattern only.
2. **Intermediate**: Forward pattern at full speed.
3. **Advanced**: Forward then immediately backward (return trip) at game speed.

# Basketball Warm-Up Drills

## Objective
Prepare the body for the specific movements of basketball through cone shuffles and supplemental exercises that develop flexibility, strength, balance, and coordination while correcting alignment errors before they become ingrained.

## Setup
- **Cone Drills**: Full court; cones placed at free-throw line, half-court, and opposite free-throw line
- **Supplemental Exercises**: Court sideline, a step or low box (≤10 inches), bench or wall
- **Equipment**: Cones (3–8), a step or box, balance board (teeter-totter), tennis balls, medicine balls (light rubber), basketball

## Execution

### Advanced Cone Drill #1 — Basic Shuffle Run
1. Place one cone at the near free-throw line, one at half-court, one at the far free-throw line.
2. Sprint forward to the first cone.
3. Shuffle laterally around the cone without crossing your feet; use active arms.
4. Sprint to the second cone, shuffle in the **opposite** direction.
5. Sprint to the third cone, shuffle in the original direction.
6. Pump arms hard in a sprinting manner between cones; do not drop head or eyes when transitioning from shuffle to sprint.

### Advanced Cone Drill #2 — Wide-Spacing Shuffle + Reaction
1. Same as Drill #1 except place **two cones 3 feet apart** at half-court and **two cones 6 feet apart** at the far free-throw line.
2. Shuffle through the wider spacing, maintaining active hands and sound technique.
3. **Variation A**: Shuffle in a figure-8 pattern through the paired cones.
4. **Variation B**: Coach stands at the cones and points/calls the shuffle direction as the player arrives — adds a reaction component.

### Advanced Cone Drill #3 — Partner Cone Drill (1-on-1 Conversion)
1. Two players 10 feet apart run the Drill #1 pattern to half-court.
2. After passing half-court, one player receives the ball; the other becomes the live defender all the way to the basket.
3. Forces footwork under defensive pressure at game speed.

### Advanced Cone Drill #4 — Multiple Cone Weave
1. Place **four cones** across the near free-throw line; repeat at half-court.
2. Sprint from baseline to the first set of cones.
3. Shuffle **through** the cones (go in front of first cone, behind last cone).
4. Sprint to the next set; shuffle in the **opposite** direction.
5. **Variation**: Run on the diagonal after the first shuffle set — requires performing on both left and right sides.

## Supplemental Pre-Practice Exercises

### 1. Leg Swings — Front to Back
- Stand on a step so the swinging leg does not touch the ground.
- Swing leg from front to back; maintain **neutral pelvis and upper back** throughout.
- Avoid rounding the lower/upper back on forward swing or over-arching on the back swing — both indicate loss of core strength.
- Keep knee and foot **pointed straight forward** when the leg swings behind the body.
- Inability to swing behind without compensation → increased risk of lower-back and hamstring problems.

### 2. Leg Swings — Side to Side
- Same step; swing the leg in a **cross pattern** across the standing leg to both front and back sides.
- Keep the swinging leg pointed straight forward; keep the pelvis square.
- Do not over-arch the lower back to allow the leg to clear.
- Athletes with lower-back problems or kneecap pain often struggle with this variation.

### 3. Step-Ups
- Box height: **no more than 10 inches**; one leg at a time; ≤15 reps per leg.
- Knee must stay in line with toes throughout the movement.
- Knee falling **inward** → tendency toward knee tendonitis.
- Knee swaying **outward** → cartilage risk.
- Strengthens leg and hip muscles; wakes up joint receptors for proper alignment → improved efficiency and power.

### 4. Balance Board
- Stand on a teeter-totter style balance board; knees unlocked; core activated.
- Arms held in an **athletic position** — do not use upper-body sway to cheat the balance.
- Balance must originate from legs and core.
- **Progression**: Perform chest passes, outlet passes, and shooting motions while maintaining balance.
- Board movement naturally decreases as balance improves.

### 5. Tennis Ball Sit-Ups
- Lie on a bench; feet straight up (or knees slightly bent).
- Perform a crunch and switch one tennis ball hand-to-hand **behind the legs**; return to start between reps.
- Perform both clockwise and counterclockwise.
- **Progressions**: 1 ball → 2 balls → 3 balls (2 in one hand, 1 in the other). More balls = more time held at top = greater core demand and hand coordination.
- Secondary benefit: increases hamstring flexibility.

### 6. Wall Tosses
- Use a solid wall at various distances.
- Balls used: light rubber medicine ball → basketball → tennis ball.
- **Movements**: chest passes, outlet passes, one-hand baseball passes, side rotation passes.
- Core is the **source of power**: knees unlocked; generate force upward from legs, through core, to extremities.

## Coaching Points
- "Never warm up at 100 percent speed" — injury risk, technique breakdown, and stamina drain for the rest of practice. Basketball rarely demands 100% straight-line speed; change of direction and velocity are the rule. [S1, p.227]
- Consistent warm-up routine = less soreness, fewer injuries, improved overall performance. [S1, p.231]
- **For injured players**: have them perform the warm-up on the sideline rather than sitting — addresses alignment, strength, flexibility, and balance while injured. [S1, p.231]
- Floor stretches are best done **after practice**, not before, when the body can be shut down.
- Post-practice recovery: walk or ride a stationary bike (no resistance) for 5 minutes to flush lactic acid and reduce next-day soreness. [S1, p.232]
- A mini version of this warm-up before any workout or competition (summer workouts included) is recommended. [S1, p.232]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Cone Drill #1 (single cone shuffle-run); leg swings; step-ups only.
2. **Intermediate**: Add Cone Drill #2 with figure-8 variation; add balance board without ball; 2-ball tennis sit-ups.
3. **Advanced**: Cone Drill #3 (live 1-on-1 conversion) + Cone Drill #4 (diagonal weave); balance board with shooting motion; 3-ball tennis sit-ups; full wall toss sequence.

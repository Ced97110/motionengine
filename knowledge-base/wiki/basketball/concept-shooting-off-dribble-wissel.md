# Shooting Off the Dribble (Wissel)

## Summary
Hal Wissel provides a detailed system for shooting off the dribble, emphasizing consistent pickup mechanics — specifically always picking up the ball in front of the shooting knee with the shooting hand on top — to prevent sidespin and ensure the hand faces the rim at release. He distinguishes between strong-side dribble shots, weak-side crossover shots, the step-back jump shot, and the shot-fake step-through, each with specific footwork and ball-handling mechanics [S7, pp.12-14].

The core principle is that forward, backward, or lateral momentum must be converted to upward momentum by picking up the ball correctly with flexed knees, preventing floating in any direction.

## When to Use
- Anytime a ball-handler attacks off the dribble and finds space to pull up
- When the defense plays back, enabling a pull-up at the elbow or mid-range area
- Step-back: when the defense closes out aggressively and the offensive player needs to create space
- Shot fake step-through: when the defender bites on the shot fake with hand raised, creating a lane to attack

## Key Principles
1. **Always dribble to the front of the shooting knee** — not to the side; pick ball up facing basket [S7, p.12]
2. **Shooting hand on top, balance hand under** — this guarantees the shooting hand faces the rim when the ball is brought up for the shot [S7, p.12-13]
3. **Never rotate the ball into position** — picking up with hands on the sides and rotating produces side-spin under pressure [S7, p.13]
4. **Jump behind the ball on the last dribble** — ensures you land square to the basket, not drifting forward [S7, p.13]
5. **Knees flexed on pickup** — converts momentum to upward energy; prevents floating in any direction [S7, p.13]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG, SG, SF**: Primary users of all off-dribble shooting techniques

## Variations

### Strong-Hand Side (Same Direction as Shooting Hand)
1. Dribble with the **outside hand** (farther from defender); keep eyes on basket
2. Create space with one long dribble past the defender's body
3. Push off the pivot foot; aim dribble past defender
4. Protect ball with inside hand and body
5. **Jump behind the ball** on the last dribble; pick up in front of shooting knee
6. Shooting hand on top, balance hand under; jump straight up; shoot; land in balance [S7, p.13]

### Weak-Hand Side (Opposite Direction to Shooting Hand)
1. Dribble with outside hand; keep eyes on basket
2. Create space with one long dribble past defender
3. Execute a **second (crossover) dribble** to the front of the shooting-side knee
4. Jump behind ball on last dribble; pick up in front of shooting knee
5. Shooting hand on top, balance hand under; jump straight up; shoot; land in balance [S7, p.13]

### Step-Back Jump Shot
1. Read the defender; fake a drive (shot fake or shoulder fake)
2. **Quick step back** with outside foot from the defender, simultaneous with an outside-hand dribble back
3. This creates space between ball handler and defender
4. Jump behind ball; pick up in front of shooting knee; shooting hand on top, balance hand under
5. Shoot jump shot; **exaggerate follow-through of shoulders, head, and shooting hand toward basket** to counter lean-back tendency [S7, pp.13-14]

### Shot Fake Step-Through Jump Shot
1. Be a credible shooting threat — make an **aggressive** (not slight) shot fake; ball aggressively brought above forehead, knees remain flexed [S7, p.11]
2. Read the defender's hand position
3. If defender's hand is **up on the ball side**: step through with the inside foot past the defender's lead foot, moving head and shoulders under the defender's arm
4. Protect ball with head and shoulders, move ball away from defender's reach
5. Shoot jump shot; be strong — expect contact; complete the shot for possible three-point play [S7, p.14]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Reaching to the side for the ball** → Dribble to front of shooting knee; don't reach — pick it up squared to basket
2. **Picking up with hands on sides → sidespin** → Always shooting hand on top, balance hand under
3. **Floating forward/backward/sideways** → Pick up with knees flexed; convert momentum upward
4. **Insufficient shot fake** → A slight upward move won't freeze the defender; bring the ball aggressively above the forehead
5. **Step-back lean** → Exaggerate follow-through toward basket to compensate for any backward body lean [S7, pp.15-16]

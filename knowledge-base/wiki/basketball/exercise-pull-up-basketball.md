# Pull-Up (Basketball Strength Training)

## Objective
Build upper-back and lat strength — the pulling foundation for rebounding, driving the ball up through contact, and defending — through a full-range vertical pull.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: Pull-up bar or lat pulldown machine handles
- **Players**: 1 athlete (spotter optional)
- **Location**: Weight room

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Grasp the bar with a pronated (overhand) grip at or slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Begin from a full hang with elbows fully extended and feet off the floor. Look straight ahead.
2. Maintaining a rigid, upright upper-body position, squeeze the shoulders back together and pull the body upward until the **upper chest meets the bar** (or shoulders meet the handles).
3. Pause briefly at the top.
4. Allow the elbows to extend, lowering the body back toward the floor in a **controlled manner**.
5. Lower until the elbows are **fully extended** before the next rep.

## Coaching Points
- **"Chest to the bar"** — full range of motion is required; partial reps limit lat development
- Avoid swinging/kipping to complete the movement — momentum reduces the training stimulus
- **Brace the core** throughout to protect the back and prevent midsection flexion
- Ensure elbows extend completely at the bottom of each rep
- Relax the neck; do not let shoulders shrug up

## Spotting Guideline
A spotter can assist by pushing on the **middle back**, or lifting under the shins/ankles if the athlete has flexed knees.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use a resistance band looped around the bar for assistance — reduces bodyweight load
2. **Intermediate**: Strict bodyweight pull-ups with full range of motion
3. **Advanced**: Add a pause at the top; wrap a towel around the bar for grip challenge; flex hips to bring knees to chest during the upward movement; add weight via a belt

## Variations
- **Chin-up**: Supinated (underhand) grip — increases biceps involvement
- **Neutral grip**: Palms facing each other — reduced shoulder stress
- **Alternated grip**: One hand pronated, one supinated
- **Hip-flexed pull-up**: Knees driven to chest during the upward phase throughout the entire set
- **Extended pause**: Hold at top or bottom for additional time under tension

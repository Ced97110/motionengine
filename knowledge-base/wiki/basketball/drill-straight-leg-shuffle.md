# Straight-Leg Shuffle

## Objective
Increase hip strength and elastic ankle strength by running with straight legs and dorsiflexed feet, emphasizing fast ground contact and hip pull-through.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Open area, typically 20-40 yards
- Performed during warm-up or as part of a speed mechanics circuit

## Execution
1. Begin running forward while keeping both legs straight (minimal knee bend)
2. Keep the foot **dorsiflexed** (toes up) throughout the movement
3. Emphasize **fast ground contact** on the ball of the foot
4. At each ground contact, actively **pull through with the hips** — hip extension drives momentum
5. Keep posture upright; do not lean back
6. Maintain coordinated arm swing in proper sprinting mechanics [S8, p.25]

## Coaching Points
- "Pull through with the hips" — the emphasis is on active hip extension, not passive leg swing
- Dorsiflexion must be maintained — toes up prevents heel contact
- Fast ground contact time is the goal; slow, labored steps defeat the purpose
- Arms should maintain proper sprint mechanics throughout

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Very slow pace, exaggerating the straight-leg and dorsiflexion positions
2. **Intermediate**: Increase tempo while maintaining strict straight-leg form
3. **Advanced**: Extend over longer distances; follow immediately with a sprint start to transfer mechanics [S8, p.25]

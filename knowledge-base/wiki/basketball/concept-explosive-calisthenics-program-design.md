# Explosive Calisthenics Program Design

## Summary
The Explosive Calisthenics program design system organizes six movement chains (jumps, power pushups, kip-ups, front flips, back flips, muscle-ups) into progressive training templates ranging from entry-level two-exercise sessions to advanced six-chain splits. The core philosophy is that explosive training is **not an endurance sport** — every rep must be performed at maximum power with complete recovery between sets. Volume is intentionally low; quality governs quantity [S9, pp.281-284].

For basketball coaches, these templates provide a scalable framework for adding explosive bodyweight training before or alongside strength/sport sessions, using the neural activation benefit of explosive work to potentiate subsequent strength training.

## When to Use
- Pre-season and in-season athletic development for explosive power
- When athletes need to improve vertical jump, first-step quickness, and reactive strength
- As a pre-activation protocol before strength or basketball practice sessions
- When gym equipment is unavailable (bodyweight-only settings)

## Key Principles
1. **Warm up thoroughly before every session** — joint rolling, higher-rep heating work, tension-flexibility stretches, then 2-3 easy reps of the target movement [S9, p.281]
2. **Warming up lowers neurological inhibition** — shifting through power gears actually makes athletes stronger; this is why warm-up attempts matter in Olympic lifting [S9, p.281]
3. **Keep reps crisp and fresh** — when fatigue arrives, stop the set; explosive training degrades rapidly when done tired [S9, p.282]
4. **Use the Rule of Three and Rule of Six** — adjust sets/reps within these parameters as conditioning improves [S9, p.282]
5. **Consolidation training throughout the day** — short "greasing the groove" mini-sessions (5 reps every 1-2 hours) accelerate skill acquisition without causing fatigue [S9, pp.284-285]
6. **Sequence skill acquisition** — master jumps and power pushups (step 6) before adding kip-ups; master kip-ups before adding flips [S9, pp.282-283]
7. **Every 6-8 weeks, change something** — rotate exercises, vary sets/reps, keep training stimulating [S9, p.288]

## Program Templates

### Pure Power (Beginner)
The simplest explosive template — two exercises, minimal volume:
- **Day 1:** Jump work (3×2), Power pushups (3×2)
- **Day 2:** Off
- Repeat

Can be completed before strength, cardio, or basketball practice after a thorough warm-up [S9, p.281].

### Sacred Trinity (Beginner + Skill Introduction)
Adds kip-up skill work once jumps and power pushups reach step 6:
- **Day 1:** Jump work (6×2), Kip-up work (5-10 min)
- **Day 2:** Power pushups (6×2), Kip-up work (5-10 min)
- **Day 3:** Off
- Repeat

Provides six kip-up sessions over eight days for accelerated skill progress [S9, p.282].

### 4-Leaf Clover (Intermediate — adds front flip)
Swaps one kip-up session for front flip introduction:
- **Day 1:** Jump work (6×2), Kip-up work (5-10 min)
- **Day 2:** Power pushups (6×2), Front flip work (5-10 min)
- **Day 3:** Off
- Repeat

Note: train front flip before back flip — earlier stages of front flip carry less fear factor [S9, p.283].

### "25s" (Consolidation Single-Chain Focus)
For athletes wanting to master one chain rapidly:
- Perform 5 single reps of the target step, 5 times per day, at least 2 hours apart = 25 reps/day
- Advance to next step when all 25 reps are acceptable
- Optional math variants: 3 reps/hour × 5 hrs = 15; 5 reps/hour × 10 hrs = 50 [S9, p.284]

### 2-Day Split (Advanced — all six chains)
Splits all six chains across two training days:
- **Day 1:** Jump work (6×3), Power pushups (6×3), Kip-up work (5 min), Back flip work (10 min)
- **Day 2:** Off
- **Day 3:** Jump work (6×3), Power pushups (6×3), Front flip work (10 min), Muscle-up work (10 min)
- **Day 4:** Off
- Repeat [S9, p.285]

### The Muscleman (Integration with Strength Training)
Pairs explosive chain work with related strength exercises:
- Jump work (5-10 min) before legs/squats
- Power pushup work (5-10 min) before chest/pushups
- Kip-up work (5-10 min) before midsection/leg raises
- Front flip work (5-10 min) before shoulders/handstand pushups
- Back flip work (5-10 min) before back/low back/bridging
- Muscle-up work (5-10 min) before lats/upper back/pullups

The moderate explosive volume **charges the nervous system** (neural innervation), actually increasing strength in the subsequent strength exercises — not depleting it [S9, p.286].

### Johnny Kung Fu (Advanced — high frequency)
Higher frequency with specialization by movement type:
- **Day 1:** Jump work (6×3), Kip-up work (5 min), Back flip work (10 min), Front flip work (10 min)
- **Day 2:** Power pushups (6×3), Muscle-up work (10 min)
- **Day 3:** Off
- Repeat

Day 1 = jumping skills; Day 2 = upper-body power. Suitable for athletes who have reached master steps and want maintenance or specialization [S9, p.287].

## Common Mistakes
1. **Too many reps when tired** → Stop when power output drops; explosives are about maximum effort, not volume
2. **Skipping the warm-up** → Even in consolidation training, joint loosening and 2-3 easy reps are required before work sets
3. **Adding too many chains too fast** → Add one new chain at a time; overload causes burnout quickly
4. **Treating explosive work like endurance training** → High rep sets at submaximal intensity defeat the purpose
5. **Ignoring consolidation training** → The "25s" approach dramatically accelerates skill progress when athletes are stuck

# Quick Shooting Drill for Two Players (Drill 95)

## Objective
Develop high-volume shooting with proper technique in a fast-paced two-player format that emphasizes crisp passing and sound rebounding.

## Setup
- **2 players** with **2 basketballs**
- Half-court — 4 different floor areas used in sequence
- 2 minutes total (4 areas × 30 seconds each = 15 sec per player per area)

## Execution
1. Both players begin with a basketball.
2. **Player 1 shoots**.
3. **Player 2 chest-passes** to Player 1 immediately after Player 1 lands.
4. **Player 2 rebounds** Player 1's first shot.
5. Sequence continues for **15 seconds** — players alternate roles.
6. **Player 1 changes spots** on the floor; the drill continues for **30 more seconds** from that spot (15 sec for each player).
7. Four different areas are used to complete the **2-minute drill**.

## Options
1. Require Player 1 to use a **slide step** in either direction before receiving the second pass for a jump shot.
2. Player 2 may use a **bounce pass** instead of a chest pass.

## Coaching Points
- All passes must be **crisp** — passes that are soft or off-target disrupt timing.
- Rebounders must use **proper rebounding technique** — don't just grab the ball; box out and secure.
- "Don't let shooters get lazy!" — every shot must be **picture perfect**.
- Prioritize **technique over volume** — never sacrifice form for speed.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary shooter — develop rhythm between passer and shooter.
2. **Intermediate**: Add slide step (lateral footwork) before the catch.
3. **Advanced**: Player 2 varies the pass type (chest, bounce, overhead) to simulate game passes.

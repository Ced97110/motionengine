# Side Lunge

## Objective
Develop lateral lower-body strength including hip abductors, adductors, quads, and glutes — the muscle groups that drive defensive slides, direction changes, and lateral cuts in basketball.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Dumbbell in each hand (at sides, neutral grip) OR a single dumbbell/kettlebell held between the legs with both hands
- Feet approximately shoulder-width apart
- Open floor space

## Execution
1. Take **one large step to one side** with either leg.
2. After the lead foot is fully planted, **push the hips back** and flex the knee of the lead leg (trailing leg knee stays extended).
3. The lead knee should **point in the same direction as the lead foot**.
4. Side-lunge to a position where the **lead knee is over the lead foot** and the **lead thigh is approximately parallel to the floor** (b).
5. Push the lead foot off the floor to **return to the beginning position**.
6. Pause and repeat by stepping to the **other side** with the other leg.

## Coaching Points
- Lead knee must **not travel forward beyond the line of the toes**
- **Do not let the back collapse** while lowering — maintain a neutral spine and upright chest
- Keep the **chest and head up** throughout
- The trailing leg stays straight — a bent trailing knee indicates the step is too narrow
- Push the hips back first (like a hip hinge), then flex the knee — not purely a knee-bend movement
- **Inhale** during the downward movement; **exhale** after the sticking point of the upward movement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight side lunge — focus on hip hinge pattern and depth
2. **Intermediate**: Dumbbell/kettlebell side lunge with moderate load
3. **Advanced**: Continuous alternating side lunges (lateral walking lunge pattern) with heavier load

## Common Mistakes
1. **Knee traveling past the toes** → increased patellofemoral stress → cue "push the hips back first"
2. **Back rounding at the bottom** → cue "chest and head up, neutral spine"
3. **Trailing knee bending** → step too narrow → increase lateral step width
4. **Not reaching parallel** → insufficient hip mobility or step length → work on hip flexor and adductor mobility first

# Triple-Threat Position

## Summary
The triple-threat position is the universal starting stance for offensive basketball. Called "triple threat" because from this stance a player can instantly pass, drive, or shoot. It is the proper starting position for nearly every one-on-one move, and every perimeter cut should end in this position upon receiving the ball [S7, p.20].

Every perimeter player — and ideally every player regardless of position — must develop this stance as their automatic default upon catching the ball.

## When to Use
- Immediately upon catching the ball at any perimeter spot
- After completing any cutting action (V cut, L cut, reverse) to receive a pass
- As the default "ready" position when facing a defender with live dribble
- Any time a player wants to keep all three offensive options open before committing

## Key Principles
1. **Shooting hand behind the ball** — wrist bent backward, elbow bent, ready to shoot
2. **Non-shooting (guard) hand in front** — arm bent about 45 degrees, placed in front and slightly below the ball to protect it
3. **Ball position** — on the side of the body, between chest and hip; not held high (slower to shoot) or too low (harder to pass)
4. **Knees bent** — stay low; this enables explosive first steps or jump shots
5. **Trunk slightly forward** — leans forward for balance and drive readiness; never upright
6. **Eyes up** — watch the defender and the floor, not the ball
7. **Feet toward the basket** — turn immediately on catch so the basket is directly in your field of view

## Body Alignment

| Body Part | Position |
|-----------|----------|
| Shooting hand | Behind ball, wrist cocked backward |
| Elbow (shooting) | Bent, pointing down |
| Non-shooting hand | In front/below ball, ~45° arm angle |
| Ball location | Side of body, chest-to-hip height |
| Knees | Bent — low center of gravity |
| Trunk | Slightly forward, balanced |
| Feet | Pointed toward basket |

## Common Mistakes
1. **Holding the ball too high** → slows the drive first step; defenders can strip the ball
2. **Standing upright** (straightening the knees) → eliminates explosiveness; easy for defenders to read
3. **Not facing the basket on catch** → forces an extra pivot, costing time and dribbles
4. **Non-shooting hand positioned incorrectly** → either too far in front (blocking the shot) or behind the ball (can't protect it)
5. **Ball in the middle of the body** → should be on the side; center position limits drive options

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Catch, stop, establish triple threat with a coach check of all five points
2. **Intermediate**: Catch off a pass, arrive in triple threat, hold for 1 second, then execute move
3. **Advanced**: Game-speed catches off cuts; immediately threaten all three options within one dribble

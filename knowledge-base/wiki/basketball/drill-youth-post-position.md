# Post Position (Drill 10)

## Objective
Teach post players to establish correct low-post position relative to various defender locations, receive the entry pass, and execute an appropriate post move.

## Setup
- Individual or team drill
- Half court, low block area
- Players line up below the block/baseline; a coach stands on the wing or sideline as passer
- 1 ball per station

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure shows line of players at bottom of lane. Coach on wing/sideline. Post player cuts across the lane, establishes position on the block, receives pass from coach, executes drop step layup, rebounds, passes back to coach, and goes to end of line. -->

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up as shown.
2. As a player cuts across the lane, a coach tells the player where the (imaginary) defender is.
3. The player reacts by **getting position** on the would-be defender.
4. The coach passes the ball to the player, who executes a **drop step** for a layup.
5. The player rebounds the layup (made or missed), passes back to the coach, and goes to end of the line.

### Intermediate
1. Instead of a drop step, the coach tells the player which post move to use: **spin move**, **half-spin move**, or **face-up moves**.
2. Instead of shooting a layup, the player shoots a **power layup** or **baby hook** (based on the move chosen and which side of the floor they are on).

### Advanced
1. Add a **passive defender** — player must read the defender and establish proper position. Player decides which move to use to take advantage of that position.
2. While the player is in post position, the coach races a few steps up or down the sideline and calls out a **new defensive position** — requiring the post player to adjust position accordingly.

## Coaching Points
1. **Positioning at the big block**: 
   - If defender is **above**: nudge the defender a step up the lane (use body strength, NOT arms/hands).
   - If defender is **below**: push the defender one step toward the baseline.
   - If defender is **fronting**: take one step toward the coach to create a greater passing area for the lob pass.
2. **"On" arm**: Bend at the elbow to form a **90-degree angle**, then place the forearm against the defender's lower torso/waist to hold position.
3. **"Off" arm (signaling)**: Form a 90-degree angle with forearm **perpendicular to the floor**. Spread the off hand as a **target for the passer**.
4. **Foot positioning**: Ensure the foot on the side of the defender is **beyond the front foot of the defender** (who is in a three-quarter denial position). This allows the quick drop step to the basket for the layup.
- Coaching cue: "Use your body, not your hands" when establishing post position.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Cut, establish position vs. imaginary defender, receive pass, drop step layup
2. **Intermediate**: Coach calls the move (spin, half-spin, face-up); add power layup or baby hook
3. **Advanced**: Live passive defender; dynamic defender repositioning calls

# Upper-Body Box Shuffles

## Objective
Improve power and quickness in the upper-body pushing musculature using a box or step to create a vertical plyometric challenge. [S8, p.165]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor with a box or step
- Equipment: plyometric box or step

## Execution
1. Start in the push-up position with hands on the box.
2. Drop the hands to the floor straddling the box (hands land on either side of the box).
3. Immediately and explosively push off the floor to return hands to the top of the box.
4. Try to minimize time on the ground — get off the floor as quickly as possible.
5. Repeat for reps or time.

## Coaching Points
- Minimal ground contact time is the key metric — this is a reactive drill.
- Keep the body straight throughout; hips must not drop or pike.
- Start with a lower box; progress box height as strength and speed improve.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Lower box height (4–6 inches); slow controlled drops.
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate box (8–12 inches); focus on explosive floor push.
3. **Advanced**: Higher box; timed sets (max reps in 10 seconds).

# New York Knicks Zipper-to-Baseline Triple

## Overview
Jeff Van Gundy's 1999 NBA Finals Knicks generated consistent scoring from this combination play. A zipper screen (down screen from the ball-side elbow for a player on the ball-side block) brings Allan Houston (2) or Latrell Sprewell to the top, freeing up an elite isolation player at the top of the key while the point guard (Charlie Ward / Chris Childs) runs off triple baseline staggered screens. The play has five distinct options depending on defensive coverage. [S14, pp.57-61]

## Formation
- **1 (PG, Ward/Childs)** on the right wing
- **2 (SG, Houston)** on the ball-side block
- **3 (SF, Sprewell)** set in front of the rim
- **4 (PF)** off the left block
- **5 (C, Ewing)** at the ball-side elbow

## Phases

### Phase 1: Zipper Screen — 2 Cuts to the Top
- 1 dribbles to the right wing
- 2 cuts up off the down screen (zipper) from 5 to the top of the key
- 1 passes to 2
- 1 cuts along the baseline off the triple staggered screens set by **5** (back screen near the lane), **3** (in front of the rim), and **4** (off the left block), in that order
- 2 adjusts with a dribble to improve his passing angle
- 4 posts up after setting the last screen — 1 has option of a shot or post-feed to 4

### Phase 2: Wing PnR Alternative (for 1)
- After 1 clears the last screen, 4 has the option of stepping out of the post to run a **wing pick-and-roll** with 1 after ball reversal

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.2 — 4 steps out for PnR with 1 on the wing. -->

### Phase 3: Back-to-3 Option (if 1 is denied)
- After 3 sets the second baseline screen for 1, 3 clears the lane by cutting off 5 to the right side
- 2 can pass back to 3 (Sprewell) if 1 is denied on the baseline run

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.3 — 3 clears off 5 to right side; 2 passes to 3 if 1 is denied. -->

### Phase 4: Baseline Counter for 1 (Pop-Back)
- When 1 reads that his defender is cheating over the top to beat him to the weak side, 1 uses the counter
- 1 cuts just below the back screen from 5 and cuts back toward the right corner
- 5 may set a legal screen on 1's defender
- 2 hits 1 popping back for the shot OR post-feed to 5 (Ewing)
- New York preferred to pop Ward back to set up the Ewing post-feed

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.4 — 1 pops back to right corner; 5 sets legal screen if possible; 2 passes to 1 for shot or post-feed to 5. -->

### Phase 5: Baseline Counter to PnR
- Instead of holding the post after the counter, 5 steps out to run a **pick-and-roll with 1**

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.5 — 5 steps out from post; PnR with 1 after pop-back. -->

### Phase 6: One-on-One Action for 2 (Isolation Variant)
- When Houston has a favorable individual matchup, the team clears out instead of running the baseline triple
- 2 cuts high off the zipper from 5; 1 pops back off 5
- 3 cuts to the left corner; 4 flattens to the left block
- This creates a 1-4 low spacing that gives 2 a clear side to attack off the dribble

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.6 / 6.7 — Zipper for 2; 3 and 4 clear to corners/blocks; 2 isolates at top. -->

### Phase 7: Elbow PnR Action (Houston + Ewing)
- 1 dribbles to wing, passes to 2 off the zipper
- 1 runs the baseline triple as in the basic set
- Instead of looking for 1, 2 ignores the baseline — 5 steps up from the baseline to run an **elbow pick-and-roll** with 2
- Ewing pops for a jump shot; 2 has a clear side to operate
- 3 pops to left corner; 4 holds on the left block

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.8 — 5 steps up for elbow PnR with 2; Ewing pops; 3 corners; 4 holds block. -->

## Key Coaching Points
- The triple staggered screen creates maximum stress — three defenders must sequence their help assignments
- 2 must adjust with a dribble to maintain the proper passing angle to 1 coming off the last screen
- 4 always posts after setting the last screen — the defense must choose to cover the cutter or the screener
- The isolation variant requires all four teammates to immediately read 2's hesitation and clear — no hesitation from teammates
- The elbow PnR is the safety valve when the baseline triple is denied and the isolation isn't available
- "New York likes to pop Ward back to set up the post-feed to Ewing" [S14, p.59]

## Counters
- Defender cheats on baseline triple → pop-back counter (Phase 4)
- Pop-back denied → PnR with 5 (Phase 5)
- Baseline triple overloaded → isolation spacing (Phase 6)
- Isolation denied → elbow PnR (Phase 7)

# Renegade Row (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop combined pushing and pulling upper-body strength along with core anti-rotation stability — building the shoulder protection, passing speed, and rebounding balance needed in basketball.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Two dumbbells of equal weight
- Open floor space

## Execution
1. Assume a **push-up position** with both hands gripping the handles of the dumbbells. Arms extended, both feet firmly on the ground. Back flat and in line with hips, shoulders, and legs — rigid plank.
2. While **stabilizing the left arm** on its dumbbell, perform a **single-arm row with the right arm**: raise the dumbbell to just below the chest, keeping the elbow close to the body.
3. **Critical**: Keep shoulders, hips, and legs in line — **avoid any rotation** during the pull.
4. Return the dumbbell to the starting position.
5. Execute the same motion with the **other arm**.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions for each arm.

## Coaching Points
- "Hips square to the floor — do not let the hip rotate toward the pulling arm."
- The anti-rotation demand is the point: core and hip muscles must fire to resist the rotational pull force.
- Wide feet provide a wider base of support — beginners can spread the feet further apart to reduce rotation.
- Keep the elbow tight to the ribs on the pull — no swinging.
- "Both dumbbells are your foundation — the supporting arm is working as hard as the pulling arm."
- This is a **pushing AND pulling** exercise simultaneously — do not think of it as a row with a push-up base; both actions are active.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform on knees (modified push-up position) to reduce stability demand; use light dumbbells.
2. **Intermediate**: Standard renegade row, wide foot stance for stability.
3. **Advanced**: Narrow foot stance (increases anti-rotation challenge); add a push-up between each row.

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Triceps brachii, pectoralis major, posterior deltoid, latissimus dorsi
- **Secondary**: Pectoralis minor, anterior deltoid, anconeus, trapezius, rhomboid major, rhomboid minor, biceps brachii

## Basketball Focus
The renegade row targets the full upper body — **both pushing and pulling muscle chains** — with the added demand of core anti-rotation stability. Basketball applications:
- **Passing and receiving speed**: a stronger upper body allows faster ball release and reception
- **Improved balance and posture** when rebounding or shooting under contact
- **Shoulder injury prevention**: the combination of push and pull in one movement creates balanced shoulder joint strength
- Specifically: pectoralis major, pectoralis minor, and anterior deltoid are loaded during the stabilizing (push) phase; rhomboid major and rhomboid minor are strengthened during the pulling phase

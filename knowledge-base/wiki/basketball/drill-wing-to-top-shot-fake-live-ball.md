# Wing to Top Shot Fake (SF) Live-Ball Move Shooting (Workout #11-19)

## Objective
Develop the shot-fake live-ball move at and beyond the three-point line — catching on a balanced two-foot stop, using a convincing shot fake to freeze or elevate the defender, then attacking off the dribble for a scoring opportunity.

## Setup
- 1 shooter, 1 rebounder/passer (or solo with self-pass spinout)
- Half court; three-point arc
- Shot chart tracking sheet (FGA, FGM, FTA, FTM columns)
- Both sides of the floor used

## Execution
1. Shooter starts on the wing and moves toward the top of the key (or back to the wing) at game speed.
2. Player receives the ball on a **two-foot stop** beyond the three-point line.
3. After the catch, the player performs a **shot fake (SF) live-ball move** — presenting the ball upward to simulate a shot, freezing the imaginary or live defender, then attacking with a dribble move past the arc for a pull-up jumper or drive.
4. The shot is taken beyond the three-point line or just inside depending on the defender's response.
5. Repeat until **10 shots** are taken from each side.
6. Intersperse **5 free throws** between each set of 10 field-goal attempts.

**Full workout structure (each side):**
- Wing to Top SF Shots: 10
- FTs: 5
- Wing to Top SF Shots: 10
- FTs: 5
- Wing to Top SF Shots: 10

**Total shots per workout: 80 field-goal attempts + 20 free throws** (both sides combined).

**Performance targets:**
- FG: 36-60 made (60%)
- FT: 17-20 made (85%)

## Coaching Points
- The shot fake must be **convincing** — ball goes up at full shooting speed, eyes on the rim, not at the defender
- "Show the shot, sell the fake" — a lazy fake does not move a good defender
- After the fake, quickly rip to the hip and attack the dribble before the defender can recover
- Keep both feet on the floor during the fake to maintain live-ball status (do not lift the pivot foot)
- Two-foot stop on the catch gives maximum pivot foot options — choose the foot that puts the defender on their heels
- Practice going both left and right off the fake
- Track all attempts on the shot chart

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary practice — catch on two feet, show the fake, dribble one direction and shoot a pull-up; no movement pattern yet
2. **Intermediate**: Add the wing-to-top movement pattern at game speed; predetermined direction after the fake
3. **Advanced**: Add a live closeout defender; shooter reads the defender's reaction to the fake and attacks the appropriate side

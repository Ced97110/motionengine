# Round-Off and Cartwheel (Acrobatic Combinations)

## Summary
The round-off is an advanced acrobatic linking movement that converts forward momentum into backward momentum, enabling combination flip sequences. It is similar to a cartwheel — both rotate the athlete laterally — but the round-off finishes facing 180° from the starting direction, allowing the athlete to transition into back handsprings or back flips.

The **cartwheel** is considered a basic progression leading to the round-off. Both movements add lateral rotational power to the athlete's training repertoire, complementing the front-and-back rotational power developed by the flip chains [S9, p.167].

## When to Use
- After mastering at least Step 9 (running front flip) of the front flip chain
- When combining acrobatic movements for extended sequences
- As a training tool to add lateral rotational power to complement the flip chains
- Note: "None of this is necessary for increased power — it's a skill feat" [S9, p.166]

## Key Principles
1. **The round-off turns you around** — start facing forward, finish facing backward (180° rotation)
2. **Forward momentum becomes backward momentum** — this is the mechanical purpose of the round-off
3. **The cartwheel adds lateral rotational power** — just as flips add front/back rotational power
4. **Combination sequences require both flip chains to be well-developed** — do not attempt until back flip work is also progressing
5. **Mastering the cartwheel before the round-off** — the cartwheel is the foundational prerequisite

## Acrobatic Combination Sequences (Post Round-Off)
Once the round-off is available, the following sequences become possible:
- Round-off → back handspring
- Round-off → back flip
- Round-off → back handspring → back flip
- Handstand → roll → front flip (different conceptual approach, no round-off required)

## The Cartwheel
- Rotates the athlete **sideways** through 360° with alternating hand-foot contacts
- Adds **lateral rotational power** to complement forward/backward flip power
- Prerequisite to the round-off
- Can be performed for multiple reps as a lateral power drill

## Common Mistakes
1. **Attempting combinations before mastering individual flips** → dangerous; build both flip chains first
2. **Under-rotating the round-off** → finishing at 90° rather than 180° kills backward momentum
3. **Rushing to combinations for aesthetics rather than power** → "if it's power and explosiveness you want, you don't really need to move beyond the standing front flip" [S9, p.168]

# Shot Fake / Step Fake

## Summary
The shot fake/step fake is a live-ball move used to get a defender to shift his weight upward on the fake, creating space for the offensive player to drive around the defender and generate a scoring opportunity for himself or a teammate. When executed properly, it is one of the most effective tools in a player's individual scoring arsenal because it forces the defender to make a choice — and punishes either decision. [S3, p.12]

The "step fake" is a critical companion to the shot fake: by stepping as well as showing the ball, the offensive player establishes his dominant pivot foot and protects against a travel violation. [S3, p.12]

## When to Use
- When receiving a pass on the perimeter, wing, elbow, or post with a closing or over-aggressive defender
- When a defender is in the air or leaning on a prior fake
- When the ball handler wants to attack in a specific direction based on defensive positioning
- On any live-ball possession where a dribble has not yet been used
- In post situations when a defender commits to a direction

## Key Principles
1. **Catch on a two-foot jump stop** — this gives the offensive player maximum pivot options before making a move. [S3, p.12]
2. **Establish the dominant pivot foot** — use an aggressive shot fake/step fake; the step fake physically commits one foot as the pivot, preventing a travel call. [S3, p.12]
3. **Always step fake** — the step fake is not optional; it legally establishes the pivot and eliminates the travel call. [S3, p.12]
4. **Bring the ball to the opposite hip** — after the shot/step fake, ripping the ball to the opposite hip generates momentum in the attack direction. [S3, p.12]
5. **Read the defender** — dictate the direction of the fake based on the defender's reaction. Go where the defender gives you. [S3, p.12]
6. **Determine the scoring opportunity** — after the defender reacts, choose which scoring option to execute (see Scoring Opportunities below). [S3, p.12]
7. **Execute at game speed** — all workouts using the shot fake/step fake must be done at game speed. [S3, p.13]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Primary user of shot fake off the dribble drive; must read the point of attack defender's hips and shoulders.
- **SG**: Wing catch-and-fake into pull-up or drive; a key weapon when receiving off screens.
- **SF**: Wing and elbow shot fakes; must be able to attack both directions off the fake.
- **PF**: Post-entry fakes and perimeter fakes in stretch-4 situations.
- **C**: Post shot fakes when receiving the ball on the block or elbow; set up the hook, turn-around, or drop-step.

## Scoring Opportunities Off the Shot Fake/Step Fake
The following are the complete scoring options available after a shot fake/step fake [S3, p.13]:

### Attack
Drive directly at the basket past the rising defender; finish with a lay-up or contact finish.

### Attack / Crossover
Fake one way, then cross over to attack the basket from the opposite side; used when the defender over-commits to the attack side.

### Stride Stop
Take 1–2 dribbles and stride stop into a mid-range jump shot; effective when the defender recovers but gives up the mid-range.

### Stride Stop / Step-Through
Fake the stride-stop shot, then step through the defender for a lay-up; used when the defender reaches in on the stride stop.

### Big Hop
After the fake, take a long, explosive hop forward to gather for a power lay-up or contact finish; effective against bigger, shot-blocking defenders.

### Hop Back
After the fake, hop backward (away from the basket) to create separation for a three-point shot; counters a hard-charging defender.

### Hop Back to Roll Out
Hop back, then roll out with a dribble to relocate for a different shot angle or to reset the offense.

### Hop Back to Crossover
Hop back and immediately crossover dribble to attack the opposite direction, leaving the defender stranded on the initial fake side.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Shot-faking without a step fake** → Always use the step fake to establish the pivot foot; a shot fake alone leaves the player vulnerable to a travel call if the foot moves afterward.
2. **Soft, slow fake** → The fake must be aggressive and convincing — show the ball above shooting height to get the defender airborne.
3. **Not reading the defender** → Players who pre-decide their move lose the advantage the fake creates; the fake is only valuable if the player reads the reaction and responds accordingly.
4. **Ball stays at the chest after the fake** → After the fake, rip the ball to the opposite hip to generate momentum; leaving it high makes the dribble vulnerable to a strip.
5. **Catching flat-footed** → Not catching on a two-foot jump stop eliminates pivot options; always land with both feet ready.

## Variations
### 1-Dribble Versions
Used to create a rhythm shot close to the catch point — stride stop, big hop, or hop back after just one dribble.

### 2-Dribble Versions
Extend the scoring action further from the catch point, allowing the player to probe the defense before pulling up.

### 3-Point Hop Back
Specifically hop back behind the three-point line for a rhythm three; a primary weapon for guards and wings who are catch-and-shoot threats.

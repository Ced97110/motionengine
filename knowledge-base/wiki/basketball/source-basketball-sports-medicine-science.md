# Basketball Sports Medicine and Science — Summary

## Overview
*Basketball Sports Medicine and Science* (edited by Lior Laver et al., ESSKA 2020) is a comprehensive multi-author reference text bringing together sports medicine physicians, surgeons, strength and conditioning specialists, sport psychologists, and performance scientists. The book covers the full spectrum of basketball athlete care: from biomechanics and physical demands through injury epidemiology, rehabilitation, return-to-play protocols, psychological support, and performance optimization.

The text is organized into numbered parts and chapters written by internationally recognized experts in their respective fields. It treats the basketball athlete as a complete biopsychosocial system rather than simply a collection of muscles and joints, making it uniquely valuable for coaching staff, medical teams, and performance departments working with high-level basketball programs.

## Key Themes
1. **Injury epidemiology and prevention** — position-specific injury rates, risk factor identification, and prehabilitation protocols
2. **Rehabilitation and return to play** — progressive, evidence-based pathways for ankle, knee, shoulder, and ACL injuries
3. **Sport psychology** — mental health prevalence, eight mental skills, anxiety-performance relationships, injury psychology, and gaze behavior science
4. **Legal and ethical framework** — duty of care distinctions across high school, college, and professional levels; HIPAA; informed consent
5. **Performance science** — nutrition, travel effects, load management, and body composition by position

- **Part VII** — Psychological Aspects in Basketball
  - **Ch 77** — The Importance of Sport Psychology in Basketball (LaBode-Richman & Groenewal): mental health prevalence, eight mental skills, injury psychology, biopsychosocial model (→ wiki pages: [[concept-sport-psychology-basketball]], [[concept-mental-health-injury-recovery-basketball]])
  - **Ch 78** — Sport Psychology in Basketball: Performance Under Pressure (Sighinolfi): perception behavioral control, gaze behavior, quiet eye period, anxiety and shooting percentage, coping strategies (→ wiki pages: [[concept-quiet-eye-basketball-shooting]], [[concept-basketball-anxiety-performance]])

## Unique Contributions
This source is the only text in the wiki that addresses:
- **Clinical sports medicine** applied specifically to basketball (injury classification, surgical considerations, return-to-play criteria)
- **Sport psychology as a medical discipline** — mental health screening recommendations, biopsychosocial injury model, kinesiophobia management
- **Legal duty of care** distinctions across competition levels
- **Gaze behavior science** (Quiet Eye research) applied to basketball shooting performance
- **Biofeedback technology** (HRV monitors) for basketball performance optimization

# Basic 55-Second Shooting Drill

## Objective
Simultaneously develop shooting, offensive rebounding, outlet passing, cutting, and catching-and-delivering passes in a game-realistic, competitive format where individual and group performance is tracked and penalized.

## Setup
- **Players**: 3 per group
- **Court area**: Half court (one basket)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball, stopwatch or shot clock
- **Roles**: Rebounder (R), Passer (P), Shooter (S)
- Groups compete against each other — losers receive a penalty (sprint, conditioning, etc.)

## Execution
1. Three players are assigned starting roles: R (under the basket), P (passing position), S (shooting zone).
2. Clock starts. S is in their designated shooting area.
3. P passes to S; S catches (feet and hands already ready) and shoots as quickly as possible.
4. R rebounds every shot — made or missed — and outlet passes to P.
5. P quickly delivers the pass back to S for the next shot. Sequence repeats continuously for **55 seconds**.
6. At the end of 55 seconds, a **5-second rotation** occurs — all three players quickly move to their next role:
   - R → P → S → R (rotate)
7. After **three rotations** (3 × 55 sec = 2:45 total), every player has worked all three roles.
8. Record made shots and attempts for each shooter from each area.

## Standards by Zone

### Perimeter Shots off the Pass (55 sec per shooter)
- ~13 attempts per shooter in 3 minutes
- 60% = ~7–8 made; 50% = ~6–7 made

### Perimeter Shots off Dribble After One Shot Fake (55 sec per shooter)
- ~9–10 attempts per shooter in 3 minutes
- 50% = ~4–5 made

## Coaching Points
- **Quick role transitions** are mandatory — 5-second window only; wasted time kills drill value
- Shooters work on **shot-specific footwork** — the catch-into-shot preparation is as important as the shot itself
- Passers work on game-specific pass types from positions they occupy in the offense
- Rebounders work on **proper offensive rebounding technique** and crisp outlet passes
- "Work on the shots you take in games, from the spots you take them"
- Set **individual standards** for each shooter; set **group standards** for each three-man unit
- Losing individuals and groups receive a penalty — creates real competitive pressure
- A **bubble defender** (with football bubble pads) can be added to increase physicality for inside shots

## Bubble Defender Variation
For inside-shot zones:
- Add a defender (X) wearing football bubble pads who continuously bumps the shooter.
- Rotation: R → S → X → R after each 55-second window.
- Shooter must maintain focus and shooting form despite controlled physical contact.
- Second bubble pad variant: **both non-shooters** wear pads and harass the shooter, who also chases their own offensive rebounds and immediately attempts stickback shots.
- 3 minutes → ~22–24 stickback attempts; 50% = ~11–12 made.

## Rapid Fire 55-Second Variant (Diagram 1-27)
Three players, **two basketballs**:
- Both non-shooters (P1 and P2) each have a ball and act as rebounders/passers simultaneously.
- P1 passes, S shoots; P2 immediately passes second ball to S as soon as first pass is caught.
- S must **instantly reset** (feet and hands ready) after each shot to receive the next pass.
- 3 minutes → ~22–24 attempts per shooter; 60% = ~13–14 made; 50% = ~11–12 made.
- **Coach warning**: Monitor closely — speed must not create bad habits in the shooter.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard 55-second rotation — catch and shoot only, no move required
2. **Intermediate**: Shot fake required before every shot; faster outlet pass tempo
3. **Advanced**: Rapid Fire (2 balls) or Bubble Defender — maximum realism and physicality

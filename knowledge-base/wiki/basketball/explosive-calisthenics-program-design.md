# Explosive Calisthenics Program Design

## Summary
Paul Wade's *Explosive Calisthenics* provides six named progressive program templates that guide athletes from introductory explosive work to full mastery of all six explosive movement chains (jumps, power pushups, kip-ups, front flips, back flips, and muscle-ups). The key principle throughout is that **explosive training is not an endurance sport** — reps must be crisp, fresh, and executed at maximum power. When form degrades or fatigue sets in, the session ends.

All programs require a thorough warm-up before work sets. Consolidation training (distributing mini-sessions throughout the day) is compatible with every template and is the preferred method for breaking through plateaus on specific steps. [S9]

## When to Use
- **Off-season** athletic development for basketball players wanting to improve explosive power, agility, and quickness without gym equipment
- **In-season** maintenance using lower-volume templates (Pure Power, Sacred Trinity)
- **Supplementary pre-workout** (Muscleman style): perform 5-10 min of explosive work immediately before strength training to "charge" the nervous system via neural innervation
- When an athlete is "stuck" on a specific movement step — use "25s" consolidation protocol

## Key Principles
1. **Always warm up first** — joint rolling, higher-rep heating movements, tension-flexibility stretching, 2-3 easy sets of target movement
2. **Warming up lowers neurological inhibition** — the body gets stronger as you shift through warm-up gears; this is why warm-up attempts are crucial in power sports [S9, p.1]
3. **Explosive training is not endurance** — keep reps crisp; stop when you start to tire
4. **Rule of Three / Rule of Six** — adjust sets and reps according to these internal guidelines (referenced in the book; not detailed in this chunk)
5. **Consolidation training compatibility** — even in a split routine, brief joint loosening + a few easy reps suffice before each mini-session
6. **Gain proficiency in one chain before adding the next** — reach approximately Step 6 in jumps and power pushups before adding kip-ups; add kip-ups before flips
7. **Pre-existing strength routines** — integrate explosives before body-part training to increase neural drive; go carefully as explosives are full-body and can disrupt split-routine recovery
8. **Every 6-8 weeks, change something** — the program is an approach, not scripture; mix variables (sets, reps, order, frequency) to suit life and instincts [S9, p.7]

## Program Templates

### Pure Power (Beginner)
- **Day 1:** Jump work — 3×2; Power pushups — 3×2
- **Day 2:** Off
- Repeat
- *Purpose:* Ultra-low volume intro for athletes new to explosive work. Can be done before strength or cardio sessions.

### Sacred Trinity (Intermediate)
- **Day 1:** Jump work — 6×2; Kip-up work — 5-10 min
- **Day 2:** Power pushups — 6×2; Kip-up work — 5-10 min
- **Day 3:** Off
- Repeat
- *Purpose:* Continue power training while accumulating kipping practice (6 sessions in 8 days).
- *Prerequisite:* Step 6 of both jump and power pushup chains.

### 4-Leaf Clover (Intermediate+)
- **Day 1:** Jump work — 6×2; Kip-up work — 5-10 min
- **Day 2:** Power pushups — 6×2; Front flip work — 5-10 min
- **Day 3:** Off
- Repeat
- *Purpose:* Begin front flip chain after Step 6-7 kip-up proficiency; front flip before back flip due to lower fear factor in early stages.

### "25s" (Single-Chain Mastery)
- Any single chain: 5×1 rep every 2 hours (up to 10 hours) = 25 reps/day
- Take days off as needed
- *Purpose:* Maximum focus on one chain; advance step when all 25 daily reps are in acceptable form.
- *Variation:* Alternate two chains each hour (no more than two).

### 2-Day Split (Advanced)
- **Day 1:** Jump work — 6×3; Power pushups — 6×3; Kip-up work — 5 min; Back flip work — 10 min
- **Day 2:** Off
- **Day 3:** Jump work — 6×3; Power pushups — 6×3; Front flip work — 10 min; Muscle-up work — 10 min
- **Day 4:** Off
- Repeat
- *Purpose:* Work all six chains across two focused sessions; avoids cognitive/physical overload of doing all six in one session.

### The Muscleman (Integration with Strength Training)
- Jump work (5-10 min) before legs/squat day
- Power pushup work (5-10 min) before chest/pushup day
- Kip-up work (5-10 min) before midsection/leg raises
- Front flip work (5-10 min) before shoulders/handstand pushups
- Back flip work (5-10 min) before back/bridging
- Muscle-up work (5-10 min) before lats/upper back/pullups
- *Purpose:* Neural innervation boost — explosive work increases strength during subsequent strength sets.

### Johnny Kung Fu (Advanced Frequency)
- **Day 1:** Jump work — 6×3; Kip-up work — 5 min; Back flip work — 10 min; Front flip work — 10 min
- **Day 2:** Power pushups — 6×3; Muscle-up work — 10 min
- **Day 3:** Off
- Repeat
- *Purpose:* Higher-frequency advanced program; Day 1 = jumping/agility skills, Day 2 = upper-body power. Can integrate specialist "Going Beyond" work for athletes who have reached Master Steps.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Treating explosives like endurance work** → Stop when quality drops; never grind through tired reps
2. **Skipping the warm-up** → Always warm up before explosive work, even in consolidation training
3. **Adding too many chains too fast** → Build a solid base in jumps and pushups first; add one chain at a time
4. **Following the program too rigidly** → Add rest days whenever feeling stale; the program is an approach, not scripture
5. **Explosives throwing off split-routine recovery** → Ease in gradually; skip mini-sessions when fatigued

# NBA Team Medical Coverage

## Summary
The NBA operates a comprehensive, mandated medical coverage system for all 30 teams. Prior to each season, teams must review an Emergency Action Plan (EAP) coordinated with local paramedics. The 2019-2020 season introduced formal mental health mandates. Away-game medical coverage follows a "local team coverage" paradigm, with team physicians traveling primarily for playoff and selected preseason games [S10, pp.147-148].

This infrastructure represents the gold standard for professional basketball medical care and provides a model for lower-level programs to adapt within their resource constraints.

## When to Use
- Pre-season planning for any basketball program's medical coverage
- Reference for developing Emergency Action Plans at all levels (high school, college, professional)
- Template for mental health integration into athlete support programs

## Key Principles

### Emergency Action Plan (EAP)
1. Review the EAP annually before each season in coordination with local paramedics [S10, p.147]
2. Medical transportation must be stationed in close proximity to the court with direct access to surface streets
3. Dedicated paramedics/EMTs serve players and coaches only — separate resources serve venue patrons
4. Required equipment includes: 6-lead ECG with cardiac monitoring, pulse oximetry, cardiac defibrillator, oxygen delivery, intubation equipment, IV access kits, IV fluids, selected medications, and a 7-ft spine board with cervical stabilization pads [S10, p.147]
5. Defibrillator batteries must be checked before every game and training session [S10, p.166]

### Mental Health (NBA Mandates, 2019-2020)
1. Each team must have a licensed, board-certified consulting psychiatrist working with 1-2 additional mental health clinicians (psychiatrists, psychologists, or social workers) [S10, p.147]
2. A written mental health emergency action plan must be distributed to all staff who regularly interact with players
3. The plan must specify which professional is "on call" and how to reach the consulting psychiatrist for emergency room or hospital admission [S10, p.148]
4. Services should be provided in a designated private space within the training facility
5. Integrated services should include nutrition, sleep, stress reduction, mindfulness, and physical/mental recovery modalities [S10, p.148]
6. Players must be educated on availability and confidential nature of mental health services

### Game-Day Coverage
1. Each NBA game venue is equipped with X-ray imaging capabilities [S10, p.148]
2. Team physicians routinely travel for post-season playoff games and selected neutral-site preseason games
3. For regular-season away games, medical issues requiring physician evaluation are handled by the home team's healthcare providers
4. EMT services are present at every home game

### Vaccination Requirements (Table 14.3)
- MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
- Tetanus and pertussis
- Varicella
- Hepatitis B
- Influenza [S10, p.147]

## NBA Season Structure
- **Preseason**: ~19 days, 5-6 games, including international venues
- **Regular Season**: ~177 days, 82 games (41 home, 41 away) — teams never play the same opponent at the same location in back-to-back games
- **Playoffs**: ~62 days, 16 teams, up to 4 rounds of best-of-7 series (16-28 games total)
- **Total year**: 365 days; time off ~107 days [S10, p.148]

### Injury Documentation
- Centralized Electronic Medical Record (EMR) system established in 2012 [S10, p.149]
- Accessed and updated by team physicians and athletic trainers; audited by the NBA for compliance
- Allows transfer of medical records when players are drafted, traded, or acquired
- Pre-2012 data was team-collected without standardized quality control — limits historical research quality

## Common Mistakes
1. Assuming the team physician travels to all games → at the NBA level, away-game physician coverage is handled by the home team
2. Failing to review the EAP annually → required annually and before any participation
3. Using the same mental health emergency contact for all situations → the EAP must specify who is on call for each situation

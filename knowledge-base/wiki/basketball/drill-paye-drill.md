# Paye Drill

## Objective
Teach offensive players to read every defensive stance and coverage and execute the correct attacking move — before the dribble, during the dribble, and after the dribble — in a live 1-on-1 setting.

## Setup
- **Players:** 2 per basket (attacker + coach/defender); can expand to pairs at each of six baskets
- **Court area:** Any half-court spot — corner, wing, top of key
- **Equipment:** One basketball per pair
- **Duration:** 10-second series, repeated multiple times; break into three separate sessions (before/during/after the dribble)

## Execution

### Part I: Before the Dribble (Beginner)
1. The coach/defender sets a specific stance: front-foot-to-pivot-foot, front-foot-to-free-foot, or parallel — with tight or loose coverage, with or without an overplay.
2. The attacker **identifies** the defensive stance out loud and names the correct counter move.
3. The series advances to the attacker **executing** the correct move against the identified stance.
4. The offensive-defensive exchange continues for **10 seconds**, then a new series begins.

**Attacking principles — Before the dribble:**
- **Loose coverage** → shoot immediately; do not dribble
- **Tight, no overplay** → direct drive (front-foot-to-free-foot) or crossover step (front-foot-to-pivot-foot)
- **Overplay left** → attack left with a crossover step (toward the overplay, not away from it)
- **Defender stationary and tight** → use a jab step or jab-step crossover to get the defender moving
- **Attack toward the overplay** before the dribble; attack away from the overplay during the dribble
- Jab-step direct drive and jab-step crossover are counter moves to each other

**Defensive reactions:**
- Front-foot-to-pivot-foot → defender reacts with a swing step
- Front-foot-to-free-foot → defender reacts with a swing step
- Advance step by defender → attacker treats it as a front-foot coverage and attacks it
- Retreat step by defender → attacker pulls back and shoots, or initiates a crossover/spin dribble-drive
- Swing step by defender → attacker pulls back and shoots, or uses crossover/spin dribble-drive for significant separation

### Part II: During the Dribble (Intermediate/Advanced)
1. Players advance to the full dribbling series instead of just the rocker step and initial coverage read.
2. Defender chooses: (a) slide steps to stay even with the attacker, or (b) hustle into an overplay to cut off dribble.
   - **Defender stays even** → attacker executes a step-back move for a jump shot (creates separation)
   - **Defender overplays during dribble** → attacker uses crossover dribble-drive or spin dribble-drive — **attack away from the overplay** during the dribble (opposite of before-dribble rule)
3. Designate the free-throw circle as the **moment of decision** point:
   - If defender plays straight up → jump stop, execute step-back jump shot
   - If defender overplays → spin dribble for layup, or crossover dribble for layup

**Key distinction:** Before the dribble, attack toward the overplay. During the dribble, attack away from the overplay — dribbling into an overplay causes charging fouls.

### Part III: After the Dribble (Advanced — teach after low-post moves are learned)
1. At the end of the dribble, the attacker executes a **jump stop** — preserving choice of pivot feet.
2. Two scoring options from the jump stop against an overplaying defender:
   - **Step-back jump shot:** Step back with the outside foot (Step 1), bring inside foot back to heel-toe position (Step 2), square shoulders to basket, leap for jump shot
   - **Spin move:** Plant toes of inside foot, swing outside foot 180° (Step 1), immediately spin to face basket (Step 2), end in heel-toe position, leap for jump shot
3. Third option: land on both feet, pump-fake, read defender reaction, execute appropriate move
4. **Rule:** Attacker may NOT shoot after the dribble unless separation has been created. If no separation, attacker must pass.

## Coaching Points
- Constantly correct attackers who "see" things in the defense that are not there — the read must be accurate
- Ensure defenders know all stances, steps, and slides to legitimately challenge the attacker
- The goal on offense: uncontested shot with separation
- The goal on defense: deny separation — force the attacker into a contested, off-balance attempt
- "A quality shot is one that is on balance, within the shooter's range, and taken after separation has been created" [S15, p.40]
- Separate the drill into at least three sessions: before the dribble, during the dribble, after the dribble — run each for several days before combining

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Attacker verbally identifies the defensive stance and names the correct counter move before executing
2. **Intermediate:** Attacker silently reads and executes the correct counter — 10-second live series; defensive coach adds rocker-step reactions (advance, retreat, swing steps)
3. **Advanced:** Live defender replaces the coach; players explain to each other why specific moves would have worked; name alternative options; run all three parts consecutively in one session

**Advanced options:**
- 6-basket gym: divide squad into 5 pairs, observe from midcourt; players coach each other
- After drill mastery: players name other options they could have used and explain why those options would have worked

# Shot Fake → Hop Back

## Summary
The shot fake hop back is an advanced counter-movement offensive weapon. Instead of attacking past a defender after a shot fake, the ball handler uses the dribble(s) to drive the defender forward, then explodes backward off a hop to create shooting space — often stepping behind the three-point line for an open three-pointer. This is a high-level "space creator" that exploits defenders who are conditioned to guard drives after seeing a shot fake.

The hop back with 1 dribble is typically used for a three-pointer ("Hop Back 3s"). The 2-dribble version covers more ground and can be used from farther out. [S3]

## When to Use
- Against help-defenders who close out aggressively and are on their heels
- When a defender has been conditioned to respect your drive after multiple shot fake drives
- Late in games when the three-point shot is critical
- Against mismatches where the slower defender cannot recover to your backward movement
- As a counter to full-court pressure or trapping situations where space is created by backing out

## Key Principles
1. **Sell the drive first** — the dribble direction must convincingly look like an attack drive; if the defender doesn't bite, the hop back may not create enough space.
2. **Explosive backward two-foot hop** — both feet leave and land simultaneously, moving backward far enough to clear the defender's reach (and ideally step behind the three-point line on hop-back 3s).
3. **Stay balanced** — land with knees bent, balanced for the immediate shot; don't lean backward on the shot.
4. **Quick release** — the hop back creates only a momentary window of space; shoot quickly off the gather.
5. **1-dribble version = shorter coverage** — for defenders right in front; typically a three-point shot from just behind the line.
6. **2-dribble version = more ground** — drive further to force the defender back further, then bigger hop back creates more space.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG**: Primary users — perimeter-focused three-point creators.
- **SF**: High-value in isolation wing situations.

## Variations
### 1-Dribble Hop Back 3 (Left and Right)
Fake, 1 dribble in attack direction, explosive hop backward behind the arc, shoot three. [S3, Workouts #3-13, #3-14, #3-15]

### 2-Dribble Hop Back (Left and Right)
Fake, 2 dribbles (more penetration), explosive hop backward, shoot mid-range or three depending on starting position.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Insufficient drive commitment** → Defender reads hop-back immediately if the drive isn't convincing.
2. **Landing off-balance backward** → Lean backward kills the shot; land on a balanced base.
3. **Not landing behind the arc** → For the 1-dribble Hop Back 3, make sure the hop clears the three-point line — practice the exact distance.
4. **Slow release** → The space created is momentary; hesitation allows the defender to close back out.
5. **Traveling on the gather** → Be precise with dribble-ending footwork; the hop counts as a jump stop gather, not an additional step.

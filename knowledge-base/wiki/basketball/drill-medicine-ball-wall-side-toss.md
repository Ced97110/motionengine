# Medicine-Ball Wall Side Toss

## Objective
Enhance explosive rotational mechanics and changes in direction in the upper body and core. [S8, p.159]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Solid wall
- Equipment: 1 medicine ball

## Execution
1. Begin facing the wall in an athletic position with the medicine ball held at the side.
2. Using a rotational throwing motion, toss the ball so it strikes the wall directly in front of the body.
3. Receive the rebound and immediately repeat.
4. Perform for reps or time, then switch sides.

## Coaching Points
- Drive the throw from the hips and core, not just the arms — this is a rotational power exercise.
- Stay in an athletic base throughout; do not drift off-balance during the rotation.
- The rebound catch trains reactive hand speed — stay ready.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Facing-wall stance; moderate tempo.
2. **Intermediate**: Perpendicular stance (side-on to the wall) for greater rotation range.
3. **Advanced**: Parallel stance; toss the ball so it rebounds to the other side of the body, forcing bilateral catching and throwing.

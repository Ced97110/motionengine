# Side-to-Side Dribble (Drill #21)

## Objective
Develop no-look ball control and ambidextrous dribbling touch by dribbling the basketball in front of and behind the body from hand to hand.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1 per ball (individual or full-team lines ~15 feet/4.5 m apart)
- **Court area:** Stationary or full court
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per player
- **Duration:** 2 minutes

## Execution

### Beginner (Stationary)
1. Dribble in front of the body from hand to hand without watching the dribble.
2. Dribble behind the body from hand to hand without watching the dribble.

### Intermediate (Walking)
1. Line up as in Drill 20 (4 lines at baseline).
2. Walk down the floor dribbling ball from side to side in front of the body.
3. Walk down the floor dribbling the ball from side to side behind the body.

### Advanced (Racing)
1. First line races down the floor in proper dribbling position, dribbling from side to side:
   - First trip: waist-high dribble (speed-dribbling position)
   - Second trip: low and quick (control-dribbling position)
2. Divide into teams for a dribbling race — guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Coaching Points
- Dribble using **only the first pads of the fingers**
- Feet spread **at least shoulder-width apart** — tell players to squat if it enables a more rapid dribble
- Ball can be kept **low and rapid** (control dribble) or **higher** (speed dribble) — both are valid depending on game situation
- **Do not watch the dribble** — eyes straight ahead at all times
- Distinguish between speed dribble (slightly flexed knee/torso, ball out front) and control dribble (significantly flexed, ball to the side, off-arm 90° and parallel to floor)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Stationary front and behind-the-back side-to-side without watching
2. **Intermediate:** Walking full-court front then behind
3. **Advanced:** Racing with speed vs. control-dribble height changes; competitive team format

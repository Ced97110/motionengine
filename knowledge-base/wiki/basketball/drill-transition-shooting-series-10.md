# Transition Shooting Series: 10 Consecutive Shots

## Objective
Train shooters to execute correct shooting mechanics and footwork at game speed while fatigued, simulating real transition scoring opportunities in a full-court setting. [S3, p.161]

## Setup
- 2 players: 1 shooter, 1 passer/rebounder
- Full court
- No equipment needed (cones optional for spot markers)
- Passer positions under the basket and adjusts passing angle after each rebound

## Execution

### Workout #6-1: 10 Consecutive Transition Shots (3-Point)
1. Shooter starts on the right side, spots up for a three-point shot and shoots.
2. Immediately sprints to the baseline underneath the basket.
3. Sprints out to half court on the **opposite (left) side** of the floor.
4. Turns and sprints back to the left side of the floor — rhythms up into a three-point shot.
5. After each shot: sprint to half court, then back to the next spot in the sequence:
   - Left corner / wing
   - Left lane line extended
   - Top of the key
   - Left lane line
6. After the 5th shot from the left side, sprints the right lane to the opposite end and repeats the process.
7. Shoot **10 consecutive shots without stopping**.
8. Repeat **2 times total**, with **10 free throws between repetitions**.

*Note: The passer gets the rebound and improves the passing angle for the next shot.* [S3, p.162]

### Workout #6-2: 10 Consecutive Shot Fake (SF) Shots
- Same full-court pattern as #6-1.
- Instead of spotting up for a three, shooter catches and executes a **shot fake** first.
- After the live-ball move, shooter takes advantage of any scoring opportunity available (including three-point shots, stride stop, big hop, etc.).
- Be creative and aggressive with the live-ball move — do not settle for the same shot each time.
- Repeat **2 times**, with **10 free throws between repetitions**. [S3, p.163]

### Workout #6-3: 10 Consecutive Rip Step Shots
- Same full-court pattern as #6-1.
- Shooter executes a **rip step** move upon catching the ball.
- After completing the rip step, takes advantage of any scoring opportunity (stride stop, big hop, three-point shot, etc.).
- Be creative and aggressive on the live-ball move.
- Repeat **2 times**, with **10 free throws between repetitions**. [S3, p.164]

### Workout #6-4: 10 Consecutive Combination Shots
- Same full-court pattern as #6-1.
- Shooter executes a **combination move** (e.g., jab step + shot fake, step-back + drive) upon catching.
- After completing the combination, takes advantage of any scoring opportunity.
- Repeat **2 times**, with **10 free throws between repetitions**. [S3, p.165]

## Target Volume and Standards
| Workout | Total Shots | 3PT Goal | FT Goal |
|---------|------------|----------|---------|
| #6-1 through #6-4 | 30 per rep (60 total) | 12-20 (60%) | 8-10 (80%) |

## Coaching Points
- **Rhythm is everything**: the shooter must "rhythm up" into each shot — don't arrive early and stand flat-footed waiting for the ball.
- **Sprint hard between shots**: the conditioning demand is real; if the shooter slows down, the drill loses its game-speed value.
- **Passer reads the movement**: the passer must track the shooter and lead them correctly into each spot.
- **No two consecutive shots the same**: especially in #6-2 through #6-4, vary the scoring decision to build true game versatility.
- **Free throws between reps**: use the 10-FT break to simulate pressure free throws after exhaustion.
- Coaching cue: *"Sprint, rhythm, shoot — no walking, no waiting."*

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reduce to 5 consecutive shots; allow jogging instead of full sprints.
2. **Intermediate**: 10 consecutive shots at 75% sprint effort; 3PT only (#6-1).
3. **Advanced**: 10 consecutive shots at 100% effort; rotate through all four workout variations (#6-1 through #6-4) in one session.

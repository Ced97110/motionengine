# Medicine-Ball Wall Chest Passes

## Objective
Improve total-body transmission of power through explosive medicine-ball chest passes against a wall — directly transfers to passing strength and upper-body plyometric conditioning.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Solid wall
- 1 medicine ball (4–10 lbs depending on player level)
- Stand 3–6 feet from the wall

## Execution
1. Stand facing the wall holding the medicine ball at chest height.
2. Perform an **explosive chest pass** to the wall — push the ball as hard as possible.
3. **Receive the ball with arms extended** before performing the next pass (do not let the ball pull the arms all the way back to the chest between reps).
4. Repeat for a set number of repetitions, for time, or for distance.

## Coaching Points
- The goal is total-body power transfer, not just arm strength — engage the legs and core with each throw.
- Arms extended on receipt creates a pre-stretch (SSC) that amplifies the next explosive push.
- Keep feet active — slight knee bend, athletic stance throughout.
- "Snap and catch" — wrists snap on release, hands soft on receive.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two-handed chest pass, stationary, moderate speed.
2. **Intermediate**: One-arm chest pass (each side) for unilateral development.
3. **Advanced**: Perform while moving laterally up and down the wall — adds direction change to the drill.

# Team Defensive Movement Principles (Youth)

## Summary
Defensive team movement is triggered by three events: the ball moves (via pass), the ball handler moves (via dribble), or a player's defensive assignment moves (via cut). Every defender must know which stance to play and how to respond to each trigger. This framework covers the complete man-to-man defensive decision tree for all five players on the court. [S15, p.303]

## When to Use
- In every half-court man-to-man defensive possession
- When installing man-to-man defense for the first time with a youth team
- As a daily review before any team defensive drill session

## Key Principles
1. **Know your defensive stances.** Every defender must know: front-foot-to-pivot-foot, front-foot-to-free-foot, and parallel — and when to use each based on the offensive player's pivot foot. [S15, p.303]
2. **Force opponents to dribble.** Overplay the assignment to prevent direct passes and force the offensive player to use slower dribble moves (crossover, spin) rather than direct drives.
3. **Ball side = denial always.** On the strong side (ball side), never allow a penetrating pass. Execute fence slide and deny the wing on every ball-side receiver. [S15, p.303]
4. **Help side = flat triangle + ball-you-man.** On the weak side, be two-thirds the distance from the ball and one-third the distance from your assignment. Stay one step off the line between your assignment and the ball. [S15, p.303]
5. **No middle cuts.** Force all cuts to go backdoor by jumping to the ball — always make your assignment cut backdoor. [S15, p.303]
6. **Move on the pass, not on the catch.** Defenders jump to the ball while it is in the air — not on fake passes and not after it arrives.
7. **Stop the breakaway dribbler.** When a ball handler beats their defender, the first help-side defender closes the gap; the second rotates to cover the basket. [S15, p.299]
8. **Close out on skip passes.** When the ball is skip-passed to a weak-side receiver, the help-side defender must sprint-close out — arriving as the ball arrives — overplaying the free-foot side.

## Player Responsibilities
- **On-ball defender:** Use front-foot-to-pivot-foot or front-foot-to-free-foot stance; advance step when ball is dead; retreat step on jab; swing step on crossover; tight overplay when dribble is picked up.
- **Ball-side off-ball defender (one pass away):** Fence slide; arm in passing lane, thumb down; off-arm into running lane; deny the wing; allow backdoor if overplayed.
- **Help-side off-ball defender:** Flat triangle position (2/3 from ball, 1/3 from assignment); ball-you-man; interception stance; intercept any direct (line-drive) pass; close out on lob skip pass.
- **Post defender:** Body check the flash cut across the lane; deny the flash pivot entry; front or three-quarter position on the low block.

## Variations
### Strong-Side Defense
All receivers one pass away are denied using fence slide. Force backdoor cuts as the offensive price of overplaying.

### Weak-Side Defense
Flat triangle positioning. Intercept line-drive passes. Close out on skip passes. Deny flash cuts.

### Rotation
Below the midway line of the free-throw lane = stop the ball. Above the line = cover the basket. The key defender calls: hedge, rotate, or trap. [S15, p.298-299]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Moving on the catch, not the pass** → Defender is a half-step behind on every ball movement. Fix: "Defenders move while the pass is in the air." [S15, p.307]
2. **Jumping to a fake pass** → Offensive player exploits the overreaction. Fix: Only move on a real pass that is in the air.
3. **Allowing middle cuts** → Offense gets easy layups from the middle. Fix: Jump to the ball and force all cuts backdoor.
4. **Ball-side defender playing help** → Creates open catch-and-shoot opportunities. Fix: Ball side is ALWAYS denial.
5. **Help-side defender too far from their assignment** → Cannot close out in time on skip pass. Fix: Stay 1/3 distance from the assignment at all times.

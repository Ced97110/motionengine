# San Antonio Spurs Early Offense — Corner Fill

## Overview
The San Antonio Spurs' Corner Fill is the foundational early-offense action of Gregg Popovich's high-low system, built around interchangeable twin towers (David Robinson and Tim Duncan at the 4 and 5 positions). The action attacks before the defense can organize a double-team on the dominant low post, creating a high-low two-man game with three scoring reads [S14, pp.1–4].

This system can be run **without calls** — all actions are dictated by the point guard's decisions and ball movement.

## Formation
Full-court transition formation:
- **1 (PG)**: Ball handler pushing up the floor
- **3**: Sprints to fill ball-side (right) wing
- **4 (first big down)**: Sprints to front of rim, then posts up on ball-side (right) low block
- **2**: Fills left lane / weak-side wing
- **5 (second big down)**: Trails to high-post position near top of the key

After the pass from 1 to 3, 1 cuts to the ball-side corner.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Wing Pass Entry
- 1 pushes the ball up the floor and passes ahead to 3 filling the right wing
- 4 (first big down) runs to the middle of the lane (early post-feed read from 3), then seals his defender on the ball-side block
- 1 cuts to the ball-side corner after the pass to 3
- 2 fills the left wing
- 5 trails to the high-post position near the top of the key

**Option 1 — Direct Post Feed**: 3 feeds 4 directly on the right low block for a post score [S14, p.3].

### Phase 2: High-Low Action
- If the direct post feed is unavailable, 3 passes to 5 at the high post
- 4 seals his defender and punches to the front of the rim
- 5 executes the **high-low pass** to 4 for a close-range finish

> *This is the signature action for the Spurs — David Robinson (5) feeding Tim Duncan (4) inside from the elbow* [S14, p.3].

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.1b — 3 passes to 5 at high post; 4 seals and cuts to front of rim; 5 makes high-low pass to 4. -->

### Phase 3: Ball Reversal to Weak-Side Post
- If the high-low pass is denied, 5 reverses the ball to 2 and dives hard to the **left block**
- 4 spaces out to a rebounding position off the right block
- 2 makes a **post feed to 5** on the left block
- 3 and 1 spot up at the top and wing to provide spacing and passing lanes out of a potential post double-team

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.2 — 5 reverses to 2; 5 dives to left block; 2 feeds 5; 3 and 1 spot up for spacing. -->

## Corner Fill with Dribble Entry
If the wing pass from 1 to 3 is **denied**, 1 initiates the action with a **dribble entry**:
- 1 drives the ball at 3, who slides to the corner
- 1 now occupies the wing role
- All three options (direct post feed, high-low, ball reversal) remain available with 1 in the wing role
- On the pass to the high post, the high-low pass is the first read

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.3 — 1 dribbles at 3; 3 slides to corner; 1 takes the wing; high-low to 5 is first read on pass to 4 at high post. -->

## Corner Fill with Pick-and-Roll Action
When the defense takes away post feeds, 5 creates a **corner pick-and-roll** [S14, p.4]:

1. 1 hits 2 (on the left side) and cuts to the corner. Player 5 posts up ball-side block; 4 holds high post; 3 fills left wing.
2. 3 passes to 1 in the corner.
3. **Option 1 — Back screen for 2**: 5 steps up to set a back screen for 2, who cuts off 5 looking for a lob pass from 1.
4. **Option 2 — Corner PnR**: 5 sets a second screen on the ball for 1. 4 dives to weak-side block. 3 slides to the top as a safety outlet. 1 reads: pass to 5 on the **roll** or 5 **pops** toward the baseline for a short jump shot or catch-and-drive.
5. **Option 3 — Ball reversal**: If 1 passes to 3 and no shot is available, 3 swings to 2 who has cleared to the wing, then 2 makes a post feed to 4 on the left block.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.4 — Corner PnR with 5 setting back screen then ball screen for 1; reads: roll, pop, or reversal to 4. -->

## Key Coaching Points
- **4 and 5 are interchangeable** — the system works with either big in either post spot; flip the lineup based on matchups.
- **2 and 3 are interchangeable** — either wing can be the primary ball receiver.
- **Point guard's decisions drive everything** — this system can run without calls because all actions flow from 1's choices.
- **Spacing is critical** — perimeter players who crowd the post eliminate the passing lanes needed to beat post double-teams.
- **Pop vs. roll read** — when 5 sets the corner PnR, the Spurs prefer popping Robinson toward the baseline for the short jumper and catch-and-drive. Coaches should designate their preferred option based on their 5's skill set.
- **Emphasize early feed timing** — the post player must establish position before the defense sets; 4 running hard to the front of the rim is the trigger.

## Counters
- If the defense drops a player to take away the high-low pass: 5 reverses to 2 and dives (Option 3 / Phase 3)
- If the defense fronts the low post: 5 to 4 high-low lob is the read
- If the wing pass is denied: use dribble entry variation
- If the defense switches on the corner PnR: 5 (the bigger player) seals the smaller switching defender for a post mismatch

# Show-and-Go-Opposite Drop-Step Post Move

## Summary
The show-and-go-opposite drop-step is the primary low-post scoring move when a post player's defender is shading to one side. After catching the ball (with the universal two-foot hop and chin-the-ball sequence), the post player shows the ball slightly over the defender's shoulder as bait, then drop-steps in the opposite direction using a reverse pivot. The move is completed with a power dribble, a "chicken step" to advance toward the basket, and a finish at 45 degrees to the basket [S6, pp.187-190].

The coaching cue is: *"Show and go opposite."* The move works toward both the baseline and the middle of the lane depending on which side the defender is shading [S6, p.189].

## When to Use
- When the post defender is shading or leaning to one side (high/middle or low/baseline)
- After reading the defender over the top shoulder during the catch sequence
- Both from the right and left sides of the lane
- From a stationary post-up or after a flash cut

## Key Principles
1. **Read the defender first** — Chin the ball, look over the top shoulder; if the defender is visible (on the high side), drop-step toward the baseline; if not visible (on the low/baseline side), show the ball low and drop-step toward the middle [S6, p.187].
2. **Show the ball as bait** — Raise the ball SLIGHTLY above shoulder height (still two-handed, chinning position) over the side where the defender is — just enough for the defender to see it and lean toward it; protect the ball throughout [S6, p.189].
3. **The pivot foot is the TOP foot** (for a drop-step toward the baseline) — The top foot (higher on the lane line, toward the free-throw line) is the pivot foot; the lower/baseline foot is the free foot that drop-steps [S6, pp.188-189].
4. **Contact foot determines everything** — If the defender is on the baseline (low side), the contact side is the low side; the pivot foot, free foot, and show shoulder are all opposite compared to a defender on the high side [S6, p.188].
5. **Sit on the defender's thigh during the drop-step** — As the drop-step is made, sit down on the thigh of the post defender to seal him off before lifting the pivot foot [S6, p.190].
6. **Power dribble before lifting the pivot foot** — Slam the ball down hard with both hands between the feet. The ball must leave the hands before the pivot foot is lifted [S6, p.190].
7. **Chicken step while the ball is in the air** — After the power dribble, while the ball is still bouncing up, take a quick "chicken step" to advance location toward the basket [S6, p.190].
8. **Two-foot jump stop again** — Land from the chicken step with a simultaneous two-foot jump stop — this re-establishes the choice of pivot foot for the finish [S6, p.190].
9. **Finish at 45 degrees, NOT square** — The power shot off the drop-step is the ONLY shot that should not be taken with shoulders square to the basket. The 45-degree angle protects the ball and the shot from helpside defenders rotating over [S6, p.190].

## Footwork Detail (Drop-Step Toward Middle — Defender on Baseline Side)
- Contact side = low (baseline) side = left side
- Raise ball toward left shoulder to show (bait)
- Left foot = pivot foot; left foot = free foot for the drop-step
- Drop-step toward the middle with the left (free) foot
- Finish: left-handed lay-up or baby hook, OR front pivot off inside (right) foot to square up for a turn-around jump shot, OR fake the jump shot into an up-and-under move

## Footwork Detail (Drop-Step Toward Baseline — Defender on High Side)
- Contact side = high (middle) side
- Raise ball toward right shoulder (if on right side) to show (bait)
- Top foot = pivot foot; baseline foot = free foot for the drop-step
- Drop-step toward the baseline with the lower (free) foot
- Power dribble → chicken step → two-foot jump stop → power shot at 45° off the glass

## Finishing Options After Drop-Step
| Situation | Finish |
|-----------|--------|
| Clear path to basket | Left-handed lay-up or baby hook (toward middle) |
| Helpside defender arrives | Power shot at 45° off the glass |
| Defender recovers | Front pivot off inside foot → turn-around jump shot |
| Defender jumps on turn-around | Shot fake → up-and-under move |

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF/C (post player)**: Complete universal catch routine; read the defender; show the ball; drop-step; seal with the backside/leg; power dribble before lifting pivot foot; chicken step to advance; 45° power shot.
- **Perimeter players**: Remain spaced on the perimeter after the post entry pass; be ready to receive a kick-out pass if the post player reads a double-down before executing the move.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Squaring up to the basket on the power shot** → Helpside defenders can block the shot; correction: always finish at 45° on this move — it is the ONLY exception to the square-up rule.
2. **Lifting the pivot foot before the power dribble** → Traveling violation; correction: the ball must leave the hands (power dribble completed) before the pivot foot lifts.
3. **Showing the ball too high / too far** → Defender can deflect or steal the ball; correction: show only slightly above shoulder height, both hands still cradling the ball.
4. **Not sitting on the defender's thigh** → Defender recovers to contest; correction: actively sit down and make contact with the drop-step to hold the seal.
5. **Skipping the chicken step** → Post player ends up too far from the basket; correction: always take the chicken step while the power dribble ball is in the air to advance location.

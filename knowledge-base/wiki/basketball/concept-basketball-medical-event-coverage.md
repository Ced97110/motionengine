# Medical Coverage of Basketball Events

## Summary
Effective medical coverage of a basketball event — from a local league game to an Olympic competition — requires advance venue assessment, properly equipped medical spaces, established physiotherapy areas, optimized circulation routes for medical personnel, and a fully prepared Emergency Action Plan. The complexity and resources scale with competition level, but the minimum standards remain constant: all events require adequate medical space, mandatory equipment, and direct ambulance access [S10, pp.103-104].

## When to Use
- Applied by team medical directors and event organizers before every competition
- Scales from small local facilities to newly constructed international arenas
- Blueprint review and venue walk-through should occur before the first competition at any new venue

## Key Principles
1. **Know the venue in detail before competition begins**: Study blueprints of all facility levels; attend infrastructure meetings with equipment and services departments; complete a guided tour with all medical staff [S10, p.103]
2. **Identify all medical spaces**: Some arenas have large, well-equipped medical rooms; others have minimal space — prepare accordingly
3. **Medical room must be directly accessible to EMS and to the playing court**: The layout must allow ambulances to reach the medical room AND allow rapid transport from court to medical room without obstruction
4. **Separate spectator medical room required**: At least one additional medical room for spectators, located in a different area from the team medical space
5. **Physiotherapy space near locker rooms**: At minimum, a stretcher + ice bags; ideally located adjacent to the team's locker room [S10, p.104]
6. **Practice emergency circulation routes**: All medical personnel must rehearse the EMS entry and exit routes at the court level before competition

## Venue Assessment Checklist
- Arena infrastructure characteristics
- Location and quality of medical spaces
- Physiotherapy space availability and equipment
- Circulation routes within the arena for medical personnel
- Press room, locker rooms, VIP areas (all require medical access planning)
- EMS vehicle entry and exit points
- Proximity of medical room to playing court and to EMS access

## Mandatory Medical Room Requirements
- Well lit, properly ventilated, high hygiene standards
- Direct and rapid access from the playing court
- Direct and rapid access for ambulances from outside the arena
- Includes at minimum: examination couch (2.40m × 0.60m), adjustable stool, mobile lamp, storage cabinet, ice machine, toilet with washbasin [S10, Table 9.2]

## Mandatory Equipment List (per Table 9.2) [S10, p.104]
**Facility**:
- Waiting room, toilet, ice machine, examination couch, chair, table, cabinet

**Minor Wound Management**:
- Sterile gauzes and dressings, antiseptics, gauze roller bandages, sterile cotton swabs, cellulose dressings
- Suture kit: suture thread, sterile incise drapes, surgical gloves
- Band-aids, adhesive tape, elastic support bandages
- Immobilization splints (upper and lower extremity)
- Compression splints (upper and lower extremity)
- Plaster bandages
- Local anesthetics
- Burns treatment (silver sulfadiazine cream)

**Vital Signs / Basic Assessment**:
- Stethoscope, sphygmomanometer

**Critical Care / Advanced**:
- Endotracheal cannula, laryngoscope, Mayo's tube
- Manual bag-mask resuscitator
- Plasma expanders, IV infusion kit
- Anti-allergic medication: corticosteroids
- Cardiac arrest medications: adrenaline, lidocaine 5%, atropine, bicarbonate 1 Molar
- Anti-hypertensive medication: Adalat tablets
- Bronchodilator sprays (Ventolin)
- Oxygen bottle with facemask
- **Automated external defibrillator (AED)**

## Scaling by Competition Level
| Level | Key Differences |
|---|---|
| Local | Familiar facility, established protocols, minimal modifications needed |
| National/Regional | May involve new or renovated venues; require blueprint study and guided tour |
| European/World Championships | Complex multi-arena facilities; multiple medical rooms for multiple teams and spectators; dedicated medical staff meetings with equipment/services departments |
| Olympic Games | Maximum complexity; newly built or significantly modified arenas; comprehensive staff briefings; multiple medical spaces at multiple levels |

## Common Mistakes
1. **Failing to assess a new venue before competition** → Blueprint review and walk-through must occur before the first event at any unfamiliar location
2. **Medical room not accessible to ambulances** → Both the playing court AND the ambulance entry point must be directly connected to the medical room
3. **Single medical room for athletes and spectators** → Spectator medical room must be separate and located in a different arena area
4. **Physiotherapy area without ice** → Minimum physiotherapy setup: stretcher + towels + ice bags at all times

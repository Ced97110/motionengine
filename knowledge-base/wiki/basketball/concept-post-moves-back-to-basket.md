# Post Moves — Back to the Basket (the iconic post player's System)

## Summary
an iconic post player's comprehensive system for low-post and high-post back-to-basket moves. The foundation is the drop step, which the iconic post player calls "a lost art" and the base of every back-to-basket move. Each move has specific counters based on defensive reactions. a legendary post player is cited as the greatest master of the drop step; Hakeem Olajuwon as the best at the spin. [S7, pp.42-49]

## When to Use
- When the post has received the ball with back to the basket at the low or mid post
- When the defender is behind, overplaying high or low, or recovering
- The high post back-to-basket situation (drop step and drive, spin and drive)

## Key Principles
1. **Master the drop step first** — it is the base of every back-to-basket move.
2. **Set up above the block** — at low post, set yourself above the block to make enough space to go to baseline or middle.
3. **Start the drop step while ball is in the air** — but do not release contact with the defender, or the defender can steal the ball.
4. **Being parallel to the board after the drop step** gives the advantage of shooting with rim protection, avoiding the weak-side helper.
5. **Stay low on the legs at all times** — standing straight loses power and allows the defender to push you off.
6. **Keep ball at chest level, middle of body during any spin** — prevents the defender from slapping it from behind.
7. **Turn head to watch the basket in advance** while rotating — helps locate the defender and focus eyes on the rim.

## Player Responsibilities
- **C/PF**: Read the defender's position before and during the ball flight; initiate the drop step while the ball is still in the air; execute the appropriate counter when the defense adjusts.

## Variations

### Drop Step to the Baseline
**Setup:** Ball on the wing. Post sealed on the ball side with defender on the high side. Arm bent 45° leaning on defender's chest or overplaying elbow.

**Defender on high side — shoot with one or no dribble:**
Long drop step with baseline foot, toes pointed toward baseline, heel-then-toe landing. "Hook" back foot around defender's back foot. Turn front foot to baseline, rotate body parallel to backboard. Finish with dunk or power shot.
- *With one dribble:* Dribble between the legs to protect the ball; bounce very hard on the floor; bend legs simultaneously to jump explosively. **Key:** Power shot must lean torso/arms FORWARD (not vertical) to prevent the defender from blocking from behind.

**Defender behind — fake middle, power dribble, baseline shoot:**
Ball received, hold with both hands elbows out. Turn head inside lane (check defender and cutters). Bring ball toward outside shoulder (nearest midcourt) to fake middle. When defender reacts and slides, drop step to baseline, hook defender's rear foot, point toe toward basket, power dribble with both hands, power shot or dunk.

**Defender recovers to baseline — counter moves:**
- **Jump Hook:** One step into lane with the same foot, dribble once (inside body), toes toward sideline. Jump on two feet, bring ball laterally over head, protect with forearm and shoulder, release with wrist flip. Stay LOW on legs throughout.
- **Hook:** Without dribble (or with). Forward foot toes toward basket. Jump off front foot, bring ball outside with arm nearest midcourt. Nonshooting arm bent 45° pointing toward basket lifts ball to shoulder level; shooting arm extends overhead, ball released with wrist flip — fingers end up pointing to center of basket. **Key:** Turn head and watch basket in advance.
- **Jump Shot:** If squared to basket in balanced position with enough space.
- **Up-and-Under:** Shot fake — ball up with both hands slightly above forehead, legs bent, looking at rim. As defender jumps, take a long balanced stride through and OVER the defender's body with the outside foot (nearest midcourt). Bring ball from one ear to the other (NOT down to chest — no space and loses time). Shoot under the defender's body. Turn body as slim as possible to get through quickly.

### Drop Step to the Middle
**Defender on low side — shoot with no dribble or one dribble:**
Drop step to the middle using same baseline technique. Options: dunk, power shot off glass, jump hook, hook, jump shot, up-and-under.

**Defender recovers on drop step to middle:**
Use baseline foot as pivot foot, reverse, take one dribble (in front of body to protect), go up for dunk or shot off the glass.

### Spin Move
Used when the defender stays behind and pushes with a forearm. Quick spin around to shoot immediately — preferably without a dribble.
- Most common to baseline; can also go to the middle.
- Keys: Stay low. Use foot nearest baseline or midcourt as pivot foot. Rotate on the heel. Point toe of pivot foot toward basket. Take a long step with the other leg.
- Keep ball at chest level in the middle of the body throughout the spin (prevents steal from behind).
- *Reference:* Hakeem Olajuwon was the greatest at the spin-and-go.

### High Post — Back to Basket
Two primary options due to distance from the basket:
- **Drop step and drive:** Same technique as low post. Long quick step to rim, dribble once, shoot off glass or dunk.
- **Spin and drive:** Fake one side and spin, OR spin immediately on receiving the ball. Shoot after no dribble or one hard dribble.
- **Important:** Check that the lane is free before executing — the move is so quick that there is little chance for a countermove if another defender is in the lane.

## Additional Moves
### Jump Stop
Used at high post after a drop-step, up-and-under, or spin. The post must be in the air when he picks up the dribble. When a defender is in the path, pick up the dribble with both hands, push laterally with the opposite foot, hop and land in the opposite spot in the lane. Then choose: jump shot, power shot, jump hook, shot fake, up-and-under.

### Power Slide
Requires teammates to clear the low-post area. Lean inside shoulder on defender's chest at the catch, simultaneously make a power slide dribbling hard with the hand away from the defender toward the lane (one dribble, two maximum). Force the defender back inside the lane. Finish with a contact shot under the rim. Counter if defender overplays: drop step and power shot, jump hook, or hook.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Releasing contact with the defender before the drop step is complete** → Defender can steal or deflect the ball. Maintain contact throughout.
2. **Power shot with arms vertical** → Defender blocks from behind. Lean torso and arms forward.
3. **Ball brought down to chest on up-and-under step** → No space, loses time. Keep ball from ear to ear.
4. **Ball away from body during spin** → Defender slaps it from behind. Keep at chest level in the middle of the body.
5. **Legs not bent enough on jump hook in lane** → Lose power, easy for defender to push away.

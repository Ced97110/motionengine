# Open-Court Dribble Moves (Workout #7-3)

## Objective
Develop the ability to attack a defender at full speed in the open court using a variety of dribble moves, then convert the resulting scoring opportunity. [S3, p.174]

## Setup
- 1 player (can add passer/rebounder)
- Full court
- 1 cone placed **~2 feet outside the three-point line** on the right side of the floor (at the wing/lane extended area)
- Move cone to left side, then middle, as drill progresses

## Execution
1. Player starts on the right side of the floor at **half court**.
2. **Dribbles full speed** directly at the cone.
3. Attacks the cone and executes the designated **dribble move** from the list:
   - Roll Out (alternate hands)
   - Roll Out Crossover (alternate hands)
   - Roll Out Between the Legs (alternate hands)
   - Roll Out Behind the Back (alternate hands)
4. After completing the move, converts **any available scoring opportunity** (pull-up, drive, three-pointer, stride stop, big hop, etc.).
5. Gets own rebound, sprints up the **opposite side** of the floor, and repeats the same move on that side.
6. Shoots **10 free throws** before moving to the next dribble move.
7. **Alternate hands** throughout — work both strong hand and weak hand.
8. After completing all moves from the right side, switch to the **left side**.
9. When left side is complete, move cone(s) to the **top of the key** and work the middle.

**Volume per Starting Position:**
- Roll Out × 6
- FTs × 10
- Roll Out Crossover × 6
- FTs × 10
- Roll Out Between the Legs × 6
- FTs × 10
- Roll Out Behind the Back × 6

**Total shots: 54 per starting position × 3 positions = 162 total** [S3, p.174]

## Coaching Points
- **Full speed attack**: approach the cone at game speed — slow approaches allow defenders to recover and eliminate the advantage of the move.
- **Two feet outside the line**: the cone placement simulates a realistic on-ball defender position; attacking from half court is too early.
- **Move must be clean**: do not palm the ball during roll outs or between-the-legs dribbles — clean footwork and hand placement matter.
- **Immediate shot decision**: after the move, the scorer must read the open opportunity and commit — no hesitating.
- **Free throws between sets**: simulate pressure free throws under fatigue; alternate hands on FTs as well if a player is working weak-hand development.
- Coaching cue: *"Attack the cone like it's your defender — full speed, hard move, score."*

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use only the roll out (basic change of direction); reduce to 3 reps per side.
2. **Intermediate**: Add roll out crossover and between the legs; 6 reps per side.
3. **Advanced**: Full sequence including behind the back; full 3-position workout (right, left, middle) at game speed; integrate with [[drill-racetrack-dribble]] for a complete session.

- [[creating-your-own-shot]] — attacking a defender at full speed and reading the scoring opportunity
- [[concept-combination-scoring-moves]] — the roll out variations are live-ball combination moves in transition
- [[drill-1-on-1-box-dribbling]] — the box dribble moves learned in the box are applied here at full speed

# Cooldown — Ankle Mobility

## Objective
Restore ankle range of motion after a session of jumps, landings, and hard cuts.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A wall or sturdy chair for balance
- A line on the court (free-throw line or sideline)

## Execution
1. **Ankle pumps — 20 reps per foot.** Stand tall, lift one foot, slowly point and flex the ankle through full range. Switch feet. Cue: "Reach the toes long, then pull them back to your shin — full travel both ways."
2. **Wall ankle rocks — 10 reps per side.** Stand a foot from a wall in a half-kneeling stance, front foot flat. Rock the front knee forward over the toes until it touches the wall, then back. Switch legs. Cue: "Heel glued to the floor — if it pops up, scoot the foot closer to the wall."
3. **Slow heel raises — 15 reps.** Stand tall on both feet, rise slowly onto the balls of your feet over a count of three, lower over a count of three. Cue: "Slow up, slow down — this is recovery, not conditioning."
4. **Single-leg balance — 30 seconds per foot.** Stand on one foot, the other floating just off the ground. Look at a fixed point. Cue: "Quiet feet — let the small muscles around the ankle work."
5. **Walking toe-touch — 10 steps.** Walk forward, on each step bring the back foot up to a straight-leg toe touch with the opposite hand. Cue: "Easy reach — do not force the leg up."

## Coaching Points
- Players who roll an ankle most often have the least patience for slow heel raises. They need this segment most.
- Watch the wall ankle rock for cheating: a heel that lifts is a missed rep.
- Single-leg balance exposes fatigue. Wobbling at the end of practice is a flag, not a failure.
- "Look up, breathe, settle" — for the balance segment.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hold a wall during single-leg balance.
2. **Advanced**: Close the eyes during single-leg balance for the last 10 seconds.

# Minnesota Timberwolves Hawk Combination Play

## Overview
Flip Saunders used this set to feature Kevin Garnett (3/4) in multiple roles. The play's initial action is a Garnett post-up. If denied, it evolves into a "moving Hawk screen" — a back screen for the 2 — followed by a weak-side double screen, while the back screener (3/Garnett) cuts to the wing for the primary option: a pick-and-roll with the point guard (Terrell Brandon). The play provides scoring options for all five players. [S14, pp.61-63]

## Formation (Hawk Combination)
- **1 (PG, Terrell Brandon)** at the top with the ball
- **2 (SG, Malik Sealy)** on the weak side
- **3 (SF/PF, Kevin Garnett)** on the right block
- **4 (PF, Joe Smith)** on the weak-side
- **5 (C, Dean Garrett)** on the weak-side

> **Hawk Cut Definition:** A player moving on an angle from the weak to the ball side of the floor off a stationary screen. Minnesota's version makes this a "moving Hawk" — the screener is also cutting. [S14, p.62]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Initial Post-Up Attempt
- 3 (Garnett) attempts to post up on the right block
- If he receives the ball: execute post play
- If denied inside: he cuts up the lane to set a moving Hawk back screen for 2

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.11 — Garnett post-up attempt; if denied, Garnett cuts up lane. -->

### Phase 2: Moving Hawk Screen → Double Screen → PnR
- 3 (Garnett) cuts up the lane and sets a **back screen for 2**
- 2 (Sealy) cuts off 3's back screen and continues through the lane
- 2 runs off a **weak-side double screen** set by 4 (Joe Smith) and 5 (Dean Garrett)
- After setting the back screen, 3 cuts to the **right wing** to set up a **pick-and-roll with 1 (Brandon)**
- Brandon drives hard off the Garnett screen looking for:
  1. Brandon's lane jump shot (primary)
  2. Garnett popping to the right baseline (isolation for KG — second option)
  3. 2 (Sealy) cutting off the weak-side double — additional option for 1
- 4 slices over the top of 5 after setting the double screen — another passing option for 1

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.12 — 3 back-screens for 2; 2 runs weak double (4+5); 3 wings for PnR with 1; Garnett pops baseline. -->

### Phase 3: Counter — Double Screen Continuity
- Counter: Instead of looking for his own shot off the PnR, 1 passes to 2
- 3 (PnR screener) rolls toward the basket and continues across the lane, cutting off a **second double screen** set by 4 and 5
- 2 swings the ball to 3 cutting to the post
- 4 cuts off 5 into the lane to complete the action

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.13 — 1 passes to 2; 3 rolls cross-lane off second double (4+5); 2 swings to 3 posting up; 4 cuts off 5. -->

### Traditional Hawk to PnR (Left-Side Variation)
- 2 cuts off a **stationary 4** (high post) and continues across the lane
- 2 runs off staggered screens from 3 and 5
- 4 cuts to the wing for a pick-and-roll with 1, who looks for the pass to 2
- Primary focus shifts to **4**: he continues across the lane and cuts off a **second set of staggered screens** from 3 and 5
- 2 feeds 4 cutting to the post or toward the short corner

## Key Coaching Points
- The Garnett post-up is the first read — don't skip past it if it's there
- The PnR is the key to the whole play — Brandon must drive hard off the screen to collapse the defense
- "The Terrell Brandon–Kevin Garnett pick-and-roll is the key to the play. Brandon drives hard off the screen looking to get into the lane for his jump shot" [S14, p.62]
- Sealy (2) must be a credible shooting threat to keep the double screen defenders honest
- On the counter: 1 must resist the urge to shoot and make the swing pass to 2 to start the second-double action

## Counters
- If Garnett (3) gets the ball in the post initially → execute directly
- If the PnR forces a drop coverage → Garnett pops to baseline for an isolation catch-and-shoot
- If the double screen is switched → screener who received the switch seals and calls for the ball

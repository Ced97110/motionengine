# Flex Bump Option Footwork

## Summary
The bump option is a real-time adjustment in the flex offense that allows the guard (01) to physically signal the post screener (04) to change roles. When 01 is about to use 04's flex back screen and reads that a switch is coming (or that 04's defender X4 is not respecting 04's inside position), 01 bumps one of 04's shoulders to communicate which direction 04 should reverse pivot into the lane. This keeps each player in their natural role — post players inside, perimeter players outside — while exploiting defensive switches. [S6, pp.256-260]

The shoulder bumped is a non-verbal communication system that tells 04 precisely where his defender is and which pivot direction to use.

## When to Use
- When the defense wants to switch the flex back screen cut (X4 switching to pick up 01)
- When the offense prefers to keep 04 inside and 01 outside based on individual skill sets
- When X4 is not honoring 04's inside post position and the offense wants to exploit it
- As a counter whenever the defense tries to anticipate the traditional flex action

## Key Principles
1. **Shoulder contact = direction signal**: When 01 bumps the TOP (right) shoulder of 04, the defender is on the low side — 04 reverse pivots by swinging his top (right) foot around off the left pivot foot to seal X4 and duck in. When 01 bumps the LOWER shoulder, 04 reverse pivots off the right pivot foot and swings the low (left) foot to seal and post up on the low side [S6, pp.258-259].
2. **Pivot foot is the contact foot**: In either scenario, the pivot foot is the contact foot (the foot closest to the defender). The free foot swings around to seal the defender [S6, p.259].
3. **04 reads, seals, and chases the ball**: After the bump signal, 04 ducks into the lane in the signaled direction and chases the basketball, keeping eyes on the ball and passer at all times [S6, p.259].
4. **01 replaces at the top**: After bumping 04, 01 pops back out to the top of the key and remains as a perimeter player, becoming a potential jump shooter or ball-reversal option [S6, p.258].
5. **Role assignments switch**: The bump officially switches 01's cutting assignment with 04's flare screening assignment from the standard flex action. 04 becomes the interior player; 01 becomes the perimeter player [S6, p.259].
6. **01 must fake first**: A major coaching point is to have 01 first fake an inside cut off 04's presumed screen (drawing X4 to shade inside) before bumping 04 and popping out. This gives 04 even more separation to seal X4 as he slides to the ballside mid-post [S6, p.260].
7. **Perimeter pivot off inside heel**: 01 at the top must be ready to pivot off the inside heel on any pass from 05 to become an immediate shooter [S6, p.260].

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (01 — Guard/Bump communicator)**: Read X4's position on 04 as you approach the back screen; if a switch is coming or 04's position should be exploited, bump the appropriate shoulder; fake inside cut first; pop out to the top of the key; pivot off inside heel for catch-and-shoot.
- **PF (04 — Back screener/Interior player after bump)**: Feel which shoulder 01 bumps; execute the corresponding reverse pivot (contact foot = pivot foot); seal the defender; duck into the lane and chase the ball; use proper post receiving footwork and scoring moves.
- **C (05 — Reversal passer)**: Receive the reversal and be ready to deliver to 04 in the lane, 01 at the top, or 03 on the perimeter; read all three options in sequence.

## Variations
### 01 Sets Down Screen for 04 (Traditional Bump)
More traditional bump action: 01 cuts up the lane line to receive 03's down screen and uses it. This places 01 at the new weakside high elbow area as a potential jump shooter or ball reversal player [S6, p.260].

### 01 Sets Flare Screen for 03 (Preferred Bump)
Preferred variation: instead of receiving 03's down screen, 01 sets a flare screen for 03. Either way, 01 ends up at the weakside high elbow as a shooter or reversal player [S6, p.260].

## Common Mistakes
1. **01 does not read X4's position before the bump** → The bump must be based on a real read of where X4 is guarding; bumping blindly wastes the signal.
2. **04 pivots the wrong direction** → The pivot direction is dictated by which shoulder is bumped; players must drill the signal/response pattern until it is automatic.
3. **01 skips the fake inside cut** → The inside fake is what draws X4 inward, creating the separation 04 needs to seal. Skipping it gives X4 an easier recovery path.
4. **04 does not keep eyes on ball after sealing** → The seal is only valuable if 04 simultaneously tracks the passer; ball-blind post seals lead to traveling violations or missed passes.
5. **Perimeter players not ready to shoot** → The spacing of the bump option depends on all perimeter players being immediate shooting threats; lazy positioning collapses the defense's need to guard the perimeter.

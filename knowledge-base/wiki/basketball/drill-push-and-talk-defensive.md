# Push-and-Talk Defensive Drill

## Objective
Train individual on-ball defensive footwork (all four movements) across a prescribed directional route with 6 direction changes, while simultaneously requiring defenders to communicate and avoid collisions — bridging technique and game awareness. [S6, pp.275-277]

## Setup
- **Players**: 5–10 defenders lined up at the baseline
- **Court**: Full court with cone/line markers at key points (baseline, mid-court, various sideline and lane positions — Points A through F)
- **Equipment**: Court lines serve as waypoints; no additional equipment required

## Execution
1. **X1 starts** at the baseline and runs the full directional route:
   - **Advance step** to approximately mid-court near Point A
   - **Drop-and-swing step → push-off step → slide-step** to the free throw line
   - Continue sliding as quickly and forcefully as possible to **Point D** (one side of the court)
   - **Direction change** (drop-and-swing with left foot as pivot → push-off with left foot → slide-steps) across court to **Point E**
   - **Direction change** (drop-and-swing with right foot as pivot → push-off with right foot → slide-steps) back through the center of the free throw lane
   - Push off back to the **original baseline**
2. **X2 begins** when X1 reaches the free throw line for the second time
3. **X3 begins** when X2 reaches the free throw line for the second time
4. **X4 and X5** repeat the same staggered start — result: 5 defenders executing slide-steps simultaneously on the court
5. Each defender must keep **head up and eyes open** to communicate and avoid collisions with teammates [S6, p.276]

## Coaching Points
- Alternate pivot foot on each direction change — left foot pivot going right; right foot pivot going left
- "As quickly and as hard as you can" on every slide-step segment — effort is measured
- Communication is mandatory — call out positions to avoid collisions: this mirrors game communication requirements
- Each direction change must use the full reverse pivot technique, not an open-step crossover
- Watch for the lead arm to swing back on each drop-and-swing step

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the route to learn the waypoints and foot assignments; then slow jog
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed with 30-second rest between reps; correct technique at pause points
3. **Advanced**: Full speed with 5 defenders moving simultaneously; add a ball to one defender (live dribbler variation where X1 guards an actual ball handler through the route)

# Basketball For Coaches (50 Basketball Plays) — Summary

## Overview
*50 Basketball Plays* by Coach Mac (Trevor McLean, basketballforcoaches.com, 2017) is a practical play-design ebook covering baseline out-of-bounds (BLOB), sideline out-of-bounds (SLOB), and half-court set plays against both man-to-man and 2-3 zone defenses. The book is structured as a catalog of ready-to-use plays with court diagrams, key personnel requirements, step-by-step instructions, and coaching points.

The author explicitly targets coaches at all levels, including youth coaches (whom he advises to limit themselves to 1-2 plays plus one BLOB), and emphasizes selecting plays that match your team's personnel rather than adopting plays arbitrarily. Every play category includes multiple options to keep the defense guessing while starting from the same formation.

## Key Themes
1. **Personnel-first play selection** — match play design to your best players' strengths (shooters, post players, playmakers).
2. **Formation deception** — use plays that start from the same formation and have players avoid telegraphing actions with their body language.
3. **Zone attack principles** — specific BLOB and set play strategies to exploit the 2-3 zone's structural weaknesses (forcing defenders to guard multiple threats).
4. **Youth coaching philosophy** — fewer plays are better; prioritize skill development over complex play memorization.
5. **Read-and-react options** — every play includes counters based on defensive decisions.

## Unique Contributions
This source provides a dense, immediately actionable catalog of drawn-up plays with specific personnel assignments, formation setups, sequential instructions, and defensive counters — all in a coaching-cue style. It is especially strong on BLOB design against the 2-3 zone, an area underrepresented in other sources. The youth coaching caution (limit plays, prioritize skill) is a notable philosophical contribution.

## Notable Quotes
> "If you're coaching a youth basketball team, I do not recommend implementing more than 1 or 2 plays on your team." — Coach Mac, p.2

> "Choose plays that begin in the same formation. By choosing plays that start in the same formation the defense won't be able to predict what you're going to run before you even start the play." — Coach Mac, p.2

> "Select plays that utilize your team's strengths. Don't simply flip to any page in this eBook or choose a basketball play because you think it will 'look cool'." — Coach Mac, p.3

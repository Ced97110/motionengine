# SAQ Periodization

## Summary
Periodization is the systematic, cyclical alteration of training frequency, intensity, and volume throughout the year to achieve peak athletic performance at the most important competitions. For SAQ training, periodization structures the annual program into phases — each with specific objectives — to prevent overtraining, manage DOMS risk, and peak athletes at the right time [S8, pp.3-5].

The three planning levels are: **macrocycle** (full annual plan, all phases together), **mesocycle** (a single phase lasting weeks to months), and **microcycle** (the smallest unit, typically ~1 week of training).

## When to Use
- Applied year-round — every athlete's SAQ program should be periodized
- High-intensity SAQ sessions concentrate in the **1-2 months pre-season** (2 days/week, 30-45 min)
- In-season: reduce volume but maintain intensity; use low-intensity SAQ as movement education
- Off-season: high volume, low-to-moderate intensity foundation work

## Key Principles
1. **Macrocycle = full year** — all mesocycles combined constitute the annual plan [S8, p.5]
2. **Mesocycle = a phase** — may span several weeks or months; objectives shift across phases (e.g., strength base → explosive power → competition speed) [S8, p.5]
3. **Microcycle = ~1 week** — the weekly training schedule including frequency and rest days [S8, p.5]
4. **Inverse relationship of intensity and volume** — early in program: high volume, low intensity; approaching competition: low volume, high intensity [S8, p.4]
5. **Novice progression** — start at 40-50% intensity (2×/week) → increase frequency to 2-3×/week as athlete adapts [S8, p.4]
6. **Rest days are non-negotiable** — novices need 2-3 days between sessions; experienced/advanced need at least 1-2 [S8, p.6]
7. **DOMS adaptation** — 24-72 hours of soreness is expected when new exercises are introduced; repeat bouts reduce DOMS over several weeks [S8, p.5]

## Athlete Classification for Periodization
| Level | Definition | Frequency | Intensity |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Novice | Just beginning; adolescent or adult new to sport | 2×/week | 40-80% |
| Experienced | 1-5 years training; competitive | 2-3×/week | 50-90% |
| Advanced | National/international; near genetic limits | 3×/week | 70-100% |

*[S8, p.4]*

## Common Mistakes
1. **Maintaining high volume at high intensity** → as competition approaches, drop volume and increase intensity [S8, p.4]
2. **Ignoring rest between sessions** → eccentric SAQ loading requires recovery; novices especially vulnerable to DOMS and injury [S8, pp.5-6]
3. **Misclassifying athlete level by age** → use training age, not chronological age, to determine appropriate phase [S8, p.4]
4. **Skipping the strength foundation phase** → high-intensity SAQ mesocycles require a prior 6+ month strength base [S8, p.3]

# Bridge Kip (Step 4 of Kip-Up Chain)

## Objective
Develop enough lower-body power to launch the head and upper-back off the floor using a leg-arc and palm push — the foundational movement pattern of the complete kip-up.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat open floor or mat
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Sit on the floor with knees bent and heels on the ground.
2. Roll back and swing legs up while placing hands in the **bridge position** (by ears, fingers pointing toward feet).
3. Roll onto upper-back and shoulders. Squeeze into a tight ball with bent knees close to the head — this is the spring position.
4. To gain momentum, **"unravel"** by exploding legs in an arc — UP then DOWN toward the ground — while simultaneously pushing DOWN through the palms.
5. Use momentum to lift the head and upper-back off the floor. Push through the arms to assist. As feet strike the floor, the athlete should be supported only by palms and both feet.
6. Hold this bridge position for a moment.
7. Lower the body back down slowly and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- The goal at this stage is ONLY to lift the head and upper-back — do NOT try to land on your feet yet
- "Knees close to the head" creates the tightest coil and generates the most spring
- Leg arc and palm push must happen **simultaneously**, not one then the other
- If head and shoulders don't lift: begin by just practicing the kick arc while keeping shoulders in contact — they will lift with practice

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice kicking legs in arc, striking feet on ground, without lifting head/shoulders — just feel the momentum
2. **Intermediate**: Add the palm push to get head/upper-back off the floor into a semi-bridge
3. **Advanced**: Improve bridge quality — extend arms and legs more fully until reaching a full bridge hold [S9, p.3]

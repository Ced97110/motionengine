# Triangle Drills With Commands

## Objective
Improve quickness of the foot plant and change of direction by sprinting around a triangle of cones and reacting to coach's verbal direction changes. [S8, p.192]

## Setup
- 1–2 players (athlete + coach)
- 3 cones placed in a triangle, 5 yards apart on each side
- Open court or field space

## Execution
1. Start at the designated cone and sprint as fast as possible around the triangle.
2. Stay close to the cones — tight turns are the goal.
3. After 2 to 4 turns around the triangle, the coach gives a verbal command to change direction.
4. Athlete immediately changes direction (now running the triangle in the opposite direction).
5. Continue until the drill time expires.

## Coaching Points
- Cue: "Lean into the cone — cut tight and drive out."
- Plant foot hard into the ground at each cone to redirect — do not drift wide.
- Keep the body low during cone cuts — upright posture bleeds speed.
- React to the coach's command instantly without rounding the next cone first.
- Watch for: wide arcs around cones, slow reaction to direction command, upright posture during cuts.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Sprint the triangle without a direction change command — focus on tight cone cuts.
2. **Intermediate**: Triangle sprint + coach direction change command as described.
3. **Advanced (Complex Variation — Triangle Chase Drill)**: Two athletes — one on the line between two cones, one (chaser) at the third cone. On the coach's command, both sprint the triangle; drill ends when the chaser tags the other athlete. [S8, p.192]

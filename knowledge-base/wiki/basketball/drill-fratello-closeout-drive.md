# Fratello Closeout-Drive Drill

## Objective
Train a single defender to sprint-close out and physically cut off a baseline driver using body contact (chest, not hands), then recover and repeat on the opposite wing.

## Setup
- 1 coach at mid-court with the ball
- 2 offensive players at the wing positions (one on each side)
- 1 defender starting in the middle of the three-second lane
- Half-court only; offensive players can ONLY drive baseline, not shoot

## Execution
1. Coach passes to one offensive wing player
2. The offensive player immediately drives hard to the baseline
3. The defender sprints from the lane to close out, slides into the path, and cuts off the baseline drive — **hitting the dribbler with his chest** (not pushing with hands)
4. Offensive player returns the ball to the coach (no shot)
5. Coach reverses the ball to the opposite wing player, who drives baseline
6. Defender must recover, sprint, close out, and cut off the new driver
7. Continue for prescribed reps or time

## Coaching Points
- Closeout is a SPRINT — not a jog — from the paint to the ball
- Body contact is the goal: "hit him with your chest, not your hands"
- Stay low through the closeout so you can absorb contact without fouling
- Recover in a wide defensive stance — do not stand upright while retreating
- Offensive player can only drive; this keeps the drill focused on cut-off mechanics
- "Close the gap before they can turn the corner"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Coach holds the ball; defender practices the closeout sprint path without live offense
2. **Intermediate:** Full drill as described; 2 offensive players
3. **Advanced:** Allow the offensive player to shoot if defender is late — adds a game consequence

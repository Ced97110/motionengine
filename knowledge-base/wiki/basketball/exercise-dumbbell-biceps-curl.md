# Dumbbell Biceps Curl (Basketball Strength Training)

## Objective
Build unilateral biceps brachii and brachialis strength through an alternating curl with progressive forearm supination — identifying and correcting bilateral strength imbalances while building elbow flexor strength for rebounding, ball security, and driving through contact.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: Two dumbbells
- **Players**: 1 athlete
- **Location**: Weight room

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Start in an athletic stance. Hold dumbbells by the sides of the body with a **neutral grip** (palms facing the body). Stand tall; knees slightly flexed.
2. **Flex one elbow** and move the wrist forward and toward the front of the shoulder.
3. **Gradually rotate (supinate) the dumbbell** to a palms-up position through the **top half of the movement** until the dumbbell reaches shoulder level. Keep the other arm stationary.
4. **Lower the dumbbell** by moving the hand forward, down, and away from the shoulder — keeping the elbow tight to the body. **Pronate gradually** so the dumbbell returns to a neutral position at full elbow extension.
5. Repeat with the other arm.

## Coaching Points
- The **supination (rotation)** should happen gradually through the top half of the curl — not all at once at the peak
- Elbow stays **tight to the side of the body** throughout, especially on the lowering phase
- Keep the **non-working arm stationary** while the other arm curls
- Do not swing the body to initiate the movement
- Core stays braced to prevent low-back involvement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use lighter dumbbells, slow tempo (2 up / 3 down) — master the supination pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate weight with full supination and controlled eccentric
3. **Advanced**: Incline dumbbell curl (seated on incline bench) — increases stretch at the bottom; hammer curl (neutral grip throughout) — emphasizes brachialis and brachioradialis

## Variations
- **Hammer curl**: Maintain neutral grip throughout — shifts emphasis to brachioradialis
- **Concentration curl**: Seated, elbow braced on inner thigh — complete biceps isolation
- **Incline curl**: Seated on incline bench — greater stretch at the bottom of the range
- **Cable curl**: Constant tension throughout the range

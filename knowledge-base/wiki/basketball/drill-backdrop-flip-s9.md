# Back-Drop Flip (Front Flip Chain — Step 8)

## Objective
Attempt a true no-hands 360° forward flip for the first time, embracing an incomplete rotation (landing feet-then-glutes) as the necessary stage of building rotation power toward the running front flip.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- **Maximum padding absolutely required** — thick mattress, gymnastics crash mat, or equivalent; this is the one drill where the author explicitly departs from "minimal equipment" philosophy
- Clear space (20 feet minimum)

## Execution
1. Take several steps run-up.
2. Perform a **block jump with both feet**, throwing hands DOWN (NOT to the floor — hands do NOT contact in a flip).
3. Jump hard simultaneously with: throwing hands down AND pulling the curled upper body down at the hips.
4. **Immediately pull knees to the chest** — this is the TUCK; it accelerates rotation.
5. As you rotate over (legs passing the head), begin extending the legs. (Extension brings feet to floor before back, protecting the spine.)
6. Feet contact the floor first and act as shock absorbers; then glutes and back contact. Can spread force by touching down with hands.

## Coaching Points
- **"Embrace back-drop flips as the 'missing link'"** — "For most athletes...there is gonna come a time when...you land on your ass. This is a big part of the process." [S9, p.20]
- **Hands do NOT touch the floor** — This is the critical difference from Steps 5–7; for the first time in the chain, the hands stay in the air (or come down only at landing)
- **Tuck must be immediate and deep** — "Pull your knees up to your chest" instantly after jump — delay = insufficient rotation
- **"Never land flat on your back"** — Extend legs to get feet down before the back contacts; this is why the extension phase begins even before rotation is complete [S9, p.20]
- **Padding is non-negotiable** — "Always begin this technique with maximum padding to protect your spine. Dragging a thick mattress onto the floor is a good option." [S9, p.20]
- **Progress is measurable**: Early = land mostly on glutes; Middle = land feet + fall back; Advanced = land in a semi-squat (consistently) → remove padding and advance to Step 9

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Land mostly on glutes/back — use maximum padding; feet contact first regardless of fall depth
2. **Intermediate**: Land on feet with weight forward → fall back; landing point is shifting toward feet
3. **Advanced**: Consistent semi-squat landing; weight over feet — advance to running front flip (Step 9) and remove padding

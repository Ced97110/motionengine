# Recovery Drill (#109)

## Objective
Develop loose-ball recovery instincts, competitive intensity, and 1-on-1 attack skills by racing to recover a rolling ball and immediately playing offense or defense.

## Setup
- **Players**: Two formats (see figures 1 and 2)
  - Figure 1: Two parallel lines facing mid-court; coach between lines
  - Figure 2: Numbered players arranged in mirror lines; coach calls a number
- **Court area**: Half court or full court (for transition variation)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball, optional 2 cones per line

## Execution
1. Players line up in one of two formats (two lines facing mid-court, or numbered players in symmetrical lines).
2. Coach rolls the ball down the middle of the floor, varying speed and distance on each roll.
3. **Figure 1**: The first two players in line race to recover the ball.
   **Figure 2**: Coach calls out a number; the two players with that number race to recover the ball.
4. The player who recovers the ball becomes the **attacker**; the other becomes the **defender**.
5. They play 1-on-1 back to the nearest basket; then return to the end of the line (Fig. 1) or back to their spots (Fig. 2).

## Coaching Points
- Vary the speed, angle, and distance of the roll on every repetition — unpredictability is the point
- Attackers must immediately read whether they have an advantage and attack before the defender recovers
- Defenders who lose the race must recover quickly and apply immediate pressure without fouling
- Make it competitive: keep score by guards vs. bigs, starters vs. reserves

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Basic recovery → short 1v1
2. **Intermediate**: Add two cones equidistant from each line; players must race around their cone before recovering the ball (adds reaction curve decision-making)
3. **Advanced**: Full-court transition version — after recovery, play 1v1 to the basket at the opposite end of the court

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-perimeter-one-on-one-moves]] — attacker uses any move off the recovery
- [[concept-triple-threat-position]] — attacker squares up immediately after the recovery
- [[concept-fast-break-execution]] — full-court transition variation applies fast-break principles

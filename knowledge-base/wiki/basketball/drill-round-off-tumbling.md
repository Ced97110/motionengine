# Round-Off (Tumbling Drill)

## Objective
Develop total-body agility and kinesthetic awareness through a round-off — a cartwheel variation in which the body rotates and lands with both feet simultaneously facing the starting direction. [S8, p.126]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Soft grass or open court area
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Flex laterally to the left and put your left hand on the ground.
3. Continue to turn, putting your right hand on the other side of the left hand while bringing your feet over you.
4. As your feet go over you, **rotate your body** and land with **both feet simultaneously** facing the starting position.
5. Perform the drill to **both sides**. [S8, p.126]

## Coaching Points
- The key distinction from a cartwheel: both feet land together and you face the starting direction — the quarter-turn at the top differentiates this from a standard cartwheel
- Arms fully extended in the inverted phase
- The bilateral landing must be controlled — do not collapse on impact; absorb into a slight squat
- Practice both sides; the non-dominant direction typically requires significantly more reps to equalize
- "Pop and face" — the coaching cue for the final quarter-rotation and bilateral landing

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master the cartwheel drill ([[drill-cartwheel-tumbling]]) before attempting the round-off
2. **Intermediate**: Round-off from a stationary start, both sides
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Add a sprint with a running start into the round-off
   - React to a visual cue after landing
   - Add a sport-specific skill (catch and shoot) after the landing [S8, p.126]

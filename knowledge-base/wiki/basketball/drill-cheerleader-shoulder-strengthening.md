# Cheerleader Shoulder Strengthening

## Objective
Strengthen scapular stabilizer and rotator cuff muscles through a multi-directional resistance band pattern that mimics overhead arm movements in basketball.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: resistance band
- Standing, enough space to extend arms fully in multiple directions

## Execution
1. Stand and hold one end of a resistance band in each hand, with the band held in front of the body.
2. Pinch shoulder blades together and, keeping arms straight, stretch arms out to the sides. Return to starting position.
3. Stretch arms up and diagonally toward the right shoulder. Return to starting position.
4. Stretch arms out to the sides again. Return to starting position.
5. Stretch arms up and diagonally toward the left shoulder. Return to starting position.
6. This four-direction pattern (sides → right diagonal → sides → left diagonal) constitutes the "cheerleader" pattern. Perform 3 sets of 5 repetitions of the complete pattern.
7. **Advanced:** Use a stronger resistance band.

## Coaching Points
- "Pinch shoulder blades before every rep" — scapular retraction must engage before arm movement
- Keep arms fully straight throughout; do not allow elbows to bend
- Control the return — do not snap the band back to starting position
- The diagonal directions specifically challenge the lower trapezius, a common weakness in overhead athletes

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Light resistance band, slow tempo
2. **Intermediate:** Medium resistance band
3. **Advanced:** Heavy resistance band or increase reps to 10 per pattern

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Secondary:** Biceps brachii, anterior deltoid, posterior deltoid, lateral deltoid

# Push-Push Defensive Drill

## Objective
Develop all four on-ball defensive foot movements (advance, drop-and-swing, push-off, slide) simultaneously across the entire team through a coach-simulated dribbler that every defender must react to. [S6, pp.273-275]

## Setup
- **Players**: 8–12 defenders in a mass formation spread across the court (e.g., 3 rows of 4, facing the coach)
- **Court**: Full court (two groups of 6 facing opposite coaches) or half court
- **Equipment**: Coach holds a basketball; no other equipment needed

<!-- DIAGRAM: Diagram 7-5 shows a full court with 12 defenders (X1–X12) in three rows of 4 facing a coach at each end. Zigzag movement arrows indicate all four defensive step types -->

## Execution

### Phase 1 — Rocker Step Reaction (No Live Dribble)
1. Coach stands at the front of the group, simulating an offensive ball handler with a basketball
2. Coach makes rocker-step fakes (jab step forward with either foot) — **does not dribble**
3. All defenders react with the **advance step** (when coach retreats) or **drop-and-swing step** (when coach attacks forward)
4. Coach must use both left and right foot as the rocker step foot — defenders must react to both sides
5. Coaching staff watches to verify correct pivot foot, arm swing, and balance [S6, p.275]

### Phase 2 — Live Dribble Reaction
1. Coach begins an actual dribble, simulating a ball handler attacking in various directions
2. All defenders must immediately react: **drop-and-swing** → **push-off** → **slide** to stay in front of the imaginary dribbler
3. Coach changes direction multiple times — defenders execute the complete 4-step sequence on each change
4. Quickness AND effort are emphasized — coaching staff constantly observes both [S6, p.275]

## Coaching Points
- "Physically you are here, while mentally you are there" — cue defenders to anticipate the counter change of direction after they cut off the initial drive [S6, p.273]
- Lead arm swings back simultaneously with the lead foot drop-step — arm action drives momentum
- The push-off step (not the drop-step) is the source of quickness — drive explosively off the new trail foot
- No reaching — body stays low and balanced throughout
- "Stay between the dribbler and the basket" on every slide-step

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Phase 1 only (no live dribble) — slow rocker fakes, pause and correct mechanics after each rep
2. **Intermediate**: Phase 1 + Phase 2 at moderate coach speed — correct in brief stoppages
3. **Advanced**: Phase 2 at high tempo with minimal pauses — continuous reaction with full intensity for 45–60 seconds

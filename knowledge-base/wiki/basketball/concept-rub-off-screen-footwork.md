# High-Post Rub-Off Screen Footwork

## Summary
The High-Post rub-off screen is a back screen set by the ballside high-post player (04) at the elbow area for the point guard (01) after 01 passes to the wing (02). After the pass, 01 V-cuts to set up the defender and scrapes off the inside or outside shoulder of 04's screen, cutting to the low post on the ballside block. The screener (04) has three follow-up options (pop, face, or ball-screen), making this a multi-purpose action. The source emphasizes the pivot foot mechanics for both cutter and screener and introduces the "butt screen" variant. [S6, pp.261-267]

## When to Use
- In any half-court set where the PG passes to the wing and cuts
- When the PG is a strong low-post player or when his defender (X1) cheats away from the elbow
- To create a secondary high-post threat (04) after the initial action
- Against defenses that try to switch the High-Post cut by using the High-Post bump variation

## Key Principles
1. **V-cut setup by 01**: After passing to 02, 01 V-cuts toward the middle to set up X1 for the upcoming screen. [S6, p.261]
2. **Read the defender's side and choose the shoulder**:
   - X1 does NOT jump to the ball → 01 V-cuts toward middle, rubs off the **outside (left) shoulder** of 04, looks for a quick return pass from 02. [S6, p.263]
   - X1 DOES jump to the ball → 01 V-cuts toward the ball, rubs off the **inside (right) shoulder** of 04, cuts hard to the basket; lob pass from 02 may be in order. [S6, p.263]
3. **V-cut footwork**: 01 V-cuts with either the left or right foot first, then steps toward the high-post screener (04) with the opposite foot. [S6, p.263]
4. **Screener (04) options after setting the screen**:
   - **Pop out** to the top of the key for a reversal pass.
   - **Front pivot** toward 02 and look for the high-post pass.
   - **Step out** to set an inside ball screen on 02's defender. [S6, p.264]
5. **Butt screen variant**: 04 can set the screen with his back (butt) toward the cutter (01). This requires a 180-degree front pivot off the inside foot — placing 04 at the ballside elbow, facing the basket. After 01 uses the screen, 04 makes a 90-degree front pivot off the same right foot to face 02 and become a true high-post player. [S6, p.265]
6. **Slip read**: If X4 misplays the action, 04 can slip the screen and step directly toward the basket for a quick score off 02's pass. [S6, p.264]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (01 — Cutter)**: Pass to 02; V-cut to set up X1; read X1's position; rub off correct shoulder of 04; cut to the ballside block or look for lob; if ball received, use post footwork.
- **PF (04 — Screener)**: Step up to ballside high-post elbow after 01's pass; set stationary back screen; read X4's reaction; choose pop/face/ball-screen option; in butt-screen variant, perform 180° front pivot off inside foot, then 90° pivot to face ball after screen is used.
- **SG (02 — Ball-Handler / Passer)**: After receiving 01's pass, read the options: quick return pass to 01 off the outside shoulder, lob to 01 off the inside cut, pass to 04 in the high post, or attack with the dribble.

## Variations
### High-Post Bump (Counter to Switching Defense)
If X4 cheats inside to help on X1 defending 01's High-Post cut, 01 bumps 04 to send 04 flashing to the new ballside low/mid-post area instead of cutting himself. 01 pops back to the top of the key as a perimeter player. 04 uses a reverse pivot (inside right foot as pivot, left foot swinging toward basket) to seal X4 and position in the mid-post. [S6, pp.265-267] → see [[concept-flex-bump-option]]

### Standard High-Post (No Bump)
01 uses the screen normally; 04 executes one of the three standard screener options (pop, face, ball screen). [S6, pp.261-264]

## Common Mistakes
1. **01 does not V-cut before the screen** → Without the setup V-cut, X1 can easily follow the High-Post cut; the V-cut is what pins the defender behind the screen.
2. **04 sets the screen too early or moving** → Screen must be stationary at the elbow; moving screens give X1 a path around.
3. **01 chooses the wrong shoulder** → Reading X1's position (ball-jumper vs. non-jumper) is critical; wrong shoulder means cutting into the defender rather than away.
4. **04 stands still after setting the screen** → 04 must immediately read X4 and execute one of the three follow-up options; standing still wastes the offensive advantage.
5. **In the butt-screen: 04 forgets the second (90°) pivot** → Without the follow-up pivot, 04 ends up facing the wrong direction and cannot operate as a high-post player.

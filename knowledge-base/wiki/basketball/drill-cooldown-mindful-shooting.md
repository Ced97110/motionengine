# Cooldown — Mindful Free-Throw Shooting

## Objective
End practice with controlled, focused free throws that reinforce a clean release while the heart rate is settling.

## Setup
- 2 players per basket (shooter + rebounder)
- Both free-throw lines available; players run two lines if a team is cycling through
- 1 basketball per pair

## Execution
1. **Settle in — 30 seconds.** Shooter steps to the line, takes three slow breaths, runs through the free-throw routine without a ball. Cue: "Find the same routine you would use down two with three seconds left."
2. **First 25 makes — alternating shooter and rebounder.** Each shooter takes pairs of free throws; rebounder feeds clean two-handed chest passes. Track makes, not attempts. Cue: "Slow the rhythm — this is not a time pressure drill."
3. **Mid-set reset — 30 seconds.** At 25 makes, both players step off the line, shake out the shooting arm, take three breaths. Cue: "Reset before fatigue blurs the routine."
4. **Second 25 makes — switch lines if available.** Same protocol. Total: 50 makes split across the two lines. Cue: "Same release, same finish, every shot — boring is the goal."
5. **Final shot — eyes-closed visualization, then one live attempt.** Each shooter closes their eyes, sees one make, opens their eyes, takes the shot. Cue: "See it, shoot it. Walk off whether it goes or not."

## Coaching Points
- Misses are information, not failure. A miss long means the shoulder is still firing hard — slow the release. A miss short means the wrist is dropping — finish higher.
- Watch the shooting elbow on every shot. If it drifts away from the body as practice fatigue sets in, the player is leaking force — call it out by name.
- The rebounder's job is to feed clean and stay quiet. No coaching from the rebounder.
- Keep the gym quiet during this segment. Talking during makes is a tell that the team has not bought into the focus.

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: 50 total makes as described.
2. **Advanced**: Add a "swish or pull-up" rule — any make that hits the rim costs the pair one push-up. Keeps the focus sharp on form, not on getting the ball through the net.

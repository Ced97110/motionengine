# Two-Step Drill — Post Defense (Drill 99)

## Objective
Teach the **defensive two-step** (and one-step) post coverage technique — how a defender adjusts foot position as the ball moves from one perimeter player to another, staying between the ball and the post attacker.

## Setup
- Individual or team — intermediate level
- Low block area
- **4 roles**: Player 5 (post attacker), X5 (post defender), Players 1 and 3 (perimeter passers)
- **Rotation**: 5 → X5 → 1 → 3 → end of line

## Execution

### Two-Step Technique (Defender X5):
1. **Player 5 flash pivots** to the big block area. **X5 begins above Player 5** (ball is in Player 1's hands — ball side).
2. Player 1 **passes to Player 3**. As the pass is released:
   - X5 **picks up the left foot** and places it **directly in front of 5's body**.
   - As the pass is **caught** by Player 3, X5 **picks up the right foot** and places it **slightly behind 5's body**.
   - X5's left foot should now be **half a body length above 5's body**.
3. **Player 5** tries to **put the left foot up by X5's left foot**, using the lower arm to establish a proper post-up position — a legal pushing match, no hands or arms.
4. **Start the drill with Player 5 inactive** — X5 drills on the two-step maneuver alone while Players 1 and 3 hold each pass for ~2 seconds. Then make it live.

### One-Step Technique Option:
- As the ball is passed from 1 to 3, X5 **pulls the left foot up** and places it **in front of 5's body** (shoulders now parallel to the 1-to-3 passing line).
- X5 **keeps hands down** and reaches back to feel Player 5's movement — this puts X5 in a **fronting position** instead of three-quarter.
- **When ball is in 1's hands**: play **three-quarters**. **When ball is in 3's hands**: **front**.

## Coaching Points
- The two-step is a **legal body contact battle** — neither side uses hands or arms to push.
- X5 must **react to the pass**, not the catch — the foot movement begins as the ball leaves the passer's hand.
- "Feel the post player's movement" — X5 should keep one hand low to sense 5's position.
- One-step places X5 in a **full fronting position** — requires weak-side help to cover the lob (see Drill 98).

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: X5 only — static two-step footwork drill, no live Player 5 resistance.
2. **Intermediate**: Make it live — both 5 and X5 battle for position as the ball moves.
3. **Advanced**: Add defenders X1 and X3 to prevent rapid ball movement outside.

# Small Space Training

## Summary
Small space training is a collection of explosive calisthenics drills derived from prison workout traditions — designed to build survival speed, reflexes, coordination, and spatial awareness in an area no larger than a jail cell. These drills require virtually no equipment (at most a wall) and can be performed rhythmically for high repetitions or timed sets, adding a cardio element when needed.

The key insight is that most sport speed training (sprints) builds speed over long distances (100m), which is irrelevant in close-quarters athletic situations. Basketball athletes need faster-than-eye-speed over distances of arm length to body length — the precise quality small space drills develop. Military athletes (Navy, Marines) have applied these drills in cramped conditions for generations. [S9, pp.1-2]

## When to Use
- When training space or equipment is unavailable
- As variety and freshness additions to any training session
- For developing reflexes and coordination in addition to linear speed
- As a cardio element: aim for 100 reps or 1-minute non-stop sets
- During in-season maintenance when gym access is limited

## Key Principles
1. **No equipment required** — At most a sturdy wall; nothing expensive or specialized [S9, p.1]
2. **Total-body emphasis** — Small space drills work the entire body simultaneously, not isolated muscle groups
3. **Rhythmic execution** — Most drills can be performed rhythmically, exploiting the myotatic rebound for efficiency
4. **High-rep capacity** — Aim for 100 reps or 60-second continuous sets to add cardio stimulus [S9, p.1]
5. **Short-distance speed** — Develops arm-length to body-length speed, not 100m sprint speed [S9, p.2]
6. **Grouped by primary benefit** — Drills are associated with the jump, push-up, or muscle-up chapters based on their primary physical quality, but all work the full body [S9, p.1]

## Applications for Basketball Athletes
- **Reflexes**: rapid multi-directional hand/foot speed for ball-handling and defense
- **Coordination**: whole-body spatial awareness for contact situations, rebounding fights
- **Cardio**: rhythmic high-rep sets for conditioning without court time
- **Leg speed**: drills in the jumping chapter build lower-body reaction speed
- **Arm extension speed**: drills in the push-up chapter develop passing, shot-blocking, and screening power

## Variations
### Rhythmic High-Rep Mode
Perform the drill continuously for 60 seconds or target 100 reps. Builds cardio and rhythmic coordination simultaneously.

### Timer-Based Intervals
Set a timer and perform as many reps as possible in the interval. Builds competitive intensity and tracking progress.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Treating as pure cardio** — Small space drills build power and coordination; use them with explosive intent, not just for breathing hard
2. **Pausing between reps** — Eliminates the myotatic rebound benefit and reduces training stimulus
3. **Neglecting them as "too simple"** — Military and prison athletes have used these for generations with excellent results

# A-March Walk

## Objective
Increase foot speed by building high-knee mechanics, dorsiflexion awareness, and plantar flexion power in a controlled marching tempo.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Open area, 20-40 yards
- Performed as part of a speed warm-up or mechanics progression sequence

## Execution
1. March forward using **perfect posture** and proper sprint arm action
2. On the recovery leg: bring the **knee high** and keep it **fully flexed** throughout the lift phase
3. Keep the **ankle close to the gluteus** and foot **dorsiflexed** during recovery
4. When the recovery knee reaches its highest point, the opposite (ground) foot should emphasize **plantar flexion** — pushing through the ball of the foot
5. Lower the recovery foot to the ground with control [S8, p.28]

## Coaching Points
- "Knee high, ankle dorsiflexed" — these two cues define correct A-march mechanics
- The simultaneous emphasis on plantar flexion of the ground foot trains coordination between the two legs that is critical for sprint speed
- Posture must be upright — do not lean forward or back
- Arm action mirrors proper sprint mechanics — this is a total-body coordination drill

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow march, focusing on high knee and dorsiflexion — exaggerate positions
2. **Intermediate**: Increase marching pace while maintaining form
3. **Advanced**: Progress to A-Skips (see [[drill-a-skips]]) — the same mechanics performed with a skip for added power and ground reaction [S8, pp.28-29]

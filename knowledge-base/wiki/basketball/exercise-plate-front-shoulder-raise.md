# Plate Front Shoulder Raise (Basketball Strength Training)

## Objective
Strengthen the anterior (front) deltoids to support chest-passing power, shooting through contact, and any forward overhead arm action in basketball.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: Weight plate (or dumbbells, medicine ball, barbell, or cable machine)
- **Players**: 1 athlete
- **Location**: Weight room

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Stand in an athletic stance. Hold a weight plate in front of the body with both hands, perpendicular to the arms, with wrists stiff. Flex elbows slightly but keep them stiff once movement begins. Eyes fixed straight ahead.
2. Raise the plate in **an arc in front of the body** to shoulder height. Think about moving elbows and wrists at the same time. Arms remain near full extension throughout.
3. **Contract the abdominal muscles** to maintain good posture, neutral spine, and control of the plate.
4. **Pause** when hands reach shoulder height.
5. Lower the plate back to the beginning position (top of the thighs) under control.

## Coaching Points
- Do **not lean the upper torso back** as the arms elevate
- Find a weight that can be lifted **without shrugging the shoulders**
- **Prevent rocking of the body** or rounding of the back
- Do **not fully lock the elbows**
- The plate should rise as one smooth controlled arc — no jerking

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light plate or dumbbells; wall-supported (back flat against wall) to prevent torso lean
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate weight plate with full arc, pausing at shoulder height
3. **Advanced**: Stand on an uneven surface (balance challenge); single-arm dumbbell front raise

## Variations
- **Dumbbells**: Bilateral or alternating-arm version
- **Medicine ball**: Adds grip instability
- **Barbell**: Allows heavier loading
- **Cable machine**: Provides constant tension at the bottom of the movement
- **Wall-supported**: Back flat against the wall eliminates torso cheating

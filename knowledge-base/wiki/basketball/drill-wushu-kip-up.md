# Wushu Kip-Up (Step 9 of Kip-Up Chain)

## Objective
Perform the kip-up with arms outstretched at right-angles from the body — not in the strong bridge-push position — to force the hips to generate most of the momentum and bridge toward the no-hands kip-up.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat open floor or mat
- No equipment needed
- Prerequisite: master [[drill-kip-up]] (Step 7)

## Execution
1. Sit on the floor with knees bent and heels on the ground.
2. Roll back and swing legs up while placing **arms out on the ground at right-angles from the body** (not in bridge position by ears).
3. Roll onto upper-back and shoulders. Squeeze into a tight ball with bent knees close to the head.
4. **"Unravel"** — explode legs in an arc UP then DOWN while **pushing down through the backs of the arms** (not palms in bridge position).
5. As feet touch down, keep torso and hands forward.
6. **Land on balls of both feet** into a squatting position. Keep pushing hips and chest forward until stable, then stand.
7. Squat down, roll back, and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- The key insight: "The secret to moving from regular kip-ups to hands-free kip-ups is to learn to use your arms *differently*" [S9, p.12]
- Do NOT attempt to go straight from bridge-kip arms to one arm to zero arms — the body compensates by pushing harder through the single arm
- Wushu arm position forces "much less arm power" — the correct training stimulus for progressing to no-hands
- **Regression by arm position (easiest → hardest):**
  1. Bridge position pushing through knuckles/backs of hands
  2. Partially straightened arms
  3. Arms fully extended at right-angles (hardest)
- Can be combined with straight-leg technique (Step 8) for maximum difficulty

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (regression)**: Bridge arm position, push through backs of hands (not palms)
2. **Intermediate**: Arms halfway extended, push through forearms/backs of hands
3. **Advanced**: Full wushu (arms straight at right-angles) + straight-leg style [S9, p.12]

# Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

## Objective
Isolate the hamstrings and glutes through a hip-hinge pattern to build posterior strength, protect against hamstring strains, and improve defensive positioning and cutting ability.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1
- **Location:** weight room
- **Equipment:** barbell
- **Safety:** Limit the RDL to knee height until proper technique and enhanced hamstring flexibility are achieved [S2, p.16]

## Execution
1. Stand erect holding a barbell with an overhand (palms down) grip, arms straight. Feet hip-width apart, toes pointing straight ahead or slightly turned out (up to 15°). Bar touches the thighs. Slightly flex the knees to 20–30°.
2. Keeping the low back (spine) and knees locked at this slightly flexed position, lower the bar slowly against the thighs by **extending the hips back** as if on a hinge while allowing the torso to drop forward. Do not round the back or shoulders — movement comes from the hips extending back, NOT the waist flexing forward.
3. Lower to knee height (beginner) or until a hamstring stretch is felt (advanced).
4. Return to starting position by keeping shoulders, knees, and low back locked as the hips extend forward until standing upright. [S2, pp.16-17]

## Coaching Points
- **"Push the hips back, not the waist forward"** — this is the most common technical error.
- The bar should maintain contact with the thighs throughout the movement.
- A slight knee bend (20–30°) must be maintained throughout — this protects the hamstring attachment.
- If the lower back rounds, the athlete has exceeded their current hamstring flexibility — reduce the range of motion.
- Progress depth over weeks as flexibility improves.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** RDL to knee height only, with light load to establish the hinge pattern.
2. **Intermediate:** RDL to mid-shin, full hamstring stretch, moderate load.
3. **Advanced:** Single-leg RDL to challenge balance and unilateral posterior strength.

## Concepts Taught
- [[leg-strength-for-basketball]] — builds the hamstring strength that underlies jumping and sprinting

## Basketball Application
The RDL enhances force output for running, jumping, decelerating, and cutting. It maintains proper anterior-to-posterior thigh strength ratios to prevent muscle strains and ligament injuries. Increased lower back and hip strength improves the ability to establish defensive position and react to an opponent on court. [S2, pp.16-17]

**Primary muscles:** gluteus maximus, hamstrings (semitendinosus, semimembranosus, biceps femoris), erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis)

**Secondary muscles:** rectus abdominis, transversus abdominis, iliopsoas

# Defending Off-Ball Screens

## Summary
Herb Brown's system addresses off-ball screens as the most common mechanism offenses use to create open catch-and-shoot opportunities. The universal principles are: body up to slow cutters, stand up screeners, never chase the back picker, and keep the ball-you-man triangle intact. The specific technique used varies by screen type, cutter identity (post threat vs. shooter), and game situation. [S1, pp.130-146]

## When to Use
- Any time an off-ball screen is set in your half-court defense
- Techniques apply to man-to-man defense in all game situations
- Switch-heavy approach for late-game or short-clock moments

## Key Principles
1. **Body up to the cutter** — physical contact slows his read and delays the catch
2. **Stand up screeners** — the farther from the basket you can hold up a screener, the better your coverage
3. **Never chase the back picker** — you will screen your own teammate; bump and step out instead
4. **Force cutters uphill** — away from the basket and toward sidelines; never let them go low to high freely
5. **The ball draws you** — when guarding a down screen recipient, always move toward the ball; slip inside the screener if possible
6. **Contact show by screener's defender** — gives the cutter's defender a chance to recover
7. **On-the-ball pressure** — every off-ball screen defense breaks down without it

## Specific Screen Type Coverage

### Cross Screens
- Body up to force the receiver from low to high; prevents low-post catch
- Alternative: screener's defender bumps the cutter; cutter's defender goes under and beats him to a spot above the low block
- Switch big-to-big on high cross screens
- Running-start cross screen: zone up, talk, help the screener's defender over while stopping the ball [S1, pp.130-131]

### Flex Cut
- Body up, ride, force the cutter low and out of bounds
- Stay attached to screener to prevent post-up seal after the cut
- Big-man flex cuts: aggressively switch
- Butt screen the cutter before he reaches position to force him backdoor [S1, pp.131-132]

### High-Post Cut (Vertical Post Cut)
- Post-threat cutter: body up, ride over the screen
- Non-post-threat cutter: go below screen and shortcut to eliminate further screen use on the other side [S1, p.132]

### Utah Diagonal Up Screen
- Screener's defender calls out the screen
- Cutter's defender bodies up, rides, forces over the screen and away from the basket
- Screener's defender must NOT chase the picker — position to bump, step out, hold up [S1, p.132]

### Hawk Cut
- Same as High-Post principles
- Dribbler's defender forces ball uphill so entry pass cannot reach the posting cutter [S1, pp.132-133]

### Step-Up / Open-Court Back Screens
- Trap the dribbler using the screen, OR aggressively switch and send dribbler back uphill
- Switch step-up back pick and flare with all three involved defenders in late-game situations [S1, p.133]

### Diagonal Back Screens
- Body up the cutter to take a high path
- Screener's defender bumps to stand up the cutter; don't chase the back picker [S1, p.133]

### Jam / Flare Screens
- Close distance and body up to the receiver
- Screener's defender: screen the screener or fake at the flarer to hold him up
- Watch for screener slipping and diving to the basket [S1, pp.133-134]

### Turnouts and Curls
- Hold screener up; cutter's defender locks and trails to outside shoulder and hip
- Force the cutter to curl; screener's defender contact shows
- After pass to curling player: passer's defender immediately drops to the nail
- Shoot-the-gap option: screener's defender stands up the screener, steps back, lets the defender cut in front to make the cutter fade or catch higher [S1, pp.134-135]

### Wide Diagonal Down Screens
- Shortcut to cause the receiver to fade, OR topside him and force him baseline [S1, p.135-136]

### Zipper / Straight Vertical Down Screens
- Stand up screener; stay attached; deny and force receiver away
- Or let him receive and force toward near sideline to stop ball reversal
- Never play on top of him with your back to the ball
- Key: always move toward the ball when guarding the receiver; slip inside screener if possible [S1, p.136]

### Staggered Screens (Horizontal, Vertical, Diagonal)
- Stand up screeners; bump/body cutters
- Trail on first screen; shortcut or go through the second
- If beaten, screen receiver's defender calls a switch up the line [S1, p.137]

### Horns (Elbow Area) Staggered Double
- Topside to force cutter low below both screens, or go over first and through the second
- Screeners' defenders step out to slow the cutter
- X2 locks and trails on first screen; goes through second; X4 and X5 play tandem in the lane [S1, pp.140-141]

### Loops
- Lock and trail on the outside shoulder/hip, or play inside and force cutter to the corner or sideline away from the screen [S1, p.141]

### Big-to-Big Diagonal Screen
- Body up; force receiver from low to high
- Switch equal-size defenders [S1, p.141]

### Rip / Blind (Back) Screens
- Talk and call out the screen; don't chase the back picker
- Body up and ride the cutter high away from the basket when the screen is known [S1, pp.141-142]

### Double-Doubles
- Lock and trail; screener's defenders screen the screeners to allow lock-and-trail
- May switch the second screen or shoot the gap
- On-the-ball pressure is mandatory [S1, p.146]

### Pick the Picker
- Stand up screeners; lock and trail or shoot gap on the picker receiving the screen
- May switch a weak-side perimeter man with the player defending the screener [S1, p.146]

### Fades (Off Turnout or Down Screen)
- Shoot the gap instead of locking and trailing
- Screener's defender may need to switch out to pick up the fading shooter [S1, p.139]

### Brush Screens
- Cutter's or screener's defender slows to provide help if ball carrier's defender is screened
- Weak-side nail, elbow, and box defenders must see the ball and be ready to help [S1, p.140]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Chasing the back picker** → screens your own teammate; correction: bump, step out, hold up
2. **Playing on top of down-screen receiver with back to the ball** → gives him cutting/step-up options; correction: always move toward the ball
3. **Locking and trailing on fades** → fader catches open; correction: recognize the fade early and shoot the gap
4. **Screener's defender not contact showing** → cutter's defender has no help; correction: screener's defender must show on every screen
5. **Failing to stand up screeners** → cutters can use screens at full speed; correction: make contact with the screener before he is set

# Kettlebell Swing

## Objective
Develop explosive hip extension and the triple extension movement pattern (ankle, knee, hip) that underlies running, jumping, and all Olympic lifting progressions in basketball training.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (no court markings required)
- 1 kettlebell of appropriate weight (begin light — technique first)
- **Safety**: Must be able to perform a proper squat before attempting. Consult coach and medical professional if history of low back injuries [S2, p.92]

## Execution
1. Stand upright, hold the kettlebell with both hands, arms relaxed in front of the body, kettlebell hanging between the legs. Feet are outside shoulder-width, toes turned slightly out.
2. Squat down keeping the back flat and head/eyes facing forward. Push the hips back as you descend until the kettlebell clears the groin between the legs.
3. To initiate the swing: press forearms against the groin as the kettlebell extends behind you (backswing). At the farthest point between the legs, immediately extend the hips upward and thrust them forward.
4. The hip thrust straightens the back vertically and propels the kettlebell forward in an upward arc. **Do not use the arms** to move the kettlebell — momentum comes entirely from hips and legs.
5. Fully extend the arms as the kettlebell rises to chest height.
6. Once the kettlebell reaches chest height, let it lower naturally back into its arc. Squat down slightly, keeping hips back and lower back neutral, to absorb the load.
7. Immediately repeat for the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.92–93]

## Coaching Points
- **"Arms are hooks, hips drive the bell"** — if the bell isn't rising, it's a hip problem, not an arm problem
- Watch for players rounding the lower back on the descent — flat back is non-negotiable
- The swing is a horizontal hip hinge, not a squat; hips push back, not down
- The kettlebell should float briefly at the top, not be muscled up
- Landing/receiving the backswing: brace the core, keep neutral spine

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Body-weight hip hinge practice (no kettlebell) to groove the hinge pattern, then add a light kettlebell
2. **Intermediate**: Increase kettlebell weight; focus on hip snap velocity and full triple extension at the top
3. **Advanced**: Single-arm kettlebell swing to increase core demand and shoulder stability

## Muscles Involved
**Primary**: Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, hamstrings (semitendinosus, biceps femoris, semimembranosus), quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius), gastrocnemius

**Secondary**: Anterior deltoid, multifidus, longissimus thoracis, iliocostalis, latissimus dorsi, upper trapezius [S2, p.93]

## Basketball Focus
The kettlebell swing is the **entry-level explosive exercise** for basketball players. It teaches the triple extension pattern (ankle → knee → hip) that is identical to the jump mechanics used for rebounds, shot blocks, and layups. It also develops force production into the ground — the more force pushed into the floor, the higher the jump. [S2, p.93]

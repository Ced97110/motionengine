# Pivot-and-Pass Station Drill

## Objective
Train the complete pivot-and-pass technique (jump stop → step-out → rip-through → swing-around) simultaneously with shooting footwork and on-ball defensive stance — three skills developed in one time-efficient rotating drill.

## Setup
- **Players**: Groups of 3 (scales to any multiple of 3; e.g., 12 players = 4 simultaneous groups)
- **Court area**: Half court; multiple groups can operate on the full court simultaneously — sideline hash marks, wing, and post areas
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball per group
- **From Diagram 1-50**: Multiple groups are arranged along the sideline with dribbler (01/04/07/010), defender (X2/X5/X8/X11), and shooter (03/06/09/012) in each triplet [S6, pp.81-85]

## Execution

### Station Roles
1. **Dribbler/Passer (01)**: Dribbles quickly toward the stationary defender in a semi-crouch stance, head up. Executes the four-step pivot-and-pass sequence (jump stop → step-out → rip-through → swing-around) to deliver the pass to the shooter.
2. **Defender (X2)**: Guards the dribbler/passer only — works on proper defensive stance, mirror, and ball pressure. Does NOT guard the shooter.
3. **Pass Receiver/Shooter (03)**: Receives the pass with the inside heel hitting the floor first to stop forward momentum (pivot foot established on catch). Swings the outside foot around to square up to the basket and shoots.

### Step-by-Step Execution
1. Dribbler approaches defender quickly, controlled, head up in semi-crouch.
2. Dribbler executes a two-foot jump stop (bunny hop), landing simultaneously on both feet — either foot can now become the pivot foot.
3. Dribbler attacks lateral Step-Out (east or west), holding ball beside free knee.
4. If defender counters, execute Rip-Through (front pivot, ball rips across shoe tops).
5. If defender counters again, execute Swing-Around (reverse pivot, re-attack original side or make uncontested pass).
6. Pass is delivered to the shooter — fake high/go low or fake low/go high.
7. Shooter catches the ball: inside heel hits the floor on receive (stationary option: start with heel already touching). Stops forward momentum, pivots, squares up, and shoots back to the passer.
8. After 55 seconds, rotate: Dribbler → Defender, Defender → Shooter, Shooter → Dribbler.

### Rounds
- **Round 1**: All dribblers use the **left foot** as the pivot foot.
- **Round 2**: All dribblers use the **right foot** as the pivot foot.
- Total time: ~6 minutes for 3 rotations × 2 rounds. Each player gets ~2 minutes at every station. [S6, p.85]

## Coaching Points
- **Dribbler/Passer**: "Ball beside the knee, not in the center — protect it!"
- **Dribbler/Passer**: "Step east or west — outflank the defender, don't go through them."
- **Dribbler/Passer**: "Fake high, go low. Fake low, go high."
- **Defender**: Watch for proper stance — feet active, mirroring the ball.
- **Shooter**: "Inside heel first stops your momentum — if you're floating, your heel didn't plant."
- **Shooter**: "Rise straight up, come back straight down — no drift."
- Shooters should shoot the ball back to the original passer to keep the drill rhythm high.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary dribbler — no approach; practice jump stop and step-out only, no rip-through. Shooter starts stationary.
2. **Intermediate**: Dribbler approaches at half speed; defender is passive (just provides a body). Shooter moves to receive.
3. **Advanced**: Full-speed dribble approach; defender actively contests each step; shooter uses V-cut before catching. Add a time limit for the passer — must deliver within 5 seconds or it is a turnover.

# Utah Jazz 1-4 Set — UCLA Action (Jerry Sloan)

## Overview
The Utah Jazz under Jerry Sloan used the 1-4 high set extensively, running a series of UCLA-cut-based actions featuring John Stockton (1) and Karl Malone (4). The UCLA action begins with a wing pass followed by a cut off the high post — the same initial movement that then branches into staggered screens, a back-screen post feed, or a pick-the-picker action to free either Malone or Hornacek. The dribble entry variants allow Stockton to initiate when the initial pass is denied. [S14, pp.50-55]

## Formation
- **1 (PG, Stockton)** at top of key
- **2 (SG, Hornacek)** on the right wing
- **3 (SF, B. Russell)** on the left wing
- **4 (PF, Malone)** at the right elbow
- **5 (C, Ostertag)** at the left elbow

## Phases

### Option 1: UCLA Cut to Baseline Staggered Screens
- 1 passes to 2 (Stockton to Hornacek) on the wing
- 1 makes a UCLA cut off 4 toward the right block — 4 sets a wide screen; 1 can cut inside or outside depending on his defender
- 4 pops back after 1 cuts; 2 looks to return pass to 1 for a layup, or hits 4 if X4 drops to help
- If no shot is available: **Baseline Staggered Screens** develop
  - 3 and 5 drop from the left wing and left elbow to set baseline staggered screens for 1
  - 4 looks to hit 1 cutting off the screens
  - 3 cuts to the right block after screening for 1 — if X3 helps on 1, 3 is open inside

### Option 2: UCLA Cut to Back Screen (Stockton picks for Malone)
- Same initial action: 1 hits 2, makes UCLA cut toward the right block
- Instead of cutting weak side, 1 cuts back up the lane and sets a **back screen** on 4's defender
- 4 cuts to the post; 1 pops high after setting the screen
- 2 looks to post-feed 4
- If denied: 2 hits 1 at the top for a jump shot or high-low feed to 4

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 5.9 — 1 back-screens X4; 4 cuts to post; 1 pops high; 2 feeds 4 or hits 1 at top. Fig. 5.10 — 2 hits 1 top; 1 shoots or high-lows to 4. -->

### Option 3: Dribble Entry to Back Screen
- If the 1-to-2 pass is denied, 1 dribbles at 2 on the wing (dribble handoff/chase)
- 2 cuts to the right block and up the lane to set the back screen for 4 (taking the role of 1)
- 4 cuts off 2 to the post; 2 pops high
- 1 (now holding the ball) looks to feed 4 in the post or 2 at the top

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 5.11 — 1 dribbles at 2; 2 takes the role of 1 and sets back screen for 4; 1 reads post-feed or top pass. -->

### Option 4: UCLA Set to Pick-the-Picker Action
- 1 hits 3 on the left wing and makes a UCLA cut toward the left block
- Primary option: 1 sets a back screen for 4 (Malone to left block post-up)
- 3 looks inside to 4 for the post feed OR to the top for 1, who cuts off a screen from 5
- This completes the **pick-the-picker** action (1 screens 4's man, then 5 screens 1's man)

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 5.12 — 1 UCLA-cuts, sets back screen for 4; 3 reads post-feed or top pass to 1 off 5's screen. -->

### Option 5: Dribble Entry to Pick-the-Picker
- If the 1-to-2 pass is denied (or Stockton wants the Hornacek jump shot), 1 dribbles at 2
- 2 assumes the role of 1 and sets the back screen for 4
- 2 then cuts to the top off 5 on the pick-the-picker action
- 1 has: post-feed to 4 OR pass to 2 at the top for a jump shot

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 5.13 — 1 dribbles at 2; 2 sets back screen for 4 then cuts off 5; 1 reads post or top shot. -->

## Key Coaching Points
- The UCLA cut is the engine — 4 CANNOT move, so 1 must run his defender off the screen
- Stockton earns 1-2 layups per game just off the initial UCLA cut and return pass from Hornacek
- The pick-the-picker action automatically counters any defensive stunting on the back screen
- Dribble entries preserve all the same reads — 2 simply takes the role of 1
- "Utah is especially adept at running its offense out of the UCLA set" — known for execution [S14, p.50]

## Counters
- If X4 drops to stop the post cut → 4 is open for a jump shot on the pop
- If the baseline staggered screens are switched → 3 seals whoever switches on to him and cuts back to the right block inside
- If the back screen lob is denied → 2 hits 1 at the top; 1 shoots or high-lows to 4

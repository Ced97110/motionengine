# Overhead Medicine Ball Toss

## Objective
Develop plyometric shoulder power to increase explosiveness for shooting and rebounding through repetitive overhead throwing against a wall.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: medicine ball (start with a lighter ball), solid wall
- Stand a few inches away from the wall

## Execution
1. Stand a few inches away from a wall. Hold a medicine ball overhead with arms bent to 90 degrees.
2. Toss the ball repetitively against the wall, mimicking the action of a soccer throw-in (simultaneous two-handed overhead release), for 10-second intervals.
3. Perform the prescribed number of throws.
4. **Advanced:** Use a heavier medicine ball or increase the timed intervals.

## Coaching Points
- "Mimic a soccer throw-in" — both hands release simultaneously from overhead, not a single-arm throw
- Stay close to the wall to maximize repetition rate and maintain plyometric stimulus
- Keep the core braced throughout — do not hyperextend the lower back on the release
- Progress ball weight only after form and power are consistent

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Light medicine ball, 10-second intervals, lower rep count
2. **Intermediate:** Moderate ball weight, increase interval duration
3. **Advanced:** Heavy medicine ball, extended intervals, multiple sets

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Biceps brachii, triceps brachii, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Secondary:** Anterior deltoid, middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, multifidus, erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), transversus abdominis, rectus abdominis

# Landmine Trunk Rotation — Core Rotation

## Objective
Develop core rotational strength and power by driving a barbell through a 180-degree arc — training the trunk muscles to produce and control rotational forces that occur in driving, rebounding, and defensive situations.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Empty barbell with one end wedged securely in the corner of a wall (or a dedicated landmine attachment/sleeve)
- Weight plates can be added for resistance progression

## Execution
1. Hold the free end of the barbell with arms extended in front of the body. Feet just outside shoulder-width; shoulders and hips squared off.
2. Using a rotating motion, twist the bar down and to the **right** in a smooth, semicircular pattern.
3. Once the bar reaches a complete stop at the bottom right position, reverse the motion to the left side.
4. The complete rotating motion is a **180-degree arc**. One trip across and back = one full repetition.
5. Perform **3 sets of 10-12 repetitions**. [S2, pp.44-45]

## Coaching Points
- **Control the movement** — do not use momentum or a jerky motion; the core must produce the force, not swinging arms [S2, p.45]
- The trunk is responsible for the twisting motion between the moving shoulder girdle and the stationary hip girdle [S2, p.45]
- "Smooth semicircle" — the bar path should be fluid, not choppy or rushed
- Watch for players dropping their hips into the rotation — keep them squared and stable
- Cue: "Your hips stay still; your trunk does the work"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Empty barbell only; focus on form and controlled arc
2. **Intermediate**: Add light weight plate; 3 sets × 10-12 reps
3. **Advanced — Kneeling Landmine**: Perform from a kneeling or half-kneeling position, which eliminates leg/hip muscles from the equation and isolates core rotation exclusively [S2, p.45]

### Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Rectus abdominis, transversus abdominis, internal oblique, external oblique
- **Secondary**: Gluteus medius, gluteus maximus, erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), posterior deltoid [S2, p.45]

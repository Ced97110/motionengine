# Zigzag Cone Drill

## Objective
Improve defensive footwork and lateral quickness by shuffling explosively through a line of cones in a zigzag pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface or court sideline area
- 10 cones placed in a straight line, each 1 yard (0.9 m) apart

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point athletic stance at the end of the cone line.
2. Quickly step forward and diagonally with the right foot to the right of the first cone.
3. Slide the left foot next to the right foot (lateral shuffle position).
4. Lead with the left foot to the left side of the next cone, then slide the right foot up to the left foot.
5. Continue alternating — right side, left side — through all 10 cones quickly and explosively.
6. Return to start and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- Stay in a low two-point stance throughout; do not stand up between cones
- Feet should never cross — maintain a true shuffle pattern
- Drive off the lead foot explosively; do not lazy-step
- "Quick feet, low hips" — cue players to keep knees bent on every shuffle
- Look forward, not down at the cones

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the pattern first to learn the foot sequence, then add speed.
2. **Intermediate**: Perform at full game speed; time each rep and track improvement.
3. **Advanced**: Add a sprint from the last cone to simulate transition to offense; or reduce cone spacing to 0.5 yards for tighter footwork demand.

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — lateral deceleration and re-acceleration between cones mirrors on-court defensive slides
- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — foot placement and body control during rapid direction changes

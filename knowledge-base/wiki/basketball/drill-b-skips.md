# B-Skips

## Objective
Increase stride length and stride frequency; enhance hamstring and hip performance; improve ankle-muscle stiffness at the key propulsive phase of the sprint stride.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Open area, 20-40 yards
- Performed after mastering B-March Walk; final step of the A-B form progression sequence

## Execution
1. Perform the B leg movement (recovery leg's knee blocks as high as possible) **while skipping**
2. The skip adds airborne time to allow the high-knee and extension positions to be fully realized
3. **Emphasize pawing** — sweep the extended foot down and back aggressively
4. **Drive the hips through** at each ground contact [S8, p.31]

## Coaching Points
- "Paw and drive" — the two-part cue for B-form mechanics
- "Knee blocks as high as possible" — the block is the forceful knee drive upward that creates the momentum for the extension and paw-down sequence
- Ankle stiffness at ground contact is critical — the foot should act as a rigid lever, not a shock absorber
- Upper body must stay upright; excessive forward lean indicates the athlete is rushing the drill

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Return to [[drill-b-march-walk]] if skipping disrupts form
2. **Intermediate**: B-Skips as described at controlled pace
3. **Advanced**: B-Form Runs — run while using B leg movements; directly applies the mechanics to sprint conditions [S8, p.31]

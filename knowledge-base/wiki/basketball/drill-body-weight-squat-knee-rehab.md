# Body-Weight Squat (Knee Rehabilitation)

## Objective
Restore bilateral lower-extremity strength and stability in a controlled, pain-free range of motion, establishing the baseline needed for higher-load exercises and return to basketball activity.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor space
- Optional: arms extended in front for balance, or hands on hips

## Execution
1. Stand erect with feet approximately shoulder-width apart and toes turned out slightly. Extend the arms in front or place hands on hips.
2. Slowly lower the body by extending the hips back and bending the knees until the thighs are slightly below parallel to the floor. **If pain occurs during the descent, stop just above the point of pain**.
3. Return to starting position under control.
4. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions [S2, p.145].

## Coaching Points
- "Most basketball activities, such as the jump shot, begin and end on two feet" [S2, p.145] — connect this exercise to on-court movement
- Do not shift body weight to the healthy leg — maintain equal load on both sides
- Knees should track over toes throughout the descent; watch for valgus collapse
- "Hips back and down" — not just knees forward
- After proficiency, progress to back or front squat with added weight
- Stop at the pain threshold; do not push through it

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Quarter squat (above parallel) for pain-limited patients
2. **Intermediate**: Full body-weight squat to parallel
3. **Advanced**: Below-parallel squat; then loaded squats (back squat, front squat) [S2, p.145]

# Shell Shooting (the head coach / the originating head coach Format)

## Objective
Maximize practice efficiency by running a 4-on-4 shell drill and live shooting simultaneously — no player stands idle; three teams rotate through offense, defense, and shooting.

## Setup
- 12 players split into 3 teams of 4 (plus substitutes as needed)
- One full court
- Team A: plays offense on one half
- Team B: plays defense on the same half
- Team C: goes to the opposite end for shooting (1 coach, line of shooters, 1 passer, 1 rebounder)
- Rotation: Offense → Defense → Other end (shooting) → Offense

## Execution
1. **First rotation through**: each segment lasts **6 minutes**.
2. **Second rotation through**: each segment lasts **4 minutes**.
3. Each group ends up going:
   - Twice on offense
   - Twice on defense
   - Twice at the other end for shooting
4. **Total time: 30 minutes**.

### Shell Side (Half Court)
- Standard 4-on-4 shell positioning
- Build from basic positioning; add all defensive actions needed over the course of the season
- Can incorporate PnR defense within the same possession
- Goals: take away middle, protect the 3-second lane, contest shots, block out and rebound

### Shooting Side (Other End)
- Line of shooters, 1 passer, 1 rebounder
- Shoot game-speed shots — spot-ups, off-cuts, catch-and-shoot
- Coach oversees form and shot selection

## Coaching Points
- **No dead time** — this format eliminates all waiting; every player is working every minute.
- Shooting drills on the far end should mirror shots taken out of the team's actual play sets.
- Shell side defense should mirror exactly what the team needs to defend that week.
- Rotate clockwise (Offense → Defense → Shooting) to build consistency.

## Progressions
1. **Early season**: 6-minute segments; basic shell positioning only.
2. **Mid-season**: Add PnR coverages, baseline screen defense, and post fronting to shell side.
3. **Late season**: Shorten to 4-minute segments; higher intensity; shooting drills incorporate live-ball moves.

# Clap Pull-Up (Muscle-Up Chain Step 5)

## Objective
Build explosive upper-body pulling power by releasing the bar at peak height, clapping the hands, and re-gripping — a critical reactive strength milestone on the path to the muscle-up.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Overhead horizontal bar (pull-up bar or playground bar)
- No additional equipment required

## Execution
1. Grab and hang from the overhead horizontal bar with an approximately shoulder-width grip.
2. Pull your shoulders down tight into the armpits to protect the shoulder socket.
3. Use the swing kip (hip-forward pendulum swing) to build momentum before the pull.
4. As you reach the peak of the **backswing**, thrust your hips forward while simultaneously pulling down on the bar by bending the arms and shoulders.
5. Use the combined momentum of the backswing plus the arm pull to carry yourself upward in a curved arc.
6. At the very peak of the movement, quickly lift your hands off the bar and audibly **clap them together** behind the bar.
7. Re-grip the bar and lower yourself quickly back into the swing kip position.
8. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- **Curve, not vertical**: The most efficient path is swinging up in a curve (like the kipping pull-up), not trying to get straight above the bar. When you swing up behind the bar, there is naturally plenty of room for the forearms and hands to clap.
- **Don't push away from the bar**: Just follow the natural curve — no need to drive the body away from the bar aggressively.
- **Shoulder protection**: Shoulders must be pulled down into the armpits throughout. Never hang with passive, shrugged shoulders.
- **Audible clap**: The clap should be loud — it confirms that the hands fully cleared the bar and sufficient height was achieved.
- **Re-grip timing**: Re-grip the bar before gravity pulls you fully down; lower back into the swing kip fluidly.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner regression**: Remove one hand at the top of the movement and slap the opposite forearm (one-hand release only).
2. **Intermediate**: Full two-hand clap — touch fingertips together quickly if a full clap is not yet possible [S9, p.221-222].
3. **Advanced**: Double clap or triple clap at the top of the movement.

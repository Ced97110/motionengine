# Shot Charts and Shooting Evaluation

## Summary
Knowing how to shoot correctly is only half the battle — players must **chart every workout** and **systematically evaluate their mechanics** to make measurable improvement. the shooting specialist describes this as "in many ways, the most crucial step toward evolving into a truly great shooter." A shot chart documents what was shot, where, the percentage, and coach/partner feedback. A shooting evaluation form provides a checklist to assess every mechanical element. Together, they create an accountability loop: practice → document → evaluate → adjust. [S5, pp.8-10]

Technique changes should be made in the **off-season** — in-season adjustments are difficult to implement given the volume of games, travel, and the time required for mechanical changes to become automatic. [S5, p.10]

## When to Use
- **Shot chart**: Every single practice session, year-round
- **Shooting evaluation**: When a player is struggling, during off-season skill development, or when a coach notices mechanical issues
- **Off-season**: Ideal time to make mechanical changes identified by evaluation

## Key Principles
1. **Chart every session**: Record date, time/duration, gym location, types of shots worked on, and coaching feedback from that session. [S5, p.8]
2. **Track shooting percentage**: Makes ÷ Attempts for each shot type (e.g., catch-and-shoot threes, show-and-go right/left, free throws). [S5, p.8]
3. **Record where you shot from**: Mark shooting spots on the court diagram with dots; note percentage below each spot. [S5, p.8]
4. **Use partner feedback**: Ask a coach or partner to observe technique and note what they see — e.g., "balance hand dropped too soon" or "most misses were short." [S5, p.8]
5. **Evaluate mechanics systematically**: Use the shooting evaluation form to assess every component in sequence. [S5, pp.10-13]
6. **Make changes in the off-season**: "There is very rarely a quick fix." In-season mechanics changes rarely pay off quickly. [S5, p.10]
7. **Structure every workout**: Start with one-hand form shooting → add balance hand → then game shots at game speed. "Too many players practice slower than game speed." [S5, p.10]
8. **Set goals**: Short-term and long-term shooting goals keep players motivated and provide measurable benchmarks. [S5, p.10]

## Shooting Evaluation Form — Key Checklist Areas

The full evaluation form (Figure 10.2) covers the following areas [S5, pp.11-13]:

### Grip
- Do thumbs form a T?
- Is there a "peek-through" (ball off the palms)?

### Balance Hand
- Do fingers point toward ceiling?
- Is hand on side of ball (not underneath)?
- Does thumb go back toward ear on release?
- Is ball off the palm?

### Shooting Hand
- Is ball off the palm?
- Does index finger point to eyebrow on finish?
- Are there wrinkles in the wrist (fully cocked)?

### Shooting Arm
- Is arm in an **L-shape** ("lifter"), not an S or V ("slinger" or "pusher")?
- Is elbow under the ball?
- Is elbow ahead of the ball (slinger flaw) or behind (pusher flaw)?

### Body Position and Setup
- Feet shoulder-width apart? (not too close, not too wide)
- Shoulders square?
- Both heels leave floor simultaneously?
- Both knees bend same amount simultaneously?
- Body goes straight up?
- Dominant foot forward; dominant toe pointing to target?
- Head and hips in line?
- Head stays down and level on the shot?
- Eyes watching the rim, not the ball?
- Elbow directly under the ball?

### Finish
- Elbow above the eyebrow?
- Shooting hand in the hoop?
- Balance hand up and still?
- Proper arc on the shot?

### Catching and Shooting: Right (moving left-to-right)
- Inside-foot plant?
- Toe open toward basket before planting?
- Feet squared on the shot?
- Feet land in the same spot?

### Catching and Shooting: Left (moving right-to-left)
- Inside-foot plant?
- Outside-foot reverse plant check?
- Toe open toward basket before planting?
- Feet squared on the shot?

### Shooting Off the Dribble: Right
- Dribbling with right hand?
- Inside-foot (left) plant?
- Toe open toward basket before planting?
- Picks up ball with knees flexed?
- Picks up ball with two hands?
- Feet squared on shot?

### Shooting Off the Dribble: Left
- Dribbling with left hand?
- Inside-foot (right) plant?
- (Same checklist as right, opposite foot)

## Common Mistakes
1. **Never charting workouts** → Player has no data to identify weaknesses; progress feels arbitrary and discouraging.
2. **Making mechanical changes in-season** → Without enough practice reps, changes disrupt rhythm and reduce performance; save for the off-season.
3. **Practicing without a plan** → "Before going to the gym, you need to know exactly what you will do and what you will work on." [S5, p.10]
4. **Skipping form shooting warm-up** → Jumping directly to game-speed shots without form shooting leaves the shooting motion ungrooved.
5. **Practicing only slow reps** → "Once you have the proper form and technique down, you must shoot shots at game speed." [S5, p.10]

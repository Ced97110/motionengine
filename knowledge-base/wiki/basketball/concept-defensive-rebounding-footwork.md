# Defensive Rebounding Footwork

## Summary
Every time the opponent shoots and misses, all five defenders must execute specific pivot footwork to secure defensive box-outs before pursuing the rebound. The pivot type depends strictly on whether the player is the on-ball defender (guarding the shooter) or an off-ball defender. The on-ball defender uses a **front pivot**; all four off-ball defenders use **reverse pivots**. This rule applies in man-to-man, zone, and trapping defenses. [S6, pp.282-283]

## When to Use
- On every shot by the opponent — without exception
- In man-to-man, zone, and trapping defensive schemes
- As an automatic habit, not a situational decision

## Key Principles
1. **On-ball defender (guarding the shooter) → front pivot**: X1 on the shooter (01) should front pivot — turning toward the basket — before pursuing the rebound [S6, p.282]
2. **All four off-ball defenders → reverse pivot**: X2, X3, X4, and X5 all make reverse pivots to execute the defensive box-out and seal their assigned offensive player from the rebound [S6, p.282]
3. **Universal application**: This footwork rule applies regardless of whether the defense is in man-to-man, zone, or any trapping scheme [S6, p.282]
4. **Position before ball**: The box-out (securing position via the pivot) comes before pursuing the ball — secure the body position first, then go get the rebound
5. **One-pass-away defenders** (X2, X3) and the **ballside post defender** (X4) and the **helpside defender** (X5) all have reverse pivot responsibilities [S6, p.282]

## Player Responsibilities
- **On-ball defender (any position)**: Front pivot toward the basket the moment the shot is released → box out the shooter → pursue the rebound [S6, p.282]
- **One-pass-away defenders (X2, X3)**: Reverse pivot off the foot closest to their assigned offensive player → swing the free foot to seal → box out → pursue [S6, p.282]
- **Ballside post defender (X4)**: Reverse pivot → box out the interior offensive player → pursue [S6, p.282]
- **Helpside defender (X5)**: Reverse pivot from sag position in the lane → box out the weakest-side offensive player → pursue [S6, p.282]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Off-ball defenders using a front pivot** → Turns away from their assigned opponent, who cuts behind them for an offensive rebound — correction: off-ball defenders ALWAYS reverse pivot [S6, p.282]
2. **On-ball defender using a reverse pivot** → Loses sight of the basket and the rebound trajectory — correction: on-ball defender ALWAYS front pivots toward the basket [S6, p.282]
3. **Watching the shot instead of pivoting** → Delayed box-out allows offensive player to slip into rebounding position — correction: pivot the moment the ball leaves the shooter's hands, not after it hits the rim
4. **Incomplete box-out** → Quick, passive contact without sealing — correction: swing the free foot fully around to seal the offensive player's path to the basket

# Small Space Explosive Drills

## Summary
Small space drills are a category of solo, zero-equipment speed and power exercises from Explosive Calisthenics (S9) designed to complement the main progressive calisthenics chains. Unlike the chain exercises (which are performed in low reps with full recovery and strict prerequisites), small space drills can be performed **rhythmically for higher reps** and used as warm-ups or finishing exercises in any explosive training session.

They require minimal space and no equipment, making them accessible for any setting including on-court pre-practice routines. They train muscles from angles not covered in the main chains and provide variety and conditioning volume. [S9, p.200]

## When to Use
- **Warm-up**: Before explosive calisthenics sessions or basketball practice
- **Finisher**: After main chain work to accumulate training volume
- **Ancillary work**: To address speed or power angles not hit by the main exercises
- **Any time, any place**: Zero equipment, minimal space requirement
- In-season for maintenance of athletic qualities developed in off-season

## Key Principles
1. **Rhythmic performance** — unlike chain master steps, small space drills are designed for higher-rep, rhythmic work [S9, p.200]
2. **Zero equipment** — no bars, mats, or tools needed; this is pure bodyweight athleticism
3. **Solo execution** — all drills are performed alone, making them compatible with individual player development
4. **Complementary function** — these drills supplement (not replace) the progressive chain exercises
5. **Multi-use** — the same drill can serve as a warm-up, a finisher, or a standalone conditioning piece

## Common Mistake
- Treating small space drills as the primary explosive training tool → they are supplemental; the chain progressions are the primary vehicle for building explosive capacity

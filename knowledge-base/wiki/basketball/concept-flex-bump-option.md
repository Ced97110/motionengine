# Flex Bump Option

## Summary
The bump option is a flex offense counter designed to defeat defensive switching on the flex back screen cut. Rather than having 01 cut off 04's back screen and trade positions (which a switching defense can comfortably handle), 01 physically bumps 04's shoulder as he approaches the screen. The shoulder that 01 bumps communicates to 04 which direction to reverse pivot and duck into the lane, allowing 04 to maintain his post-player role inside while 01 stays as a perimeter player — preserving each player's natural position and maximizing their individual offensive strengths. [S6, pp.257-261]

## When to Use
- Defense is switching the flex back screen cut, putting a guard on the post (04)
- Coach wants to keep post players inside and perimeter players outside
- 04 is a dominant interior scorer who should not be pulled to the perimeter
- As a scripted counter to a team that heavily switches all screens

## Key Principles
1. **01 reads the defender (X4) approach**: Just before shoulder-to-shoulder contact with 04, 01 reads whether X4 is on the high side or low side of 04. [S6, p.258]
2. **Bump the shoulder = pivot direction signal**:
   - **Bump the top (right) shoulder** → X4 is on the low side → 04 reverse pivots swinging the top (right) foot around off the left pivot foot to seal X4 and duck into the lane. [S6, p.258]
   - **Bump the lower shoulder** → X4 is on the high side → 04 reverse pivots off the right pivot foot, swinging the low (left) foot around to seal the low-side defender. [S6, p.258]
3. **04 chases the ball**: After the reverse pivot/seal, 04 ducks into the lane and moves toward the basketball (on the perimeter with either 01 or 02) in any path he chooses. [S6, p.259]
4. **01 pops back out**: After bumping, 01 replaces himself at the top of the key or the weakside high elbow — remaining a perimeter player and potential jump shooter or passer. [S6, pp.259-260]
5. **Assignment switch**: The bump officially switches 01's cutting assignment and 04's flare-screening assignment; 04 now chases the ball across the lane; 01 either uses 03's down screen (traditional) or sets a flare screen for 03 (preferred). [S6, p.259]
6. **01's catch footwork**: Whether 01 receives from 05 at the top or elbow, he must pivot off his inside heel to square up and be an immediate shooter. [S6, p.260]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (01 — Bump Reader / Perimeter)**: Read X4's position as you approach 04's back screen; bump the correct shoulder; pop back out to top or high elbow; be shot-ready to catch and shoot from 05.
- **PF (04 — Bump Receiver / Post)**: React to the shoulder bumped with the correct reverse pivot; seal defender; duck into lane and chase the ball; use post footwork on catch.
- **SF (03 — Secondary Cutter/Screener)**: In the preferred version, use 01's flare screen to cut to the weakside corner; creates spacing for 04 inside.
- **C (05 — Top Passer)**: Reverse the ball; look to 04 inside first, then 01 on the perimeter.

## Variations
### Traditional Bump (Down Screen for 01)
01 bumps 04 → 01 cuts up the lane line to receive a down screen from 03 (traditional). Places 01 at the new weakside high elbow for a jump shot or as a reversal passer. [S6, p.259]

### Preferred Bump (01 Sets Flare for 03)
01 bumps 04 → 01 sets a flare screen for 03 to use (instead of receiving a down screen). 01 then positions at the weakside high elbow. More effective because it prevents 03 from being the primary option, and forces X4 to also defend 03 on the flare. [S6, p.259]

### High-Post Bump
If X4 cheats inside on the High-Post rub-off screen anticipating the screen, 01 bumps 04 to send him flashing to the low post instead. 01 pops back to the top of the key. → see [[concept-rub-off-screen-footwork]] [S6, pp.261-263]

## Common Mistakes
1. **01 doesn't read X4 before bumping** → Bumping the wrong shoulder sends 04 into a covered space; must look at X4's side before contact.
2. **04 doesn't react to the bump immediately** → Any hesitation gives X4 time to recover; the reverse pivot must be instant on the bump.
3. **01 continues into the lane after bumping** → 01 must pop back out to the perimeter, not continue as a cutter — that defeats the entire purpose of the bump.
4. **04 uses wrong pivot foot** → Pivot foot must be the opposite of the bumped shoulder; wrong foot puts 04 sealed on the wrong side.
5. **Post player unaware of option** → 04 must know the bump code in advance; without preparation this action will not function.

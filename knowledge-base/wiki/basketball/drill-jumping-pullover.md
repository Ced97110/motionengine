# Jumping Pullover (Muscle-Up Chain Step 8)

## Objective
Learn the wrist rotation, elbow-back pull, and torso-over-bar mechanics of the bar pullover using leg assistance — bridging hip pull-up power to the full unassisted bar pullover.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Overhead horizontal bar (or a low bar)
- Stable box, chair, or base to push from (or use a low bar)
- "False grip" required: thumbs over the bar [S9, p.227]

## Execution
1. Grab an overhead horizontal bar with approximately shoulder-width grip.
2. Use a **"false grip"** — thumbs placed *over* (not under) the bar. This allows wrist rotation at the top.
3. Place one or both feet on a stable base beneath you (box, chair, etc.) or use a low bar.
4. **Bend the loaded leg/s and jump up and back** in the direction of the swing kip.
5. As you approach the peak of the jump-assisted backswing, **snap backwards with your arms**, pulling yourself toward the bar.
6. Simultaneously **clench glutes and posterior chain** to thrust your upper abs into the bar.
7. Keep **pulling back hard with the elbows** as you hurl your torso forward over the bar.
8. **Rotate your wrists** as you reach the final pull-over position.
9. Finish with: bar under upper abs, head and chest over the bar, legs tilted forward slightly, **forearms as vertical struts**. **Pause in this position.**
10. Lower under control.

## Coaching Points
- **False grip is mandatory**: Thumbs over the bar allows the necessary wrist rotation at the top. Without it, the forearms cannot rotate and the torso cannot pass over the bar [S9, p.227].
- **Relax grip at the top**: Paradoxically, a powerful initial grip must be relaxed at peak height to allow forearm rotation.
- **Train alongside hip pull-ups**: Hip pull-up power must be retained while learning pullover mechanics — train both simultaneously [S9, p.227].
- **Pause at the top**: Hold the over-bar position — bar under upper abs, forearms vertical, legs tilted forward. Initially just completing the movement without a pause is acceptable.
- **Box height for regression**: The higher the base / lower the bar, the easier the self-assist. Progress by using a lower box or a single leg.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use a low bar (pull onto bar from ground level) — maximum leg assistance.
2. **Intermediate**: Higher bar with both feet on a box; reduce the assistance by lowering the box.
3. **Advanced**: Single leg push-off from a lower box — much closer to an unassisted bar pullover [S9, p.228].

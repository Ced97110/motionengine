# Romanian Deadlift — RDL (NSCA Basketball)

## Objective
Strengthen the hamstrings, glutes, and erector spinae through a loaded hip-hinge movement — improving running speed, jumping power, deceleration ability, and reducing hamstring injury risk.

## Setup
- Barbell (or dumbbells/kettlebells for variation)
- No rack required — bar starts at hip level
- Primary muscles: gluteus maximus, semimembranosus, semitendinosus, biceps femoris, erector spinae [S11, Ch.6]

## Execution

### Beginning Position
1. Hold the barbell at **hip level** with a **pronated grip**
2. Keep chest up, shoulders back
3. Slightly flex the knees (more than a locked-out stance) and maintain this knee angle throughout

### Movement Phases
1. Lower the bar by **flexing the hips and pushing the hips back** — not by bending the knees
2. Keep the bar **in contact with the thighs** as the torso moves forward; maintain a **rigid torso and neutral back**
3. Lower until the **torso is parallel with the floor** and the barbell is aligned with the patella — or until maximum range of motion based on individual flexibility
4. Return to the beginning position by **extending the hips and standing tall** [S11, Ch.6]

**Breathing:** Inhale during the downward movement; exhale after the sticking point of the upward movement

## Coaching Points
- **"Begin with a loaded stretch on the hamstrings — perform from top down"** — feel the hamstring tension before thinking about how far to go
- **"Avoid excessive lumbar extension during all phases"** — a hyperextended lower back under load is a herniation risk
- **"Keep the bar close to the body at all times — keep shoulders over the bar"**
- The RDL is NOT a deadlift from the floor — the bar stays off the floor throughout; the movement is driven by the hips, not the knees
- Knee angle stays approximately constant throughout (slight fixed flexion)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight hip hinge against a wall to learn the push-back pattern; then add dumbbells/kettlebells for a lighter load and easier balance
2. **Intermediate**: Standard barbell RDL to maximal ROM (torso parallel or near-parallel to floor)
3. **Advanced**: Single-leg Romanian deadlift (SLRDL) — unilateral variation demanding balance, hip stability, and addressing bilateral asymmetries [S11, Ch.6]

# Chest Pull-Up (Muscle-Up Chain Step 6)

## Objective
Develop the height and pulling mechanics needed to get the chest to the bar, with forearms diagonal — a key intermediate milestone between clap pull-ups and the muscle-up's bar pullover position.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Overhead horizontal bar
- No additional equipment required

## Execution
1. Grab and hang from the overhead horizontal bar with an approximately shoulder-width grip.
2. Pull shoulders down tight into the armpits to protect the shoulder socket.
3. Build a **more powerful swing kip** than in earlier steps — the backswing should now take you higher, with your head nearing the level of the bar.
4. As soon as you are close to the peak of the backswing — when your head is nearing bar level — begin **pulling your elbows back as hard as you can**.
5. Keep pulling back until your elbows are **behind your body** and your **chest kisses the bar** (sternum touches).
6. Note that at the peak, forearms will be **diagonal**, not vertical as in regular pull-ups — this is correct form.
7. Lower yourself down quickly, back into the swing kip.

## Coaching Points
- **Direction change is the key**: Unlike earlier steps (pulling torso upward), the chest pull-up requires pulling your elbows *back* behind you — changing direction at the top rather than pulling straight up [S9, p.223].
- **Forearms diagonal at peak**: If the forearms are vertical at peak, you have not pulled the elbows back far enough.
- **Face protection**: Start conservatively — if bar contact height is miscalculated too early, the face could hit the bar.
- **Progressive bar contact height**: Begin with upper chest touching; work toward the sternum (breastbone) kissing the bar.
- **Swing kip power**: The swing kip must be more powerful at this stage than in Steps 1–4.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner regression**: Begin by touching the bar with the upper chest rather than the sternum.
2. **Intermediate**: Sternum (breastbone) to bar contact.
3. **Advanced**: Progress toward touching the bar lower and lower on the torso — upper abs → lower abs — this leads directly to the hip pull-up (Step 7) [S9, p.223].

# Youth Basketball Skill Taxonomy (Paye Skill Finder)

## Summary
The Skill Finder in *Youth Basketball Drills* provides a complete two-section taxonomy of basketball skills — Offensive and Defensive — with every skill mapped to the drill number(s) where it is taught. This is the organizational backbone of the book and serves as a rapid lookup system for coaches who identify a skill gap and need to find the right drill immediately.

The taxonomy is notable for its comprehensiveness at the youth level: it covers not only high-profile skills like shooting and dribbling but also foundational movement mechanics (advance step, swing step, fence slide) that are often neglected in youth instruction. [S15, pp.xiii–xv]

## When to Use
- When a coach identifies a specific skill gap and needs to find the teaching drill immediately
- When building a practice plan around a specific skill theme
- When cross-referencing which drills share overlapping skills to chain related sessions

## Key Principles
1. **Every skill has a home drill** — No skill is left without a specific drill that teaches it in context.
2. **Skills cluster into categories** — This allows coaches to think systemically (e.g., "all of my help-side defense") rather than drill by drill.
3. **Drill numbers in parentheses are authoritative** — When a skill appears in multiple drills, each drill teaches it from a different angle or complexity level.

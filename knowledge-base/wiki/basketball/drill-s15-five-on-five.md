# Five on Five (Youth Full-Team Scrimmage)

## Objective
Integrate all learned offensive (motion offense) and defensive (team rules) skills in a live 5-on-5 format with scalable rules matched to player ability level.

## Setup
- **Players**: 10 (5 vs. 5)
- **Court area**: Full court or half court
- **Equipment**: None
- Change formation daily
- Review motion offense rules and defensive team rules before beginning
- **Limit rules to match talent level** — beginners use 3 rules each side; advanced use full sets

## Execution
1. Players line up in a chosen formation (change daily).
2. Coach reviews the motion offense rules and defensive team rules in effect for this session.
3. Offense and defense switch roles after each score.

### Options (Competitive Variations)
1. **Stop count**: Defense must stop the offense a set number of consecutive possessions before roles switch.
2. **10 possessions**: Give offense 10 possessions; count scores. Rotate and repeat. Team that scores most wins.
3. **Limited scoring method**: Designate only specific techniques (e.g., cutting, dribbling, or screening on the ball). No other scoring methods allowed.
4. **Designated scorer**: Only one player may score. Forces the team to screen hard and create for that player rather than play one-on-one.
5. **Paint only**: The only legal shots are within 5 feet (1.5 m) of the basket. Forces pass-penetration, dribble-penetration, and rebounding emphasis.

## Coaching Points
- "Related Drills: All" — this drill synthesizes everything learned in the book
- Scale the rule set: beginners with 3 rules each side, advanced with full motion and full defensive rules
- Use the "designated scorer" option when players aren't setting screens properly — it immediately reveals the value of screening
- Use the "paint only" option to cure over-reliance on perimeter shooting

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 3 motion offense rules + 3 defensive rules, offense/defense switch each score
2. **Intermediate**: Full rule sets, stop-count format (defense earns 2–3 consecutive stops before switching)
3. **Advanced**: 10-possession competition with score tracking; limited scoring method variations

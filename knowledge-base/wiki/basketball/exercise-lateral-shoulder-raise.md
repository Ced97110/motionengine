# Lateral Shoulder Raise (Basketball Strength Training)

## Objective
Isolate and strengthen the deltoids — particularly the medial (middle) head — to support overhead actions in basketball: rebounding, shot-blocking, shooting over defenders, and passing.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: Two dumbbells (or kettlebells, or cable machine)
- **Players**: 1 athlete
- **Location**: Weight room

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Stand in an athletic stance. Hold two dumbbells in front of the thighs with a neutral, closed grip. Flex elbows slightly but keep them stiff. Eyes fixed straight ahead.
2. Raise the dumbbells **up and away from the body** until the arms are approximately parallel to the floor. The **elbows and wrists should move up together at the same time** — ahead of the forearms, hands, and dumbbells.
3. Keep the head neutral throughout.
4. Pause at the top.
5. Control the eccentric movement — lower the dumbbells back to the beginning position.
6. Maintain a slightly flexed elbow position throughout the entire exercise.

## Coaching Points
- **"Move the elbows and wrists at the same time"** — do not let one lead the other
- Do **not let the torso or head fall forward**
- Control the weight — do not let it swing (no momentum)
- Find a weight that can be raised without shrugging the shoulders

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light dumbbells, strict form, seated position if needed
2. **Intermediate**: Standing with moderate load; add a 1-2 second hold at the top
3. **Advanced**: Cable machine for constant tension throughout the range; kettlebell variation (changes weight distribution)

## Variations
- **Kettlebell**: Changes where the weight is positioned — shifts the center of mass
- **Seated**: Reduces lower-body involvement; good for learning the movement
- **Cable machine**: Provides constant tension throughout the range of motion

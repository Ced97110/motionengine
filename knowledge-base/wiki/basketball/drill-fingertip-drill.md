# Fingertip Drill (Drill #18)

## Objective
Develop fingertip touch with the basketball — teaching players to control the ball using only the pads of the fingers and a wrist-flip action, not the palm.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1 per ball (individual drill)
- **Court area:** Any standing position — can be done in lines
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per player
- **Duration:** 30 seconds

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players pass the basketball from hand to hand in front of the body using only the fingertips.
2. Players move the basketball from a low position to well over their heads as they tip the ball from hand to hand — continuously varying the height.

### Intermediate
1. Players dribble the basketball in front of the body using only the fingertips.
2. Progression order: index finger only → middle finger only → ring finger only → little finger only.
3. Practice with both the left and right hands.

## Coaching Points
- **Watch the wrists:** Players must flip their wrists with each touch — check the back of players' wrists to see if a wrinkle forms with each flip. No wrinkle = no wrist flip
- Players should use **only the fingertips** of each hand — never the palm
- "The part of the hand that must never be used to handle the ball" is the palm — this rule applies to every basketball fundamental, not just this drill [S15, p.45]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Standing hand-to-hand tips from low to high — develops the wrist-flip habit
2. **Intermediate:** Single-finger dribbling, progressing through all four fingers on each hand
3. **Advanced:** Combine with movement — perform fingertip dribbling while walking or running in lines

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-dribbling-driving-techniques]] — proper hand contact point (fingertip pads, not palm) is the foundation of all dribbling technique

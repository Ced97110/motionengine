# Medicine Ball Slam Into Vertical Jump

## Objective
Develop total-body power, core strength, and reactive jump height — training the specific motor pattern of aggressively reaching for and securing a missed shot, then immediately jumping again.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 1 medicine ball (prescribed weight — typically 6–12 lbs)
- Hard floor surface (rubber gym floor; NOT soft foam mats)
- Sufficient ceiling height for overhead arm extension

## Execution
1. Stand erect, feet shoulder-width apart, holding the medicine ball in both hands with arms fully extended overhead.
2. **Rise onto the balls of the feet** and hold for two seconds — this loads the calf complex and creates upper-body tension.
3. **Forcibly slam** the medicine ball to the floor while simultaneously bending the knees as the ball is released.
4. The instant the medicine ball makes floor contact, **immediately jump as high as possible** — the visual cue of the ball hitting the floor triggers the jump.
5. Land softly in a proper **defensive stance position** — feet wider than shoulder-width, knees bent, weight balanced.
6. Retrieve the ball and repeat for the prescribed number of reps.

> **The timing cue is critical:** the jump must occur simultaneously with the ball's floor contact, not after — this trains reactive response time. [S2, pp.122-123]

## Coaching Points
- **"Slam and spring"** — the slam and jump must feel like one continuous action
- The 2-second toe-raise pause is important — do not skip it; it builds eccentric calf loading
- Land in an athletic defensive stance — feet wide, knees bent, hands up
- Keep core braced throughout the entire movement — this is total-body integration training
- Choose a ball weight that allows a clean, fast slam — too heavy slows the reactive component

## Primary Muscles
- **At takeoff:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medius), tensor fasciae latae
- **At landing:** Same + biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus
- **Secondary:** Biceps brachii, latissimus dorsi, rectus abdominis, pectoralis major, triceps brachii, anterior deltoid [S2, p.123]

## Basketball Application
Mimics the full motor pattern of reaching up for a missed shot (overhead extension), coming down, and immediately jumping again for a putback dunk or tip-in. Develops the "second jump" explosiveness critical for offensive rebounding. [S2, p.123]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Overhead hold, bend and squat without slam — focus on landing mechanics; use light ball (4–6 lbs)
2. **Intermediate:** Add full slam and vertical jump; moderate ball (6–10 lbs)
3. **Advanced:** Increase ball weight, maximize jump height, add rim-touch target at top of jump

# Slide-Step Dribble (Drill 97)

## Objective
Teach post players the only safe dribble in the paint — the **slide-step dribble** — a single low dribble kept directly between the legs while taking one lateral slide step.

## Setup
- Individual or team
- Free-throw lane — full lane width
- One ball per player
- Players line up outside the lane

## Execution
1. First player in line executes a **single slide-step dribble** (one step) — this step ends at the **second line marking on the free-throw lane**.
2. Player **picks up the dribble** (both hands), then executes **one slide-step dribble to the middle of the lane**.
3. Picks up the ball again with both hands, then does **another slide-step dribble** — should take the player out of the lane.
4. Picks up the ball with both hands, then does **one more slide-step dribble** — should reach the **big block on the opposite lane**.
5. Player goes to the end of the line.

**Slide-step mechanics:**
- Dribble the ball **no higher than the knees**.
- Keep ball **directly beneath your crouch** — never in front of the body.
- To slide step right: use left hand, pick up right foot, step once, bring left foot up to shoulder-width apart.
- Ball remains **low and between the legs** where it cannot be slapped away.
- **Never dribble more than one step** in the post area.

6. Practice going **right one day**, left the next.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Traverse the lane with proper slide-step dribble only — focus on ball position.
2. **Advanced**: After each slide-step dribble, execute a move from the **pump fake series** — pump fake, pump fake crossover, or up-and-under.

## Coaching Points
- "Make sure the dribble is **directly between players' legs**, not out in front of them."
- Outside defenders sag to help and will deflect any dribble in front of the body.
- The slide step must be **one step only** — additional steps risk traveling or losing the ball.
- Keep the ball below the knees — high dribbles are easily tipped by help defenders.

# 3-25 Free-Shot Drill

## Objective
Require near-90% free-throw accuracy from the full team under pressure, building mental toughness, team accountability, and game-like concentration.

## Setup
- Entire team (or individual shooter)
- Free-throw line(s)
- Full court for sprints
- Running count of makes and misses

## Execution
1. Each player shoots **two free shots** in rotation.
2. The team accumulates makes — the goal is **25 total makes**.
3. The team has a **budget of only 3 misses** before the drill resets or results in consequences.
4. **Every miss**: that player (or entire team) immediately sprints the full length of the court and back before shooting continues.
5. The drill ends when the team accumulates **25 makes** with fewer than 3 misses.
6. If the team reaches 3 misses before 25 makes: assign a team consequence (extra sprints) and restart.

## Coaching Points
- To succeed, the team must collectively shoot ~90%+ — this is a high standard that demands focus and routine [S5, p.54]
- The sprint consequence creates game-like pressure and fatigue; free shots made while winded are more game-realistic
- *"Ending practice with this drill is a real test of mental toughness"* and encourages camaraderie and trust [S5, p.54]
- Coaches should observe full pre-shot routines under pressure — players should NOT rush or skip their routine to avoid sprints
- Consider having teammates encourage each shooter; this builds positive culture

## Progressions
1. **Individual**: One shooter completes the drill alone — 25 makes before 3 misses. No sprints; just personal accountability.
2. **Team (standard)**: Shared make count, shared miss count, shared sprint consequence.
3. **Advanced**: Reduce miss budget to 2; increase makes target to 30; or require that the last 5 makes be consecutive.

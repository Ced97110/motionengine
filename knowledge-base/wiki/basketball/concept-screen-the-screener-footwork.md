# Screen-the-Screener Footwork

## Summary
Screen-the-screener (StS) is an offensive concept that turns the first screener into the primary or secondary scoring threat by immediately screening them after they have set their initial screen. Because defenders are taught to help out on the initial screen, the screener's defender is often moving toward the ball-side cutter — leaving the screener vulnerable to a second screen. The key to making this action work is the initial screener's footwork: a precise front pivot and timed break up the lane line to use the second (down) screen, arriving shot-ready before the defender can recover. [S6, pp.250-252]

## When to Use
- After any off-ball back screen (especially flex or baseline back screens)
- When defensive teams help aggressively on cutters, leaving screeners unguarded
- When the initial screener is a strong perimeter shooter (medium range)
- As a counter to well-coached teams that hedge/switch on back screens

## Key Principles
1. **Screener's front pivot is the trigger**: After setting the flex back screen, 04 must front pivot off the top (right) foot immediately — this is what allows him to see the ball, the passer (05), and the upcoming down screen by 01. [S6, p.251]
2. **Break up the lane line in rhythm**: 04 breaks upward along the lane line at the correct timing to use 01's down screen. He must be getting his feet and hands ready to catch-and-shoot as he moves. [S6, p.251]
3. **Pivot off the inside heel**: On the catch from 05, 04 pivots off his inside heel (right, in this case) to square up to the basket for a medium-range shot. This footwork is the key to getting the shot off before X4 recovers. [S6, p.251]
4. **Why it works**: X4 helped out on 04's back screen for 02, then must navigate 01's down screen. The combination of these two actions gives X4 no opportunity to recover if 04's footwork is correct. [S6, p.251]
5. **Timing is critical**: The initial screener (04) times the down screen (01) so that he arrives just as the screen is set — too early means waiting and being defended; too late means the screen is no longer useful.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF (04 — Initial Screener / Screen User)**: Set flex back screen for 02; immediately front-pivot off top foot; break up lane line; read 01's down screen; pivot off inside heel on catch; shoot before X4 recovers.
- **PG (01 — Second Screener)**: After reversing ball to 05, go down into the free-throw lane to set a down screen for 04; then drift to the weakside deep corner area.
- **C (05 — Passer)**: Deliver the ball to 04 quickly as he uses 01's down screen; be ready for all three options (02 baseline, 04 off StS, 01 in corner).

## Variations
### Standard StS (Down Screen for the Back Screener)
01 reverses to 05 → 04 back screens for 02 → 01 goes down to down-screen for 04 → 04 comes off 01's screen for a medium-range shot. [S6, pp.250-252]

### Flex Back Screen + Flare Screen Variation
Instead of 01 setting a down screen for 04, 04 sets a flare back screen for 01 (01 flares to the weakside corner). 04 can then slip or stay at the elbow as a third option. [S6, p.247] → see [[concept-flex-back-screen-footwork]]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Screener waits stationary after setting the back screen** → Must immediately front pivot and start moving; standing still allows X4 to recover and take away the StS option.
2. **Wrong pivot foot on the catch** → Pivoting off the outside foot instead of the inside heel results in a slow, awkward square-up — the shot window closes before it opens.
3. **Screener uses down screen too early** → Arriving under 01's screen before 01 is set negates the pick; must read the timing of 01's movement into the lane.
4. **01 doesn't commit to the down screen** → A casual, soft down screen by 01 gives X4 room to fight over; 01 must commit the screen to truly pin X4.

# Contralateral Kick

## Objective
Develop unilateral ankle proprioception and dynamic single-leg stability by maintaining balance on the injured leg while kicking the opposite leg against resistance in multiple directions.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space
- Equipment: resistance band (attached to ankle of noninjured leg and anchored at floor level)

## Execution
1. Balance on the affected (injured) lower extremity. Attach a resistance band around the ankle of the noninjured leg at floor level.
2. Maintaining balance on the affected leg, kick with the noninjured leg against the resistance band in the sagittal and horizontal planes of motion (forward, backward, and side to side).
3. Perform the prescribed number of kicks in each direction before switching legs.
4. **Progression**: Increase resistance of the band or increase the speed of the kicks [S2, p.136].

## Coaching Points
- "Stay tall and controlled — your stance leg should barely move"
- Watch for hip drop or trunk lean on the stance side — cue upright posture
- The kicking leg drives the challenge; the stance leg is the training target
- Progress speed only after control is demonstrated at a slow pace
- Unilateral proprioception is vital for driving layups and landing from rebounds [S2, p.136]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow, controlled kicks in one direction (forward only) with light band
2. **Intermediate**: Multi-directional kicks (forward, back, side) with moderate band
3. **Advanced**: Fast kicks with heavier band; add eyes-closed variation [S2, p.136]

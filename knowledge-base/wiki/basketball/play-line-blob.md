# Line (BLOB)

## Overview
The line formation is described as the single most effective and adaptable baseline out-of-bounds alignment. Unlike plays designed to score off the inbounds pass, the Line is designed primarily to get the ball inbounds safely against hard defensive pressure and into the point guard's hands quickly. All four players in the line are active options, making it nearly impossible to fully deny.

> "If a coach could choose only one out-of-bounds alignment, the line would be it." — [S13, p.251]

## Formation
- **O3**: Inbounder on the baseline
- **O2**: Front of the line, lined up on the low block ball-side
- **O1**: Behind O2 in the line
- **O4**: Behind O1 in the line
- **O5**: Last in line (deepest from the baseline)
- All players approximately **12 inches (30 cm) apart**

## Phases

### Phase 1: Initial Cuts
- **O1** cuts toward the baseline, **goes around O2**, and sprints up the middle, **veering to the sideline** looking for the inbounds pass
- Simultaneously, **O5 jab-steps to the middle** and cuts to the **corner**, looking for the inbounds pass

### Phase 2: Screen and Second Options
- **O4 steps inside the free-throw lane** and **screens down on O2's defender**
- **O2 comes up the lane**, veers to the elbow, and moves toward the sideline — looking for the inbounds pass

### Phase 3: Final Option
- After his down screen, **O4 steps to the ball** as a possible receiver
- **O3 surveys all four options** and makes the pass to the most open player
- Regardless of who receives the pass, **O1 seeks the ball to set the offense**

## Four Inbound Options (in order)
1. **O1** — sprinting up the middle, veering to the sideline (fastest option)
2. **O5** — jab-step middle, cut to corner
3. **O2** — off O4's down screen to the elbow/sideline
4. **O4** — stepping to the ball after screening (final safety)

## Key Coaching Points
- All four players must move with **determined purpose** — defenders will try to hold or deny each cutter
- The line provides **proper spacing** — players are 12 inches apart for legal screens and maximum spacing
- **O1 getting the ball** is the priority — the PG should seek the ball immediately after the pass
- **Works against full-court pressure**: the sequential cuts make it nearly impossible to deny all options
- Coaches can **adjust player positions** in the line based on whether the opponent is trying to deny a passer, ball handler, or shooter
- The line can also be used in the **backcourt** for inbounding against a press

## Variations
### Frontcourt Line
Standard version as described — baseline frontcourt BLOB.

### Backcourt Line
Same alignment used under backcourt basket against a full-court press — same sequential cutting rules apply.

## Common Mistakes
1. Players standing still waiting for the ball → everyone must cut purposefully and simultaneously
2. PG not seeking the ball after someone else receives it → O1 must immediately find the ball to set the offense
3. Players too far apart → stay approximately 12 inches apart for legal screens

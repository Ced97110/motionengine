# Pivot-and-Pass Technique

## Summary
When a ball handler kills his dribble against a ball-pressuring defender, he must use a pivot-and-pass sequence to deliver the ball to a teammate. The pivot-and-pass technique is a four-step system — jump stop, step-out, rip-through, and swing-around — that gives the passer multiple options for attacking the defender laterally and finding an open passing lane. Mastering this skill prevents turnovers and keeps the offense moving even when the dribble ends prematurely. [S6, pp.81-84]

## When to Use
- After killing a dribble against a pressing or trapping defender
- When driving and being cut off, forcing a pass
- Any time a ball handler must pivot to find a passing angle while protecting the ball
- Against aggressive ball-denial defenders in the half court

## Key Principles
1. **Stay in a semi-crouch stance throughout** — height and leverage prevent the defender from easily deflecting the ball.
2. **Protect the ball beside the free knee** — the ball is never exposed; it rides beside the knee of whichever foot is free to step laterally.
3. **Outflank the defense by stepping due east or west** — the free foot must get laterally outside the defender's foot before passing.
4. **Fake a pass to make a pass** — "fake high and go low" or "fake low and go high" after getting the free foot outside the defender.
5. **Progress through the four steps sequentially** — each step is triggered by how the defender reacts to the previous move.

## The Four Steps

### Step 1: The Two-Foot Jump Stop
As the dribbler approaches the defender and kills his dribble, he takes a small bunny hop and lands simultaneously on both feet. This two-foot simultaneous landing allows the player to use **either foot as the pivot foot**, giving maximum flexibility for the next step. [S6, p.81]

### Step 2: The Step-Out
Choose one foot as the pivot foot based on which side of the defender to attack. For example, to attack the defender's left side, make the **left foot the pivot** foot and step the right (free) foot laterally toward the outside of the defender's left foot. Hold the ball firmly with both hands beside the free knee (right knee in this example). Once the free foot is outside the defender's foot, fake high and go low, or fake low and go high. [S6, p.81]

<!-- DIAGRAM: Diagram 1-52 — passer stepping right foot outside defender's left foot, ball beside right knee -->

### Step 3: The Rip-Through
If the defender counters the first lateral attack, execute a **front pivot** — rip the ball low and hard across the shoe tops as you step across the defender's face. This switches the attack to the opposite side of the defender. The ball lands on the inside of the free leg's knee on the new side. Once the free foot clears the defender's opposite foot, apply the same fake-and-pass principles on the new side. [S6, pp.81-82]

<!-- DIAGRAM: Diagram 1-53 — front pivot rip-through, ball moving from right knee to left knee side -->

### Step 4: The Swing-Around (Reverse Pivot)
If the defender reacts quickly to the rip-through, the passer **reverse pivots** away from the defender (off the same pivot foot) and re-attacks the defender's original lateral side, or looks to make an uncontested pass to another teammate. The ball returns beside the free (right) knee. [S6, pp.82-84]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Primary user — must master all four steps to break full-court press and handle ball-pressure situations
- **SG/SF**: Must recognize when to use the technique off a dead dribble in the half court; especially important in quick-hit offenses
- **PF/C**: Must use the jump stop and step-out when catching in the post and facing a front or three-quarter defense — pivot-and-pass to a cutter or kick-out

## Three Points of Emphasis to the Passer
1. **Protect the basketball** — always place the ball beside the free knee, never hold it in the middle of the body where defenders can strip it
2. **Step outside the defender's foot** — laterally outflank the defense by going due east or due west; do not go north or south
3. **Fake a pass to make a pass** — sell the high pass to deliver low; sell the low pass to deliver high [S6, p.84]

## Drill Structure (Rotating Stations)
The pivot-and-pass technique is best trained in a multi-station drill:
- **Station 1 (Dribbler/Passer)**: works dribble approach, jump stop, and all four pivot steps
- **Station 2 (Defender)**: works on defensive stance and ball pressure against the dribbler (does NOT guard the shooter)
- **Station 3 (Shooter)**: practices [[concept-one-two-step-footwork]] — inside heel plant to stop momentum, pivot to square up, catch and shoot

Stations rotate every 55 seconds; one full round (left-pivot foot) followed by a second round (right-pivot foot) takes six minutes. Each player gets nearly two minutes at each station. [S6, p.85]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ball held in front of the body** → correction: ball must ride beside the free knee at all times
2. **Stepping north-south instead of east-west** → correction: emphasize lateral outflanking — step due east or due west
3. **Releasing the fake too quickly** → correction: sell the fake before delivering; wait for the defender to commit
4. **Skipping the jump stop** → correction: always establish a legal pivot foot via the simultaneous two-foot landing before pivoting
5. **Dropping the pivot foot** → correction: maintain the pivot foot throughout all four steps; only reset after the pass is made

## Variations
### Left-Pivot-Foot Round
All dribblers use the left foot as the pivot foot for the first full rotation — attacks the defender's left side first (Step 2), then rip-throughs to the right side (Step 3).

### Right-Pivot-Foot Round
All dribblers use the right foot as the pivot foot — reverses all lateral directions from the left-pivot round.

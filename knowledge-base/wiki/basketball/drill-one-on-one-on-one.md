# One-on-One-on-One (#110)

## Objective
Develop competitive 1-on-1 scoring ability from the 3-point line with all live-ball and post moves in a fun, high-stakes rotating format where players must also create space to earn quality shot credit.

## Setup
- **Players**: 3 per group; roles determined by free-throw misses
- **Court area**: Half court; attacker begins anywhere on the 3-point arc
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball
- **Scoring**: 3-point shots = 3 pts; jump shots/layups = 2 pts; fouls = 1 pt for offense; first to 10 wins

<!-- DIAGRAM: Player 1 at top of arc, Player 2 defending inside arc, Player 3 waiting at baseline. After each possession, Player 3 rushes out to defend the new ball handler who dribbles back to the 3-point line. -->

## Execution
1. **Role assignment**: All three players shoot free throws. First miss = out (next player on floor). Second miss = first defender. Player who did not miss begins on offense.
2. Player 1 (offense) begins **anywhere along the 3-point line**; Player 2 defends; Player 3 waits.
3. As long as 1 scores, 1 keeps the basketball. After scoring, 1 dribbles back to the 3-point line; Player 3 must sprint out to defend or 1 has an open 3-point shot.
4. **If 2 gets the defensive rebound**: 2 dribbles back to the 3-point line; Player 3 must sprint out to defend 2. Player 1 waits.
5. **If 2 fouls 1**: 1 calls the foul, dribbles back to the 3-point line; Player 3 defends. 2 goes to wait.
6. A shot is a **quality shot** only if space was created — "no defender's hand in the face or on the ball when the shot is taken."
7. First player to 10 points wins.

## Coaching Points
- Review offensive moves with each player as the game continues — teachable moments are constant
- Correct any defensive lapse that allows a shot without creating space
- "Shoot at the exact moment space is created" — timing the shot to the separation is the key skill
- The winner of nearly every game is the team that takes the most quality shots [S15, p.223]
- Option: Have attacker call out the scoring move while dribbling back to the 3-point line — builds verbal-motor coordination
- Option: Limit to 3 dribbles — forces quicker decision-making

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Any move, no dribble limit
2. **Intermediate**: Limit to 3 dribbles; call out moves
3. **Advanced**: Shot must create space (even if it goes in, it counts only if no hand was in face or on ball)

# Pivot Footwork — Two-Foot Jump Stop and Lateral Passing Sequence

## Summary
When a ball-handler picks up their dribble, their ability to attack the defense with a live pivot depends entirely on which foot they choose as the pivot foot. The two-foot jump stop — a small bunny hop landing simultaneously on both feet — is the foundational technique because it preserves the player's right to choose **either** foot as the pivot. From there, a four-step lateral passing sequence (Step-Out → Rip-Through → Swing-Around → Reset) gives the ball-handler a systematic method of finding a passing lane around a ball defender.

The same pivoting principles apply to shooters receiving a pass: planting the inside heel first stops forward momentum and creates the axis for squaring up to the basket — producing a balanced, accurate shot.

## When to Use
- After killing the dribble against a ball-defender pressing the passing lane.
- On any catch-and-shoot situation where momentum must be stopped before releasing.
- On post catches and wing catches when a defender is contesting the pass or shot.

## Key Principles
1. **Two-Foot Jump Stop:** Land simultaneously on both feet off a small bunny hop. This preserves either foot as the legal pivot foot.
2. **Ball Protection:** After the stop, hold the ball firmly with both hands beside the knee of the free (non-pivot) foot at all times.
3. **Go East or West:** All lateral attacks on the defender must be purely east/west (sideline direction). Never drive north/south into the defender.
4. **Step-Out:** Plant the pivot foot, step the free foot outside the defender's corresponding foot to outflank them; fake high/go low or fake low/go high.
5. **Rip-Through:** If the first lateral attack is denied, rip the ball low across the shoe tops and front-pivot to attack the defender's opposite side.
6. **Swing-Around (Reverse Pivot):** If both lateral sides are denied, reverse pivot away from the defender to find an open passing angle.
7. **Heel-Plant for Shooters:** The inside heel should hit the floor just as the ball arrives at the shooting hand. The heel stops momentum and becomes the pivot axis to square up to the basket.
8. **No Floating:** Shooters must rise straight up and come back straight down. Any drift in either direction reduces shooting accuracy.

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions:** Master the two-foot jump stop so either foot can be used as a pivot foot in any situation.
- **Ball-handlers:** Know all four lateral-attack steps in sequence and read which one the defense leaves open.
- **Shooters:** Time the inside heel-plant to arrive with the ball for a zero-momentum catch-and-shoot.

## Variations
### Front Pivot (Step-Out)
Step the free foot east or west outside the defender's foot, keeping the pivot foot planted. Attack with a pass fake then deliver.

### Reverse Pivot (Swing-Around)
If both forward lateral attacks are shut down, pivot backward (away from the defender) off the same pivot foot to find an open passing lane on the original side.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Stepping north/south (into the defender)** → Correct: Always step east or west to outflank the defense.
2. **Ball held too high or away from the body** → Correct: Ball beside the free knee for protection on every step.
3. **Staggered landing (one foot before the other)** → Correct: True simultaneous two-foot jump stop to preserve pivot-foot choice.
4. **Floating on the jump shot** → Correct: Stop all momentum before releasing; rise straight up.
5. **Catching off the outside foot** → Correct: Inside heel hits first to stop momentum and enable a smooth pivot to the basket.

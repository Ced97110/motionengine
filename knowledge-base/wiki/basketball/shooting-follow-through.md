# Shooting Follow-Through

## Summary
The follow-through is the final phase of the shot and provides the last bit of power and direction to the ball. It also serves as the most valuable diagnostic tool for a coach: a frozen follow-through lets you verify every mechanical element before the shooter moves on. Correct follow-through ensures the ball travels on the proper shot line with ideal arch and backspin.

The key principle is that the **shot line** — toe, knee, hip, shoulder, elbow, wrist, and hand — must remain in perfect alignment through and past the release point. [S5, p.41]

## When to Use
- Applicable on every shot: free shots, midrange jump shots, three-pointers, form-shooting drills
- Especially important during practice to allow coach feedback before the shooter moves
- In games: freezing the finish keeps the shooter locked in to the target and on balance

## Key Principles
1. **Full arm extension**: After release, the shooting arm extends fully with the elbow finishing **above the eyebrow** and slightly in front of the body. [S5, p.41]
2. **Stay in the shot line**: Toe, knee, hip, shoulder, elbow, wrist, and hand all maintain alignment — the arm does not finish across the face or outside the shoulder. [S5, p.41]
3. **Hand in the hoop**: The shooting hand points straight toward the target with fingers angled down ~45 degrees toward the floor (coaching cue: *"put your hand in a cookie jar"*). The wrist becomes the highest point of the shooting arm. [S5, p.41]
4. **Balance hand stays up**: Both hands remain elevated after the release. Dropping the balance hand creates a power struggle — one side of the body pulls toward the target while the other pulls away, twisting the body off-line. [S5, p.41]
5. **Freeze the finish**: Hold the follow-through until the ball hits the floor (in practice) or swishes through the net. This builds muscle memory and gives coaches a clear picture of technique. [S5, p.41]
6. **Head stays still**: Eyes remain fixed on the target — do not follow the ball's flight. [S5, p.41]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Every player must develop a consistent follow-through. There are no position-specific exceptions to these mechanics.

## Variations
### Jump Shot Follow-Through
On a jump shot, the shooter remains on the toes or balls of the feet until the ball swishes through the net. The shooting arm extends, elbow above the eyebrow, hand into the hoop. Dropping the arms early also prevents the shooter from staying on the toes, causing the shot to fall short or drift. [S5, p.43]

### Free-Shot Follow-Through
Identical mechanics — elbow above eyebrow, hand into the hoop, balance hand stays up — but the shooter is stationary, making it easier to achieve a perfectly straight finish. [S5, p.43]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Dropping the balance hand** → Results in body twist and shot drift to the shooting-hand side. Correction: coach the player to keep both hands in the air until the ball hits the floor.
2. **Arm finishes across the face** → Shot pulls off the shot line. Correction: cue *"right hand stays right, left hand stays left."* [S5, p.41]
3. **Dropping arms too quickly** → Coach loses diagnostic window; player loses toes-up balance. Correction: enforce the freeze in every practice repetition.
4. **Elbow below the eyebrow** → Reduces arch; shot hits the front rim. Correction: cue *"elbow above the eyebrow"* and use the [[drill-eggs-on-the-rim]] drill.
5. **Eyes follow the ball** → Head moves, disrupting balance. Correction: cue *"find the target and hold your gaze."*

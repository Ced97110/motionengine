# 1-on-1 Full-Court Game (#108)

## Objective
Develop full-court 1-on-1 offensive and defensive skills, conditioning, and competitive scoring instincts in a sustained game-like format.

## Setup
- **Players**: 4+ in two sideline lines; 2 active at a time
- **Court area**: Full court (use side-court baskets when available to run multiple groups)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball
- **Duration**: 5 minutes; player with most baskets wins

<!-- DIAGRAM: Player 1 at one end with X1. Players X2, X3, X4 in one sideline line; Players X5 in opposite sideline. After each possession, loser goes to end of their sideline line. -->

## Execution
1. Players 1 and X1 begin by **"inbounding the ball"** — player 1 tosses the ball onto the backboard; both players fight for possession.
2. The player who secures possession becomes the attacker; the other defends.
3. They play 1-on-1 the full length of the court to the opposite basket.
4. **If 1 scores**: 1 goes to the end of the line on the scored-on side. X1 quickly tosses the ball off the backboard; X2 races out to fight for the inbounds ball. X1 and X2 play full-court 1v1.
5. **If X1 stops 1** (rebound or turnover): X1 attacks the far basket with 1 defending. If 1 scores again at the far end, X1 tosses the ball off the backboard for the next inbounds sequence.
6. The pattern continues for 5 minutes; the player who scores the most baskets wins.
7. **Option**: Only players 1 and X1 play 2-minute full-court 1v1 with no substitutes; player with most points wins; two other players then take the court.

## Coaching Points
- The backboard-toss inbound simulates jump ball situations — teach players to use their body to gain position
- "Score, then get to the end of the line fast" — no standing around
- Watch for conditioning fatigue leading to poor footwork; correct in real time
- Encourage attackers to use both directions; defenders must contain without reaching
- Side court baskets allow two groups simultaneously — maximize floor efficiency

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Substitute format (full group rotation, 5 min)
2. **Intermediate**: No substitute, 2-minute 1v1 games, track individual scores
3. **Advanced**: Add a rule that the attacker must use a different move on each possession

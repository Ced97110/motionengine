# Depth Jump Into Jump Shot

## Objective
Improve reactive jumping ability and simulate the game action of shooting over a defender by training the stretch-shortening cycle off a box depth jump.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 1 plyometric box, 12–30 inches (30–75 cm) height — choose based on current strength and ability
- Open floor space; a basket is optional for adding ball-shooting finish

## Execution
1. Stand erect on top of the box, positioned close to the front edge. Feet approximately hip-width apart.
2. **Step** (do not jump) forward off the edge of the box to lower the body toward the floor. Do not pre-jump.
3. Make simultaneous two-foot contact with the floor; immediately jump vertically as quickly as possible, simulating a full jump shot arm extension.
4. The jump should mimic game-speed jump shot mechanics — arms extend fully overhead.
5. Land softly on two feet, absorbing forces through ankle, knee, and hip flexion.
6. Perform for the prescribed number of repetitions or prescribed time.

> **Key point:** Stepping off (not jumping off) the box is essential to properly load the stretch-shortening cycle and maximize reactive strength. [S2, pp.112-113]

## Coaching Points
- **"Step, don't leap"** — a jump off the box reduces the reactive loading benefit
- Select an appropriate box height — too high risks injury and slows reactive response
- **"Touch and explode"** — ground contact time should be as brief as possible
- Simulate full jump shot arm extension at the top — this is game-specific training
- Landing in a "defensive stance position" reinforces athletic readiness [S2, p.122]
- Progress box height only after demonstrating clean mechanics at the current height

## Primary Muscles
- **At takeoff:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medius)
- **At landing:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus [S2, p.113]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Low-box depth jump (6–10 in./15–25 cm box or step), no shot simulation — focus on reactive two-foot landing only
2. **Intermediate:** Standard box (12–18 in./30–45 cm), add jump shot arm extension at top
3. **Advanced:** Higher box (24–30 in./60–75 cm), add basketball — perform actual catch-and-shoot at top of reactive jump

## Variations
### Low-Box Depth Jump Into Jump Shot
Perform the identical exercise off a smaller box (6–12 in./15–30 cm) until sufficient strength and athletic ability are established. [S2, p.113]

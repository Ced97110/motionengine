# Daily Defensive Checklist — 44 Points to Teach and Stress Every Day

## Summary
Herb Brown's 44-point defensive checklist is the most comprehensive single-document defensive teaching tool in *Let's Talk Defense*. It covers every aspect of man-to-man defense: on-ball pressure, post defense, transition, screen navigation, trapping, closeouts, ball fakes, communication, and special situations. This list is designed to be part of every practice and every film session — not as a one-time lesson but as a daily culture standard.

The checklist reflects Brown's belief that elite team defense is not a few big concepts but dozens of small precise behaviors executed simultaneously by five players. Mastering these behaviors requires daily reinforcement until they become instinctive.

## When to Use
- As a practice checklist — select 5-10 points to focus on each session
- As a film evaluation rubric — identify which points were violated in a loss
- As a player accountability tool — players can self-evaluate against the list
- Distributed at the start of the season and reviewed throughout

## Key Principles

### Ball Pressure and Penetration Prevention
1. **Stop dribble penetration.** Keep man in front; guard one yard to either side.
2. **Deny penetrating entry passes.** Overplay opponents to force catches farther out.
3. **Pressure all passers**, especially after the dribble dies. Stop ball reversal.
4. **Never lose sight of the ball.** The ball should always draw you to your man.
5. **Pick your opponent up in the backcourt.** Force the dribbler to change direction as many times as possible.
6. **Extend defensive pressure up the court.** Pick opponents up early; disrupt rhythm and concentration.
7. **When playing off the ball, prevent dribble penetration** by occupying driving lanes.

### Transition Defense
8. **Deny easy transition baskets.** Always maintain defensive floor balance.
9. **Get level with the ball.** Stop the offense from outnumbering you in transition. First man back protects the paint and builds defense out.
10. **Make the transition from offense to defense with all five players.**
11. **When you have a fast-break opportunity, the fifth player must stay back.** The fifth player in transition cannot let the defense get behind — this invites the long pass and layup.

### Rebounding
12. **Rebound defensively.** Eliminate offensive rebounds and put-backs.
13. **Concentrate on offensive rebounding** to prevent leak-outs and fast breaks.
14. **Maintain offensive floor balance** to stop transition baskets.

### Post Defense
15. **Front or three-quarter the post on the ball side.** Force man off the block; method depends on whether ball is above or below the free-throw line. Always stress fronting the low post.
16. **Double the baseline drive.** Easier when fronting; also works by stepping out on the baseline side when playing behind.
17. **Deny the high-low action** on post feeds. Pressure both the ball and the passer.
18. **Deter the weak-side duck-in to the low post.** Anticipate and beat the offensive player to the spot; ride him high or send him backdoor.

### Screen Defense
19. **Bump or chuck all cutters.** Body up, ride them up and over screens; don't give them an option.
20. **Slip screens.** Don't be picked. Try to slip inside the screen or take the offensive charge.
21. **Lock and trail on normal turnouts.** Get on the hip and outside shoulder to make the opponent curl the screen.
22. **On wide-set turnouts**: play topside to force man away from screen, OR take the shorter path through the screen (dictated by ball position). The screener's defender must stand up the screener, then step back to let teammate through.
23. **Body up and go over flare or jam screens.**
24. **Bump and stand up screeners** on every screen action.
25. **On baseline cross screens**: force the cutter away from his path, OR bump him up and go under on the baseline side, OR force the screener low and ride him out of bounds. Decide pre-possession.
26. **Switch up the line if necessary** on multiple or staggered screens. Decide: go over the first, under the second?
27. **Force men without the ball backdoor**, then open up and retreat to the goal without losing sight of the ball.
28. **Alternative to #27**: force backdoor, then snap back to defend backdoor, clearout, or isolation plays.

### Closeouts
29. **Close out and contest all shots.** Always keep a hand up. Close out under control — do not run past the shooter. Guard against ball fakes when closing out.
30. **Don't permit personal fouls leading to three-point plays.** Make opponents shoot two free throws instead. If you must foul, foul before the shot.
31. **Always take ball fakes by first stepping back off your back foot.** Never jump at a fake.

### Help Defense and Rotation
32. **Move on the pass, not on the catch.** Move immediately in the direction of the pass once the ball is in the air.
33. **Make yourself long and wide.** Get into passing lanes. Keep the opponent away from your body so he cannot feel/seal you when receiving.
34. **Work daily on help-and-recover fundamentals** to defend penetration and the draw-and-kick offensive maneuver.
35. **Teach players how to stunt, dig, and get back to their men.**
36. **Rotate to the free man in the direction of the help** when leaving a trap.

### Trapping Principles
37. **When trapping, force the passer to pass out over your outside shoulder.** This enables weak-side defenders to react and rotate.
38. **Seal the trap.** Stop or discourage penetrating passes. Read the eyes and position of the player being trapped.
39. **Hard-trap and jump out when switching.** Stop and turn the opponent uphill and back to his defender.
40. **Hard-trap the point of the ball** if the opponent is trailing the play up court and you can pin him to the sideline. Come at the dribbler from a right angle.
41. **When trapping, always turn the man with the ball uphill and back** to his defender to force him to pick up his dribble.

### Communication and Special Situations
42. **Always emphasize talking and communicating.** Call screens, switches, ball position, and rotation assignments out loud.
43. **Disrupt the opponent's offensive flow** — vary pressure, timing, and positioning.
44. **Use your hands to cause deflections** and turnovers. Active hands must accompany active feet.
45. **In short-clock situations, think about inverting and switching** on out-of-bounds plays, and switching whenever players cross or exchange positions in half-court sets.
46. **Think about incorporating pro Shadow or Tilt defensive principles** in your scheme. Weak-side help is a must.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Ball pressure; forced direction changes; deny wing entry passes; communication hub on the floor.
- **SG/SF**: Bump cutters; lock and trail; close out and contest; help and recover; transition back first.
- **PF**: Screen defense; post fronting; double baseline drive; rebound; rotate in trapping schemes.
- **C**: Low-post fronting; weak-side lob coverage; defensive rebounding; pre-rotate on big-to-big rotations.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Moving on the catch, not the pass** → Delay means the pass arrives before the defender; move the instant the ball is in the air.
2. **Not stunting and digging** → Waiting to react to penetration is too slow; stunt and dig to discourage the drive before it develops.
3. **Jumping at shot fakes during closeouts** → Step back off the back foot to maintain defensive positioning; never leave your feet on a fake.
4. **Failing to seal the trap** → Open passing lanes out of traps allow the offense to reset; all four non-ball defenders must anticipate and rotate.
5. **Weak-side defenders ball-watching** → When defending off the ball, you must see both your man and the ball simultaneously — never only the ball.

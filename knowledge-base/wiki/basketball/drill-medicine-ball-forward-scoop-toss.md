# Medicine-Ball Forward Scoop Toss, Bounce, and Catch

## Objective
Improve total-body power and reactive strength through a hip-hinge scoop throw followed by an immediate acceleration sprint — mimics the explosive extension used in rebounding and attacking the basket. [S8, p.149]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open court or outdoor surface with room to sprint 10–20 yards
- 1 medicine ball (moderate weight appropriate to athlete's strength)

## Execution
1. Begin in an upright position holding the medicine ball.
2. **Swing the ball between the legs** as you squat down (hip hinge pattern — hinge at the hips, not the waist).
3. Immediately **swing the ball upward and forward**, releasing it at approximately **shoulder height** as you extend through the hips.
4. **Sprint forward immediately** toward the ball.
5. Attempt to catch the ball after its **first bounce but before its second bounce**.
6. Reset and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- This is a **kettlebell swing pattern** applied to a medicine ball — the power comes from hip extension, not the arms [S8, p.149]
- "Hinge, swing, explode" — cue the three-phase sequence
- Arms follow the hip drive; do not muscle the ball with the arms alone
- Release at shoulder height for optimal distance-to-height ratio
- Sprint acceleration must begin the instant the ball leaves the hands
- Adjust ball weight so the athlete can realistically catch it before the second bounce

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Focus on hip-hinge mechanics without a toss (kettlebell swing substitute)
2. **Intermediate**: Light toss for controlled distance; immediate sprint
3. **Advanced**: Heavier ball; longer distance; partner adds a visual cue (drop a cone) to signal which direction to sprint after the throw, adding choice reaction

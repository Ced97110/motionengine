# Supine Basketball Core Pass

## Objective
Develop anterior chain core strength through repeated sit-up and leg-raise movements, using a basketball to keep players mentally engaged and simulate passing muscle activation.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (gym or weight room)
- 1 basketball (or weighted medicine ball for advanced variation)

## Execution
1. Lie supine on the floor with legs straight and together, arms extended overhead, holding a basketball in both hands. Pull the navel in toward the spine.
2. Sit up with arms straight overhead and pass (transfer) the basketball from hands to between the feet — squeeze the ball between the feet to secure it.
3. Return to lying flat on your back while maintaining the abdominal draw-in.
4. Squeeze the ball between your feet, lift your legs and pelvis up, and pass the ball back to your hands.
5. Slowly return to the flat supine starting position.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.36-37]

## Coaching Points
- Maintain the abdominal draw-in throughout the entire exercise — the back should not arch away from the floor
- The ball squeeze between the feet activates the hip adductors and keeps the legs connected
- "The basketball is not decoration — it keeps you honest about generating force to throw and catch"
- Using a basketball keeps players mentally engaged, improving exercise compliance [S2, p.37]
- Watch for players "throwing" the legs down carelessly — lower controlled

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use standard basketball; reduce range of motion if lower back lifts
2. **Intermediate**: Full range of motion, multiple sets, controlled pace
3. **Advanced — Weighted-Ball Pass**: Use a weighted medicine ball instead of a basketball to increase core demand significantly [S2, p.37]

### Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Rectus abdominis, transversus abdominis, iliopsoas, latissimus dorsi
- **Secondary**: Sartorius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius), triceps brachii [S2, p.37]

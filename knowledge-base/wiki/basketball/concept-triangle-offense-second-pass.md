# Triangle Offense — Second Pass Options

## Summary
After the entry pass from 1 to the strong-side wing (3) and 1's corner fill to form the sideline triangle, the wing reads the defense and chooses from four "second pass" targets. Each target opens a distinct set of scoring actions. This is the heart of the Phil Jackson/Tex Winter triangle offense: every pass creates a new decision tree, and the offense never stalls because all actions lead to continuity on the other side of the floor. [S7, pp.97-109]

## When to Use
- Half-court possession offense against man-to-man defense
- When the sideline triangle has been formed (wing, post, corner)
- When the first look (pass to the center) is not immediately available
- As the offense's primary read-and-react framework

## Key Principles
1. **The passer is the first cutter** — on a pass to the post, the passer cuts immediately to the side of the teammate they are trying to free.
2. **Players do not pre-decide** — read the defense and react; all four second-pass looks remain live on every possession.
3. **Cutters complete their cuts** — even if the pass doesn't come, maintain movement so the passer can anticipate and deliver accurately.
4. **Weak-side players spot up at the fan/garden spot** — the far wing or guard spots up at the "garden spot" (open shooting area opposite the ball) to punish double teams.
5. **Continuity is built in** — if no second-pass option produces a shot, rotate to form the triangle on the other side of the floor. "We are always in our offense and ready to operate!"
6. **The freeze spot** — weak-side player holds position to keep their defender occupied while waiting for a possible screen.

## The Four Second-Pass Looks

### First Look: Pass to the Center (5) — Split Cuts
After 3 passes to 5, two cutters simultaneously attack the post:
- **Option 1 — Split cuts:** 3 fakes inside then cuts baseline side of 5; 1 cuts as close as possible behind 3 ("split the post"). 2 spots up at fan spot; 4 near the lane for rebound.
- **Option 2 — Action zone speed cut:** 1 baseline speed cut for a possible drop pass from 5; 3 makes an over-the-top speed cut; 4 freezes in the deep basket area.
- **Screen-the-screener follow-up:** If neither cutter is open, 3 screens for 4 (screen-the-screener), 4 pops to the free-throw area for a pass from 5. 3 rolls to the basket after setting the screen as a secondary option.
- **Option 3 — Rebound screen cut:** 3 fakes toward the rebound lane but instead screens; 1 step fakes baseline then cuts off 3's screen to a position in front of 5. 5 passes to whichever of 2 or 4 is open.
- **Fan pass safety:** If no cutter is open, 5 kicks to 2 at the garden spot when his defender doubles. [S7, pp.97-100]

### Second Look: Pass to the Weak-Side Guard (2) at Top of Circle
- **Option 1 — Post duck in:** 2 shoots first or passes to 5 who ducks into the lane. If no shot, 2 can dribble weave to 1, who passes back to 3 stepping back into the corner. 4 reverses for rebounding position.
- **Option 2 — Pinch post action (backdoor):** 4 pops to the ball but is overplayed — reverses backdoor for an over-the-top pass from 2. 5 keeps defender busy at the block. If 4 can't receive backdoor, he pops to the high-side post elbow; 2 speed cuts off 4 and receives a flip pass back ("two-man play"). 2 drives, 4 reverse pivots and reads: dive cut if direct line to basket; hold for return pass if double team develops. [S7, pp.100-101]

### Third Look: Pass to the Weak-Side Wing (4) on the Backdoor Step
- **Option 1 — Drop pass to cutting 2:** 3 passes to 4 (flashing to ball-side elbow); 2 makes a backdoor speed cut; if 2's head and shoulders are past the defender, 4 drop passes knee-high to 2. 3 makes a rebound screen cut; 1 step fakes and cuts off 3's screen; 5 freezes at the block.
- **Option 2 — 3 receives and drives:** If 2 is not open backdoor, 2 continues to corner; 3 cuts to receive from 4. 3 can drive to basket, kick to 5 on the block, or kick to 2 in the corner.
- **Dribble weave continuation:** If neither 2 nor 3 can receive, 4 dribble-weaves to 1 coming out of the corner. 1 drives, takes a jump shot, or makes a fan pass to 2 or 3. 5 flashes from weak-side low post. [S7, pp.102-103]

### Fourth Look: Pass to the Strong-Side Guard (1) in the Corner
- 3 passes to 1; 3 immediately makes a banana cut to the basket; 5 gets to the high-post ball-side elbow.
- 5 screens and rolls on 1, who drives to the top of the key and can pass to 5 on the roll.
- 3 continues cut and is screened by 4 on the weak side; 2 fans away, spotting up opposite the ball.
- 1 can shoot, drive, pass to 2 (spotted up), or pass to 4 or 5 on basket penetration.
- If nothing develops, the triangle reforms on the other side with 3, 4, and 2. [S7, pp.103]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (1):** Entry passer, corner filler, cutter, second-look receiver, dribble-weave initiator.
- **SG (2):** Weak-side spotter at garden/fan spot, backdoor cutter in second and third looks, defensive balance guard.
- **SF (3):** Strong-side wing — primary second-pass decision maker; split cutter, rebound screen cutter, passer.
- **PF (4):** Weak-side wing — freeze spot holder, flash to elbow, backdoor cutter, screen-the-screener action, weak-side screen.
- **C (5):** Post receiver, split-cut target, drop passer, fan pass to garden spot, screen-and-roll initiator.

## Variations

### Solo Cut Series
Instead of cutting to the corner after the entry pass, 1 makes a speed cut to the free-throw area or basket. 4 holds at 15-18 feet; 2 holds at the top of the circle. 3 passes to 5 and makes a "solo cut" to either side of 5. If 3 can't reach 5, passes to 2, then makes a rub cut off 5 while 5 steps up to "pinch post." 4 screens for 1 on the weak side. 2 has multiple pass options: to 3, 1, 4 (basket roll), or 5 (pinch post). [S7, p.104]

### Wing Screen and Roll
After setting a screen for 1, 4 sets a side screen for 3, who drives around 4 to the lane. 1 spots up in corner or returns for dribble weave. 4 then screens a third time in the corner for 2 (defensive balance). [S7, p.108]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Stopping in midcut** → the passer may have committed to the pass, causing a turnover. Always complete the cut.
2. **Multiple players cutting simultaneously** → creates a traffic jam in the lane. Stagger cuts with court awareness.
3. **Weak-side players standing still** → defenders can collapse into the paint. Spot up at the fan/garden spot and maintain the freeze spot.
4. **Post holding the ball too long** → triggers double teams before cutters complete their action. Pass quickly and keep the ball moving.
5. **Ignoring the continuity reset** → when nothing is available, many teams stall. The triangle always flows to the other side for a new triangle.

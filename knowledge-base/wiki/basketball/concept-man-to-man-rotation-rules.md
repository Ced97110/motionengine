# Man-to-Man Rotation Rules (Help-Side Defense)

## Summary
In man-to-man defense, every player must understand ball side vs. help side. When a ball handler beats their on-ball defender and becomes a breakaway dribbler, help-side defenders must rotate immediately. The system uses an imaginary horizontal line drawn midway up the free-throw lane to assign roles during rotation. [S15, pp.298-299]

## When to Use
- Any time a ball handler beats their on-ball defender and drives toward the basket
- When the help-side defender closest to the ball (gap closer) cannot fully stop the penetration
- Late-game situations where a single breakdown can lead to an uncontested layup

## Key Principles
1. **Ball side = denial + fence slide.** On ball side, the next pass must always be contested using the fence slide and by denying the wing. [S15, p.299]
2. **Help side = stop the ball.** The primary job of help-side defenders is to help stop a breakaway dribbler before they reach the basket. [S15, p.299]
3. **First help-side defender = gap closer.** The help-side defender closest to the ball closes the gap on the breakaway dribbler. [S15, p.299]
4. **Second help-side defender = rotation.** The help-side defender nearest the basket is responsible for rotation — covering the layup pass. [S15, p.299]
5. **Two-line rule:** Draw a horizontal line midway up the free-throw lane.
   - **Below the line:** The help-side defender rotates to STOP THE BALL. [S15, p.299]
   - **Above the line:** The help-side defender rotates to THE BASKET to stop the pass for a layup. [S15, p.299]
6. **Rotation must be activated quickly** enough to stop the ball outside the lane before it reaches the basket.
7. **Three calls for the key defender (X4):** When the ball handler beats X1, the help-side defender nearest the ball (X4) calls:
   - **"Hedge"** — X4 believes X1 can recover; all defenders keep their assignments.
   - **"Rotate"** — Full rotation: X4 takes ball handler, X3 covers the layup cutter, X1 covers X3's assignment.
   - **"Trap"** — X4 and X1 trap the ball handler; X3 plays interception stance between both remaining offensive players, shading toward the basket. [S15, p.298]

## Player Responsibilities
- **Ball defender (X1):** Primary stopper; must recover if the hedging call is made; stays on ball handler if trap is called.
- **First help-side defender (X4 / below the line):** Slides across the lane to stop the ball; makes the call (hedge/rotate/trap).
- **Second help-side defender (X3 / above the line):** Rotates immediately to cover the basket area and the cutting player coming for a layup pass.
- **Ball handler (player 1):** Offensively, tries to beat X1 and then read the rotation — passes to the open cutter (player 4) if X3 rotates up.

## Variations
### Hedge (Keep Assignments)
X4 believes X1 will recover. No rotation occurs. All five defenders maintain their man assignments. Used when X1 only lost a half-step.

### Full Rotation (Rotate Call)
X4 slides to stop the ball → X3 drops to cover the basket → X1 sprints back to X3's man. This is the standard rotation. [S15, p.298]

### Trap (Double-Team)
X4 and X1 trap the dribbler in a two-man trap. X3 plays an interception stance between the two remaining offensive players (3 and 4), shading toward 4 near the basket. If the trap is broken by a pass: X3 takes the pass receiver, X1 stays on the ball handler, X4 sprints to cover the open player. [S15, p.298]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Rotating too slowly** → Ball handler scores an uncontested layup. Rotation must activate before the ball enters the lane.
2. **X3 rotating up instead of to the basket** → Player 4 catches a layup pass behind the defense. Always: below the line stops the ball, above the line covers the basket.
3. **X4 hedging when X1 cannot recover** → Gives the ball handler a straight path to the rim. X4 must read X1's recovery angle honestly.
4. **Trap leaving two offensive players for one defender (X3)** → X3 must shade toward the player nearest the basket, not split the difference.

# Close-the-Gap Stance

## Objective
Teach defenders to shift foot pressure from ball-side (for pass interception) to assignment-side (for help) the moment the ball handler begins a drive toward the defender's assignment, closing the gap and enabling a charge draw.

## Setup
- Around the court, groups of 3 at multiple baskets simultaneously
- Each player has a basketball
- Player 1 (X1's assignment), X1 (defender), player 2 (ball handler)
- Rotation: 1 → X1 → 2 → 1; continue 3 minutes

<!-- DIAGRAM 1: Both baskets shown with ①, X1, ② in groups of three on each half. Ball-you-man flat triangle visible. DIAGRAM 2: Footprint diagram showing X1 switching pressure foot as ② drives. -->

## Execution
**Beginner:**
1. Player 1 is guarded by X1. Player 1 passes to player 2 and cuts **three steps**.
2. X1 readjusts to see both player 1 and the ball (ball-you-man principle in a flat triangle).
3. X1 takes an **interception stance** with pressure on the foot nearest the ball; uses fence slides if 1 moves to receive a pass back.
4. Player 2 holds the ball for 2 seconds, then **drives toward player 1**.
5. X1 **switches foot pressure** to the foot nearest the assignment (player 1), opens up, and pivots directly into player 2's dribbling path — closing the gap.
6. If player 2 continues, X1 can draw the charge.

**Options:**
1. Allow player 2 to pass back to player 1 before driving — X1 constantly cycles between ball-you-man coverage and deny-the-wing coverage
2. When player 2 has the ball, player 2 tries to pass back to player 1 before driving — X1 uses full deny-the-wing techniques
3. Player 2 can continue the dribble-drive until X1 actually draws the charge

## Coaching Points
1. **Flat triangle position**: X1 must be **two-thirds the distance from player 2 (ball)** and **one-third from player 1 (assignment)** [S15, p.272]
2. X1 must see both ball and assignment using **peripheral vision** without moving the head
3. **Interception stance pressure** (foot nearest ball): enables faster fence slide and pass interception
4. **Gap-closing pressure** (foot nearest assignment): "This allows X1 to open up and pivot directly into the dribbling path of player 2" [S15, p.273]
5. The only difference between interception stance and closing-the-gap stance is **which foot carries the pressure**
6. On the gap close, X1 is opening a lane to get **in front of the driver** before they arrive — must anticipate the drive early
7. If player 2 continues driving after the gap is closed, X1 should be in legal charge-drawing position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Player 2 drives slowly; focus on foot-pressure switch and pivot
2. **Intermediate**: Player 2 drives at game speed; X1 must time the pressure switch correctly
3. **Advanced**: Player 2 can pass back or drive; X1 cycles between denial and gap-closing in real-time

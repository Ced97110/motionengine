# Power Skips

## Objective
Improve lower-body power and quickness through an aggressive skipping motion that maximizes height and distance with minimal ground contact time. [S8, p.174]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Full court or open space (30–40 yards)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Begin skipping with an aggressive hip extension on each step.
2. Drive the knee up forcefully while pushing off the ground foot with full triple extension (hip, knee, ankle).
3. The goal is to achieve increased distance AND height with each skip.
4. Spend the least amount of time in contact with the ground as possible — think "quick and explosive off the floor."
5. Coordinate arm drive with the opposite leg for maximum power.
6. Continue for the designated distance or time. [S8, p.174]

## Coaching Points
- "Pop off the ground — don't press into it"
- The emphasis is on height and distance per skip, not speed of travel
- Each ground contact should feel like a fast, powerful push — minimize the "loading" phase
- Eyes forward, not looking at the ground
- Keep the torso upright; don't lean excessively forward

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Normal skipping focusing on rhythm and arm drive
2. **Intermediate**: Power skips for height — drive the knee to hip level
3. **Advanced**: Power skips for distance — maximize each stride; add a reaction command at the end (sprint to a cone on coach's signal)

## Concepts Taught
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — the quick ground contact exploits elastic energy for a more powerful push-off
- [[concept-lower-body-plyometrics]] — power skips develop the hip extension strength critical for basketball first-step explosiveness
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — hip extension power from skips transfers directly to sprint acceleration

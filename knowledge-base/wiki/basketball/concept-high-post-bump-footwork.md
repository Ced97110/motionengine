# High-Post Bump Footwork

## Summary
The High-Post bump is an adjustment to the High-Post rub-off screen action where the PG (01) reads that the defense is about to switch (X4 neglecting 04 to help X1), and physically bumps 04 instead of using his screen. This signals 04 to abandon the screen role and flash to the new ballside low post. 01 replaces himself at the top of the key as a perimeter player. The move exploits X4's decision to help defensively rather than honor 04's inside position. [S6, pp.263-265]

## When to Use
- When X4 is spending attention on helping X1 rather than guarding 04 on the screen
- When the offense wants to keep 04 inside and 01 outside based on skill sets
- When defenders try to anticipate and switch the High-Post rub-off screen
- As an automatic counter within the High-Post rub-off action — only two players (01 and 04) need to change their behavior

## Key Principles
1. **Fake the inside cut first**: 01 should first fake an inside cut off 04's presumed screen to draw X4 inward. This cheats X4 into shading inside to help X1 hedge/switch, creating even greater separation for 04's reverse pivot and seal [S6, p.264].
2. **Bump replaces the cut**: Rather than cutting off 04's screen, 01 bumps 04 on the shoulder — the bump is the signal. 04 executes the reverse pivot and 01 pops to the top of the key [S6, p.263].
3. **Reverse pivot mechanics for 04**: Inside foot (right, in this case) = pivot foot. Left foot swings toward the basket on the reverse pivot. 04 seals X4 and slides to the ballside mid-post area, keeping eyes on the ball (02) at all times [S6, p.264].
4. **01 pivots off inside heel on catch**: At the top of the key, 01 is a potential jump shooter or ball-reversal player. He must pivot off the inside heel when catching 05's pass to become an immediate shooter [S6, p.265].
5. **Only two players change**: The three remaining offensive players (02, 03, 05) maintain the same positions and responsibilities as in the original High-Post rub-off. Only 01 and 04 exchange roles [S6, p.263].
6. **Two purposes**: The High-Post bump is used either (a) to counter a defensive switch, or (b) to maintain role clarity (post players inside, guards outside) based on personnel [S6, p.263].

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (01)**: Read X4's attention and X1's coverage; fake inside cut; bump 04's shoulder; pop to the top of the key; be ready to catch and shoot off inside-heel pivot.
- **PF (04)**: Feel the bump (not the cut); execute reverse pivot off inside (right) foot; swing left foot toward basket; seal X4; slide to ballside mid-post; maintain eyes on ball and passer (02); use interior post scoring footwork when receiving the pass.
- **SG (02)**, **SF (03)**, **C (05)**: Maintain original High-Post rub-off positions and responsibilities; 02 is the primary passer reading 04 in the post.

## Common Mistakes
1. **01 does not fake the inside cut first** → Skipping the fake eliminates the X4 deception that creates 04's separation; the bump is much less effective without it.
2. **04 goes to the wrong post area** → After the bump, 04's destination is the NEW ballside mid-post, not the original side; he must read the ball location.
3. **04 does not keep eyes on the passer** → After sealing, 04 must always face the ball and passer (02) to receive the pass at the right moment.
4. **01 stays in the lane after bumping** → 01 must immediately pop back to the top of the key; staying inside creates congestion and removes the spacing that makes 04's post-up work.

# Barbell Hip Thrust (NSCA Basketball)

## Objective
Develop maximal glute and hamstring strength through a full hip-extension range of motion, producing the horizontal and vertical force output required for basketball's sprinting, jumping, cutting, and deceleration.

## Setup
- Stable bench (that will not slide)
- Barbell with appropriate load
- Optional: barbell pad for comfort on the hips
- Primary muscles: gluteus maximus, semimembranosus, semitendinosus, biceps femoris [S11, Ch.6]

## Execution

### Beginning Position
1. Sit on the floor with legs extended, torso leaning against the **long side of a bench** directly behind the back — ensure the bench will not move
2. Roll the bar over and position it **directly above the hips**; flex hips and knees so feet are flat on the floor
3. Adjust torso so **shoulder blades rest on the top edge of the bench**
4. Hold bar with a **closed overhand grip** outside the hips

### Movement Phases
1. Brace the core, drive through the heels, and extend the hips by contracting the glutes to lift the bar off the floor
2. Continue extending hips and knees until the **lower legs are approximately perpendicular to the floor**
3. Return the barbell to the beginning position under control [S11, Ch.6]

**Breathing:** Exhale after the sticking point of the upward movement; inhale during the downward movement

## Coaching Points
- **"Push through the heels"** — not the toes; heel drive maximizes glute activation
- **"Be sure to reach full hip extension"** — squeeze the glutes hard at the top; a partial rep limits glute development
- **"Maintain forward eye gaze"** — prevents excessive neck extension
- Bench must be stable — any bench movement is a safety hazard
- Use a barbell pad to reduce discomfort on the hip bones, especially at higher loads

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight hip thrust using just the bench (no bar); establish full ROM and glute squeeze
2. **Intermediate**: Standard barbell hip thrust — full ROM, controlled tempo
3. **Advanced (Single-Leg Hip Thrust)**: Keep one leg up (knee of free leg fully extended) throughout the movement — doubles the load on the working glute and builds unilateral hip extension strength for single-leg jumping and cutting [S11, Ch.6]

# Horizontal Cable Core (Pallof) Press — Core Rotation/Anti-Rotation

## Objective
Train the core muscles to resist rotational force — the anti-rotation demand that mirrors fighting for rebounds, protecting the ball from stripping defenders, and maintaining defensive posture.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Cable column machine (set to middle/navel height)
- Alternative: rubber tubing anchored at navel height [S2, p.43]
- Optional variations: defensive stance, tall kneeling, half-kneeling positions

## Execution
1. Stand perpendicular to and directly in front of the cable column machine.
2. Assume an athletic stance: feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly flexed, torso upright.
3. Grasp the handle with both hands at navel height, close to the abdomen. Draw in the abdominals and flex the glutes.
4. While maintaining a straight torso, extend (press) the arms directly out in front of the body to shoulder height. This creates a rotational force that the hips and shoulders must resist.
5. **The goal is to resist rotation** — maintain a stable spinal position while holding the press for 1 to 5 seconds.
6. Return to starting position. Perform **2 sets of 10-15 reps per side**. [S2, pp.42-43]

## Coaching Points
- "The pressing motion is easy — resisting the rotation is the exercise" [S2, p.43]
- Watch for players letting the hips or shoulders rotate toward the cable machine when arms are extended — this is the failure point
- Keep the torso rigid and square throughout the press
- **Sport-specific positions**: Coach can vary stance to defensive position, half-kneeling, or tall kneeling to increase demand on core muscles [S2, p.43]
- Narrower foot stance increases lower-extremity demand and makes the exercise harder
- Cue: "Hold the ball" — when fighting for a rebound against a stripper, this is the core strength that wins

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Wide athletic stance, light resistance, short press holds (1 second)
2. **Intermediate**: Standard stance, moderate resistance, 3-5 second holds, 2 sets × 10-15 each side
3. **Advanced — Tubing Pallof Press**: Use rubber tubing; perform in defensive stance, half-kneeling, or narrow stance for sport-specific demands [S2, p.43]

### Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Multifidus, rotators, transversus abdominis, internal oblique, external oblique
- **Secondary**: Rectus abdominis, erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), tensor fasciae latae, adductor magnus, gluteus maximus [S2, p.43]

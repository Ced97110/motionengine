# Youth Basketball Drills, 2nd Edition — Summary

## Overview
*Youth Basketball Drills, Second Edition* by Burrall Paye and Patrick Paye (Human Kinetics) is described by the publisher as "basketball's best and most comprehensive youth drill book." It contains 160 drills organized to develop players from beginners to advanced athletes, skill by skill, in both offense and defense.

Burrall Paye coached for 37 years, winning 64 championships, twice earning State Coach of the Year honors, and being named Outstanding Coach by the National Federation Interscholastic Coaches Association in 1985. Patrick Paye is an assistant coach at the NCAA Division II level with 23 years of high school and collegiate coaching experience.

The book is structured around 19 skill-chapter categories (visible in its table of contents) covering shooting, dribbling, ballhandling, passing, cutting, screening, rebounding, team offense, team defense, balance and agility, and stance and footwork. The Appendix provides a complete practice-organization system including sample 30-minute and 90-minute practice plans for early, mid, and late season.

## Key Themes
1. **Progressive skill development** — Drills are ordered from beginner to advanced within each skill chapter; related drills are cross-listed in each drill's description.
2. **Youth-specific pedagogy** — Short drills (≤10 min), small groups, maximum repetitions, fun/competitive formats, one or two teaching points per drill.
3. **Whole-season planning** — The Appendix provides a complete framework for building practice schedules from preseason through late season.
4. **Motion offense as the offensive system** — The text explicitly references a motion offense with progressive rule-adding across the season (cuts → screens).
5. **Man-to-man defense built progressively** — From 1-on-1 stance/footwork through help-side and trapping as players improve.

## Chapter Breakdown
- **Ch 1–19** (inferred from appendix drill numbers): Shooting, Dribbling/Ballhandling, Pivoting, Stopping, Passing, Cutting, Screening, Rebounding, Team Offense, 1-on-1 Offense, Individual Defense, Team Defense, Balance/Agility, Stance/Footwork — covered by drills numbered 1–158+
- **Appendix** — Practice organization: how to run a successful practice and sample schedules (→ wiki pages: [[concept-youth-basketball-practice-organization]], [[concept-youth-basketball-practice-schedules]])

## Unique Contributions
The Appendix provides unusually concrete sample practice schedules that translate directly into game-day lesson plans for youth coaches. The drill-by-drill time-allocation tables for 30-min and 90-min sessions across three season phases (early, mid, late) are a rare practical resource. The text also explicitly addresses parent-volunteer assistant coaches and sandlot coaches — unusually accessible for community-level programs.

# Pin Screen Footwork

## Summary
A pin screen is an off-ball screen set by a post player (04) on the defender (X3) guarding a perimeter cutter (03) who is moving toward the ball from the opposite side. The screen "pins" the defender inside, freeing the cutter for an open perimeter shot. After setting the screen, the screener immediately reverse pivots to become a second scoring threat at the block. The play creates two simultaneous scoring opportunities and forces difficult defensive choices regardless of whether the defense hedges, switches, or trails. [S6, pp.261-264]

## When to Use
- When a perimeter player (03) cuts across the lane toward the ball from the weak side
- When the post screener (04) has a size or quickness advantage on any defender assigned to cover them
- As a counter when the defense is hedging or switching on other screen actions
- In half-court set plays that require interior + perimeter scoring options

## Key Principles
1. **Cutter setup first**: 03 must use good fakes and a V-cut to set up X3 before using the pin screen — the screen is only effective if the defender is positioned to be pinned [S6, p.263]
2. **Scrape off the lowest shoulder**: The cutter (03) scrapes off 04's lowest (inside) shoulder so the defender cannot recover around the screen [S6, p.262]
3. **Hands and feet ready before the catch**: As 03 comes off the screen, hands should be in position, shoulders turning toward the passer — prepare the pivot BEFORE the ball arrives [S6, p.262]
4. **180° front pivot on the inside heel**: As 03 scrapes off the screen, pivot off the inside heel (right foot) to square up to the basket — so the catch automatically puts 03 in triple-threat [S6, p.262]
5. **Screener reverse pivots immediately**: After the screen is set, 04 reverse pivots off the contact foot (foot closest to defender X4), swinging the free foot to seal the defender on the block [S6, p.263]
6. **Read the defensive adjustment**: If X4 helps baseline → 04 slips to mid-post block. If X4 helps high → 04 reverse pivots with top foot as pivot, gaining low-post room. If X4 switches → 04 has immediate size advantage on X3 [S6, p.263]
7. **Perimeter player reads**: 03 catches and shoots immediately off the catch. If defender closes out, shot fake and pass inside to 04 posted up [S6, p.264]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (01)**: After killing the dribble, pivot to become passer — deliver to 03 (perimeter) or 04 (block) based on defensive positioning; uses proper passer footwork from a live-dribble kill [S6, p.264]
- **SF (03)**: Use V-cut to set X3 up → scrape off 04's inside shoulder → pivot off inside heel → catch in triple threat → read: shoot, drive, or pass inside to 04 [S6, pp.261-262]
- **PF/C (04)**: Set the pin screen → immediately reverse pivot off contact foot → seal defender and post up on the block → read pass from 01 or 03 → use post footwork to score [S6, pp.263-264]
- **SG (02)**: Perimeter spacing — occupy defender, remain ready as safety valve for ball reversal [S6, p.264]

## Variations
### Pin Screen Slip
If X4 is committed to helping on the screen's baseline side, 04 slips the screen early and cuts directly to the mid-post block before X4 can recover — catches in scoring position without setting a legal screen. [S6, p.263]

### Cutter Becomes Passer
If 03 catches and a defender closes out aggressively, 03 shot fakes to freeze the closeout, then delivers a post entry pass to 04 sealed on the block. [S6, p.264]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Cutter not setting up the screen** → Result: X3 senses the pin and fights through easily — correction: always use a V-cut and change of pace to commit the defender before using the screen [S6, p.263]
2. **Cutter not preparing hands and pivot before catch** → Result: slow, off-balance catch that negates the advantage — correction: turn shoulders toward passer and identify pivot foot while still in motion [S6, p.262]
3. **Screener watching the play instead of immediately posting** → Result: post-up opportunity is lost — correction: reverse pivot the moment contact is made, do not wait to see if 03 catches [S6, pp.263-264]
4. **Wrong pivot foot on the reverse** → Result: defender slides between 04 and the basket — correction: always pivot off the foot closest to the defender, swing the free foot to seal [S6, p.263]

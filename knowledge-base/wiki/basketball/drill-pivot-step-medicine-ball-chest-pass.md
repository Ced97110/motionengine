# Pivot Step to Medicine Ball Chest Pass

## Objective
Develop explosive chest-pass power and a quick jab-step pivot footwork pattern to improve passing speed and deceptive ball movement against defenders.

## Setup
- 1 player + wall, or 2 players facing each other
- 1 medicine ball (prescribed weight — start light, typically 4–8 lbs)
- Adequate space to pivot and throw without obstruction
- Stand approximately 6–8 feet from the wall or partner

## Execution
1. Stand erect with feet approximately shoulder-width apart, holding the medicine ball at chest height with elbows bent. Slightly flex hips and knees.
2. **Pivot** on the right foot to the right (rotating away from the target).
3. Immediately **pivot back** (rotating left and forward) to the starting position.
4. At the completion of the return pivot, forcefully extend both arms and throw the medicine ball into the wall or to a partner.
5. As the ball returns, decelerate it slowly back into the chest position.
6. Perform reps in both directions (pivot right, then pivot left) for the prescribed count.

> The pivot creates rotational momentum that adds force to the chest pass — this is the transfer-of-momentum training effect. [S2, p.121]

## Coaching Points
- **"Pivot fast, pass faster"** — the pivot must be explosive to generate trunk torque
- Keep the ball close to the chest during the pivot phase — do not wind up wide
- Extend arms fully through the throw; wrist snap at release
- Decelerate the returning ball under control — eccentric loading is part of the exercise
- Equal reps to both sides — many players are dominant on one side only

## Primary Muscles
- Pectoralis major, pectoralis minor, triceps brachii, anterior deltoid
- **Secondary:** Biceps brachii, latissimus dorsi, rectus abdominis [S2, p.121]

## Basketball Application
Builds a quicker, more explosive chest pass and develops the jab-step pivot footwork used to create passing lanes and avoid defensive pressure. Footwork change of direction combined with upper-body force mirrors the exact motor pattern of pass-faking and threading passes through traffic. [S2, p.121]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Stationary two-hand chest pass against wall — no pivot; focus on arm extension
2. **Intermediate:** Add pivot step before each throw; light medicine ball (4–6 lbs)
3. **Advanced:** Heavier ball, faster pivot, eyes up (look at a target, not the ball)

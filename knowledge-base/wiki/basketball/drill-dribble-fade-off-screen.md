# Dribble Fade Off Screen Drill (Workout #5-4)

## Objective
Train the read and response when a defender slips the screen and closes out at a high angle — establish a dominant pivot foot and attack the open baseline with a dribble fade for any scoring opportunity.

## Setup
- Number of players: 2 (shooter + passer); 1 stationary screener (player or object)
- Court area: wing area, 15–18 feet from basket
- Equipment: basketball
- Passer position: top of the key
- Shooter starting position: underneath the basket

## Execution
1. Shooter starts under the basket and moves to use the stationary screener.
2. Shooter receives the pass from the passer on the wing.
3. **Read the closeout:** The defender (real or imagined) slips the screen and closes out at a **high angle**, leaving the **baseline open**.
4. Shooter establishes a **dominant pivot foot** on the catch.
5. Shooter attacks the baseline with a **dribble fade** — one dribble toward the baseline.
6. Once the baseline is attacked, the shooter finishes with any available scoring opportunity: pull-up midrange, floater, layup, or step-through.
7. Retrieve the ball and repeat.

**Volume:** 5 reps of dribble fade, then 5 FTs. Repeat 5 times for a total of **25 dribble fades + 25 FTs**. [S3, p.138]

## Coaching Points
- **Dominant pivot foot first:** The moment the ball is caught, establish the pivot foot — do not travel by fumbling through the catch.
- **Read the angle:** "The defender chooses to slip the screen and closes out on a high angle, leaving the baseline open." The cutter must see this read and attack it, not force a fadeaway. [S3, p.138]
- **Attack with conviction:** The baseline dribble must be aggressive — "attack the paint" mindset, not a tentative step.
- **Any scoring opportunity:** Once the baseline is attacked, the offense has full freedom — pull-up, runner, layup, or Euro step. Train multiple finishes off the dribble fade.
- **Free throws after each set:** FTs are not rest — they train composure and consistency between physical bursts.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Stationary screener and no defender; shooter practices the pivot-and-dribble timing only.
2. **Intermediate:** Add a live defender who closes out high; shooter must read the angle before initiating the dribble fade.
3. **Advanced:** Defender can vary the closeout angle — shooter must identify when the baseline is open vs. when to fade or curl instead.

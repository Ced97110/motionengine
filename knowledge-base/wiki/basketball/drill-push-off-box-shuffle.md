# Push-Off Box Shuffle

## Objective
Improve lower-body power and quickness by rapidly exchanging feet on a low box, training the ankle complex's elastic response and minimizing ground contact time. [S8, p.177]

## Setup
- 1 player
- A box or step no higher than halfway up the shin
- Open space in front of the box

## Execution
1. Stand facing the box. Place one foot completely on top of the box and lean slightly forward so the shoulders are over the edge of the box.
2. Quickly push off the box with the foot on top, exchanging feet in the air so the other foot lands on the box.
3. The **back foot** (the one that was on the ground) should land on the box with **ankle stiffness** to encourage an immediate elastic rebound response.
4. The goal is to spend **more time in the air** and less time in contact with the box or the ground.
5. Continue for the designated reps or time. [S8, p.177]

## Coaching Points
- "Stiff ankles — don't let the foot sink"
- The landing foot should feel like it's bouncing off a trampoline, not sinking into the surface
- The lean forward keeps the center of mass in the optimal position for quick ground departure
- Arm drive can assist with rhythm and power
- Keep the non-working leg active and ready for quick exchange

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow, controlled step-on/step-off rhythm to establish the movement pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Increase speed while maintaining stiff ankle on landing
3. **Advanced**: Maximum speed with minimal ground contact; add an auditory or visual reaction command to sprint after a set number of exchanges

- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — stiff ankle landing creates an elastic rebound that amplifies push-off power
- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — rapid foot exchanges develop ankle stability and proprioception

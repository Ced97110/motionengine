# Kip-Up (Step 7 of Kip-Up Chain)

## Objective
Execute the classic kip-up: from lying/rolling position, explosively spring to a standing squat on the balls of both feet using leg arc, palm push, and forward torso drive.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat open floor or mat
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Sit on the floor with knees bent and heels on the ground.
2. Roll back and swing legs up while placing hands in the **bridge position** (by ears, fingers pointing toward feet).
3. Roll onto upper-back and shoulders. Squeeze into a tight ball with bent knees close to the head.
4. **"Unravel"** — explode legs in an arc UP then DOWN while pushing DOWN through palms hard enough to lift them off the ground.
5. As feet touch down, **keep torso and hands forward**.
6. **Land on balls of both feet** into a squatting position. Keep head and hands forward until stable.
7. Stand upright.
8. Squat down, roll back, and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- "Impossible without an explosive waist (hips and lower back), super-fast legs and the total-body agility of a panther" [S9, p.8]
- This is a COMPLETE skill — landing in a squat and standing is the full rep; don't stop at the squat
- **Wider stance** (feet slightly wider apart on landing) makes the catch easier for first attempts
- Head and hands staying forward is the balance key — if they drop back, the athlete tips back
- The palm push is the final propulsive assist; as the kip-up is mastered, this contribution can be reduced (pathway to Step 9 and Master Step)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (regression)**: Attempt with wider foot stance on landing
2. **Intermediate**: Standard kip-up with shoulder-width landing
3. **Advanced**: Keep feet together on landing (requires maximum forward momentum) [S9, p.8]
4. **Elite**: Begin lying flat on the floor instead of rolling from sitting — eliminates rolling momentum [S9, p.11]

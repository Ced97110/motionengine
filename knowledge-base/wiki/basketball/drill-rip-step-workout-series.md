# Rip Step Workout Series

## Objective
Develop all eight rip step scoring opportunities from both left and right sides at game speed through progressive, high-volume individual workouts with embedded free throws and tracking.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court (one basket)
- No additional equipment required
- Each workout has a corresponding tracking chart: FGA, FGM, %, 3PTA, 3PTM, %, FTA, FTM, %

## Execution

All workouts follow the same structural pattern:
1. Start at a designated spot (typically the wing or top of the key)
2. Self-pass or spin out the ball to simulate a game catch
3. Catch on a two-foot jump stop
4. Execute the designated rip step move at **game speed**
5. Shoot or finish the scoring opportunity
6. After each set of field goal attempts, shoot the specified number of free throws
7. Record all makes and attempts on the tracking chart

**Key movements practiced (both L and R directions):**
- Stride Stop: rip step + 1 or 2 dribbles → stride stop pull-up
- Stride Stop/Step-Through: rip step + dribble → stride stop → step-through finish
- Big Hop: rip step + dribble → big two-foot hop finish
- Hop Back: rip step + 1 dribble → hop back pull-up or three-pointer
- Hop Back to Roll Out: hop back → roll dribble → shot or finish
- Hop Back to Crossover: hop back → crossover dribble → shot or finish
- Attack Layup: rip step → direct drive → layup
- Attack/Crossover Layup: rip step → drive → crossover finish

## Coaching Points
- **"Step with the rip, every time"** — the step foot must move with the ball rip to establish the pivot foot legally
- **"Game speed or it doesn't count"** — enforce full-speed execution on every rep; slow reps reinforce slow play
- **Both directions matter** — every move is practiced left AND right; weakness on one side will be exploited in games
- **Track everything** — use the workout tracking chart to record FGA, FGM, 3PT, and FT data for each session
- **Benchmark goals** — hold players to the percentage benchmarks listed for each workout (e.g., 60% FG, 85% FT)
- **Free throws between sets simulate game conditions** — shooting FTs while physically engaged builds mental toughness

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Workout #2-3 (layup-only, 55 shots) — focus on footwork and two-foot jump stop catch
2. **Intermediate**: Workout #2-1 (130 shots) — all eight scoring opportunities, moderate volume
3. **Advanced**: Workout #2-2 (260+ shots) — maximum volume, all moves both directions, game-speed 3s at finish

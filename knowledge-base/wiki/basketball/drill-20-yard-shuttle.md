# 20-Yard Shuttle

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction, footwork mechanics, and reaction time through a controlled sprint-touch-return sequence.

## Setup
- 1+ players
- Three parallel lines: start/finish line, a line 5 yards away, and a line 10 yards from start
- No cones required (use court lines) or mark lines with tape/cones

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance straddling the starting line.
2. Turn right, sprint 5 yards (4.6 m) to the first line, and touch it with the right hand.
3. Turn left, sprint 10 yards (9 m) to the far line, and touch it with the left hand.
4. Turn right and sprint 5 yards (4.6 m) back through the start/finish line.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Three-line layout with arrows showing: sprint 5 yards right → sprint 10 yards left → sprint 5 yards back through start. [S8, p.79] -->

## Coaching Points
- Low center of gravity on every direction change — drop the hips, do not stand upright
- Touch line with correct hand (right hand right, left hand left) to reinforce bilateral deceleration
- Drive out of each turn with a powerful arm swing
- Keep eyes up — head should find a new focus point to initiate each turn
- Time the drill for tracking improvement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the pattern first; sprint at 50% pace
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed sprint, timed
3. **Advanced (Complex Variation)**: 20-Yard Combination Agility Drill — perform a different biomotor skill on each leg of the drill (e.g., sprint → carioca → sprint) [S8, p.79]

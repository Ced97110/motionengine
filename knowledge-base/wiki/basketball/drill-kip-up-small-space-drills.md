# Kip-Up Small Space Drills

## Objective
Develop hip-snap speed, waist explosiveness, and shoulder/arm readiness for kip-ups using three zero-equipment solo drills that can be performed rhythmically in any small space as warm-ups, ancillary work, or finishing exercises.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (solo)
- **Space**: Minimal — any flat area roughly 6x6 feet
- **Equipment**: None; a soft/cushioned surface recommended for prone kip
- **Use as**: Warm-up before an explosives session, between kip-up chain sets, or standalone speed work

## Execution

### Drill 1: Bridge Push-Offs
**Purpose**: Conditions the shoulder and arm muscles for the kip-up bridge hand position.

1. Stand a short distance from a sturdy wall, facing *away* from it.
2. Bend back and place hands on the wall; bend arms as much as possible.
3. From this bridge-against-wall position, explosively push away from the wall.
4. Generate enough force to end up standing upright.
5. Reset and repeat for multiple reps.

**Progression**: Move further from the wall to increase demand. Try with one hand for a unilateral challenge. [S9, p.18]

---

### Drill 2: Sitting Kips
**Purpose**: Imparts the hip-snap mechanic essential for all kip-up styles; excellent for restoring explosive speed when fatigued.

1. Kneel down on shins and insteps in a Japanese kneeling position (easier variation: kneeling on the toes instead of insteps).
2. From this low position, explosively throw arms and torso *upward* with a fast snap.
3. Use the brief height gained to whip legs out from under you.
4. Finish in a standing position.
5. Reset to kneeling and repeat rhythmically.

**Coaching Cue**: *"A few reps of this beauty is a great addition to any routine, especially if you feel like you're slowing down."* [S9, p.18]

---

### Drill 3: Prone Kip
**Purpose**: A rare kip-up variant starting face-down; trains explosive hip drive from a completely different starting angle.

1. Lie face down with knees bent; support yourself on palms (or fists).
2. Push down hard and explosively through the hands to gain height.
3. As the body rises, whip legs underneath you.
4. Finish in a standing position.

**Note**: Can be performed from fists or flat palms. [S9, p.19]

---

## Coaching Points
- These drills can be performed rhythmically for higher reps — unlike the progressive chain steps which are done for quality
- Use Sitting Kips specifically when you sense you are losing hip-snap speed during a kip-up session — a few reps restores the feel
- Bridge Push-Offs directly train the arm/shoulder position used in Steps 4-7 of the kip-up chain
- All three drills require zero equipment and minimal space — ideal for warm-up use before practice or games
- The Prone Kip is an unusual training angle; it trains the same hip-drive pattern but from the opposite body orientation [S9, pp.18-19]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Sitting Kips — no snap** → Correction: The arm throw must be explosive — almost a "crack" upward; slow throws won't generate enough height to unload the legs
2. **Bridge Push-Offs — too close to wall** → Correction: Increase distance from wall progressively; if it's too easy you aren't getting training effect
3. **Prone Kip — bending elbows slowly** → Correction: The push must be sudden and maximal — think plyometric, not a controlled press

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bridge Push-Offs close to wall; Sitting Kips on toes (easier than on insteps)
2. **Intermediate**: Bridge Push-Offs further from wall; Sitting Kips on insteps; Prone Kip from palms
3. **Advanced**: Bridge Push-Offs single-handed; combine all three drills in a circuit as a warm-up before the full kip-up chain

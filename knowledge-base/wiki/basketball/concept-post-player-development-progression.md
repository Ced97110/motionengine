# Post Player Development Progression — an iconic post player

## Summary
an iconic post player's three-phase framework for developing post players, with specific coaching guidance for working with young big men. Most post players develop later than their counterparts, and coordination may not match size for several years. Coaches must be patient and systematic, building from the ground up rather than rushing to advanced moves. [S7, pp.49-50]

## When to Use
- When designing long-term development plans for C and PF players
- When introducing post play to a young or inexperienced big player
- When a post player is adding new moves to their repertoire

## Key Principles
1. **Be patient** — post players develop later; coordination catches up to size over time.
2. **Teach proper footwork first** — footwork is the foundation on which all other post progress is made.
3. **Progress phase by phase** — do not skip from crawling to running.
4. **Use video** — contrast earlier footage with recent improvements to prove the value of development and build confidence.
5. **Positive coaching is critical for big players** — young post players are often less confident due to social challenges of being physically different. Emphasize progress and what the player does well above all corrections.

## Player Responsibilities
- **C/PF**: Commit to the process; master each phase before advancing.

## Variations

### Phase 1 — Crawling: Getting Open
*Focus: Footwork and positioning to receive the ball.*
- Learn how to get open against three-quarter, fronting, and face-to-face defense.
- Master the reverse, step-over, duck-in, baseline cut, and ball reversal techniques.
- The post should not be working on post moves until getting open is no longer a problem.

### Phase 2 — Walking: Primary Moves and Shots
*Focus: Core back-to-basket and face-up moves.*
- Drop step (baseline and middle)
- Front pivot and reverse pivot
- Basic shots: power move, jump shot, hook, up-and-under
- Practice both facing the basket and with the back to it, diligently.

### Phase 3 — Running: Countermoves, Advanced Moves, Go-To Move
*Focus: Refinement and decision-making.*
- Countermoves to each primary action
- Advanced moves (jump stop, power slide, spin move)
- Passing out of double teams
- Mastering a go-to move — the move that works in all situations

## Common Mistakes
1. **Teaching advanced moves before footwork is solid** → Foundation is missing; moves fall apart under pressure.
2. **Rushing development because the player is physically gifted** → Physical gifts mask technique deficiencies that emerge against better competition.
3. **Correcting without praising** → Young big players need confidence. "The coach must praise and reinforce the things players do well."

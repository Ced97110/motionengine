# Wood Chop

## Objective
Develop rotational core power and oblique strength by pulling a cable diagonally across the body — simulating the trunk rotation demands of driving, rebounding, and passing through contact in basketball.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Cable machine with adjustable column
- Handle attachment set to a high position (above inside shoulder)
- Stand perpendicular to the cable column

## Execution
1. Stand with shoulders perpendicular to the cable column, feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Hips and knees slightly flexed; torso upright, chest up, shoulders down and back.
3. Reach up diagonally with both arms — the outside arm crosses the body — and grip the cable handle above the inside shoulder (beginning position).
4. With arms fully extended, pull the cable handle down in a diagonal pattern to just below the outside hip by rotating the upper spine and shoulders. **The hips and lower back stay neutral throughout the entire movement.**
5. Pause 1–2 seconds at the final rotated position.
6. Reverse the movement under control, allowing shoulders and upper spine to rotate back to the beginning position. Hips and lower back remain neutral throughout.
7. Complete all prescribed repetitions on one side, then switch sides.

## Coaching Points
- The rotation comes from the upper spine and shoulders — the hips and lower back do NOT rotate.
- Cue: "Rotate your shoulders but keep your hips square."
- Once the athlete is comfortable with the movement pattern, cue faster concentric rotation away from the column — but always control the return.
- For safety, always have the athlete control the cable back to the beginning position rather than allowing it to snap back.
- Exhale forcefully when rotating away from the cable column; inhale when returning.

## Progressions / Variations
1. **Low-to-High Chop**: Set the cable to just below hip height — the chop starts low and moves diagonally upward. Same rotation mechanics.
2. **Horizontal Chop**: Set the cable to standing shoulder height — chop horizontally across the body while standing upright.
3. **Kneeling Angled Chop**: Set to shoulder height and perform the angled chop in a tall kneeling position — eliminates lower-body contribution and isolates trunk rotation.

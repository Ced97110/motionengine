# Deadbug

## Objective
Develop deep core stability (transverse abdominis, rectus abdominis, internal/external obliques) by training contralateral limb extension while maintaining full lumbar spine contact with the floor — the foundational anti-extension core drill.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor or exercise mat
- 1 stability ball (medium size, fits between hands and knees at 90° hip/knee flexion)

## Execution
1. Lie face up on the floor with knees and hips flexed at 90 degrees. Place arms straight out in front of the body and hold a stability ball in place with the hands and knees — beginning position [S11, Ch.8].
2. Begin with a full exhalation and apply pressure with both hands and knees against the ball to engage the abdominal musculature.
3. While keeping the **entire back (neck to lower spine) in contact with the floor**, apply light pressure with the knees and hands against the ball.
4. Move **contralateral limbs** (e.g., right leg and left arm) simultaneously in a controlled manner until both reach full extension a few inches (5–8 cm) off the floor. The other hand and knee should **actively press into the ball** to provide isometric core stability.
5. Inhale during the contralateral limb extension.
6. Hold the end position for a moment, then return the contralateral limbs back to the ball.
7. Exhale as the contralateral limbs return to the beginning position.
8. Alternate sides for the prescribed repetitions [S11, Ch.8].

## Coaching Points
- "Check to see if the lumbar spine is in contact with the floor during the initial setup. If there is a gap, slide a hand under the low back and cue the athlete to tilt the hips anteriorly (tuck the buttocks under) to get the lumbar spine in contact with the floor" [S11, Ch.8]
- The stability ball pressing provides biofeedback — if the ball moves, core activation was lost
- If the athlete cannot maintain lumbar contact during limb movement, regress to a static hold of the beginning position [S11, Ch.8]
- Do not rush through the movement — slow, controlled limb extension maximizes deep stabilizer recruitment

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regressed)**: Hold the beginning position (90/90 hip/knee) with the stability ball pressed between hands and knees for a timed isometric hold
2. **Intermediate**: Full deadbug with stability ball — contralateral limb extension as described above
3. **Advanced**: Remove the stability ball — arms extend overhead and legs extend simultaneously to 2 inches off the floor; much greater demand on the deep stabilizers

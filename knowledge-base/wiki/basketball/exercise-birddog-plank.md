# Birddog Plank

## Objective
Develop anti-rotation core stability and gluteal activation (transversus abdominis, rectus abdominis, multifidus, erector spinae, gluteus maximus) through a contralateral arm-leg extension performed from a full front plank position.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor or exercise mat
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Begin in the front plank position: face down, feet hip-width apart, toes pointed down, legs and hips fully extended, elbows at 90° directly under shoulders, forearms on the floor, hands in fists with palms facing each other [S11, Ch.8].
2. Exhale completely and engage the abdominal musculature.
3. Lift the **contralateral arm and leg** (e.g., right arm and left leg) simultaneously off the floor until:
   - The right arm is fully extended above the head and level with the shoulder
   - The left leg is fully extended (glute muscles contract)
4. Inhale as the limbs lift. Hold the position for the prescribed time.
5. Return to the beginning position and repeat with the other arm and leg.
6. Continue alternating for the prescribed repetitions [S11, Ch.8].

## Coaching Points
- "Keep the hips level with the floor" as the leg lifts — it is common for the hips to rotate toward the floor on the lifting side [S11, Ch.8]
- "Make sure the athlete is not going into lumbar extension as the leg is lifted" — if the low back arches, reduce the leg lift range [S11, Ch.8]
- During the isometric hold, use deep and controlled inhalations and exhalations
- Watch for the arm reaching across (horizontal abduction) instead of straight ahead — it should align with the shoulder, not cross the midline

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regressed)**: Quadruped position (on hands and knees) — shorter lever, more stability, easier base of support [S11, Ch.8]
2. **Intermediate**: Full birddog plank with isometric hold at the top (2–3 seconds per rep)
3. **Advanced**: Dynamic variation — alternate rapidly between left and right without pausing, maintaining a rigid torso [S11, Ch.8]

# Banana (Core Flexion)

## Objective
Build inner core stability in a fully extended body position, simulating the trunk demands of jumping, rebounding, and contesting shots overhead.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (gym or weight room)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Lie supine (on your back) on the floor with legs together and arms extended overhead.
2. Pull the navel in toward the spine — this "drawing-in" activates the transversus abdominis (inner core).
3. Keeping the head between the arms, slowly raise the upper body and legs off the floor, forming a gentle banana-like curve.
4. Hold for a slow count of 30. **Do not hold your breath** — keep breathing steady and even.
5. Concentrate on holding in the abs and not letting the lower back arch away from the floor.
6. Slowly return to a flat supine position with arms, head, and legs resting comfortably.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.34-35]

## Coaching Points
- **"Navel to spine"** — cue the draw-in before lifting; without it the exercise targets the wrong muscles
- Watch for lower back arching off the floor — if it lifts, the player's core is not strong enough to hold position; regress the hold duration
- Head stays between the arms throughout — no chin jutting forward
- Breathing must remain steady; holding breath defeats the purpose and spikes blood pressure
- "This looks easy — it is not" — set player expectations before the first attempt

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hold for 15 counts; reduce duration as needed to maintain proper form
2. **Intermediate**: Full 30-count hold for multiple reps; ensure lower back stays on the floor
3. **Advanced — Rock and Roll**: Gently rock back and forth from head to toe while holding the banana position, increasing demands on the anterior chain (rectus abdominis and transversus abdominis). Alternatively, roll a quarter turn to the side and hold, which engages the internal and external obliques [S2, p.35]

### Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Rectus abdominis, transversus abdominis
- **Secondary**: Iliopsoas, sartorius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius), latissimus dorsi, sternocleidomastoid [S2, p.34]

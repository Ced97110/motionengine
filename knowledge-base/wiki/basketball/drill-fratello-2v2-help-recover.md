# Fratello 2-on-2 Help-and-Recover Drill

## Objective
Teach two defenders to communicate and execute the help-and-recover rotation when the ball is driven to the baseline or into the lane from the wing.

## Setup
- 2 offensive players, 2 defenders
- 1 (ball) at the wing area or top of the lane; 2 on the opposite wing
- X1 covers 1; X2 helps and recovers on the baseline side
- Half-court

## Execution
1. 1 drives to the baseline or into the lane.
2. As soon as 1 drives, 2 slides to the corner to receive the pass.
3. X1 stays with 1's drive as long as possible — applies on-ball pressure.
4. X2 drops to help, cutting off 1's path while sliding toward the corner to deny the pass to 2.
5. X1 and X2 must communicate verbally: "Help!" / "I've got the corner!"
6. Defense's goal: **not let the ball go to the baseline**, and not allow 2 to receive the pass in the corner.
7. Play is live until the offense scores or the defense secures the ball.

## Coaching Points
- X2 must "help and recover" — he helps stop 1, then immediately sprints back to 2 in the corner.
- Communication is non-negotiable: "X1 and X2 must communicate and not let the ball go to the baseline." [S7, p.238]
- X2 cannot fully leave 2 — must keep peripheral vision on the corner while stunting.
- If X2 over-helps, 2 is open in the corner for a pass and easy shot.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through — 1 drives in slow motion; X2 shows the help position only.
2. **Intermediate**: As described; live but at 75% speed.
3. **Advanced**: Full live 2v2; include a third offensive player in the corner to test full rotation.

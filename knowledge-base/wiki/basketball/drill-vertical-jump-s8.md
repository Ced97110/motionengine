# Vertical Jump

## Objective
Improve quickness and explosive power in the lower body — the foundational plyometric exercise for basketball rebounding, shot-blocking, and rim finishing.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space or near a wall/Vertec for measurement
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Stand with feet **shoulder-width apart** — knees and hips flexed in a **pre-stretched position**, arms back, shoulders over the toes.
2. **Quickly dip into the power position** — load the stretch.
3. **Perform the vertical jump** by sequentially extending: ankles → knees → hips.
4. **Reach the arms straight up into the air** at peak height to maximize reach.
5. Land softly on the balls of the feet with slight knee bend.

## Coaching Points
- "Dip and drive" — the quick dip creates the pre-stretch; the drive is the explosive extension.
- Arms should reach fully overhead at peak — every inch of arm extension adds to functional reach.
- Sequential joint extension is key — do not skip the ankle extension (toe-point at peak).
- Shoulders should be **over the toes** at the start, not leaning backward.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard vertical jump, focus on full triple extension and arm reach.
2. **Intermediate**: Maximum height with a measured target (Vertec or wall marker).
3. **Advanced — Reactive Variation**: Upon landing, immediately **reload and perform another vertical jump** — spend as little time on the ground as possible. This reactive variation is the true plyometric stimulus.

# Face Pull (Basketball Strength Training)

## Objective
Strengthen the posterior deltoids, trapezius, and rhomboids to improve shoulder health, scapular retraction, and postural stability — critical for basketball players who spend significant time in forward-flexed athletic positions.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: High-pulley cable machine with rope handle
- **Players**: 1 athlete
- **Location**: Weight room

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Stand facing the high-pulley cable machine. Grasp the rope handle with a closed, pronated grip (palms facing the floor). Step back until the cable is taut with arms **fully extended out in front of the face**. Point elbows out to the sides. Head in line with the spine; torso fully erect.
2. **Phase 1**: Squeeze the shoulder blades together and pull the handle toward the face.
3. **Phase 2**: When the upper arms are aligned with the shoulders, continue the backward movement — in **one smooth motion** — by pulling only with the hands (keeping upper arms stationary) until hands finish **close to the ears** and the center of the handle finishes close to the forehead.
4. Return to the beginning position under control.

## Coaching Points
- Do **not round the upper back** — especially when the arms are fully extended out in front
- Keep the **head, torso, and body in the same position** throughout the entire exercise
- The transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 should be one continuous, smooth movement — not two separate actions
- Think: "elbows lead first, then hands pull to ears"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Seated on a bench perpendicular to the machine — removes lower-body stability demand, teaches the pull pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Standing with full erect torso as described
3. **Advanced**: Single-arm variation with a handle; increased load with pause at end range

## Variations
- **Seated face pull**: Sit on a bench perpendicular to the machine for added stability

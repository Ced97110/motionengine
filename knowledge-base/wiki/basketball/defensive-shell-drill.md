# Half-Court Shell Drill (4-on-4-on-4)

## Objective
Teach all components of great man-to-man defense in a competitive team setting: helping teammates, ball-side awareness, weak-side positioning, screen navigation, post defense, and winning a game of stops. Run almost daily throughout the season. [S1, pp.61-66]

## Setup
- Half court
- 12 players in 3 teams of 4
- One team on offense, one on defense, one waiting
- A team can only score by getting **defensive stops**
- Coach can serve as passer/outlet to keep the drill flowing (optional)

## Execution — Game of Stops
- The defensive team must get stops (no basket AND no offensive rebound allowed) to score a point
- If the offense gets an offensive rebound, the defensive team loses that stop even if the offense doesn't score on the possession
- Teams can remain on defense as long as they get stops, or you can require 2-3 consecutive stops before rotation
- Rotate: defense → offense; waiting team → defense
- "We believe that this drill should be used almost daily throughout the season." [S1, p.62]

## Shell Drill Segments (Teaching Progressions)
Break down into these segments before playing the competitive game of stops [S1, pp.62-66]:

### 1. Support — Ball Movement (Figure 5.26)
- Pass the ball overhead every 2 seconds around the perimeter
- Defenders must move **on the catch**, not on the pass
- Teach defenders to always be level and even with the ball and the basket on the weak side

### 2. Guard-to-Guard Entry with Weak-Side Exchange (Figure 5.29)
- 1's dribble is live → X2 is in a help (not deny) position
- On the weak-side down screen: X1 steps back and lets X3 slide in front of him on the guard-to-forward interchange

### 3. Guard-to-Forward Entry; Strong-Side Guard Screens Away (Figure 5.30)
- Guard screens away and exchanges with weak-side guard
- X1 steps back and lets X3 slide through on the screen exchange

### 4. Guard-to-Forward Entry; Strong-Side Guard Cuts Through (Figure 5.31)
- Strong-side guard cuts to opposite wing; weak-side guard replaces to swing the ball
- Weak-side forward pins down for the original passer on the ball reversal
- X1 slides up and through the screen set by 4 because the ball is on the other side of the court (help position)
- Rule: if 2 had the ball, X1 would **lock and trail** on 1's hip and outside shoulder (one pass away from the ball)

### 5. Guard-to-Forward Entry; Guard Buries in Corner; Slice Cut (Figure 5.32)
- Guard buries in strong-side corner, setting up a slice cut by the strong-side wing on ball reversal
- X1 follows to the corner after getting level with the ball on the first pass
- On return pass from 3 to 1, X3 steps to the ball, riding and taking away 3's inside cut
- X2 and X4 are drawn to the ball to provide weak-side help

### 6. Guard-to-Forward Entry; Weak-Side Flash Post (Figure 5.33)
- Blind pig action: weak-side forward flashes to pinch post; plays 2-man action with weak-side guard
- X4 calls out the pinch post flash and permits X2 to slide through OR go below 4
- If 3 has ball and 4 sets flare screen for 2: X2 fights over the screen as X4 helps and prevents 4 from slipping to the basket
- Option: predetermine to trap X2 and X4 on the pinch post action

### 7. Guard-to-Forward Entry; Strong-Side Guard Sets Cross Screen (Figure 5.34)
- Strong-side guard sets low cross screen for weak-side forward to cross and post up
- X4 bodies up to force 4 from low to high on his cut
- X1 follows the screener (1) and provides help by slowing 4 on the cut, OR rides 1 out of bounds
- Pick-the-picker: 2 screens down for 1; X2 tries to stand 2 up and let X1 through OR X2/X1 switch

### 8. Guard-to-Forward Entry; Hand Back and Slice Cut (Figure 5.35)
- Strong-side forward hands back to guard; weak-side forward slices to strong-side post
- X3 steps back and lets X1 slide through to cover 1, OR X3 and X1 switch, OR trap the hand back
- X4 bodies up to 4 and forces him low to high on his cut across the lane

### 9. Help and Recover — Dribble Penetration (Figure 5.36)
- Perimeter players drive between two defenders to train closeouts and stopping penetration
- **Baseline drive rotation** (Figure 5.37): 4 drives baseline and beats X4; X3 (drawn to ball on dribble) crosses lane from weak side to double-team 4 with X4; weak-side X1 and X2 rotate clockwise to protect the basket (box) and foul line/lane (plug/nail position)

### 10. Wing Pick-and-Roll Defense (Figure 5.38)
- Guard-to-forward dribble out entry; weak-side forward crosses lane to set wing screen-and-roll with strong-side guard
- X4 follows across the floor and contact shows to stop PnR penetration as X1 fights over the screen
- X4 must recover to the screener unless scheme calls for big-to-big prerotation on the wing PnR

### 11. Dribble Handoff (DHO) with Guard-to-Forward Exchange (Figure 5.39)
- Strong-side guard-to-forward DHO with weak-side guard-to-forward exchange simultaneously
- X1 steps back and lets X3 slide in front on the DHO between 1 and 3
- X2 steps back and lets X4 slide through on the weak-side interchange

### 12. Guard-to-Guard Entry; Passer Cuts Diagonally to Opposite Baseline (Figure 5.40)
- 1 passes to 2 and cuts diagonally through the lane to the opposite baseline
- X1 must move on the pass and be prepared to cover 1 on the cut from weak to strong side as ball is passed 2→4
- This action (cutting a guard off the high post) is used in Blast, High-Post, and Hawk offenses

## Coaching Points
- "This drill teaches players many things about how to play great man-to-man defense. First it teaches players to help their teammates." [S1, p.62]
- "The dribble or the pass always has to draw weak-side defenders toward the ball" [S1, p.62]
- Move on the **catch**, not on the pass
- Offensive rebounding ends a stop — rebound with ALL FOUR defensive players
- Modify the drill segments to match whatever offensive sets your opponents run

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Segment 1 (support/ball movement) only — establish positioning habits
2. **Intermediate**: Segments 1-5 in sequence before competitive game
3. **Advanced**: Full competitive game of stops with all 12 segments; require 2-3 consecutive stops before rotation

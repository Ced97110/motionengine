# San Antonio Spurs Early Offense — Guard Through, Cross Screen & High Post Dive

## Overview
These three actions are secondary variations in Gregg Popovich's San Antonio Spurs early-offense system, all sharing the same transition footprint as the [[play-san-antonio-early-offense-corner-fill]]. They are designed to give the offense different reads based on how the defense reacts to the primary corner-fill action. All actions can be run to either side of the floor [S14, pp.6–7].

## Formation
Identical to the Corner Fill transition structure:
- **1 (PG)**: Ball handler
- **2 / 3**: Wing fillers (interchangeable left/right)
- **4**: First big player down to the offensive end
- **5**: Second big player (trailer)

## Phases

### Variation A: Guard Through (Left Side)
When the offense is initiated on the **left side** [S14, p.6]:

**Phase 1 — Entry**
- 1 passes ahead to 2 on the left wing
- 1 cuts **through** to the **weak-side corner** (right corner)
- 4 (first big down) cuts to the front of the rim and then to the **ball-side (left) low-post** position
- Instead of holding at the high post, **5 sets a screen for 3** to bring 3 up to the **top of the key**

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.6a — 1 passes to 2 (left wing), cuts through to right corner; 4 runs through rim to left low block; 5 screens for 3 to bring 3 to the top. -->

**Phase 2 — First Reads**
- **Option 1**: 2 makes a low post feed to 4 on the left block
- **Option 2**: 2 passes to 3 (now at the top) for a shot, or 3 makes a **high-low feed** to 4 on the left block

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.6b — 2 can feed 4 directly, or pass to 3 at top for high-low. -->

**Phase 3 — Ball Reversal**
- If the high-low pass is denied, 3 **reverses the ball to 1**, who slides up from the weak-side corner
- 5 dives hard to the **right box**
- 1 makes the **post feed to 5** on the right block

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.7 — 3 reverses to 1 in corner; 5 dives to right block; 1 feeds 5. -->

### Variation B: Through with Cross Screen
A specific action off the guard-through structure that requires a **call** to alert all five players [S14, p.6]:

**Phase 1 — Entry and Setup**
- 1 hits 2 and begins a **through cut** toward the weak side
- 5 cuts to the **ball-side low post**; 4 **holds at the high post**
- 2 passes to 4 at the high post; 4 reverses to 3 on the wing

**Phase 2 — Cross Screen**
- 1 quickly cuts **back across the lane** to set a **cross screen for 5**
- 3 makes a **post feed to 5**, who has used 1's cross screen to gain low-post position

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig 1.8 — 1 cuts through then re-cuts across to screen for 5; 3 feeds 5 off the cross screen. -->

> *Coaching note: This action will likely require a specific call to execute properly, as 1's re-cut across the lane is counter-intuitive without advance communication* [S14, p.6].

### Variation C: High Post Dive Action
An alternative early-offense initiator when the wing pass is unavailable [S14, p.6]:

**Phase 1 — Dribble Drive at High Post**
- Players fill the same positions as above
- Instead of passing to or dribbling at the wing, **1 drives the ball at the high-post player (4)**
- As 1 dribbles toward the top, **4 dives to the left box**, trying to establish low-post position

**Key Read**: 1 attacks the high post with a dribble, which cues 4 to vacate the high post and dive to the low block — 1 then looks to deliver the post feed immediately as 4 establishes position.

<!-- DIAGRAM: referenced as Fig beginning of high-post dive section — 1 dribbles at 4; 4 dives to left block; 1 reads the post feed. -->

## Key Coaching Points
- **All variations run from the same transition footprint** — the defense cannot predict which variation is coming from the initial transition setup.
- **Guard through is a deception tool** — 1 cutting through rather than filling a corner changes spacing and defensive responsibilities.
- **Cross screen requires communication** — build a call for this; 1's back-cut across the lane will be confused with a basket cut if not pre-communicated.
- **High post dive timing** — the dive must happen as 1's dribble moves toward the top, not after; teach 4 to read the dribble direction as the trigger.
- **All actions run to either side** — the left-side examples mirror exactly to the right side; 2 and 3 roles are interchangeable.
- **Point guard drives the system** — "the focus and continuity of the Spurs' early offense are dictated by the action of the point guard and movement of the ball" [S14, p.1].

## Counters
- If 5's screen for 3 (Guard Through) is switched: 5 re-seals on the switch for an interior mismatch pass from the top
- If 4 is denied on the cross screen: 1 (the screener) may be open rolling to the basket after setting the cross screen
- If the defense reads the high-post dive: 1 can abort the drive and use 4 as a ball screen at the high post (converting to the corner PnR action from the Corner Fill system)

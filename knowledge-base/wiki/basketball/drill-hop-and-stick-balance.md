# Hop-and-Stick Balance Drills

## Objective
Increase the ability to maintain balance in dynamic movement situations — directly simulating the landing and stabilization demands of basketball cuts, layups, and defensive slides. [S8, p.134]

## Setup
- 1 player (solo drill)
- Open space: 10–15 yards (9–14 meters) of flat surface or court
- No equipment required; optional: coach or training partner to give verbal commands

## Execution

### Drill 109A: Forward Hop and Stick
1. Hop forward continuously for 10 yards (9 meters).
2. On every **other** landing, immediately freeze and hold the position ("stick").
3. Maintain single-leg balance on the landing foot; do not let the trail foot touch the ground during the hold.

### Drill 109B: Lateral Hop and Stick
1. Hop laterally for 10 yards (9 meters).
2. On every **third** landing, immediately freeze and hold.
3. Keep hips square and core braced during the stick.

### Drill 109C: Medial Hop and Stick
1. Hop medially (toward the midline) for 10 yards (9 meters).
2. Stick and hold on a **verbal command** from the coach or partner.
3. React to the command — do not anticipate.

### Drill 109D: Forward Side-to-Side Hop and Stick
1. Hop forward while alternating laterally (side-to-side pattern) for 10 yards (9 meters).
2. On every **third** landing, immediately freeze and hold.

## Coaching Points
- "Stick it like you mean it" — the hold must be a controlled freeze, not a wobble
- Keep center of mass over the base of support during every stick
- Land softly — heel-to-toe absorption, not a flat-footed thud
- **Head turning**: add "shake your head no" during all movement to increase vestibular difficulty and better simulate game demands [S8, p.134]
- Coach should vary the stick command (every one, every other, every third, or random) to develop reactive balance
- Watch for knee caving inward (dynamic valgus) on landings — cue "knee over second toe"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Forward hop and stick every other landing; no head movement; no verbal commands
2. **Intermediate**: Lateral and medial hop variations; add head turning; stick on every third landing
3. **Advanced**: Forward side-to-side plus all variations at game speed; stick on random verbal command; use a Crazy Z-ball or uneven surface for unstable landing challenges

- [[quickness-and-reaction-time-training]] — reactive balance (sticking on command) trains recognition reaction and movement time
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — the hop-and-absorb pattern uses the SSC to build elastic strength

# Training for Speed, Agility, and Quickness — Summary

## Overview
*Training for Speed, Agility, and Quickness* (2nd edition), edited by Lee E. Brown (EdD, CSCS*D, FACSM) and Vance A. Ferrigno (CSCS*D), is a comprehensive athletic development resource published by Human Kinetics (Champaign, IL, 2005). The book presents 195 numbered drills organized into five physical quality categories — Speed, Agility, Balance, Quickness, and Reaction Time — plus sport-specific sample programs for basketball, soccer/lacrosse, and other sports. Each drill includes an emphasis, difficulty level, and equipment list.

The book's foundational philosophy is rooted in the General Adaptation Syndrome (GAS/supercompensation model), the strength-before-power periodization principle, Al Vermeil's four-tier athletic hierarchy (Strength → Power → Elastic/Reactive Strength → Speed), and the distinction between skill and athleticism. The training system addresses the full spectrum from basic running mechanics to advanced Olympic lifts, plyometrics, and reactive agility drills with unpredictable stimuli.

The book serves as both a drill encyclopedia and a periodized programming guide, with customizable 6-week program templates organized by need (speed, agility, quickness, or combinations) and weekly drill progressions.

## Key Themes
1. **Five physical quality pillars**: Speed, Agility, Balance, Quickness, Reaction Time — each trained with specific drills and progressions.
2. **Periodized programming**: 6-week sample programs that progress from isolated qualities to combined attributes (e.g., speed+agility+quickness together in weeks 4-6).
3. **Drill taxonomy by level**: Every drill classified as Basic, Intermediate, or Advanced with equipment requirements specified.
4. **Reactive and open-loop agility**: Advanced drills incorporate unpredictable stimuli (partner mirrors, ball drops, commands) to bridge the gap between closed agility drills and real game chaos.
5. **Sport-specific application**: Dedicated sample programs for basketball, soccer/lacrosse, and other sports with goals and 6-week drill progressions.

## Chapter Breakdown
- **Ch 1: Introduction / Foundations** — GAS model, strength-before-power periodization, Al Vermeil's hierarchy, skill vs. athleticism distinction (→ wiki pages: [[general-adaptation-syndrome-basketball]], [[strength-before-power-periodization]], [[basketball-physical-qualities-hierarchy]], [[skill-vs-athleticism-basketball]])
- **Ch 2: Speed** — 50 speed drills covering running mechanics, acceleration, stride length, stride frequency, resisted/assisted running, and top-end speed (→ wiki pages: [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]], [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]])
- **Ch 4: Agility** — 55 agility drills covering direction change, body position transitions, cone patterns, ladder work, tumbling, and sport-specific cutting (→ wiki pages: [[concept-agility-training]])
- **Ch 5: Balance** — 5 balance drills for single-leg static and dynamic stability with progressive load (→ wiki pages: [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]])
- **Ch 6: Quickness** — 65 quickness drills spanning lower-body power, medicine ball, upper-body explosive actions, ladder patterns, and reaction integration (→ wiki pages: [[rate-of-force-development-basketball]], [[concept-lower-body-plyometrics]])
- **Reaction Time chapter** — 30 reaction drills adding unpredictable visual/partner stimuli to movement patterns (→ wiki pages: [[concept-reaction-time-basketball]])
- **Sample Programs** — Basketball, Soccer/Lacrosse, and customizable 6-week templates organizing drills by need category

## Unique Contributions
- The most exhaustive drill catalog in the wiki (195 drills) organized by physical quality, level, and equipment — a complete practitioner's menu.
- Drill Finder index (pp.241-251) enables coaches to rapidly locate drills by name, number, emphasis, level, and equipment.
- Reaction time chapter distinguishes between *programmed agility* (closed loop, predictable) and *reactive agility* (open loop, unpredictable) — critical for sport transfer.
- Sport-specific 6-week templates demonstrate how to blend multiple physical qualities across a training block.
- Medicine ball upper-body quickness drills (15+ variations) are uniquely detailed for basketball pushing/passing power.

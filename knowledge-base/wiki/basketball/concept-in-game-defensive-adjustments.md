# In-Game Defensive Adjustments

## Summary
Del Harris identifies three strategic elements that drive in-game adjustments: **momentum** (positive/negative/zero), **game pace** (possessions per minute), and **matchup changes**. Coaches who rely only on intuition are at a disadvantage; systematic charting provides objective evidence that separates real trends from noise. Defensive adjustments — changing from man to zone, trapping a key player, or switching press type — are generally more effective than offensive adjustments at shifting momentum. [S7, pp.262-265]

## When to Use
- Any time the opponent scores on 4 consecutive possessions (alert threshold)
- Any time your team fails to score on 4 consecutive possessions
- At halftime when reviewing possession charts
- When matchups are causing repeated breakdowns

## Key Principles
1. **Three momentum phases**: Positive (stick with your plan), Zero/up-for-grabs (stimulate with a play call or substitution), Negative (change matchups, lineup, or scheme — likely defense first).
2. **Don't trust feel alone**: "We've found it's best not to trust 'feel' for momentum and pace unequivocally." Use a possession chart. [S7, p.263]
3. **Pace benchmark**: >1.75 possessions per minute = fast pace; <1.75 = slow pace. A possession doesn't end until the ball is surrendered (offensive rebounds and knocked-out balls are same possession).
4. **Defensive changes shift momentum best**: Zone ↔ man conversions, type-of-press changes, and trapping a key player "can potentially bother the opponent's offense while stimulating ours." [S7, p.262]
5. **Subtle beats dramatic**: A subtle adjustment is easier to execute and less confusing than an overhaul. Don't panic, but don't be stubborn.

## The Possession Chart

### Simple Chart (Table 16.1)
- Number each possession 1-21 for each team
- Circle the possession number if the team scores; put an X through it if no score
- Chart keeper alerts coach when either team has scored (or failed to score) on 4 possessions in a row

### Complex Chart (Table 16.2)
Each row captures:
- **HBO** (How Ball Obtained): jump ball, steal, made FG, offensive rebound, etc.
- **FGA-FG**: shooter's number (circled if scored)
- **FTA-FT**: free throw attempts/makes
- **HBL** (How Ball Lost): travel, turnover, etc.
- **Play action**: name of the set used
- **Assist**: assisting player's number
- **FB/SEC**: fast break or secondary break
- **Comments**: defensive changes, substitutions, time/score, key moments

## Key In-Game Charts
- **PnR chart**: Location (top, wing, corner, elbow, high-wing, transition) and outcome — target <1.0 PPP defensively on PnR
- **Man-to-man vs. zone chart**: Possessions and points allowed in each defense type; also tracks combo defenses (box-and-one, triangle-and-two, press) and mid-possession shifts
- **Low-post defense chart**: Entries into the low post; outcome (trap/dig/1v1); PPP — determines whether to increase or decrease post trapping
- **Fast-break chart**: Transition defense stops; fast-break opportunities generated and conversion rate
- **Penetration chart**: Number of live-ball penetrations allowed in the frontcourt; triggers zone consideration or matchup changes for troubled on-ball defenders

## Matchup Changes
- Going "small" increases tempo and defensive pressure; going "big" enables post-up offense and physical defense
- Zone lineups may differ from man lineups; press lineups may differ from zone lineups
- "Going small on a big man works well; going big on a small player... forces him to shoot over the longer defensive hand." [S7, p.264]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Abandoning a system after 2 bad possessions** → wait for the chart to confirm a genuine trend (4 in a row) before making changes
2. **Making only offensive adjustments** → defensive adjustments disrupt the opponent's rhythm more effectively
3. **Overthinking a subtle fix** → a small adjustment (one defensive tweak, one substitution) is often enough to stop momentum without confusing the whole team
4. **Ignoring the possession clock count** → if you want a fast pace (>2 possessions/min) and only have 8 possessions after 6 minutes, act immediately to speed up

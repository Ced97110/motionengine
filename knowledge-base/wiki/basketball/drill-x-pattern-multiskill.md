# X-Pattern Multiskill

## Objective
Improve transitional movement and cutting ability through diagonal sprints, direction changes, and backpedaling in an X-shaped cone pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4 cones forming an X-pattern:
  - Start / Cone 4: bottom-left (0,0)
  - Cone 2: bottom-right (10,0)
  - Cone 1: top-left (0,10)
  - Cone 3: top-right (10,10)
- 10 yards between cone lines; diagonal ~14 yards

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance at the start (cone 4, bottom-left).
2. Sprint 10 yards (9 m) to cone 1 (top-left), plant on the inside foot and turn.
3. Sprint diagonally 14 yards (13 m) across to cone 2 (bottom-right), plant on the outside foot and change direction.
4. Backpedal 10 yards (9 m) to cone 3 (top-right), plant on the outside foot and change direction.
5. Sprint diagonally 14 yards (13 m) across to cone 4 (bottom-left) to the finish.

## Coaching Points
- **Inside foot plant** at cone 1 for a turn; **outside foot plant** at cones 2 and 3 for change of direction — teach the difference explicitly
- Diagonal sprints require maintaining direction and speed — "attack the diagonal"
- Backpedal segment: stay on balls of feet, hips down, ready to transition
- Head snaps to new visual target at each cone

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the pattern slowly; identify each foot plant type
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed, timed
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Start from various positions (lying, four-point stance) [S8, p.91]
   - Change cone distances
   - Change the biomotor skill on each leg
   - Cut with inside or outside leg at each cone
   - Put inside hand on ground during turns

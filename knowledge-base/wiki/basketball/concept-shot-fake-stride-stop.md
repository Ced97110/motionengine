# Shot Fake into Stride Stop

## Summary
The shot fake into stride stop is a foundational offensive scoring move in which the ball handler shows the ball upward to freeze or elevate a defender, then immediately attacks off the dribble (1 or 2 dribbles) and stops under control with a stride stop (two-foot sequential stop) to create a balanced pull-up jump shot. It is one of the most reliable ways to create mid-range scoring opportunities against aggressive, shot-blocking defenders.

This move is performed going both left (L) and right (R), and with either one or two attack dribbles, requiring the player to be competent in all four combinations before adding further complexity.

## When to Use
- When a defender is overplaying or jumping at shot fakes
- On catch-and-shoot situations where the defender closes out hard
- In the midrange game when driving all the way to the rim is not available
- Late in shot clocks when a reliable pull-up is needed
- After setting up a defender with multiple straight drives (they start jumping on fakes)

## Key Principles
1. **The fake must be convincing** — extend the ball upward toward your shot pocket with realistic shooting motion; a weak fake gets no reaction.
2. **Immediate attack** — the instant the defender's weight shifts or feet leave the floor, attack in one explosive dribble; hesitation kills the advantage.
3. **1-dribble vs. 2-dribble reads** — 1 dribble = defender is right in front; 2 dribbles = defender is a step back or more ground must be covered.
4. **Stride stop = balance** — stop on two feet sequentially (non-shooting foot then shooting foot), not a jump stop; this loads the legs for an explosive upward shot with full shooting-foot control.
5. **Alternate directions every rep in practice** — must be equally proficient going left and right or the defense will simply force one direction.
6. **Free throw interleaving** — practice FTs between every set of driving reps to simulate game conditions (elevated heart rate, fatigue).

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF**: Primary practitioners — all perimeter players need this move as a core mid-range weapon.
- **PF**: Also applicable on face-up opportunities from elbow or short corner.

## Variations
### 1-Dribble Stride Stop (Left)
Fake, 1 dribble left, stride stop, shoot.

### 1-Dribble Stride Stop (Right)
Fake, 1 dribble right, stride stop, shoot.

### 2-Dribble Stride Stop (Left)
Fake, 2 dribbles left, stride stop, shoot — covers more distance or shakes a persistent defender.

### 2-Dribble Stride Stop (Right)
Fake, 2 dribbles right, stride stop, shoot.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Flat or lazy fake** → Raise the ball fully to shot pocket height, keep eyes on rim to sell it.
2. **Waiting on the attack** → Attack within one split-second of the fake; any pause allows the defender to recover.
3. **Jump stop instead of stride stop** → Jump stop removes foot pivot control; stride stop preserves the shooting foot and gives better balance.
4. **Drifting sideways on the shot** → Stop square to the basket; feet should point at the target after the stop.
5. **Skipping free throws in practice** → Always shoot FTs between sets — they train composure and simulate late-game situations.

# Defensive Checklist — Daily Principles

## Summary
Herb Brown's comprehensive daily defensive checklist distills the most important principles of sound man-to-man defense into actionable, teachable points. The checklist covers on-ball pressure, help-and-recover responsibilities, post defense, transition defense, trapping, and rebounding. These are intended to be reviewed, drilled, and reinforced every single practice throughout the season. [S1, pp.47-50]

## When to Use
- Daily — these are foundational principles, not situational adjustments
- Use as a pre-practice review checklist
- Reference specific points when addressing a breakdown seen on film or in practice

## Key Principles

### On-Ball Defense
1. Provide a full-bodied show to help and recover — don't just flail arms at the ball. Force passes over your outside shoulder and look to draw the offensive foul. [S1, p.47]
2. When helping a teammate in trouble, run forward to a spot in front of the ball rather than backpedaling. "It is always easier and faster to run forward to a spot in front of the ball." [S1, p.47]
3. When defending the dribbler in the high pick-and-roll, play up and tough on the ball to fight over screens. Let ball position dictate going over or under. [S1, p.47]
4. When defending a high-post player with the ball on the perimeter, pressure up and play him as if his dribble is used. Don't sag and let him feed the post or reverse the ball. [S1, p.47]

### Off-Ball / Help Defense
5. Weak-side defenders must always know where the ball is; strong-side defenders must always know where their man is. [S1, p.48]
6. Drop two steps down and two steps in the direction of the ball every time a pass is made — jump to the ball while it is in the air — to force cutters away from the basket. "The ball should always draw you to it." [S1, p.47]
7. Don't permit easy inside basket cuts. Ride the cutter over or under screens. Stay connected. [S1, p.47]
8. When defending a post feeder on the wing, get your butt to the baseline and dictate where the cutter goes. Don't give cutters a choice. [S1, p.47]

### Post Defense
9. Stand up offensive big men in transition — meet them early before they seal and get low post position. [S1, p.48]
10. Take away position on the post AND the perimeter — meet post players early and stand them up. [S1, p.48]
11. Make big men put the ball on the floor and dribble when they drive. Pressure them when used as passers. [S1, p.48]
12. When your team fronts the post, perimeter defenders must pressure passers to make it difficult to enter the ball. The low-post defender must also sit on the legs of the man he is guarding to prevent jumping. Always provide weak-side help when fronting the post. [S1, p.50]

### Screen Defense
13. Prohibit defenders from chasing back pickers/screeners and accidentally screening their own teammate. [S1, p.48]
14. Body up or butt-screen offensive players when defending against cross screens. [S1, p.48]
15. Stand up screeners and cutters to prevent easy catch-and-shoot opportunities. [S1, p.49]

### Trapping
16. Trap hand-backs and zone off of the ball. [S1, p.48]
17. Look to trap when the ball handler has his back to the other defenders. Always send the trapped offensive player back to his defender or uphill and away from the goal. [S1, p.48]
18. Rotate completely when the basketball is thrown out of a trap or double team. Always try to pick up the free man in the direction of the help. [S1, p.48]

### Rebounding
19. When rebounding, never jump until you know when and where the ball is likely to bounce. [S1, p.47]
20. Always have five defensive rebounders. Long missed shots result in long rebounds — perimeter defenders must grab them. [S1, p.48]

## Vital Defensive Tips
1. Teach switching on defense to steal the ball, pick up the dribble, draw an offensive foul, or take the opponent out of its planned offense — "switch and take something away." [S1, p.48-49]
2. A team can never be selective in effort if it wants to consistently win. [S1, p.49]
3. "Defense is a combination of determination, trust, and communication." [S1, p.49]
4. Take away opponents' easy baskets and putbacks. Initiate and anticipate — be the aggressor and make opponents react to your aggressiveness. [S1, p.49]
5. The purpose of extending defense is to disrupt opponents and make them go to options they don't regularly practice. Stop the ball in transition before teams can initiate early offense. [S1, p.49]
6. Set the defensive tone from the opening tip. Referees will allow physical defense established early; they may penalize intensity that only appears late in games. [S1, p.49]
7. The three keys to winning any game: keep opponents off the free-throw line, eliminate fast-break layups, prevent offensive rebounds. [S1, p.50]
8. Make players aware of time, score, and situation during every timeout. [S1, p.50]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Know where the ball is at all times; be ready to help on dribble penetration
- **SG**: Help-side awareness; slide through screens with communication
- **SF**: Ride cutters, body up on cross screens, contest all catch-and-shoot opportunities
- **PF**: Front or three-quarter post players; sit on legs to prevent jumping; stand up big men in transition
- **C**: Provide weak-side post help; front when perimeter defenders pressure passers; five-man rebounding

## Common Mistakes
1. Backpedaling to help → run forward to a spot in front of the ball instead
2. Flailing arms at the ball on a show → provide full-bodied show and force passes over the outside shoulder
3. Sagging off high-post player with the ball → pressure up and play as if dribble is used
4. Giving cutters a choice of direction → get butt to baseline and dictate one direction
5. Jumping prematurely on rebounds → wait until you know when and where the ball will bounce
6. Weak-side defenders ignoring the ball → the ball should always draw weak-side defenders toward it
7. Defenders chasing screeners and accidentally screening their own teammate → prohibit this immediately
8. Playing passive defense early and increasing intensity only when trailing → set physical tone from tip-off

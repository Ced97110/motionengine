# Arm Bar Ball Protection

## Summary
The arm bar is a ball-protection technique used while driving past a defender. The non-dribbling arm (the arm between the ball-handler and the defender) is bent in an L-shape with the fist closed, creating a legal physical barrier that prevents the defender from reaching the dribble or poking the ball free. The term "bar" is used because it keeps the defender out, much like a physical bar used for protection. [S5, pp.93-94]

This is a foundational technique for all dribble-drive situations — pull-up jumpers, drives to the rim, and show-and-go moves.

## When to Use
- Any time a player is driving past a defender (pull-up jump shot, drive to the rim, show-and-go)
- Especially important when a beaten defender tries to scramble back and reach for the ball
- Used on both strong-side and weak-side drives

## Key Principles
1. **Identify the arm bar arm** — it is always the arm closest to the defender or the basket:
   - Right-handed shooter going **right**: left arm is the arm bar
   - Right-handed shooter going **left**: right arm is the arm bar
   - Left-handed shooter: mirror image [S5, p.94]
2. **L-shape, closed fist** — bend the arm at approximately 90 degrees with the hand closed in a fist; the fist tenses the muscles, making the bar stronger and harder to knock down [S5, p.94]
3. **Keep the arm tight to the body** — extending the arm bar outward away from the body will initiate contact with the defender and draw an offensive foul; tight to the body = legal and effective [S5, p.94]
4. **Open the fist when picking up the ball** — upon establishing shot line and going into the shot, open the fist:
   - Going to strong side: pick up the ball with two hands
   - Going to weak side: open the fist and "load the gun" (bring weak hand under the ball for alignment) [S5, p.94]
5. **Always protect the ball, no matter the fake** — the beaten defender will try to scramble back; the arm bar prevents steals throughout the drive [S5, p.99]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Any player who drives to the basket or pulls up off the dribble needs the arm bar

## Common Mistakes
1. **Open hand on the arm bar** → Defender can easily knock it down and reach the dribble; close the fist [S5, p.94]
2. **Extending the arm bar away from the body** → Causes offensive foul; keep it tight, tucked to the body [S5, p.94]
3. **Forgetting to open the fist when picking up the ball** → Leads to an unbalanced catch or a loaded-gun error; consciously release the fist into the ball-pick-up motion [S5, p.94]
4. **Not adjusting for direction** → The arm bar arm changes depending on which direction the player drives; pre-determine based on direction, not habit [S5, p.94]

# Power Snatch From Hang Position

## Objective
Develop explosive total-body power, overhead shoulder stability, and core strength — directly simulating the movement patterns of jump shots and rebounds.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Barbell (begin with empty bar or light weight for novices)
- Must have mastered the snatch pull from hang before attempting
- **Safety**: Advanced exercise — prioritize form, not weight. When performing Olympic lifts, the amount of weight lifted should NOT be the priority, especially for novices [S2, p.98]

## Execution
1. Approach the barbell and grasp it with hands **significantly wider than shoulders** (snatch grip). Place the bar against the midthighs, just above the knees.
2. Pick up the bar and assume an erect standing position with knees slightly bent.
3. Move the hips back (without changing knee angle) to lower the bar to a position **slightly above the knees** (hang position).
4. The chest is over and in front of the barbell. Upper back is straight, lower back in neutral position. Arms are relaxed and completely straight.
5. Quickly extend the lower extremities as the hips move forward, **maintaining the knee bend** so the barbell slides up the front of the legs.
6. Fully extend the knees and ankles, and **quickly shrug the shoulders** — this causes the barbell to rise vertically.
7. Once the barbell reaches its highest point, **slightly bend the knees** (drop under the bar). Conclude by **extending the arms** to receive the barbell as it travels overhead and slightly behind the head.
8. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.98–99]

## Coaching Points
- **"Hips, knees, shrug"** — these three happen sequentially but nearly simultaneously
- The shoulder shrug is the key to getting bar height — don't skip it
- Receiving the bar: elbows lock out, bar is balanced slightly behind the head — not in front
- "Drop, don't catch" — players should bend into the bar, not wait for it to come down
- Start with an empty bar — the movement pattern is the skill; load is secondary
- Watch for players bending elbows early — arms stay straight until the shrug is complete

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Snatch pull from hang (no catch) — groove the pull pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Power snatch from hang with light load, focusing on bar path and overhead catch
3. **Advanced**: Power Snatch From Box (place bar on boxes 3–12 inches / 6–30 cm high at various positions relative to the knee — above, at, or below — to train explosive dead-stop starts)

## Muscles Involved
**Primary**: Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, semitendinosus, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius, trapezius, lateral deltoid, anterior deltoid

**Secondary**: Gastrocnemius, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, rectus femoris [S2, p.99]

## Basketball Focus
The power snatch from hang position **simulates the movement of jump shots and rebounds** — the same hip extension, triple extension, and overhead stability are required when elevating for a shot or battling for a rebound. Stabilizing the bar overhead also builds the shoulder stability and core strength needed to maintain body control when off the floor and absorbing contact. [S2, p.99]

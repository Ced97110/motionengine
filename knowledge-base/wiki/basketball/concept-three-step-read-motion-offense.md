# Three-Step Read — Motion Offense Weak-Side Cutting System

## Summary
The Three-Step Read is the foundational weak-side decision-making system for the motion offense. Every off-ball player must constantly read their defender's positioning relative to two measurements — **spacing** (distance from ball) and **alignment** (position relative to the ball-attacker line) — and select the correct cut accordingly. When every player executes this read correctly, help defense becomes impossible and the offense generates constant open looks without called plays.

This system teaches players to exploit defensive errors automatically rather than running predetermined routes. It is the simplest and most effective way to teach motion offense cutting to young players because it reduces all cutting decisions to three readable situations.

## When to Use
- Any time an off-ball player is on the weak side without the ball
- In the motion offense as a continuous background read on every ball movement
- As a teaching tool for 2v2 play before progressing to 5v5
- When the ball handler has the ball and the off-ball player's defender may be out of position

## Key Principles

### The Two Measurements Every Off-Ball Player Must Make
1. **Spacing**: Proper help-side spacing = defender plays **two-thirds of the distance** from the ball to the off-ball attacker
2. **Alignment**: Proper alignment = defender is **one step off the straight line** between the ball and the attacker, in an interception stance

### The Three Reads

**Read 1 — Defender in proper spacing AND proper alignment → V-Cut (Replace)**
- No attacking cut is available; the defense is correct
- Off-ball player uses a V-cut to replace themselves on the perimeter
- This keeps the defender occupied and prevents them from offering effective help
- "V-cut keeps X busy and unable to offer great help from the weak side" [S15, p.225]

**Read 2 — Defender TOO DEEP (below the ball-attacker line) → Middle Cut**
- Defender is not on the straight line between ball and attacker; there is a gap in the middle
- Off-ball player dips toward the high side to further compel the defender lower, then middle cuts for the layup
- The dip exaggerates the defender's mistake by pulling them even deeper

**Read 3 — Defender TOO HIGH (above the ball-attacker line) → Backdoor Cut**
- Defender is above the line, giving the attacker the baseline side
- Off-ball player dips toward the high side to encourage the defender even higher
- Off-ball player plants the strong foot and sprints backdoor for the pass and layup
- Signal: a closed fist tells the passer a backdoor cut is coming

### Additional Rules
- Any time a defender takes an eye off the attacker to watch the ball → immediate middle cut or backdoor cut
- If the defender is closer to the ball than two-thirds distance → attacker passes to the opposite player; receiver can fake and drive before defender can close out
- If the defender is too close to the receiver → receiver moves into the defender, then selects the appropriate cut (middle or backdoor)

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Learn and execute all three reads; in the motion offense every player will be off-ball regularly
- **Ball handler (Player 1)**: Read when the weak-side player's cut is open and lead the cutting receiver with the pass
- **Off-ball player (Player 2)**: Continuously read X2's spacing and alignment as you move up and down the weak side

## Variations
### 2v2 Version (Intermediate)
Player 1 is allowed to dribble up and down the frontcourt while Player 2 moves on the weak side. X2 must continuously reposition. When X2 makes a mistake, Player 2 immediately cuts. If X2 holds position, Player 1 can drive (triggering rotation rules for X2).

### Advanced 2v2 with Defenders on Both Sides
Add X1 on Player 1. Now both strong-side and weak-side reads are active simultaneously. Players 1 and 2 must communicate and exploit whichever defender is out of position first.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Off-ball player hesitates on the read** → must execute the cut the instant the defensive error appears; delayed cuts allow recovery
2. **Ball handler doesn't see the weak-side cut** → develop a habit of split vision between the ball handler and off-ball players
3. **Pass doesn't lead the cutter** → always throw ahead of the cutter's path, not into their body
4. **Off-ball player V-cuts when a more aggressive cut is available** → using a V-cut when a middle or backdoor is open is wasted opportunity
5. **Defender X2 is not corrected when out of position** → the drill breaks down if coaches allow sloppy defensive positioning

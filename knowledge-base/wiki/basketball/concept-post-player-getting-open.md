# Post Player Getting Open: Footwork Techniques

## Summary
An offensive post player must win what coaches call **"the battle of the feet"** before any scoring can occur. Getting open in the post requires specific footwork techniques — the V-cut, rip/scrape move, swim move, and contact-reverse pivot — to defeat aggressive post defenders who deny position, jam cuts, or physically bump flash cutters. [S6, pp.172-180]

The foundational principle is simple: the post player must read how the defender is playing him, then take the defender in the direction they are already going — lower if they play low, higher if they play high — before reversing course with the decisive cut. All three techniques share a common footwork base: a **V-cut** setup followed by a **front crossover pivot** to create separation and flash to the ball.

> "You can't win the war unless you win the battle." — Coaching phrase for post players [S6, p.173]

## When to Use
- Any time the post defender is actively denying the low block entry pass
- When X5 jams the flash post cut physically (swim move)
- When the ball reverses to the opposite side and the defender plays the duck-in cut from the high side (contact-reverse pivot)
- For perimeter players facing aggressive denial — the rip and swim moves work equally well on the perimeter [S6, p.179]

## Key Principles
1. **Beat the defender down the floor first.** Hustle to the spot just above the low block (at the first notch above the block) and establish position before the defender arrives. [S6, p.173]
2. **Read the defender before moving.** The post player has his back to the basket and can see teammates, his defender, how he is being played, and ball location — all factors inform which technique to use. [S6, p.174]
3. **Take them where they are already going.** If the defender plays low, start lower; if high, start higher. Create exaggerated positioning that the defender follows, then cut opposite. [S6, p.176-177]
4. **Get close before cutting.** The closer the offensive player gets to the defender before making the decisive cut, the less reaction time the defender has to recover. [S6, p.178]
5. **Rip the contact arm through (or over).** The scraping/ripping action eliminates the defender's pursuit angle on a flash cut — they cannot close the gap. [S6, p.175]
6. **Post up at the first notch above the block.** After any of these moves, establish position at or above the block (never below) for the best drop-step angle. [S6, p.176]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF/C (Post Player):** Execute V-cut reads, front crossover pivot, rip/swim arm technique, and contact-reverse pivot to get open against all defensive coverages.
- **PG/SG/SF (Perimeter Players):** The swim move and rip move apply equally on the perimeter against denial defense; same footwork, same principles. [S6, p.179]

## Variations

### Technique 1: V-Cut with Rip/Scrape Move
Used when space is available to rip the arm through the defender.

**If defender plays low:**
- Teaching phrase: *"If they start low, you start lower (but never below the block)."*
- Concept: Take defender lower, then V-cut high using a front crossover pivot; rip the contact arm across the defender's chest as you flash. [S6, p.176]

**If defender plays high:**
- Teaching phrase: *"If they start high, you start higher."*
- Concept: Take defender higher, then V-cut low; rip the contact arm past the defender's baseline shoulder as you flash. [S6, p.177]

**Footwork detail (defender playing low — cut to the high side):** Post player on the left side of the lane, back to basket. (1) Plant inside (left) foot in the lane. (2) Step right foot. (3) Front crossover pivot with the left foot across the defender. Simultaneously rip the left arm across the defender's chest and scrape off. Flash to ball across the lane. [S6, p.175]

**Footwork detail (defender playing high — cut to the low side):** (1) Step with top (right) foot high. (2) Step with lower (left) foot. (3) Front crossover step with the top (right) foot. Rip the right arm past the defender's baseline shoulder as you cut low across the lane. [S6, p.175]

### Technique 2: Swim Move
Used when the defender **physically jams** the flash cut with body contact, leaving no space to rip through.

- Same V-cut setup and front crossover pivot footwork as the rip move.
- Instead of ripping through, the offensive player **swings the contact arm OVER** (swims over) the defender as the front crossover step is made.
- Gets the offensive cutter on the high side of the defender, closer to the ball than the defender. [S6, p.178]
- Key teaching phrase: *"Get into the defender's body before you make your swim move."* — The closer you get, the less reaction time the defender has.

**Teaching phrases:**
- *"If they jam you low, take them lower and swim high."*
- *"If they jam you high, take them higher and swim low."* [S6, pp.177-178]

**Works on the perimeter too:** A wing player (03) facing denial uses a V-cut toward the basket (right foot first), then a front crossover step with the left foot and a swim move over X3 to get between defender and ball to receive the initial wing pass. [S6, p.179]

### Technique 3: Contact and Reverse Pivot Move
Used when the defender jams the **duck-in cut** from the high side, using physical contact to prevent it.

- Ball is reversed to the opposite side of the court (03 receives).
- X4 plays on the high side of 04 and physically jams the duck-in.
- 04 **steps into X4's body**, placing the contact foot (right foot — the lower baseline foot) **between X4's feet**.
- Executes a **strong and quick reverse pivot** by swinging the left foot toward the baseline while holding the left arm and elbow up as an **arm bar**.
- 04 ends up on the **low (baseline) side** of X4, sealing with the high foot (left) and arm/elbow.
- Sits down on X4's low leg to maintain the seal.
- Post up at or above the first notch above the block. [S6, pp.179-180]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Starting from below the block** → Never start below the block; minimum position is the first notch above. Best drop-step angles require this positioning.
2. **Not getting close before cutting** → The V-cut must bring the offensive player into the defender's body; cutting from distance gives the defender time to recover.
3. **Ripping too early** → The front crossover pivot and rip must happen simultaneously; an early rip gives the defender warning.
4. **Using rip move when jammed** → If the defender is physically tight, switch to the swim move (arm over instead of through).
5. **Perimeter players ignoring these techniques** → Swim and rip moves are equally effective against perimeter denial; all players should practice both.

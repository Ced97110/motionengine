# Off-Screen Shot Selection Framework

## Summary
Every time a player catches the ball off a screen, they face a decision: which scoring action to use? This decision must be based on **reading the defender's coverage angle** — not on habit, pre-programming, or gut feeling. This framework organizes the six primary off-screen reactions by defensive coverage type, allowing players to develop genuine decision-making. [S3, pp.141-180]

The framework is built around two primary catch positions (wing and elbow) and three defensive coverages (beaten, high/outside, trailing):

| Defender Position | Catch Location | Correct Reaction |
|---|---|---|
| Beaten / under screen | Wing | Fill shot (catch-and-shoot, 15–20 ft) |
| High-angle closeout (baseline open) | Wing | Dribble fade (attack baseline) |
| Trailing / low-side (middle open) | Wing | Dribble fill (attack middle) |
| Chasing over the top | Elbow | Curl shot (catch-and-shoot, 10–15 ft) |
| Trailing directly behind | Elbow curl | Dribble curl (continue curl into paint) |

## When to Use
- Any time a player receives the ball coming off a down screen, lateral screen, or back screen
- Set plays designed to free shooters on the wing or at the elbow
- Half-court offense — anytime an off-ball player uses a screen in the flow
- Individual skill development workouts to build decision-making under a stationary screener

## Key Principles
1. **Read before you receive** — begin reading the defender before the ball arrives; the decision should be made on the catch
2. **Establish the pivot foot immediately** on every catch — this is the foundation for all five live-ball moves
3. **High-angle closeout = baseline open** → dribble fade [S3, p.141]
4. **Trailing / low-side = middle open** → dribble fill [S3, p.155]
5. **Defender beaten** → fill shot [S3, p.148]
6. **Defender chasing over the top** → curl shot at the elbow [S3, p.162]
7. **Defender directly trailing the curl** → dribble curl (continue into the paint, don't stop) [S3, p.171]
8. **Combine the reads** in practice — never pre-decide the move before reading the defense

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF (cutter/shooter)**: Read the defender's position and angle, establish pivot foot, execute the correct reaction
- **Screener**: Set a legal screen that forces the defender to make a specific choice — angle of the screen determines which reads are available
- **Passer**: Deliver the ball to the shooting pocket at the right time and the right location so the receiver can immediately read and react

## Variations
### Wing-Based Reads
Fill, dribble fade, and dribble fill all start from a wing catch after a screen. The shoot/attack direction depends on defender angle. [S3, pp.141-161]

### Elbow-Based Reads
Curl and dribble curl both happen when the cut targets the elbow area. Whether the player shoots or continues into the paint depends on whether the defender is to the side or directly behind. [S3, pp.162-179]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Pre-deciding the move** → always using the fade or always using the fill regardless of defense; force players to read
2. **Not establishing a pivot foot** → attempting a live-ball move without a pivot, risking a travel call
3. **Catching off-balance** → poor V-cut or inaccurate pass makes all reads harder; fix the cut and pass first
4. **Hesitating after reading** → identifying the correct read but waiting too long to execute; the read-to-action gap must be instantaneous
5. **Using the wrong read** → going baseline when the defender is low; reinforce "high = baseline open, low = middle open"

# B-March Walk

## Objective
Improve hip-extension mechanics and enhance hamstring firing during the stride cycle — targeting the power phase of the sprint that propels the body forward.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Open area, 20-40 yards
- Performed after A-March Walk as part of the A-B form progression sequence

## Execution
1. March as in the [[drill-a-march-walk]] with perfect posture and proper arm action
2. Bring the recovery leg knee up high (same as A-March)
3. **Allow the recovering leg to extend in front** after the high-knee raise — the foot kicks out forward
4. **Paw down** from the extended position — pull the foot back toward the body in a sweeping motion
5. **Drive the hips through** as the foot makes contact with the ground [S8, p.30]

## Coaching Points
- "Paw down and drive the hips through" — these two cues define B-form mechanics
- The pawing motion simulates the powerful backward sweep of the leg just before ground contact in maximal sprinting
- Hip drive is the source of propulsive force — without it, the drill becomes a passive leg swing
- Watch for athletes who paw without driving the hips; the combination is what activates the hamstrings properly

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow march with exaggerated forward extension and deliberate paw-down
2. **Intermediate**: Marching tempo while maintaining all three components: high knee → extend → paw down + hip drive
3. **Advanced**: Progress to [[drill-b-skips]] — same mechanics with a skip for added power [S8, pp.30-31]

# Front Squat

## Objective
Develop quad-dominant lower-body strength for basketball acceleration, deceleration, and cutting by loading the barbell across the front of the shoulders.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1 (individual strength exercise)
- **Location:** weight room / squat rack
- **Equipment:** barbell, squat rack, collars; padding optional for cross-grip variation
- **Note:** Performed with approximately 20% less weight than the back squat [S2, p.13]

## Execution
1. Stand erect in a squat rack with the barbell positioned in front of the neck across the deltoid muscles, weight evenly distributed across the shoulders.
2. Choose grip style:
   - **Olympic grip** — hold the bar in the fingers with elbows raised parallel to the floor (requires wrist flexibility; used in catching during power exercises).
   - **Cross grip (easier)** — palms down, right hand holds bar at left deltoid, left hand at right deltoid; elbows remain at shoulder level throughout.
3. Keep the back as straight as possible and slowly descend by bending at the hips and knees until the thighs are slightly lower than parallel to the floor. During descent, keep elbows up and in line with the shoulders.
4. Achieve the bottom position without bouncing.
5. Reverse direction by extending hips and knees to ascend back to the starting position. [S2, pp.12-13]

## Coaching Points
- **"Elbows up"** — dropping the elbows during the descent causes the torso to pitch forward and shifts load to the lower back.
- The more erect body position (vs. back squat) places less stress on the low back and increases quadriceps recruitment.
- Do not bounce out of the bottom position — control the turnaround.
- Knees should track over the toes throughout the movement.
- Use the cross grip when introducing this exercise to players with limited wrist flexibility.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Goblet squat with a single dumbbell to learn the upright torso position before adding a barbell.
2. **Intermediate:** Front squat with Olympic or cross grip at moderate load, full range of motion.
3. **Advanced:** Increase load progressively; pair with plyometric box jumps in the same session to potentiate power output (contrast training).

## Concepts Taught
- [[leg-strength-for-basketball]] — develops the quad, glute, and posterior-chain strength that underlies every jump and cut
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — force output into the floor drives first-step quickness; deceleration strength allows sharp cuts

## Basketball Application
The front squat develops strength of the low back, hips, and lower extremities for optimal force output and deceleration. The ability to beat an opponent to the basket depends on the ability to accelerate, decelerate, and change direction — all of which are built by this exercise. [S2, pp.12-13]

**Primary muscles:** gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, hamstrings (semitendinosus, semimembranosus, biceps femoris), quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius)

**Secondary muscles:** erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), rectus abdominis, external oblique, internal oblique, adductor longus, adductor brevis, gastrocnemius

# Heavy Sled Pulls

## Objective
Enhance starting power and stride length by dragging a weighted sled across a 15- to 20-yard sprint.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A weighted sled with a harness attached around the athlete's waist or shoulders
- Open flat surface (field, track, gym floor if appropriate)
- Distance: 15 to 20 yards (14 to 18 meters) per rep

## Execution
1. Attach the weighted sled to your body via harness.
2. Assume your explosive start position of choice.
3. At signal (self or coach), drive out explosively, emphasizing a low body angle and powerful first strides.
4. Drag the sled continuously for the full 15–20 yard distance.
5. Emphasize acceleration mechanics throughout — do not allow the resistance to break down posture.
6. Walk back to start, rest fully, and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- "Drive the ground back, not the body forward" — cue an aggressive push against the ground with each stride.
- Maintain forward lean without collapsing at the hips.
- Arms drive hard and low during the acceleration phase — elbows at 90 degrees.
- Weight on the sled should be heavy enough to provide resistance but not so heavy it shortens stride length dramatically or causes technique breakdown.
- This drill closely simulates first-step power needed in basketball for transition, cutting, and driving to the basket.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light sled (10–15% bodyweight), 15 yards, 4 reps
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate sled (20–25% bodyweight), 20 yards, 5–6 reps
3. **Advanced**: Heavy sled (30%+ bodyweight), 20 yards, 6–8 reps with minimal rest

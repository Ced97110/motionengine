# Triangle Offense — Pressure Releases

## Summary
The Triangle Offense includes a comprehensive set of pressure-release mechanisms designed to overcome aggressive front-line defense that prevents the initial wing entry pass. These concepts — moment of truth, line of truth, lag principle, blind pig, wing reverse, and blur screen — give the triangle built-in answers for every type of overplay, allowing it to function against the most aggressive defensive schemes. [S7, pp.105-109]

## When to Use
- When the defense overplays wings to prevent the entry pass
- When the lag guard (2) is also denied, forcing a deeper reset
- When aggressive help or trapping prevents normal triangle operations
- When the defense is cheating on known patterns (trigger the blind pig or wing reverse as a counter)

## Key Principles

### Moment of Truth
The **moment of truth** is the specific moment when the point guard (1) picks up his dribble three feet in front of his defensive man. This is the signal for the wing (3) to pop out and receive the entry pass. 3 must time his pop-out precisely so he receives the ball at the wing position exactly as 1 reaches the moment of truth. [S7, p.105]

The **line of truth** is the imaginary line across the floor three feet in front of the defensive player guarding the ball handler. [S7, p.105]

### Lag Principle
If 1 reaches the moment of truth and 3 is not open on the pop-out:
1. 1 passes to 2, who lags **behind** the line of truth by three or more feet as a safety valve (a guard-to-guard pass).
2. 2 passes to 4 as 4 pops out to receive.
3. All three players must coordinate movement and timing for a successful wing entry; considerable dribbling is required. [S7, p.105]

### Blind Pig Action
If 2 (the lag receiver) is also overplayed:
1. **4 flashes** to the top of the lane.
2. **1 quickly passes to 4**.
3. **2 speed-cuts backdoor** down the back side and, if open, receives a drop pass from 4 cutting to the basket.
4. If 2 is not open: 2 continues to the **weak-side freeze spot**; 1 cuts over the top of 4 and receives the ball from 4, then drives to the basket or dribbles to a wing/corner operating spot. [S7, p.106]

**Blind Pig continuations:**
- **4 can pass to 3 and screen down for 1** (1 moves to top of floor; 2 holds freeze position)
- **4 can pass to 1 and set a screen for 1**
- **4 drives other side for dribble weave**, meets 2 coming off 3's down screen, centering the court
- **1 isolation**: 4 sets staggered screen (3 first, 4 second) for 2; 1 is isolated one-on-one on one side while 2 comes off the stagger screens [S7, pp.106-107]

### Wing Entry on the Blind Pig
Variant when 1 cannot pass to 4:
1. 1 passes to 3 instead.
2. **4 immediately back-screens for 1**.
3. 1 speed-cuts; 3 looks for a high over-the-top pass to 1 (note: the right side of the court is cleared).
4. After the screen for 1: 4 sets a **side screen-and-roll for 3** (wing screen and roll); 1 spots up in the corner or wing.
5. After screening for 3: 4 sets a **third consecutive screen** — this time for 2 in the corner.
6. After screens: 4 continues to weak-side rebound position; 3 posts down after kickoff pass to 1; 5 positions in the lane to rebound. [S7, p.108]

### Blur Screen
If 2 is overplayed OR has violated the lag principle:
1. **2 cuts first**; **4 cuts second**, right off 2's tail — this sequential cut is called a "blur screen."
2. 4 receives the ball from 1.
3. 1 speed-cuts off the back side of 4.
4. 3 screens for 2 coming up for defensive balance.
5. 1 can also make a high pass to 2 if open. [S7, p.109]

### Wing Reverse
If 1 cannot pass to 2 or 3:
1. 1 passes to **5 at the ball-side elbow** (high post).
2. **3 immediately cuts backdoor** ("wing reverse" — triggered by the pass to the high post).
3. 5 drop-passes to 3 if 3 is **head and shoulders past** his defender.
4. If 3 is not open: 1 screens on 2 ("guard squeeze action"); 2 cuts to 3 feet in front of 5; 5 passes to 2 if open; after the screen 1 rolls to the free-throw area and can receive from 5. [S7, p.109]

## Common Mistakes
1. **3 not timing the pop-out** → Must read 1's position and coordinate the moment of truth precisely; arriving too early or too late kills the entry.
2. **2 lagging too close to the line of truth** → Must be 3+ feet BEHIND the line of truth as a safety valve, not level with it.
3. **1 not triggering the blind pig quickly** → Once 2 is overplayed, the key is speed — 4 must flash and 1 must pass before 4's defender can recover.
4. **Cutters stopping mid-cut** → Both 2 and 1 must complete their cuts even when blocked; stopping creates turnovers.
5. **Failing to form the triangle after failed pressure releases** → Every failed entry re-establishes a new triangle on the other side; players must hustle to re-form.

# Stability-Ball Hops

## Objective
Improve quickness in the upper-body pushing musculature through reactive balance challenges in a push-up position. [S8, p.154]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (any area)
- Equipment: 1 stability ball

## Execution
1. Place feet on top of the stability ball and hands on the floor in a push-up (plank) position.
2. Keeping the core tight and hips level, begin hopping the hands backward and forward.
3. Then hop side to side, maintaining balance on the ball with the feet throughout.
4. Do not allow the abdominals and hips to sag — maintain a firm, straight body position throughout.
5. Perform for a set number of reps, for time, or as part of a circuit.

## Coaching Points
- "Keep the hips up — no sagging" is the critical cue; a sagging core defeats the purpose of the drill.
- The goal is short ground-contact time with the hands — reactive, not slow.
- Start with small controlled hops before increasing amplitude.
- Monitor the athlete for ball slippage; ensure proper footwear or bare feet for grip.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform stationary plank on the stability ball before attempting any hops.
2. **Intermediate**: Hop backward-forward or side-to-side individually before combining patterns.
3. **Advanced**: Combine backward, forward, and lateral hops in a continuous pattern; increase tempo or add a time constraint.

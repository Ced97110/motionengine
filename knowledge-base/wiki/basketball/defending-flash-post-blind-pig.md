# Defending the Flash-Post (Blind Pig) Action

## Summary
The flash post — also called the "blind pig" — occurs when a weak-side post player reads perimeter overplay and flashes to the high/mid post to receive the ball. Herb Brown's approach is to deny the flash pass whenever possible, and when the post catches, to immediately contest passing lanes, protect the wing defender, and assign the low-block defender to cross the lane and stop any backdoor cut. [S1, pp.148-149]

## When to Use
- When the defense is aggressively overplaying perimeter receivers, creating blind-pig opportunities for weak-side posts
- Against offenses that predetermine flash reads when the wing is denied
- Anytime a post player vacates the block and flashes to the elbow or mid-post area

## Key Principles
1. **Deny first** — Overplay and deny the flash-post pass if at all possible. Force the post to flash wider or higher to receive. [S1, p.148]
2. **Post defender: wide stance, bother the passer** — If the ball is received at the post, the post defender steps back into a wide stance, bothers the passer's vision, and occupies the passing lanes. [S1, p.148]
3. **Wing defender keeps man in front** — The wing's defender (X2) must keep his man in front of him. If he loses his man, he either opens up and retreats to the goal or snaps back to impede/deflect a pass to his man. [S1, p.148]
4. **Weak-side defenders drawn to ball** — All weak-side defenders are pulled toward the ball. The nearest defender forces the initial receiver to receive the pass high (farther from the basket). [S1, p.148]
5. **Low-block defender crosses the lane** — It is the *responsibility of the defensive player on the low block* to come across the lane and stop any offensive player attempting to go backdoor. This is a critical, pre-assigned rotation. [S1, p.148]
6. **Fill nail, elbow, box** — Weak-side defenders should occupy the nail, elbow, and box areas for rotational coverage. [S1, p.148]

## Player Responsibilities
- **Post defender (X4)**: Step back into wide stance on flash catch; pressure passer; occupy passing lanes. [S1, p.148]
- **Wing defender (X2)**: Keep wing man in front; if beaten, open up or snap back to impede pass. [S1, p.148]
- **Low-block defender (X5)**: Cross the lane immediately to cut off any backdoor cutter. [S1, p.148]
- **Weak-side defenders**: Draw to the ball; fill nail, elbow, and box positions. [S1, p.148]
- **Nearest defender**: Force the initial flash receiver to catch the ball as high/far from the basket as possible. [S1, p.148]

## Common Mistakes
1. **No one covers the backdoor** → Offense gets a layup. Correction: Low-block defender has an absolute assignment to cross the lane on flash-post action. [S1, p.148]
2. **Post defender attacks the ball instead of passing lanes** → Passer finds a cutter or open perimeter player. Correction: Step back, widen, and control passing lanes rather than lunging at ball handler. [S1, p.148]
3. **Wing defender chases his man and vacates passing lane** → Gut pass reaches the middle. Correction: Open up, retreat to goal, or snap back rather than losing floor position. [S1, p.148]

# Mat Walk (Knee Rehabilitation)

## Objective
Restore dynamic balance and lower-extremity proprioception during movement — foundational exercise in the jumper's knee rehabilitation program for players returning from injury.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A thin mat approximately 5-10 yards (4.5-9 m) long
- No additional equipment required

## Execution
1. Stand erect on the lateral edge of a thin mat.
2. Walk with one foot placed directly in front of the other in tightrope fashion. The midline of each foot runs along the edge of the mat — half the foot is on the mat, half off.
3. Walk the full length of the mat without allowing either foot to touch the floor (i.e., maintain the narrow-base tightrope walk).
4. Perform the prescribed number of mat walks (lengths).

## Coaching Points
- "Walk the line" — the narrow foot placement challenges medial-lateral balance throughout the entire gait cycle
- Do not allow the arms to flail for balance — cue players to keep arms controlled
- Watch for trunk sway; the torso should stay relatively still
- This drill simulates the balance demands of cutting around a defender — throwing the center of mass outside the base of support and recovering
- Progress to adding perturbations (light shoulder taps) once the basic walk is mastered

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow tightrope walk, full foot on mat edge
2. **Intermediate**: Increase walking speed; add forward-to-backward reversal
3. **Advanced**: Partner perturbations (light taps to shoulders) while walking the mat

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Gastrocnemius, soleus, tibialis anterior, extensor hallucis longus, extensor digitorum longus, flexor hallucis longus, flexor digitorum longus, tibialis posterior, peroneals, interossei, lumbricals
- **Secondary**: Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, hamstrings, quadriceps, erector spinae, rectus abdominis

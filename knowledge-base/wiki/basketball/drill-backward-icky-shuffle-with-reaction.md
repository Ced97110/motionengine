# Backward Icky Shuffle With Reaction (Quick Feet)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, court sense, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, total-body agility, and general athleticism. [S8, p.219]

## Setup
- 1 player, agility ladder
- Start on the **right side** of the ladder, **facing backward** (away from the direction of travel)
- Coach positioned behind the player or to the side

## Execution
1. Start on the **right side** of the ladder, **facing backward**.
2. Step **laterally with the right foot** and place it in the first square.
3. Step with the **left foot** to be inside the same square.
4. **Drop-step with the right foot** to the right side of the ladder; step back with the **left foot** to the next square position.
5. Bring the **right foot** to the same square as the left foot.
6. **Drop-step to the left side** with the left foot; step back with the **right foot** to the next square.
7. Repeat this backward pattern to the end of the ladder.
8. **Simultaneously**, react to the coach's visual commands (e.g., finger count) throughout the drill.

## Coaching Points
- Hips stay low and facing backward — avoid twisting to face forward
- Drop-step is a critical defensive basketball skill: "Same footwork as a defensive drop-step into a contest"
- Divide attention between footwork and the coach's visual cues
- "Drop and drive, keep those hips back!"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master forward Icky Shuffle and backward walking pattern before combining
2. **Intermediate**: Add coach finger-count cues
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Odd/even filtering
   - Coach semicircle for peripheral vision
   - Multiple coaches [S8, p.219]
   - Sport-specific skill at end of ladder

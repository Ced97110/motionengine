# One-Arm Wall Push-Away

## Objective
Build explosive unilateral pressing power and "bulletproof" elbow tendons through a progressively angled push-away from a wall using one arm.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A flat wall
- No additional equipment needed
- Feet positioned well back from the wall to create an angled lean

## Execution
1. Lean against a wall with **one arm** at approximately elbow height. Keep feet well back from the wall to create a significant forward lean.
2. Bend the single arm as if performing a one-arm wall push-up, lowering the chest toward the wall.
3. From the bottom position, **explosively push yourself away from the wall** until you are standing upright with the arm extended.
4. Catch yourself, reset the lean, and repeat.
5. Complete all reps on one side before switching [S9, p.1].

## Coaching Points
- "Feet further back = harder" — increasing the distance of the feet from the wall increases the body-lean angle and therefore the load on the pressing arm
- This is a *pushing* plyometric — the goal is explosive extension, not controlled pressing
- "Protect the elbow by building gradually" — the primary benefit of this exercise is tendon resilience in the elbow complex; do not rush progressions
- Watch for: athletes using the non-working hand to assist — must be single arm only
- Finish fully standing (arm extended) before resetting — partial reps reduce the tendon-conditioning benefit

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Feet close to the wall, minimal lean. Slow, controlled push-away building confidence in single-arm pressing.
2. **Intermediate**: Feet further back, faster explosive push. Finish fully upright.
3. **Advanced**: Feet as far back as possible, almost horizontal lean — essentially a one-arm push-up from the wall performed explosively [S9, p.1].

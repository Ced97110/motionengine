# Containing-Opponent Drill

## Objective
Improve the ability to change direction and react to an opponent by having one athlete attempt to break through a defensive wall of 3–4 players over an 80-yard course. [S8, p.189]

## Setup
- 4–5 players (1 attacker + 3–4 defenders)
- Cones marking a 20 yards wide × 80 yards long (18 × 73 meters) rectangle
- Defenders line up across the width at one end, attacker stands on the end line

## Execution
1. 3 or 4 defenders face the attacker in a line across the 20-yard width, acting as a wall.
2. Defenders backpedal and move side to side to prevent the attacker from sprinting through their line.
3. Attacker attempts to find and exploit a gap to sprint through the defensive line.
4. Drill continues the length of the course (80 yards) or until the attacker breaks through.

## Coaching Points
- Cue (defenders): "Communicate and stay connected — close gaps fast."
- Cue (attacker): "Read the gaps — change direction instantly when you see space."
- Defenders must maintain a connected line, not letting gaps form between players.
- Attacker should use explosive cuts and direction changes, not just straight sprints.
- Watch for: defenders crossing their feet or losing lateral position, attacker telegraphing direction changes.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 2 defenders vs. 1 attacker over a shorter distance (20 yards).
2. **Intermediate**: 3 defenders vs. 1 attacker over 40 yards.
3. **Advanced**: Full 4 defenders vs. 1 attacker over 80 yards as described, with defenders allowed to sprint if attacker breaks through.

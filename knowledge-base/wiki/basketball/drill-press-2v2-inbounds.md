# Full-Court Press 2-on-2 Inbounds Drill (O'Brien)

## Objective
Extend the 1-on-1 press drill to 2-on-2, practicing inbounds denial in both White (inbounder covered) and Black (inbounder free) press alignments, and triggering a live trap off the speed dribble.

## Setup
- Half-side of the court only (imaginary out-of-bounds along the center line)
- Inbounder is now a live player (not the coach)
- **White Press version**: one defender covers the inbounder; the other covers the receiver
- **Black Press version**: the defender is in the off position — not guarding the inbounder; both defenders focus on receivers

## Execution
1. Coach designates White or Black press alignment before each rep
2. Defenders work to deny the inbounds pass in the limited half-court space
3. If the ball is successfully inbounded, play is **live 2-on-2**
4. Defenders look to **force the speed dribble** and get a trap
5. When trapping: do NOT worry about leaving a man open — focus on trapping and getting a piece of the pass
6. If the pass is completed after the trap, defenders sprint out and **back-tip** the new ball handler

## Coaching Points
- Narrow space: should be very difficult to inbound against either press — builds front-line confidence
- Emphasize deflecting the inbounds pass on every rep — "believe you can deflect the next pass"
- Speed dribble triggers the trap — reinforce this constantly in drill work
- Both defenders must communicate: "Ball!" on the catch, "Trap!" when speed dribble occurs
- White press builds aggression at the inbounder; Black press builds denial skill on receivers

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach calls White or Black before each rep; walk through positions before live play
2. **Intermediate**: Full live 2-on-2 with both press variants
3. **Advanced**: Offense plays freely with a third player as safety valve behind the half-line

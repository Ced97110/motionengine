# In-and-Out Dribble Maneuver (Drill #28)

## Objective
Develop the in-and-out dribble — a one-handed fake crossover that attacks the gap between the defender's feet without actually crossing the ball over to the other hand.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- 4 lines at one baseline
- Use **tape to mark 2×2 foot (.6×.6 m) boxes** at midcourt and approximately 20 feet (6 m) from each baseline (3 boxes per lane)
- 1 basketball per player
- Duration: 1 minute

<!-- DIAGRAM: Four columns of players at baseline. Three taped 2×2 boxes per column at midcourt and ~20 ft from each baseline. Players execute the in-and-out move at each box, with straight-line dribble segments between boxes. [S15, p.63] -->

## Execution
1. Players line up in four columns at the baseline.
2. Players dribble down the court, **stopping at each box** to execute the **in-and-out dribbling fake**.
3. **The in-and-out move**: Bring the dribble in front of the body with hand on top of the ball (semi-high bounce); when the ball is out in front, **pull it back** to its original side by cupping the side of the ball — simulating a crossover without committing to one.
4. After the fake, **explode in an almost straight line** down the floor with a hard speed dribble for a step or two. This simulates blowing past the defender.
5. Before the next box, **return to a control-dribble stance**.
6. At the far end, execute a **reverse (or front) pivot** and return, using the in-and-out at each box.
7. Players dribble **down and back with the right hand first** (learning the move going both toward the center and toward the sideline), then **down and back with the left hand** before passing.
8. **Jump-stop** about 10 feet (3 m) from the next teammate and deliver a **chest or bounce pass**.
9. All players repeat the sequence.

## Options
- **Race format**: Guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Advanced Progression
1. **Alternating dual-ball**: One ball in each hand; alternate bounces while driving.
2. **Simultaneous dual-ball**: Both balls touch hands and floor at exactly the same time.

## Coaching Points
- **Eyes downcourt**: Eyes on the downcourt area — **never** on the ball or the move during execution.
- **Step in, then step out**: After the step in (with head/shoulder fakes), the step out should be in an **almost straight line** down the floor to leave the defender behind.
- **Explosion timing**: Explode on the **step out** — not during or before the fake.
- **One hand only**: The in-and-out uses only one hand; there is no crossover. Keep the ball to the side and under control.
- **Ball protection**: Keep ball 2–3 feet from the knee; body between ball and defender.
- **Fingerpads**: Always dribble with the first pads of the fingers.
- **Keep defenders on their heels**: Use excessive head and shoulder fakes before every move.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary — practice the pull-back motion without moving. Focus on keeping one hand on top, then beside the ball.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-court bilateral drill with box stops and pivot/pass at end.
3. **Advanced**: Dual-ball; add a live defender between each box.

# Rim Runs Drill (D'Antoni Secondary Break)

## Objective
Train big men to set a side screen on the wing, then roll opposite to the basket to receive a lob pass — the "rim run" action that creates layup and dunk opportunities on the secondary break.

## Setup
- Half court (run simultaneously on both halves)
- One coach **with a ball** on a wing
- One coach **without a ball** on the opposite wing
- Line of players in the middle of the half-court

## Execution

### Version 1: Roll to Lob
1. Coach with ball dribbles toward the baseline
2. First player in line steps up and sets a **side screen** for the dribbling coach
3. Coach goes around the screen and reverses the ball — passing it to the coach on the other side
4. After setting the screen, the player **rolls to the basket on the side opposite the ball**
5. Receives a lob pass from the second coach for a layup or dunk [S7, p.172]

### Version 2: Short Corner Jump Shot
1. Same setup: coach dribbles baseline, player sets side screen, coach reverses to the other side
2. Instead of rolling to the rim, the player **cuts straight to the opposite short corner**
3. Receives the ball in the short corner and takes a jump shot [S7, p.173]

## Coaching Points
- The roll must go **opposite the ball** — the screener exploits the gap created when the defense follows the ball reversal
- Timing of the roll: leave immediately as the coach reverses the ball — don't wait
- For the lob: player must "show a target hand" above their shoulder as they roll to the rim
- For the short corner variation: player must stop under control in the short corner and be ready to shoot immediately on the catch
- Run simultaneously on both halves to get more reps per minute — all big men work at the same time [S7, p.173]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk-through angles — where the screen is, where the roll goes
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed with coaches, both versions
3. **Advanced**: Add a live defensive player trailing the cutter to make the lob catch and finish competitive

# Dip Progression Chain (Muscle-Up Supplemental Training)

## Objective
Systematically build the pressing strength required for the muscle-up's dip-press phase — from the easiest bent-knee floor dip through to horizontal bar dips — using a 10-step progressive overload chain.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Various surfaces: floor, parallel bars, perpendicular bar, horizontal bar
- Stable box or bench for foot placement in early steps
- No other equipment required

## Execution

### Step 1: Bent Dips
- Feet on the floor, knees well-bent — takes the bulk of bodyweight off the arms.
- Hands on a low surface (bench or low bar) behind the hips.
- Lower and press the hips toward the floor and back up.

### Step 2: Straight Dips
- Same as bent dips but legs straightened.
- Straight legs force the upper body to take on significantly more of the load [S9, p.234].

### Step 3: Feet-Elevated Dips
- Feet raised onto a box or object, shifting center of gravity backward toward the hands.
- More load transferred to the arms and shoulders.

### Step 4: Feet-Up Parallel Bar Dips
- First parallel bar variation — easiest way to begin work on the bars.
- Feet resting on a box or ledge forward of the bars [S9, p.234].

### Step 5: Self-Assisted Parallel Bar Dips (Version 1)
- Full parallel bar dip with one leg pushing off a surface on the positive (pressing) rep to assist through sticking points [S9, p.235].

### Step 6: Self-Assisted Parallel Bar Dips (Version 2)
- Similar self-assistance but pushing off the instep (top of foot), making the self-assist harder.

### Step 7: Parallel Bar Dips (Classic)
- Full unassisted dip on the parallel bars.
- **Lean back** to emphasize triceps; **angle forward** to involve shoulders and chest more [S9, p.235].

### Step 8: Legs-Forward Parallel Bar Dips
- Feet thrust forward in front of the hips.
- Changes center of gravity, making the press significantly harder.

### Step 9: Perpendicular Dips
- Bar runs perpendicular (right-angle) to the athlete's body.
- Great intermediate stage between parallel bars and the horizontal bar [S9, p.236].

### Step 10: Horizontal Bar Dips
- Dipping on top of a horizontal pull-up bar.
- Achieving this signals sufficient pressing strength to complete a muscle-up [S9, p.236].

## Coaching Points
- **No fixed progression standards**: Milk each step until you are expert at it, then move on. Intuition-based progression [S9, p.233].
- **Dips for the muscle-up, push-ups for life**: Dips are an ancillary drill to support muscle-up development; push-ups are the preferred long-term pressing exercise [S9, p.233].
- **The horizontal bar dip is the goal marker**: A comfortable horizontal bar dip means muscle-up pressing strength is present.
- **Leaning forward vs. back on parallel bar dips**: Leaning forward activates more chest/shoulder; leaning back isolates triceps more.
- **Russian dips** (lower to elbows on parallel bars, shift forward, press back up) mimic the pull-over pattern and serve as a useful ancillary drill [S9, p.236].

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Steps 1–3 (floor and bench-based)
2. **Intermediate**: Steps 4–7 (parallel bar entry and classic dip)
3. **Advanced**: Steps 8–10 (legs forward, perpendicular, horizontal bar)

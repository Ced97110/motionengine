# 40-Yard Backpedal-Forward

## Objective
Improve agility, ability to transition from backpedal to sprint (and sprint to backpedal), and conditioning.

## Setup
- 1+ players
- Three parallel lines: starting line, line 5 yards away, line 10 yards away
- Athlete starts with back to the starting line

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance with your back to the starting line.
2. Backpedal 5 yards (4.6 m) to the first line, touch it with either foot, sprint forward back to the starting line, and touch it with either foot.
3. Backpedal 10 yards (9 m) to the second line, touch it with either foot, sprint forward back to the starting line, and touch it with either foot.
4. Backpedal 5 yards (4.6 m) to the first line, touch it with either foot, and sprint back to the starting line.

## Coaching Points
- The backpedal-to-sprint transition is the most important skill — plant the foot hard, drop the hips, and drive forward
- Maximize forward lean immediately on transition to sprint
- Stay on balls of feet throughout the backpedal — do not heel-strike
- "Get your hips through" on every backpedal-to-sprint change
- Eyes forward during the backpedal — simulate tracking an offensive player

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow, controlled execution with emphasis on clean transitions
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed, timed
3. **Advanced**: React to coach's signal for when to pivot (not at a preset line)

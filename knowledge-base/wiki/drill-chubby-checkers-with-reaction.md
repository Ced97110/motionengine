# Chubby Checkers With Reaction (Quick Feet)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, court sense, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, first-step quickness, and general athleticism. [S8, p.222]

## Setup
- 1 player, agility ladder
- Start in a two-point stance, **facing the ladder on its far left side**
- Coach positioned at the side or end of the ladder

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance facing the ladder, positioned to the far left side.
2. Jump **laterally to the right** with both feet — land with the **left foot inside the first square** and the **right foot outside** the first square.
3. Immediately upon landing, jump **laterally to the right** again with both feet — land with **both feet outside** the ladder to the right.
4. The hips should rotate so that feet are **diagonal to the ladder** when the left foot is in the square, and **perpendicular** to it when both feet are outside.
5. Repeat this sequence, advancing the length of the ladder.
6. **Simultaneously**, react to the coach's visual commands throughout the drill.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Chubby Checkers pattern on p.222 — lateral bilateral jumps with rotating foot alignment: one foot in/one out (diagonal), then both outside (perpendicular), advancing down the ladder -->

## Coaching Points
- Hip rotation drives the foot angle change — the feet follow the hips
- Quick, explosive lateral jumps with minimal ground contact time
- Keep the body balanced over the center of mass between each jump
- "Hips lead the feet — rotate and land!"
- Divide attention: footwork is automatic, eyes track coach cues

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Learn the lateral bilateral jump pattern without cues
2. **Intermediate**: Add coach visual cues at game speed
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Odd/even number filtering
   - Coach semicircle for peripheral vision
   - Multiple coaches at the periphery [S8, p.222]
   - Sport-specific skill at end of ladder

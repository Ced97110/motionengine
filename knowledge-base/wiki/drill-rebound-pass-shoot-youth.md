# Rebound-Pass-Shoot (Drill 94)

## Objective
Develop shooting technique, rebounding pivots, and game-speed passing in a continuous multi-player rotation that integrates agility and conditioning.

## Setup
- **4 players**: Player 1 (shooter), Player 2 (passer), Players 3 & 4 (rebounders)
- Half-court, near the basket
- Both Player 1 and Player 4 begin with a basketball
- 4 areas of the floor used; total drill time = 4 minutes

## Execution
1. **Line up** per the formation: 1 on the wing/elbow area, 2 near the lane, 3 and 4 under the basket on each block.
2. **Player 1 shoots** for 30 seconds.
3. **Player 4 immediately passes** to Player 2 as soon as Player 1 lands; Player 2 passes to Player 1 for another shot.
4. **Players 3 or 4 rebound** Player 1's shots and pass to Player 2, who passes back to Player 1.
5. After 30 seconds, **players rotate**: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 1.
6. After a complete rotation, the **shooter lines up on the opposite side** of the floor. Each player shoots for 30 seconds for another 2 minutes.
7. Repeat across **four floor areas** to complete the 4-minute drill.

## Coaching Points
- **Technique over quantity** — do not count shots taken; poor technique defeats the purpose.
- Quickness, not speed: crisp movements without sacrificing form.
- If emphasizing rebounding: watch for proper **body positioning and pivots** — one strong-side, one weak-side rebounder; cover both primary and secondary rebounding areas.
- Tell cutters to **dip before the cut** — all cuts must be crisp and quick.
- "Don't let shooters be lazy" — every shot must be picture perfect.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Straight catch-and-shoot with no movement — develop basic shooting form.
2. **Intermediate**: Player 1 makes a **cut** before receiving the pass; or uses a **rocker-step or dribble move** before shooting.
3. **Advanced**: Players 3 and 4 face **outside** on each shot, then execute reverse pivot (Player 3) or front pivot (Player 4) before rebounding.

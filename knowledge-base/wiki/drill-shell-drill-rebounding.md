# Defensive Shell Drill — Rebounding Emphasis

## Objective
Train both offensive and defensive rebounders to react correctly when a shot is taken from multiple court locations, practicing box-out assignments for defenders guarding the shooter, one-pass-away players, and two-plus-pass-away players simultaneously.

## Setup
- **Players:** 4 offense vs. 4 defense (half court).
- **Court area:** Half court.
- **Equipment:** One basketball.
- Standard 4-on-4 shell drill alignment: 4 offensive players spread around the perimeter.

## Execution
1. **Defense allows ball movement:** Defenders let the perimeter players pass the ball around for a coach-specified number of passes.
2. **Defense allows a shot:** After the target number of passes, the defense allows a player to shoot from their current location.
3. **Shot triggers box-outs:** All four defenders immediately execute box-out assignments based on their distance from the shooter:
   - **Defender on the shooter:** Box out the shooter.
   - **Defender one pass away:** Box out their offensive player using appropriate technique.
   - **Defender two-plus passes away:** Box out their offensive player; they have the farthest box-out responsibility.
4. **Offense attacks the boards:** All four offensive players attempt to defeat their box-out and offensive rebound using the three escape techniques (butt-spin, swim, step-back slash).
5. **Offensive rebounders:** Locate the ball as it is shot, anticipate where it lands off the rim/backboard, then try to get there.
6. **Rotate:** After several shots, offense and defense switch roles.
7. Players rotate court locations (left wing, right wing, corner, above FT line extended) so all distances and angles are practiced.

## Coaching Points
- **Defenders:** "Find your man first, then find the ball — make contact, hold, then go get it."
- **Offensive rebounders:** "Locate the ball off the shooter's hand — start moving before the box-out is set."
- Use different shooters from different spots every rep to change the defensive scenarios continuously.
- Watch offensive rebounders for the step-back-slash read: "Attack the shoulder they're NOT looking over."
- Players must constantly reevaluate positioning as passes move around — distance from the ball determines technique.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Defenders hold box-outs for 3 full seconds (no offensive resistance) to build muscle memory.
2. **Intermediate:** Offense competes to reach the ball; defense must secure the rebound to win the rep.
3. **Advanced:** Live and continuous — made shots or secured defensive rebounds trigger immediate outlet and fast break; builds transition habits out of the rebounding drill.

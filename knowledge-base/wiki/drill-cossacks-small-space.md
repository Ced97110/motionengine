# Cossacks (Small Space Drill)

## Objective
Build lower-body power through a stretched range of motion and develop tension-flexibility (supple strength) and balance through alternating low-stance lateral leg extensions — compensating for the shallow range of motion typical in jump training.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment
- Small flat space (2-3 feet wide is sufficient)
- Named after the traditional dance of the Cossack people

## Execution
1. Assume a low squat stance with feet wide apart.
2. Bob up and down slightly while stretching one leg out to the side in a low lateral extension.
3. Alternate legs, stretching each out in turn while maintaining the low squat position.
4. Perform rhythmically for reps or time (can be done for higher reps than the progressive jump chain steps).
5. Focus on feeling the stretch in the hips, adductors, and ankles on each extended leg.

## Coaching Points
- Jump training builds power in a shallow (mid-range) range of motion — Cossacks fill the gap by training power in the stretched position
- "To power-up the knees, hips and ankles *on the stretch*, you need Cossacks" [S9, p.75]
- Can be performed rhythmically for higher reps — works well as a warm-up or finisher
- Builds tension-flexibility (supple strength) — the ability to be strong while stretched, not just flexible
- Also develops single-leg balance as weight shifts to the loaded leg

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: High stance, small lateral extension — feel the stretch gently
2. **Intermediate**: Standard low-stance alternating extensions
3. **Advanced**: Deeper squat, full lateral leg extension (toes of extended leg pointing up); add a slight hop/pulse between extensions

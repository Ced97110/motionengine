# Shooting Trigger Words and Anchor Words

## Summary
Hal Wissel's trigger/anchor word system is the bridge between correct shooting mechanics and confident shooting performance. **Trigger words** (cue words) are concise, one-syllable personalizations that activate correct mechanics during the shot. **Anchor words** reinforce successful outcomes in the shooter's mind. Together, they reduce conscious thought — which disrupts rhythm — and promote automatic execution. [S7, pp.8-9]

The system is used both in practice (to build mechanical habits) and in games (to maintain focus and quickly recover from misses). Mental practice — relaxing, speaking key words, and visualizing the shot going in — is as important as physical repetition.

## When to Use
- Every shooting repetition in practice
- After a missed shot (immediate correction + visualization sequence)
- Pre-game visualization routines
- When a player's rhythm has become slow or uneven (overthinking signal)

## Key Principles
1. **Words should be concise** — preferably one syllable; they must fire at the speed of the shot
2. **Words should be personalized** — players experiment with multiple options until they find what works for them
3. **Trigger words key mechanics** — they activate specific technical components of the shot
4. **Anchor words reinforce success** — they condition the brain to expect and visualize a made basket
5. **Say them aloud** — speaking words aloud is more effective than thinking them silently
6. **Trigger + anchor = complete cue string** — a three-word rhythm string (trigger–trigger–anchor) said in the cadence of the shot is ideal for beginners

## Trigger Word Examples
| Word | What It Keys |
|------|--------------|
| "High!" | Start shot high; prevent lowering the ball |
| "Straight!" | Shooting hand goes straight to basket; don't turn wrist |
| "Front!" | Shooting hand facing the rim |
| "Point!" | Release off the index finger |
| "Up!" | High arc |
| "Through!" | Complete follow-through (shoulders, arm, wrist, finger) |
| "Head in!" | Head and shoulders toward basket on follow-through; prevents leaning back |
| "Legs!" | Use leg power for the shot |
| "Down and up!" | Leg down-and-up rhythm and range |

## Anchor Word Examples
"Through!" "In!" "Yes!" "Net!" "Swish!" "Whoosh!" "Face!"

## Three-Word Rhythm String Example
If trigger words are "legs" and "through" and anchor word is "yes":
→ **"Legs–through–yes!"** spoken in the rhythm of the shot motion, one syllable per beat, said aloud.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Words that are too long or complex** → They don't fire at shooting speed; trim to one syllable
2. **Using generic words that don't mean anything to the player** → Words must be personally resonant; help each player find their own
3. **Only using trigger words, not anchor words** → Anchor words are what prevent negative reinforcement after a miss
4. **Thinking the words rather than saying them aloud** → Speaking aloud is more neurologically effective

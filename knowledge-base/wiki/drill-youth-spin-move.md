# Spin (Reverse) Move (Drill 14)

## Objective
Teach the spin (reverse) move — a 360-degree pivot with the back to the defender that repositions the dribbler past the defensive player — used against overplay or as a fake into the counter half-spin.

## Setup
- Individual drill (teaching): one coach, one player
- Drilling: players line up in three lines at mid-court; coach at the free-throw line area
- Mark a **2-foot × 2-foot (.6m × .6m) square** using tape near a line (e.g., free-throw line)
- Dribbler starts ~10 feet (3m) away, dribbles toward the coach
- Begin with a control dribble; progress to a speed dribble
- This move is the **counter to the half-spin move**

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Dribbler starts ~10 feet away and dribbles toward the coach (control dribble).
2. At the square, dribbler performs the spin move.
3. Coach moves in the direction of the step-out fake — teaching the *when* while teaching the *how*. Coach may also begin in an overplay position.

## Coaching Points

### When to Use
- Against a defender who is overplaying the dribbling side
- Against a defender who retreats from a jab step (setting up the spin to attack the retreating space)
- This move is the counter to the half-spin

### How to Execute (Right-Hand Dribble Example)
1. Dribbler **fake-steps to the right** with the right foot. May also use head and shoulder fakes. If overplayed to the right, no fake is needed.
2. Dribbler places the **left foot aligned to the middle of the defender** (can be a jab step with the left foot).
3. Dribbler **reverse pivots** — executing a **360-degree turn** — putting the back to the defender, using the **left foot as the pivot foot**.
4. Dribbler **cups the ball with the right hand** and pulls it all the way around during the pivot. Does NOT change hands until the pivot is complete.
5. Dribbler positions the **right foot at least even with — preferably beyond — the defender's right foot**.
6. Dribbler swings the right foot all the way around and **well in front of the defender**, then **explodes to the basket** in a straight-line dribble.

### Progression
- After mastering with the dominant hand, practice with the **weaker hand**.

## Common Mistakes
1. Changing hands before the pivot is complete → ball gets stolen; cup and protect through the full turn
2. Right foot doesn't get past the defender's right foot → no positional advantage gained
3. Spinning into the defender instead of around them → left foot must align to defender's middle, not outside
4. Losing speed on exit → explode immediately once the right foot lands in position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Control dribble into spin move with passive coach-defender
2. **Intermediate**: Speed dribble into spin move
3. **Advanced**: Use in combination with the half-spin counter (Drill 15/16)

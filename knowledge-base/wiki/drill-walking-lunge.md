# Walking Lunge

## Objective
Develop lower-body strength, dynamic balance, and hip flexor conditioning through a continuous lunge-walking pattern that closely mimics the multi-directional demands of basketball.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- A pair of dumbbells held in each hand at the sides, neutral grip
- Open floor space (gym floor, corridor, or court)
- Feet slightly narrower than shoulder-width at start

## Execution
1. Take **one large step forward** with either leg.
2. Flex the knee of the lead leg until the front thigh approaches **parallel to the floor**.
3. Keep pressure on the front foot; use the rear foot for balance.
4. As the front knee flexes, the heel of the rear foot lifts and the rear knee flexes.
5. Lunge to a position where the **front knee is over the front foot** and the **rear knee is 1-2 inches (3-5 cm) off the floor** (b).
6. **Instead of pushing back** to the start (as in the forward step lunge), **push through the front foot** to step the **rear foot forward** (c) — this creates continuous forward lunge-walking.
7. Alternate legs to lunge-walk forward for prescribed distance or reps.

## Coaching Points
- **Torso stays erect** — do not lean forward or backward
- Back knee points at the floor and stays in line with the body
- Adjust stride length — too long or too short disrupts balance and depth
- Arms hang perpendicular to the floor throughout — **do not let dumbbells swing** forward and backward with each rep
- **Inhale** during the downward movement; **exhale** after the sticking point of the upward movement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight walking lunge — master step width, depth, and upright posture
2. **Intermediate**: Dumbbell walking lunge (light-moderate load)
3. **Advanced**: Dumbbell walking lunge with a longer distance target (20-40 yards); barbell variation if space allows

## Common Mistakes
1. **Leaning forward** → shifts load to toes → cue "chest tall, eyes forward"
2. **Step too long or too short** → imbalance → adjust stride until full depth achieved
3. **Rear knee smashing the floor** → lack of eccentric control → cue "hover 1-2 inches above floor"
4. **Dumbbells swinging** → disrupts posture → cue "arms dead at sides, perpendicular to floor"

# Reverse Lunge

## Objective
Build unilateral quad, glute, and hip strength to improve horizontal and vertical propulsion, deceleration, direction change, and proprioception for basketball.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1
- **Location:** gym floor or weight room
- **Equipment:** bodyweight, or dumbbells for added resistance
- Can be used as a lead-up exercise to the [[exercise-reverse-step-down-from-box]] [S2, p.20]

## Execution
1. Stand erect with feet shoulder-width apart. Hands on hips or holding a dumbbell in each hand.
2. Step directly back with the right leg and lower the body toward the floor by bending both hips and knees while maintaining an erect posture and a vertical tibia on the left (forward) leg. Descend until the left leg is bent to 90° at the hip and knee.
3. Return to the starting position.
4. Complete all repetitions with the right leg stepping back, then repeat with the left leg stepping back. [S2, p.20]

## Coaching Points
- **"Keep the front shin vertical"** — forward knee drift past the toes increases patellar stress.
- **"Erect torso"** — leaning forward shifts load away from the glutes and quads.
- Step directly back — lateral deviation reduces the training stimulus and increases ankle instability risk.
- Maintain neuromuscular knee control — do not let the front knee collapse inward.
- Beginners: start with bodyweight before adding dumbbells.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Bodyweight reverse lunge, emphasis on posture and range of motion.
2. **Intermediate:** Dumbbell reverse lunge with full 90° descent.
3. **Advanced:** Reverse lunge to [[exercise-reverse-step-down-from-box]] progression; add a knee drive at the top for explosive power.

## Concepts Taught
- [[leg-strength-for-basketball]] — quad and glute strength for propulsion and deceleration
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — deceleration pattern mirrors the lunge position during cuts

## Basketball Application
Strengthens muscles of the thighs and hips for both horizontal and vertical body propulsion, and enhances the ability to decelerate and change direction to avoid an opponent. Balance and proprioception improvements assist in positioning of the lower extremities during running, cutting, and jumping, and contribute to injury prevention. [S2, pp.20-21]

**Primary muscles:** quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius), gluteus maximus, gluteus medius

**Secondary muscles:** hamstrings (semitendinosus, semimembranosus, biceps femoris), gluteus minimus, adductor magnus

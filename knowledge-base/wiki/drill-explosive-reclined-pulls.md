# Explosive Reclined Pulls

## Objective
Improve power and quickness in the upper-body pulling musculature through explosive rope pulls from a reclined position. [S8, p.165]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Secured bar or hook at height
- Equipment: thick rope (at least 2 in. / 5 cm diameter, non-slippery surface) draped over the bar/hook

## Execution
1. Hold the rope with arm extended, body reclined to 45 degrees, straight with a tight core.
2. Explosively pull on the rope to quickly elevate the body.
3. Regrip the rope at the new position (higher on the rope) before the body descends.
4. Repeat the explosive pull-and-regrip for prescribed reps.

## Coaching Points
- The regrip between reps is the plyometric element — it demands fast hands and strong forearms.
- Core must remain rigid — a hollow body position prevents energy loss through the trunk.
- Use a thick, non-slippery rope for safety; thin ropes allow slippage.
- Recline angle can be varied — shallower angle = greater difficulty.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slower controlled pull with deliberate regrip; use a 60° recline angle.
2. **Intermediate**: 45° recline; increase pull speed.
3. **Advanced**: 30° recline (nearly horizontal); maximize explosiveness and regrip speed.

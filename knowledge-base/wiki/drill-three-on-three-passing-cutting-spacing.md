# Three-on-Three Passing, Cutting, and Spacing (Drill 64)

## Objective
Develop sharp cutting and accurate passing by prohibiting dribbling in a live 3-on-3 setting — players must score entirely through motion offense cuts and passes while maintaining 15-foot spacing.

## Setup
- 3 offensive players, 3 defenders
- Half court
- No dribbling allowed
- Rotate: offense ↔ defense after 2 minutes
- Equipment: 1 basketball, optional cones

## Execution

### Intermediate
1. Three offensive players and three defenders spread across the half court.
2. Defenders play live defense — offense makes the cuts dictated by how defenders play.
3. **No dribbling**: players must score through cutting and passing only, adhering to all motion offense rules.
4. After 2 minutes, offense and defense switch roles.
5. Example sequence:
   - Player 1 V-cuts to the side high post. Player 2 passes to 1 and **immediately backdoor-cuts** (Rule 1: use the cut the defender gives you).
   - Player 3 simultaneously **backdoor-cuts** on the baseline (Rule 3: every player must move on every pass).
   - Player 2 V-cuts back to a side post position (maintaining 15-foot spacing).
   - On the next pass, all three must make another cut — the sequence is infinite and unprescribed.
6. Drill continues until the offense scores or loses possession.

### Options
1. **Pass limit**: designate a maximum number of passes; offense must score within that number or switch with defense
2. **Defense earns rotation**: defense must stop the offense two consecutive times before switching to offense
3. **Cut emphasis**: award points each time the offense successfully creates and uses a specific cut type
4. **Layups only**: restricting to layup finishes demands many more cuts and higher cutting quality
5. **Turns format**: give each team the ball 5 times; most points wins

## Coaching Points
- "Do not allow dribbling in this drill" — this is the defining constraint; enforce it strictly [S15, p.134]
- Each player must maintain 15-foot spacing — without the dribble, spacing awareness becomes critical
- Every player must move on every pass — with no dribble, the rule becomes very visible when violated
- Reinforce the two primary cutting rules: use the cut the defender gives you; maintain 15-foot spacing
- The no-dribble constraint forces passers to be more accurate and cutters to be crisper with their timing

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Live 3-on-3, no dribbling, all four rules apply
2. **Advanced**: Pass limits + layups-only restriction + defense earns rotation with two consecutive stops

# Cutting, Outlet Pass, and Shooting Drill

## Objective
Train proper footwork and handwork for cutters/shooters, rebounders, and outlet passers simultaneously in a game-realistic multi-player drill that reinforces inside-heel pivoting, shot creation off the cut, and proper rebounding/outlet techniques. [S6, pp.169-171]

## Setup
- **Players:** 4 pairs (8 players) per basket
- **Court area:** Full perimeter around the basket (from corner to corner)
- **Equipment:** One basketball per pair
- **Formation:** One player in the lane (passer/rebounder); one player on the perimeter (cutter/shooter). Cutter starts facing the basket.
- **First session:** Catch and shoot in front of the three-point line
- **Second session:** Catch and shoot behind the three-point line

## Execution
1. **Cutter starts** facing the basket and cuts **to the right**, getting hands and feet ready to catch and shoot. Inside foot = left foot (pivot foot).
2. **Passer** delivers a pass to the cutting shooter.
3. **Cutter/shooter** catches the pass: drives the inside (left) heel into the floor, swings the free (right) foot around to square up the shoulders, and shoots.
4. **Rebounder** (the original passer) rebounds the shot while the shooter prepares for the next cut.
5. **Rebounder practices outlet pass technique:** Pivot off the **outside foot** (away from the imaginary opponent), making a **front pivot**; swing the inside foot around; execute a **two-hand overhead outlet pass** back to the cutting shooter. [S6, p.170]
6. When the rebounder secures the rebound, the **cutter immediately begins the next cut in the same direction**, moving around the perimeter.
7. The cutter continues rotating around the perimeter until reaching the deep corner, then runs out-of-bounds.
8. Sprint across the baseline and restart in the deep corner on the **opposite side**, cutting to the left (inside foot = right foot).
9. After a designated number of shots, **passer and cutter switch roles**.

## Variations

### Variation 2 — Shot Fake Before Dribble
After catching the pass, the cutter/shooter does **not** shoot immediately. Instead:
- Catch → **shot fake** → 1 or 2 dribbles → shoot off the dribble
- Same inside-heel pivot footwork applies after the dribbles
- Same free-foot square-up required

### Variation 3 — Game-Realistic Shot Fake + Required Dribbles
- Execute a game-realistic shot fake, then dribble a **required number of times** (set by coach) before shooting.

### Counterclockwise Direction
- Run drill going counterclockwise so players shoot while cutting to the left, training the right foot as the pivot foot.

## Coaching Points
- **Cutter's inside heel** must drive down the instant the ball touches the shooting (target) hand — identical timing whether coming from a cut or from a dribble.
- **Rebounder:** Pivot off the outside foot (not toward the imaginary opponent) before making the outlet pass — this simulates game-realistic outlet passing technique.
- **Hands ready early** — fingers spread, shooting hand positioned before the catch.
- **Full square-up** — free foot arc must be complete before shooting; half-squares result in fading or off-line shots.
- Add **individual, group, or team goals** (makes per rotation, consecutive swishes) to build pressure and competition. [S6, p.169]
- This drill works going in **both directions** — do not let players only cut in their comfortable direction.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Version 1 only — immediate catch-and-shoot, inside-heel pivot, both directions.
2. **Intermediate:** Add the shot-fake → 1-dribble variation (Variation 2).
3. **Advanced:** Shot-fake + required 2 dribbles before shooting; move shooting spots to behind the three-point line; add team make-quota competition.

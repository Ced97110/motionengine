# Bull in the Ring (Rebounding)

## Objective
Develop offensive rebounding technique, body positioning, tipping skill, pump fakes, and mental toughness through a competitive multi-player battle in the lane.

## Setup
- 4+ players; 3 active in the lane at one time, others in line outside the lane
- Coach positioned at the free-throw line or elbow area to shoot
- One basketball
- Half court, inside the lane

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players 1, 2, and 3 begin inside the lane. Coach shoots the basketball.
2. All three battle for the rebound. The rebounder may:
   - Put the ball back up immediately (catch and shoot before landing)
   - Tip the ball into the basket with fingertips
   - Use a jab step and roll, then shoot
   - Pump fake to get the other two off balance, then shoot
3. The other two players may gently bump the ball-holder with their bodies (physical contact, no slapping or fouling).
4. If the shot is missed, all three battle for the next rebound.
5. **First player to score 3 baskets** rotates out to the end of the line. Player 4 steps into the lane.
6. All remaining players keep their cumulative totals (they do not reset to 0 on rotation).
7. If the ball ricochets outside the lane, it goes back to the coach who shoots again.

### Intermediate
- Same as beginner, but **when a new player rotates in, all scores reset to 0**. Every new entry starts a fresh competition.

### Advanced
- Instead of the coach shooting, the coach **tosses** to one of the three players. That player shoots. A made basket counts as one of the three needed to rotate out.

## Coaching Points
- **Catch the rebound with both hands** and shoot before landing if possible — the highest-percentage second-chance shot.
- If two-hand catch is not possible, **tip with fingertips** — never slap.
- When coming down with the ball, use a **pump fake** to get defenders off their feet before going back up.
- **Body positioning** is the foundation of this drill:
  - Lean slightly forward toward the basket; weight on toes
  - Hips in semicrouched position for catlike quickness
  - Knees flexed no more than 135 degrees
  - **Elbows out** from the body — use arms to prevent opponents from getting better position
  - Fingers spread wide, pointing up toward the rim, hands relaxed and flexible
- **Approaching the rebound**:
  - Time your jump to touch the ball at the **highest point** of the jump
  - Use **two hands** to catch — never slap to the other hand
  - After catching, bring ball down in a quick, jerky "plucking" motion while spreading feet in a spread-eagle
  - Land in a balanced, spread position with ball secured at chest; be ready to pump fake
  - If offensive and intending to blast out: swing ball low
  - If defensive: hold ball overhead for outlet pass

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Score keeps; clean contact only; coach shoots.
2. **Intermediate**: Scores reset on each rotation — every entry is a fresh start, increasing pressure.
3. **Advanced**: Coach tosses instead of shooting; player receiving catch must shoot, adding a made-basket route out.

# Incline Pop-Up (Power Pushup Step 1)

## Objective
Develop the initial joint conditioning and elastic pushing power needed for all harder explosive pushup variations, using a reduced-bodyweight incline position.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any solid, secure surface approximately chest height (table, bleacher, box, step)
- No other equipment needed
- Can be done indoors or outdoors

## Execution
1. Find a solid, secure base around chest height
2. Place hands on the surface at approximately shoulder-width
3. Set feet wide enough for balance (beginners wider, advanced narrower)
4. Keep legs, hips, and torso aligned; body slightly inclined toward the surface
5. Bend arms and shoulders, dipping torso a few inches toward the surface (~1/3 depth)
6. Immediately push explosively off the surface, straightening arms
7. Push hard enough so hands leave the surface by several inches
8. As gravity reverses movement, "catch" the surface with hands again
9. Use the elastic rebound to immediately repeat — do NOT pause at the bottom

## Coaching Points
- "Dip, don't lower" — this is a quick elastic movement, not a controlled descent
- Hands should visibly leave the surface on each rep
- Keep hips and trunk aligned — no sagging or piking
- "Apply the rebound" — catch and immediately go again, like a bouncing ball
- Ensure the surface is completely stable before starting — this applies to ALL explosive work [S9, p.84]
- The lower the surface, the greater the bodyweight through the arms — progress by lowering the surface over time

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use a table or work surface at full chest height; wide foot stance
2. **Intermediate**: Lower surface to a bunk, bed, or box height; moderate foot stance
3. **Advanced**: Use the bottom steps of a staircase; reduce foot stance width; advance to Step 2 (kneeling push-off)

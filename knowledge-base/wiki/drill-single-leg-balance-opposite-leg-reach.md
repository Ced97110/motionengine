# Single-Leg Balance With Opposite-Leg Reach

## Objective
Increase single-leg balance by using the opposite (non-stance) leg as a driver through anterior, posterior, and medial reach patterns. [S8, p.131]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor area
- Optional: cones placed at reach targets for anterior, medial, and posterior positions

## Execution
Perform all drills on the left leg first; repeat with a right-side stance and left-leg reach.

1. **Anterior Reach**: Standing on the left leg, reach the right leg forward as far as possible without losing balance. Return without touching the right foot to the ground. Complete all reps without right-foot ground contact. [S8, p.131]

2. **Posterior Reach**: Standing on the left leg, reach the right leg backward as far as possible without losing balance. Return without touching the right foot to the ground. Complete all reps without right-foot ground contact. [S8, p.131]

3. **Medial Reach**: Standing on the left leg, reach the right leg medially (across the body) as far as possible without losing balance. Return without touching the right foot to the ground. Complete all reps without right-foot ground contact. [S8, p.131]

## Coaching Points
- The goal is to complete all reps **without the reaching foot touching the ground** — each touch is a rep failure
- Keep a slight bend in the stance knee — do not lock the joint
- Hips should stay level throughout — resist the tendency to hike the hip on the reaching side
- "Reach and hold" — extend fully before returning; do not bounce or rush the reach
- Progress to more challenging directions after mastering the basic three

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reach with foot touching the ground for balance assistance
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill without ground contact as described
3. **Advanced**: Add perturbation (partner light push) during the balance hold; perform on an unstable surface (BOSU/foam pad)

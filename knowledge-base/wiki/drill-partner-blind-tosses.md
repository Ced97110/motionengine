# Partner Blind Tosses (Sports-Ball Reaction)

## Objective
Improve upper- and lower-body quickness and reaction by responding to a stimulus the athlete cannot anticipate visually — trains peripheral vision and pure reaction speed. [S8, p.152]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Open space
- 1 tennis ball or sport-specific ball (Crazy Z-ball optional for advanced variation)

## Execution
1. Player A (reactor) stands in a **ready athletic position** facing away from Player B (tosser).
2. Player B stands **directly behind** Player A and tosses a ball over one of Player A's shoulders — without announcing which shoulder.
3. Player A reacts the instant the ball appears in their peripheral vision and **sprints to catch the ball before it bounces twice**.
4. Reset and repeat, alternating which shoulder the ball goes over.

## Coaching Points
- Player A must maintain a **true ready position** — not leaning, not tilting toward one side in anticipation
- "React to what you see, not what you expect" — punish anticipated movements by occasionally tosses over the same shoulder twice
- The speed of the first step is the primary performance variable — track improvement in ability to catch before second bounce as distance increases
- Feet should be actively weight-cycling (slight bounce/rock) to enable quicker first step

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Tennis ball tossed gently over a consistent shoulder; catch after first bounce is acceptable
2. **Intermediate**: Random shoulder selection; must catch before second bounce
3. **Advanced**: Use a **Crazy Z-ball** (irregular bounce pattern) — forces continuous tracking adjustment and eliminates any predictive trajectory reading [S8, p.152]

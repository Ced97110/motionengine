# 30-Yard T-Drill

## Objective
Develop agility, conditioning, and flexibility in the hip abductors and adductors while improving multi-directional strength.

## Setup
- 1+ players
- Mark a T-shape: start at the bottom of the T, a marked spot 5 yards forward, lines extending 5 yards left and right from that spot (total 10 yards wide)
- Use cones or court lines

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Sprint forward 5 yards (4.6 m) to the marked spot on the ground (bottom of the T-crossbar).
3. Side-shuffle right 5 yards (4.6 m) and touch the right line with the right hand.
4. Shuffle back left 10 yards (9 m) and touch the far left line with the left hand.
5. Shuffle right 5 yards (4.6 m) back to the marked center spot.
6. Touch the marked spot with either foot and backpedal 5 yards (4.6 m) through the start line to the finish.

<!-- DIAGRAM: T-shape with start at bottom, 5-yard sprint up the stem, 5-yard shuffle right, 10-yard shuffle left, 5-yard shuffle back right, backpedal down the stem. [S8, p.80] -->

## Coaching Points
- Stay in a lateral shuffle — no crossing feet during the side-to-side legs
- Keep a low center of gravity throughout the shuffle segments
- Touch each line cleanly — sloppy touches indicate insufficient deceleration
- Drive arm action on the sprint and backpedal segments
- Use short, choppy steps on the backpedal to minimize stopping distance

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk-through at 50% speed; emphasize clean direction changes
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed timed execution
3. **Advanced**: Add a defensive slide instead of shuffle on the lateral legs

# Stack Double (BLOB vs Man-to-Man)

## Overview
A BLOB play out of the stack set specifically designed to free the team's best shooter for an open midrange or three-point shot on the wing. Uses a ball-reversal sequence and a staggered double screen to get the shooter space off the dribble fake. [S4, pp.25-26]

## Formation
- **3** (inbounder): baseline
- **2** (best shooter), **4**, **5**: stacked on ball-side edge of the key
- **1** (PG): weak-side elbow (exception to the stack)

## Phases

### Phase 1: Ball Entry to Perimeter
- **4** pops out to the perimeter and receives the inbound pass from **3**.
- Simultaneously, **2** drags their defender lower by taking steps toward the hoop and calling for the ball (decoy move).

### Phase 2: Ball Reversal to Point Guard
- After inbounding, **3** sprints the baseline and clears to the **weak-side wing**.
- **1** cuts to the top of the key and receives a pass from **4**.

### Phase 3: Dribble Fake to Move Defense
- **1 takes a dribble in the opposite direction** (away from 2's side) to get defenders moving toward the middle and open the passing lane to 2. [S4, p.26]
- This misdirection is essential — **1 must sell the fake** toward the top of the key to shift the defense. [S4, p.26]

### Phase 4: Staggered Screen for Shooter
- **4 and 5 immediately set a staggered double screen** for **2**.
- **2** reads their defender and cuts off the staggered screen to the wing.
- **1** delivers the pass to **2** for either the midrange or **three-point shot on the wing**.

## Key Coaching Points
- **Players should not face the direction they are going** at the beginning of the play — telegraphing movement tips off the defense. [S4, p.26]
- **1 must sell the fake dribble** toward 3 at the top of the key to shift defenders across before passing to 2. [S4, p.26]
- **2 must read their defender** coming off the staggered screen — going over vs. under depending on how the defender plays.

## Counters
- If 2 is denied off the staggered screen → 1 can look for 3 on the weak-side wing.
- If neither option exists → reset and run half-court offense.

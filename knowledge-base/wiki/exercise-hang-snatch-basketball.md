# Hang Snatch (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop total-body explosive power and overhead shoulder stability from an above-knee starting position, combining the accessibility of the hang position with the overhead demand of the snatch for basketball athletes.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Barbell with appropriate load (lighter than hang clean)
- No spotter required

## Primary Muscles Trained
Gluteus maximus, biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, soleus, gastrocnemius, deltoids, trapezius [S11, p.18]

## Execution
1. **Beginning position**: Stand with feet hip- or shoulder-width apart, toes turned out slightly. Grip bar with closed pronated grip, elbows fully extended. Hands should be 6–8 inches (15–20 cm) wider than power clean grip. Under control, lift the bar from the floor keeping back flat and bar close to body. From standing, **lower the bar along the thighs to mid-thigh, at the knees, or just above the knees** (hang position).
2. **Explosive pull**: Explosively extend hips, legs, and ankles while keeping elbows fully extended. Thrust shoulders up and slightly back; drive hips forward and up. Rise onto toes before arms begin pulling. Keep bar close to body.
3. **Overhead catch**: As the lower body becomes fully extended, rapidly shrug the shoulders and start the arm pull. Pull the body under the bar and rotate hands under the bar while dropping to a quarter squat. **Catch the bar overhead with elbows fully extended.** Stand up.
4. **Return**: Under control, lower bar to the thighs, then to the hang position for the next repetition. After the last rep, lower the bar to the floor.

## Breathing Guidelines
- Take a deep breath before initiating the pull from the hang position
- Exhale during the aggressive, explosive jumping action [S11, p.18]

## Coaching Points
- **"The exercise should be one fluid movement from mid-thigh, at the knees, or just above the knees to overhead"** [S11, p.19]
- **"Be explosive"** [S11, p.19]
- Three acceptable hang heights (mid-thigh, at knees, above knees) allow adjustment based on athlete flexibility and training goals
- Can catch in a deep squat position or taller position
- Wide grip requires shoulder flexibility — ensure adequate range of motion before loading

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hang pull — same pull pattern without overhead catch requirement
2. **Intermediate**: Hang snatch with tall/quarter-squat catch at light-to-moderate loads
3. **Advanced**: Hang snatch with heavier loads and deep-squat catch

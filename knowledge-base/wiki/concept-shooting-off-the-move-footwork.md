# Shooting Off the Move: Cutting or Dribbling

## Summary
Shooting off a dribble and shooting off a cut-then-catch are fundamentally the same action. The timing, positioning, and rhythm of the feet and hands should be nearly identical in both scenarios. The better a player unifies these two mechanics, the more effective and consistent a scorer he becomes. As Coach John Wooden always preaches: "Be quick, but not in a hurry." [S6, p.161]

The foundational rule: the shooter pivots off the **inside heel** of the inside foot — the foot closest to the basket when moving laterally. This immediately arrests lateral momentum so the shooter rises straight up and lands in the same spot. Players who float left or right during their jump greatly reduce accuracy. [S6, p.162]

## When to Use
- Any time a player is receiving a pass off a cut and shooting immediately
- Any time a player is pulling up off the dribble for a jump shot
- Any lateral approach — moving left or moving right — requires this footwork
- Both half-court offense (set plays, isolations) and transition situations

## Key Principles
1. **Inside heel stops lateral momentum** — driving the heel of the inside foot straight down into the floor is the key mechanical action that allows a straight-up jump. [S6, p.162]
2. **Free foot swings to square up** — after the inside heel plants, the free (outside) foot swings around to face the basket, squaring the shoulders before the jump. [S6, p.162]
3. **Last dribble = lowest and hardest** — on a pull-up, the final dribble before the shot should be the lowest and hardest dribble in the sequence. This generates upward momentum for the jump shot launch. [S6, p.162]
4. **Hands arrive as if catching a pass** — the shooting hand and guide hand should reach the shooting pocket in the same rhythm and position whether coming off a dribble or a cut. The two scenarios should be as similar as possible. [S6, p.162]
5. **Dribble with the outside hand** — when approaching the basket from the left side, the outside (left) hand is farthest from the defender and is the dribbling hand. When approaching from the right, the right hand is the outside dribble hand. [S6, p.162]
6. **Good shooters do not fall away or float** — the pivot mechanism (inside heel driving down) ensures the shooter rises and lands in the same spot. Drifting left or right is a fundamental error. [S6, p.162]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Master the inside-heel pivot from both sides. The inside foot and the pivot direction change depending on which side of the court the player is moving toward.

### Moving Left (Left Side of Court)
- Outside hand = left hand (dribbling hand)
- Inside foot = right foot
- Pivot off the right heel
- Free foot (left) swings around to square to the basket [S6, pp.162-163]

### Moving Right (Right Side of Court)
- Outside hand = right hand (dribbling hand)
- Inside foot = left foot
- Pivot off the left heel
- Free foot (right) swings around to square to the basket [S6, p.163]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Floating or drifting on the jump** → drive the inside heel down hard at the exact moment the last dribble hits the floor; this stops lateral momentum before the jump.
2. **Rushing the catch into the pocket** → the hands should arrive with the same rhythm as catching a pass — rushing creates a disrupted shooting motion.
3. **Outside foot planting first (wrong pivot foot)** → always establish the inside foot as the pivot foot; using the outside foot causes the body to continue drifting sideways.
4. **Soft final dribble** → the last dribble must be the lowest and hardest — a soft final dribble kills the upward momentum needed for a clean jump shot.
5. **Shoulders not squared** → if the free foot doesn't fully swing around, shoulders stay angled and the shot pulls offline.

## Variations
### Shooting Off a Cut (Pass Catch)
The cutter drives the inside heel down at the exact moment the ball contacts the shooting hand. The free foot swings around simultaneously as the ball is pulled into the shooting pocket. [S6, p.162]

### Shooting Off the Dribble (Pull-Up)
Identical footwork — the inside heel drives down at the same moment the last dribble hits the floor. The hands arrive at the pocket in the same rhythm as a catch. [S6, p.162]

### Outside Foot Pivot Exception
When a shooter has a strong preference, the outside foot can be used as the pivot foot in half-court offensive settings (zone or man) with stationary shooters. Coaches should allow this exception only rarely and only in appropriate contexts. [S6, p.161]

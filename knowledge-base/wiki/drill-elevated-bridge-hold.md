# Elevated Bridge Hold

## Objective
Build gluteal, erector spinae, and core stability strength through an isometric hip extension hold with feet elevated — developing the posterior chain strength needed for jumping, driving, and deceleration on the basketball court.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Floor space + a bench or plyometric box
- No equipment required beyond bench

## Execution
1. Lie face up on the floor with both feet elevated on a bench.
2. Flex the ankles so that toes point up toward the ceiling (dorsiflexed).
3. Flex knees and hips to approximately 90 degrees.
4. Fully extend the arms at the sides of the body with palms facing down.
5. Head contacts the floor with chin slightly tucked (beginning position).
6. Drive the heels into the bench surface to lift the hips off the floor into a neutral spine position. Upper body is supported only by the shoulders.
7. Actively drive through the heels to fully extend the hips — hold this isometric position for the prescribed time.
8. Lower with control and repeat for prescribed sets.

### Alternating Leg Lift Variation
- From the bridge hold position (both feet on bench, hips extended), take one foot off the bench and fully extend the knee.
- Hold the single-leg extended position for 1–2 seconds, then return to the bench.
- Alternate legs; continue until prescribed repetitions are complete.
- This variation builds single-leg hip stability critical for one-leg take-offs and landing mechanics.

## Coaching Points
- From a side view, the athlete's knees, hips, and shoulders should form a straight line at the top position.
- Cue: "Drive your heels into the bench and squeeze your glutes."
- Do not allow the hips to sag or rotate during the hold.
- Focus on controlled breathing — matched inhales and exhales throughout the hold.
- For the leg-lift variation, instruct the athlete to keep driving the planted heel to maintain hip height on the support side.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Floor bridge — both feet flat on floor directly below knees (easier lever arm).
2. **Intermediate**: Elevated bridge hold with both feet on a bench as described.
3. **Advanced**: Elevated bridge hold with alternating leg lift — single-leg stability demand.

# Bag Hops With 180-Degree Turn (Bag Drill)

## Objective
Improve quick foot movement and hip flexibility while providing plyometric training through bilateral lateral hops over bags with a 180-degree rotation on each hop. [S8, p.118]

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4-6 agility bags in a straight lateral line, spaced approximately 2-3 feet apart
- Open court or grass area

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Hop laterally over bag 1, rotating 180 degrees while in the air.
3. Land between bags 1 and 2 facing the opposite direction.
4. Immediately hop over bag 2, rotating 180 degrees in the opposite direction.
5. Continue hopping and rotating alternately through all four to six bags. [S8, p.118]

## Coaching Points
- The rotation happens **in the air** — do not land first then turn
- Land softly on the balls of both feet simultaneously to protect joints and maintain reactivity
- Minimize ground contact time — this is the plyometric component
- Keep the core tight through every rotation
- Arms assist the rotation — use them to initiate the turn in mid-air

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice 180° rotational jumps in place without bags before adding lateral movement
2. **Intermediate**: 4 bags at moderate speed
3. **Advanced**: 6 bags at maximum speed with minimum ground contact time

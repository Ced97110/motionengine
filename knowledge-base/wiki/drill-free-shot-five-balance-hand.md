# Five Free Shots, Five Balance-Hand Layups

## Objective
Efficiently develop three skills simultaneously: free-shot shooting under fatigue, weak-hand (balance-hand) layups, and weak-hand ball handling.

## Setup
- 1 player per basket
- Free-throw line and lane
- 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Player shoots a **free shot** from the free-throw line using correct technique.
2. Player **freezes the follow-through** and holds until the ball hits the floor.
3. Player then **attacks the rim** and finishes a **layup with the balance (non-dominant) hand**.
4. Player **dribbles back to the free-throw line hard with the balance hand**.
5. Repeat for **5 total free shots and 5 balance-hand layups**.

## Coaching Points
- Economizes practice time — three skills in one drill without players realizing the extra workload [S5, p.54]
- The layup and dribble-back create **fatigue** so the subsequent free shot is taken in a game-like winded state
- Insist on the **freeze-follow-through** on every free shot before moving; do not allow players to skip it
- The balance-hand layup forces players to use their weak hand — cue *"attack the rim, soft touch"*
- Dribbling back with the balance hand builds weak-hand ball control; cue *"low and hard dribbles, protect the ball"*

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk back to the free-throw line after the layup; no dribble requirement.
2. **Intermediate**: Jog/dribble back with balance hand as described.
3. **Advanced**: Increase to 10 reps; add a defensive close-out before each free shot to simulate fatigue at game intensity.

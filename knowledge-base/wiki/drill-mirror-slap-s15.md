# Mirror Slap

## Objective
Develop reactive quickness, defensive and offensive footwork, and the ability to read and mirror an opponent's movements in real time.

## Setup
- **Players**: All players in pairs distributed across the court
- **Court area**: Any open space — pairs can be anywhere
- **Equipment**: Optional — basketballs for advanced variation
- **Duration**: 2 minutes (30 seconds to 1 minute per leader, then switch)

## Execution
1. One player is Player A (leader), the other is Player B (mirror). Face each other.
2. Player A executes a movement; Player B tries to mirror it as quickly as possible.
3. Player A leads for 30 seconds to 1 minute; then roles switch.
4. Coach observes Player A for precision of technique and Player B for speed of reaction.

**Beginner Movements:**
- Touch head with right hand, left hand, both hands
- Touch knee with right hand, left hand
- Run in place
- Pat the floor rapidly with both feet

**Intermediate Movements (add to beginner):**
- Jab step, rocker step, in-and-out move, spin, half-spin, front pivot, reverse pivot
- Jab step and roll, jab step and go (after rebounding techniques are learned)
- Reverse pivot / front pivot — player B reacts with same movement

**Advanced Movements (add to all):**
- Full defensive footwork: interception stance, front-foot-to-pivot-foot stance, front-foot-to-free-foot stance, advance step, retreat step, swing step, slide step, fence slide
- Triple-threat position, V-cut
- In-and-out defensive fake: step in with front foot, strike front hand forward quickly ("like the strike of a snake"), step back quickly — attempting to make the attacker change direction or hesitate with the dribble
- Give both players a basketball

## Coaching Points
- Watch Player A for correct technique execution
- Watch Player B for reaction speed — the goal is to eliminate any delay
- Cue for in-and-out defensive fake: "strike like a snake, then step back quickly"
- The drill develops the ability to read movements — a skill as valuable as physical quickness
- Advanced players with basketballs must maintain ball control while executing all movements

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Simple touch-and-tap patterns to establish the mirror concept
2. **Intermediate**: Add offensive and defensive fakes and pivots
3. **Advanced**: Full defensive footwork vocabulary with optional balls in hand

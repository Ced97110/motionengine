# Michael Holton's Close-Out Drill

## Objective
Build correct closeout technique — sprinting to the ball handler, breaking down into a defensive stance, and tracing/pressuring the ball — with live ball movement and skip-pass reactions. [S1, pp.208-209]

## Setup
- 4–8 players split evenly into defenders (X's) and offensive players (O's)
- Half-court: two O's spaced on the wings or elbows, two X defenders positioned near the paint
- X defenders start with the ball
- Each defender closes out 3–4 times (~30 seconds) before rotating; all players rotate from defense to offense

## Execution
1. **First pass:** On the coach's whistle or command, X defenders pass the ball to the O offensive players and immediately sprint to close out on them (Figure 15.1).
2. **Trace the ball:** The X's close out under control, break into a low defensive stance, and trace (pressure) the basketball. The O's move the ball side to side, faking passes to stress the defender's footwork.
3. **Skip pass reaction:** On the second coach's whistle, the O in possession passes across to the O on the opposite side.
4. **Pivot and close:** The X defender pivots, opens up, sprints across the lane, and closes out on the new ball handler under control — stopping dribble penetration, not flying by (Figures 15.2–15.4).
5. Continue for the prescribed rotation period, then swap offense and defense.

## Coaching Points
- "Close out under control — chop your feet as you arrive, high hand on the shot, low hand on the drive."
- Defenders must **not** fly by the ball handler — close out to stop penetration first, contest shot second
- Stay in a stance while tracing; do not stand straight up after the closeout
- On the skip, pivot open (don't back-pedal), run on the pass — arrive before the catch if possible
- Watch for lazy footwork on the pivot: "Open and run, don't drift!"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** No live dribble by the offense; O's only fake passes. Focus solely on closeout footwork and stance.
2. **Intermediate:** O's are allowed one live dribble after the closeout — defender must stay in front.
3. **Advanced:** O's can shot-fake and drive; defender must closeout, contain, and recover — full competitive 1-on-1 allowed.

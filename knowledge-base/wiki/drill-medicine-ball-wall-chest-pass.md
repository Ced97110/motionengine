# Medicine-Ball Wall Chest Passes

## Objective
Improve total-body transmission of power through rapid, explosive chest passes against a wall. [S8, p.157]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Solid wall
- Equipment: 1 medicine ball (appropriate weight for athlete)

## Execution
1. Stand facing the wall in an athletic stance, holding the medicine ball at chest height.
2. Perform a chest pass to the wall, catching the return with arms extended.
3. Immediately perform the next pass without pausing.
4. Continue for a predetermined number of reps, for time, or for distance (moving laterally down the wall).

## Coaching Points
- Receive the ball with arms extended — this pre-loads the elastic energy for the next pass.
- Full extension at the end of each pass (arms fully stretched) is required to capture the plyometric demand.
- Consistent rhythm beats random bursts — build a steady cadence then gradually increase rate.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two-arm passes from athletic stance for prescribed reps.
2. **Intermediate**: Perform with one arm only (isolates dominant and non-dominant sides).
3. **Advanced**: Perform while moving laterally up and down the wall; increase ball weight.

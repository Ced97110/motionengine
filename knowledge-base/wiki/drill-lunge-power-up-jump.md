# Lunge With Power-Up Jump

## Objective
Improve lower-body power and quickness by combining a forward lunge with an explosive two-foot jump landing. [S8, p.175]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open court or field space
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Step into a lunge position and shift your weight toward your front leg.
2. Drive off the front leg into a forward motion and then land on two feet.
3. Step out with the other leg and repeat.
4. Continue alternating legs for the designated distance or repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- Cue: "Drive through the front leg — land soft on two feet."
- Emphasize pushing through the hip of the front leg, not just lifting off the back leg.
- Land with both feet simultaneously in a stable, slightly bent-knee athletic position.
- Avoid collapsing the knee inward on landing (valgus) — watch for dynamic valgus.
- Arms should drive upward during the jump phase to maximize height.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walking lunges without a jump — focus on balance and depth.
2. **Intermediate**: Lunge with power-up jump as described, moderate pace.
3. **Advanced**: Continuous lunge jumps at maximum speed with immediate reaction drill (sprint to cone on landing).

- [[concept-lower-body-plyometrics]] — unilateral power development

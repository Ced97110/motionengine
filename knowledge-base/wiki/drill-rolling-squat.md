# Rolling Squat (Kip-Up Step 2)

## Objective
Develop the forward momentum generation needed for a full kip-up by following a rolling sit-up all the way through to a standing position.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat surface (mat recommended for beginners)
- No equipment

## Execution
1. Sit on floor with knees bent, heels on the ground.
2. Roll onto back, bringing legs above body (keep knees bent).
3. Place hands palms-down alongside ears, fingers pointing toward feet.
4. Roll onto upper-back and shoulders; bring knees close to head.
5. Roll back into starting position, but allow momentum to push hips off the ground.
6. Arms reach forward; finish in a low squat position.
7. Extend into full standing position.
8. Squat back down, roll back, and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- "Use the backward swing to generate the forward momentum — let the roll do the work"
- The ability to generate forward momentum from the roll is the key determinant of whether a full kip-up succeeds
- Arms reaching forward help maintain balance in the squat landing
- Do not stop at the squat — extend all the way to standing each rep

## Progressions
1. **Regression**: When glutes touch the ground, place hands on floor and push up with fingers
2. **Standard**: Arms out front, stand fully from the rolling momentum
3. **Progression**: Fold arms across chest — removes arm assistance, forces legs and waist to generate momentum entirely

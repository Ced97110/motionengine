# Pride Drill (Zigzag Full-Court Defensive)

## Objective
Develop on-ball defensive footwork, competitive pride, and the ability to contain a live dribbler across the full court by applying the drop-and-swing / push-push technique in a game-realistic 1-on-1 setting. [S6, pp.277-280]

## Setup
- **Players**: 3 pairs (6 players) simultaneously; additional pairs line up and rotate
- **Court**: Full court divided into 3 vertical dribbling alleys
- **Equipment**: 3 basketballs (one per pair)
- **Starting positions**: Offensive players at one baseline in their respective dribbling alleys; defenders matched up

## Execution
1. Three offensive-defensive pairs begin simultaneously, one in each dribbling alley
2. **Offensive player** attempts to dribble to the far baseline; tries to beat the defender and turn in one direction without leaving the alley
3. **Defender** must turn the dribbler at least **3 times** on the way up the full court using proper drop-and-swing → push-off → slide-step footwork
4. Defender gets **in front** of the dribbler — draws the charge if the ball handler does not change direction [S6, p.277]
5. Defender **never reaches** for the ball — movement and footwork only
6. All three pairs run simultaneously to simulate game density and communication demands
7. At the far baseline (STOP), players rotate (offense becomes defense, defense goes to end of line)

## Coaching Points
- "Never allow the offensive player to beat you on the dribble and squeeze by on the edge of your alley" [S6, p.278]
- "Move your feet — do not reach" — constant coaching cue throughout
- Defender must draw the charge, not just redirect: establish legal guarding position in the dribbler's path
- Hands-behind-back variation: lock hands behind the back to eliminate reaching and force footwork — "requires X1 to use his footwork to become quicker and more of a defensive hustler" [S6, p.278]
- Change start and finish points regularly to prevent boredom and increase game realism [S6, p.278]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Defender locks hands behind back — offense at 50% speed; focus on foot pattern only
2. **Intermediate**: Normal hands; offense at 75%; must turn dribbler 3× minimum
3. **Advanced**: Full speed; vary the offensive scenario at the start (skip pass + close out, ball screen initiation, off-the-ball screen → ball) and at the end (killed dribble, charge opportunity, boxing out the shooter) [S6, pp.278-280]

### Advanced Starting Scenarios (vary regularly)
- Defender closes out on a skip pass receiver before the dribble begins
- Offense uses a hypothetical ball screen to initiate the drive
- Defender starts as a help defender and must recover to ball [S6, p.279]

### Advanced Finishing Scenarios (vary regularly)
- Defend a killed dribbler at the far end
- Take a defensive charge
- Dive for a loose ball
- Box out and rebound a jump shot off the dribble
- Deny a give-and-go cut after the dribble is killed [S6, pp.279-280]

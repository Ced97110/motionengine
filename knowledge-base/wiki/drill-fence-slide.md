# Fence Slide

## Objective
Build the fence-slide denial stance and lateral movement skill that allows defenders to deny passes and stay connected to cutters along the perimeter.

## Setup
- **Beginner**: Full court, 5 lanes, one player per lane
- **Intermediate/Advanced**: Half court, coach with ball at the top, offensive player (1) at wing, defender (X1) in denial, player line at side
- Rotation: end of line → offense → defense
- Duration: 1 minute

## Execution
**Beginner:**
1. Players line up five per row across the court baseline.
2. Players go the **full length of the court and back** in fence-slide position.
3. One repetition with left side up, next repetition with right side up.

**Intermediate:**
1. X1 is in denial fence-slide position on the wing between the coach and player 1.
2. Player 1 uses a V-cut to get open.
3. Once 1 receives the pass from the coach, 1 front-pivots or reverse-pivots into triple-threat position, then passes back to the coach.
4. Coach can allow rocker-step movement once 1 receives the pass, compelling X1 to use retreat step, advance step, and swing step.

**Advanced:**
From triple-threat position, 1 and X1 play 1-on-1 live.

## Coaching Points
**Fence-slide foot position (right side up):**
1. Right foot points straight ahead, directly under the right shoulder
2. Left foot is **perpendicular** to the right foot with the heels of both feet in the same straight line
3. To get there: put heels together at 90° to each other, then step one full step forward with the right foot
4. First forward slide step is with the right foot (~2 feet); then bring the left heel up near the right heel — use slide steps only
5. Defender's **ear should be on the imaginary attacker's chest** — not on the ball or the feet
6. Right arm extends out at shoulder height or slightly lower; palm pointing outward with thumb toward the floor
7. "Imagine a taut string between 1 and X1. When 1 steps toward X1, the string becomes loose. X1 should immediately step back to keep the string tight" [S15, p.265]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Full-court fence-slide runs both directions, alternating lead foot
2. **Intermediate**: Denial on a live V-cut receiver with coach passing
3. **Advanced**: Live 1-on-1 after the catch from triple-threat position

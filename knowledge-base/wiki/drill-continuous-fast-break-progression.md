# Continuous Full-Court Fast-Break Progression Drill (2v1 → 5v5)

## Objective
Simulate every numerical fast-break transition scenario in one continuous drill — 2-on-1 through 5-on-5 — developing transition offense and defense at game speed with proper rotations. [S1, pp.209-211]

## Setup
- Divide squad into four teams; one team at each hash mark on the sides of the court
- Two offensive players with a ball at one free-throw line; one defender at mid-court
- Additional groups ready to step in from each hash mark as the drill progresses

## Execution
### Phase 1: 2-on-1
1. Two O players attack the lone mid-court defender toward one basket (2-on-1 fast break).
2. Defender attempts to stop the break or force a bad shot.
3. Once the break is scored or stopped, the lone defender plus a **wing player from the corner** become a 2-man offensive unit.

### Phase 2: 3-on-2
4. The new 2-man group attacks the far basket against **two defenders who have run up from opposite corners** to defend.
5. Once completed, the two defenders plus a wing player from the nearest hash mark become a 3-man offensive group.

### Phase 3: 4-on-3
6. The 3-man group attacks the basket against **three new defenders** stepping in from the far end. A 4th player steps in from the hash mark to make it 4-on-3.

### Phase 4: 5-on-4
7. The three defenders plus two players from the hash mark run a 5-on-4 fast break at the opposite end against four defenders.

### Phase 5: 5-on-5
8. Whether the 5-on-4 scores or not, the four defenders plus one more player from the hash mark get the ball (as in a real game) and go **5-on-5** against the five offensive players who just played.
9. Players continually rotate through all positions. Drill continues until the coach ends it.

## Coaching Points
- Defensive objective in every outnumbered situation: "**Guard the goal. Stop the ball. Make them pick it up.**"
- Force the ball handler to pick up (by faking/feinting), cause a turnover, or slow the ball until teammates recover
- Do not foul — make them earn difficult shots
- Offense: attack in proper lanes, push pace, fill the floor
- Transition emphasis: **sprint every time the ball changes hands** — no jogging allowed

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate:** Run only 2v1 → 3v2 sections for younger teams before adding 4v3 and 5v4.
2. **Advanced:** Add live referee who calls fouls; penalize lazy transition defense with a sprint.
3. **Competitive:** Track stops per team; defensive team that earns the most stops over a set period wins.

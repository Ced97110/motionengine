# Drop-Step Baseline/Middle Drill (#114)

## Objective
Teach the two foundational low-post drop steps — drop-step baseline (power layup) and drop-step middle (baby hook) — including jump-stop mechanics, check-baseline read, and ball/head/shoulder fakes.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2-5 per group (1 active, rest in line; coach as passer)
- **Court area**: Low-post area, big block; change the cutting angle daily
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball per group

## Execution

### Phase 1 (Beginner — Teach in Two Parts)
**Part 1: The Cut and Jump Stop**
1. Player 1 cuts toward the coach (from the right side of the court to the left-side low block in the diagram — change cutting angle daily).
2. Player 1 cuts and lands a step or so **above the big block** and executes a **jump stop**.
3. Player goes to end of the line; repeat with all players learning the cut-and-jump-stop.

**Part 2: Add the Drop-Step**
4. After all players have mastered the cut and jump stop, add the drop-step moves.
5. Player 1 cuts to the block, jump-stops, **turns head slightly to check baseline first** (checking the defender's shoulder position and peripherally their feet).
6. **If baseline is open** (cannot clearly see defender's shoulder): Drop-step baseline — plant drop-step foot as far as possible, at least equal to the defender's foot, square up parallel to baseline, power layup.
7. **If baseline is blocked** (can clearly see defender's shoulder): Drop-step middle — drop-step with the opposite foot, slide-step dribble if needed, baby hook.
8. After successful completion, player goes to end of line; player 2 cuts. Drill continues.
9. **Change sides of the court the next day** — the opposite foot becomes the drop-step foot.

### Drop-Step Baseline Full Mechanics
1. Jump-stop; choose pivot foot
2. Drop-step as far as possible; place foot beyond the defender's foot if possible; square parallel to baseline
3. Dive toward basket with power layup; both hands on ball until released on backboard
4. Option: throw buttocks toward middle to create slight contact with defender (prevents shot block)
5. If too far from basket: slide-step dribble closer
6. If defender is taller and on your back: pump fake series

### Drop-Step Middle Full Mechanics
1. Check baseline → see defender's shoulder → choose drop-step middle
2. Drop-step with middle-side foot as far as possible
3. Slide-step dribble if needed; spring into air, baby hook over front of rim
4. If defender not off-balance: pump fake instead; up-and-under if they bite; second pump fake then power layup if they don't

## Coaching Points
- "Always check baseline first" — this is the read that simplifies everything [S15, p.232]
- Jump stop is non-negotiable — it preserves pivot foot choice
- The drop step must be a **direct drive**, not a curved arc — circuitous drop steps fail
- "Don't hook the non-dribbling arm — keep it straight and strong"
- Teach all players (not just post players) — all motion offense players cycle through the post
- Coach all six teaching points after every 2-3 reps, not just after mistakes

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through cut → jump stop; then add drop-step baseline only; then add drop-step middle; change sides next day
2. **Intermediate**: Add ball fakes and head-and-shoulder fakes: throw head slightly backward and to the drop-step-away side, raise ball over opposite shoulder, turn torso toward middle — then execute the opposite drop step. Teach in two parts: (a) the fake, (b) picking up the foot
3. **Advanced**: Full sequence — drop-step middle → slide-step dribble → pump fake → up-and-under; or drop-step baseline → slide-step dribble → pump fake → power layup

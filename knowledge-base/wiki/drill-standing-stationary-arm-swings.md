# Standing Stationary Arm Swings

## Objective
Improve running mechanics and speed by teaching correct upper-body arm action while in a stationary position — the foundation of proper sprint technique.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Any flat surface — court, gym floor, field
- Can be performed in a line or spread out

## Execution
1. Stand with feet together
2. Swing the arms in a sprinting motion — each arm moves as one piece
3. Elbow bent at approximately 90 degrees at all times
4. Keep the hands relaxed (open or lightly closed)
5. Hands come up to about **shoulder level** in front of the body
6. Hands pass the **gluteus** in the back swing
7. Arm action is strictly forward and backward — **never cross the midline of the body**
8. Thumb side of the hand points forward and up; do not let the wrist move
9. Continue for the prescribed duration or repetition count [S8, p.23]

## Coaching Points
- "Aggressive arm action is a must" — vigorous arm swing helps counteract the rotational forces generated by the legs
- Watch for arms crossing the midline — cue: "cheekbone to back pocket"
- Ensure the swing originates from the **shoulder**, not the elbow
- Hands should stay relaxed; tension in the hands spreads to the shoulders and wastes energy
- This drill is most effective when performed before any running drills so the feel is fresh

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standing stationary arm swings — feet together, focus on range of motion
2. **Intermediate**: Seated Arm Swings — sit on the floor with legs straight out in front; swing arms as described. Helps train the correct position of the hands at the lowest point of the swing by avoiding contact with the ground
3. **Advanced**: Weighted Arm Swings — use light dumbbells (enough resistance to provide training stimulus without altering mechanics); Contrast-Resisted Arm Swings — swing with 1-2 lb weights for 10-20 reps, then immediately drop weights and perform 10-20 swings without resistance [S8, p.23]

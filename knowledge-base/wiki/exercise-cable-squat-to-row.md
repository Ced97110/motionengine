# Cable Squat to Row

## Objective
Develop simultaneous lower-body (squat) and upper-body pulling (row) strength in a single compound movement, building the kinetic chain integration required for basketball's full-body physical demands.

## Setup
- Dual cable unit with handles at approximately chest height
- 1 athlete
- Weight room / strength training area

## Execution
1. Stand facing the dual cable unit, feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointed slightly out
2. Hold cable handles with arms extended straight in front at chest height; palms face each other (neutral grip)
3. With arms extended, sit back into a deep squat position — keep heels flat on the floor, chest and head up
4. Pause briefly at the bottom of the squat
5. Stand up, and simultaneously pull both arms in to the sides of the chest (row)
6. Return to the starting position with arms extended and repeat [S11, Ch.6]

**Breathing:** Inhale (deep breath) before the squat; exhale during the row (upward/concentric phase)

## Coaching Points
- "Squeeze the shoulder blades **down and back** while pulling"
- "Sit deep" — do not cut the squat short
- Keep the chest up throughout; do not allow forward lean to collapse
- Synchronize the stand and row — they should finish at the same time
- Heels stay flat through the entire squat descent

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reduce cable weight; focus on squat depth and neutral spine before adding row power
2. **Intermediate**: Standard execution — full ROM squat + full row simultaneously; adjust cable height to change row angle
3. **Advanced**: Perform single-arm rows; increase load; add a pause at the bottom of the squat

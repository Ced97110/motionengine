# Spinouts To and From the Baseline

## Objective
Develop proper catch-and-shoot footwork from multiple passing angles, specifically simulating down-screen and inbounds-screen situations where the ball arrives from the top or from the baseline.

## Setup
- 1 player (solo drill)
- Wing or elbow area; both sides of the basket
- 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Spin the ball in the direction that simulates the desired screen scenario:
   - **Ball arriving from the top** (simulating a down screen): shooter cuts toward the ball from the baseline, plants the inside foot, and rises into the shot.
   - **Ball arriving from the baseline** (simulating an inbounds screen): shooter cuts from above, plants the inside foot, and rises into the shot.
2. On every catch, plant the inside foot first, then bring the second foot in:
   - **Right-handed shooter, left side of basket**: plant left (inside) foot → bring right foot all the way into the shot line.
   - **Left-handed shooter, left side of basket**: plant left (inside) foot → stub the right foot to maintain proper shot alignment.
3. Catch in rhythm, square up, and shoot with full follow-through. Freeze the follow-through.
4. Repeat from both sides of the basket.

**Key teaching point:** "Stub your foot when appropriate to maintain a proper shot line when squaring to the basket." [S5, p.2]

## Coaching Points
- The inside pivot foot rule applies regardless of the angle of the screen — always plant inside foot to the target.
- Watch for players who over-stride past the shot pocket; insist on a compact, balanced landing.
- Emphasize catching **all** angles — down screens are the most common at every level, but inbounds screens are equally important game situations.
- Drill must be performed from **both** sides of the basket.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow spinouts from one direction only (top or baseline); focus on footwork without worrying about making the shot.
2. **Intermediate**: Add the shot; alternate direction (top one rep, baseline the next) to simulate reading the screen.
3. **Advanced**: Mix sides, increase spin velocity for game-speed passes; combine with a defensive closeout to develop shot-fake options.

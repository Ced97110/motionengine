# Power Snatch From Floor

## Objective
Develop the highest level of explosive power and linear acceleration ability by training the full range of the snatch from a dead-stop floor position.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Barbell (lighter than hang position — increased range of motion and technical demand)
- Must have mastered the power snatch from hang before attempting
- Taller athletes may struggle with this position due to joint mobility requirements [S2, p.101]

## Execution
1. Approach the barbell and grasp it with hands **significantly wider than shoulders** (snatch grip).
2. Lift the bar off the floor by **raising hips and shoulders simultaneously**. The barbell stays close to the body until it is just above the knees.
3. The chest is over and in front of the barbell. Upper back is straight, lower back neutral. Arms are relaxed and completely straight.
4. Quickly extend the lower limbs as the hips move forward. **Maintain the knee bend** as the barbell slides up the front of the legs.
5. Fully extend the knees and ankles as you quickly **shrug the shoulders**, causing the barbell to rise vertically.
6. Once the barbell has reached its highest point, **slightly bend the knees** to drop under. Conclude by extending the arms to receive the barbell overhead and slightly behind the head.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.100–101]

## Coaching Points
- **First pull (floor to knee) is SLOW and controlled** — this is the preparatory phase; explosion comes at and above the knee
- "Hips and shoulders rise together" — if the hips shoot up first, you lose the bar close to the body and the leverage advantage
- The floor start requires greater hip flexibility — if an athlete cannot maintain a flat back in starting position, work mobility before adding this variation
- Analogy: like a NASCAR driver — slow around the track, then explosive to pass. The slow first pull is the setup; the explosive pull above the knee is the acceleration moment [S2, p.101]

## Progressions
1. **Prerequisite**: Power snatch from hang position must be mastered first
2. **Box variation bridge**: Power Snatch From Box with bar set below the knee — trains the dead-stop quality without full floor mobility requirements
3. **Full floor**: Power snatch from floor with full range and increasing load as technique stabilizes

## Muscles Involved
**Primary**: Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, semitendinosus, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius, trapezius, lateral deltoid, anterior deltoid

**Secondary**: Gastrocnemius, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, rectus femoris [S2, p.101]

## Basketball Focus
The floor start directly trains the **ability to accelerate** — the same slow-to-explosive pattern used when a player must go from a stationary position to a full sprint. It is the most transferable variation for first-step quickness and top-end acceleration. [S2, p.101]

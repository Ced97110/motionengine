# Cheerleader (Resistance Band Shoulder Strengthening)

## Objective
Strengthen the scapular stabilizers and rotator cuff through a multi-directional resistance band pattern, facilitating proper muscle firing for shooting, defending, and blocking.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Standing position
- Equipment: resistance band (one band, hold each end)

## Execution
1. Stand and hold one end of a resistance band in each hand, hands together in front of the body.
2. Pinch the shoulder blades together.
3. Keeping the arms straight, **stretch arms out to the sides** (T position). Return to start.
4. **Stretch arms up and toward the right shoulder** (diagonal up-right). Return to start.
5. **Stretch arms back out to the sides** again. Return to start.
6. **Stretch arms up and toward the left shoulder** (diagonal up-left). Return to start.
7. This four-movement sequence = 1 repetition. Perform **3 sets of 5 repetitions**.
8. **Advancement**: Use a stronger resistance band.

## Coaching Points
- "Pinch the shoulder blades before you start pulling" — set the scapula every time
- Arms must stay straight — bending the elbows reduces the scapular stabilizer demand
- The diagonal arm movements (toward right and left shoulders) mimic overhead shooting and passing positions
- If the band snaps back uncontrolled, the resistance is too high; use a lighter band [S2, p.159]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Lighter resistance band; perform just the T-position stretch to master the scapular pinch
2. **Intermediate**: Full four-movement cheerleader pattern, 3 × 5
3. **Advanced**: Stronger resistance band; increase to 3 × 8 repetitions

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis)
- **Secondary**: Biceps brachii, anterior deltoid, posterior deltoid, lateral deltoid

# Ladder Stride Run

## Objective
Enhance stride timing and stride frequency while teaching quick turnover at near-sprint pace with proper upright sprint posture.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder (sticks 18 inches / 46 cm apart)
- Open court or field

## Execution
1. Set up agility ladder flat on the ground with rungs 18 inches (46 cm) apart
2. Run through the ladder **as fast as possible**, touching **one foot down between every other rung** (every second box)
3. Emphasize **upright posture** throughout
4. Maintain sound arm mechanics and proper leg action [S8, p.33]

## Coaching Points
- One foot per every other rung forces a longer stride pattern — more closely simulating actual sprint mechanics than the speed run
- "Upright posture with sound arm and leg mechanics" — this drill should feel like sprinting, not shuffling
- The stride pattern teaches athletes to cover more ground per foot contact — training stride length and frequency simultaneously
- Progress from [[drill-ladder-speed-run]] (two feet per rung) to this drill as a natural advancement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: [[drill-ladder-speed-run]] with two feet per rung
2. **Intermediate**: Ladder Stride Run as described — one foot per every other rung
3. **Advanced**: Full-speed sprint immediately following the ladder exit — transfers the stride pattern to open sprinting [S8, p.33]

# Push Get-Up

## Objective
Integrate upper and lower body explosive power by transitioning from a push-up into a standing position in one fluid movement, training the ability to rapidly change body levels — a critical basketball skill.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface
- No equipment required
- Can be used as warm-up, finisher, or ancillary drill in any explosives session

## Execution
1. Start in the push-up position — palms below shoulders, body aligned and horizontal.
2. Dip down (lower toward the floor as in a push-up).
3. Explode up from the floor, simultaneously bringing legs in beneath the body.
4. Finish in a standing position.
5. Can be performed rhythmically for higher reps or used as a standalone explosive movement.

## Coaching Points
- "One explosive movement — push and bring the legs in at the same time"
- This is the best way to get up off the floor after a set of push-ups
- Works as a preliminary drill for the Aztec push-up — teaches the body to compress and extend from a low position to standing
- Can be used as a warm-up or finishing exercise in an explosives session
- Performs well rhythmically for higher repetitions

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow controlled transition from push-up to squat to standing
2. **Intermediate**: Single explosive movement — push and stand in one motion
3. **Advanced**: Use as a rebound exercise — land and immediately descend back into push-up, repeat

# Dodge Ball (Whole-Body Reaction)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed and total-body reaction time to visual stimuli by evading thrown balls. [S8, p.214]

## Setup
- 2–4 players: use a wall to rebound thrown balls
- 5+ players: form a circle with throwers on the outside and evaders in the middle
- Equipment: soft foam balls (multiple)

## Execution
1. Two to four players can play against a wall; five or more play in a circle formation.
2. Multiple players throw soft balls simultaneously toward evading players.
3. Evading players react to the trajectory of each thrown ball and use whole-body movement (dodge, duck, lateral shuffle, jump) to avoid contact.
4. Rotate throwers and evaders on a set time interval (e.g., every 30–60 seconds).

## Coaching Points
- Stress **split-second decision-making** — the visual stimulus (incoming ball) must trigger an immediate movement response
- Keep the athletic stance (slight knee bend, weight on balls of feet) between evasions
- Do not telegraph movements — react, don't anticipate
- "Stay light on your feet — react to what you see, not what you expect"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: One ball at a time, slow throws, against a wall
2. **Intermediate**: Two simultaneous balls, circle formation
3. **Advanced**: Multiple balls, faster throws, smaller evader circle

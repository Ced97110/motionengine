# Rip Step Stride Stop / Step-Through

## Objective
Develop the rip step into a stride stop with a step-through counter finish — teaching players to read the defender and step through for a layup or floater instead of shooting the jump shot off the stride stop.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court; elbow or wing area
- Ball only
- Choose volume level (80, 120, or 240 total shots)

## Execution
1. Begin in triple threat on the wing or elbow.
2. Execute the **rip step**: rip the ball low across the body to protect it and initiate the drive.
3. Take **1 dribble** (or **2 dribbles**) to attack the lane.
4. Execute a **stride stop** — lead foot first, shooting foot squares — but instead of shooting the pull-up, **step through** with the non-pivot foot underneath or around the defender.
5. Finish with a layup, floater, or runner depending on defender position.
6. Alternate **left (L)** and **right (R)** with free throws between each set.

**Set Order (all volume levels):**
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Stride Stop/Step-Through (L)
- Free Throws
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Stride Stop/Step-Through (R)
- Free Throws
- [Repeat 1-dribble cycle once more]
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Stride Stop/Step-Through (L)
- Free Throws
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Stride Stop/Step-Through (R)
- Free Throws
- [Repeat 2-dribble cycle once more]

## Coaching Points
- The stride stop and step-through must feel like a single fluid motion — not two separate actions
- "Read the defender at the stride stop — if they're upright and in your way, step through; if they're on their heels, shoot"
- The step-through foot should clear the defender's near foot — step to the side, not into contact
- Keep the ball protected (low and tight to the body) throughout the step-through
- Track FGA, FGM, FTA, FTM in a shot chart journal [S3]
- "Stay low through the finish — don't rise up before you step through"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #2-7, 80 shots)**: 5 reps per set, slower pace — focus on the stride stop → step-through transition
2. **Intermediate (Workout #2-8, 120 shots)**: 10 reps per set, game speed — simulate live defender reads
3. **Advanced (Workout #2-9, 240 shots)**: 20 reps per set, full conditioning volume, both dribble counts

# Six-Basket Free Throws

## Objective
Build free-throw consistency and pressure tolerance by requiring players to make a designated number of consecutive free throws before rotating to the next basket, with reward/penalty stakes to simulate game-like pressure [S13, p.121].

## Setup
- **Players**: Full squad, split into groups of 2-3 per basket
- **Court area**: Full court with 6 baskets (standard multi-basket practice facility)
- **Equipment**: One ball per group
- **Assistants**: Coaches assigned to each basket to rebound and monitor

## Execution
1. Split the squad into groups of 2-3 players and assign each group to one of the six baskets
2. Each player shoots free throws consecutively — they shoot until they miss
3. If the player makes the required number (5 or 10) without missing, they rotate immediately to the next basket
4. A miss sends the player to the back of the line at that basket
5. Each player must complete the designated consecutive-make goal at all six baskets to finish the cycle
6. **Reward**: Players who complete the cycle early may leave practice early
7. **Penalty**: Players who have not completed the cycle must stay late until finished

## Coaching Points
- Set the consecutive-make number carefully — poor FT shooters may struggle to make even 5 in a row; adjust the goal by ability level
- Assign assistant coaches to each basket for accountability and immediate technique feedback
- Emphasize the three requirements: proper mechanics (balance, grip, aim, release, follow-through), concentration, and pressure tolerance [S13, p.118]
- Use this drill regularly — free-throw improvement requires daily repetition, not occasional practice
- Call out shooters who rush their routine under peer pressure; the routine must be the same whether shooting alone or with an audience

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Make 3 consecutive before rotating
2. **Intermediate**: Make 5 consecutive before rotating
3. **Advanced**: Make 10 consecutive before rotating; time limit added for urgency

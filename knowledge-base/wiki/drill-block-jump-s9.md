# Block Jump (Power Jump Chain Step 4)

## Objective
Master the skill of blocking — converting horizontal running momentum into vertical jump momentum — an essential athletic pattern for basketball fast-break finishes, layups off a sprint, and advanced acrobatic movements.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any space with a short run-up (5-10 feet minimum)
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Take a short run-up — a handful of steps (3-5); more steps is not better at this stage
2. Swing arms up and take one big final step, then plant BOTH feet together simultaneously
3. As you plant, knees can bend slightly to dip down
4. Immediately use your total body to "punch" down through the floor with both feet; swing arms DOWN simultaneously
5. This downward punch transfers running momentum into the floor, bouncing you upward
6. Take off vertically, extending the body fully as you jump as high as possible
7. Land softly on the balls of both feet; arms fall naturally to sides
8. Reposition and repeat

## Coaching Points
- **"Punch the floor" is the key cue** — The downward arm/foot punch is what converts horizontal into vertical momentum [S9, p.18]
- **Both feet together on plant** — A one-foot takeoff is NOT a block jump; both feet must land simultaneously
- **Short run-up** — Too many steps generates too much horizontal speed to control; start with 2-3 steps
- **Think of the final big step as a skip** — This mental cue helps athletes find the rhythm of the transition [S9, p.18]
- **Go vertical, not forward** — The goal is to redirect momentum upward; athletes who drift forward have not blocked effectively
- Watch for: one-foot takeoff — cue "plant both feet at the same time like you're stopping on a dime"
- Watch for: landing forward (not vertically) — cue "punch straight down, not backward"

## Basketball Application
The block jump is identical to the footwork used when a basketball player drives at full speed and converts into a vertical jump stop or a gather-step layup finish. Players who can block effectively generate more height at the rim from a running start than players who rely on static vertical jumping alone.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 2-3 steps only; focus on feet-together plant at slow speed before adding momentum
2. **Intermediate**: 4-5 steps at moderate run speed; full punch and vertical extension
3. **Advanced**: Combine blocking with a tuck position in the air (Step 7) or catch tuck (Step 8) to prepare for front flip chain

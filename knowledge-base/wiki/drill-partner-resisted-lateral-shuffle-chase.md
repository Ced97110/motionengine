# Partner-Resisted Lateral Shuffle and Chase

## Objective
Improve lower-body quickness, lateral reaction ability, and change-of-direction speed through partner resistance and a reactive chase component. [S8, p.187]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Open space
- No equipment required (optional: resistance band around waist)

## Execution
1. Athlete starts in an **athletic stance** facing a sideline, ready to side-shuffle.
2. The partner places a hand on the **hip of the side the athlete will shuffle toward** (the lead hip).
3. On the coach's command, the athlete begins to side-shuffle; the partner **resists the shuffle** by pressing against the lead hip.
4. After a brief resistance phase, the partner **releases the hand** and **sprints in the same direction**.
5. The athlete must immediately **chase the partner** and try to **tag** them as quickly as possible. [S8, p.187]

## Coaching Points
- During the resistance phase: stay in a low athletic stance — don't straighten up to fight the resistance
- "Explode the moment you feel the release" — the release signal triggers the reactive sprint
- The chase sprint uses the momentum built against the resistance — teach athletes to feel and exploit this energy
- The partner should vary the duration of the resistance phase (1-3 seconds) to prevent anticipation
- This drill directly simulates the explosive lateral first step needed in basketball defense

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Side-shuffle without resistance — establish proper athletic position
2. **Intermediate**: Add partner resistance for 2–3 seconds before release
3. **Advanced**: Partner varies resistance duration and direction (can release to either side) — athlete must react to visual cue of release

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — the resistance-release mechanism mimics the reactive first step needed to contain a dribbler
- [[defensive-transition-principles]] — lateral quickness is foundational for all defensive positioning
- [[rate-of-force-development-basketball]] — the instant of release requires maximum lateral force output

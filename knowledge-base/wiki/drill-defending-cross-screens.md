# Defending Cross Screens Drill

## Objective
Teach defenders proper execution, communication, and technique when the offense runs a cross screen (small guard screening for a big center or power forward low on opposite blocks).

## Setup
- 10 players, 5 vs 5 on the half court
- O5 on the right-side low block; O1 at the top of the key or perimeter
- O3 at the free-throw line extended (ball receiver)
- Coach or manager provides initial pass cue

## Execution
1. O1 initiates by passing to O3 at the free-throw line extended.
2. O1 runs a hard cut to the same-side low block, working into position to set a cross screen for O5.
3. X1 must be alert and **bump O1** as he moves across the free-throw lane before he can get a good angle to screen X5.
4. X1 forces O1 **down and low**, preventing O1 from getting an elevated screening angle on X5.
5. X5 steps **toward O1's screen** while maintaining:
   - Vision on the ball
   - Position relative to O5
   - **Hand contact** with O5 at all times
6. X5 **pushes O5 down toward the end line** (under the block), and X1 steps directly into the passing lane in front of O5, preventing O3's pass.
7. X5 maintains contact with O5 and works to maneuver O5 off the low block.
8. **Switch scenario**: If the cross screen is designed to produce a switch — X5 steps back, ensures O5 does not cross the lane on the inside. X5 pushes with arms and forces O5 toward the baseline. X1 then **bumps O5**, slowing him down to give X5 time to regain position.

## Coaching Points
- **"Bump the screener early"** — X1 must make physical contact with O1 before the screen is fully set; this disrupts angle and timing.
- **"Stay connected"** — X5 must never lose hand contact with O5 throughout the entire cross-lane action.
- **"Force baseline, not middle"** — pushing O5 toward the end line takes him away from the high-percentage post area.
- The reason O1 screens for O5 is to produce a **switch** — if X5 and X1 don't communicate, the mismatch is automatic.
- **Talk constantly**: "Screen right!" "I've got ball!" "Push him!" [S13, pp.207-208]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through each step slowly — show screener path, defender bump technique, then X5's push before any live action.
2. **Intermediate**: 5v5 half speed with O3 able to pass to O5 or reverse — defense must complete communication and deny before the live pass.
3. **Advanced**: O3 can pass to O5 in the post (if defense fails) OR reverse to O1 after O1's screen — defense must stay connected through two reads.

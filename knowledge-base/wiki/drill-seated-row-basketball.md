# Seated Row (Basketball)

## Objective
Build latissimus dorsi and upper-back strength to stabilize the shoulder girdle, maintain posture, prevent injury, and generate the physicality needed to score through contact in the paint.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Cable column machine with a seated row setup, or a dedicated seated row machine
- Neutral-grip handles (palms facing each other)

## Execution
1. Assume a **seated position** facing the cable column machine. Place both feet securely on the platform with a **slight bend in both knees**.
2. Grasp the exercise handles with **palms facing each other** (neutral grip) to initiate the movement.
3. Stabilize the upper back musculature. Pull the handles at a **controlled speed** toward the lower chest while keeping elbows to the sides.
4. **Do not let elbows pass the sides of the body.** Keep the spine erect — do not lean back excessively.
5. Maintaining a stable torso, return the handles to the starting position by extending the arms at a **slow, controlled tempo**.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Tall spine — do not lean back to pull the weight. That's cheating your lats."
- Elbows stay tight to the body — flared elbows reduce lat activation.
- "Squeeze the shoulder blades together at the peak of each pull."
- Controlled return is essential — the eccentric phase builds stability and prevents injury.
- **Band Row variation**: can be performed standing using elastic tubing or bands, or on a cable column with a straight bar — adds standing stabilization demand.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Seated cable row with light weight, focus on spinal neutrality and elbow path.
2. **Intermediate**: Increase resistance; maintain perfect tempo on return.
3. **Advanced**: Band Row standing — elastic band or cable row from standing position adds core and hip stability.

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Latissimus dorsi
- **Secondary**: Trapezius, rhomboid major, rhomboid minor, teres major, posterior deltoid, biceps brachii

## Basketball Focus
The upper back stabilizes the **shoulder girdle and maintains posture** — especially important because basketball players tend to have **longer torsos** that demand stronger back support. Key applications:
- Protects against injuries when **driving to the hoop** through contact
- Builds the strength to **fend off opponents** and create scoring openings with the body in the paint
- The stronger, more powerful athlete scores more reliably through physical contact under the boards

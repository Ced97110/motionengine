# Calf Pop-Up

## Objective
Strengthen the gastrocnemius, soleus, and Achilles tendon complex to improve jump height and second-jump elasticity for shooting, shot-blocking, and rebounding.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1
- **Location:** weight room or step platform
- **Equipment:** secure flat elevated surface (step, calf-raise machine platform, or stair edge); fixed structure for balance support (bar, wall, or machine handles)
- Calf-raise machine can be used for added load [S2, p.28]

## Execution
1. Stand erect with the balls of both feet on the edge of a secure flat surface. Hold on to a fixed structure with both hands for balance.
2. Slowly lower both heels toward the floor, stretching the Achilles tendons fully.
3. Reverse direction to **quickly pop up** onto the toes — the concentric phase is explosive.
4. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions. [S2, pp.28-29]

## Coaching Points
- **"Slow down, fast up"** — the lowering phase is controlled; the push-up phase is explosive to build tendon elasticity.
- Full range of motion — heels below the platform level on the descent is essential for Achilles stretching.
- Do not bounce at the bottom — use the stretch-shortening cycle deliberately with a brief pause.
- Balance hand contact with the support structure should be light (fingertip touch) rather than weight-bearing.
- Progress to single-leg calf pop-ups for unilateral strength.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Bilateral calf pop-up on a step, bodyweight.
2. **Intermediate:** Bilateral calf pop-up on calf-raise machine with added load.
3. **Advanced:** Single-leg calf pop-up on step, bodyweight, explosively; progress to loaded single-leg variation.

## Concepts Taught
- [[leg-strength-for-basketball]] — calf/Achilles complex is the final link in the jumping chain

## Basketball Application
Enhancing the strength of the gastrocnemius, soleus, and Achilles tendon complex helps jump higher during shooting and rebounding. Strengthening this muscle-tendon complex also enhances Achilles tendon elasticity — after missing a jump shot, a player can immediately jump again for the offensive rebound; a defender can jump to block a shot then immediately rebound. Enhanced elasticity provides a distinct second-jump advantage over an opponent. [S2, pp.28-29]

**Primary muscles:** gastrocnemius, soleus

**Secondary muscles:** tibialis posterior, peroneals

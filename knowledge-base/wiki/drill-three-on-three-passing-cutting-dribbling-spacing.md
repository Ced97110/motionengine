# Three-on-Three Passing, Cutting, Dribbling, and Spacing (Drill 65)

## Objective
Integrate all four motion offense rules into a fully live 3-on-3 setting with dribbling, driving, dribble moves, rocker steps, and all cut types — the most complete motion offense development drill in the progression.

## Setup
- 3 offensive players, 3 defenders
- Half court
- Full dribbling allowed
- Rotate: offense ↔ defense after 3 minutes
- Equipment: 1 basketball

## Execution

### Intermediate
1. Three offensive players and three defenders spread across the half court.
2. Defenders play live defense. Offensive players make the cuts dictated by how defenders play.
3. Dribbling is allowed — players cut, pass, and dribble, activating all dribble moves (in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin, pump fake, pump fake crossover) and the rocker step.
4. Players can score by cutting, faking, and driving while adhering to all motion offense rules.
5. After 3 minutes, offense and defense switch roles.
6. Example sequence (one of an infinite number):
   - Player 2 dribbles toward Player 1 who is overplayed by X1: this is a **dribbling entry** into the motion offense [S15, p.136]
   - Player 1 **backdoor-cuts** (Rule 4: cut when dribbler comes toward you)
   - Player 3 **V-cuts** to keep 15-foot spacing (Rule 2)
   - Player 2 passes back to Player 3 — now all three must move again (Rule 3)
   - Player 1 flash pivot cuts; Player 2 V-cuts to replace himself
   - The moves continue until the offense scores
7. Ball receivers can **square up to triple-threat position** on receiving the pass.
8. Players can **drive off the rocker step** or any dribbling offensive move.
9. Players can make a **move at the end of the dribble** to get off a jump shot or layup.

## Coaching Points
- This drill is the culmination of the cutting and passing chapter — all individual skills are now combined [S15, p.136]
- The "dribbling entry" concept (dribbling toward an overplayed teammate to trigger a backdoor cut) is a key teaching point specific to this drill
- Watch for players who do not react to a dribbler approaching — Rule 4 still applies even with full dribbling allowed
- Receivers should square up to triple-threat before reading their defender — do not skip this step in the drive to keep moving
- Encourage shot fakes and pump fakes at the end of the dribble for higher-percentage finishing

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Live 3-on-3 with dribbling; all four rules; read-and-react
2. **Advanced**: Add a pass limit before scoring; or restrict to layups only; or require defense to earn 2 consecutive stops before rotating

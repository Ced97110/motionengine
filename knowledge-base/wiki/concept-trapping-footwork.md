# Trapping Footwork — Releasing and Rotating After the Escape Pass

## Summary
When a double-team trap is broken by an escape pass, every trapper must use precise pivot footwork to release and rotate to their next defensive assignment as quickly as possible. The key technique: each trapper reverse pivots off the **outside foot** (foot away from the ball handler), swinging the **inside foot** toward the landing spot of the escape pass. Critically, trappers should not watch the ball in flight — they should **anticipate** where the escape pass will land and immediately pursue that spot, trying to beat the pass to its destination. [S6, pp.280-282]

## When to Use
- Any time a double-team trap is used: full-court press, three-quarter court, half-court traps, post double-teams
- When the escape pass is made from the trapped ball handler to a perimeter or corner receiver
- After the trap is broken — the pivot triggers the entire defensive rotation

## Key Principles
1. **Reverse pivot off the outside foot**: The foot away from the trapped ball handler is the pivot foot — the inside foot swings in the direction of the escape pass [S6, p.281]
2. **Anticipate, don't watch**: Do not track the flight of the ball before moving — anticipate the landing spot and move immediately [S6, p.281]
3. **Beat the pass**: The goal is to arrive at the new location before or as the ball arrives — impossible without proper footwork [S6, p.281]
4. **Different roles after release**: Some trappers re-trap at the new location; others rotate to off-ball positions to become interceptors or defenders — assignment depends on direction of escape pass and team scheme [S6, p.281]
5. **Non-trappers close out**: A defender not involved in the initial trap (e.g., X4) must begin closing out to form the next trap as soon as the escape pass is made [S6, p.281]

## Application Example
**Diagram 7-8 / 7-9 scenario**: X1 and X2 are trapping 01 near the wing. 01 escape passes to 02 in the deep corner.
- X4 (non-trapper) closes out toward 02 to form the new trap
- X2 releases from the initial trap: **reverse pivots off the left (outside) foot**, swings right foot toward the corner, and sprints to re-trap with X4
- X1 releases from the trap: **reverse pivots off the right (outside) foot**, rotates to a new off-ball defensive position
[S6, pp.281-282]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Watching the ball flight before moving** → Delayed rotation, offense gains free catch-and-attack opportunity — correction: anticipate and move simultaneously with the pass, not after [S6, p.281]
2. **Pivoting off the inside foot (toward the ball handler)** → Defender rotates away from the escape pass direction — correction: always pivot off the OUTSIDE foot [S6, p.281]
3. **Trappers hesitating to see what the ball handler does** → Escape pass beats the rotation — correction: drill the reverse pivot as a conditioned reflex, not a decision [S6, p.281]
4. **Non-trappers waiting** → Nobody closes out to the new ball location — correction: X4 must begin closing out the moment the trap forms, not when the escape pass is thrown [S6, p.281]

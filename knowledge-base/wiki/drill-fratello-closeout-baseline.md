# Fratello Closeout–Baseline Drive Drill

## Objective
Train the defender to close out quickly from the paint and cut off the dribbler's baseline drive using the body (chest), not the hands.

## Setup
- 1 coach, 2 offensive players, 1 defender
- Coach has the ball in the middle of the court
- Two offensive players at the wing positions
- Defender starts in the middle of the three-second lane

## Execution
1. Coach passes to one wing offensive player.
2. That offensive player drives hard to the baseline.
3. Defender sprints out to close out, slides, and **cuts off the baseline path by hitting the dribbler with the chest** (not pushing with hands).
4. At this point, the offensive player passes the ball back to the coach (offense can **only drive, not shoot**).
5. Coach immediately reverses the ball to the other wing offensive player, who drives to the baseline.
6. Defender must recover, close out, and cut off the second wing's baseline path.
7. Drill continues with alternating wing passes.

## Coaching Points
- Close out under control — sprint then chop feet into a closeout stance before contact.
- **Use the chest, never the hands** — hands push = foul; chest contact = good defense.
- The offensive player is restricted to driving only; this isolates the closeout skill.
- Defender must recover fully between reps — simulates the exhaustion of a real game.
- "Not pushing him with his hands" — the key technical cue. [S7, p.238]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach-controlled, slow passes; offense just catches and holds the ball.
2. **Intermediate**: As described; offense drives at 75% speed.
3. **Advanced**: Allow offense to shoot after the drive; defender must contest with body and hands.

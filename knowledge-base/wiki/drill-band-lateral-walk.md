# Band Lateral Walk

## Objective
Develop lower-extremity strength and lateral ankle stabilization for injury prevention and explosive lateral movements, while reinforcing the athletic defensive stance position.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (at least 10-15 feet)
- Equipment: resistance band placed around both ankles

## Execution
1. Stand with knees slightly bent in a minisquat position. Place a resistance band around both ankles.
2. Maintaining the squat position and keeping feet in neutral (no toeing in or out), walk sideways against the pull of the resistance band.
3. Complete the prescribed distance or number of steps in both directions (right and left).
4. **Progression**: Use a heavier resistance band [S2, p.139].

## Coaching Points
- "Stay in the squat — don't stand up between steps"
- Feet must stay neutral throughout — avoid turning the feet in or out
- Keep tension on the band at all times; don't let the feet come together and release tension
- "Lateral hip strength allows more stability and explosiveness at the hips when cutting to avoid an opponent" [S2, p.139]
- This drill trains the same athletic position used in defensive slides — connect it to on-court movement
- Watch for trunk lean; keep the spine upright throughout

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light band, slow pace, short distance
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate band, steady pace, longer distance
3. **Advanced**: Heavy band, quick lateral steps mimicking defensive slide tempo [S2, p.139]

# Defensive Shell Drill — Rebounding Variant

## Objective
Train all five players to execute correct box-out and offensive rebounding techniques simultaneously from every court position — on-ball, one pass away, and two passes away — by incorporating live shooting into the standard shell drill framework.

## Setup
- **Players**: 4-on-4 (standard shell formation) or 5-on-5
- **Court area**: Half court
- **Equipment**: Multiple basketballs for coaches
- **Formation**: Perimeter offense at 4 spots (corners + wings or guard positions); defense matched up; post if using 5v5 [S6, pp.88-90]

## Execution
1. Defense allows the perimeter players to pass the ball freely around the perimeter (no live defense of passes in basic version).
2. After a predetermined number of passes (coach decides), defense allows a player to shoot.
3. **On the shot**, every defender executes their box-out role based on their distance from the ball:
   - **On-ball defender**: Box out the shooter directly.
   - **One-pass-away defender**: Box out the nearest offensive opponent who is one pass from the shooter.
   - **Two-passes-away defender**: Box out the weak-side offensive opponent furthest from the ball.
4. Offensive players use any of the three escape techniques (butt-to-butt spin, swim move, step-back slash) to defeat their box-out assignment and attack the offensive board.
5. Both offensive and defensive rebounders must **constantly reevaluate positioning** as ball movement shifts shooting angles.
6. Players rotate from various court locations (both sides, above and below FT-line extended).
7. After several shots, offense and defense **swap roles** — all players develop both sets of skills. [S6, pp.88-90]

## Three Defensive Box-Out Roles (by Ball Proximity)
1. **On the ball (shooter's defender)**: Direct box-out on the shooter; first priority is to locate and seal.
2. **One pass away**: Defend and box out the nearest perimeter threat — the player who could have received the pass instead of the shot.
3. **More than one pass away (weak side)**: Help-side defender must crash the weak-side glass and prevent opponent from cutting to the offensive board. [S6, p.89]

## Coaching Points
- **Offense**: "Locate the ball first, then determine where it will land — beat the box-out to that spot."
- **Defense**: "Check first, then find the ball in the air — don't watch the shot, watch your opponent."
- Watch for defenders making contact but then releasing early — "hold the seal until the ball is secured."
- Rotate shooting locations each round — corner, wing, elbow, point — so all box-out angles are practiced.
- The drill must have different shooters from different locations to vary defensive scenarios.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach dictates the shot location; defense knows where the shot comes from — work fundamentals of each box-out role.
2. **Intermediate**: Random shooting — defense must recognize shooter's position and self-assign box-out roles.
3. **Advanced**: Live perimeter passes with defensive pressure; offense can drive and kick; full competitive rebounding — make/miss leads to continued possession or stop.

# Pallof Press and Variations

## Objective
Build anti-rotation core stability (transverse abdominis, rectus abdominis, internal/external obliques — and for overhead: quadratus lumborum and anti-lateral-flexion) using a cable machine to create rotational resistance in basketball-specific stances.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Cable machine with a single handle attachment
- Kneel perpendicular to the cable column (kneeling Pallof) or stand perpendicular (standing variations)

## Execution

### Standard Pallof Press (Kneeling)
1. Grasp the handle with both hands from the cable column and kneel perpendicular to the cable column.
2. Align shoulders over hips, hips over knees. Keep torso upright, chest up, shoulder blades pulled down and back.
3. Tuck elbows in next to the rib cage and hold the cable handle close to the chest — beginning position [S11, Ch.8].
4. Press the cable **straight out from the chest** with both hands until elbows are fully extended and the handle is directly in line with the shoulders.
5. Hold this isometric position for the prescribed time while maintaining neutral spine, chest up, shoulders down and back.
6. Return the handle to the chest. Breathe with controlled inhales and exhales throughout [S11, Ch.8].

### Standing Pallof Press
Progress to an athletic stance: feet hip-width apart, slight knee and hip flexion. Same press mechanics as above but increased demand on the lower body and spinal stabilizers [S11, Ch.8].

### Standing Pallof Press With Lateral Step
1. Stand in a wide, low athletic/defensive stance perpendicular to the cable column, feet wider than shoulders, hips and knees flexed.
2. Press cable straight out from chest until elbows are fully extended — rotational force from the cable is greatest at this point.
3. While holding the press, take **one lateral step away** from the cable column (pause briefly), then **one lateral step toward** the column (pause briefly).
4. Either return the cable to the chest between steps or hold the press and continue the lateral stepping pattern [S11, Ch.8].
5. Coaching cues: maintain neutral alignment through hips, torso, and shoulders; contract gluteal muscles to keep pelvis neutral.

### Standing Pallof Overhead Press
1. Stand perpendicular to the cable column, feet hip-width apart, slight knee/hip flexion, torso upright, chest up [S11, Ch.8].
2. Tuck elbows and hold cable handle close to the chest.
3. Press the cable **straight up overhead** until the handle is above the head.
4. With the cable overhead, the core musculature is challenged isometrically to resist **lateral flexion** of the spine — a different anti-lateral-flexion demand.
5. Hold for the prescribed time maintaining neutral spine and hips. Breathe with controlled inhales and exhales [S11, Ch.8].

## Coaching Points
- "Maintain neutral alignment through the hips, torso, and shoulders. If any compensations occur, reduce the weight" [S11, Ch.8]
- "To help keep the pelvis neutral, cue the athlete to contract the gluteal muscles" [S11, Ch.8]
- The greatest rotational demand occurs when the arms are fully extended — this is where athletes will feel the most anti-rotation tension
- Lateral step variation directly mimics defensive shuffling in a low stance — highly basketball-specific
- Overhead press variation trains anti-lateral-flexion — important for contested shooting, overhead rebounding, and shot blocking

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Kneeling Pallof press — stable base, minimal lower-body demand
2. **Intermediate**: Standing Pallof press — adds lower-body stability demand in an athletic stance
3. **Advanced**: Standing Pallof press with lateral step — combines anti-rotation with dynamic lateral movement in a defensive stance
4. **Most Advanced**: Standing Pallof overhead press — anti-lateral-flexion isometric in an athletic stance

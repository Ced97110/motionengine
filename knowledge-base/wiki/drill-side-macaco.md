# Side Macaco

## Objective
Develop the one-arm shoulder pivot strength, hip thrust power, and rotational mechanics needed for the back handspring, using a lateral leg swing that is less intimidating than a full backward flip.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Open flat floor space (minimum 6 feet clearance behind and to the side)
- No equipment required
- **Prerequisite**: Solid handstand — do not attempt without this [S9, p.184]

## Execution
1. Feet fairly close together; squat down and lean back.
2. Place one palm on the floor **behind you**, thumb pointing away from you, inner-elbow facing **out** (this allows the arm to rotate like an axle — critical for shoulder safety [S9, p.185]). Keep the arm locked.
3. Thrust up through your hips while swinging your free arm up and over your head.
4. Push off the floor with your legs — the main pushing leg should be on the **same side** as the arm swinging overhead.
5. With your locked arm as a pivot, swing your furthest leg up and **around the side of your head** in an arc; the other leg follows beneath it.
6. Touch down with your foot **behind the level of your hand**.
7. As your feet touch down, push through your pivot arm and use momentum to stand up. (The main swinging foot may touch down first.)

## Coaching Points
- **Critical tip**: Inner-elbow must face out from the start — "This is a rotating motion; your arm must revolve like an axle." [S9, p.185]
- Watch for athletes bending the pivot arm — it must remain locked throughout or the shoulder absorbs dangerous shear force
- "The height of your swinging legs is the progression variable — start small, feet close to the floor."
- Legs should reach **head height** as the mastery goal for this step before advancing to back macaco
- For this step, legs should NOT go above head height — that belongs to back macaco [S9, p.184]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Begin with very low leg swing — feet barely leave the floor; focus on arm position and hip thrust mechanics.
2. **Intermediate**: Swing legs to mid-thigh height; maintain locked arm throughout.
3. **Advanced**: Legs reach head height with full hip extension — ready to progress to back macaco (Step 5).

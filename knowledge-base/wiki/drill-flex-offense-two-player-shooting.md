# Flex Offense — Shooting With Two Players

## Objective
Practice the baseline-cut and vertical-screen reads that are the core of the flex offense by shooting off both a baseline cut and a high pop in alternating pairs.

## Setup
- 2 players per repetition (rotating lines of 2 behind the baseline)
- 2 coaches (or passers) positioned at the low-post areas on each side of the lane — they receive the initial pass and return it to shooters
- Each player in line has a ball
- Half court

## Execution
1. Two players at the front of the line step onto the court to the low-post positions (one on each side).
2. Each player passes the ball to the coach/passer on their side.
3. Player 2 cuts baseline and comes off the screen of Player 3 (or the cone/screener), reading over or under based on the drill setup.
4. Player 3 pops high after the screen.
5. Both receive passes from the coaches/passers, shoot, grab their own rebounds, and go to the **opposite** line from where they started.
6. On the next repetition, the roles are inverted: 3 cuts baseline off 2's screen, and 2 pops high.

## Coaching Points
- "Shoulder-to-hip" on the screen — the cutter should scrape the screener's shoulder to lose the defender
- Hands ready before the catch — call for the ball with a target hand
- The popping player must square up immediately on the catch and be ready to shoot
- Coaches/passers should deliver the ball on time so the shooter is catching in rhythm, not waiting
- Watch that the popping player doesn't come too high — proper spacing is at the three-point line or wing area

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No defenders; focus on footwork, timing of the cut, and reading over vs. under
2. **Intermediate**: Add a passive defender on the cutter; cutter must read the defender's position before committing to a direction
3. **Advanced**: Live defender; add the post roll option — coaches can also pass to the screener/5 who rolls to the basket after the screen

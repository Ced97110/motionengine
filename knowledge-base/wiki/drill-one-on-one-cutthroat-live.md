# One-on-One Cutthroat Live (Workout #4-26)

## Objective
Test and develop live-ball combination moves in a fully competitive, game-realistic 1-on-1 setting where offensive skills are challenged by live defense.

## Setup
- 2+ players (multiple players rotate in cutthroat format)
- Any area of the floor (half court preferred, can be played anywhere)
- 1 basketball
- Score is kept — first player to 21 points wins
- Live defense required [S3]

## Execution

### How to Start Play
- Defense rolls the ball out to the offense, OR
- A coach makes a pass to the offensive player using various cut types to simulate game catches [S3]

### Scoring
- First player to reach **21 points** wins
- Standard scoring (2-point field goals; adjust as needed for 3PT attempts)
- **Make-it/take-it rule**: Once the offense reaches 3 points, they keep possession on every made basket ("make it, take it") [S3]

### Rules for the Offense
1. Must **catch with a rip** (ball secured below the chin with two hands, elbows out)
2. Must have a **dominant pivot foot** established before making a move
3. Must **score off a live-ball move or combination live-ball move**
4. Maximum of **three dribbles** to create a scoring opportunity
5. Violation of any rule = **offense loses their turn** [S3]

### Rules for the Defense
1. If the defender **fouls**, they lose their turn
2. If the defender gets a **stop** (turnover, missed shot), they rotate to offense [S3]

## Coaching Points
- "Be creative — find a rhythm and tempo that works best for you"
- Remind players it's okay if moves don't work at first; keep working on them [S3]
- The make-it/take-it rule after 3 points forces both sides to compete — offense wants to stay on offense, defense wants the ball
- The 3-dribble limit is non-negotiable — it forces decisive, efficient offensive actions
- Coach should observe: Is the player establishing a pivot foot? Are they catching with a rip? Are they using true live-ball combos or just dribbling?
- "One-on-one cutthroat is the best way to improve and develop live-ball moves in a competitive situation" [S3]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Allow 4-5 dribbles; remove the make-it/take-it rule; focus only on establishing pivot foot
2. **Intermediate**: Standard rules with 3-dribble limit; coach-called violations
3. **Advanced**: Add a shot clock (5 seconds), reduce to 2 dribbles, play to 15 with win-by-2

# Combo Hop Back / Roll Out Drill

## Objective
Train the hop-back/roll-out two-move sequence: create space with a hop back, then counter a closing defender by rolling the ball forward into a new live-dribble attack — bilaterally off 1 and 2 dribbles.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court — three-point line area transitioning to the paint
- Shot tracking table (FGA, FGM %, FTA, FTM %)

## Execution
1. Player starts inside or at the three-point line with a live dribble.
2. **1-Dribble Hop Back to Roll Out Left**: take 1 left-hand dribble, hop backward to gain space, then immediately roll the ball forward with the left hand (roll-out dribble) to re-initiate a live dribble attack toward the basket.
3. Finish with a pull-up jump shot, layup, or floater depending on the read.
4. Shoot 5 (Workout #4-16), 10 (Workout #4-17), or 20 (Workout #4-18) reps going left.
5. Step to the free-throw line; shoot 5–10 FTs with full routine.
6. **1-Dribble Hop Back to Roll Out Right**: mirror going right.
7. Follow the L/R/FT cycle for 1-dribble roll-outs, then repeat the entire structure for **2-dribble** hop-back/roll-outs.
8. Total: 4 or 8 combo sets + 4 or 8 FT clusters depending on volume.

**Volume Progressions:**
- **Workout #4-16 (40 shots)**: 4 sets × 5 combos + 4 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 12–20 (60%) | FT goal: 17–20 (85%)
- **Workout #4-17 (80 shots)**: 4 sets × 10 combos + 4 × 10 FTs | FG goal: 24–40 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (85%)
- **Workout #4-18 (120 shots)**: 4 sets × 20 combos + 4 × 10 FTs | FG goal: 48–80 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (85%)

## Coaching Points
- **The read**: the roll-out is triggered when the defender follows the hop back and closes the gap — if they stay, shoot the hop back shot instead
- **Roll, don't push**: the roll-out dribble is a controlled forward push of the ball — not a forced through-the-legs dribble — maintaining live dribble legality
- **Same hand**: the roll-out should use the same hand as the initiating dribble to maintain ball security (left dribble → left roll-out)
- **Explode through the roll**: as soon as the ball is rolling forward, the player's body must accelerate to catch up — the roll-out creates an advantage only if the player attacks immediately
- Cue: **"Hop back. Defender follows. Roll through. Attack."**
- Track whether finishes are layups vs. pull-ups — advanced players should develop both as live reads

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (40 shots)**: 5 reps per set; practice the hop back and roll-out as two separate stationary skills before linking them
2. **Intermediate (80 shots)**: 10 reps per set; add the live dribble initiation and work at 75% speed
3. **Advanced (120 shots)**: 20 reps per set at game speed; have a passive defender react to the hop back to make the roll-out decision live

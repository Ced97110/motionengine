# Glute-Ham Raise

## Objective
Build elite-level hamstring and glute strength through simultaneous hip extension and knee flexion, directly improving jumping, sprinting, and deceleration for basketball.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Glute-ham bench (GHD machine)
- Roller pad positioned at the **mid to lower thigh**
- Feet firmly on the foot plate (some shorter athletes may not reach the platform fully)

## Execution
1. Start face-down on the glute-ham bench with the torso **parallel to the floor** — ear, hip, knee, and ankle should form a straight line (a).
2. **Push feet into the foot plate** and raise the torso by contracting the hamstrings while maintaining full hip extension (b).
3. Knees flex to lift the body into a **vertical upright position** (c).
4. Keeping the hamstrings engaged, allow the knees to slowly extend and the torso to lower back to the starting position.
5. Maintain the ear-hip-knee-ankle alignment throughout the movement.

## Coaching Points
- **Do not hyperextend the lower back** — hip extension should come from the glutes, not lower back compression
- Control the speed of both the upward and downward movements — this is not a speed drill
- "Squeeze the glutes" at the top of the movement
- No pressure or contraction should be felt in the lower back when extending the glutes
- **Exhale** after the sticking point of the upward movement; **inhale** during the downward movement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Assisted glute-ham raise with a resistance band for upward assistance
2. **Intermediate**: Bodyweight glute-ham raise with controlled tempo
3. **Advanced**: Add load (hold a plate at the chest) or increase tempo

## Common Mistakes
1. **Hips flexing during the raise** → takes tension off hamstrings → cue "stay tall through the hips"
2. **Lower back contraction felt** → indicates glute extension is insufficient → focus on squeezing glutes before initiating
3. **Uncontrolled descent** → too fast, loses eccentric stimulus → cue "fight gravity on the way down"

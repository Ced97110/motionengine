# Weakest Spot Make-Goal Workouts (Workouts #11-13, #11-14, #11-15)

## Objective
Eliminate cold zones by forcing players to make a fixed number of shots from their three most difficult spots — building the mechanics, mental toughness, and repetitions needed to become a complete shooter.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court
- Player self-selects 3 spots on the court where they struggle the most
- Throw ball to self (spinout), sprint after every rebound

## Execution

### Workout #11-13: Make 10 at Weakest Spots
1. Player identifies **3 spots** on the floor where they have the most trouble shooting (e.g., opposite-hand wing, baseline corner, deep mid-range).
2. At Spot 1: throw ball out, sprint to it, square up, and shoot — **must make 10 shots** from this spot.
3. Attempts are unlimited — only makes count toward the goal.
4. Hustle after every rebound; no walking.
5. Once 10 makes are recorded at Spot 1, move to Spot 2 and repeat.
6. Once 10 makes at Spot 2, move to Spot 3.
- **Total makes required:** 30

### Workout #11-14: Make 20 at Weakest Spots
- Same structure, but **make 20 shots** at each spot before advancing.
- **Total makes required:** 60

### Workout #11-15: Make 30 at Weakest Spots
- Same structure, but **make 30 shots** at each spot before advancing.
- **Total makes required:** 90

## Coaching Points
- "Your weakest spots become your most-guarded spots — defenders know. You have to fix them."
- Players must be honest about their weaknesses; don't select medium-difficulty spots as 'weakest.'
- Hustle after every rebound — this is a conditioning and mental fortitude drill.
- Track FGA and FGM per spot on a shot chart to measure progress over time.
- Do not force technique changes mid-workout — focus on reps and makes; address mechanics with a coach separately.
- Pairing this workout with [[drill-favorite-spot-make-goals]] in the same session creates balanced shooting development.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #11-13):** Make 10 per weakest spot — introduction to deliberate practice at hard zones.
2. **Intermediate (Workout #11-14):** Make 20 per spot — extended exposure, more refinement.
3. **Advanced (Workout #11-15):** Make 30 per spot — highest-volume weakness elimination for elite development.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Choosing 'medium' spots instead of truly weakest spots** → Players must be honest; review shot charts to identify real cold zones.
2. **Walking after rebounds** → Insist on full sprint — fatigue is part of the drill's purpose.
3. **Changing spots before reaching the make goal** → Make the number or stay at the spot — accountability is the entire point.

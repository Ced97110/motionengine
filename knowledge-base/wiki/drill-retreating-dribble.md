# Retreating Dribble (Drill #27)

## Objective
Develop the retreat dribble — a backward slide-step escape move that allows ball handlers to avoid double teams and traps without picking up the dribble.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- 4 lines at one baseline
- 1 basketball per player
- Duration: 1 minute

<!-- DIAGRAM: Four columns of players at baseline. Dribble paths show zigzag 45-degree angles up the floor with retreat segments indicated by curly/zigzag arrow symbols. [S15, p.61] -->

## Execution
1. Players line up in four columns at the baseline.
2. Players **dribble slowly**, then **stop and dribble backward** a few steps before circling a step or two and going down the floor at a new angle.
   - *Key concept*: Instead of picking up the basketball after dribbling into a double team or trap, the player backtracks a few steps then speeds off in another direction, hoping to leave the trapping defenders behind.
3. Players dribble approximately 15 feet (4.5 m), **retreat**, then **attack at a 45-degree angle**; retreat again; then attack in the **opposite direction at a 45-degree angle** with the opposite hand — continuing this pattern to the far end.
4. Players dribble down with the **right hand** and back with the **left hand**.
5. At the far end, execute a **reverse (or front) pivot** before returning, using the retreat dribble every third or fourth dribble.
6. Players **jump-stop** about 15 feet (4.5 m) from the teammate next in line and deliver a **chest or bounce pass**.
7. All players repeat the sequence.

## Options
- **Race format**: Guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Advanced Progression
1. **Alternating dual-ball**: One ball in each hand; alternate bounces while driving down the floor.
2. **Simultaneous dual-ball**: Both balls touch hands and floor at exactly the same time.

## Coaching Points
- **Never turn your back**: When retreat-slide stepping backward, players must **never turn their back on defenders** — slide-step backward while keeping eyes downcourt.
- **Timing the explosion**: Try to time the explosive forward move with the moment defenders **rise up out of their low, trapping stance**.
- **Head/shoulder fakes**: Use fakes to make defenders think you are going to split them or attack the opposite direction before the actual escape move.
- **Ball position**: 2–3 feet (.6–.9 m) from the knee; body between ball and defender at all times.
- **Eyes up**: Eyes straight ahead, not on the basketball.
- **Slide-step mechanics**: During the retreat, use a **slide-step dribble** in control stance; shift to speed stance to explode away.
- **Fingerpads**: Dribble with the first pads of the fingers only.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the retreat pattern — 3 forward dribbles, slide back 2 steps, attack new angle. No defenders.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed bilateral retreat drill with pivot and pass at the end.
3. **Advanced**: Dual-ball versions; add a live defender in the backcourt to simulate actual trapping pressure.

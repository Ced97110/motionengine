# Front Foot to Pivot Foot

## Objective
Teach defenders to use the front-foot-to-pivot-foot stance — the most effective on-ball defensive stance — eliminating the jump shot and the drive away from the pivot foot while practicing reactive footwork against jab step, crossover, and reverse pivot.

## Setup
- Half court, free-throw line area
- Groups of 3: player 1 (offense), X1 (defense), line of additional players
- Rotation: offense → defense → end of line
- Coach designates which foot is the pivot foot each repetition
- Duration: 3 minutes

<!-- DIAGRAM: Coach at the top of the key, ① at the free-throw line area, X1 defending ①, line of players outside the lane. -->

## Execution
1. Players line up; coach tells player 1 which foot to establish as the pivot foot.
2. X1 rolls the ball to 1 (simulating a catch or dribble pickup).
3. X1 must immediately place **front foot up near 1's pivot foot** — 3 inches away, slightly across from 1's pivot foot.
4. Player 1 performs the following sequence in order:
   - Jab step
   - Jab-step crossover
   - Reverse pivot
5. Observe and correct X1's footwork at each stage.
6. Repeat as many times as needed until footwork is perfect.

**X1's footwork responses:**
- **Jab step**: no reaction needed — already in retreat-step position; close enough to prevent uncontested jump shot
- **Jab-step crossover**: use the swing step; attacker's body is sideways to basket (can't shoot); attacker can't get free foot in front of swing-step foot
- **Reverse pivot**: swing step first (Step 1), then reposition left foot up against attacker's new pivot foot (Step 2); attacker must dribble, at which point defender is on the shooting shoulder

## Coaching Points
1. Front foot is placed **3 inches from the pivot foot** and **2-3 feet directly across** — neither too close nor too far
2. This stance **eliminates the jump shot** (defender too close) and **eliminates the drive away from the pivot foot** (attacker gains no advantage)
3. The only option left for the attacker is to drive toward the pivot foot — and the defender is already on that shoulder
4. **Defender's body position**: crouch with knees bent to near-sitting; able to touch floor with palms; head above center, under attacker's armpits; torso slightly forward; arms wide to both sides
5. On the jab-step crossover: "The attacker cannot shoot because the body is sideways to the basket... And the attacker cannot get the front foot in front of the defender's swing-step foot" [S15, p.266]
6. On the reverse pivot: the defender's quick two-step maneuver gets the left foot back up against the new pivot foot before the dribble begins
7. "Under no circumstances would the defender ever want the front foot up against the attacker's free foot" [S15, p.266] — that gives the attacker a jab-step direct drive

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through each movement slowly — jab, crossover, pivot — stopping after each for correction
2. **Intermediate**: Full sequence at game speed with coach calling the next move
3. **Advanced**: Live 1-on-1 from the established pivot foot — attacker tries to score, defender uses full stance system

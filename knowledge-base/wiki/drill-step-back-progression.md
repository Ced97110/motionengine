# Step-Back Shooting Progression Drill

## Objective
Build the step-back move from correct footwork mechanics through game-speed application, covering weak-side and strong-side variations and the step-back-rise-up-and-go counter.

## Setup
- 1-2 players per basket
- Half court; begin at the top of the key, then progress to wings
- Ball (spinout for solo; partner passing for pair work)
- Adequate space to simulate dribble drives into the paint

## Execution

### Stage 1 — Footwork Only (No Shot)
1. Starting at the top of the key, dribble toward the paint
2. **Weak-side**: Attack with the weak hand, plant the shooting (inside) foot, push off and step back at an angle to the weak side; land on balance in a shooter's stance
3. **Strong-side**: Attack with the strong hand, plant the nonshooting (inside) foot, push off and step back; square/drift to the basket; land on balance
4. Repeat 10-15 times each side, focusing only on the push-off and landing — no shot yet [S5, p.110]

### Stage 2 — Top of Key, Live Dribble, Midrange
1. **Weak-side step back**: Attack the paint with weak hand → plant shooting foot → step back at midrange distance → shoot
2. Shoot for makes, shooting strings, or three-strikes style
3. **Strong-side step back**: Attack with strong hand → plant nonshooting foot → square to target → step back at midrange → shoot [S5, pp.110-111]

### Stage 3 — Extend to Three-Point Range
Once midrange is consistent, begin the dribble drive from further out so the step back lands behind the three-point line [S5, p.111]

### Stage 4 — On-the-Move Wing Routine
From either wing, spin the ball out in four directions and incorporate step backs:
1. Right wing → spin to baseline → drive weak hand → weak-side step back
2. Right wing → spin to baseline → drive strong hand → strong-side step back
3. Right wing → spin to middle → drive weak hand → weak-side step back
4. Right wing → spin to middle → drive strong hand → strong-side step back
5. Repeat from left wing (8 total combinations across both wings) [S5, p.111]

### Stage 5 — Combination Moves
Layer in previously learned skills:
- Catch on the move → stub toe → shot fake → show-and-go (crossover) → strong-side step back [S5, p.111]
- Catch → jab → jab-and-go drive → step back when help arrives

### Stage 6 — Step Back, Rise Up, and Go Counter
1. Execute the step back — partner/defender simulates closing out on the step-back shot
2. As the "defender" approaches: raise head and shoulders (simulate shot gather)
3. As the "defender" commits → lower head and shoulders → drive past for pull-up or layup
4. Variation: after weak-side step back with left hand → defender closes left → immediately crossover right → pull-up jump shot [S5, p.111]

## Coaching Points
- "Always push off the inside pivot foot — not the outside foot" [S5, p.108]
- Check landing position every time: shooting foot forward, toe to target, knees bent, shoulders in front of feet
- On the strong-side step back: "Stay low or you will drift and shoot off-balance" [S5, p.109]
- Cue for landing: "Chest over toes — if your chest is behind your toes, your shot goes short"
- Emphasize building from footwork up — "This move needs to be built from the ground up" [S5, p.110]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Footwork only (Stage 1) — weak-side step back only; no ball, no shot
2. **Intermediate**: Stage 2 with ball; weak-side step back to midrange shot; then strong-side
3. **Advanced**: Full wing routine (Stage 4) + combination moves (Stage 5) + step-back-rise-up-and-go counter (Stage 6)

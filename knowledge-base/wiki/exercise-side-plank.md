# Side Plank

## Objective
Build lateral isometric core stability (transverse abdominis, rectus abdominis, quadratus lumborum, multifidus, erector spinae, internal/external obliques) for resisting lateral force in post positioning, defensive slides, and contact situations.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor or exercise mat
- No equipment (suspension trainer for advanced variation)

## Execution
1. Lie on the floor on one side of the body. Stack the feet, knees, hips, and shoulders on top of each other and in line [S11, Ch.8].
2. Position the elbow of the bottom arm directly under the shoulder so that the upper arm is perpendicular to the floor. Forearm contacts the floor.
3. Begin by driving the **elbow and the side of the feet into the floor** to lift the hips up until they are in neutral position and in line with the head, feet, and spine.
4. At this point, the elbow and the bottom side foot become the only two points of contact with the floor.
5. Hold the side plank position for the prescribed time. Breathe with slow, controlled inhales and exhales [S11, Ch.8].
6. Lower, then change to the other side.

**Suspension Side Plank Variation**: Feet are placed inside suspension strap loops at ankle height while the elbow is on the floor. Drive forearm into floor and bottom ankle into the strap simultaneously to achieve a neutral hip position. This is the most demanding variation [S11, Ch.8].

## Coaching Points
- "Squeeze the buttocks and push the hips forward to keep aligned with the knees and shoulders" [S11, Ch.8]
- "Keep neck aligned with the rest of the spine" — the head should not drop or crane upward [S11, Ch.8]
- The non-support arm can be extended upward to keep shoulders stacked and increase stability demand [S11, Ch.8]
- Watch for the hips dropping toward the floor — the most common compensation; reduce hold time if this occurs

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regressed)**: Flex the knees to 90 degrees — lower body is supported at the bottom knee, shorter lever, easier hold [S11, Ch.8]
2. **Intermediate**: Full side plank on elbow and foot edge for timed holds
3. **Advanced (Progression)**: Extend the bottom elbow so the palm is on the floor — requires strong shoulder stabilization [S11, Ch.8]
4. **Most Advanced**: Suspension side plank with feet in TRX loops — requires maximal core, hip, and ankle engagement simultaneously [S11, Ch.8]

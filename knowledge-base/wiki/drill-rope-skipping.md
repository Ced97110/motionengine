# Rope-Skipping

## Objective
Improve quickness and elastic strength in the lower body — the continuous rhythmic ground contacts develop ankle stiffness, calf reactivity, and Achilles tendon elasticity critical for court quickness.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Jump rope (standard or weighted)
- Open floor space or court
- Optional: spot markers on the floor for target jumps

## Execution
1. **Skip rope** while jumping to designated spots on the ground — standard bilateral skip.
2. Can be performed with **both legs or a single leg** depending on the training goal.
3. Perform for time (e.g., 30 seconds per set) or a set number of jumps.

## Coaching Points
- Land on the **balls of the feet** — never flat-footed.
- Minimize ground contact time — the ankle should act like a spring.
- Maintain an athletic posture — slight forward lean, core engaged.
- Arms should stay relatively still — the wrist drives the rope, not the arms.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard bilateral skip in place, moderate pace.
2. **Intermediate — Variable Patterns**: Change skip patterns in response to coach commands — both-leg or single-leg variations while responding to cues.
3. **Advanced — Weighted Rope-Skipping**: Perform with a weighted rope to increase demand on the calves and forearms.
4. **Advanced — Side-Skipping**: Skip to the left or right — the arm and knee sequence remains consistent while moving laterally; the most challenging variation.

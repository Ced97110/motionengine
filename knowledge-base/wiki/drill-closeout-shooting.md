# Closeout Shooting

## Objective
Train the shooter to receive a pass and shoot efficiently under active defensive pressure (a closing-out defender), eliminating the high-low-high catch and forcing a true low-to-high shooting motion.

## Setup
- 2 players per basket
- Half court, 7 spots around the basket (midrange distance)
- 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Shooter starts in a balanced athletic stance at a chosen spot.
2. Passer delivers the ball from under the basket to the shooter's shot pocket.
3. **Immediately after releasing the pass**, the passer sprints directly at the shooter, taking several short choppy steps to slow down and establish defensive positioning.
4. The passer mirrors the ball with a hand to contest the shot.
5. Shooter takes a one-two step into the catch, reads the closing-out defender, and shoots.
6. Rotate through 7 spots. Use a set number of makes per spot, or shoot strings style. Shooters and passers can alternate shots and positions.

## Coaching Points
- **"Always go from low to high."** The closeout is specifically designed to stop the shooter from catching high and then going low (high-low-high). Catching low and rising eliminates the extra time the defender needs to get there.
- **Proper closeout mechanics for the defender:** Sprint directly at the shooter → chop steps near the shooter → hand up to mirror the ball. Do not foul and do not leave the feet prematurely.
- **Shooter must still call for the ball.** Even with a closing-out defender, the call and hand target are non-negotiable.
- **The closeout teaches urgency.** A shooter who learns to be efficient under pressure becomes a much greater threat in games.
- **Passer's sprint simulates a rotating defender** — common in any good defensive scheme.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Passer closes out slowly to let shooter focus on the catch mechanics.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed closeout, shooter focuses on low catch and rise; no shot fakes allowed.
3. **Advanced**: Shooter can use shot fakes or jab-then-shoot actions against the closing-out defender; add three-point range.

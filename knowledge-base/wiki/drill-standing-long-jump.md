# Standing Long Jump

## Objective
Improve lower-body power through a maximal bilateral horizontal jump, training the same kinetic chain used for first-step acceleration. [S8, p.172]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor or field with measuring tape/marks
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Stand with both feet about shoulder-width apart or slightly narrower.
2. Load the legs by flexing the knees and hips and cocking the arms backward.
3. Propel the body up and out for distance by extending the legs and using the arms to help thrust the body forward.
4. Land with both feet and note distance achieved.

## Coaching Points
- "Load and launch" — the loading phase (hip and knee flex + arm cock) must be quick and deliberate.
- Arm swing contributes 10–15% to jump distance — coach athletes to use their arms aggressively.
- Emphasize forward propulsion angle, not just vertical — jump out, not just up.
- Track distance over time as a power development metric.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Single standing long jump; measure distance.
2. **Intermediate**: Multiple consecutive standing long jumps (bounding).
3. **Advanced**: Triple standing long jump (3 consecutive); add weighted vest.

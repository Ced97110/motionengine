# 4 × 4s

## Objective
Increase hip flexor range of motion and stride length by alternating continuously between High Knees and Butt-Kickers over a 33-yard distance, performed 4 times each.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open flat surface — field, track, or court
- Distance: 33 yards (30 meters) per rep

## Execution
1. Begin moving forward down the 33-yard lane.
2. Continuously alternate between **High Knees** and **Butt-Kickers**:
   - High Knees: thigh drives to parallel with the ground, foot dorsiflexed
   - Butt-Kickers: heel drives up to the gluteus on the recovery phase
3. Perform each drill **4 times** (sets) over the 33-yard distance.
4. The combination of both movements, when executed correctly, begins to simulate full sprint mechanics.

## Coaching Points
- Transition smoothly between high knees and butt-kickers — do not stop between drill types.
- The integration of these two movements creates the complete leg cycle of sprinting: knee drive (high knees) + heel recovery (butt-kickers).
- Arms must remain active and coordinated throughout — 90-degree bend, alternating with legs.
- Do not rush through at the expense of form — mechanics must be correct for the neural pattern to transfer.
- Use as part of warm-up or immediately before sprint work to activate the sprint mechanics pattern.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the alternating pattern slowly for 15 meters; emphasize mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Light jog pace through 33 yards, 4 sets of each
3. **Advanced**: Near-sprint speed through 33 yards, 4 sets of each; challenge transition smoothness

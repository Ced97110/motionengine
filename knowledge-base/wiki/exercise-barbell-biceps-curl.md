# Barbell Biceps Curl (Basketball Strength Training)

## Objective
Strengthen the biceps brachii, brachialis, and brachioradialis — the elbow flexors that support pulling actions in rebounding, holding a defender off the ball, driving through contact, and ball security.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: Barbell (or EZ-curl bar, dumbbells, or cable machine)
- **Players**: 1 athlete
- **Location**: Weight room

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Start in an athletic stance. Hold a bar about shoulder-width apart with a **supinated (underhand) grip**. Stand up tall; knees slightly flexed. Bar rests on the thighs.
2. Curl the bar by **flexing the elbows** and moving the hands toward the front of the shoulders. The bar should travel in an **arc**.
3. **Elbows remain at the side of the body** throughout — do not let them drift forward or backward.
4. After reaching the top, **lower the bar back toward the thighs** in a steady, controlled manner until elbows are fully extended.

## Coaching Points
- Do **not swing the body** to move the bar — this is body English, not a biceps curl
- **Pause at the top and bottom** to eliminate momentum
- Do **not allow the elbows to leave the side** of the body when lowering
- Keep the **core tight** to prevent the low back from flexing or extending
- "Squeeze at the top" — brief isometric contraction at peak elbow flexion

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: EZ-curl bar (reduces wrist stress); lean against a wall to prevent body sway
2. **Intermediate**: Straight barbell with full arc, strict form
3. **Advanced**: Split stance for anti-rotation stability; dumbbell variation for bilateral independence

## Variations
- **Split stance**: Staggered foot position — increases core anti-rotation demand
- **EZ-curl bar**: Reduces wrist pronation stress
- **Wall-lean**: Back against a wall prevents any torso sway
- **Cable machine**: Provides constant tension at the bottom of the movement
- **Dumbbell curl**: Bilateral or alternating — allows supination through the range

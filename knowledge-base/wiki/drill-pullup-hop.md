# Pull-Up Hop (Muscle-Up Chain — Step 4)

## Objective
Teach the body to create "airtime" above a horizontal bar by briefly releasing both hands at the top of a kipping pull-up — the critical bridge between the kipping pull-up and the clap pull-up, and by extension, the muscle-up.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Full-height horizontal overhead bar
- No additional equipment needed
- Prerequisites: mastery of [[drill-kipping-pullup]] (Step 3)

## Execution
1. Grab and hang from an overhead horizontal bar, shoulder-width grip, shoulders packed down.
2. Initiate a **swing kip** (Step 1) to build momentum.
3. At peak backswing, thrust hips forward and pull down on the bar, using combined momentum and arm pull to carry upward.
4. At the **very peak of the upward movement**, quickly **lift both hands off the bar** for a fraction of a second.
5. Re-grip the bar immediately and lower back into the swing kip position.
6. Repeat, progressively building height during the airtime phase [S9, p.19].

## Coaching Points
- **"Create airtime"** — the goal is to teach the body that it can generate enough force to leave the bar, even momentarily. This is the same skill concept as pop-ups for clap push-ups [S9, p.19]
- Begin by alternately lifting *one hand* at a time to feel the skill, before attempting both hands simultaneously
- Progress by increasing the height of the hands-off-bar phase — an advanced practitioner should be able to lift palms **6 inches or more** from the bar at the peak [S9, p.19]
- Watch for: athletes who simply loosen their grip without truly releasing — full release creates the neurological adaptation needed
- Quality over quantity — a few reps with true airtime are more valuable than many with partial release

## Progressions
1. **Beginner/Regression**: Build maximum speed in the kipping pull-up first. Then begin by alternately lifting one hand off the bar at peak — not both simultaneously [S9, p.19].
2. **Intermediate**: Release both hands for a fraction of a second at the peak. Re-grip immediately.
3. **Advanced**: Build 6+ inches of palm clearance from the bar. Progress to [[drill-clap-pullup]] (Step 5 of the chain) once consistent airtime is achieved [S9, p.19].

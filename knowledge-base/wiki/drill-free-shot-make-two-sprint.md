# Make Two, Sprint to Baseline

## Objective
Condition players to shoot consecutive free shots while winded, simulating end-of-possession free-throw situations in games.

## Setup
- 1 player per basket (can run multiple baskets simultaneously)
- Free-throw line, full court for sprints
- Running make count target: **20 total makes**

## Execution
1. Player shoots **two free shots**.
2. If both are made: player **sprints to the opposite baseline and back**, then returns to shoot two more.
3. If a shot is **missed**: player **immediately sprints down and back**, then returns to the line.
4. Goal: accumulate **20 total makes**.
5. Note: only makes count toward the total — missed shots restart the consecutive-makes sequence.

## Coaching Points
- *"You don't want to get in the habit of shooting one for two at the line"* — the drill penalizes any miss with a sprint, reinforcing the standard of making both shots [S5, p.55]
- Periodic sprinting keeps the drill **gamelike** — players in games are winded when stepping to the line
- Require the **full pre-shot routine** even after a sprint; this is when players are most tempted to skip it
- Players must maintain **consistent technique under fatigue** — coaches should watch for form breakdowns late in the drill
- *"The real secret to mastering free shots is to shoot more of them."* [S5, p.55]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk to baseline instead of sprint; target 10 makes.
2. **Intermediate**: Sprint as described; target 20 makes.
3. **Advanced**: Increase to 30 makes; add a second consecutive-miss rule (two misses = two sprints).

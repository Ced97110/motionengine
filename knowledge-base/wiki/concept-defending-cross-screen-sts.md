# Defending Cross-Screen, Screen-the-Screener Sets

## Summary
When defenses effectively disrupt a basic cross screen, offenses counter by adding a third screener in the play — creating a cross-screen + screen-the-screener (STS) combination. This layered action is more difficult to defend because it requires coordinated effort among three defender pairs simultaneously. Defending this set requires talking, bumping, denying, and possibly switching on the final screen to prevent an easy basket [S13, p.209].

## When to Use
- When the offense recognizes that the defense is successfully "forcing down" crossing action on a basic cross screen and adjusts by adding a third screener
- Against teams that run cross-screen + pin-down STS to free a catch-and-shoot shooter at the free-throw circle
- In any man-to-man scheme where scouting reveals the opponent runs complex multi-screen half-court sets

## Key Principles
1. **Three-pair coordination** — three defender pairs (X2/X5, X2/X4, X4/O2) must execute simultaneously, not sequentially.
2. **Force the cross screener down and low** — X2 forces O2 down and low so O2 cannot get a good angle to screen X5; identical to the basic cross screen rule.
3. **X5 maintains vision and hand contact on O5** — step toward the screen while keeping eyes on both ball and opponent.
4. **X4 must bump O4's pin-down screen on X2** — the big issue is X4 slowing up O4's pin-down on X2 as O5 moves across the lane. This disruption may cause O2 to commit a three-second violation.
5. **Consider switching on the last screen** — against well-set screens, switching on the final pin-down (X4 bumping O4) may be the only way to prevent the catch-and-shoot at the free-throw circle.
6. **Three offensive options exist** — O3 has the post (O5), the free-throw circle (O2), and ball reversal to O1; all three must be covered by the rotating defense [S13, p.209].

## Player Responsibilities
- **X2**: Force O2 (cross screener) down and low, deny the angle; then fight through O4's pin-down to prevent O2's catch-and-shoot at the free-throw circle.
- **X5**: Step toward O2's screen while maintaining vision, position, and hand contact with O5; prevent O5 from getting clean post position.
- **X4**: Bump O4 immediately on the pin-down to slow the screen on X2; this may cause O2 to commit a 3-second violation. X4 must then recover to O4 on the right-side block.
- **X3**: Defend ball (O3 at free-throw line extended); be alert to all three passing options.
- **X1**: After O1's movement to the right-side free-throw line extended, maintain position to prevent backside skip passes.

## Formation
- O1: Top of key/middle → moves to right-side free-throw line extended after pass
- O2: Left block → executes cross screen for O5
- O5: Right block → relocates to left block off O2's cross screen
- O4: Middle of free-throw lane → sets pin-down on X2 as O5 crosses
- O3: Left side, free-throw line extended (ball)

## Common Mistakes
1. **X4 ignores O4's pin-down on X2** → X4 must bump O4 immediately; this is the "big issue" that breaks this defense.
2. **Forgetting the third option (ball reversal to O1)** → X1 must maintain awareness even after O1 relocates.
3. **No communication between X2 and X4** → Without calling out O4's pin-down, X2 has no chance to fight through it.
4. **Switching too late** → If a switch is needed, it must be called before the screen is set, not after.

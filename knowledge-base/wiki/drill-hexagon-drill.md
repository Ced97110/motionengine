# Hexagon Drill (Hex-and-Dot Drill)

## Objective
Improve agility by jumping rapidly with both feet outside each side of a hexagon in sequence, both clockwise and counterclockwise, while being timed. [S8, p.129]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Tape, chalk, or cones to mark a hexagon on the floor — each side approximately 2 feet (61 cm) long
- Stopwatch for timing

<!-- DIAGRAM: A hexagon with 6 sides numbered 1-6 clockwise. Arrows show bilateral jumps outward from the center across each consecutive side. The athlete starts in the center facing a fixed direction throughout the entire drill. [S8, p.129] -->

## Execution
1. Create a hexagon with each side approximately 2 feet (61 cm) long — sides can vary.
2. Begin in the **middle of the hexagon** facing a predetermined fixed direction.
3. **Always facing that same direction**, jump with both feet outside each side of the hexagon in sequence (clockwise).
4. Return to the center after clearing each side, then immediately jump to the next side.
5. Complete the full circuit clockwise, then immediately repeat **counterclockwise**.
6. The entire drill is **timed** — record completion time for both directions. [S8, p.129]

## Coaching Points
- The athlete faces the same direction throughout — this is what makes the drill challenging on non-dominant sides
- Both feet must clear the line/side on each jump — no dragging feet
- Return to center fully between each jump — do not shortcut by hopping directly from side to side
- "Face forward, jump fast" — the core coaching cue
- Use as both a training drill AND a testing/assessment tool for agility

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow deliberate jumps, focus on footwork and direction
2. **Intermediate**: Timed full drill both directions
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Use single-leg hops instead of bilateral
   - Vary the size of the hexagon (smaller = faster footwork demand)
   [S8, p.129]

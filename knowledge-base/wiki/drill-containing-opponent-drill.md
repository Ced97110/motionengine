# Containing-Opponent Drill

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction and react to an opponent by attempting to break through a defensive wall of 3–4 players who backpedal and side-shuffle to contain the attacker. [S8, p.189]

## Setup
- 4–5 players (1 attacker + 3–4 defenders)
- Lines and cones over a distance of approximately **20 yards (18 meters) wide** by **80 yards (73 meters) long**
- Defenders start on the end line, facing the attacker

## Execution
1. The attacker stands on the end line.
2. Three or four defenders face the attacker in a **line formation** (acting as a wall).
3. The attacker tries to **sprint through the wall** of defenders.
4. The defenders **backpedal** and **move side-to-side** to contain the attacker and prevent them from breaking through.
5. The drill continues until the attacker gets through or reaches the end of the course. [S8, p.189]

## Coaching Points
- Defenders: maintain proper athletic stance — "stay low and connected to the attacker's hips"
- Defenders must communicate to shift the wall as a unit — one player cannot drift away from the group
- The attacker should use head fakes, hesitations, and change-of-direction moves to create gaps
- This drill simulates the attacker trying to find an open passing lane or driving gap against a defensive wall
- Rotate so every player takes a turn as the attacker

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 2 defenders, shorter distance (20 yards)
2. **Intermediate**: 3 defenders, full 80-yard length
3. **Advanced**: 4 defenders; attacker given a basketball to dribble while trying to beat the wall

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — the attacker must use deceleration and direction changes to beat defenders
- [[defensive-transition-principles]] — defenders practice reading and containing a single offensive player
- [[rate-of-force-development-basketball]] — explosive lateral movements by defenders require maximum RFD

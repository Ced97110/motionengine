# BLOB Stack Zone (Ball-Side Stack)

## Overview
A basic BLOB vs 2-3 zone play ideal for youth basketball. Players begin in a stack formation on the ball-side edge of the key. The action clears out two low zone defenders to create a gap for a quick midrange shot, with a corner 3 as a built-in counter. [S4, pp.17-18]

## Formation
1 (inbounder) is out of bounds on the sideline. 2, 5, 4, and 3 are stacked vertically on the ball-side edge of the key from top to bottom. 3 is positioned to drag toward the top of the key. [S4, p.17]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Clear the Low Defenders
- 2 immediately cuts hard to the corner, calling for the basketball to drag the low wing defender (x3) out with them.
- 3 clears out to the top of the key to occupy the top zone defender (x1) and provide a safety outlet.
- 5 steps toward the middle of the key and sets a screen on the middle zone defender (x5).

### Phase 2: Gap Cut and Primary Scoring Action
- With x3 dragged to the corner and x5 screened by 5, a gap opens in the lane.
- 4 steps into the gap and calls for the basketball from 1 for the open midrange shot.
- 1 must read x3: if x3 stays to cut off the pass to 4, pass to 2 in the corner for the catch-and-shoot three-pointer.

## Key Coaching Points
- Timing of screens and cuts is crucial — 4 must **wait for the gap to open** before stepping up and calling for the ball. [S4, p.18]
- 4 must have hands up and be ready to **catch and shoot immediately** — no time to take a dribble or lower the basketball. [S4, p.18]
- 2 must **call loudly** for the basketball while cutting to the corner to ensure x3 commits to following them. [S4, p.18]
- 1 reads x3: one defender is responsible for two offensive players — **someone will always be open**. [S4, p.18]

## Counters
- If x3 stays and denies the pass to 4, 2 is open in the corner for the catch-and-shoot three. [S4, p.17]

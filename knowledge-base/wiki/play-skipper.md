# Skipper

## Overview
A quick-hitter designed to get a team's best shooter an open three-point shot from the corner off a skip pass. A post screens the back-side of the low zone defender (X4) as the shooter slides to the corner, then receives a long skip pass for the shot. Best suited for high school teams and older due to the difficulty of the skip pass. [S4, p.46]

## Formation
1-3-1 set. 1 at top, 3 on the weak-side wing, 2 (best shooter) on the ball-side wing, 4 at the high post, 5 as the low post player — positioned on the **same side as the best shooter** (2). [S4, p.46]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Wing Entry
- 1 passes to 3 on the wing (opposite side from the shooter, 2).
- The ball is now on the weak side, drawing zone attention.

### Phase 2: Screen and Cut
- 5 sets a screen on the **back side** of X4 (the low ball-side zone defender).
- 2 slides down from the wing to the corner, using 5's screen to get open behind the three-point line.
- 5 must **not set the screen until the pass is about to be made** — setting it too early telegraphs the action and allows X4 to fight through before 2 arrives. [S4, p.46]

### Phase 3: Skip Pass for Shot
- 3 makes a strong skip pass to 2 in the corner for the open three-point shot.
- To prevent the pass from being tipped, 3 should use an **overhead pass**. [S4, p.46]

## Key Coaching Points
- 5 must time the screen precisely — set too early and X4 defeats the action; set too late and 2 is already in the corner without a screen. [S4, p.46]
- "Use an overhead pass" for the skip pass to ensure it clears the zone defenders and isn't tipped. [S4, p.46]
- Practice the skip pass timing and 2's catch-and-shoot mechanics repeatedly.
- This play may be too advanced for young teams due to the long skip pass requirement. [S4, p.46]

## Counters
- If X4 fights over 5's screen, 2 can curl slightly to the baseline instead of the corner.
- If the skip pass lane is denied, 3 can look to 1 at the top for a reset.

# Thoracic Spine Mobility

## Objective
Restore flexibility and rotational mobility in the thoracic spine (upper back) to increase shoulder range of motion for shooting and defending.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: none for beginner; a stick or broomstick for advanced variation
- Floor space needed

## Execution
1. Assume a quadruped position (on all fours — hands and knees on the floor).
2. Lift one arm and place the hand behind the head.
3. Rotate toward the arm behind the head, feeling the stretch in the upper back.
4. Hold for 3 to 5 seconds. Perform 30 repetitions on each side.
5. **Advanced variation:** Stand and hold a stick behind your back. Slightly bend both knees and flex the hips to approximately 75 degrees. Rotate from side to side.

## Coaching Points
- The rotation should come from the thoracic (upper) spine, not the lower back or hips
- Keep the base stable — hips stay level in quadruped; knees bent and hips hinged in standing version
- "Feel the stretch in the upper back toward the arm" — cue athletes to rotate fully, not just lift the elbow

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Quadruped rotation, 3–5 second holds × 30 reps each side
2. **Advanced:** Standing stick rotation, hips at 75-degree flexion

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Spinalis, longissimus, external oblique, internal oblique

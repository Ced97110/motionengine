# Inverted Row (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop posterior shoulder and upper-back strength — particularly latissimus dorsi and posterior deltoid — to improve long-range shooting ability, defensive physicality, and straight-body control.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Squat rack with safety supports set so that when lying on the floor the arms are fully extended reaching the bar
- Barbell safely secured across the supports
- Alternatively: TRX suspension straps or blast straps

## Execution
1. Lie on the floor beneath the bar. Adjust the rack's safety support height so arms are fully extended when gripping the bar from the floor.
2. Grip the bar in an **overhand grip** (palms facing forward), arms and legs fully extended.
3. Pull the body up toward the bar, making sure **hips and trunk rise simultaneously** — maintain a rigid plank.
4. End the pull with the **chest at the bar**; keep elbows close to the body throughout.
5. Lower back to the ground in a controlled manner, keeping hips and trunk in a straight line, until arms are fully extended.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Hips up — your body is a plank from ankles to shoulders."
- Do not let the hips sag during the pull or the descent.
- Keep elbows tracked close to the ribs — flared elbows reduce lat engagement.
- Unlike the pull-up, this exercise places more emphasis on the **posterior deltoid**.
- "Chest to bar — full range of motion every rep."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Tabletop Position — bend knees to 90 degrees with feet flat on the floor to reduce the load; perform prescribed reps.
2. **Intermediate**: Standard inverted row with legs fully extended.
3. **Advanced**: TRX or Blast Straps — each arm works independently, significantly increasing stabilization demand and difficulty.

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Latissimus dorsi, biceps brachii, brachialis, brachioradialis, posterior deltoid
- **Secondary**: Rhomboid major, rhomboid minor, teres major, teres minor, infraspinatus, external oblique, trapezius

## Basketball Focus
The inverted row builds a strong back with an emphasis on the **posterior deltoids**, making it directly applicable to:
- **Long-range shooting ability** — posterior shoulder strength supports the full shooting arm extension and follow-through
- **Defending opponents** — back and shoulder strength improves the ability to hold positioning under physical pressure
- **Body control** — the straight-body requirement transfers to on-court athletic positioning

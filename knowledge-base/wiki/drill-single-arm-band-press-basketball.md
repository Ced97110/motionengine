# Single-Arm Band Press (Basketball Strength Exercise)

## Objective
Develop unilateral pressing strength in the pectoralis major, pectoralis minor, and anterior deltoid — particularly on the weaker nondominant side — using an exercise band that can be performed anywhere without gym access.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Exercise resistance band (adequate resistance)
- Secure anchor point (post, rack, door frame)
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Attach an exercise band securely to an anchor at approximately shoulder height. Loop the band so it fits in the palm of the right hand.
2. Turn 180 degrees to face away from the anchor. Walk forward until the desired tension is achieved in the band.
3. Assume a staggered stance with the left foot in front of the right foot.
4. Flex the right elbow to 90 degrees, keeping the arm close to the body at shoulder height.
5. Maintaining a solid, erect posture and the staggered stance, extend the elbow and shoulder straight forward until the arm is fully extended — simulating a pressing/passing motion. Keep the band at shoulder height and control throughout.
6. In a slow, controlled manner, return the arm to the starting (90-degree elbow) position.
7. Repeat with the band in the left hand and right foot forward in the staggered stance.
8. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions on each side.

## Coaching Points
- Lead leg is opposite to pressing arm (left foot forward when pressing right arm) — mimics throwing/passing mechanics
- "Push straight ahead" — no lateral drift; simulate the line of a chest pass
- Keep the torso stable throughout — the staggered stance engages the core stabilizers
- Focus on the nondominant arm — this exercise addresses bilateral imbalances that are common in basketball players
- Control the band on the return — avoid letting it snap back

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Lighter band with full range of motion focus
2. **Intermediate**: Standard band press as described; both sides equally
3. **Advanced**: Increase band resistance; add a slight hip rotation to simulate driving-past-defender mechanics

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Pectoralis major, pectoralis minor
- **Secondary**: Anterior deltoid, triceps brachii

## Basketball Transfer
Ideal for players without gym access. The unilateral action directly addresses nondominant-arm weakness, which affects dribbling and passing. The staggered stance activates the core stabilizers in the same way the body is loaded when driving to the basket against a defender. [S2, pp.80-81]

# Kip-Up Small Space Drills

## Objective
Three equipment-free solo drills that develop the shoulder/arm conditioning, hip-snap, and floor-to-standing mechanics of the kip-up chain — usable as warm-ups, finishing exercises, or variety work.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Minimal space required
- Equipment: wall (for bridge push-offs); none otherwise
- Can be performed rhythmically for higher reps
- Work well as warm-ups or finishing exercises in any explosives session [S9, p.18]

## Execution

### Drill A: Bridge Push-Offs
1. Stand a short distance from a sturdy wall, facing AWAY from it.
2. Bend backward and place hands on the wall, bending arms as much as possible.
3. Once arms are well bent, **push away from the wall fast enough to end up standing**.
4. Conditions shoulder and arm muscles for the kip-up hand position.
5. **Too easy?** Move further from the wall, or try using just one hand. [S9, p.18]

### Drill B: Sitting Kips
1. Kneel down on shins and insteps (Japanese-style). Easier variation: on toes.
2. **Throw arms and torso upward with a snap**, using the small amount of height gained to whip legs out from underneath.
3. Finish in a standing position.
4. A "great example of a speed drill" that imparts the **hip-snap** needed for real kips.
5. Perform a few reps whenever feeling like speed is slowing down. [S9, p.18]

### Drill C: Prone Kip
1. **Lay face down** with knees bent, body and palms (or fists) supporting you.
2. Push down hard through hands to gain enough height to **whip legs in underneath** you.
3. Finish standing.
4. Can be done from palms or fists. [S9, p.19]

## Coaching Points
- These drills are ancillary — they supplement the chain, not replace it
- Bridge push-offs directly condition the arm/shoulder position required for Steps 4–7
- Sitting kips are the most sport-applicable of the three: the kneeling-to-standing hip snap mirrors the movement needed in loose-ball scramble situations
- Prone kip trains kip-up mechanics from a face-down position, simulating recovery from a fall in competition
- All three can be performed rhythmically for multiple reps

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bridge push-offs (standing close to wall, both hands)
2. **Intermediate**: Bridge push-offs (further from wall), sitting kips from toes
3. **Advanced**: Bridge push-offs (one hand), sitting kips from shins/insteps, prone kip from fists → palms

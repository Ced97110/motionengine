# Straight-Leg Kip-Up (Step 8 of Kip-Up Chain)

## Objective
Perform the kip-up with legs kept STRAIGHT during the roll-back and hip-extension phase, dramatically increasing hip/waist demand and removing thigh/glute assistance.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat open floor or mat
- No equipment needed
- Prerequisite: master [[drill-kip-up]] (Step 7)

## Execution
1. **Sit on the floor with legs STRAIGHT** (not bent).
2. Roll back and swing legs up, **keeping them straight**, while placing hands in the bridge position.
3. Roll onto upper-back and shoulders. Swing straight legs over head — knees should come close to the head even with legs straight.
4. **Explode legs up** by straightening at the hips while simultaneously pushing down through palms hard enough to lift them off the ground.
5. At take-off, **bend the legs and whip them underneath** as you continue the forward motion begun by the torso and arms.
6. As feet touch down, keep torso and hands forward.
7. **Land on both feet** into a squatting position. Stand upright.
8. Squat down, roll back, and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- "With straight legs, the hips and waist are forced to do the work, making the drill much tougher" [S9, p.10]
- The leg bend during straight-leg variation happens AFTER take-off — not before
- Regression is continuous: just gradually reduce the knee-bend from Step 7 over time
- "The greater the knee-bend, the easier the kip-up" — use this as a tunable dial
- Starting from flat on the floor (not rolling) adds further difficulty for all steps

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (regression)**: Begin Step 7 (standard kip-up) and gradually reduce knee-bend
2. **Intermediate**: Full straight-leg kip-up from seated roll
3. **Advanced**: Begin lying flat on floor instead of seated — eliminates all rolling momentum [S9, p.11]

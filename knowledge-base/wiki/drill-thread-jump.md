# Thread Jump (Step 9)

## Objective
Develop unilateral explosive jumping and tuck mechanics by threading the jumping foot through a loop formed by the opposite hand gripping the other foot — a safe progression step toward the suicide jump.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any open space (no equipment required)
- Optional: a towel or belt slung around the ankle as a larger loop for beginners

## Execution
1. Grip the end of one foot with the *opposite* hand, forming a loop. Keep the standing leg straight and the trunk as upright as possible. [S9]
2. Dip slightly with the loaded (standing) leg and explode upward, tucking the jumping knee as high and close to the chest as possible while staying upright.
3. At the peak of leg movement, whip the linked hand and foot *under* the jumping foot so the loop ends up behind you. Key coaching cue: **lift the jumping leg up, then pull the hole back** — do not try to jump the foot through the hole [S9].
4. After the thread, throw the leg back down for landing.
5. Upon landing, catch most weight on the ball of the foot.
6. Reposition and repeat, or immediately perform a *reverse thread jump* (starting with hand/foot behind the jumping leg and pulling forward).

## Coaching Points
- "Lift your jumping leg up, and then pull the hole back" — the most common mistake is trying to jump the foot through the loop [S9]
- Begin with a loose grip around the toes so you can bail quickly if needed [S9]
- The term *thread* comes from breakdancing — thread the foot through the gap like threading a needle
- If you lose the movement, simply release the foot for a safe landing
- A towel or belt around the ankle creates a larger, more forgiving loop for learners [S9]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Loose grip at the end of toes; towel/belt loop if needed [S9]
2. **Intermediate**: Standard — grip end of toes with opposite hand
3. **Advanced**: Grip the outside of the foot (smaller loop); then attempt the *reverse thread jump* (hand/foot starting behind the jumping leg, pulled forward) [S9]

# Show-and-Go Closeout 1-on-1-on-1 Drill

## Objective
Develop the ability to read a defender's closeout direction and execute the correct strong-side or weak-side show-and-go move in a competitive, game-speed environment.

## Setup
- Groups of 3-4 players per basket
- Half court; start from wing or shooting spots around the perimeter
- No extra equipment needed
- One offensive player, one defensive player; remaining players waiting

## Execution
1. **Phase 1 — Stationary reads**: Offensive player receives a pass; defender closes out either to the left or to the right. Offensive player reads the direction and executes the show-and-go to the opposite side — "if the defender closes out left, show and go right and vice versa." [S5, p.102]
2. **Phase 2 — Competitive 1-on-1**: If the offensive player scores, they stay on offense and get a new defender. If the defender gets a stop, they rotate to offense.
3. **Phase 3 — 1-on-1-on-1**: With 3-4 players, each player tracks their own score. After each basket, the scorer gets a new defender; the stopped offensive player goes to the end of the line and a new defender steps in.
4. **Duration**: Play for 5 minutes. The player with the most baskets at the end wins.

## Coaching Points
- Watch for players going the same direction as the defender's closeout — correct immediately
- Remind players to stay low on the show; a half-hearted fake will not freeze the defender
- Insist on the ball being ripped to the hip before the dribble — no palming or carrying
- Cue: **"Read the closeout, attack the gap"**
- This drill is specifically designed to develop a **scoring mindset** through competition [S5, p.102]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Partner drill — passer throws, closes out; offensive player shoots the show-and-go with no defense pressure; work from seven spots
2. **Intermediate**: Add a live defender who must close out; offensive player reads and goes (as described in execution)
3. **Advanced**: Full 1-on-1-on-1 competitive format with score tracking; add consequences (e.g., points deducted for incorrect reads — going same direction as the closeout)

# Post Defensive Coverage Types

## Summary
Post defenders use different coverage positions depending on where the ball is on the court, the offensive player's tendencies, and the team's defensive scheme. Understanding each type — from behind to full fronting — is essential for both defenders learning to execute and post attackers learning to counter. The most common coverage is the **two-step** (three-quarter position), but elite defenses shift coverage as the ball moves [S15, pp.205-210].

## When to Use
Post defenders select their coverage based on:
- Location of the ball (ball-side vs. weak side)
- Strength of the post player
- Team scouting plan
- Whether weak-side help is available for lob coverage

## Key Principles

1. **Playing Behind**: Defender is directly behind the post player — gives up the catch but fights for position after. Post attacker: catch in sitting position, hold defender at bay with buttocks, attack with post moves.

2. **Three-Quarter Position** (most common): Defender is diagonal — one foot above the post player's lead foot, one foot below. Cuts off the direct entry pass without fully fronting. The two-step mechanism adjusts this as the ball moves.

3. **Two-Step Defensive Coverage**: As the ball moves from one perimeter player to another, the defender picks up one foot (in front of the post player's body) then the second foot (slightly behind), arriving in a three-quarter or near-fronting position. Begins when the pass is released, completes on the catch [S15, p.206].

4. **One-Step Defensive Coverage**: As the ball moves, defender pulls only the near foot in front of the post player's body — shoulders parallel to the passing line. Creates a **full fronting position** (vs. three-quarter from two-step). Use: three-quarter when ball is on the weak side; front when ball is on the strong side [S15, p.207].

5. **Full Fronting**: Defender is entirely between the ball and the post player. Requires weak-side help to cover the lob. Offensive counter: overhead lob pass from perimeter (see Drill 98).

## Player Responsibilities
- **Post Defender (X5)**: Read ball position → trigger appropriate coverage step → battle for the dominant foot without using hands/arms.
- **Weak-Side Defender**: Provide lob coverage when full fronting is used.
- **Post Attacker (5)**: Read which coverage is being played → select the correct offensive counter.

## Variations

### Offensive Counters by Coverage Type

| Coverage | Post Attacker Counter |
|---|---|
| Behind | Sit low, hands out, attack with drop step or face-up |
| Three-quarter | Push defender farther out, then drop step or roll step |
| Two-step | Use the **roll step** — allow the move, reverse pivot 180° |
| One-step | Overhead lob from the pass-side perimeter player |
| Full fronting | Overhead lob, or push defender far outside and step-to-basket |

## Common Mistakes
1. **Two-step defender moves too early** (before the pass) → Correction: begin the step as the ball leaves the passer's hand.
2. **Full fronting without weak-side help** → Attacker gets a free lob for a layup. Correction: never front without communicating lob coverage to a teammate.
3. **Post attacker fighting the two-step instead of rolling** → Wasted energy, poor results. Correction: allow the defender to commit, then execute the roll step reverse pivot [S15, p.208].

# Mass Footwork Rotation Drill

## Objective
Develop all five players' offensive footwork simultaneously — perimeter passer/driver AND post receiver — through timed rotation that covers all four positional roles in a single practice block. [S6, p.266]

## Setup
- **Players**: 12 (an entire team) divided into three 4-man groups
- **Court**: Half court (3 groups can use different sections of the court if space allows, or run the drill in 4-minute waves using one basket)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball per group
- **Configuration per group**: 1 ball handler (perimeter), 1 offensive screener, 1 offensive cutter/post player, 1 dummy defender (on ball handler or pass receiver)
- **Coach**: places limitations on dribbles allowed and post scoring options

## Execution
1. Coach assigns each group of 4 to positions: ball handler, screener, cutter/post, dummy defender
2. Set a timer for **55 seconds** — all three groups execute the specific offensive footwork simultaneously
3. Ball handler and post player work on the footwork of receiving and attacking; screener practices setting and slipping screens; dummy defender provides token resistance
4. Coaches observe with "a keen eye to detail" and critique in "a serious but positive manner" [S6, p.265]
5. At the 55-second mark, all players have **5 seconds** to rotate to the next position in their group (ball handler → screener → cutter/post → defender → ball handler)
6. In **4 minutes**, all four spots are covered — every player has practiced all four phases
7. **Later in practice**, repeat the drill two or more times with each group **starting in a different location** on the floor
8. Over **12 total minutes**, each player practices offensive footwork as both a perimeter passer/feeder and as a post player receiving the ball and attacking the basket

## Coaching Points
- Observe foot placement, pivot foot identification, and body position on every catch
- Insist on **game speed** once the mechanics are established — "Every drill must integrate maximum game-realistic conditions" [S6, p.265]
- Correct silently or briefly — use the 5-second rotation window for quick feedback
- Cue post players: "Reverse pivot the moment you set the screen — don't watch the play"
- Cue perimeter players: "Hands ready and shoulders turning BEFORE the pass arrives"
- Limit dribbles (1 or 2 max) to force quick decision making

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Dummy defender plays passive (arms down, no movement) — offense focuses solely on footwork mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Dummy defender applies token pressure (arms up, contests without contesting hard) — offense must read and react
3. **Advanced**: Live defense on both ball handler and pass receiver; offense must execute correct footwork under maximum pressure at game speed

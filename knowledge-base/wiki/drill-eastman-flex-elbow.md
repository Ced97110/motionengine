# Eastman Flex Elbow Drill

## Objective
Develop precise cut-fake and high-elbow catch-and-shoot mechanics simulating the flex offense's perimeter action.

## Setup
- Half-court
- 1 coach at mid-half-court with two balls
- Player starts at the low-post spot

## Execution
1. Player fakes a cut into the lane (selling the defender on a basket cut)
2. Player comes high to the right elbow, receives the pass from the coach, and shoots a jump shot
3. Player immediately cuts back into the lane (fake again)
4. Player comes high to the left elbow, receives the second ball from the coach, and shoots
5. Run for a set number of shots or a set time
6. **Focus**: precision of every detail, from the quality of the cut fake to the footwork and release on the jump shot

## Coaching Points
- The cut fake must be convincing — plant and sell the basket cut before redirecting high
- Stay low coming out of the cut; rise into the shot naturally
- "Perfect feet" — identical footwork both times, both elbows
- Ball comes to the shoulder-chin area (right shoulder for right-handed shooter)
- Wrists of the shooting hand should be bent back on the catch
- Coach corrects every mistake — "this is a teaching drill"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary reception at the elbow, no cut fake
2. **Intermediate**: Full cut fake + elbow reception as described
3. **Advanced**: Add a live defender at the elbow who contests the shot

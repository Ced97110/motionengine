# NBA Shooting Drill

## Objective
Simulate a full NBA scoring possession (3PT attempt → rebound read → midrange or post finish → free throw) in a self-contained individual workout that builds decision-making, conditioning, and shooting from all three distances.

## Setup
- Players: 1 (individual drill)
- Court area: full basket area — three-point line, paint, and free-throw line
- Equipment: one basketball
- Duration: 2 minutes per round (5 possessions per half)

## Execution
1. Player shoots from **beyond the 3-point line** — if made, counts **3 points**
2. Whether made or missed, player retrieves the rebound:
   - **If rebounded out on the court** (long carom): player makes a move, takes ONE dribble, and shoots a jump shot — counts **2 points** if made
   - **If rebounded under the basket** (short carom): player executes a pump fake, pump fake crossover, or pump fake up-and-under for a close-range score — counts **2 points** if made
3. Player then goes to the **free-throw line** and shoots — counts **1 point** if made
4. Each player gets **5 possessions per half** = 5 three-point attempts + 5 short jumpers or layups + 5 free throws
5. Total possible points per half = 5×3 + 5×2 + 5×1 = **30 points per half** / **60 points per game**
6. **Goal**: score at least 50% of the time (30 points to win the game)
7. Track results week over week — raise the winning threshold progressively
8. Play a "regular NBA schedule" using a favorite team for ongoing motivation

## Coaching Points
- The 3-point attempt must come from beyond the line — no toe-on-the-line shots
- The rebound read is a real decision: long carom → midrange; short carom → post finish; teach players to read the ball path before it lands
- **One dribble only** on the midrange follow-up — this forces a decisive one-motion attack, not a dribble wander
- Pump fake technique: freeze the imaginary defender with a convincing fake before the crossover or up-and-under
- Track the score every session — the numbers provide accountability and motivation
- "From week to week, players keep adding to the number of points they have to score to win" — progressive difficulty is built into the drill design

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: standard 5-possession format; goal = 30 points
2. **Intermediate**: increase winning threshold by 5 points each week
3. **Advanced**: require a shot fake on the three-point attempt before shooting; add defensive pressure from a passive defender on the midrange follow-up

# Stride Stop (One-Two) Drill (Youth)

## Objective
Teach players the stride stop (one-two step) footwork that enables a quicker jump shot under defensive pressure by establishing the shooting foot as the back foot and the pivot foot as the front foot.

## Setup
- Players line up on the baseline in multiple vertical lines across the court
- Coach positions in front and backpedals to give visual commands
- Full court
- Basketballs added at intermediate level

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up on the baseline
2. They run downcourt at half-speed
3. When coach says "stride stop," players execute the stride stop
4. On "go," players sprint again
5. Coach may give visual command (sprint step toward team) in addition to or instead of voice command

**Option**: If players have trouble stride-stopping, have them sprint toward the coach and stride-stop on approach. Coach observes and corrects technique.

### Intermediate
- Give each player a ball; designate a dribbling move to execute
- At the end of the dribbling move, the player stride-stops
- Lines on the court serve as stop points; player dribbles to next line and repeats

### Advanced
1. Players execute a designated dribbling move then a stride stop, then pivot (reverse or front)
2. Sequence: dribble back to start, execute dribbling move + stride stop + pivot, progressing down the floor: free-throw line extended → half-court → far free-throw line extended → far endline
3. Same as above but alternate dribbling moves (in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin) and alternate front/reverse pivots

## Coaching Points
1. **Lower center of gravity first**: Greater speed = lower buttocks; "sit in a chair"
   - Exception: if stride-stopping for a jump shot, players may slow slightly before the stop — "Players will know this spot, but defenders will not"
2. **Footwork sequence**: Sprint to the stop spot → place left foot down heel-to-toe → swing right foot forward and land on the toe → left foot is the pivot foot → stopped in one-two step fashion
3. **Back position**: Straight, maybe slightly forward; torso bent at waist; more speed = more bend
4. **Knee angle**: Bent at least 90 degrees but never more than 150 degrees; player should be on toes of both feet, ready to spring into the air for a jump shot
5. **Freeze the pivot foot**: "When the stop is completed, the player must not move the left foot"
6. **Shooting advantage**: "The stride stop allows shooters a quicker shot" — use this stop to shoot a jump shot under defensive pressure

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Running at half-speed with coach visual/verbal command; no ball
2. **Intermediate**: Dribbling move into stride stop on court lines, with ball
3. **Advanced**: Dribbling move + stride stop + front or reverse pivot in full-court sequence

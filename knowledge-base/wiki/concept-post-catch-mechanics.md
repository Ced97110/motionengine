# Post Catch Mechanics

## Summary
Before executing any post move, an offensive post player must master the catch itself. The moment a post player receives an inside pass, a precise sequence of mechanics — a simultaneous two-foot hop, chinning the ball, and a shoulder-turn read — determines which foot becomes the pivot foot, protects the ball, and allows the player to read the defense before making a scoring decision.

These mechanics are universal: they apply whether the player has flashed to the ball, duck-in cut, spun off a screen, or was stationary on the block. Every post move described in the S6 system (drop-step, Olajuwon whirl, front outside pivot, reverse-pivot post move) begins with these exact same catch mechanics. [S6, pp.186-187]

## When to Use
- Every time a post player receives an inside pass in any post scoring location
- After any form of getting open (V-cut, duck-in, spin move, swim move, flash cut)
- When stationary on the block waiting for the ball

## Key Principles
1. **Two-foot hop simultaneous with the catch** — the post player takes a small hop and lands on both feet at the exact same instant the ball arrives in his hands. The hop must happen *as* the ball is caught, not before or after, to avoid a traveling violation. [S6, pp.186-187]
2. **Simultaneous landing = free pivot choice** — landing on both feet at the same time gives the post player the legal freedom to choose *either* foot as the pivot foot, and therefore the opposite foot as the free foot. This maximizes offensive options. [S6, p.186]
3. **Chin the ball immediately** — both hands grip the ball and pull it to the chin, elbows extended outward. This protects the ball from deflections and reaching defenders. *"The post player chins the ball to protect the ball from defenders reaching and deflecting the ball away."* [S6, p.186]
4. **Elbows out** — keep elbows extended to push defenders farther away from the ball. [S6, p.186]
5. **Snap the head to look over the high shoulder** — the "high shoulder" is the shoulder away from the basket (toward the perimeter/top of the key side). This read serves two simultaneous purposes: [S6, pp.187-188]
   - Searching for collapsing double-down defenders rotating from the defensive perimeter
   - Reading the individual post defender's position (high side vs. low/baseline side)
6. **Read the double-down first** — if a double-down defender is collapsing, kick the ball out to the open perimeter teammate for a probable open shot. This discipline forces defenses to stop doubling, which in turn gives the post player more isolated one-on-one opportunities. [S6, p.187]
7. **Read the post defender's position** — after verifying no double-down:
   - If defender is on the **high side** → show-and-go-opposite **toward the middle** (drop-step or Olajuwon toward baseline)
   - If defender is on the **low/baseline side** → show-and-go-opposite **toward the middle** with the higher foot as the free foot (drop-step middle)
   - If defender **cannot be seen/felt** (neither fronting nor making contact) → use a front outside pivot or reverse-pivot post move to square up and locate the defender [S6, pp.187-188]
8. **Do not dribble unless advancing to the basket** — the dribble is a premium. The post player should not use the dribble until he has made his reads and is ready to attack. [S6, p.186]
9. **Stagger feet if stationary** — if catching the ball without moving, the post player staggers feet so the contact foot (closest to the defender) is farther forward to help seal off the defender before the hop. [S6, p.186]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF/C (post player):** Execute the two-foot hop simultaneously with the catch. Chin the ball. Elbows out. Snap head to look over the high shoulder. Read double-down defenders. Read post defender's position. Then initiate the appropriate post move. [S6, pp.186-188]
- **Perimeter players:** Occupy defensive attention on the perimeter and be ready to receive a kick-out pass if the post player sees a double-down. Recognize the kick-out opportunity and shoot or attack immediately. [S6, p.187]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Hop does not land simultaneously with the catch** → traveling violation. Fix: practice catch-and-hop timing until they are synchronized.
2. **Ball not chinned immediately** → vulnerable to reaching defenders stripping the ball. Fix: form habit of bringing ball to chin on every catch in every drill.
3. **Elbows in instead of out** → defenders can crowd the post player. Fix: lock elbows outward as part of the chin motion.
4. **Head does not snap over the high shoulder** → post player is blind to double-down defenders and cannot read individual defender. Fix: make the shoulder-snap an automatic reflex on every single catch.
5. **Dribbling before reading** → wastes the dribble, limits post move options. Fix: read first, dribble only when attacking.
6. **Two separate foot landings** → establishes pivot foot too early, limits post move options. Fix: train the simultaneous two-foot landing as an isolated skill before adding moves.

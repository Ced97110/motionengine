# Partner Shooting (Midrange)

## Objective
Develop catch-and-shoot midrange jump shot in a two-player setting, reinforcing calling for the ball, one-two step footwork, balanced landing, and frozen follow-through while also training the passer to deliver game-quality passes.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2 (can scale to full team using multiple baskets)
- **Court area**: Half court, midrange shooting spots (7 spots around the basket)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball per pair
- **Roles**: Shooter and passer/rebounder

## Execution
1. The shooter starts in a balanced athletic stance at a designated midrange spot.
2. The passer stands under the basket holding the ball.
3. The shooter calls for the ball (verbal demand, eye contact, hand target).
4. The passer delivers the ball to the shooter's shot pocket.
5. The shooter takes a one-two step to meet the ball, catches it in the shot pocket, sights the target, and shoots.
6. The shooter strives to **catch in a balanced position** and **land in a balanced position** while freezing the follow-through.
7. The passer rebounds — whether make or miss — and resets under the basket.
8. The shooter completes repetitions from all 7 spots (going for a set number of makes, or shooting strings style).
9. Players follow up misses with a **weak-handed layup** to add conditioning and develop the weak hand.
10. Players alternate shooter and passer/rebounder roles.

## Coaching Points
- The shooter must **call for the ball** every single rep — verbal demand, eye contact, and hand target [S5, p.61].
- The passer must deliver **good passes** to the shot pocket. "If you never practice throwing good passes in practice, you will never throw good passes in games" [S5, p.65].
- Watch for high-low-high catching pattern (catching high, bringing ball down, then rising) — the shot pocket catch must be consistent from reception through release.
- Enforce frozen follow-through — coach watches that both hands hold position until the ball is through the net.
- Landing balanced reinforces shot discipline and readiness to "shoot and stay."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Passer feeds from directly under the basket (simulates an ideal, straight-on pass). Focus on technique over makes.
2. **Intermediate**: Set a makes goal from each spot before advancing (e.g., 5 makes). Add strings-style competition.
3. **Advanced**: Add faking elements before each catch (shot fake, jab step); passer feeds at game speed from various angles to simulate passes coming from the wing or elbow rather than directly under the basket [S5, pp.65-66].

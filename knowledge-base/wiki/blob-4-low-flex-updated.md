# BLOB 4-Low Flex

## Overview
From a 4-low formation, the ball is inbounded to a player cutting to the top of the key. The play flows into a flex screen for a post layup, then a screen-the-screener (pin down) action freeing the best shooter (the inbounder) at the top of the key for a catch-and-shoot. [S4, p.20]

## Formation
4-low formation with all four field players below the free-throw line. Bigs (4 and 5) are on the ball-side. Best shooter (2) is the inbounder. Best post player (5) starts outside the three-point line on ball-side. [S4, p.20]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Inbound and Ball Movement
- 4 cuts to the top of the key and receives the inbounds lob pass from 2.
- 3 waits an extra second, then cuts to the top of the key and receives the pass from 4.
- The delay by 3 is critical — cutting too early tips off the defense.

### Phase 2: Flex Screen for Post Layup
- 2 (now stepped inbounds) sets a flex screen for 5.
- 5 flex cuts toward the ball-side low block, looking for the pass from 3 and an open layup.
- This is the primary scoring option.

### Phase 3: Screen-the-Screener for Shooter
- After 2 sets the flex screen, 4 sets a pin down (down screen) for 2.
- 2 cuts to the top of the key, looking for the catch-and-shoot off a pass from 3.
- This screen-the-screener action is the secondary scoring option.

## Key Coaching Points
- 3 must make **smart decisions** with the basketball — hit the open player at the right time and with an accurate pass. [S4, p.20]
- **Every player must set strong screens** — weak screens allow defenders to fight through and the play breaks down. [S4, p.20]
- The delay on 3's cut to the top of the key is critical; cutting too early will tip off the defense.
- Best shooter (2) inbounds so they can become a shooting option after stepping inbounds.

## Counters
- If the flex cut for 5 is denied, look immediately to 2 coming off the pin down at the top of the key.

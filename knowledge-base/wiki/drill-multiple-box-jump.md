# Multiple-Box Jump

## Objective
Develop quicker floor reaction time and total lower-body explosiveness to jump higher for rebounds and jump shots, and to improve court quickness and change of direction.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Row of 2–4 plyometric boxes, each 12–30 inches (30–75 cm) height — select based on strength and athletic ability
- Boxes spaced to allow a clean landing-and-jump sequence between each
- Player starts 1–2 feet (30–60 cm) in front of the first box, feet shoulder-width apart

## Execution
1. Stand erect facing the row of boxes. Position feet approximately shoulder-width apart, 1–2 feet from the first box.
2. Flex the knees, hips, and trunk (countermovement), then immediately jump onto the first box by quickly extending the entire body while throwing the arms upward.
3. Land softly on the box, giving way by flexing the knees, hips, and trunk to reduce impact forces.
4. Upon landing on the first box, immediately jump off toward the floor.
5. As the feet make floor contact, immediately jump onto the next box.
6. Continue this floor-to-box-to-floor pattern for the prescribed number of boxes.

> **Key point:** The "off the floor" phase between boxes must be as explosive and reactive as possible — minimize time on the ground between boxes. [S2, pp.114-115]

## Coaching Points
- **"Quick feet, soft landing"** — absorb on top of the box, explode off immediately
- Arm drive is essential on each takeoff — "throw the arms"
- Keep the trunk upright; avoid excessive forward lean
- Monitor for valgus knee collapse on box landings
- Begin with 2 boxes and lower heights; add boxes and height progressively

## Primary Muscles
- **At takeoff:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medius)
- **At landing:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus [S2, p.115]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** 2 boxes at 12 in. (30 cm); slow, deliberate jumps focusing on form
2. **Intermediate:** 3–4 boxes at 18 in. (45 cm); focus on reactive floor contacts
3. **Advanced:** 4 boxes at 24–30 in. (60–75 cm); maximum speed, minimum contact time

## Variations
### Long Jump
Simulate the box jump pattern but on the floor, jumping for maximum distance on each rep. Perform 3–4 long jumps, focusing on quick floor reaction. Good when boxes are unavailable. [S2, p.115]

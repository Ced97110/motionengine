# Jump to the Ball Drill (#146)

## Objective
Train all defenders to immediately reposition — "jump to the ball" — on every pass, establishing correct on-ball and off-ball defensive stances simultaneously.

## Setup
- Half court, two lines of players at the top of the key area
- 2 offensive players (1 and 2) with the ball, 2 defenders (X1 and X2)
- Multiple pairs: next players in line rotate in after each sequence
- Rotation: offense → defense → end of line; next player in each line becomes the new attacker

<!-- DIAGRAM: Players 1 and 2 face each other at the top of the key. X1 guards player 1, X2 guards player 2. On each pass, both defenders jump (slide step) toward the new ball location. -->

## Execution
1. Line players up as shown. Rotation from offense to defense to end of line; next player in each line moves out front to become the new attacker.
2. Player 1 passes to player 2 → **X1 and X2 both jump to the ball** (quick slide step or two toward ball, toes grabbing the floor — NOT a literal jump off the ground). [S15, p.307]
3. Player 2 passes back to player 1 → **X2 and X1 again jump to the ball**.
4. Continue for several passes before rotation.

### Teaching Points
1. The player on the ball uses **front-foot-to-pivot-foot, front-foot-to-free-foot, or parallel** stance.
2. The off-ball player plays in the **interception stance**.
3. Defenders move **while the pass is in the air** — they do not react to fake passes.
4. "Jump to the ball" = a quick slide step or two with toes grabbing the floor. [S15, p.307]

## Coaching Points
- Defenders must move on the FLIGHT of the pass, not after the catch.
- Fake passes: defenders do NOT react — only move on real passes.
- The off-ball defender must see both the ball and their assignment simultaneously.
- "Two-thirds of the distance from the ball, one-third of the distance from their assignment" — and one step off the line between assignment and ball ("ball-you-man"). [S15, p.303]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner** (base drill): Simple pass-and-react between two players; both defenders jump to every pass.
2. **Option 1 — Fakes**: Ball handler does a jab step and jab-step crossover before passing. Defenders react with the appropriate technique (retreat step, swing step) then recover when the pass is actually thrown.
3. **Option 2 — Live backdoor**: After several passes, coach makes the drill live — the weak-side attacker can middle-cut or backdoor cut into strong-side wing position. Defender on the wing plays denial. Wing V-cuts to get open. Then they play 1-on-1.
4. **Option 3 — Help side + rotation**: After several passes, coach sends the non-pass-receiver on a cut to the opposite corner, creating a help-side defender who must use flat-triangle/ball-you-man principles. Coach can allow: (a) ball handler to drive → activates full rotation drill, or (b) skip pass to corner → help-side defender must close out on the pass receiver. [S15, p.306]

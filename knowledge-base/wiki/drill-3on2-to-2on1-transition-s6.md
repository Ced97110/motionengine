# 3-on-2 to 2-on-1 Transition Drill (S6 Version)

## Objective
Train the basket man / ball man defensive transition roles and the 3v2 defensive technique while simultaneously developing 3-man fast-break execution and offensive rebounding / boxing-out fundamentals — all within a continuous, efficient drill structure.

## Setup
- **Players**: 5 active players (3 offense, 2 defense) + rotating groups waiting
- **Court area**: Full court
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball
- **Starting formation (Method 1)**: 2 defenders (X1, X2) seated at the **sideline hash mark** on the offensive side; 3 offensive players (01, 02, 03) on the offensive **baseline** [S6, p.97]
- **Additional players**: dummy group (X3, X4, X5) in a 3-on-3 starting alignment on the defensive end for Method 2

## Execution

### Method 1: Seated Start
1. Three offensive players (01, 02, 03) begin on the baseline in three wide lanes.
2. Two defenders (X1, X2) are literally seated at the offensive sideline hash mark.
3. Ball is advanced via dribbling and/or passing in three wide lanes.
4. Defenders scramble to their feet and sprint back to defend their basket.
5. First defender back: runs to the middle of the free throw lane, calls **"Basket!"**
6. Second defender back: settles in the lane and approaches the dribbler up to the top of the key, calls **"Ball!"**
7. 3v2 fast break is played live to a finish (score or defensive stop).

### Method 2: 3-on-3 With Rebound Trigger
1. Three offensive players (01, 02, 03) guard three dummy players (X3, X4, X5) in a defensive stance.
2. Two original offensive players (X1, X2) make 1-2 passes then shoot and intentionally miss — triggering a defensive rebound.
3. Defenders 01, 02, 03 (now becoming offense): box out, secure the rebound, and run the 3-man fast break in three wide lanes.
4. X1 and X2 sprint back as fast as possible, loudly communicating basket/ball assignments.
5. Dummy players (X3, X4, X5) step off court and rotate into the 01/02/03 roles next. [S6, pp.98-99]

### 3v2 Defensive Execution
1. **Ball man (X1)**: Stops dribble penetration — approaches dribbler no further than the **top of the key**; does not overextend.
2. **Basket man (X2)**: Protects the low blocks; takes both the **first and second perimeter passes** from the dribbler.
3. **Wing pass read**: When offense passes to either wing, basket man (X2) rotates out to defend the ball; ball man (X1) immediately drops to protect the basket. Roles swap.
4. Both defenders must buy time for recovering teammates without fouling.

### After the 3v2 Concludes
- If defense gets the stop → they become the new offense (2-on-1 heading the other way, or next group rotates in).
- If offense scores → they transition or rotate based on coach's rules.
- Cycle continues continuously.

## Coaching Points
- **"Basket! Ball! — both players must call their role every single time."**
- Ball man: "Don't go above the top of the key — you'll get beat on the skip pass."
- Basket man: "You own the first and second passes — don't abandon the paint until the wing catch happens."
- "The swap on the wing pass is the entire system — if you don't swap, you give up a wide-open layup."
- For the 3-man break: "Stay in your lane — don't collapse to the middle; space is the advantage."
- Watch for both defenders chasing the ball — immediately correct with "Who's got basket?"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk-through — defenders start standing; offense walks in three lanes. Stop after every swap and verify positions.
2. **Intermediate**: Method 1 (seated start); game-speed offense; defense communicates and executes swaps correctly.
3. **Advanced**: Method 2 (3-on-3 rebound trigger); add a 2-on-1 continuation after the 3v2 concludes (the two defenders who just played 3v2 now attack going the other direction as 2v1); continuous full-court.

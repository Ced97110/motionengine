# Eggs on the Rim Drill

## Objective
Perfect follow-through mechanics by increasing shot arch and ensuring the shooter targets the center of the basket — without hitting the rim.

## Setup
- 1 player per basket
- Any comfortable shooting range
- No equipment beyond a ball; the entire drill is visualization-based

## Execution
1. Player assumes a standard shooting stance with correct shooting-arm and balance-arm position.
2. Before shooting, player **visualizes eggs standing upright on the rim** — one at the front, one at the back, one on each side.
3. The goal of every shot: *"Don't break the eggs."* — the ball must clear the rim cleanly and swish through the center of the net.
4. Player shoots, focusing on achieving maximum arch so the ball passes cleanly through the interior of the basket without contacting the rim.
5. Shots that rattle in are tolerated (they still count) but the player is encouraged to keep chasing swishes.
6. Coach reinforces: *"Elbow above the eyebrow, hand in the hoop — that's what keeps you off the rim."*

## Coaching Points
- This drill works on two things simultaneously: (1) mechanical follow-through with elbow above eyebrow and hand-in-the-hoop position; (2) visual focus on the center of the basket [S5, p.42]
- Shots that rattle in are acceptable results but NOT the goal — the frustration of a rattle motivates shooters to improve arch
- Pair this drill with the [[drill-freeze-follow-through]] for a complete follow-through warm-up routine
- *"Pretend you are putting your hand in a cookie jar"* — coaching cue for the hand-in-the-hoop position [S5, p.41]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 5-8 feet from the basket; pure form focus with no defender.
2. **Intermediate**: Move to free-throw line distance; add the freeze-finish after each shot.
3. **Advanced**: Extend to three-point range at various spots; challenge player to reach a swish quota (e.g., 5 clean swishes before moving to the next spot).

# One-on-One Lane Drill (Drill 104)

## Objective
Develop intelligent one-on-one perimeter decision-making — teaching players not just **how** to make a move but **when** — within the spatial constraints of motion offense spacing (15-foot lane).

## Setup
- Individual drill — 2 players per group
- Multiple groups running simultaneously at different baskets or in different 15-foot lanes at the same basket
- **15-foot (4.5 m) lane** taped or imagined on the court
- Change lanes from day to day

## Execution
1. **Players line up** per the figure. Player 3 (offense) and X3 (defense) switch roles after a set time.
2. **Offensive player must stay inside the 15-foot lane** and must score within **3 dribbles**.
3. Offense **begins with a rocker step** and any dribble move.
4. **Defense tries to force the offense to change direction** as many times as possible within those 3 dribbles.
5. **Offense must create separation** from the defender to get their jump shot off.
6. Rotate roles; change the 15-foot lanes from day to day.

## Options
1. **Limit moves**: Restrict offensive player to one move (e.g., only the spin) or two moves to force mastery of specific skills.
2. **Front foot to pivot foot defense**: Instead of forcing direction changes, defenders play front foot to pivot foot and stay on the shooting shoulder.
3. **Defense earns rotation**: The defense must **stop the attacker twice in a row** before rotating to offense.
4. **Offense stays on**: Offensive player remains on offense as long as they create separation — whether they score or not.

## Coaching Points
- **15 feet is enough space** if technique is good and only 1–2 dribbles are used to create separation.
- Players learn to be **patient** — don't force a bad shot out of the one-on-one play.
- Defense wins by forcing **maximum direction changes** in 3 dribbles.
- "Create separation" — the goal is a quality shot, not a rushed one.
- This drill teaches players **when** to make a move (reading the defender) not just how.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No defense — player executes moves in the lane without a defender to build muscle memory.
2. **Intermediate**: Add passive defense — defender slides but doesn't reach.
3. **Advanced**: Full live defense; defense must stop twice before rotating; offense stays if successful.

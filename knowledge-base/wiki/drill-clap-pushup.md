# Clap Pushup (Power Pushup Step 4)

## Objective
Build explosive upper-body torso power and quick hands by generating enough airtime in a pushup position to clap hands together before landing.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor surface
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Squat down, place palms on floor, stretch legs out behind
2. Palms below shoulders at shoulder-width or wider
3. Feet at comfortable width (wider for beginners, closer for advanced)
4. Keep legs, hips, torso aligned and horizontal
5. Dip arms/shoulders ~1/3 depth toward floor
6. Immediately push explosively off floor, straightening arms
7. Explode upward as hard as possible; in mid-air, quickly clap hands together — clap must be **audible**
8. Return palms downward to "catch" the floor with hands again
9. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- The clap should be audible — not a "flutter" between fingertips [S9, p.91]
- "Avoid fluttering" — doing half a dozen rapid claps is a trick, not power; the hands look like they're fluttering rather than truly clapping, and the airtime required encourages worming [S9, p.91]
- Build toward a solid double, then triple clap before moving to body strikes
- Elevating feet increases intensity (more bodyweight through arms) — use a chair or box [S9, p.91]
- Clap pushups build great torso power, quick hands, and shoulder toughness — valued by boxers, martial artists, and football players [S9, p.90]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hands close together (less travel distance to clap); single clap
2. **Intermediate**: Hands at full shoulder-width; develop double clap
3. **Advanced**: Triple clap; feet elevated; advance to Step 5 (chest-strike pushup) — begin striking body with hands rather than adding more airtime claps

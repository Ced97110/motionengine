# Mirroring Partner Sprints

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction by continuously alternating between sprinting forward and backpedaling, mirroring a partner's movements over a 20-yard course. [S8, p.188]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Lines or cones marking a 20-yard (18-meter) course
- Start: partner stands 5 yards (4.6 meters) ahead of and facing the athlete

## Execution
1. Both athletes face each other at the start line, with the partner **5 yards ahead** and facing the athlete.
2. Athlete **sprints forward** for 10 yards (9 meters); partner simultaneously **backpedals** the same distance.
3. Athlete **stops and backpedals** as the partner **stops and sprints forward** 5 yards (4.6 meters).
4. Try to always **maintain the same distance** between each other throughout the drill.
5. Repeat the sprint/backpedal sequence until you reach the 20-yard (18-meter) line. [S8, p.188]

## Coaching Points
- Both athletes should be in constant communication visually — the follower mirrors the leader's transitions
- "Stop sharp — no drift" on the direction change; a drifting stop creates distance advantage for the partner
- Stay in athletic stance during the backpedal — don't stand tall
- Vary who leads (athlete vs. partner) on alternating sets
- This directly simulates the defensive transition moment when a player must switch from chasing to retreating

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk/jog version to establish the pattern and distance maintenance
2. **Intermediate**: Game-speed sprint-backpedal sequence as described
3. **Advanced**: Partner can randomly change direction mid-sequence; athlete must react and mirror — adds unpredictability

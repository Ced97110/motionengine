# E-Movement Cone Drill

## Objective
Improve multi-directional agility, shuffle endurance, and body-position transitions by moving through a complex E-shaped 6-cone pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 6 cones in an E-shape:
  - Cones 1 and 2: starting line, 10 yards apart (cone 2 is the start)
  - Cones 3 and 4: 5 yards in front of cones 1 and 2
  - Cones 5 and 6: 5 yards in front of cones 3 and 4

<!-- DIAGRAM: Three rows of two cones each (10 yards wide), stacked 5 yards apart. Player starts at cone 2 (right side of bottom row) and moves in E-pattern up and back. See [S8, p.106] -->

## Execution
1. Start at cone 2 (right, bottom row).
2. **Shuffle** from cone 2 to cone 1.
3. **Sprint** from cone 1 to cone 3 (middle row, left).
4. **Shuffle** from cone 3 to cone 4.
5. **Shuffle back** from cone 4 to cone 3.
6. **Sprint** from cone 3 to cone 5 (top row, left).
7. **Shuffle** from cone 5 to cone 6.
8. **Shuffle back** from cone 6 to cone 5.
9. **Backpedal** from cone 5 to cone 2.
10. **Shuffle** from cone 2 to cone 1.

## Coaching Points
- Shuffle segments demand defensive-stance discipline — no foot crossover
- Sprint transitions are critical: drive out of each shuffle with explosive first step
- Backpedal from top to middle row must be controlled and quick
- This drill has 9 movement segments — staying mentally organized is part of the training

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through all 9 segments, calling out each movement type.
2. **Intermediate**: Game speed; self-time each run.
3. **Advanced**: Reduce rest between reps; combine with an A-Movement set for a full circuit.

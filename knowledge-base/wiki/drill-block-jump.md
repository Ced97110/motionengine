# Block Jump (Power Jump Chain — Step 4)

## Objective
Master the "blocking" skill — converting horizontal running momentum into vertical jump height — which directly transfers to fast-break layup take-offs and is an essential prerequisite for advanced acrobatic movements.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Requires 10-15 feet of run-up space
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Take a short run-up — "a handful of steps is better than too many" [S9, p.18]
2. Swing arms up and take one big final step, then plant both feet together
3. Allow a slight knee bend as you dip, then immediately "punch" down through the floor with both feet
4. Swing arms downward simultaneously as feet punch the floor — this transfers momentum to push you up
5. Take off vertically, extending the full body as high as possible
6. Land softly on balls of feet; arms fall to sides
7. Reposition and repeat

## Coaching Points
- The "punching down" is counterintuitive but critical — you push energy INTO the floor so the floor pushes you UP [S9, p.18]
- "Think of the final big step before the block as a skip" — helps athletes find the rhythm of the feet-together transition [S9, p.18]
- Keep the run-up short; a long run-up creates too much horizontal momentum to convert efficiently
- Watch for athletes jumping diagonally rather than vertically — the block should convert ALL horizontal momentum into vertical
- This drill also exploits the myotatic rebound at the foot-plant moment

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 2-3 steps only; master the two-feet-together transition at slow speed before adding speed
2. **Intermediate**: Full 4-5 step run-up with maximum blocking force; linear body position in air
3. **Advanced**: Jump into a tuck position (Step 7) or catch-tuck (Step 8) at the peak — combines blocking with advanced tuck skill

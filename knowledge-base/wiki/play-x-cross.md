# X-Cross

## Overview
A half-court set play from the 1-4 high formation that uses 5 as a stationary high-post screen. Players 1, 2, and 4 all use 5 as a screen before a flex screen creates the primary scoring action. [S4, p.77]

## Formation
5 is stationed at the high post (elbow/top of key area) and must remain stationary. 1 is at the top of the key with the ball. 2 and 4 are at the wings/elbows. 3 is at the weak-side corner or wing. [S4, p.77]

## Phases

### Phase 1: High-Post Screen Setup
- 1, 2, and 4 all utilize 5 as a stationary screen at the high post to create movement and confusion for defenders.
- 5 must hold their position — any movement risks a moving screen call.
- The first pass to the wing should go to the player who will be the primary recipient coming off the flex screen. [S4, p.77]

### Phase 2: Flex Screen Action
- Once the wing receives the pass, the flex screen is executed on the weak side.
- The player using the flex screen cuts hard to the rim looking for a catch and finish.
- The passer and screener read the defense for secondary options.

## Key Coaching Points
- "5 must hold their position on the high post as 1, 2, and 4 use them as a screen. If 5 moves, a moving screen may be called." [S4, p.77]
- "If possible, the first pass to the wing should go to the player you want coming off the flex screen." — This determines which side the primary action runs toward. [S4, p.77]
- The stationary high-post screen is the engine of the play; emphasize footwork and stillness for 5.

## Counters
- If the defense anticipates the flex cut, the player can counter with a straight cut backdoor to the rim.
- If 5's defender helps off, 5 can receive a quick pass at the high post for a midrange look.

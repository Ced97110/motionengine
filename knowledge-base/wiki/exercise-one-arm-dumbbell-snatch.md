# One-Arm Dumbbell Snatch (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop unilateral explosive total-body power mimicking basketball's inherently one-sided movement demands — catching, shooting, posting, and defending all involve asymmetric force production.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Single dumbbell (appropriate load)
- No spotter required

## Primary Muscles Trained
Gluteus maximus, biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, soleus, gastrocnemius, deltoids, trapezius [S11, p.16]

## Execution
1. **Beginning position**: Hold a dumbbell in one hand, assuming the same beginning position and posture as the power snatch. **Lower the dumbbell between the legs to a position 3–4 inches (8–10 cm) below knee level** (hang position rather than floor start).
2. **Explosive pull**: Perform an explosive jumping action in the same manner as the second pull of the power snatch — extend hips, legs, and ankles; thrust shoulders up and slightly back; rise onto toes.
3. **Overhead catch**: At the top of the jump, **turn the wrist over** to receive the dumbbell at arm's length overhead.
4. **Stand up**: Stand to an upright position. The dumbbell should be in line with the body's center of gravity, even with or slightly back over the shoulder.

## Breathing Guidelines
- Take a deep breath before initiating the pull
- Exhale during the aggressive, explosive jumping action [S11, p.16]

## Coaching Points
- A set can be performed all with the same hand, or by **switching the dumbbell between hands before each repetition** to train bilateral symmetry [S11, p.17]
- Can catch in a deep squat position or a taller position with knees and hips slightly flexed
- "To mimic the unilateral nature of basketball, it is important to perform one-arm (and single-leg) total body resistance training exercises" — key coaching rationale [S11, p.10]
- Keep the working shoulder engaged throughout; avoid lateral trunk lean that transfers load away from the target musculature

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Kettlebell high pull (one arm) — teaches the explosive pulling pattern without the overhead catch
2. **Intermediate**: One-arm dumbbell snatch at moderate load with tall catch
3. **Advanced**: Increase load; add deep-squat catch; alternate hands each rep for coordination challenge

# Lift, Extend, Flip Drill

## Objective
Teach the complete three-phase shooting propulsion sequence — lift, extend, flip — using verbal cues first without a ball, then with a ball thrown to a partner, building muscle memory for the catapult motion.

## Setup
- Players: pairs facing each other, OR entire team in two lines facing each other
- Court area: open floor — no basket needed for the primary drill
- Equipment: one basketball per pair (added in Step 6)
- Lines should be about 10 feet (3 m) apart

## Execution
**Phase 1 — No ball, verbal cues:**
1. Players face each other in the L-position (lower arm perpendicular to upper arm, wrist cocked)
2. Coach commands **"Lift"** — players raise their upper arms to no greater than 45° from parallel to the floor; wrist remains cocked
3. Coach commands **"Extend"** — players extend their upper arms so there is very little sway at the elbow; wrist still cocked
4. Coach commands **"Flip"** — players wave goodbye, leaving palms facing down and fingers pointing toward the floor
5. Repeat steps 2-4 until the sequence is smooth; then combine: **"lift-extend-flip"** called rapidly so it sounds like one word

**Phase 2 — With basketball:**
6. Partners 10 feet (3 m) apart; one side lifts, extends, and flips the ball to the other side
7. The ball should reach its apex approximately **two-thirds** of the distance between shooter and receiver
8. The ball should arc at least **15 feet (4.5 m) high**
9. The receiver catches with both hands
10. The shooter steps forward slightly with the same foot as the shooting hand

## Coaching Points
- **Check the L-position** before every rep: lower arm perpendicular to upper arm; upper arm parallel to the floor?
- **"Wrinkle behind the wrist"** — confirm the wrist is cocked before every lift command
- **Ball on the pads** — spread and relax the hand to find the shooting cup before each rep
- **Straight-stick check**: the ball should be in a straight line with the elbow, the knee, and the toe of the front foot [S15, p.185]
- **Height target**: the ball must arc at least 15 feet high — this ensures a proper trajectory; a flat catapult means the upper arm was not lifted enough
- **Fingers down**: after the flip, the hand finishes with fingers pointing to the floor and palm facing the target — freeze this position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: verbal cues, no ball — build the movement pattern
2. **Intermediate**: add ball, receiver catches with both hands
3. **Advanced**: receiver catches the return with only the shooting hand, keeping it balanced in the cup

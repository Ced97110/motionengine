# Four-Corner Stopping, Pivoting

## Objective
Integrate dribbling moves, stops, pivots, passes, and V-cuts in a high-tempo team drill using an 8-player rotation system that eliminates dead time.

## Setup
- 8 players total: 4 on the court, 4 out of bounds at the four corners
- All 4 on-court players each have a basketball
- Half court (diagram shows the court with 4 corner positions labeled 5, 6, 7, 8 and 4 on-court positions 1, 2, 3, 4)
- Duration: 3 minutes continuous

## Execution

### Intermediate
1. Line players up as shown: 4 players on the court, one in each corner; 4 players out of bounds at the four corners
2. All 4 on-court players have a basketball and begin dribbling toward the middle simultaneously
3. When players reach the middle, they jump-stop (or stride-stop) and then pivot using either a front pivot or a reverse pivot
4. The 4 out-of-bounds players V-cut simultaneously; they must time their cuts to correspond with the pivot of the on-court players
5. The on-court players pass the ball to their V-cutting teammate using a bounce pass, chest pass, or overhead pass
6. Rotation: Player 1 → spot 6; Player 2 → spot 7; Player 3 → spot 8; Player 4 → spot 5; drill continues for 3 minutes

### Advanced
1. As players drive to mid-court, they execute a dribbling move: in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin, or hesitation
2. They may also use a rocker step (jab step, jab-step direct drive, jab-step crossover, jab-step pullback) before beginning their dribble to mid-court

## Coaching Points
1. **Enforce proper fundamentals on all components**: dribble, stop, pivot, AND pass; players take short cuts if allowed — do not permit it
2. **Break it down if needed**: drill only dribble + stop + pass with no cuts or offensive moves; focus on weak spots
3. **Communication**: Have players call out how they are being defended and what move they will use to defeat the defense (before the dribble and during the dribble)
4. V-cutting teammates must time their cuts to arrive at the correct moment — the cut too early or late results in a bad pass angle
5. The passer must read which pass type is appropriate for the distance of the receiver

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Basic 4-on-4 version with simultaneous drives, stops, pivots, and passes to V-cutters
2. **Advanced**: Add dribbling moves on the drive and rocker steps before the drive

# Carioca

## Objective
Develop balance, flexibility in the hips, footwork coordination, and lateral speed through a continuous crossover stepping pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player (can run in lines with unlimited players)
- Full court or any open space — typically run sideline to sideline or end line to end line
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point athletic stance (feet shoulder-width, slight knee bend, chest up).
2. Step with the right foot crossing over in front of the left leg.
3. Move the left foot to the left (in the direction of travel) behind the right leg.
4. Step with the right foot behind the left leg.
5. Continue this alternating cross-front / cross-behind pattern while traveling laterally.
6. Run continuously for the desired distance, then return in the opposite direction (leading with the left leg over).

## Coaching Points
- Hips must rotate with each crossover step — stiff hips prevent the full carioca pattern
- Eyes stay forward, head neutral — do not look down at feet
- Arm swing should mirror leg drive to maintain balance and speed
- Stay on the balls of the feet throughout — no flat-footed stepping
- Start slow to establish the rhythm, then progressively increase speed
- "Get your hips through" on every crossover step

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow walk-through pace, coach counts the pattern aloud (over, side, behind, side)
2. **Intermediate**: Game-tempo lateral speed both directions, 10-20 yards
3. **Advanced**: Max-effort lateral speed; add a sprint out of the last carioca step to simulate defensive transition

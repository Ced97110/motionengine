# Set Play: 32 Lob (vs 2-3 Zone)

## Overview
Designed to get an athletic perimeter player a backdoor lob against a 2-3 zone. Ball movement overloads the strong side, forcing zone rotation, then a screen by 5 on the weak-side low defender opens the lane for 3 to cut hard and catch the lob pass from 1. Reserve for 1-2 uses per game. [S4, pp.35-36]

## Formation
3-out 2-in formation. 1 at the top, 2 and 3 on wings, 4 and 5 inside. [S4, p.35]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Force Zone Rotation
- 1 passes to 2 on the perimeter.
- 2 **dribbles hard toward the corner**, forcing x4 (low zone defender) to step up and defend the ball.
- This forces x5 to shift over to cover 4 — creating a defensive rotation.
- Simultaneously, 3 **slides quietly to the weak-side corner** behind the sight lines of the defense.

### Phase 2: Reset and Set Up the Screen
- 2 passes back to 1 on the wing.
- Having forced the entire zone to rotate toward the strong side, 5 establishes position **behind x4** and **nudges x4 up the lane**, creating space on the baseline.

### Phase 3: Backdoor Lob
- 3 **cuts hard to the rim** behind the screen from 5.
- 1 throws the lob pass to 3 for the easy finish (dunk or layup). [S4, p.35]

## Key Coaching Points
- This is a **surprise play** — don't use it more than once or twice per game. [S4, p.36]
- Lob plays **energize the crowd and your team** — ideal when you have an athletic finisher. [S4, p.36]
- 3 must time the cut perfectly — going too early tips off the defense.
- 1 must be able to throw an accurate lob pass on cue. [S4, p.35]
- 5's screen on x4 is the key action — must create enough separation for 3's cut.

## Counters
- If the lob is unavailable, 3 can pull up in the short corner for a midrange shot.

# 1-on-1 Baseline Touch Drill

## Objective
Develop competitive live 1-on-1 scoring and defense in a game-realistic situation created by a competitive sprint — who earns the ball determines who attacks.

## Setup
- Half court
- Coach behind the foul line with a ball
- **Perimeter version:** Two players at the middle of the free-throw line
- **Bigs version:** Two big men at the free-throw line; one chair with a ball in the middle of the lane

## Execution

### Perimeter Players Version
1. Coach yells "Go!"
2. Both players sprint from the foul line to the baseline, touch it with one foot.
3. Both cut out to the wing area.
4. **The first player to get out** to the wing receives the ball from the coach.
5. Live 1-on-1 until a basket is scored or the defender gets the ball.

### Bigs Version
1. Same start, but the sprint and cut happen **inside the lane**.
2. One chair with a ball is in the middle of the lane — the first big to get open picks up the ball and plays 1-on-1 inside.

## Coaching Points
- Sprint to the baseline — no half-effort, because half-effort means the opponent gets the ball
- The first player out must be ready to receive — "be shot-ready before the ball arrives"
- Defender uses every tool to prevent the score; offense uses all live-ball moves
- This drill tests whether players have internalized the scoring techniques they've been drilling
- For bigs: reinforce post footwork, square-up mechanics, and contact finishing [S7, pp.311-312]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Slow sprint to baseline, half-speed 1-on-1 — focus on technique
2. **Intermediate:** Full competition — no constraints on offense or defense
3. **Advanced:** Loser stays as defender for the next pair — creates longer defensive streaks to build toughness

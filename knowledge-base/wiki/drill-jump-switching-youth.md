# Jump Switching

## Objective
Train defenders to execute the jump switch (or hedge-and-recover) on on-ball screens while coordinating with help-side defenders who must cover the roll and prevent open shots.

## Setup
- 3 offensive players (1 at top, 2 at wing, 3 at opposite wing) and 3 defenders
- Reserve line waiting behind the arc
- Half court
- Rotation: offense → defense → end of line after each player has defended at least one jump switch

## Execution
1. Player 1 passes to player 2, immediately sets a screen for player 2.
2. After screening, player 1 rolls to the basket.
3. X1 **aggressively jump-switches** into the path of dribbling player 2 — at least a half-body above player 2 in the direction of the dribble.
4. X2 must decide: slide through and below the screen OR fight over the top. **Pick one technique and teach it to the entire team.**
5. X3 (help-side) must cover player 1's roll to the basket in either case.
6. For drill continuity: player 1 goes to baseline, breaks back outside to open wing. Player 2 can then pass to player 3 or back to player 1 and go screen again. Another jump switch occurs.
7. During teaching phase: do NOT allow a shot; do NOT allow player 2 to pass to rolling player 1. Maximize reps.

## Options
1. **Hedge and Recover**: X1 jumps out a half-body above player 2 to force them back toward the screener, then immediately hustles back to assignment. X2 goes over or through the screen and recovers. No switch.
2. **Trap**: X1 and X2 trap player 2. X3 must cover both player 3 and player 1.
3. **Live drill**: Play until player 1 makes a pass to player 2 or player 3. X2 and X3 start in fence-slide denial stances.
4. **Fast break option**: Allow defense to fast break on any steal.
5. **Advanced**: Allow player 2 to drive baseline (activates the rotation drill). Allow player 2 to split the two defenders and drive toward basket (rotation drill).

## Jump Switching Technique
1. X1 must align at least a half-body (up to a full body) higher than dribbling player 2, in the direction of the dribble. Purpose: force player 2 to dribble further out or back toward the screener.
2. Just before player 2 appears to change direction and split the two defenders, X1 **fully recovers** to a normal guarding position on player 2. X1 must anticipate this move.
3. X2 switches assignments — covers player 1 on the roll to basket.
4. X3 (help-side) helps on rolling player 1 before returning to assignment.

## Coaching Points
- Settle on ONE technique (jump switch vs. through vs. over) and teach it consistently to the whole team
- The jump switch must be aggressive and fundamentally sound — half a body above the dribbler
- Make sure the screen (player 1) is set correctly first
- Player 1's roll must keep eyes on player 2 (see related screening drills)
- X3 must use flat triangle, ball-you-man, and interception stance to be in position to help on the roll
- If trapping: make sure trap is set correctly and interception/deflection techniques are active
- "Help-side defenders: see ball, see man — be ready to cover the roll"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary — no dribble; just walk through the switch communication
2. **Intermediate**: Live jump switches, no shot allowed, focus on X1 and X2
3. **Advanced**: Full live play; allow all options (split, baseline drive, shot, fast break)

# Bench Press (Basketball Strength Exercise)

## Objective
Develop upper-body pressing strength in the pectoralis major, pectoralis minor, anterior deltoid, and triceps to improve passing power, physical play under the basket, and upper-body durability.

## Setup
- 2 players (1 lifter + 1 spotter — **spotter is mandatory**)
- Flat weight bench with barbell rack
- Appropriate barbell weight plates
- **Safety Tip**: Always have a spotter present

## Execution
1. Lie on your back on a flat weight bench with knees bent at 90 degrees and both feet flat on the floor.
2. Grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder width, wrapping thumbs fully around the bar.
3. Retract (pinch) the scapula to create a stable platform from which to push — this protects the shoulder joint.
4. Fully extend the arms to lift the bar off the rack.
5. Slowly lower the barbell by bending the elbows, maintaining upper arms at a **45-degree angle** to the body throughout.
6. Lower until the bar touches at approximately midchest (nipple level). **Do not bounce the bar off the chest.**
7. Under a slow, controlled exhalation, push the bar away from the chest. Keep the low back flat on the bench. Maintain the 45-degree upper-arm angle as arms extend back to start.
8. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Scapula back and down" — retract before unracking to create a solid base
- Upper arms stay at 45° to the torso — not perpendicular (shoulder stress) and not straight alongside the ribs
- "Don't bounce" — touch and press; bouncing reduces muscular tension and risks rib/sternum injury
- Keep lower back flat on the bench throughout — no arch
- Exhale on the press, inhale on the descent
- Spotter: hands ready but not touching bar unless the lifter asks or fails

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Push-up until sufficient strength to safely bench press with a bar
2. **Intermediate**: Barbell bench press at moderate weight (8–12 rep range) focusing on form
3. **Advanced (Dumbbell Bench Press)**: Use dumbbells instead of a barbell — increases range of motion and forces each arm to work independently, addressing imbalances

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Pectoralis major, pectoralis minor
- **Secondary**: Anterior deltoid, triceps brachii

## Basketball Transfer
The bench press is the next progression from the push-up for building upper-body strength. Stronger chest and shoulder muscles help absorb physical contact under the basket, improve chest-pass explosiveness, and support physicality during defensive positioning and screening. [S2, pp.76-77]

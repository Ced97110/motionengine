# 55-Yard Sprint-Backpedal

## Objective
Develop acceleration mechanics and stopping ability through alternating sprint and backpedal intervals.

## Setup
- 1+ players
- Open space with markers at 5, 10, 15, and 20 yards from start
- No equipment needed beyond line markers

## Execution
1. Sprint forward 10 yards (9 m).
2. Backpedal 5 yards (4.6 m).
3. Sprint forward 10 yards.
4. Backpedal 5 yards.
5. Sprint forward 10 yards.
6. Backpedal 5 yards.
7. Sprint forward 10 yards to the finish.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Zigzag vertical pattern showing alternating forward sprint (10 yd) and backpedal (5 yd) legs stacked vertically, four sprint legs and three backpedal legs total. [S8, p.86] -->

## Coaching Points
- Accelerate with 100-percent effort on every sprint leg [S8, p.86]
- Maximize forward lean when initially accelerating — "lean into the start"
- Minimize braking distance by quickly dropping your center of gravity on the transition to backpedal
- Use short, choppy steps to minimize stopping distance [S8, p.86]
- This drill specifically targets the deceleration-reacceleration window — that 1-2 step transition is where basketball plays are won and lost

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 50% effort, emphasize transition mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed, timed
3. **Advanced**: React to coach's command for each transition rather than predetermined yards

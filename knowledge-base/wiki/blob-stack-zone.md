# Stack (BLOB vs 2-3 Zone)

## Overview
A basic baseline out-of-bounds play against a 2-3 zone, common and highly effective at the youth level. It clears two low zone defenders and creates a gap for a post player to receive a pass for a quick midrange shot, with a corner three as the counter option. [S4, pp.17-18]

## Formation
Players 2, 5, 4, and 3 set up in a vertical stack on the ball-side edge of the key. Player 1 is the inbounder on the baseline.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Drag the Low Wing Defender
- **2** immediately cuts hard to the corner, calling for the basketball to drag out the low wing zone defender (x3).
- **3** clears out to the top of the key to occupy the top defender (x1) and provide a safety valve.
- These two cuts must be active and convincing — if defenders don't follow, the pass goes immediately.

### Phase 2: Screen the Middle Zone Defender
- **5** steps toward the middle of the key and sets a screen on the middle zone defender (x5).
- This screen creates a gap in the zone for 4 to exploit.

### Phase 3: 4 Steps Into the Gap
- With x3 dragged to the corner by 2 and x5 screened by 5, **4** steps into the gap and receives the inbound pass from **1** for the open midrange shot.
- **1** must read x3: if x3 stays to guard the gap, **2** is open in the corner for the catch-and-shoot three-pointer.

## Key Coaching Points
- Timing of screens and cuts is critical — **4 must wait for the gap to open** before stepping up and calling for the ball. [S4, p.18]
- **4 must have hands up**, ready to catch and shoot immediately. There is not enough time to dribble or lower the ball. [S4, p.18]
- **2 must be calling for the basketball** actively as they cut to the corner to ensure the defender follows. [S4, p.18]
- **1 reads x3** — the defense cannot guard both 2 and 4, so one will always be open. [S4, p.18]

## Counters
- If x3 stays and denies the pass to 4 → 2 is open in the corner for the three-pointer.
- If neither option is available → 3 at the top of the key is the safety valve.

# Deny the Flash Pivot Drill (#148)

## Objective
Train defenders to body-check and deny a post player flashing across the lane to the high-post area, preventing a dangerous interior pass.

## Setup
- Half court
- 3 players: player 1 (passer at top of key), player 5 (post player on the low block), X5 (defender)
- Line of waiting players
- Rotation: 1 → X5 → 5 → end of line

<!-- DIAGRAM: Player 1 at top of key with ball. Player 5 starting at the low block, flashing across the lane toward the high-post elbow. X5 must body-check 5 as they cross the lane, then use fence-slide denial once 5 cuts upward. -->

## Execution
1. Player 5 breaks on a **flash-pivot cut** toward the high-post area. X5 tries to deny this cut.
2. Player 1 attempts to pass to player 5. Player 5 has the option to **backdoor cut** if X5 overplays.
3. Player 1 can also throw an **overhead flip pass** to the backdoor cutter.
4. When player 5 receives the pass, player 5 front-pivots (or reverse-pivots) into triple-threat position; passes to player 1; rotation commences.

## Coaching Points
- **X5 first body-checks player 5** as player 5 cuts across the lane — **full torso against torso**. The defense is allowed this contact as much as the offense. The defense must **get there first**. [S15, p.309]
- Once player 5 cuts up the lane, X5 uses the **deny-the-wing fence slide** techniques from Drill 147 (arm in the passing lane, thumb down, off-arm into running lane).
- If X5 arrives first and maintains physical contact, player 5 cannot get a direct catch at the high post.
- If X5 overplays, player 5 goes **backdoor** — the price of poor positioning is a layup.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Base drill — body check, fence slide, pass/rotate.
2. **Intermediate**: When player 5 receives the pass (wherever they get it), player 5 front-pivots and gets in triple-threat; player 5 and X5 play **1-on-1**.

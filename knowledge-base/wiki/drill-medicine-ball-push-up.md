# Medicine Ball Push-Up

## Objective
Build explosive upper-body pushing strength to improve passing speed, rebounding grip strength, and the ability to hold position against physical defenders while boxing out.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 1 medicine ball placed on the floor
- Standard push-up starting position — hands on floor, ball between arms, below chest
- Hard, flat floor surface

## Execution
1. Assume a push-up position with the medicine ball placed on the floor between the arms, directly below the chest.
2. Lower the chest toward the medicine ball until the chest **slightly touches** the ball.
3. Upon chest contact with the ball, **push away from the floor with explosive force** — enough for the hands to fully leave the floor.
4. Immediately place both hands on top of the medicine ball as they clear the floor.
5. Lower the chest again until it touches the ball on top.
6. From the top of the ball, **forcibly push the hands off** the ball and return to the floor push-up starting position, stabilizing the landing.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

> **Plyometric intent:** Each transition (floor → ball → floor) must be explosive — this is not a slow strength exercise. [S2, pp.124-125]

## Coaching Points
- Maintain a rigid plank body line throughout — no sagging hips
- **"Explode every contact"** — both the floor push and the ball push must be reactive
- Land hands on top of ball with fingers spread and wrists stable
- As fatigue builds, reduce reps rather than compromise form — injury risk increases with form breakdown
- Beginners should start with hands on ball (stable) and work toward the full alternating pattern

## Primary Muscles
- Pectoralis major, pectoralis minor, triceps brachii, anterior deltoid
- **Secondary:** Biceps brachii, latissimus dorsi, rectus abdominis [S2, p.125]

## Basketball Application
Explosive upper-body strength translates directly to quicker, harder chest passes, stronger rebounding grabs, and improved boxing-out — the player who wins a contested rebound is often the one with the stronger upper body maintaining position. [S2, p.125]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Standard push-ups on the floor — build base pushing strength before adding plyometric component
2. **Intermediate:** Hands on ball push-up (ball stationary on floor) — practice the unstable surface element
3. **Advanced:** Full alternating floor-to-ball-to-floor explosive push-up with minimal contact time

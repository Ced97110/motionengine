# Vertical Jump (Plyometric Drill)

## Objective
Improve quickness and explosive power in the lower body through a maximal bilateral counter-movement vertical jump. [S8, p.171]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor or court area
- Optional: vertical jump measurement device (e.g., Vertec)

## Execution
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, knees and hips flexed in a pre-stretched position, arms back, shoulders over the toes.
2. Quickly dip into the power position (rapid counter-movement dip).
3. Perform a vertical jump by sequentially extending the ankles, knees, and hips.
4. Reach both arms straight up into the air at the peak of the jump.
5. Land and reset, or for the complex variation: upon landing, immediately reload the legs and perform another vertical jump, spending as little time as possible on the ground.

## Coaching Points
- "Dip quick" — the speed of the counter-movement dip determines the elastic energy stored.
- Sequential extension (ankle → knee → hip) must be intentional at first; athletes often skip ankle extension.
- Arms drive upward and assist — arm swing is a critical power amplifier.
- For the reactive version: minimal ground contact is the performance variable.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Single maximal jump with full reset between reps.
2. **Intermediate**: Consecutive jumps with moderate ground contact.
3. **Advanced (Reactive)**: Minimal ground contact between jumps — measure reps per 10-second period.

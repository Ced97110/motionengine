# Fade Shot Off Screen Workout (Workouts #5-1, #5-2, #5-3)

## Objective
Develop the fade shot off a screen — reading the defender playing over the top, fading away from the screen to an open spot 15–20 feet from the basket, and shooting a catch-and-shoot jump shot.

## Setup
- Number of players: 2 (shooter + passer); 1 additional player or stationary object as screener
- Court area: both sides of the floor (left and right wings, 15–20 feet from basket)
- Equipment: basketball; a stationary screener (player or cone/chair)
- Passer position: top of the key
- Shooter starting position: underneath the basket

## Execution
1. Shooter starts under the basket and approaches the stationary screener.
2. Shooter sets up the defender (imaginary or live) — jabs or sells the curl cut before fading.
3. Shooter fades away from the screener to the wing at 15–20 feet.
4. Passer at the top of the key delivers a pass to the fading shooter in rhythm.
5. Shooter catches ready to shoot — inside pivot foot planted — and fires the jump shot.
6. Shooter retrieves the ball, returns under the basket, and repeats.

### Volume Levels:
| Workout | FG Attempts | FT Attempts | FG Target | FT Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| #5-1 (25 shots) | 25 | 25 (5 FTs after each set of 5) | 15/25 = 60% | 21/25 = 84% |
| #5-2 (50 shots) | 50 | 50 (5 FTs after each set of 5 per side) | 30/50 = 60% | 43/50 = 86% |
| #5-3 (100 shots) | 100 | 50 (5 FTs after each set of 10 per side) | 60/100 = 60% | 43/50 = 86% |

For **#5-2 and #5-3**, split reps between left side and right side of the floor in equal blocks. [S3, pp.135-137]

## Coaching Points
- **Set up the defender:** The shooter must take a step or two toward the basket (selling the curl) before fading — a straight fade with no setup is easy to defend.
- **Shoulder-to-shoulder off the screen:** Even on a fade, the shooter should brush the screener's shoulder as they fade to force the defender to go around.
- **Inside pivot foot:** On the catch, the foot closest to the basket (inside foot) is the pivot — plant it and square the body to the hoop.
- **Catch ready to shoot:** The shooting hand and elbow should be in L-position before the catch — "catch and lift, don't dip."
- **Hold the follow-through** until the ball hits the floor to reinforce muscle memory.
- **Both sides equally:** The 100-shot workout (left and right columns) deliberately builds bilateral fade shot competency.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (#5-1, 25 shots):** Stationary object as screener; shooter practices timing and footwork without defensive pressure.
2. **Intermediate (#5-2, 50 shots):** Live screener; shooter must verbalize "fade" before cutting; alternate left/right sides.
3. **Advanced (#5-3, 100 shots):** Full left/right alternating workout at game speed; maintain 60% FG threshold.

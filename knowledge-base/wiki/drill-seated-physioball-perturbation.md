# Seated Physioball Perturbation

## Objective
Restore proprioceptive awareness in the ankle during the acute phase of ankle rehabilitation by using a seated position and clinician-applied perturbations.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 clinician/coach/partner
- Table or bench (edge of)
- Equipment: Physioball (stability ball)

## Execution
1. Sit on the edge of a table with the injured foot resting on a Physioball in a neutral ankle position.
2. Maintain the neutral position of the foot and ankle as a clinician or partner provides perturbations to the Physioball (pushing/rocking it in various directions).
3. Begin with predicted patterns of perturbation (regular, rhythmic movements).
4. **Progression**: Move to unpredicted patterns (random directions and timing) or close the eyes to remove visual compensation and force pure proprioceptive response [S2, p.132].

## Coaching Points
- The foot must stay in neutral — watch for the player allowing the ankle to roll into inversion or eversion
- "Keep the foot still as I move the ball"
- Begin slowly and gently; this is an acute rehab drill — pain should never be provoked
- Progress to eyes closed only when the player can control predicted perturbations comfortably
- This drill is for the acute phase — not for healthy athletes in regular training

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Acute)**: Predicted perturbations with eyes open
2. **Intermediate**: Unpredicted perturbations with eyes open
3. **Advanced**: Unpredicted perturbations with eyes closed [S2, p.132]

# Post-Up Defense

## Summary
Herb Brown's post-up defensive system is built on a clear rule: **front all post-ups when the ball is at or below the free-throw line**. The post defender sits on the knees of the posted player to root him off the block and protect against the lock-and-lob. Great ball pressure is non-negotiable whenever fronting. Above the free-throw line, three-quartering is acceptable. [S1, p.137]

## When to Use
- Any time an offensive player establishes position on the low or mid post
- Ball at or below the free-throw line: front
- Ball above the free-throw line: three-quarter or body up
- High-low action: extra vigilance on the nail man to stop high-post feed

## Key Principles
1. **Front when ball is at or below the FT line** — deny any direct pass to the post
2. **Sit on the knees of the posted player** — this roots him off the post and physically prevents him from jumping to receive a lob
3. **Protect against the lock-and-lob** — stay connected and guard the inside to eliminate the lob angle
4. **Great ball pressure is a must** — fronting only works with active, physical pressure on the passer
5. **Three-quarter when ball is above the FT line** — still maintain contact and body up
6. **Nail/elbow/box defenders must be active** — X (nail) must stop any high-low flash, X (elbow) and X (box) provide weak-side help structure
7. **High-Low Action**: Pressure the passer at the high post — treat him like a driver. Low-post defender fronts and sits on knees with hands up. Must stay connected. [S1, p.139]

## Player Responsibilities
- **Post Defender (X5/X4)**: Front when ball is below FT line. Sit on knees to root opponent off block. Three-quarter when ball is above FT line. Stay connected to prevent lob.
- **Ball-Side Defender (X1)**: Aggressive on-ball pressure — never allow an uncontested entry pass
- **Nail Defender (X2)**: Cover the nail, prevent any high-low pass to a flashing big. Must stop 4 on the nail to stop high-low action.
- **Weak-Side Box (X4)**: Cover the weak-side block
- **Weak-Side Elbow (X3)**: Cover the weak-side elbow

## Variations
### Three-Quarter Defense (Ball Above FT Line)
When ball is above the free-throw line, body up and three-quarter the post player — don't fully front but maintain contact and contest the catch.

### Trapping the Post Isolation
On an isolation on the wing: X1 and X2 are trappers, X3 and X5 are interceptors, X4 is deep goaltender. X3 and X4 can invert if possible. [S1, p.130]

### High-Low Action Special Defense
Pressure the high-post passer as if he will dribble-drive. Post defender fronts and sits on knees of low-post player with hands up. If high-post makes a lock-and-lob pass, the weak-side defender must rotate across the lane to stop it. [S1, p.139]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Three-quartering when ball is below FT line** → gives the offense the lock-and-lob angle; must front fully
2. **Insufficient ball pressure** → fronting without ball pressure allows easy lob passes over the defender
3. **Post defender not sitting on knees** → posted player can jump and receive lob; use the "sitting on knees" physical position to pin him
4. **Nail man not active on high-low** → a flashing big receiving a hi-lo pass can create an easy layup
5. **Losing connection after the catch** → stay attached through the catch, triple-threat, and first dribble

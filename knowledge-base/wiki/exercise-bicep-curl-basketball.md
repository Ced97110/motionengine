# Bicep Curl (Basketball)

## Objective
Build elbow flexion strength in the biceps brachii for ball security, rebounding pulls, and body control in contact situations.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Weight room (barbell, dumbbells, or cable machine)
- Standing or seated on a bench/stability ball

## Execution
1. Stand (or sit on a bench/ball) holding a barbell or dumbbells with a supinated (underhand) grip, arms fully extended at the sides.
2. Keeping the elbows fixed at the sides and wrists stiff, curl the weight upward by flexing the elbows.
3. Continue curling until the elbows are fully flexed and the weight is near shoulder height.
4. Lower the weight in a controlled manner back to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the prescribed number of repetitions.

**Hammer Curl Variation**: Keep hands in a neutral (hammer) grip throughout — palms facing each other — which shifts emphasis slightly to the brachialis and brachioradialis.

**Cable Variation**: Attach a straight bar or rope to a low cable pulley; perform the same curl motion, which provides constant tension through the full range of motion.

## Coaching Points
- "Minimize movement of the elbows forward or backward during the exercise" — elbows stay pinned to the sides [S11]
- "Keep the wrists stiff throughout the exercise" — avoid wrist flexion/extension [S11]
- "Do not swing the weights or arch the back to move the weights" — use only elbow flexion, no momentum [S11]
- Watch for the upper body swaying backward — if it occurs, reduce the weight

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Seated dumbbell curl on a bench to eliminate any trunk compensation
2. **Intermediate**: Standing barbell curl or alternating dumbbell curl — trains unilateral strength and balance
3. **Advanced**: Cable curl with various grips (rope, EZ-bar, straight bar) for constant tension through full ROM; add a static hold at peak contraction

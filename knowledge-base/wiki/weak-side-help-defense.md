# Weak-Side Help Defense

## Summary
Weak-side help defense is the defensive backbone of Herb Brown's system. When the ball moves away from a defender, that defender must immediately shrink toward the ball — not wait. The core rule: **move on the pass or dribble, never on the catch.** Defenders off the ball must be able to see both their man and the ball at all times; they should never have their backs to the basketball.

Brown references pro 'Shadow' and 'Tilt' principles as organizational frameworks for weak-side rotation. The philosophy blends man-to-man and zone principles — players guard their man but use zone instincts when off the ball on the weak side, shrinking and clogging driving and passing lanes. [S1, pp.12-14]

## When to Use
- Any time the ball moves away from a defender (pass or dribble)
- When ball penetrates the paint — all weak-side defenders collapse to prevent layups
- When a double-team or dig occurs — rotation is predetermined based on pass direction
- On any dribble drive: defenders off ball shrink to clog the lane or take a charge

## Key Principles
1. **Never have your back to the ball** — Weak-side defenders must maintain ball vision at all times to react to passes and dribble penetration. [S1, p.14]
2. **Move on the pass or dribble, not on the catch** — React and help while the ball is in the air. If you wait until the ball is caught, you are always a step late. [S1, pp.12-13]
3. **The dribble or pass draws you to the ball** — Every ball movement triggers weak-side defender movement toward the ball to shrink the floor. [S1, p.12]
4. **Shrink the court and pack the paint** — Off-ball defenders clog driving and passing lanes; establish position to draw offensive fouls on ball-handler penetration. [S1, p.12]
5. **Guard your buddies** — Each defender is responsible for helping the two defensive teammates nearest to them on either side. Communication, trust, and covering each other's back. [S1, p.14]
6. **Communicate** — Talk on every possession. Talk when switching, helping, rotating. Defense is a team act requiring verbal and visual communication.
7. **Make the offense throw an extra pass** — Proper weak-side positioning forces additional passes, increasing the chance of mistakes and reducing shot quality. [S1, p.12]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Pressure the ball on the strong side; communicate weak-side assignments to teammates; call rotations.
- **SG**: Body up on all cutters (straight-basket and weak-side cuts); never allow free cuts through the lane.
- **SF**: Body up and go over all weak-side flares; shrink to paint on ball penetration.
- **PF**: Dig and double the ball when post receives it; rotate to stop kick-out passes.
- **C**: Provide last-line shot-blocking weak-side help; control the paint; prevent straight-line drives.

## Variations
### pro Shadow Principles
Defenders on the weak side rotate to form a 'shadow' that mirrors the ball's position — the further the ball is from the basket, the more compressed the weak side becomes. [S1, p.11]

### pro Tilt Principles
The entire defense 'tilts' toward the ball — all defenders angle their bodies to have a direct passing-lane relationship to both their man and the ball simultaneously. [S1, p.11]

### Double Stack Turnout Defense
When defending a double stack turnout, the on-ball defender pressures the passer and immediately jumps back toward the ball to clog the passing lane and prevent the receiver from penetrating or shooting. [S1, p.13]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Moving on the catch** → Move while the ball is in the air; if you wait for the catch you surrender a step of reaction time. [S1, p.13]
2. **Back to the ball** → Defenders off the ball must maintain ball vision; pivot to see both man and ball simultaneously. [S1, p.14]
3. **Not shrinking on ball movement** → As soon as the ball moves away, begin moving toward the ball to shrink the court — don't stay home. [S1, p.12]
4. **Helping defender getting beaten** → Help defenders must be positioned to help without being beaten themselves; don't give up easy kick-out baskets. [S1, p.11]

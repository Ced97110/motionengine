# Standing Pallof Overhead Press

## Objective
Build anti-rotation and anti-lateral-flexion core strength by pressing a cable overhead from a perpendicular athletic stance — simulating the trunk stability demands of posting, screening, and contact resistance on the court.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Cable machine with adjustable column
- Handle attachment
- Stand perpendicular to the cable column, feet wider than shoulder-width

## Execution
1. Grasp the cable handle with both hands and stand perpendicular to the cable column.
2. Assume an athletic, defensive stance — hips and knees slightly flexed, chest up, shoulder blades back.
3. Tuck elbows into the rib cage and hold the cable handle close to the chest (beginning position).
4. Press the cable handle straight up overhead until the handle is directly above the head with arms fully extended.
5. At the top position, the core musculature contracts isometrically to resist lateral flexion of the spine.
6. Hold the overhead position for a moment.
7. Return the handle under control back to the starting chest position.
8. Repeat for the prescribed number of repetitions, then switch sides.

### Lateral Step Variation
- After pressing overhead, take a lateral step back toward the cable column while holding the overhead position, then pause before returning or continuing repetitions.
- This increases the anti-lateral-flexion challenge by dynamically shifting the center of mass.

## Coaching Points
- Athlete must maintain neutral alignment through hips, torso, and shoulders throughout — if any compensation occurs, reduce weight immediately.
- Cue: "Contract your glutes to keep your pelvis neutral."
- Do not allow the torso to lean away from or toward the cable column.
- Eyes forward; do not twist the spine to initiate the movement.
- The isometric hold at the top is where the core benefit is maximized — don't rush through it.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform in tall kneeling position (both knees on floor) — reduces rotational challenge.
2. **Intermediate**: Standing Pallof overhead press as described.
3. **Advanced**: Standing Pallof overhead press with lateral step — adds a dynamic stability challenge.

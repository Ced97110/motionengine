# 40-Yard Sprint (Agility Line Drill)

## Objective
Develop agility and conditioning through a multi-line sprint-touch-return pattern totaling 40 yards.

## Setup
- 1+ players
- Three parallel lines: starting line, first line 5 yards away, second line 10 yards away
- Use court markings or cones

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance on the starting line.
2. Sprint 5 yards (4.6 m) to the first line, touch it with the right hand, return to starting line, touch with the left hand.
3. Sprint 10 yards (9 m) to the second line, touch it with the right hand, return to starting line, touch with the left hand.
4. Sprint 5 yards (4.6 m) to the first line again, touch it with the right hand, and return to the starting line.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Multi-lane pattern with labeled legs 1-6; first line at 5 yards, second line at 10 yards; athlete sprints out-and-back to each line in sequence. [S8, p.82] -->

## Coaching Points
- Lower the center of gravity on every direction change — drop hips, do not stand tall
- Alternate right/left hand touches to train bilateral deceleration and turning
- Maximize forward lean on each acceleration phase
- Use short, choppy steps to minimize braking distance before each touch
- Time and track results

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 50% speed, walk-through pattern first
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed, timed
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Combine biomotor skills during each leg (carioca, backpedal, shuffle)
   - Start from various positions (lying, sitting)
   - Add tumbling at each turn [S8, p.82]

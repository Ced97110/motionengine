# Mass Ballhandling and Pivoting Drill

## Objective
Develop ballhandling, pivot-passing, and trap-beating skills simultaneously across the entire team in a time-efficient, high-repetition mass format — combining the pivot-and-pass fundamentals with continuous attack vs. trapping defenders.

## Setup
- **Players:** 12 (full roster); organized into groups of 4 along sidelines or across the half court
- **Court area:** Half court or full court — multiple groups operating simultaneously
- **Equipment:** One basketball per group
- **Roles per group (4 players):**
  - 01: Initial passer/ball handler
  - X2, X3: Two trapping defenders
  - 04: First pass receiver/shooter (positioned farther from the ball handler than in the 3-player version)

## Execution
1. 01 dribbles toward the trapping defenders (X2 and X3), kills the dribble with a two-foot bunny-hop landing.
2. 01 uses the step-out → rip-through → swing-around (split-the-trap) sequence to defeat both trappers and complete a pass to 04. [S6, p.142]
3. 04 pivots simultaneously on the catch (front pivot off the inside heel to square up) and immediately works on key offensive fundamentals upon receiving the pass:
   - Cutting aggressively to meet the ball
   - Pivoting correctly on the catch
   - Squaring up to the basket in triple-threat position
   - Shooting with proper form OR pivoting and passing to a secondary target
4. **Rotation every 55 seconds:**
   - 01 → X2's spot
   - X2 → X3's spot
   - X3 → 04's spot
   - 04 → 01 (new ball handler/passer)
5. After 4 minutes (all 4 rotations): each player has practiced all four roles once.
6. **Second 4-minute sequence:** Repeat all roles using the **opposite foot as the pivot foot** (both as passer and as receiver). Full bilateral training in 8 minutes total. [S6, p.142]

## Coaching Points
- Ensure 04 (the pass receiver) **breaks up aggressively to meet every pass** — do not wait for the ball to arrive. [S6, p.142]
- Monitor the ball handler for clean footwork: step laterally, protect beside the knee, rip low.
- Watch pass receivers for simultaneous catch-and-pivot: the pivot happens at the moment of contact with the ball.
- One coach can observe a cluster of groups operating in the same court area.
- Use the same scoring competition format as the Pivot-and-Pass Drill if intensity needs to increase.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Run as a 3-player pivot-and-pass drill first (single defender) before introducing the trapping version.
2. **Intermediate:** Add the second trapper and the 4-player format as described above.
3. **Advanced:** Extend the series — after 04 catches and pivots, introduce a secondary trapper who attacks 04, forcing 04 to also use pivot-and-pass techniques. Full chain of trap-beat → pass → trap-beat. [S6, p.142]

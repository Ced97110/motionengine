# Kettlebell High Pull (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop explosive shoulder power and fast-twitch muscle activation through a high-velocity multi-joint pulling movement — directly transferring to jumping height and rebounding explosiveness.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Kettlebell (or barbell for variation) on the floor
- Open floor space, feet shoulder-width apart

## Execution
1. Stand erect with feet **shoulder-width apart**, toes turned slightly outward, kettlebell on the floor between the feet.
2. **Squat** while keeping the back flat and grasp the kettlebell in an **overhand grip with both hands**.
3. Holding the kettlebell with both arms extended, **rise from the squat**, pushing through the heels, keeping the back flat.
4. Once the kettlebell reaches **waist height**: **shrug the shoulders**, **thrust the hips forward**, and **flex the elbows explosively** to pull the kettlebell to chest level.
5. At the top: elbows **parallel to the shoulders**, back erect, **heels off the floor**.
6. Keeping the kettlebell close to the body, slowly lower back to the starting position — relax shoulders, hips, and knees into the squat position until the kettlebell rests on the floor between the feet.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Drive through the heels, shrug the shoulders, and pop the hips — all in one explosive chain."
- The explosive phase (waist to chest) must be **high velocity** — this is what stimulates fast-twitch muscle fibers.
- Elbows parallel to shoulders at the top — do not let the kettlebell drift forward.
- Keep the kettlebell close to the body throughout — especially on the lowering phase.
- "Control the descent — don't drop the kettlebell."
- This exercise can be performed with a **barbell** as well: start with the barbell in line with the tops of the shoelaces.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light kettlebell with emphasis on learning the hip drive and shrug sequence.
2. **Intermediate**: Standard kettlebell high pull with moderate weight at full speed.
3. **Advanced**: **Barbell High Pull** — greater load potential; start with barbell at shoelace level.

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid
- **Secondary**: Infraspinatus, supraspinatus, teres minor, trapezius

## Basketball Focus
The kettlebell high pull is an excellent **multi-joint explosive exercise** that develops:
- **Strong shoulders** for contested shooting, passing, and rebounding
- **Explosive power for jumping** — the triple-extension (ankle, knee, hip) pattern mirrors the jump mechanism
- **Fast-twitch muscle fiber activation** — moving at high velocity stimulates the nervous system in ways that pure strength training cannot

This exercise is one of the most basketball-specific pulling movements because the explosive hip-drive and arm-pull sequence directly mimics the power output needed to **out-jump opponents for rebounds** and **contest shots**.

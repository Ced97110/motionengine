# NBA Loop Action

## Summary
The NBA "Loop" is one of the most common early-offense actions in professional basketball, typically used after made free throws or in transition to half court. The core action uses **staggered screens by the 4 and 5** to free the best shooter (2) to the top of the key or wing. The shooter reads the defensive response to choose between a catch-and-shoot jump shot or a catch-and-drive on the cleared side. Post-up continuations (Milwaukee Bucks variation) add a second scoring tier when the perimeter shot is unavailable. [S14, pp.41-43]

The play was popularized by teams like the New Jersey Nets (Calipari), Milwaukee Bucks (Karl), and Philadelphia 76ers (Larry Brown), each adding their own wrinkles to the fundamental stagger-screen concept.

## When to Use
- As an **early-offense action** after made free throws before the defense sets up
- When the best perimeter shooter is being denied or chased off the ball
- To generate a high-quality catch-and-shoot or catch-and-drive for the primary scorer
- When bigs can serve as stagger screeners and then post up after the screens
- As a secondary break play off transition

## Key Principles
1. **Stagger timing**: The screeners must be set when the ball handler begins his dribble — the cutter's read happens in motion.
2. **The two-read**: The shooter must instantly read the defender:
   - Defender **chases hard over the top** → catch and **drive** on the cleared side
   - Defender **takes the shortcut/cheats** through the weak side of the screens → **raise up for the jump shot**
3. **Clear the corner**: A third player clears to the ball-side corner, giving the shooter space to drive if the shot is denied.
4. **Ball handler's angle**: Ball handler dribbles to the opposite side from the screens, setting up a proper passing angle to the shooter at the top.
5. **Post continuity**: The inside screener (5 or 4) can immediately post up after setting screens, converting the action into a post-up two-man game.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (1)**: Dribble to the opposite side. Create a clean passing angle. Hit the shooter at the top. Read to swing the ball if the post continuity develops.
- **SG/SF (2) — Shooter**: Cut off the staggered screens. Read the defender immediately. Shoot or drive accordingly. If catching at the top, look for the post feed on the continuation.
- **PF (4)**: Set the second (top) screen of the stagger. After screening, either:
  - Calipari/Basic: stay as a screener/safety
  - Karl variation: cut into the lane to screen for 5's curl back to the block
- **C (5)**: Set the first (lower) screen of the stagger. After screening:
  - Basic: remain in the post area
  - Karl variation: use 4's interior pick to curl across the lane to the right block for a post-up feed
- **SF (3)**: Clear to the ball-side corner to create driving space for 2.

## Variations
### Basic Loop (New Jersey Nets — Calipari)
Shooters cut to the **ball side** of the screens (not weak side), reading the defense for shot or drive. Cleared corner gives space for the drive [S14, p.43].

### Loop to Post-Up (Milwaukee Bucks — Karl)
After **1** hits **2** at the top: **4** (second screener) cuts into the lane and sets a pick for **5**; **5** curls across to the right block for a post feed from 2. If denied, chain: 2→1→3→inside to 4 on left block [S14, pp.43].

### Baseline Staggered Screen + Lob (Philadelphia 76ers — Larry Brown)
Cutter (2) comes off the stagger and the passer (3) cuts off a back screen by 4 — lob option added on top of the basic loop [S14, pp.44-45].

## Common Mistakes
1. **Screeners set screens too early** → Shooter has to wait, losing rhythm. Correction: screeners time the cut to the ball handler's dribble initiation.
2. **Shooter doesn't read the defender** → Tries to shoot when the drive is open (or vice versa). Correction: practice both reads in every repetition.
3. **Corner player doesn't clear** → Creates traffic for the drive read. Correction: 3 must relocate to the ball-side corner as a priority.
4. **Bigs post up too late** → The post-up continuity window closes. Correction: 5 begins the curl as soon as 4 sets the interior pick.

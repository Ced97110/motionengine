# One-on-One Multiple Skills Drill (#152)

## Objective
Review all individual defensive fundamentals — help-side positioning, post defense, rotation, closeout, and flash pivot denial — in one extended sequence from the initial pass to the wing through full post coverage.

## Setup
- Half court
- Players 1, 2, 3 on offense; X1 as the lone defender
- Coach passes to initiate (acts as a 4th passer)
- Rotation: 1 → X1 → 2 → 3 → end of line; first in line becomes new 1
- Walk through each figure first; then run live, stopping after each 1-on-1 play to realign

## Execution

### Figure 1 — Ball Side to Help Side (Three Parts)
**(a) Jump to the Ball:**
Player 1 passes to player 2. X1 **jumps to the ball** — cannot allow a middle cut. Player 1 backdoor cuts to the side away from the ball.

**(b) Flat Triangle / Ball-You-Man:**
X1 is now a **help-side defender**. X1 must be in the **flat triangle** with ball-you-man techniques.

**(c) Rotation:**
Player 2 passes to player 3. X1 adjusts to get proper flat triangle and ball-you-man position on the new ball location. Player 3 drives to the basket. **X1 rotates**. If player 3 continues driving, **X1 must draw the charge**. [S15, p.316]

### Figure 2 — Help Side to Strong Side (Two Parts)
*(Continuing from Figure 1 positions. Player 3, instead of driving, dribbles back to the corner and passes to player 2. X1 returns to help-side.)*

**(a) Skip Pass Closeout:**
Player 2 **skip-passes** to player 1. X1 must **close out on the pass receiver**. Player 1 and X1 play **1-on-1** using all defensive footwork. If player 1 creates separation and shoots, X1 and player 1 battle for the rebound.

**(b) Flash Pivot Denial:**
After player 1 or X1 gets the rebound (or player 1 passes to player 2), the ball returns to player 2. Player 1 returns to weak-side position and **flash pivot cuts**. X1 must **deny the flash pivot cut**. If the pass is made inside, player 1 and X1 go 1-on-1. [S15, p.316]

### Figure 3 — Post Defense
Player 1 moves back to the center of the lane before trying to establish low-post position. X1 uses one of the post-defense techniques:
- **Fronting** the post
- **Two-step post defense** (maintaining lead-foot/trail-foot position)
- **Three-quarter position**

## Coaching Points
- Walk through each figure before going live — allow the coverage to be understood before executing at speed.
- Stop the drill live after each 1-on-1 play to realign and correct.
- "Your team defense is only as good as your weakest defender." [S15, p.302] — use this drill as a late-season check-up on individual defenders.
- The sequence mirrors actual game defensive responsibilities: initial denial → help-side positioning → rotation → closeout → flash denial → post defense.

## Progressions
1. **Walk-through**: Run each figure at half speed with narration.
2. **Live (intermediate)**: Run continuously, stop after each 1-on-1 phase.
3. **Advanced**: Run the entire sequence without stopping; coach evaluates and corrects afterward.

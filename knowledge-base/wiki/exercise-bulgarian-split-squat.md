# Bulgarian Split Squat (Single-Leg Squat)

## Objective
Develop unilateral lower-body strength — glutes, hamstrings, and quadriceps — to improve jumping, acceleration, deceleration, and single-leg stability for basketball.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Weight room / any open space with a bench or box approximately knee height
- Equipment: barbell (high bar position), or two dumbbells/kettlebells, or a single dumbbell/kettlebell

## Execution
1. Position a barbell across the shoulders in the high bar back squat position (or hold dumbbells/kettlebells at the sides).
2. Stand in front of a bench or box approximately knee height.
3. Take a moderate step forward with one leg and place the instep of the rear foot flat on top of the bench.
4. Allow both knees to be slightly flexed; torso should be fully erect — this is the beginning position.
5. Allow the hip and knee of the front leg AND the knee of the rear leg to flex at the same rate so that the body lowers **down** rather than **forward**.
6. Keep the instep of the rear foot flat on the bench throughout.
7. Continue the descent until the top of the front thigh is parallel to the floor.
8. Extend the hip and knee of the front leg to return to the beginning position.
9. Complete all reps on one leg, then switch.

**Breathing:** Inhale during the lowering phase; exhale during the upward phase.

## Coaching Points
- "Lower straight down — think elevator, not escalator" — the body drops vertically, not forward
- Keep the front knee in line with the front foot; **do not let the front knee extend beyond the toes**
- Both feet and knees should point in the same direction throughout
- Wrists should be stiff and the bar/dumbbells controlled at all times
- The torso stays fully erect — avoid forward lean

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight only — rear foot elevated on bench, no load, focus on balance and depth
2. **Intermediate**: Add a barbell (high bar) or two dumbbells; descend to parallel
3. **Advanced**: Increase load progressively; single dumbbell held ipsilateral (same side as supporting leg) or contralateral (opposite side) for added core demand

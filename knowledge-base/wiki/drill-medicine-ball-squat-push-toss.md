# Medicine-Ball Squat, Push Toss, Bounce, and Catch

## Objective
Improve reactive elastic strength and total-body power by combining an explosive squat-to-throw with a sprint-and-catch sequence that demands maximum acceleration. [S8, p.148]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open court or indoor surface
- 1 bouncing rubber medicine ball (must be able to bounce)

## Execution
1. Hold the medicine ball at **chest height**.
2. **Squat down** while maintaining an upright posture.
3. Explosively **push-toss the ball upward and forward** for maximum height and distance.
4. Immediately **sprint forward** to chase the ball.
5. You must catch the ball after its **first bounce but before its second bounce**.
6. Reset and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- Select ball weight carefully: a ball that is too light travels too far to retrieve in time [S8, p.148]
- The squat provides the loading (eccentric) phase — "load deep, explode fast"
- Full triple extension at the hips, knees, and ankles at ball release — same mechanics as a vertical jump
- Sprint acceleration must be immediate — no hesitation after the throw
- This is a **reactive drill** — the ball dictates the speed and direction of the sprint

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light toss for minimal distance; focus on squat mechanics and clean extension
2. **Intermediate**: Increase throw distance; must catch before second bounce
3. **Advanced**: Partner calls a direction change mid-sprint (catch to the left or right) adding a recognition reaction component

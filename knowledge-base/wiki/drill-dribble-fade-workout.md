# Dribble Fade Workout (Workout #5-4: 25 Dribble Fade Shots)

## Objective
Develop the dribble fade response when a defender slips the screen and closes out at a high angle — attack the baseline with a dribble and finish or shoot off the movement.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2–3 (shooter, passer, optional rebounder/screener)
- **Court area**: Wing to baseline, both sides
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball, stationary screener (player or object)
- **Passer position**: Top of the key
- **Shooter start position**: Under the basket
- **Screen position**: Wing [S3]

## Execution
1. Shooter starts under the basket and sets up the screen with a V-cut
2. Cut off the screen and receive the pass on the **wing** from the passer at the top of the key
3. The defender (simulated or live) **slips the screen** and **closes out at a high angle**, taking away the direct shot
4. The baseline is now open — shooter **establishes a dominant pivot foot** immediately on the catch
5. Attack the **baseline** with a controlled dribble fade (dribble toward the baseline/corner)
6. Use any available scoring action: pull-up jump shot, floater, layup, or step-back off the drive [S3]

### Workout #5-4: 25 Dribble Fades
- 5 sets: Dribble Fade × 5 → FTs × 5
- Total: 25 dribble fade attempts + 25 free throws [S3]

## Coaching Points
- The key read: "If the defender closes high, the baseline is yours — take it"
- Establish the pivot foot **immediately on the catch** — don't drift into the dribble
- Keep the dribble low and protected as you attack the baseline
- "Any scoring opportunity" can be used once baseline has been attacked — teach players to read the final defender, not just execute a scripted move [S3]
- This drill directly trains the response to a defender who slips the screen — a common defensive adjustment
- Track makes and attempts; FT routine after each set builds the mental discipline

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Scripted — always go baseline, always take the pull-up; remove live pressure
2. **Intermediate**: Add a live defender who decides to slip or go over the screen — shooter must read and respond
3. **Advanced**: Defender can also contest the baseline drive, forcing a finish through contact or a kick-out pass

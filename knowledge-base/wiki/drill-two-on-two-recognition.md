# Two-on-Two Recognition (Drill 63)

## Objective
Develop real-time read-and-react cutting decisions in a live 2-on-2 setting where defenders play full defense and offensive players must recognize which cut to make without the structure of scripted drills.

## Setup
- 2 offensive players (1 and 2), 2 defenders (X1 and X2)
- Half court
- Rotate: offense → defense → end of line after 2 minutes
- Equipment: 1 basketball
- **Prerequisite**: Drills 53–62 must be completed before using this drill [S15, p.132]

## Execution

### Intermediate (base level for this drill)
1. Players line up as shown. After 2 minutes, offense rotates to defense, defense goes to end of line.
2. X1 and X2 play **live defense** — the offense must take advantage of what the defense dictates.
3. A sample sequence (will vary every time):
   - Player 2 dribbles into frontcourt; Player 1 sees X1 sagging → **middle cut**. Player 2 does not pass, so Player 1 comes back to the wing.
   - Player 2 passes to Player 1; X2 jumps toward the pass → **Player 2 backdoor-cuts**. Player 1 does not pass; Player 2 cuts to short corner.
   - Player 1 dribbles outside; Player 2 **flash pivots** (cut to open ball-side area). Player 1 passes to Player 2. X1 turns to watch the pass → **Player 1 backdoor-cuts** for a layup.
4. "Throughout this set of cuts, the offense is trying to score" — pass for a driving layup whenever the opportunity arises [S15, p.132]
5. At end of 2 minutes, rotate and explain any mistakes.

**Option**: Players call out reasons for moves while making them (easier coaching feedback).

### Advanced
1. **Time limit**: limit the number of cuts and passes the offense may make before they must score. Start with 5 passes. If the defense prevents a score within that limit, teams rotate.
2. If defense forces a turnover, teams rotate.
3. **Layups only**: restrict the offense to finishing with layups only (no jump shots) — this forces many more cuts.

## Coaching Points
- This drill is most effective **after** teaching man-to-man defense — defenders who know how to defend make more realistic reads for the offense [S15, p.132]
- Allow the offense to run freely for the 2 minutes, then give **specific feedback** at the rotation on any incorrect reads
- Watch for backdoor cuts when middle cuts were available — correct these immediately
- The flash pivot cut (cutting to the ball-side high post when a teammate is driving) is a new cut introduced in this context
- Reinforce that players do not need to wait for a pass to make a cut — any ball movement (pass or dribble) triggers the cutting rule

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Live defense; full 2-on-2; players read and react; optional verbalization
2. **Advanced**: Pass limit before scoring; defense earns rotation with a stop or forced turnover; layups-only variation

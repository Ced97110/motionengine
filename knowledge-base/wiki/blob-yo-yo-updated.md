# BLOB Yo-Yo

## Overview
A simple, effective BLOB play from a box formation. Both guards simultaneously set up-screens for the two post players, who explode to the basket for layups. The inbounder (PG) reads the defense and passes to the open cutter. [S4, pp.30-31]

## Formation
Box formation with bigs (4, 5) on the elbows and guards (2, 3) on the low blocks. 1 (good passer) inbounds from the sideline. [S4, p.30]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Simultaneous Up-Screens
- 2 and 3 step up and set up-screens for 4 and 5 respectively.
- Screeners should **not face the direction they plan to screen** at the start — they should face each other to disguise the play. [S4, p.31]
- Screeners must seek out the defenders actively — the screen is the screener's only priority.

### Phase 2: Post Players Explode to the Basket
- 4 and 5 immediately cut to the **outside** of the screen and explode to the basket.
- They must stay on their own sides of the lane — if they converge, one defender can guard both passing lanes. [S4, p.31]
- Both show target hands as they cut to the rim.

### Phase 3: PG Decision and Pass
- 1 reads the defense and passes to 4 or 5 for the layup finish.

### Phase 4: Safety Valve
- If the pass to 4 or 5 is unavailable, 3 cuts to the wing and receives the pass for a midrange shot or to bring the ball back out and reset the offense. [S4, p.30]

## Key Coaching Points
- Screeners must **not face their cut direction** at the start of the play — body language gives away the play. Face each other instead. [S4, p.31]
- **Screeners must actively hunt the defender** and set strong screens — the screen must be the screener's singular focus. [S4, p.31]
- 4 and 5 must stay wide on their sides of the lane when cutting — convergence allows one defender to deny both passes. [S4, p.31]
- 1 must be a quality passer who can deliver accurate passes at rim-level while under time pressure. [S4, p.30]

## Counters
- 3 wings out as the safety valve for a midrange shot or reset. [S4, p.30]

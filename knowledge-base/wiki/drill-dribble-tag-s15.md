# Dribble Tag (Drill #33)

## Objective
Develop ball security, quick hands, peripheral vision, and competitive instincts by trying to slap an opponent's basketball out of a circle while protecting your own dribble.

## Setup
- Individual pairs (or full team rotating)
- Half court — use **existing court circles** (3 circles available on most courts)
- 1 basketball per player (Intermediate: 2 balls per player)
- Other teammates wait their turn outside the circle
- Duration: 1 minute per round

<!-- DIAGRAM: Three court circles shown on a half-court diagram. Each circle contains two players (O inside, X outside waiting). [S15, p.71] -->

## Execution
### Beginner
1. Two players enter a circle, each with one basketball.
2. Both players **dribble simultaneously**. Each player tries to **slap the opponent's basketball out of the circle** while keeping their own ball alive.
3. Any fake may be used — including momentarily forsaking your own ball to go slap the opponent's.
4. The player whose ball is knocked out of the circle loses. The winner stays; two new players enter.

### Intermediate
1. **Double ball**: Both players use **two basketballs** and must keep both alive. Both of the opponent's balls must be slapped out to win. If one ball is slapped away, continue with the remaining ball.
2. **King of the Circle**: Only one new challenger enters to play the current winner — best for organizing by position (post players on one circle, guards on another).

## Coaching Points
- **Eyes up**: Remind players constantly to keep eyes up — not looking at their own ball.
- **Fingerpads**: Players dribble with the front joints (first pads) of the dribbling hand.
- **Strike like a snake**: Use the **in-and-out defensive fake** to time the opponent's dribble. When the ball leaves the opponent's hand, **dart in** and strike at the ball, then **quickly pull back** to original position.
- **Ball protection**: Use your body to shield your own ball while attacking the opponent's — keep the ball low and to the side.
- **Timing**: Don't lunge at the ball — time the strike to when it leaves the opponent's palm and is at its highest point.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: One ball each, large circle (use center court circle), focus on not losing your own dribble first.
2. **Intermediate**: One ball each in smaller circles (key circle), add competitive pressure.
3. **Advanced**: Two balls each; King of the Circle format.

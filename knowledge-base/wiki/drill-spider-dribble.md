# Spider Dribble (Drill #22)

## Objective
Develop fingertip touch, hand-eye coordination, and hand quickness through rapid alternating front-to-back hand taps with the basketball held low between the legs.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1 per ball (~15 feet/4.5 m apart in team format)
- **Court area:** Stationary
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per player
- **Duration:** 30 seconds

## Execution

### Beginner (Live Dribble)
1. In a semi-crouched position, drop the ball directly below the crouch.
2. Begin with **right hand in front of right knee**, **left hand behind left knee**.
3. Tap one dribble with the right hand → quickly move right hand **behind** the right knee.
4. Simultaneously bring left hand **in front** of the left knee → tap the ball with the left hand → move left hand back behind the left knee.
5. Right hand (now behind right knee) taps the ball and moves to the front again.
6. Continue this rapid alternating front/back hand movement — each movement slightly taps the ball to keep the dribble alive.

### Option (No-Bounce Variation)
- Hold the ball with right hand in front and left hand behind the body, directly under the crouch.
- Release the ball and simultaneously move the right hand behind and the left hand in front.
- **Catch** the ball without allowing it to bounce — may toss it slightly upward for a better catch.
- Continue rapid hand movement throughout the drill.
- Players look directly ahead throughout — not at the ball.

## Coaching Points
- Use only the **first pads of the fingers** — not the palm
- Keep the ball **low** and dribble **rapidly**
- Feet spread **at least shoulder-width apart** — squat for a more rapid dribble if needed
- **Do not watch the dribble** — eyes straight ahead
- In the no-bounce option: players must look **directly ahead**, training peripheral hand-eye coordination

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Live dribble tapping between alternating front/back hand positions
2. **Intermediate:** No-bounce variation — catch and release without a bounce
3. **Advanced:** Increase speed; compete for maximum taps in 30 seconds

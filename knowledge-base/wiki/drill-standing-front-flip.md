# Standing Front Flip (Master Step)

## Objective
Achieve the ultimate calisthenics explosive benchmark — a standing bilateral forward aerial somersault — developing jump velocity, hip/waist explosiveness, arm speed, and plyometric landing absorption.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Flat open surface; padding strongly recommended until athlete is confident
- No equipment required
- NOTE: This is an expert-level exercise. Only attempt after mastering all prerequisite steps in the front flip chain.

## Execution
1. **Starting position**: Stand with feet less than shoulder-width apart, arms extended above the head.
2. Rise onto the toes to generate a small initial "bounce" and pre-stretch the calves.
3. Dip down at the knees and hips into a quick quarter-squat loading position.
4. Explosively drive the legs **upward** while simultaneously snapping the torso and arms **downward** — the opposing forces create mid-air rotation.
5. The instant the feet leave the floor, **tuck the knees tightly into the chest**. Speed of the tuck is everything in the standing version — athletes may grip their shins or knees to enhance the tuck position.
6. As rotation completes and your feet come over, **whip the legs out beneath you** rapidly.
7. Land on the **balls of the feet** with knees bent to absorb shock; extend arms forward for balance if needed.

## Coaching Points
- "Speed is of the essence — the standing flip is won or lost in the first 0.3 seconds."
- The upward leg push and downward arm/torso snap are simultaneous — not sequential.
- Tuck tighter = rotate faster. Gripping the shins helps maintain the tuck position.
- The gap in difficulty between the running front flip (Step 9) and the standing version is enormous — do not rush this transition.
- Use floor padding (back-drop front flip variation where you land feet then glutes) to drill the rotation without full commitment as a bridge step.
- Only one pathway to mastery: drill the easier steps over and over until the components are automatic.

## Progressions
1. **Regression**: Use thick padding; drill the "back-drop front flip" variation (land on feet then drop to glutes) to build rotation confidence.
2. **Intermediate**: Attempt on grass with light padding, focusing on height over speed.
3. **Advanced (Master)**: Execute on bare surface with clean feet-only landing, improving speed and form on each rep.

## What This Exercise Tests
According to [S9, p.164], the front flip tests ALL components of supreme human speed and agility simultaneously:
- **Jump velocity** (explosive leg drive)
- **Hip and waist explosiveness** (tucking)
- **Arm speed** (downward arm throw)
- **Plyometric force absorption** (landing mechanics)

> "Make no mistake — the front flip is THE explosive exercise par excellence." [S9, p.164]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Insufficient leg drive** → not enough height for full rotation; focus on maximally explosive upward push
2. **Sequential rather than simultaneous arm/leg action** → kills rotation; practice the arm snap separately
3. **Late tuck** → under-rotation; tuck knees the instant feet leave the ground
4. **Landing with stiff legs** → injury risk; absorb with bent knees on balls of feet
5. **Attempting too soon** → skipping prerequisite steps leads to dangerous falls; master the running front flip first

# Cooldown — Hip Flow

## Objective
Open the hips and quiet the glutes after a session of defensive slides, drives, and changes of direction.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A clean strip of court — sideline, baseline, or near the bench
- No equipment

## Execution
1. **Standing hip swings — 10 reps per leg.** Hold a wall or chair for balance. Swing one leg forward and back like a pendulum, low and easy. Switch legs. Cue: "Loose, not high — let the leg fall, do not throw it."
2. **Half-kneeling hip stretch — 45 seconds per side.** Drop into a lunge, back knee on the floor, front shin vertical. Squeeze the glute on the back leg and gently shift your hips forward until you feel a stretch in the front of the back hip. Switch sides. Cue: "Belt buckle up to the ceiling, ribs stacked over hips."
3. **Figure-four glute stretch — 45 seconds per side.** Sit on the floor, lie back, cross your right ankle over your left knee. Pull the left thigh toward your chest with both hands. Switch. Cue: "Keep the foot on top flexed — protects the knee."
4. **Seated butterfly — 60 seconds.** Soles of the feet together, knees fall open. Lean forward from the hips, not the low back. Cue: "Long spine, chest leads, hips melt."
5. **Lying knees-to-chest — 30 seconds.** On your back, hug both knees in. Rock side to side gently to massage the low back. Cue: "Slow rock, slow breath."
6. **Stand up tall, two breath cycles.** Reset before walking off the court.

## Coaching Points
- The half-kneeling hip stretch is the most useful position for guards who slide all practice. Spend the time there.
- "Glute squeeze" is the cue that lets players actually find the stretch in the hip flexor — without it, they arch the low back and miss the target.
- Watch for players who bounce in the butterfly — bouncing teaches the hip to fight the stretch.
- Pair with the breathing work in [[drill-cooldown-breath-recovery]] for a full down-regulation block.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Skip the figure-four if balance on the floor is uncomfortable; sub a standing version against a wall.
2. **Advanced**: Add a 30-second 90/90 hip switch — sit with both legs bent at 90 degrees, one in front and one to the side, then rotate hips to swap which leg is in front.

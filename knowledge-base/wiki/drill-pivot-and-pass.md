# Pivot-and-Pass Drill (Dribble–Pivot–Pass–Shoot)

## Objective
Develop the four-step pivot-and-pass sequence — two-foot jump stop, step-out, rip-through, and swing-around — while simultaneously training pass-receivers to catch off the inside heel and square up for a balanced jump shot.

## Setup
- **Players:** Groups of 3 (dribbler/passer, defender, pass-receiver/shooter); run multiple groups simultaneously.
- **Court area:** Half-court sideline to basket (four groups can work the right sideline simultaneously as shown in Diagram 1-50).
- **Equipment:** One ball per group.
- Groups are stacked along the sideline: dribbler/passer at bottom, defender beside them, pass-receiver/shooter higher toward the basket.

## Execution

### Station 1 — Dribbler/Passer
1. **Approach:** Dribble quickly and in a controlled manner, head up, in a semi-crouch stance toward the defender. Practice both hands.
2. **Step 1 — Two-Foot Jump Stop (Diagram 1-51):** As the dribbler kills the dribble, take a small bunny hop and land simultaneously on both feet. This allows either foot to become the pivot foot.
3. **Step 2 — Step-Out (Diagram 1-52):** Choose a pivot foot (e.g., left). Laterally step the free foot (right) toward the outside of the defender's opposite foot. Hold the ball firmly with both hands beside the free knee. Fake high and go low, or fake low and go high to pass around the defender.
4. **Step 3 — Rip-Through (Diagram 1-53):** If the defender counters, rip the ball low and hard across the shoe tops and front-pivot across the face of the defender. Attack the defender's opposite side with the same step-out-and-pass technique.
5. **Step 4 — Swing-Around (Diagram 1-54):** If the defender counters again, reverse pivot (away from the defender) off the same pivot foot and attack the defender's original lateral side. If all three steps are countered, reverse pivot and make an uncontested pass to a teammate or restart.

**Three Points of Emphasis for the Passer:**
- Protect the basketball beside the free knee at all times.
- Step outside the defender's foot (go east or west, not north or south).
- Fake a pass to make a pass — fake high, go low; fake low, go high.

### Station 2 — Defender
- Guards the dribbler/passer in a proper defensive stance.
- Works on defensive fundamentals and reactions only against the dribbler; does not defend the shooter.

### Station 3 — Pass-Receiver/Shooter
- **Stationary:** Start with the inside (pivot) heel already touching the floor.
- **On the move:** Chop up steps to time the inside heel hitting the floor just as the ball hits the shooting-hand palm.
- The heel-plant stops forward momentum completely.
- Once stopped, use the heel as the pivot point to swing the outside foot around and square up completely to the basket.
- Rise straight up and come back straight down — no floating in either direction.
- Shoot back to the original passer.

### Rotation (every 55 seconds)
- Dribbler/passer → defender station
- Defender → pass-receiver/shooter station
- Pass-receiver/shooter → dribbler/passer station
- Rotation takes less than 5 seconds; drill resumes immediately.
- After the third 55-second round, restart with all dribblers using the **right foot** as the pivot foot.

**Total time:** ~6 minutes for all three players to complete all three stations twice.

## Coaching Points
- "Step east or west — never north or south" when attacking the defender laterally.
- "Protect the ball beside the free knee" on every step-out and rip-through.
- "Fake high, go low — fake low, go high" on every pass attempt.
- Watch the shooter: "Rise straight up, come back straight down — no drift."
- Remind the receiver to chop steps on the move to time the heel-plant with the ball arrival.
- Defender must stay in a legal stance — no reaching, no fouling.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Stationary two-foot jump stop only; no defender. Work on the heel-plant catch and squared-up shot.
2. **Intermediate:** Add the defender and practice Step 2 (step-out) and Step 3 (rip-through) with a passive defender.
3. **Advanced:** Live defender at full intensity; all four steps available; passer reads which lane is open in real time.

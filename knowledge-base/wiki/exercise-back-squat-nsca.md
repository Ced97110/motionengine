# Back Squat (NSCA Basketball)

## Objective
Build bilateral lower-body strength in the glutes, hamstrings, and quadriceps — the primary muscles powering jumping, sprinting, cutting, and deceleration in basketball.

## Setup
- Power rack with barbell set at **shoulder height**
- Two spotters recommended for heavy loads
- Optional: box or cubed pad behind athlete for box squat variation; Hatfield bar for tall athletes
- Primary muscles: gluteus maximus, semimembranosus, semitendinosus, biceps femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris [S11, Ch.6]

## Execution

### Beginning Position (Athlete)
1. Set bar on rack at shoulder height
2. Grasp the bar and position on upper back in one of two positions:
   - **High bar**: bar rests at the base of the neck on the back of the shoulders (shirt collar)
   - **Low bar**: bar sits 2-3 inches (5-8 cm) below high bar — across posterior deltoids at middle of trapezius
3. Lift elbows to "set" the bar firmly in place on upper back and shoulders
4. Take small steps back from rack; feet **shoulder-width apart**, toes slightly pointed out

### Movement (Athlete)
1. Flex hips and knees in unison so the **torso-to-floor angle remains constant** throughout
2. Maintain **neutral spine, elbows up, chest up**
3. Continue descending until **top of thighs is approximately parallel to the floor**
4. Reverse: press through the feet, extend hips and knees at the same rate to maintain torso angle
5. Return to the beginning position

### Two-Spotter Protocol
- Spotters stand at the ends of the bar with underhand grip
- Help lift bar off rack, then release and stand ready with hands under bar ends
- Mirror athlete's squat movements, hands ready but not touching
- After the set: help return bar to rack

**Breathing:** Inhale on the way down; exhale after the sticking point on the way up [S11, Ch.6]

## Coaching Points
- "Pick a point on the wall before you squat — keep looking at it on the way down, then drive back up"
- "Keep the chest up throughout the movement"
- "Keep the weight over the heels and midfoot — never shift to the toes"
- Do not shrug or lean back at lockout
- Neutral head position throughout

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Box Squat)**: Place a box or cubed pad behind the athlete — squat to lightly touch the box without sitting; keeps the torso braced at contact; teaches correct depth without reaching failure in an unsafe position [S11, Ch.6]
2. **Intermediate**: Standard back squat to parallel, controlling tempo on descent
3. **Advanced (Hatfield Squat)**: Use a Hatfield bar (padded parallel arms draped over shoulders with neutral-grip handles) — helps taller athletes with longer arms maintain bar position more firmly on upper back [S11, Ch.6]

# 3-on-2 to 2-on-1 Transition Drill

## Objective
Train the basket man and ball man roles in a 3-on-2 fast-break defensive tandem, and teach how the defense transitions to a 2-on-1 counterattack after stopping or scoring.

## Setup
- **Players:** 5 per group (3 offensive players, 2 defenders); can chain multiple groups.
- **Court area:** Full court.
- **Equipment:** One basketball.

**Starting Method A (Simple):**
- Two defenders (X1, X2) seated at the sideline hash mark.
- Three offensive players (O1, O2, O3) on the baseline.
- Ball is advanced; defenders scramble up and sprint back.

**Starting Method B (Game-Realistic, Diagram 1-62):**
- 3-on-3 at one end: X3, X4, X5 (with ball) vs. O1, O2, O3.
- X1 and X2 start on the perimeter.
- X3, X4, X5 make 1-2 passes then shoot and miss (controlled).
- O1, O2, O3 box out, secure the rebound, and run a 3-man fast break.
- X1 and X2 sprint back as fast as possible, communicating basket man / ball man roles.
- X3, X4, X5 step off the court.

## Execution

### 3-on-2 Defensive Tandem (Diagrams 1-61 through 1-65)

1. **First defender back** sprints to the **middle of the free throw lane** and calls out **"Basket!"** — becomes the Basket Man.
2. **Second defender back** positions in the lane up to the top of the key and calls out **"Ball!"** — becomes the Ball Man.
3. **Ball Man** stops the dribbler's penetration toward the rim. Forces the ball handler to pick up the dribble or pass.
4. **Basket Man** protects the blocks and is responsible for both the first and second perimeter passes.
5. When the ball is passed to either wing:
   - **Basket Man rotates out** to defend the wing ball (Diagram 1-63).
   - **Ball Man drops immediately** down the lane to protect the basket.
6. This rotation continues: the two defenders alternate ball/basket responsibilities with every wing pass (Diagram 1-64).
7. Defenders must **buy time** for their three remaining teammates to sprint back on defense.

### 2-on-1 Counter (after the 3-on-2 ends)
- If defense forces a turnover or secures a defensive rebound after a shot, the two defenders (now 2 players with the ball) immediately go the other way 2-on-1 against one of the original offensive players.
- The remaining two offensive players are the trailer defenders — they sprint back.
- The 2-on-1 is then run the full length of the court.

## Coaching Points
- "Loud communication is mandatory — yell Basket or Ball the moment you know your role."
- Ball Man: "Stop penetration first — force the pass. Do NOT commit fully to the dribbler."
- Basket Man: "Protect both blocks — you own the first AND second perimeter passes."
- On wing pass: "Basket Man rotates OUT, Ball Man drops IN — automatic, no hesitation."
- Defenders: "Buy time — buy time — the other three are coming."
- Stress that in a **turnover** situation, no predetermined basket man / ball man assignment exists — whoever gets back first takes the basket.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Walk through tandem rotations without a live ball; coach points to where the pass goes.
2. **Intermediate:** Live 3-on-2 with seated start; defenders must scramble and communicate.
3. **Advanced:** Method B start (3-on-3 with box-out and outlet); add the 2-on-1 counter after every possession.

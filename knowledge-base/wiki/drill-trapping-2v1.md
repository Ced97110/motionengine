# Trapping Drill (2-on-1 Contain)

## Objective
Teach two defenders to trap and contain one ball handler without fouling, being split, or allowing a sideline/baseline beat — the core execution of Herb Brown's trapping system. [S1, p.165]

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 offensive ball handler, 2 defenders, 1 coach
- **Court area**: Backcourt (backcourt version) OR near a corner on the front court (front-court version)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Coach passes the ball to the offensive player near a corner (front court) or in the backcourt.
2. Two defenders (X4 and X1) immediately converge at a right angle to trap the ball handler.
3. Defenders come from the blind side if possible — *not* from directly behind.
4. Trappers seal with inside legs crossed to close the gap and prevent splitting.
5. Trappers use hands up to trace, deflect, or steal — chest contact to apply pressure (no arm fouling).
6. The ball handler tries to survive the trap: dribble out, change direction, or pass if a passer is available.
7. **Backcourt version**: defenders try to contain the dribbler for 8-10 seconds without fouling.
8. **Front-court version**: defenders try to trap and contain without allowing the ball handler to split them or beat them on either side of the court.
9. Rotate through offense and defense so all players practice both roles. [S1, p.165]

## Coaching Points
- **"Come at a right angle, from the blind side"** — not behind; blindside traps are harder to read and escape. [S1, p.152]
- **"Inside legs crossed"** — closing the gap between the two trappers prevents splitting the trap. [S1, p.162]
- **"Bump with your chest, not your hands"** — body contact is legal; reaching in creates fouls and gaps. [S1, p.162]
- **"Hands up — trace the ball"** — active hands force lob passes that interceptors can steal. [S1, p.162]
- **"Force over the outside shoulder"** — trappers must angle their bodies so the only available pass goes outward, never to the middle (gut pass). [S1, p.152]
- Watch for defensive players who step back instead of closing — that gap is how offensive players split. Correct immediately.
- Watch for defenders who lunge with arms — foul trouble destroys trapping effectiveness.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 2-on-1 with a stationary ball handler who can only pivot (no dribble). Defenders must get level and touch the ball handler with both chests.
2. **Intermediate**: Add live dribble (as described above) in the backcourt for 8 seconds. Timer creates pressure.
3. **Advanced**: Add a third offensive player as an outlet receiver. The two defenders must both trap AND deny the outlet pass — tests the trap's integrity and teaches defenders to force over the outside shoulder while sealing the middle.

# Flex Back Screen Footwork

## Summary
The flex back screen is the first of two off-ball screens in the flex man offense, triggered when 01 reverses the ball to 05. The screener (04) sets a baseline back screen for 02, then immediately front pivots and steps up the lane line to set a flare back screen for 01. This two-screen sequence creates three simultaneous scoring threats — 02 (baseline cutter), 01 (flare cutter to the weakside wing/corner), and 04 (screener slip or catch at the elbow) — making it extremely difficult to defend. [S6, pp.244-250]

## When to Use
- In the flex man offense whenever the ball is reversed
- When the defense is helping on the baseline cut and leaving the screener open
- When the defense keys on only one player and leaves the perimeter or the screener vulnerable
- As a platform for multiple counters including screen-the-screener, veer option, and bump option

## Key Principles
1. **Screener faces the cutter**: As soon as 01 reverses the ball to 05, 04 must pivot so his back points toward the middle of the lane — giving the proper screening angle to block X2. [S6, p.245]
2. **Front pivot after screen**: After 02 uses the back screen and cuts baseline, 04 immediately front-pivots off the higher foot (right foot) — the lower foot swings through — to quickly locate the ball, the new passer (05), and X1. [S6, pp.246-247]
3. **Three scoring threats**: The front pivot allows 04 to step up the lane line and flare-screen X1 for 01. This creates: 02 (inside), 01 (flare cut to weak-side corner), and 04 (slip or elbow shot). [S6, p.247]
4. **Perimeter spacing**: The flare screen forces X4 and X1 to stay on the perimeter, reducing lane congestion and freeing 02 inside. [S6, p.247]
5. **Catch-ready footwork**: 01 should V-cut inside first (to set the defender up for the flare screen), plant the inside foot, and scrape off 04's flare screen toward the weakside wing/corner — opening to the ball and basket simultaneously for a quick shot. [S6, pp.247-248]
6. **04 as third option**: After setting the flare screen, 04 can slip to the elbow or step into the lane. On catch from 05, 04 front-pivots off the inside (right) heel to square up to the basket. [S6, p.249]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF (04 — Back Screener)**: Pivot to face 02 on ball reversal; set back screen; immediately front-pivot off higher foot; step up lane line to flare-screen X1; on a ball receipt, pivot off inside heel to shoot.
- **SG (02 — Primary Cutter)**: Cut baseline off 04's back screen; understand you are transitioning from perimeter player to post player; use post-catch footwork in the lane.
- **PG (01 — Flare Cutter)**: After reversing to 05, V-cut inside to set defender up; plant inside foot; scrape off 04's flare screen to weakside wing/corner; be shot-ready on arrival — square up off inside foot, hands ready.
- **C (05 — New Passer/Ball-Handler)**: Read all three options; deliver the ball to the right player at the right time.

## Variations
### Traditional Flex (Screen-the-Screener)
01 reverses to 05, then goes down to set a down screen for 04 in a screen-the-screener action. 04 front-pivots off the top foot after the back screen, breaks up the lane line, times 01's down screen, and pivots off the inside heel to square up for a medium-range shot before X4 can recover. [S6, pp.249-251]

### Flex Veer Option
Used when the defense overplays the baseline cutter. 03 cuts off 05's back screen on the low side, giving 05 space to duck in high side — exploiting the position advantage on X5. [S6, pp.252-256] → see [[concept-flex-veer-option]]

### Flex Bump Option
01, approaching 04's planned back screen, reads X4 and physically bumps 04's shoulder to communicate the direction of the reverse pivot and duck-in. [S6, pp.257-261] → see [[concept-flex-bump-option]]

## Common Mistakes
1. **04 faces wrong direction on the back screen** → Must have back pointing toward the middle of the lane to get the correct angle on X2; wrong angle = no screen.
2. **04 pauses after setting the screen** → Must immediately front-pivot and move up the lane line; delay kills the second screening action and reduces the timing for 01's flare cut.
3. **01 does not V-cut before the flare** → Without the inside V-cut setup, X1 can easily follow the flare cut and the defender isn't pinned behind 04.
4. **02 stays on the perimeter mentally** → 02 must understand he is now a post player in the lane and use proper interior catch-and-score footwork.
5. **04 slips too early** → If 04 slips his own flare screen before X4 steps inside, the timing advantage is lost; must wait until X4 cheats inside before slipping.

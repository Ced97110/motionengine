# Jab Step Progression Drill

## Objective
Build the complete jab step skill set from stationary basics through on-the-move application with both feet, culminating in live defender reads and competitive scoring.

## Setup
- 1-2 players per basket
- Half court; seven shooting spots around the arc (elbows, wings, corners, top)
- Ball (use spinout for solo work or partner passing for pair work)
- Optional: cones to mark wing areas

## Execution

### Stage 1 — Stationary Jab-Rise-Shoot
1. Stand at one of the seven shooting spots in a balanced triple-threat stance
2. Jab with the **shooting foot** — only 1-2 inches forward, directly at an imaginary defender
3. Pause and read: imaginary defender retreats → rise straight up into a balanced jump shot
4. Shoot for makes, shooting strings (e.g., 5 in a row), or three-strikes style
5. Repeat from all seven spots [S5, p.104]

### Stage 2 — Straight-On Catch Jab-Rise-Shoot
1. Receive a pass (from partner or spinout) straight on at a shooting spot
2. Use a one-two step catch → **pause** (critical — do not one-two step directly into the shot) → jab → rise and shoot
3. The pause creates time to read the defender; imaginary or live defender retreats → shoot
4. Work all seven spots from midrange; then extend to three-point range [S5, p.104]

### Stage 3 — On-the-Move Jab (Wing Work)
1. From either wing, spin the ball out toward the **baseline** → catch on the inside pivot foot → jab → choose action (shoot, jab again, go)
2. From either wing, spin the ball out toward the **middle** → catch on inside pivot foot → jab → choose action
3. Repeat from both sides of the floor (4 total directions)
4. Note: when spinning toward the middle on the right wing (right-handed player), the shooting foot becomes the pivot foot → practice the **an elite scorer-style nonshooting-foot jab** [S5, pp.103-104]

### Stage 4 — Jab-and-Go and Jab-Jab Combos
1. Drill the **jab-jab-jumper**: jab → imaginary defender stays → jab again → defender retreats → shoot; keep both jabs short
2. Drill the **jab-jab-and-go**: jab → defender stays → jab again → defender freezes → drive past with a long last step along the defender's hip; use arm bar to protect ball; decide: pull-up or layup [S5, pp.105-107]

### Stage 5 — Live Defender Reads
1. Defender closes out after passing the ball
2. Offensive player reads: if defender closes right → jab left and go or jab and shoot; if defender stays → jab-jab-jumper or jab-jab-and-go
3. Keep competitive score; optionally **deduct points for incorrect reads** (e.g., jabbing into the defender rather than away from the closeout) [S5, p.104]

## Coaching Points
- "Jab short and jab *at* the defender — never to the side and never overstriding" [S5, p.103]
- Watch for players one-two-stepping directly into their shot without pausing — enforce the pause
- Cue for nonshooting-foot jab: "It feels awkward at first — that's OK. Drill it until it's natural"
- Remind players: always jab with the shooting foot first before advancing to the nonshooting foot
- Insist on proper landing: balanced, shooting foot slightly forward, toe to the target, knees bent

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary jab only; one foot; seven spots; no defender
2. **Intermediate**: On-the-move catch from wings; both sides; shooting foot jab
3. **Advanced**: Nonshooting-foot jab; jab-jab combos; live defender; competitive scoring with point deductions for bad reads

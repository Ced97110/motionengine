# Spin Screen Move (Interior Back Screen for Post)

## Summary
The spin screen move is described as "almost unbeatable" for getting a post player the ball in the lane. It is a two-player action: a weak-side player (often a smaller offensive teammate) sets a **spin (back) screen** on the ball-side post defender when the ball is reversed to the top of the key. This frees the original ball-side post player to receive the ball deep in the lane for maximum scoring opportunities. [S6, pp.180]

Unlike the individual rip, swim, and contact-reverse pivot moves, the spin screen move requires coordinated teammate action — making it a significant upgrade in difficulty for defenders to counter.

## When to Use
- After a ball reversal to the top of the key from the wing
- When the ball-side post defender is denying the post effectively and individual moves alone are insufficient
- When a smaller/quicker teammate can screen the post defender (size mismatch advantage for the screener)
- In half-court offense from any set where the weak-side post player can set an interior back screen

## Key Principles
1. **Weak-side post player (02) initiates the screen** when the ball is reversed to the top of the key by setting a spin (back) screen on the ball-side post defender (X5). [S6, p.180]
2. **A smaller teammate as spin screener** is advantageous — it is harder for a big defender to navigate around a smaller, quicker screener without fouling. [S6, p.180]
3. **The screen is an interior back screen** — the screener's back is to the basket/defender, creating a seal on the high side of the post defender.
4. **Post player (05) reads the screen** and uses it to flash or duck in to the low block on the ball side to receive the entry pass.
5. **Ball-side post position:** After using the screen, 05 should post up at or above the first notch above the block — not below the block — to maintain the best drop-step angle. [S6, p.180]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF/C (05 — ball-side post):** Read the ball reversal to the top; use the back screen to duck in or flash to the low block; catch and post up at or above the first notch.
- **PF/C/SF (02 — weak-side screener):** Set the spin (back) screen on the ball-side post defender when the ball is reversed; hold the screen long enough for the post player to use it.
- **PG (01):** Reverse the ball to the top of the key to trigger the action; make the entry pass to 05 once the screen takes effect.

## Variations
### Smaller Teammate as Screener
When a guard (SG or SF) serves as the spin screener instead of a big, the mismatch forces the post defender to choose between navigating around a quicker player or fouling — creating even more difficulty than when two big players are involved. [S6, p.180]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Screener sets the screen too early** → The spin screen should be triggered by the ball reversal, not before; timing is everything.
2. **Post player doesn't read the screen** → 05 must see and use the screen, not drift away from it.
3. **Posting up below the block** → Always post at or above the first notch for best drop-step options both directions.
4. **Screener leaves the screen too soon** → Hold the screen until 05 has cleared the defender, then roll or seal.

# Shooting Mechanics (Wissel)

## Summary
Hal Wissel presents a six-component sequential shooting mechanics system: Sight → Balance → Leg Motion → Hand Position → Elbow-In Alignment → Follow-Through. Each component must flow rhythmically into the next. Good mechanics without being mechanical is the goal — shots should be smooth and rhythmic, not robotic. Focus on only one or two mechanics at a time during practice to avoid overcomplicating the motion [S7, pp.6-8].

The system applies to all shots — catch-and-shoot, off the dribble, jump shots, three-pointers, and free throws — with minor adjustments for each situation.

## When to Use
- Introducing shooting fundamentals to any player
- Diagnosing and correcting specific shooting errors
- Building a consistent shot from scratch
- Pre-season shooting clinics and individual workout design

## Key Principles

### 1. Sight
- Eyes focus on the front of the rim (just over it) for all shots except bank shots [S7, p.6]
- Bank shots aim for the **top near corner of the box** on the backboard at a **45-degree angle** (the "45-degree funnel") [S7, p.6]
- See the target as early as possible; maintain focus until the ball reaches the goal
- **Never** follow the ball's flight through the air or watch the defender's hand
- "Concentrating on the target helps eliminate distractions" [S7, p.6]

### 2. Balance
- Feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing straight ahead
- Shooting-foot (strong-side foot) **forward**; align the toe of the back foot with the heel of the front foot (toe-to-heel relation) [S7, p.7]
- Knees flexed — failure to flex = throwing from behind the head or shoving from the hip
- Head over waist and feet, slightly forward
- Shoulders relaxed, inclined toward the basket

### 3. Leg Motion
- **Down-and-up action** — bend knees, then fully extend them
- Say "down and up" from shot start to release as a trigger
- Legs and shooting arm move **together** — as legs go up, arm goes up
- At full leg extension, back/shoulders/arm extend smoothly forward and upward
- Use the down-and-up leg motion for rhythm, NOT lowering the ball
- Keeping the ball high = quicker release, fewer errors [S7, p.7]

### 4. Hand Position — Block and Tuck
- **Block and tuck**: shooting hand **behind** the ball facing the rim; nonshooting hand **under** the ball
- Both hands relaxed, fingers spread comfortably; thumb of shooting hand relaxed (not spread wide)
- Ball rests on **finger pads**, not the palm — creates a natural cup
- Nonshooting (balance) hand: elbow points slightly back and to the side, weight of ball on ring and little fingers
- Shooting hand: index finger at the midpoint of the ball — ball releases off the index finger
- Ball held **between ear and shoulder** on the shooting side, above the shoulder [S7, p.7]

### 5. Elbow-In Alignment
- Shooting elbow **in** = ball aligned with the basket
- If a player lacks flexibility, first get shooting hand behind the ball and facing front, THEN move elbow as far in as flexibility allows [S7, p.8]
- Elbow out = follow-through goes right (for right-handed shooter)

### 6. Follow-Through
- After releasing off the index finger, keep shooting arm **up** and fully extended
- Index finger points **straight to the target**
- Shooting hand palm faces **slightly down**; balance hand palm faces **slightly up**
- Eyes remain on the target
- Hold the arm in complete follow-through until the ball reaches the basket, THEN react to rebound or defend [S7, p.8]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Master all six components in sequence; identify and focus on one or two mechanics per practice session rather than all at once.

## Variations
### Jump Shot
- Align ball above shoulder between ear and shoulder; forearm at right angle to floor
- Jump straight up — fully extend ankles, knees, back, shoulders
- Land in balance in the same spot as takeoff (no floating)
- **Inside jump shot**: jump then shoot at the top of the jump; arm/wrist/fingers provide most force
- **Outside jump shot**: lift ball simultaneously with upward extension of legs, back, shoulder [S7, p.11]
- When closely guarded: jump higher than the defender, align ball a little higher [S7, p.11]

### Three-Point Shot
- Set up far enough behind the line to avoid stepping on it
- Use more leg force for power, less for height — "feel that you're shooting the ball as you jump, not at the top"
- Smooth rhythm and complete follow-through are especially critical at long range
- Can step into the shot for additional range
- Land in balance in the same spot [S7, p.11]

### Quick Release / Shooting Off the Catch
- Give passer a good target: hands above shoulders in shooting position, knees slightly flexed
- Block-and-tuck the catch: shooting hand behind ball facing rim, balance hand under ball — **never** catch with hands on sides and rotate
- Jump behind the ball on slightly off passes
- "Down" comes just before the catch; shot goes up as legs extend — one-count motion [S7, pp.12-13]
- On passes from strong-hand side: far hand (nonshooting) blocks the pass, reset nonshooting hand under ball
- On passes from weak-hand side: shooting hand blocks the pass, tuck nonshooting hand under, reset shooting hand

### Shooting Off the Dribble
- When open: dribble to front of shooting knee, pick ball up facing basket
- Shooting hand on **top** of ball, nonshooting hand under — this ensures hand faces rim during lift
- Never pick up with hands on sides (causes side-spin)
- Pick up with knees flexed to prevent floating [S7, pp.12-13]
- To strong-hand side: one long dribble with outside hand past defender, jump behind ball, pick up, shoot
- To weak-hand side: second (crossover) dribble to front of shooting knee, jump behind, pick up, shoot

### Step-Back Jump Shot
- Read defender; fake drive
- Quick step back with outside foot simultaneous with outside-hand dribble back
- Jump behind ball, pick up in front of shooting knee; shoot
- Exaggerate follow-through of shoulders/head/shooting hand toward basket to counter any lean-back tendency [S7, p.13]

### Shot Fake Step-Through Jump Shot
- Make an aggressive shot fake; read defender's hand
- If defender's hand is up on ball-side: step through with inside foot past defender's lead foot; move head and shoulders under defender's arm
- Protect ball with head/shoulders, move away from defender's reach; shoot jump shot
- Expect contact — be strong for possible three-point play [S7, p.14]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ball short** → More leg force; complete follow-through; maintain even rhythm
2. **Ball long** → Higher arc (45-60°); head/shoulders forward-upward on follow-through
3. **Inconsistent short/long** → Full elbow extension on follow-through (not leaning back)
4. **Right rim miss (right-hander)** → Square up; set ball on shooting side of head between ear and shoulder; elbow in
5. **Left rim miss (right-hander)** → Square up; stop shoving from hip; use legs for power; arm/wrist/fingers straight to basket
6. **Side-spin (ball circles out)** → Don't lower ball for rhythm; shooting hand must start behind ball facing front; release off index finger; practice keeping nonshooting thumb and index squeezed together
7. **Shots hit rim hard (palm contact)** → Relax shooting thumb; set ball on finger pads, palm off ball
8. **Eyes following ball** → Fix eyes on target (just over front of rim) until ball reaches basket
9. **Reaching for the ball** → Give good target with hands up; jump behind the ball
10. **Lowering ball before shooting** → Catch in position; keep ball high; shoot in one motion
11. **Floating after dribble** → Pick ball up in front of shooting knee with knees flexed; convert horizontal to upward momentum [S7, pp.14-16]

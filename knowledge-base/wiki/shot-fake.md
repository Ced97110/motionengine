# Shot Fake

## Summary
A shot fake is a ball-faking maneuver in which the offensive player simulates the beginning of a shooting motion to trigger the defender's natural shot-blocking instinct, causing the defender to leave their feet or lunge. Once the defender is out of position, the offensive player either shoots immediately (if the defender didn't bite) or goes by for a pull-up or drive to the rim.

The shot fake is one of the two primary fakes used to create shots (the other being the jab step). Its effectiveness relies entirely on it looking exactly like a real shot — same speed, same lift, same stance. A shot fake that "looks like a fake" is ineffective. [S5, pp.96-99]

## When to Use
- When a defender closes out hard and is likely to jump to contest
- When the offensive player has caught the ball in a triple-threat position and wants to create a driving lane
- As the setup for a show-and-go (shot-fake then drive)
- In late-clock situations where a direct shot is not available
- Off catches from spinouts and pass-and-catch scenarios at game speed

## Key Principles
1. **Think shot first** — always be in a balanced, athletic shooting position before deploying the fake; this makes the fake look real [S5, p.96]
2. **Stay in triple-threat position** — knees bent, ball at shot pocket, feet set; the fake flows naturally from a prepared shooting stance [S5, p.96]
3. **Lift the ball to head height** — raise the ball exactly as in a real shot attempt; this is what sells the fake to the defender [S5, p.96]
4. **Keep your feet still** — do not take a "negative step" (stepping away from the basket) when lifting the ball; feet remain planted so you can immediately shoot or drive [S5, pp.96-97]
5. **Execute at game speed** — the fake must be performed at the same speed as a real shot; too fast and the defender doesn't have time to react; too slow and it looks fake [S5, p.98]
6. **Stay in your shot line** — bring the ball up on the fake and return it directly to the shot pocket, then immediately go into the real shot if shooting [S5, p.98]
7. **Stay low throughout** — if you straighten up on the fake, you must reload (bend again) before driving or shooting, giving the defender recovery time [S5, p.98]
8. **"A fake is not a fake if it looks like a fake"** — the fake must be indistinguishable from an actual shot attempt [S5, p.98]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Shot fakes apply to any player who catches the ball and may be contested, from guards on the perimeter to post players in the low post

## Variations
### Shot Fake → Immediate Shot (Defender Doesn't React)
If the defender does not leave their feet on the shot fake, the offensive player immediately goes into the real shot. This reads as a delayed shooting opportunity. [S5, p.96]

### Shot Fake → Show-and-Go Drive
If the defender jumps on the fake, the offensive player drives by using a show-and-go move (covered in detail under [[show-and-go]]). [S5, pp.98-100]

### Shot Fake → Pull-Up Bank Shot
Combine a shot fake with a dribble drive toward the basket, finishing with a pull-up bank shot at 12–15 feet. A high-percentage compound move. [S5, p.86]

## Shot Fake Drill Progressions
1. **Stationary catch, top of the key** — receive the ball, shot-fake, shoot. Drill for a set number of makes or "strings" style.
2. **Spinout or straight-on pass-and-catch from multiple spots** — work from all seven spots (baseline corners, wings, top) at mid-range then long range.
3. **On-the-move catch, inside pivot foot, from either wing** — spin out toward the baseline then toward the middle; 10 makes in each direction = 40 makes total from both sides.
4. **Pass-and-catch competitive closeout** — two players alternate shots, one closes out; first to a set number wins; loser runs a sprint. [S5, pp.97-99]

## Common Mistakes
1. **The negative step** — stepping away from the basket when lifting the ball; can be called as a travel; also limits corner use due to out-of-bounds risk → keep feet planted [S5, pp.96-97]
2. **Faking too fast** — the defender has no time to react, making the fake useless → slow the fake to match real shot speed [S5, p.98]
3. **Straightening up on the fake** → Stay in the athletic stance throughout; being low is essential for the subsequent shot or drive [S5, p.98]
4. **Shot fake looks different from a real shot** — telegraphing the fake → practice fakes in the exact same motion as real shots so they are indistinguishable [S5, p.98]
5. **Only practicing fakes in coach-directed drills** — leads to unnatural, cumbersome fakes in games → individual players must drill fakes daily on their own time [S5, p.98]

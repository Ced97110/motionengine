# Half Kip (Step 6 of Kip-Up Chain)

## Objective
Fuse the leg-arc (Step 4) and torso-throw (Step 5) into a more explosive single pattern until the athlete can land on both feet — even momentarily — before tipping back.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat open floor or mat
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Sit on the floor with knees bent and heels on the ground.
2. Roll back and swing legs up while placing hands in the **bridge position** (by ears, fingers pointing toward feet).
3. Roll onto upper-back and shoulders. Squeeze into a tight ball with bent knees close to the head.
4. **"Unravel"** — explode legs in an arc UP then DOWN while pushing DOWN through palms hard enough to lift them off the ground.
5. As feet touch down, **continue throwing torso and hands forward**.
6. **Land on both feet**, even if only for a fraction of a second, before falling back onto butt.
7. Roll back and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- This step is about fusing two previously separate patterns (arc + throw) into one explosive action
- Athletes often "discover" this step by accident while trying Step 7 — tipping backwards IS the half kip
- "Catching" a stable object or a partner's hands can verify the feel of the landing position, but true progress comes from repetition
- Do NOT over-rely on partner assists — the body must learn to generate the extra power through drilling
- Center of gravity must project forward enough to momentarily balance on both feet

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Barely touch feet before sitting back — just confirm feet are reaching the ground
2. **Intermediate**: Hold the squat for 1 full second before sitting back
3. **Advanced**: Hold the squat fully and rise to stand = [[drill-kip-up]] achieved [S9, p.6]

# Basketball Medical Team Structure

## Summary
Elite basketball programs require a multidisciplinary medical team working in close collaboration to prevent injuries, maintain player health, and facilitate safe return to play. The structure varies by level (European club, NCAA college, NBA) but shares core elements: a head team physician, athletic trainers/physiotherapists, strength and conditioning coaches, and ancillary specialists. Effective collaboration between all staff — including coaches — is the cornerstone of optimal player health management [S10, pp.126-145].

## When to Use
- Organizing a new athletic program's medical infrastructure
- Establishing communication protocols between medical and coaching staff
- Planning preseason medical screenings
- Managing in-season injury workflows

## Key Principles
1. **Interdisciplinary collaboration** is non-negotiable — physicians, physiotherapists, S&C coaches, nutritionists, and mental health staff must communicate daily
2. **The head team physician** coordinates care among all providers and makes final return-to-play decisions
3. **Athletic trainers/physiotherapists** are the daily frontline — they should detect subtle changes (muscle stiffness, joint swelling) before a player complains
4. **The S&C coach** is responsible for bringing the player back to the court after injury, progressing load systematically
5. **The nutritionist** manages in-season and travel nutrition, monitors body weight/fat%, and approves supplement use in coordination with WADA regulations
6. **Sports psychologists** address mental health and performance psychology — free throw imagery, mindfulness, anxiety management
7. All products consumed by players must be approved by the team physician to ensure WADA compliance [S10, p.126]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Report any pain, stiffness, or abnormal sensations to the physiotherapist promptly — the medical team cannot help what they do not know about

## Medical Staff Roles by Level

### Elite European Club
- **Head physician** (orthopedic surgeon): diagnoses injuries, approves return to play, heads triage
- **Head physiotherapist**: daily maintenance, injury triage, bracing/taping, detects subclinical issues
- **Second physiotherapist**: supports head PT during practices and games
- **Strength & conditioning coach**: preseason conditioning, in-season load management, rehabilitation progression
- **Nutritionist**: in-season diet, away-game menus, supplement oversight, body composition monitoring
- **Orthopedic surgeon**: screening, MRI interpretation, surgical decisions
- **Consulting specialists**: dentist and ophthalmologist are specifically noted as essential for basketball [S10, p.128]

### College (NCAA)
- **Team physician** (primary care sports medicine or orthopedic): primary provider, sideline coverage, coordinates specialists
- **Certified athletic trainers**: daily care, injury evaluation, treatment protocols, medical liaison between athletes/coaches/specialists
- **S&C coach**: personalized programs covering strength, endurance, flexibility, coordination, speed, agility, power
- **Sports psychologist**: mental health, performance psychology (progressive relaxation, visual imagery, mindfulness for FT shooting) [S10, pp.135-136]

### NBA
- **Head team physician** (primary care or orthopedic sports medicine fellow): present at all home games
- **Additional team physicians**: subspecialty coverage
- **Team dentist**: present at all home games
- **Head athletic trainer + assistants**: daily frontline care
- **Physical therapist**: rehabilitation
- **S&C specialist**: performance optimization
- **Massage therapist**: recovery
- **Sports performance psychologist**: mental wellness and performance
- **Director of sports performance** (some teams): liaison between management and medical staff
- **Consulting physicians** (ad hoc): cardiology, neurology, psychiatry, orthopedic subspecialties (foot/ankle, hand, spine), ophthalmology, pulmonology [S10, pp.145-146]

## Variations
### Local Organizing Committee (LOC) for Major Events
For events like EuroBasket, a Chief Medical Officer (CMO) and LOC must be formed months in advance. Responsibilities include: venue/site management, hotel clinics, accreditations, emergency action plans, VIP and spectator medical services, and a nutritionist to prevent food/water contamination in team hotels. A medical information guide sheet is distributed to all visiting medical staffs at least 1 week before the event [S10, pp.130-133].

## Common Mistakes
1. **Siloed communication** → Establish clear daily check-in protocols between PT, physician, S&C, and coaches
2. **Ignoring subclinical signs** → Physiotherapists must actively assess all players, not wait for complaints
3. **WADA non-compliance** → All supplements must be physician-approved; nutritionist advises but physician approves
4. **Return-to-play without full clearance** → Only return when completely pain-free with full ROM; exceptions require explicit physician-coach-player agreement

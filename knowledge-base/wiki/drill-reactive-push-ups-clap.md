# Reactive Push-Ups With Clap

## Objective
Improve upper-body power and reaction to a stimulus by performing synchronized clap push-ups with a partner. [S8, p.186]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Both in push-up position, heads facing the same direction
- Positioned approximately 1 yard (0.9 meters) apart

## Execution
1. Both athletes assume a standard push-up position with heads facing the same direction and spaced 1 yard apart.
2. Athlete 1 performs a push-up with a clap at the top.
3. Athlete 2 reacts to Athlete 1's movement and attempts to perform their push-up and clap at exactly the same time.
4. Continue in sequence, with Athlete 2 always reacting to Athlete 1's initiation.

## Coaching Points
- Cue: "Watch your partner out of the corner of your eye — react the instant they move."
- The reactor must minimize lag time between seeing the partner's push-up and matching it.
- Full push-up range of motion required — chest to the floor.
- Clap should be crisp and quick — not a hang at the top.
- Watch for: partner 2 initiating before partner 1, incomplete range of motion, or slow reaction lag.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard partner push-ups without clap, finding synchronization rhythm.
2. **Intermediate**: Clap push-ups with reaction as described.
3. **Advanced**: Reactor must also be given a secondary task (e.g., touch a cone) after the push-up set.

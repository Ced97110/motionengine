# Box Jump (Knee Rehabilitation)

## Objective
Restore explosive jumping ability with reduced joint impact forces by landing on top of a box rather than the floor, allowing a player to output maximum power during the final phase of jumper's knee rehabilitation.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Plyometric box
- Start height: 12 inches (30 cm); increase gradually

## Execution
1. Stand erect facing the box. Start with a 12-inch (30 cm) box and gradually increase height.
2. Lower into a squat by extending the hips and bending the knees.
3. Reverse direction explosively and jump vertically as high as possible. Land softly on top of the box.
4. Perform the prescribed number of box jumps [S2, p.147].

## Coaching Points
- "Land softly" — a soft, controlled landing on top of the box is the therapeutic goal; crashing down defeats the purpose
- "Vertical jumping onto a box allows you to output a high level of power but lowers the impact forces placed on the lower extremities" [S2, p.147]
- This is the final exercise in the jumper's knee progression — the player must have passed the step-down readiness test before beginning
- Step down from the box (do not jump down) between repetitions to minimize cumulative landing stress early in the progression
- Connect to the game: "This exercise restores your ability to explode while on the basketball court, whether accelerating, jumping for a rebound, or shooting the ball" [S2, p.147]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 12-inch (30 cm) box, low rep volume (3-5 reps), step down after each jump
2. **Intermediate**: Increase box height, higher rep volume
3. **Advanced**: Maximum height box, continuous jumps, progress to flat-ground jumping [S2, p.147]

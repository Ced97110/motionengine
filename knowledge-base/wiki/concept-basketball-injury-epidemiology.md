# Basketball Injury Epidemiology

## Summary
Understanding basketball injury patterns across demographic groups enables the medical team to prepare targeted prevention programs, preseason screening protocols, and in-season monitoring systems. Overall injury incidence ranges from **1.94 to 9.9 per 1,000 player hours (AE)** depending on level of play and setting. Lower limb injuries account for the majority of all basketball injuries [S10, p.617].

## When to Use
- Designing preseason screening programs
- Prioritizing medical resources during the season
- Educating coaching staff on injury risk patterns
- Planning strength and conditioning programs to address common injury sites

## Key Principles

### Injury Distribution by Body Region
1. **Lower limbs** — most common
2. Head and neck — second most common
3. Upper limb — third
4. Spine and pelvis — least common of the four main regions [S10, p.617]

### NCAA Injury Surveillance — Most Common Injuries [S10, p.618]

**Men:**
- Ankle sprain: 26.2%
- Knee injury: 7.4%
- Upper leg contusion: 3.9%
- Patellar injury: 3.7%
- Concussion: 3.6%
- Muscle strain lower back: 2.0%
- Muscle strain pelvis/hip: 1.9%

**Women:**
- Ankle sprain: 24.6%
- Knee injury: 15.9%
- Concussion: 6.5%
- Patellar injury: 2.4%
- Nose fracture: 1.7%
- Shoulder subluxation: 1.4%
- Muscle strain lower back: 1.3%

### High School Level Patterns
- Game injuries > practice injuries: 3.27 vs. 1.4 per 1,000 AE
- Women have **higher** injury rates than men: 2.08 vs. 1.82 per 1,000 AE
- Same game > practice pattern holds at collegiate level [S10, p.617]

### NBA Shoulder Injury Incidence (2013/14–2018/19)
- Players with shoulder injury: 1–3% per season
- Players missing ≥1 game: <1–2%
- Recurrence within 1 year: 5–15%
- Primary injuries: instabilities, rotator cuff, AC sprains, shoulder strains
- Unique finding: shoulder stabilization procedures = significantly smaller PER decline and games missed vs. ACL or Achilles repairs [S10, p.627]

### Season Timing
- **Highest injury rate: first month of season** — rapid workload increase + inadequate rehabilitation from prior injury [S10, p.618]
- Implication: preseason preparation and gradual load introduction are critical injury prevention tools

### Injury Classification for Treatment Planning

**Acute injuries:**
- Specific event, previously normal tissue
- Ankle sprain, muscle strain, finger injury, knee ligament, shoulder instability, contusion

**Chronic/Overuse injuries:**
- Insidious onset, microtrauma exceeds tissue repair capacity
- Patellar tendinopathy, patellofemoral pain, Achilles tendinopathy, rotator cuff tendinopathy, lower back conditions, stress reactions/fractures [S10, p.618]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Equal preparation for all injury types** → Ankle sprains account for >24% of all injuries — disproportionate prevention resources should target ankle health
2. **Ignoring sex differences** → Women have significantly higher knee injury rates (15.9% vs. 7.4%) — ACL prevention programs are particularly important for women's basketball
3. **Neglecting the first month** → The highest-risk period is the season's start; gradual load increases and preseason conditioning are the primary preventives
4. **Treating all concussions the same as ankle sprains** → Women's basketball concussion rate (6.5%) requires dedicated protocols

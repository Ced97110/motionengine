# Crossover Move (Drill 13)

## Objective
Teach the crossover dribble — stepping outside to draw the defender, then quickly crossing the ball to the opposite hand to drive past — primarily used against an overplaying or straight-up defender.

## Setup
- Individual drill (teaching): one coach, one player
- Drilling: players line up in three lines at mid-court; coach at the free-throw line area
- Mark a **2-foot × 2-foot (.6m × .6m) square** using tape near a line (e.g., free-throw line)
- Dribbler starts ~10 feet (3m) away, dribbles toward the coach
- Begin with a control dribble; progress to a speed dribble
- This move is the **complement of the in-and-out move**

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Dribbler starts ~10 feet away and dribbles toward the coach (control dribble).
2. At the square, dribbler executes the crossover move.
3. Coach moves in the direction of the step-out fake — teaching the *when* while teaching the *how*.

## Coaching Points

### When to Use
- When the defender is **overplaying** the dribbling side
- When playing the dribbler **straight up**
- When the dribbler can get the defender to put a foot forward **opposite the side of the dribble**

### How to Execute (Right-Hand Dribble, Defender Overplaying Right Side)
1. Dribbler **steps outside** the defender with the right foot, **faking with the head and shoulders** to the right.
2. May dribble the ball slightly outside if skilled enough. To do this: right hand stays almost on top of the ball but **slightly to the left top** — pushes ball slightly right before bringing it back left.
3. Bringing the ball back to the left: **lower the ball to mid-lower-leg height** and push it through with **great speed**. *(Note: Ball is exposed here — defender can deflect it.)*
4. When stepping right and dribbling slightly right: right foot should be **forward of defender's left foot**. When ball comes through to the left, the right foot follows and is placed **beside the defender's right foot**.
5. Left foot is brought **forward violently** and as far forward as possible. The dribbler **explodes in a straight line toward the basket**.

### Progression
- After mastering with the dominant hand, practice with the **weaker hand**.

## Common Mistakes
1. Ball crosses too high → keep it below mid-lower-leg height on the crossover to reduce steal risk
2. Step-out fake too short → must step outside the defender's foot to sell the fake
3. Right foot doesn't get to beside the defender's right foot → loses the positional advantage of the crossover
4. Left foot explosion is timid → drive violently once the right foot is in position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Control dribble into crossover with passive coach-defender
2. **Intermediate**: Speed dribble into crossover
3. **Advanced**: Chain with in-and-out as a counter (see Drill 16 Combination Dribbling)

# Post Screening (Drill 103)

## Objective
Teach post players to screen for perimeter players and for each other, emphasizing screen-and-come-back vs. screen-and-fade reads and proper defensive coverage of post screens.

## Setup
- Team — intermediate to advanced
- Half-court
- **Two phases** (10 minutes each phase)
- Coach reads the defense and makes the entry pass

## Execution

### Phase 1: Post Screens for Perimeter Players

**Formation** (Figure 1): Player 1 (top), Player 3 (wing), Player 5 (post), X1, X3, X5
**Rotation**: 1 → X1 → 3 → X3 → 5 → X5 → 1

1. **Player 5 can screen for either Player 3 or Player 1**.
2. Both Players 1 and 3 must **set their defenders up** before using the screen.
3. When Player 5 goes to screen, **Player 5 calls out the receiver's name** (communication!).
4. After setting the screen, Player 5 must choose:
   - **Screen and come back to the ball** (pop to the high post area), OR
   - **Screen and fade** (move away from the ball to the opposite block).
5. **Coach reads the defensive coverage** of the screen and passes to the correct primary receiver.

### Phase 2: Post-to-Post Screening (High-Low Roll Back)

**Formation** (Figure 2): Player 4 (screener), Player 5 (receiver), X4, X5
**Rotation**: 4 → X4 → 5 → X5 → end of line

1. **One post player screens for the other post player.**
2. **Screener rolls back high** after the screen → creates a **high-low passing post offense** (4 at high post, 5 at low post, or vice versa).
3. **X4 must always go over the top of the screen** (proper defensive technique).
4. **X5 guards the big block** until X4 recovers — then X5 picks up Player 5 (X5 can bump Player 4 slightly before recovering to Player 5).
5. Coach reads defense and passes to correct primary receiver.

## Options (All Skill Levels)
1. **Replace coach with an offensive player** — drill the screen-and-entry-pass decision.
2. **When using a perimeter player**: if a pass goes to the high post (Player 5 in Figure 2), Player 4 can try to seal X4 — you now have the high-low passing drill with a perimeter player helping make the entry pass.

## Coaching Points
- **Screener must call the receiver's name** before setting the screen — communication prevents confusion.
- "Screen and come back" vs. "screen and fade" — the screener reads the defense AFTER contact.
- In Phase 2, the high-low roll creates two post threats simultaneously — the defense cannot cover both if the roll-back is quick.
- X4 going **over the top** prevents the lob to a curling post player.
- X5 bumping the screener is a **legal hard defensive play** — teach this early.

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Walk-through each phase without live defense — establish roles and reads.
2. **Advanced**: Full live defense on both phases; coach as wild card passer.

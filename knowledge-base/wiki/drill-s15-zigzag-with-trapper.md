# Zigzag With a Trapper

## Objective
Develop dribble-direction changes and ball-pressure resistance for the offense while teaching channeling and trap-arrival timing for two defenders, in a continuous full-court zigzag framework.

## Setup
- **Players**: 3 per group (1 attacker, 2 defenders: X1 and X2)
- **Court area**: Full court
- **Boundary lines**:
  - **Beginner**: Sideline and an imaginary line from lane line to opposite lane line
  - **Advanced**: The full-court extension of the two free-throw lane lines (very narrow — ideal for advanced dribbling and trapping)
- Divide squad into two groups: one on the left side, one on the right side of the court simultaneously
- Rotate: player 1 → X1 → X2 → player 1; continue until all have played each position

## Execution

### Beginner (Simple Zigzag — No Trapper)
1. Player 1 dribbles with X1 playing tight and aggressive but not allowing 1 to drive by.
2. X1 pushes 1 toward the sideline (boundary). Player 1 must change direction using **spin dribble or crossover dribble**.
3. X1 then pushes 1 to the other boundary before compelling another direction change. **Forcing 3 direction changes in half-court is great.**
4. Player 1 goes against X1 down the court and back; then rotate so both play offense and defense.
5. The next two players in line begin when the current pair reaches the midcourt line.

### Intermediate (Add the Trapper X2)
1. Player 1 dribbles until X1 forces a direction change.
2. **When player 1 changes direction, X2 arrives to set the trap.** Both X1 and X2 stay with player 1 until 1 is forced to pick up the dribble.
3. Once player 1 picks up the dribble:
   - X1 **sprints downcourt** to become the next trapper.
   - Player 1 waits until X1 is in position, then begins dribbling against X2.
4. When X2 forces player 1 to change directions, **X1 arrives to set the trap**.
5. This procedure continues **down the court and back** before players rotate.
6. Rotation: player 1 → X1 → X2 → player 1.

### Advanced (Narrow Lane)
- Place players in the free-throw lane. Boundary lines become the full-court extensions of the two free-throw lane lines — a very narrow corridor.
- This narrow space demands precise dribble control and exact trap footwork.

## Coaching Points
- X1 must never allow player 1 a **vertical drive** — always cut off the straight line
- X2 must **time arrival** precisely to the direction change — arriving too early or too late misses the trap
- When player 1 picks up the dribble, all defenders must **get in passing lanes** immediately (dribble used — 5-second turnover)
- The narrow lane (advanced) is "ideal for teaching advanced dribbling and trapping techniques" [S15, p.335]
- Offensive players should practice **both spin and crossover** direction changes — defenses will look for habits

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 1v1 zigzag only — boundary lines, 3 direction changes in half-court
2. **Intermediate**: Add X2 trapper — arrives on direction change, X1 recovers to become next trapper
3. **Advanced**: Narrow free-throw lane boundaries; allow in-and-out, crossover, spin, and half-spin dribble counters

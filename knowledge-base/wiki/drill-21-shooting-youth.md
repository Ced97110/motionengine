# 21 Shooting Drill

## Objective
Build shooting accuracy, passing, and rebounding in a high-rep competitive team format — the race to 21 creates authentic game pressure within a controlled drill structure.

## Setup
- Players: two teams (2-5 players per team), one ball per team
- Court area: half court — coach selects specific shooting location (changes day to day)
- Equipment: two basketballs
- Duration: 6 minutes (two rounds to 21 — one per side)
- Default location shown: corner of free-throw line and lane line (both teams shoot from equal areas)

## Execution
1. Line two teams up as shown — one team at each side of the free-throw area at equal distances from the basket
2. The first player in line for both teams shoots simultaneously, then rebounds the shot (made or missed)
3. The rebounder passes the ball outside to the next teammate in line, then goes to the end of the line
4. Coach designates which type of pass to use (chest, bounce, or overhead)
5. The second player shoots, rebounds, and passes — this continues until one team scores 21 baskets
6. Teams count their score aloud as they make baskets
7. Teams switch sides and race to 21 again so players get practice shooting from both sides of the court
8. **Rule**: if a member of Team A touches Team B's basketball (or vice versa), the opposing team is awarded one basket

## Coaching Points
- Enforce correct shooting mechanics (straight-stick) even during the competition — do not allow players to rush and abandon form
- The "interference" rule teaches spatial awareness and ball security in a high-energy environment
- Change the shooting location from day to day so players get comprehensive court coverage over time
- Monitor pass quality — the drill degrades quickly if passes are sloppy; demand proper chest/bounce/overhead technique
- "Count out loud" — this keeps energy high and makes both teams aware of the score

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: standard drill from close range (free-throw area)
2. **Intermediate**: move to three-point line spots; same format
3. **Advanced**: add a "shot fake before shooting" requirement — every player must execute a shot fake before releasing

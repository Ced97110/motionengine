# Vertical Leap — Explosive (Power Jump Chain — Step 3)

## Objective
Coordinate upper and lower body for maximum vertical jump height, adding arm swing to the squat jump pattern to maximize upward momentum.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface — no equipment required
- Optional: chalk mark on a wall to measure progress (Sargent Jump Test)

## Execution
1. Stand feet slightly wider than shoulder-width, body flexed, arms by sides
2. Dip down into a squat (~1/3 depth for most athletes; advanced can go deeper)
3. Explode upward using full lower-body power
4. As you lift off, swing arms up above the head for maximum upward momentum
5. After take-off, allow legs to move naturally
6. Land softly on balls of feet; allow arms to fall back to sides
7. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- "Many high-level agility movement patterns require optimal arm explosiveness" — front flips and back flips depend on arm drive [S9, p.16]
- Begin with moderate arm swings and progress to full overhead extension over time
- The Sargent Jump Test (reach height against a wall) is the best simple way to track progress [S9, p.16]
- Limited ceiling space: bend arms at the elbow to avoid hitting ceiling while still getting arm-drive benefit [S9, p.17]
- Watch for athletes swinging arms late — arm and leg power must be coordinated at take-off simultaneously

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Moderate arm swing (partial range) — focus on coordination of arm and leg timing
2. **Intermediate**: Full overhead arm swing with maximum jump height; elastic rebound between reps
3. **Advanced**: Measure reach height and track weekly progress via Sargent Jump Test; aim to increase reach height by 2-4 inches over an 8-week progression

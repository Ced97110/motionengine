# Isolation Defense Strategies

## Summary
When an opponent isolates a dominant one-on-one player, the defense must choose a pre-game strategy and commit to it. Del Harris identifies three principal options: playing straight man-to-man; trapping from the top (forcing middle); or pushing to the baseline for a corner trap. A fourth option — shifting into a 2-3 zone around the ball defender — can also be used when help is needed but a full trap is too risky. [S7, p.259]

## When to Use
- Against teams that feature one or two elite isolation scorers (wing or corner iso)
- When the defense is physically overmatched one-on-one and needs help
- When a particular player is on a scoring run and a defensive adjustment is needed
- Late-game shot-clock situations where a quick 2 must be prevented

## Key Principles
1. **Pre-game decision**: Choose the approach before the game; scouting determines whether to go 1v1, trap, or zone-up so the team is never surprised.
2. **Trap from top**: Ball defender (X2) pushes the ball handler toward the middle; a second defender (X1) rotates to trap. Defenders X3 and X4 rotate to cover open players.
3. **Baseline trap**: Ball defender shades the ball to the baseline; X5 steps out to trap with X2; X4 fronts the post (5); X3 V-cuts back to cover 4.
4. **Zone shift**: All four teammates fill the zone spots (2-3 alignment) around the on-ball defender — ready to help on penetration or double-team. Must be pre-planned with a verbal call.
5. **Consistency**: "How to execute the traps and rotations... your team must be equally prepared to cover the low post as it is to cover the pick-and-roll game." [S7, p.259]

## Player Responsibilities
- **On-Ball Defender (X2)**: Primary containment; influences ball to desired direction (middle or baseline) per the game plan
- **X1 (top trap)**: Rotates from the top to trap 2 in the isolation when middle trap is called
- **X5 (baseline trap)**: Steps out to trap when ball is shaded to baseline; immediately rotates after trap pass
- **X4**: Fronts the post (5) on baseline trap rotation; rotates to cover 5 on a top trap if X5 is occupied
- **X3**: V-cuts back to cover 4 on baseline trap rotation; covers weak side until X5 can get there

- Particularly useful against certain PnR combinations and low-post attackers

## Common Mistakes
1. **No pre-game plan** → defense hesitates under pressure when the iso is triggered; choose the coverage in advance
2. **Ball defender going for steals** → contain first; let the trap partner arrive before reaching
3. **X4 not fronting the post on baseline trap** → if X5 steps out to trap, someone must take the post — X4 must automatically front 5 to prevent a dump-off pass
4. **Zone shift without a signal** → zone-and-man confusion creates open layups; always use a verbal call to trigger the shift

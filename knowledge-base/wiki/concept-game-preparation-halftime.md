# Game Preparation and Halftime Adjustments

## Summary
pro-level game preparation requires that coaches rehearse nearly all defensive schemes, anticipated matchups, and backup options before tip-off. This preparation makes in-game adjustments faster and more precise. The halftime break (14 minutes) is brief, requiring efficient staff communication and focused player address. The postgame cycle closes the loop and feeds into preparation for the next game. [S7, pp.297-298]

## When to Use
- Before every game during the preparation week
- At halftime when adjustments are needed based on first-half observations
- In post-game review to determine what to carry forward vs. discard

## Key Principles

### Pre-Game Preparation
1. **Rehearse in advance.** With rare exceptions, all defensive schemes, anticipated matchups, and backup options should have been rehearsed before the game starts.
2. **Preparation enables in-game flexibility.** Because preparation is thorough, adjustments can be made during time-outs, at quarter breaks, or on the fly without confusion.

### Halftime Process
1. Staff meets briefly while players attend to personal needs and see the trainer.
2. The 14-minute break is short — coaches' meeting and team address must be **brief and focused**.
3. Adjustments are based on observations from the first half.
4. Coaches use a structured **halftime adjustment form** to organize notes (see Figure 18.3 example).
5. Address the team, then confer individually with key players.

### Halftime Adjustment Form (Figure 18.3 Example)
A real pro halftime form example (vs. Charlotte, 3-16-07, LAL 102, OPP 93) included:
- **Defense notes:** P/Roll coverage with Felton — contain dribble, no splits; Isolation with Wallace — weak-side help was slow — anticipate
- **Offense notes:** P/Rolls were effective — keep good spacing; Hurt them inside — ready for quicker 2x's — Read and Respond
- **Key adjustment:** Single-Double with Carroll — better close-outs
- **Drawn plays:** Multiple 2x vs EB action diagrams
- **Post-game notes:** More focus on boards — block-outs were soft (18 second-chance pts); Transition defense was good — gave up early pts/improved in 2nd half (8 fast break pts total)
[S7, p.298]

### Postgame Analysis
1. Coaches meet immediately after the game for quick postgame thoughts.
2. Team postgame comments assembled.
3. The following day at practice: depth of analysis depends on upcoming schedule — may range from a few minutes to a few hours.
4. However long postgame review takes, preparation for the *next* game is always looming.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Under-preparation** → Scrambling to adjust in-game to things that should have been rehearsed in practice.
2. **Wasted halftime** → Coaches talking too long; players not getting enough individual feedback.
3. **No structure for halftime notes** → Use a consistent form so nothing is missed under time pressure.
4. **Dwelling too long on the past game** → Postgame review should be proportional to the upcoming schedule.

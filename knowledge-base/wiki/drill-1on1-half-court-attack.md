# 1-on-1 Half-Court Attack Drill

## Objective
Simulate a half-court offensive attack from the wing, developing dribble-drive instincts and competitive 1-on-1 defense in a realistic game-speed chase scenario.

## Setup
- Full to half court
- Two chairs a few feet from the midcourt line (set apart left and right)
- Offensive player: corner near the baseline and sideline, with a ball
- Defensive player: near the lane
- Coach signals the start

## Execution
1. Coach yells "Go!"
2. Offensive player dribbles hard, **curls around the chair in front of him**, and attacks the rim.
3. Defensive player **runs around the other chair** and tries to beat the offensive player to the inside spot.
4. Live 1-on-1 until the offensive player scores or the defender gets the ball.

## Coaching Points
- Offensive player: dribble *hard* around the chair — not a casual jog
- Goal for offense: beat the defender to the inside and finish with whatever move is needed (layup, pull-up, floater)
- Goal for defense: sprint to take the inside position before the offensive player can establish it
- "Shoulders–hips" — get shoulders below the defender's hips when attacking
- "Feet first, ball second" — get the body in position before putting the ball down [S7, p.312]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Defender starts 2 steps behind to give offense more time to practice the attack
2. **Intermediate:** Equal start (standard version)
3. **Advanced:** Defender gets a slight head start — offense must overcome the positional disadvantage

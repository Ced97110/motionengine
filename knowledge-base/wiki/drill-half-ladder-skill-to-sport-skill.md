# Half Ladder Skill to Sport-Specific Skill

## Objective
Improve lower-body quickness while simultaneously developing reaction time and sport-specific skill integration (catching or controlling a basketball mid-ladder run). [S8, p.184]

## Setup
- 2 players (athlete + coach/partner)
- Half agility ladder on court or field
- Coach positioned on the opposite side of the ladder from the athlete
- Basketball or sport-specific ball

## Execution
1. Athlete performs any of the agility ladder drills (quick feet, bunny jumps, hop-scotch, one-leg hop, etc.) while looking at the coach on the opposite side of the ladder.
2. At any point during the run, coach throws a ball to either side of the ladder.
3. Athlete reacts in mid-run to the side to which the ball is thrown.
4. Athlete catches or hits the ball while continuing to complete the ladder.

## Coaching Points
- Cue: "Eyes up — see the coach, not the ladder."
- The athlete must maintain ladder footwork quality while tracking the coach and ball.
- Ball throws should be random and varied (left, right, early, late) to maximize reaction training.
- This drill purposefully combines physical and cognitive demands to simulate game conditions.
- Watch for: looking down at the ladder when ball is thrown, breaking footwork pattern to catch.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Simple ladder drill (quick feet) + coach throw after completing the ladder.
2. **Intermediate**: Any ladder drill + coach throw in mid-run as described.
3. **Advanced**: Complex ladder pattern (lateral, backward) + mid-run ball reaction + immediate action (dribble, pass, or shoot).

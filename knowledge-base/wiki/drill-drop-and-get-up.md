# Drop and Get Up (Crazy Z-Ball Drill)

## Objective
Improve the ability to change direction and reaction time by performing a push-up on the ground while a Z-ball (unpredictably bouncing ball) is in the air, then scrambling up to catch it. [S8, p.128]

## Setup
- 1-2 players
- 1 Crazy Z-ball (or similar unpredictably bouncing rubber ball)
- Open indoor court or gym floor

## Execution
1. Throw the Crazy Z-ball **up into the air** (straight overhead or slightly forward).
2. Immediately get onto the ground and perform a **push-up**.
3. Scramble back up to your feet and catch the ball **before it bounces twice**.
4. **Partner variation**: A partner releases the Z-ball while the athlete is already on the ground — adds reaction time challenge since the bounce direction is unpredictable.
5. **Harder variation**: Catch the ball before its **first** bounce. [S8, p.128]

## Coaching Points
- The push-up must be a full rep — do not cheat reps to get up faster; build the conditioning demand
- The scramble to feet must be explosive — this simulates getting off the floor after a charge or loose-ball dive
- With the Z-ball, the bounce is unpredictable — train eyes up immediately upon rising to track the ball
- "Floor fast, up faster" — the coaching cue: efficient descent AND explosive ascent
- Partner variation is more game-realistic because the athlete cannot time the ball's trajectory

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Throw ball up, simply get down and stand back up (no push-up) before 2 bounces
2. **Intermediate**: Full push-up before 2 bounces
3. **Advanced**: Partner releases ball; athlete catches before first bounce

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — explosive recovery from the floor
- [[concept-ankle-proprioception]] — rapid weight shift and balance upon standing

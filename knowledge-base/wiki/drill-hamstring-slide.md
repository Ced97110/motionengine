# Hamstring Slide

## Objective
Develop posterior chain strength (hamstrings and glutes) in a hip-bridge position to improve jumping power, deceleration, and injury resilience for basketball.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Flat floor space with a slide board
- No additional equipment needed beyond the slide board

## Execution
1. Lie on the floor in a **supine position** with lower legs perpendicular to the floor and heels centered on the slide board.
2. Extend arms out to the sides to stabilize the body.
3. Press hips into a **bridge position** by squeezing the glutes — maintain this position throughout.
4. **Slide the feet forward** until the legs are fully extended (eccentric hamstring phase).
5. Keep the hips up as the body lowers; do not let the hips drop.
6. **Squeeze the glutes and pull the heels back** to return to the starting position (concentric phase).

## Coaching Points
- Start with hips up in a true bridge position — "squeeze the glutes at the top"
- Keep the toes dorsiflexed (toes up) as the knees extend and flex
- Drive the heels into the floor during the return phase
- Do not allow the hips to sag — loss of hip extension shifts work away from the hamstrings
- **Inhale** during the forward slide; **exhale** after the sticking point of the backward movement

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bilateral hamstring slide with controlled tempo (3 sec out, 2 sec in)
2. **Intermediate**: Standard bilateral slide at moderate speed
3. **Advanced**: Single-leg variation — keep one leg elevated with knee fully extended throughout; this doubles the demand on the working leg's hamstrings and glutes

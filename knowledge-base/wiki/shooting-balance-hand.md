# Shooting Balance Hand

## Summary
The balance hand (also called the guide hand or off-hand by some coaches, though those terms are discouraged) exists for one purpose: to help balance the ball and lift it. It does not shoot the ball, guide the ball, or push the ball. Misuse of the balance hand — placing it in front of the ball, on top of the ball, or allowing it to shoot — is one of the most common sources of shooting inaccuracy. Proper placement with the T-thumb technique, palm off the ball, on the side of the ball eliminates lateral movement and allows both eyes to see the target. [S5, pp.18-19]

## When to Use
- Added after the shooting hand grip and elbow are correctly set
- Verified on every shot and during all form shooting drills
- Isolated and retrained when a player's elbow flares out or shot veers sideways

## Key Principles
1. **Balance hand is on the SIDE of the ball, not the front or top** — side placement allows the shooter to see the target with both eyes and eliminates side-to-side drift [S5, p.18]
2. **Palm does NOT touch the ball** — fingertips and fingerpads only, just like the shooting hand [S5, p.18]
3. **T-thumb check** — if both thumbs came together, they would form the letter T; the balance-hand thumb forms the top of the T [S5, p.18]
4. **It does NOT shoot the ball** — calling it a "guide hand" incorrectly implies it guides the ball into the basket; calling it an "off hand" or "nonshooting hand" sends negative messages; "balance hand" is the correct term and concept [S5, p.18]
5. **Shooting hand stays centered** — when adding the balance hand, do NOT move the shooting hand to the side; the shooting hand must remain under the ball and in the center [S5, p.18]
6. **Efficient movement = quick release** — extra motion added by an incorrectly placed balance hand slows the shot; especially critical for players developing a quick release [S5, p.18]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: After every catch, verify that the balance hand is on the side (not front, not top) before lifting into the shot. This becomes automatic only through thousands of correct repetitions.

## Variations
### Balance-Hand Form Shooting Drill
Start 2 feet from the basket. Set shooting arm in L-position, add balance hand on the side of ball. Have a partner or coach verify the positioning of both arms before each shot. Make 5 shots, take a half-step back, repeat. Goal: 5 makes from 10 spots. Progress to "strings" (consecutive makes or shoot-till-miss) when the basic routine becomes too easy. [S5, p.19]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Balance hand in front of the ball** → Blocks target view, blocks defender view, affects shot direction → Move hand strictly to the side [S5, p.18]
2. **Balance hand on top of the ball** → Forces an extra release motion mid-shot, slows the release → Place on the side immediately when gripping [S5, p.18]
3. **Shooting with two hands** → Shooter unconsciously moves shooting hand to the side when balance hand comes in, creating a two-hand shot → Shooting hand must always stay under and centered [S5, p.18]
4. **Balance hand causing elbow to flare** → Balance hand pulls the shooting arm out of alignment → Drill shooting-hand-only form shooting first to ingrain elbow position before adding balance hand [S5, p.16]

# Channeling (Defensive Dribble Direction)

## Objective
Teach defenders to control the direction of a dribbler by staying slightly ahead of and beside the ball-handler, funneling them into a preset trap location.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2 per group (1 attacker, 1 defender); rotate from player 1 → X1 → end of line
- **Court area**: Full court — one group on left side, one group on right side simultaneously
- Rotate sides after two executions by each player
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball; coach stands behind the dribbler (hidden from defender) to signal the direction

## Execution
1. X1 rolls the ball to player 1. Player 1 waits before beginning the dribble.
2. **Coach signals X1** (behind player 1) which direction to channel:
   - **Fanning**: Force the dribbler outside (toward the sideline)
   - **Funneling**: Force the dribbler inside (toward the middle)
   - Player 1 does NOT know the direction — this is realistic.
3. As the ball rolls to player 1, **X1 sprints to within 1–2 meters of player 1** and comes under control.
4. X1 positions **a quarter of a player's length to the side of and slightly ahead of the dribbler** — the overplay position that compels a speed dribble.
5. As player 1 crosses the midcourt line, X1 **races to get in front of 1**, forcing 1 to change direction. X1 is now funneling 1 to the inside.
6. This is the moment when a **run-and-jump or trap** (drills 155–158) is activated by teammates.
7. Drill each defender on both fanning and funneling on one side of the court before rotating to the other side.

### Advanced Progressions (after several days of drilling)
1. Allow more advanced attackers to use **in-and-out or crossover** moves to try to get past the defender.
2. After mastering those: allow **spin or half-spin moves**.
3. In team defense: when fanning or funneling is called as a team concept, all help-side defenders know the direction and can position to offer even greater help.

### Channeling Coaching Definition
- **Channeling** = forcing the dribbler in one specific direction. "Defenders know where the trap is to be set. It is imperative that all defenders know how to channel the attacker." [S15, p.333]
- To compel the channeled direction: use **quick shuffle-steps** and stay **slightly ahead of and slightly beside** the dribbler. Distance between defender and attacker varies with relative speed — drilling determines the optimal distance.

## Coaching Points
- "Make sure the defender uses shuffle-steps. This keeps the defender in control of the dribbler." [S15, p.333]
- "Make sure the defender knows the distance between him- or herself and the attacker so the attacker cannot turn the corner and head in a direction other than the channeled direction." [S15, p.333]
- Under no circumstances should X1 allow player 1 to get a **vertical drive** once 1 crosses the midcourt line
- The hidden coach signal is critical: X1 learns to position from their overplay angle, not from what the dribbler does

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary ball roll, defender channels with no counters allowed
2. **Intermediate**: Attacker may use in-and-out or crossover to beat the channeling
3. **Advanced**: Attacker uses spin or half-spin; full-team channeling with all help-side defenders positioning accordingly

# No Rim Shooting Drill

## Objective
Force pure shooting arc and touch by requiring every made shot to pass cleanly through the net without touching the rim — the ultimate accuracy training drill.

## Setup
- Players: 2 per basket (one shoots, one rebounds); 4 per basket acceptable
- Court area: half court — close range through midrange
- Equipment: one basketball per pair
- Duration: 10 minutes

## Execution
**Five shooting angles:**
- a. Out the left baseline
- b. 45-degree angle on the left side of the basket
- c. Up the middle of the court (straight-on)
- d. 45-degree angle on the right side of the basket
- e. Out the right baseline

<!-- DIAGRAM: Five angles drawn from the basket — left baseline, left 45°, straight middle, right 45°, right baseline — shooter starts 3 ft from basket and steps back. -->

**Execution sequence:**
1. One shooter starts at angle **a**, approximately 3 feet (1 m) from the basket
2. Shoot until making a shot that **does NOT touch the rim** — pure swish only
3. Step back **one full step** (now ~6 ft/1.8 m from basket)
4. Shoot until making a no-rim shot; step back again
5. Continue stepping back three times total (beginner: up to ~9 ft/2.7 m)
6. Advance to angle **b**, then **c**, then **d**, then **e** — same process at each angle
7. One player shoots, the other rebounds; complete one full angle before switching roles

## Coaching Points
- **Arc is everything**: a shot that clips the rim has insufficient arc; the straight-stick flip must generate more height — "lift the upper arm higher"
- This drill reveals immediately whether a player's arc is consistent — a player who regularly hits the front rim is releasing too flat; a player who hits the back rim is releasing too high or too close
- **"Touch and feel"**: close-range no-rim shots develop the softness of touch that transfers to all midrange and long-range shooting
- Do not rush — the goal is quality of make, not speed; make-to-advance is the rule
- **Bank shot option** (team variant): for all angle shots EXCEPT the straight-on middle (angle c), require bank shots without rim contact — extremely advanced but builds elite bank-shot accuracy

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: step back 3 times (max ~9 ft); all angles
2. **Advanced**: step back 5 times (last shot each angle = ~15 ft/4.5 m)
3. **Team competition**: two pairs race through all 5 angles; first pair to complete all angles wins
4. **Bank shot variation**: all non-straight-on angles must be bank shots without rim contact

## Concepts Taught
- [[concept-straight-stick-shooting]] — the no-rim requirement enforces proper arc; if a player consistently hits the rim, their lift-extend-flip sequence is incomplete
- [[bank-shot]] — the bank shot variation of this drill directly applies bank-shot technique
- [[shooting-follow-through]] — proper follow-through with the fingers pointing down is the most reliable way to generate consistent arc

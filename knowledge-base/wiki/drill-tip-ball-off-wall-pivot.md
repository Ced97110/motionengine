# Tip Ball Off the Wall and Pivot

## Objective
Develop offensive tipping technique, quick jumping, balance, and 360° pivot agility using a wall as a rebounder substitute.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 basketball, a solid wall
- No court markings required — any wall space works
- Duration: 1 minute continuous

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Player faces the wall holding a basketball.
2. Tip the ball off the wall **5 times right-handed**, then catch with both hands.
3. Execute a complete **360° turn** using the **left foot** as the pivot foot. This should move the player approximately 1 yard (1 m) from the start spot. Beginners may need more than 2 steps to complete the full turn.
4. After completing the turn, face the wall again. Immediately tip the ball **5 times left-handed**.
5. After 5 left-hand tips, roll **360° to the right**, using the **right foot** as the pivot foot.
6. Continue alternating for 1 minute.

### Advanced
1. Tip **2 times**, then tip the ball slightly higher and to the left (1 yard/meter). Execute the 360° left roll.
2. Tip **twice left-handed**, then tip the ball 1 yard to the right. Roll right. Continue for 1 minute.
3. Same as above, but before pivoting, perform a **jab step in the same direction** as the roll.

## Coaching Points
- To perform the roll: pivot **strongly** on the foot in the direction desired. The player may jump slightly into the air to add a twist, accelerating the turn.
- All tips should be executed with the **front two joints of the fingers** (fingerpads). The tip is like a catch followed by a quick **flip of the wrist** — not a slap.
- Keep jumps quick and decisive — this drill conditions both the body and the mind to react rapidly.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 5 tips → catch → 360° pivot. Larger rest between pivots.
2. **Intermediate**: 2 tips → tip aside → 360° pivot without catching. Tighter sequence.
3. **Advanced**: Add a jab step before each 360° pivot, simulating the offensive rebound jab-step-and-roll action.

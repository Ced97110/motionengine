# Defensive Step (Youth)

## Objective
Master the four foundational individual defensive steps — advance, retreat, swing, and slide — that allow defenders to control ball handlers before and during the dribble.

## Setup
- Offense and defense line up on opposite sides of the midcourt line
- Pairs work simultaneously
- Slide step variation: players use the full court perimeter following arrows
- 1 basketball per pair

## Execution

### Advance Step
1. Offense and defense line up on opposite sides of midcourt; switch roles every 30 seconds.
2. Attacker establishes pivot foot (right for 15 sec, left for 15 sec).
3. Attacker begins with forward foot exaggerated forward; defender is a half-step back from front-foot-to-pivot-foot stance.
4. Attacker brings forward foot back to semiparallel. Defender **advance-steps** back into front-foot-to-pivot-foot stance.
5. Attacker again exaggerates forward foot (jab-step position); defender repeats.
- **Cue**: Front foot steps to attacker's pivot foot first, trail foot widens the base. Arm corresponding to front foot raises high to discourage jump shot. Defender does not leave floor until shooter does — then puts hand between shooter's arms straight toward ball.

### Retreat Step
1. Same setup as advance step.
2. Attacker jab-steps farther than normal from pivot foot.
3. Defender **retreat-steps** (trail foot drops a half-step, front foot follows to maintain balance). Arm and hand extend straight in direction of the fake. Stay low.
- If jab is away from pivot foot rather than forward, retreat step goes more in that direction.

### Swing Step
1. Same setup as advance step.
2. Attacker uses a reverse pivot (spin move).
3. Defender executes the **swing step** (one-two tactic): front foot steps wider than usual just as attacker is halfway through the spin (slightly backward to create new front-foot-to-pivot-foot stance). Second step pulls trail foot up to attacker's pivot foot and slightly to the side.
- The attacker cannot shoot while spinning because they are sideways to basket — use that time to reset.

### Slide Step
1. All players slide-step around the full court perimeter following arrows on the diagram.
2. On each slide step, player taps palms on the floor.
3. Always face the center of the court.
4. Start from alternating sides on different days. Players may go around the court twice as the season progresses.

## Options / Progressions
1. Attacker steps back into shooting position → let shooter jump shoot; defender raises arm and hand to contest but does not leave floor first.
2. Attacker pump-fakes then shoots; defender must NOT react to pump fake.
3. Attacker uses all phases of the rocker step series.
4. Attacker jab-steps then dribbles in direction of jab — defender must be in position to draw the charge.
5. Attacker dribbles a few steps to test whether defender stays in overplay or parallel.
6. Attacker dribbles then shoots; if defender is in proper position they are on attacker's shooting shoulder.
7. Use front-foot-to-free-foot stance variation instead of front-foot-to-pivot-foot stance.

## Coaching Points
- Advance step: "Step to the pivot foot first — raise the hand on the same side to take away the shot"
- Retreat step: "Trail foot drops first — extend hand in direction of the fake"
- Swing step: "Match their spin — step wide then pull the trail foot; you're building a new stance"
- Slide step: "Tap the palms — stay low, face center court"
- Eyes on belt buckle; head steady as a bucket of water on top

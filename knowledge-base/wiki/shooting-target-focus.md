# Shooting Target Focus

## Summary
A consistent shooter must have a consistent target. The center of the basket is the only aiming point that remains constant regardless of where on the court the player shoots from. Targeting the front rim from straight-on breaks down from the corner; targeting the center of the hoop works from every angle. Keeping eyes on the center of the basket — and never following the ball's flight — is a discipline that must be trained consciously until automatic. [S5, pp.19-20]

## When to Use
- On every shot from every location
- As a focal point cue during all shooting drills
- Reinforced especially when teaching corner shots and baseline shots where rim visibility changes

## Key Principles
1. **Always target the center of the basket** — it is the one consistent aiming point from every court location [S5, p.19]
2. **"Consistent target, consistent shooter"** — varying your target varies your consistency [S5, p.20]
3. **Focus on the middle of the hoop, not a specific spot** — since the center has no physical dot, the shooter focuses on putting the ball in the middle of the open space [S5, p.20]
4. **Aiming at the center allows margin for error** — a shot can be slightly long, short, left, or right and still go in; front-rim targeting leaves no margin [S5, p.20]
5. **Never follow the flight of the ball** — eyes remain on the target through and after the release [S5, p.20]
6. **Bank shot exception** — when deliberately shooting a bank shot, the target shifts to the top corner of the small square on the backboard closest to the shooter [S5, p.20]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Lock eyes on the center of the basket before the ball is caught and keep them there through the entire shot motion and follow-through. Do not watch the ball in flight.

## Variations
### Bank Shot Targeting
On bank shots (typically from 45-degree angles on either side of the basket), shift focus from the center of the hoop to the top corner of the backboard square nearest the shooter. The backboard becomes the target, not the rim. [S5, p.20]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Targeting the front of the rim** → Works from straight on but fails from angles; inconsistency from shot to shot → Switch all targeting to center of hoop [S5, pp.19-20]
2. **Targeting the back of the rim** → Ball goes long, especially on close shots → Refocus to center [S5, p.19]
3. **Following the flight of the ball** → Head and eyes drift upward, disrupting balance and follow-through → "Eyes on the target, not the ball" [S5, p.20]
4. **Different players using different targets** → Team-wide inconsistency, no common coaching cue → Establish center-of-hoop as the team standard [S5, p.19]

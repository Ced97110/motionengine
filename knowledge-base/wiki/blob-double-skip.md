# BLOB: Double Skip (vs 2-3 Zone)

## Overview
Double Skip is a 2-3 zone BLOB play that uses two skip passes to move the zone from side to side, exhausting defenders laterally, before springing the best shooter (the in-bounder) open on the weak-side wing off a double flare screen. The shooter arrives from out of sight of the zone defenders. [S4, pp.11-12]

## Formation
**1-4 High (modified).** Post players (**4** and **5**) are positioned on the wings of the 1-4 high (slightly wider). Guard **1** is at the top/key area. **3** is at a wing/short corner position. **2** (best shooter) is the in-bounder out of bounds on the baseline.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Strong-Side Load
- **5** sprints to the strong-side corner.
- **3** sprints to the strong-side short corner simultaneously.
- **2** inbounds the ball to **5** in the corner.

### Phase 2: First Skip Pass
- **5** (or dribbles to the wing to improve angle) skip passes the ball across to **4** on the weak side.
- **1** may optionally screen in on **x1** to make the skip pass easier and safer.
- **1** spaces up to the top of the key once **4** catches the skip pass.

### Phase 3: Double Flare Screens
- As **4** receives the skip pass:
  - **3** sprints up to set a flare screen on the top wing zone defender (**x2**).
  - **5** simultaneously screens in on the bottom wing zone defender (**x3**).
- Timing is critical: **3** should arrive to screen at the same moment the skip pass is thrown so the action is not telegraphed. [S4, p.12]

### Phase 4: In-Bounder Cuts to Wing
- **2** sprints behind the bottom wing zone defender (**x3**), using the screen from **5**, and positions on the weak-side wing.
- **4** skip passes to **2** as they arrive at the wing — **2** should have an open three-point shot. [S4, p.12]

## Key Coaching Points
- **5** can dribble to the wing to improve the angle on the first skip pass. [S4, p.12]
- **3** and **5** can slip their flare screens and show themselves to **4** if the wing defenders anticipate and fight over the screens.
- **4** can use a pass fake before the skip to **2** in order to freeze or shift the zone away from **2**'s cutting lane. [S4, p.12]
- **Timing is paramount**: screens must arrive simultaneously with the pass so the action isn't revealed early.

## Counters
- **If wing defenders fight over flare screens**: 3 and 5 slip their screens and offer themselves to 4 as alternative pass targets.
- **If skip pass is denied**: 1 at the top of the key is available as a relay.

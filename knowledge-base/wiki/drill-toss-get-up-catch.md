# Toss, Get Up, and Catch (Medicine-Ball Drill)

## Objective
Improve level-change capability and the transition from power to agility by performing a supine chest pass with a medicine ball then scrambling to standing to catch it before it hits the ground. [S8, p.130]

## Setup
- 1 player
- 1 medicine ball (6-10 lbs recommended for basketball athletes)
- Open court or gym floor

## Execution
1. Lie down on your back.
2. Hold the medicine ball in both hands at chest level.
3. Perform a **chest pass** — explosively throw the ball straight up into the air.
4. **Scramble up** as quickly as possible.
5. **Catch the ball** before it hits the ground. [S8, p.130]

## Coaching Points
- The chest pass should be explosive and vertical — enough height to give time to stand but not so high it becomes trivial
- The scramble is the key physical demand — both the movement skill (getting to feet fast) and the core strength required to do so
- Catch with bent arms and soft hands — this is also a receiving skill
- "Throw it high, chase it fast" — the coaching cue balancing toss height and scramble urgency
- Coaches can vary ball weight to adjust difficulty

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use a lighter ball and throw higher to allow more time to stand
2. **Intermediate**: Standard weight ball with moderate throw height
3. **Advanced**: Heavier ball, lower throw, or require the catch before the first bounce

- [[concept-ankle-proprioception]] — rapid weight shift and balance upon standing

# Sit-Up — Basketball Preseason Core Drill

## Objective
Strengthen the abdominal muscles to build a core that prevents strains, tears, and injuries — establishing foundational trunk stability for all basketball movements.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2 (one performing, one holding feet)
- **Court area**: Any flat surface (gym floor)
- **Equipment**: None
- **Duration**: 45-second work period; 2-minute rest between stations in the preseason circuit

## Execution
1. The performing player lies on his back with knees bent, feet flat on the floor, and hands on each side of the head (do not interlace fingers behind the neck).
2. A teammate kneels at the performing player's feet and holds them firmly in place throughout the set.
3. The performing player curls his torso upward toward the knees.
4. A repetition is complete when the elbows touch the knees.
5. Lower back to starting position (controlled descent) and repeat.
6. Count total repetitions completed in 45 seconds. Record the number.
7. The daily goal is set by the average repetitions achieved the previous day — each player and group works to improve their count daily.

## Coaching Points
- Hands stay at the sides of the head — do not pull on the neck.
- Feet must remain flat on the floor; the partner's job is critical.
- Full range of motion — elbows must contact the knees for the rep to count.
- "Establishing a strong core of abdominal muscles helps prevent strains, tears, and injuries that often take a season to heal" [S13, p.6].
- Track results daily. The recorded average becomes the next day's goal.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reduce the time window to 30 seconds; allow a pause at the top.
2. **Intermediate**: Full 45-second standard as written.
3. **Advanced**: Add a twist at the top (elbow to opposite knee — oblique emphasis); extend to 60-second sets.

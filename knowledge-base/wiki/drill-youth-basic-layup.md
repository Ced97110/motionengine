# Basic Layup (Drill 8)

## Objective
Teach players the correct mechanics of shooting a layup from both the left and right sides of the court, while simultaneously drilling bounce passes, chest passes, and rebounding.

## Setup
- Team drill; 4 players minimum, more in rotating lines
- Half court, at the basket
- Two lines: one shooting line (line 1, baseline left of basket) and one passing/rebounder line (line 2, wing)
- Two additional players serve as rebounders (players 3 and 4)
- Begin with shots from the **right side** of the basket; after 90 seconds, switch to the **left side**

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Player 2 throws a **bounce pass** to player 1, who shoots the layup.
2. Player 2 goes to the end of line 1; Player 1 goes to the end of line 2.
3. Player 4 rebounds the shot (made or missed). As 4 rebounds, player 3 runs toward the basket.
4. Player 4 passes to player 3, who shoots the layup.
5. Both 3 and 4 go to ends of opposite lines.
6. Repeat until each player masters the layup.

### Intermediate
- Same as beginner, but a coach stands beside the shooter and **gently pushes** the shooter as the shot is taken, simulating contact.

### Advanced
- Same as intermediate, but allow passers to throw **chest passes** instead of bounce passes.

## Options (All Levels)
1. **Three-Line Layup Drill**: Player 1 passes to 2, who passes to 3 at the free-throw line. Player 1 cuts behind 2; player 3 passes to 1 for the layup. Player 2 cuts behind 3 and races for the rebound. Lines rotate (1→3's line, 2→1's line, 3→2's line).
2. Run drills without letting the ball touch the floor — keeps players hustling.
3. Let the receiver catch the pass, then toss the ball from hand to hand before shooting (ball-handling warm-up).
4. Let the receiver catch the pass away from the basket, then dribble-drive to the basket.

## Coaching Points
- **Jump mechanics**: The layup is shot from a **high jump, not a broad jump**. The shooter goes straight up with a slight lean forward.
- **Opposite-leg takeoff**: Right-handed shooters jump off the **left leg**; left-handed shooters jump off the right leg.
- **Shooting hand position**: Hand is always on the out-of-bounds side, **behind the ball**, palm pointing upward. As the ball is released, the wrist flips slightly.
- **Target**: Place the ball in the **small square above the rim**. The ball should not go any higher after hitting the square.
- **Bounce pass technique**: Hold ball with both hands (one on each side), elbows flexed. Step forward as you deliver. Flip wrists so thumbs finish pointing down, palms end pointed outward. Elbows fully extend. Aim to land the ball so it bounces up near or slightly above the receiver's beltline. Passer stays in a low "sitting" position — ready to cut.
- **Chest pass technique**: Hands outside the ball (both sides), thumbs pointing up, palms in. Step forward, snap wrists — thumbs end pointing down, palms pointing out. Deliver near the receiver's chest. Receiver watches the ball all the way into the hands.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two-line layup with bounce pass, no defensive contact
2. **Intermediate**: Add coach contact (gentle push) to simulate game pressure
3. **Advanced**: Chest passes + dribble-drive option from away from basket

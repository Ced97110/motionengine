# Combo Hop Back Drill

## Objective
Train the hop back — a backward two-foot hop off the live dribble that creates space behind a defender for a three-pointer or midrange pull-up — bilaterally off 1 and 2 dribbles.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court — three-point line area
- Shot tracking table (FGA, FGM %, 3PTA, 3PTM %, FTA, FTM %)
- Note: track 3-point attempts separately from regular FG attempts

## Execution
1. Player starts inside the three-point line with the ball in triple-threat or live dribble.
2. **1-Dribble Hop Back Left (3-point)**: take 1 hard left-hand dribble toward the paint, then quickly hop backward — both feet simultaneously — landing behind the three-point line. Rise and shoot the three.
3. Shoot 5 (Workout #4-13) or 10 (Workout #4-14) or 20 (Workout #4-15) reps going left.
4. Step to the free-throw line; shoot 5–10 FTs with full routine.
5. **1-Dribble Hop Back Right (3-point)**: mirror going right.
6. Repeat L/R/FT cycle a second time for 1-dribble hop backs.
7. **2-Dribble Hop Back**: repeat the entire L/R/FT structure with 2 dribbles before the hop-back gather. Note: 2-dribble hop backs often result in a midrange pull-up, not a three, depending on where the player starts.
8. Total: 4 sets of combos + 4 FT clusters (or 8 sets at higher volumes).

**Volume Progressions:**
- **Workout #4-13 (40 shots)**: 4 sets × 5 combos + 4 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 6–10 (60%) | 3PT goal: 6–10 (60%) | FT goal: 17–20 (85%)
- **Workout #4-14 (80 shots)**: 4 sets × 10 combos + 4 × 10 FTs | FG goal: 12–20 (60%) | 3PT goal: 12–20 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (85%)
- **Workout #4-15 (120 shots)**: 4 sets × 20 combos + 4 × 10 FTs | FG goal: 24–40 (60%) | 3PT goal: 24–40 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (85%)

## Coaching Points
- **Hop direction**: the hop moves the player backward (away from basket) to gain separation, NOT sideways
- **Two feet simultaneously**: the hop-back landing must be two-foot simultaneous to legally use either foot as a pivot
- **Land in shot-ready position**: the catching and landing must happen in one motion — catch the ball as the feet land, not before or after
- **Three-point range**: the 1-dribble hop back should routinely land behind the arc — start close enough to the arc that the hop reliably clears it
- **Separate 3PT tracking**: this is one of the few combo drills with explicit 3-point tracking (3PTA/3PTM columns) — monitor three-point percentage independently
- Cue: **"Attack. Gather. Hop back. Shoot."**

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (40 shots)**: 5 reps per set; practice hop back without a dribble — just hop backward and shoot — to groove the landing and release
2. **Intermediate (80 shots)**: 10 reps per set; add 1 live dribble at 75% speed
3. **Advanced (120 shots)**: 20 reps per set at game speed; add a passive defender to simulate the closeout that makes the hop back necessary

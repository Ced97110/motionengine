# Flash Cut

## Summary
A flash cut is a weak-side off-ball move from the low block or short corner to the high post or elbow, timed to arrive as the ball is entering or during a defensive shift. It attacks the seam between a high and low defender (zone) or pulls the help defender out of the paint (man). The flash is not a slow relocation — it is a timed, angular cut into an open window for a one-beat catch.

## When to Use
- Against 2-3 and 3-2 zones where the short-corner-to-elbow seam is exploitable
- Against man defenses that pre-rotate help on ball-side drives
- As a trigger to follow with a dribble-handoff or split-cut action once the flash catches

## Key Principles
1. Time the cut to the ball's release, not to the ball-handler's first look.
2. Attack the seam between defenders — the elbow area, not the free-throw line's center.
3. Shoulders turned to the ball on arrival. The catch must be shot-ready or pass-ready instantly.
4. Read on catch: high-post shot, dribble-drive into the lane, or dump-off to the sealing low-post.
5. If the pass does not arrive, clear to the opposite elbow — never stand still.

## Player Responsibilities
- **Cutter (4 or 5)**: timing, angle, shoulders-to-ball on arrival, three-way read on catch
- **Passer (1, 2, or 3)**: read cutter's shoulders, deliver chest-high, leave room for the read
- **Low-post teammate**: seal on the flash, prepare for dump-off

## Common Mistakes
- Cutter moves too early — defender tracks the flash, catch is contested
- Cutter arrives without shoulders-to-ball — burns a beat on the square-up, read options collapse
- Passer feeds the flash chest-high but after the cutter is past the seam — turnover or reset

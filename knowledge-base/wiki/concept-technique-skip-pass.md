# Skip Pass

## Summary
A skip pass is a cross-court delivery that bypasses the nearest adjacent receiver, targeting the opposite wing or corner. It exploits a defense that has rotated or shifted too far ball-side — typical vs a 2-3 zone or a scrambling man rotation. The pass is two-handed, thrown over the top of the defense with a slight arc, and lands in a shooter's pocket for a one-count catch-and-shoot. Skip passes that sail flat or arrive behind the receiver are costly turnovers.

## When to Use
- Against a zone that overloads ball-side coverage
- After a ball-reversal action where the defense is scrambling to close out
- Off a double team at the elbow or post — the skip punishes the trap's second defender
- As the release valve on a drive that stalls above the three-point line

## Key Principles
1. Two-hand delivery, ball over the head — a one-handed skip is a turnover waiting to happen.
2. Arc the pass — flat skips get tipped at the high point.
3. Deliver to the shooter's outside shoulder so the catch is square to the basket.
4. The passer's eyes hold the nearest help defender before the release — do not stare at the skip target.
5. Receiver is already squared up at the catch — no late turns.

## Player Responsibilities
- **Passer (1, 2, or 3)**: two-hand over-the-top delivery, eye hold on the near defender before release
- **Receiver (2 or 3)**: square to the basket on catch, shot-ready one-count
- **Weak-side big**: seal the low post or flash to the elbow on the defensive close-out

## Common Mistakes
- Passer telegraphs the skip — rotating defender closes the lane
- Receiver starts moving toward the ball instead of holding the catch window
- Skip thrown flat — high-point defender deflects

# Overhead Press (Basketball Strength Exercise)

## Objective
Develop shoulder strength (anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid, supraspinatus) and overhead stability for basketball skills performed above the rim — shooting, rebounding, and shot-blocking.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Squat rack with barbell at upper-chest height
- Appropriate weight plates
- **Safety Tip**: Do not arch or overextend the lower back when pressing overhead

## Execution
1. Stand in a squat rack with the barbell set at approximately upper-chest height.
2. Walk into the rack until the upper chest touches the bar.
3. Grip the barbell with hands slightly wider than shoulder width, thumbs wrapped around the bar.
4. Lift the bar slightly off the rack and position it on the upper chest and anterior deltoids.
5. Take one or two steps back; position feet slightly wider than shoulder width.
6. Maintain an erect posture. Take a deep inhalation, then press the barbell overhead with a slow, controlled exhalation — fully extending the arms. The bar travels directly over the head, concluding with arms fully extended and in line with both ears.
7. Slowly lower the barbell back to the upper chest, controlling the descent while maintaining erect posture.
8. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Bar travels in a straight line over the head" — no forward or backward drift
- Maintain erect posture — do not lean back or hyperextend the lumbar spine
- Core must be braced throughout — the standing position requires rectus abdominis and back extensors to stabilize the torso
- Arms fully extended at the top; bar in line with ears
- "Many athletes are weakest in overhead exercises" — start with conservative weight and build
- Control the descent — avoid dropping the bar back to the chest

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Seated dumbbell overhead press — reduces stability demand, allows independent arm training
2. **Intermediate**: Standing barbell overhead press at moderate weight (8–12 reps)
3. **Advanced**: Standing overhead press with progressive overload, or single-arm landmine press for rotational overhead strength

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid, supraspinatus
- **Secondary**: Pectoralis major, posterior deltoid, trapezius, supraspinatus, triceps brachii

## Basketball Transfer
Many basketball players are weakest overhead, yet the majority of basketball skills — shooting, rebounding, shot-blocking — occur above the rim. The overhead press directly builds the muscles needed for these actions. Standing variation also forces core stabilization that mimics game conditions. [S2, pp.78-79]

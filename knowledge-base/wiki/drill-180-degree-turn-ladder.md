# 180-Degree Turn (Agility Ladder Drill)

## Objective
Improve agility, balance, hip flexibility, and quickness by performing continuous 180-degree bilateral jumps through an agility ladder. [S8, p.114]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder laid flat on the court or grass
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance, straddling the first rung of the ladder (feet outside both edges, one foot on each side).
2. Jump and turn 180 degrees with both feet simultaneously.
3. Land straddling the next rung of the ladder, now facing the opposite direction.
4. Immediately repeat the half-turn into the next square.
5. Continue through the entire length of the agility ladder without pausing. [S8, p.114]

## Coaching Points
- Keep the jump tight and controlled — this is about rotation speed, not height
- Land softly on the balls of both feet simultaneously
- Keep the head up and core engaged through every rotation
- Maintain rhythm — hesitation between squares kills the drill's purpose
- "Land, turn, land" — get to a consistent rhythm cue

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow walk-through of the rotation pattern without jumping — feel the hip rotation
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed bilateral jumps as described
3. **Advanced**: Perform the drill facing perpendicular to the ladder (body sideways to the direction of travel) — increases hip rotation demands significantly [S8, p.114]

# Wall Slides

## Objective
Improve knee lift and enhance stride frequency by performing butt-kicker mechanics without allowing the heel of the recovery leg to travel behind the body.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Any open flat area, typically 20-40 yards
- Performed during warm-up or mechanics circuit after butt-kickers

## Execution
1. Perform the drill in the same way as [[drill-butt-kickers]]
2. Key constraint: **do not allow the heel of the recovery leg to travel behind the body**
3. The heel must stay in front of or directly under the body during recovery
4. This forces greater forward knee drive and improves overall knee lift mechanics [S8, p.27]

## Coaching Points
- The critical difference from butt-kickers: "Do not allow the heel of the recovery leg to travel behind the body"
- Keeping the heel in front requires more hip flexor activation and produces higher knee lift
- Athletes often let the heel swing back — coach must watch carefully from the side
- Maintain upright posture and correct arm mechanics

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk slowly, consciously restricting heel to front of body
2. **Intermediate**: Jogging pace maintaining the heel-forward constraint
3. **Advanced**: Increase speed; use as a progression cue in sprint warm-ups before speed runs [S8, p.27]

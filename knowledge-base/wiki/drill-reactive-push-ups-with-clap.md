# Reactive Push-Ups With Clap

## Objective
Improve upper-body power and reaction to a visual stimulus by performing explosive clap push-ups while mirroring a partner's movement. [S8, p.186]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Open space (gym floor or flat surface)
- Both athletes in push-up position, heads facing the **same direction**, positioned approximately **1 yard (0.9 meters) apart** side-by-side

## Execution
1. Both athletes assume a push-up position with heads facing the same direction, about 1 yard apart.
2. Athlete 1 performs a **clap push-up** (explosive push-up where hands leave the floor and clap before returning).
3. Athlete 2 **reacts to athlete 1's movement** and attempts to perform their own clap push-up at the **same time** as athlete 1.
4. The goal is to match the timing as closely as possible through visual reaction. [S8, p.186]

## Coaching Points
- Athlete 2 must watch Athlete 1 peripherally — don't telegraph the movement
- The reactive athlete should focus on the shoulder/hip initiation of the movement, not the hands
- Core must remain rigid — no sagging hips during the clap push-up
- This drill trains the same reactive neural pathways used in tip-passes, shot-blocking attempts, and deflections

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice standard clap push-ups individually before partnering
2. **Intermediate**: Partner reaction at normal distance (1 yard)
3. **Advanced**: Increase the reactive difficulty by having Athlete 1 vary timing and cadence unpredictably

## Concepts Taught
- [[upper-body-strength-pushing]] — the explosive clap push-up is an upper-body plyometric developing chest/shoulder power
- [[rate-of-force-development-basketball]] — reacting to a partner's movement and matching timing tests upper-body RFD
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — the push-up descent pre-loads the chest for an explosive concentric push-off

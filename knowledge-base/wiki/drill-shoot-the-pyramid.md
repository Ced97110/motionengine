# Shoot the Pyramid (6-5-4-3-2-Till You Miss)

## Objective
Build pressure shooting, consecutive make streaks, mental toughness, and free-throw discipline through a descending-pyramid consecutive-make challenge.

## Setup
- 1 player solo (can be done with multiple players competing simultaneously or in sequence)
- Half court; any area outside the paint
- 1 basketball
- Tracking log: Date / FGA / FGM / % / FTA / FTM / % / Did you shoot the pyramid? / Consecutive makes on "shoot till you miss"
- **No layups** — all shots must be jump shots or live-ball pull-ups off a dribble

## Execution

### Phase 1: Make 6 in a Row
1. Player takes shots (off one or two dribbles — live-ball moves) and tries to make 6 consecutive shots.
2. If a shot is missed at any point, the player stops, makes **2 consecutive free throws**, then restarts the attempt at 6.
3. The player stays at the "6" level until 6 consecutive shots are made.

### Phase 2: Make 5 in a Row
4. After completing 6 in a row, the player makes **2 free throws**, then attempts to make 5 consecutive shots.
5. Any miss → make 2 FTs → restart at 5 in a row.
6. Stay at level 5 until completed.

### Phase 3-5: Make 4, 3, 2 in a Row
7. Repeat the same structure descending through 4, 3, and 2 consecutive makes.
8. Each level requires 2 made FTs before moving on.

### Phase 6: Shoot Till You Miss
9. After completing "2 in a row," the player makes 2 FTs, then shoots continuously until they miss.
10. Record the consecutive make total as the final score for the session.

### Scoring / Competition
- **Goal:** Complete the full pyramid (6-5-4-3-2-till you miss) in the fewest total shot attempts possible.
- Track total FGA and total FGM across the session.
- The "shoot till you miss" streak at the end is the competitive highlight — players try to set personal records.

## Coaching Points
- **Pressure is the point** — the mandatory free throws after each miss simulate the psychological consequence of mistakes; players learn to reset and refocus
- **Enforce no-layup rule** — this is a skill drill, not a finishing drill; all shots should require footwork and mechanics
- **Encourage self-competition** — compare each session's total FGA to previous sessions; a lower FGA means fewer misses and better shooting efficiency
- **Use the free throws intentionally** — after a miss, the 2 FTs are a mindfulness reset: routine, breath, re-focus
- "The pyramid teaches you to stay calm after a miss — make your free throws and get right back to work."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Reduce pyramid to 4-3-2-till you miss; allow all spot shots (no mandatory dribble)
2. **Intermediate**: Full 6-5-4-3-2-till you miss with live-ball moves (SF, jab step, or rip step into pull-up)
3. **Advanced**: All three-point shots only; or impose a time limit per level (e.g., 60 seconds to make 6 in a row)

## Variations
### Three-Point Pyramid
All shots taken from behind the arc — develops three-point shot pressure and streak shooting.

### Live-Ball Moves Pyramid
Player must use a different scoring move (SF Stride Stop, SF Big Hop, Rip Step, jab-and-go, step-back, etc.) for each consecutive make — prevents reliance on one spot.

### Team Pyramid
Multiple players compete simultaneously on separate baskets or take turns; player who completes pyramid in fewest attempts wins a team competition reward.

# Penetration Drill (#113)

## Objective
Teach motion offense dribble penetration, help-side defensive positioning, and off-ball cut reads simultaneously in a controlled 3-on-3 half-court setting.

## Setup
- **Players**: 6 (3 offense, 3 defense); waiting players in lines at three perimeter spots
- **Court area**: Half court; coach with ball at the top of the key
- **Rotation**: Offense → Defense → End of line; rotate after 3 repetitions (1→2's line, 2→3's line, 3→1's line)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball with coach

<!-- DIAGRAM: Coach at top with ball. Players 1, 2, 3 at three perimeter spots (top of key and two wings) with defenders X1, X2, X3. Player 1 penetrates left; Player 2 has options (stay, backdoor, cut behind drive); Player 3 V-cuts. -->

## Execution
1. Players line up in three groups at three perimeter spots.
2. Coach begins with the basketball and passes to one attacker (in the diagram, Player 1). **All players are stationary until the coach passes the ball.**
3. Once Player 1 has the ball, all defenders make necessary adjustments — **two-thirds of the distance from the ball, one-third from their assignment**. All defenders jump to the ball.
4. **First phase (positioning)**: Player 1 dribble penetrates right or left. Off-ball attackers (2 and 3) stay motionless. Coach holds the drill until convinced defenders have reached proper positioning. Then Player 1 passes to 2 or 3 — drill continues.
5. **Second phase (cuts added)**: Once defenders are positioning correctly, allow cuts.
   - Player nearest the penetration drive: option to stay, cut backdoor, or cut behind the dribbling Player 1
   - Far-side player (Player 3): V-cuts to keep their defender occupied
6. Once Player 2 or 3 receives the pass, they execute a dribble penetration move. The remaining two offensive players make the same reads described for Players 2 and 3 in the original drive.
7. **Option**: Allow only layups to create more dribbling penetration practice reps.

## Coaching Points
- "You are teaching motion offensive rules by adding dribbling penetration to your cuts and screens and 1v1 play" [S15, p.228]
- Make sure defenders close the gap properly when the ball is driven at them
- Make sure defenders close out properly when the ball is passed after penetration
- Penetration must result in a layup, easy jump shot after separation, or a perfect pass — "don't let attackers get lazy with their execution"
- Hold the drill motionless to fix positioning before allowing live play — see the problem, stop, correct, resume

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Positioning phase only — drive, freeze, check defender positions, pass
2. **Intermediate**: Add cuts — nearest player reads drive and cuts or stays; far player V-cuts
3. **Advanced**: Full live 3v3 with all cuts, passes, and secondary penetrations — continuous until a score or defensive stop

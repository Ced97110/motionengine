# Monkey Flip

## Objective
Bridge the gap between the floor-started back macaco and the standing back handspring by learning to initiate the backward rotational pattern from a standing position with explosive hip drive.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Open flat floor space (minimum 6 feet clearance behind)
- No equipment required
- **Prerequisite**: Comfortable back macaco (Step 5)

## Execution
1. Begin **standing** with feet fairly close together.
2. Squat down while dropping your pivot palm toward the floor — begin to explode **before** the palm touches down; your loaded palm should be just about to make contact as your feet leave the floor.
3. As you explode up, thrust through the hips and swing the other arm up over your head and next to your ear, twisting to reach the ground behind you.
4. Reach for the floor with your non-loaded (swinging) arm as you swing your legs up and over your head — lead with the swinging arm.
5. Flip fully over yourself and touch down with your feet behind where your back was.
6. As your feet touch down, push through your arms and use momentum to stand up.

## Coaching Points
- The key difference from back macaco: "In the macaco, your hand is already on the floor when you start. In the monkey flip, you jump from standing — your hand is OFF the floor." [S9, p.189]
- "The backwards fall/jump should ADD to your swinging momentum — don't fight it."
- Watch for athletes placing the hand down too early (turning it into a back macaco); the timing shift is the whole point of this step
- Inner-elbow still must face out when pivot hand makes contact — same shoulder safety rule applies

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Begin in a semi-squat with pivot hand only about 1 inch from the ground — build to greater distances over time [S9, p.189]
2. **Intermediate**: Hand is 6–12 inches from the floor at the start of the jump; feet clear the floor before hand contact.
3. **Advanced**: Full standing start with delayed and wide pivot arm placement — pivot arm swings further back from the body with each attempt.

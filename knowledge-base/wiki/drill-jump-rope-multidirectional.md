# Jump Rope With Multidirectional Jumps

## Objective
Improve lower-body power and quickness by combining jump rope with rapid foot-position changes, training multi-planar ankle and leg elasticity. [S8, p.178]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Standard jump rope
- Open space

## Execution
1. Begin jumping rope.
2. Start in a **straddle position** — feet hip-width apart.
3. After jumping rope, switch to a **split-foot position** — one foot forward, one foot back.
4. After the next jump, return to the straddle position.
5. Continue repeating this sequence (straddle → split → straddle) as **fast as possible**. [S8, p.178]

## Coaching Points
- The transition between foot positions should be instant — don't pause between position changes
- Emphasize quick, light contacts — think "hot coals"
- Maintain good posture; do not lean or hunch over
- Eyes forward, not on the rope

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Basic jump rope in straddle only to establish rhythm
2. **Intermediate**: Add the split-foot pattern as described
3. **Advanced**: Increase rotation speed; add a third jump position (e.g., crossover foot) for an additional sequence jump

## Concepts Taught
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — rapid rope jumps develop ankle elastic strength
- [[concept-lower-body-plyometrics]] — multi-planar foot positions mirror the lateral and linear stances used in basketball
- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — multidirectional jumps challenge ankle coordination under rapid movement demand

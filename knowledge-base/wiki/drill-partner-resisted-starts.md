# Partner-Resisted Starts

## Objective
Enhance starting power and stride length by running against a partner-applied resistance during the first 8–10 strides of acceleration.

## Setup
- 2 players (runner + resistance partner)
- Open flat surface — court, field, or track
- Optional: a towel around the runner's waist as a resistance tool
- No other equipment required

## Execution
1. The runner assumes their starting position of choice.
2. The resistance partner positions themselves in one of two ways:
   - **Front-resistance**: Partner stands in front of the runner, placing both hands on the runner's shoulders and pushing back.
   - **Rear-resistance**: Partner stands behind the runner, using hands or a towel looped around the runner's waist to resist the forward drive.
3. At signal, the runner explosively drives forward while the partner provides steady resistance.
4. The drill ends after 8 to 10 strides.
5. Switch roles after each set.

## Coaching Points
- Resistance partner should apply consistent, moderate pressure — enough to challenge but not break down running mechanics.
- Runner should maintain a powerful forward lean, not allowing the resistance to cause them to stand upright too early.
- Drive arms explosively — "pump the arms and the legs will follow."
- Towel variation allows for a clean, natural arm action without partner interference.
- Keep sets short — quality over quantity; full rest between reps.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Front-resistance method, 6 strides, light partner pressure
2. **Intermediate**: Rear-resistance (towel), 8–10 strides, moderate pressure
3. **Advanced**: Rear-resistance, 10 strides, heavy partner resistance, immediately followed by a free sprint (contrast)

# Reverse Pivot Drill (Youth)

## Objective
Teach players to execute a proper reverse pivot after a dribble stop, developing ball protection and passing-out-of-pressure footwork.

## Setup
- Players line up along the baseline in multiple lines (one line per lane)
- Each player in the first position has a basketball
- Full court or half court
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up on the baseline in multiple vertical lines (see diagram — 5 lines across)
2. The first player in each line dribbles (control or speed dribble) to the far endline
3. At the endline, the player executes a jump stop or stride stop
4. The player then performs the reverse pivot (swing free foot away from the defender)
5. After the pivot, the player passes to the second player in line using a chest pass, bounce pass, or overhead pass
6. The second player dribbles to the endline and repeats

### Intermediate
- Each player executes a controlled dribble anywhere on the court
- When coach calls "reverse pivot," every player jump-stops and executes the reverse pivot immediately
- On "go," players start dribbling again
- Variation: players execute dribbling moves (spin dribble, etc.) while waiting for the reverse pivot command

## Coaching Points
- **Establish the pivot foot**: Left foot is the pivot foot; plant it firmly before lifting the right foot
- **Ball to chin**: At the end of the stop, bring the ball just under the chin with both hands (no palms) — this is the triple-threat stance
- **Swing away from defender**: Pick up the right foot and pivot 180 degrees by swinging it away from the defender (not toward them — that is the front pivot)
- **The reverse pivot moves players farther away from their defender** — this is its key distinguishing feature from the front pivot
- Watch for traveling: pivot foot must never move after it is established
- Advantages to teach: allows watching teammates' cuts longer, necessary for the screen-and-roll (a front pivot lets the defender recover to stop the roll), moves away from basket at end of scoring fake

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Dribble to endline, jump stop, reverse pivot, stationary pass to line partner
2. **Intermediate**: Coach command-based reverse pivots with controlled dribble anywhere on court; add spin dribble variations while waiting for command
3. **Advanced**: Combine dribbling move + jump stop + reverse pivot; also add step-through move and dribble twice before reestablishing pivot feet via jump stop or stride stop

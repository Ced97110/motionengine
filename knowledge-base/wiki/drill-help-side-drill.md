# Help-Side Drill

## Objective
Train off-ball defenders to immediately jump toward the ball on a pass, establish a flat triangle with proper 2/3-1/3 positioning, and continuously adjust using peripheral vision as both the ball and assignment move.

## Setup
- Half court
- 3 players: player 1 (ball → assignment), player 2 (ball handler on the wing), X1 (defender)
- Rotation: 1 → 2 → X1 → 1 every 10 seconds
- Duration: 2 minutes total

## Execution
**Beginner:**
1. Player 1 begins with the ball; X1 is in proper on-ball stance (front-foot-to-pivot-foot, front-foot-to-free-foot, or parallel).
2. Player 1 **skip passes to player 2**. X1 is now a help-side defender.
3. As the pass is in the air, X1 **jumps toward the ball** — sliding into the flat triangle (2/3 distance from ball, 1/3 from assignment).
4. X1 gets into the flat triangle in **interception stance**.
5. Player 2 holds the ball 2 seconds; then player 1 begins moving up and down the court.
6. X1 must constantly adjust the flat triangle using fence-slide steps, always seeing both ball and assignment without moving the head.
7. Rotate after 10 seconds.

**Options:**
1. Allow player 2 to dribble the ball up and down the sideline — X1 must adjust as the ball moves
2. Allow both: player 1 moves up/down the court AND player 2 dribbles the ball along the sideline
3. Allow player 1 to go 1-on-1 against X1 to keep X1 honest in help-side coverage

**Intermediate:**
1. Allow player 2 to drive to the basket → X1 activates the rotation
2. Allow player 2 to drive AND X1 must draw the charge

## Coaching Points
1. X1 must **jump toward the ball as the pass is in the air**, not after the catch
2. Target position: "two-thirds distance from the ball and one-third distance from the assignment" [S15, p.274]
3. X1 must be in **interception stance** with fence-slide step readiness
4. "X1 must see both using peripheral vision" — not looking at either ball or assignment but both simultaneously [S15, p.275]
5. When player 1 moves, X1 uses the fence-slide step to maintain the flat triangle — head stays level
6. When player 2 dribbles, the reference point (ball) moves — X1 must slide to maintain the 2/3 ratio relative to the new ball position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Ball stationary, assignment stationary — practice jump-to-ball and flat triangle setup only
2. **Intermediate**: Ball stationary, assignment moves — X1 adjusts triangle while maintaining interception stance
3. **Advanced**: Both ball and assignment move; player 2 can drive; X1 reads, adjusts, and helps

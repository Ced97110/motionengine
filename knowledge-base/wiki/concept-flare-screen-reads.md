# Flare Screen Reads — the source coach

## Summary
A flare screen is set by the high post (with his back to the sideline) for a teammate above the three-point line or at the free-throw line extended. With the ball in the hands of a player past the free-throw line extended, the high post sets a side screen. The screened player reads whether to flare away (for an open three) or cut back behind the screen. [S7, p.56]

## When to Use
- When a perimeter shooter needs to get open off the three-point line against a defender who is overplaying the baseline cut
- As a secondary action after other screen actions freeze the defense
- When the post player can set a screen from a high elbow or wing position

## Formation
- Ball handler (1) past the free-throw line extended with the ball.
- Screener (5) at the high post, back to the sideline, setting a side (flare) screen for the cutter (2).
- Cutter (2) positioned to use the screen and attack toward the three-point line.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Setting the Screen
- Screener (5) positions at the high post with back to the sideline.
- Establishes wide, low stance at 90° to the defender's path.
- Cutter (2) sets up the defender and reads the defender's positioning.

### Phase 2: The Two Reads

**Read 1 — Defender Slides Behind the Screen** [S7, p.56]
- Defender goes under (behind) the screen — taking an inside path — which opens the perimeter.
- **Cutter (2)**: **Flares away** from the screen, moving toward the three-point line for an open catch-and-shoot.
- **Screener (5)**: Cuts to the basket (opposite of the cutter's direction) as the secondary scoring option.

**Read 2 — Defender Anticipates the Flare Cut** [S7, p.56]
- Defender reads the flare cut early and fights over the screen to stay between the cutter and the three-point line.
- **Cutter (2)**: Cuts **behind the screen** (back-cut to the basket) to exploit the overplaying defender.
- **Screener (5)**: Comes **high** as a safety valve for the ball handler.

## Key Coaching Points
- The flare screen is most effective when the cutter has previously cut baseline (conditioning the defender to chase baseline), then uses the flare screen to go the opposite direction.
- The screener's back-to-sideline position is critical — it creates the proper angle for the defender to have difficulty fighting over.
- Ball handler must read the cutter's final direction and deliver a pass in rhythm — on the flare, the catch-and-shoot must happen immediately.
- Screener should sprint to the basket on Read 1 — this creates a simultaneous two-threat attack: perimeter shooter + rim cutter.

## Counters
- If the defense switches: screener (5) is now on a perimeter player — exploit with a post-up.
- If the defense denies the flare pass: look for screener (5) slip to the basket before the screen is fully set.

# Flat Pop-Up (Power Pushup Step 3)

## Objective
Develop explosive upper-body pushing power from the floor, generating progressive airtime as the foundation for all clap and body-strike pushup variations.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor surface (smooth or padded)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Squat down, place palms on the floor, and stretch legs out behind
2. Palms below shoulders, at shoulder-width or wider
3. Feet at a comfortable width (beginners wider, experts closer)
4. Keep legs, hips, and torso aligned and horizontal — no sagging or piking
5. Bend arms and shoulders, dipping torso ~1/3 of the way toward the floor
6. Immediately push explosively off the floor, straightening arms
7. Push hard enough to allow hands to leave the floor
8. As gravity reverses movement, "catch" the floor with hands again
9. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat — no pause at bottom

## Coaching Points
- "Your hands will barely clear the floor at first — that's okay. Build height over time" [S9, p.89]
- This is the **basis of almost all explosive pushups** — master it before clap pushups (Step 4)
- Many athletes fail at clap pushups because they skipped pop-ups; pop-ups are the bridge [S9, p.88]
- Focus on maximizing airtime progressively — treat each session as an opportunity to get the hands slightly higher
- Keep the body rigid — any worming (hip sag) reduces the explosive quality

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Try to generate minimal hand clearance from floor; slow regular pushups → increase speed until hands leave floor
2. **Intermediate**: Build steady airtime; hands clearing floor by 1-3 inches consistently
3. **Advanced**: Maximize airtime height; when hands fly upward easily, advance to Step 4 (clap pushup)

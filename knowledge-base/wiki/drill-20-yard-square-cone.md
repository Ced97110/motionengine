# 20-Yard Square

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction, body position, transitions between movement skills, and cutting ability through a four-corner cone drill.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4 cones at corners of a 5 × 5 yard square
- Cone 1: start (bottom-left), Cone 2 (top-left), Cone 3 (top-right), Cone 4 (bottom-right)

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance at cone 1.
2. Sprint 5 yards (4.6 m) forward to cone 2 and make a sharp right cut.
3. Shuffle right 5 yards (4.6 m) to cone 3 and make a sharp cut back left.
4. Backpedal 5 yards (4.6 m) to cone 4 and make a sharp left cut.
5. Left-shuffle 5 yards (4.6 m) back to cone 1.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Square with cones at corners; Cone 1 (bottom-left, start) → sprint up to Cone 2 → shuffle right to Cone 3 → backpedal down to Cone 4 → shuffle left back to Cone 1. [S8, p.90] -->

## Coaching Points
- Each corner is a direction change AND a movement skill transition — practice each transition type
- Stay low throughout — the backpedal segment especially tempts players to stand tall
- Sharp cuts at each cone — do not round the corners
- Plant on the inside foot at each corner for efficient direction change

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk-through, identify each movement skill before executing
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed, timed
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Start from different positions (lying, four-point stance) [S8, p.90]
   - Change cone distances to match sport demands
   - Change the movement skill on each leg for variety
   - Cut with inside or outside leg at each cone
   - Cut outside of cone or circle around it
   - Put inside hand on ground during turns

# Explosive Dip Variations (Power Dips)

## Objective
Develop upper-body speed-strength and reactive pressing power on the parallel bars through progressive explosive dip variations — from walking dips to clap dips to 180-degree power dips.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Parallel bars (standard gym dip bars or outdoor playground bars)
- No additional equipment
- **Prerequisite**: Athlete must be able to perform several strict parallel bar dips before attempting any explosive variation [S9, p.238]

## Execution

### Variation 1: Walking Dips
- Perform a standard parallel bar dip, then "step" one hand along the bar.
- Forces joints and muscles to absorb and redirect a small shock load as the hand relocates [S9, p.238].
- **The foundation of all explosive dip work**.

### Variation 2: Hopping Dips
- From the top of the dip position, perform a press powerful enough to **lift both hands off the bars**.
- Land softly, re-gripping the bars, then repeat.
- As power increases, elevate higher on each hop.
- Variation: **Long-hop dips** — hop forward along the bar (and backward) instead of just up and down [S9, p.239].

### Variation 3: Clap Dips
- From the top of the dip, perform an explosive hop that lifts the hands, **clap both hands together** in the air, then re-grip the bars.
- Arms must move entire bodyweight explosively — significantly harder than clap push-ups.
- **Warning**: Build up progressively. Attempting clap dips before sufficient hopping power is an injury risk [S9, p.240].

### Variation 4: 180 Power Dips
- Explosive dip hop with enough power and agility to **spin 180° in mid-air** and re-catch the bar facing the opposite direction.
- Adds explosive power AND rotational agility to the pressing movement [S9, p.240].
- Progression target: 360° spin (extremely rare/advanced).

### Variation 5: Swing Dips
- Dipping with a lateral swinging component along the bars.
- Combines pressing power with lateral dynamic control.

## Coaching Points
- **Progress sequentially**: Walking → Hopping → Clap → 180 Power. Do not skip steps [S9, p.238].
- **Clap dips are brutal**: "If you think clap pushups are tough, try this number, where your arms have to move your entire bodyweight explosively!" [S9, p.240]. Build up carefully.
- **Hopping dips develop reactive catching**: The hands and forearms get a plyometric workout by catching the bars on landing — train this consciously.
- **180 power dips**: Add a rotational agility challenge on top of pure pressing power — valuable for basketball court agility.
- **Korean Dips** (bar behind the body): An extreme advanced variation where the dip bar is placed behind — requires elite shoulder mobility and strength [S9, p.237].
- **L-Hold Dips**: Parallel bar dips with legs locked out in an L-sit position — shifts center of gravity forward, significantly harder while also training the midsection [S9, p.237].

## Progressions
1. **Entry**: Walking dips — minimal shock load
2. **Intermediate**: Hopping dips — lift off the bar
3. **Advanced**: Clap dips — full upper-body plyometric
4. **Elite**: 180 power dips — explosive + rotational
5. **Superhuman**: Korean dips (one-arm dips off a wall top) [S9, p.237]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Attempting clap dips before mastering hopping dips** → injury risk; build sufficient hop height first.
2. **Landing with stiff elbows** → absorb the catch by allowing slight elbow bend before reversing.
3. **Insufficient power from the press** → return to strict parallel bar dips to build base strength.

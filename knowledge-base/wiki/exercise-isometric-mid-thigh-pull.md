# Isometric Mid-Thigh Pull

## Objective
Develop and assess maximal lower-body and total-body force production via an isometric pull at the mid-thigh position — a direct indicator of peak strength for basketball's jumping and sprinting demands.

## Setup
- Power rack with safety catches set at **mid-thigh height**
- Barbell placed **under** the safety catches (catches prevent the bar from moving)
- Optional: a dedicated isometric mid-thigh pull device with a fixed bar
- Optional: force plate beneath the athlete for quantifying output [S11, Ch.6]
- 1 athlete; no spotter required (isometric — bar does not move)

## Execution
1. Stand with feet **hip-width apart**, bar near the mid-thigh
2. Grip the bar with a **closed pronated grip**, hands shoulder-width apart, elbows fully extended
3. Set the chest up, shoulders back, neutral spine
4. With chest up and elbows fully extended, **attempt to extend the legs and hips** while pulling as hard as possible into the immovable bar
5. Exert **maximal effort for approximately 5 seconds**
6. Relax and return to the beginning position
7. Rest adequately between trials [S11, Ch.6]

**Breathing:** Deep breath before initiating; exhale during the maximal effort to extend legs and hips

## Coaching Points
- **"Pull as hard as possible"** — this is a maximal effort exercise; partial efforts defeat the purpose
- **"Push the feet into the floor"** — think leg drive, not just arm pull; total-body activation
- Maintain neutral head position throughout
- Do not allow the lower back to round — proximal stability is essential
- Ideal for testing: use a force plate to measure peak force and rate of force development (RFD)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice the body position (hip hinge at mid-thigh) without load before adding bar contact
2. **Intermediate**: Standard isometric mid-thigh pull — 3-5 trials of 3-5 seconds each
3. **Advanced**: Perform on a force plate to quantify peak force and RFD; use single-leg variation to identify bilateral asymmetries; trap bar variation for athletes with grip limitations

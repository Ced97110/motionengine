# Miami Heat Pick-and-Roll into Baseline Screening Action

## Overview
Pat Riley's system uses PnR as the surface action to trigger primary baseline scoring for Miami's shooters. Tim Hardaway (1) runs the wing PnR with Alonzo Mourning (5); however, the true objective is freeing Dan Majerle (2) and Jamal Mashburn (3) off baseline screens for jump shots. Hardaway will still shoot or drive if the defense misplays the initial PnR. [S14, pp.39]

## Formation
- **1** (Hardaway) handles the ball on the left wing sideline
- **5** (Mourning) is the PnR screener
- **2** (Majerle), **3** (Mashburn), and **4** (P.J. Brown) form a **triple stack on the weak side** (right side)

## Phases

### Phase 1: Wing Pick-and-Roll
- **1** dribbles the ball down the sideline to the left wing position
- **1** dribbles off the ball screen set by **5**
- **5** rolls after the screen

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.12a — 1 dribbles left wing. 5 screens for 1 and rolls. 2, 3, 4 in triple stack on right (weak) side. -->

### Phase 2: Simultaneous Baseline Screen Cuts
- As **1** dribbles off **5**'s screen:
  - **5** rolls, then **sets a down screen for 2**
  - **2** cuts off **5**'s down screen
  - **4** (bottom of triple stack) sets a down screen for **3**
  - **3** dips into the lane and cuts off **4** toward the wing
- **1** continues toward the top of the key

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.12b — After PnR, 5 rolls then down-screens for 2. 4 sets down-screen for 3. 2 and 3 cut off their respective screens toward wings/top. 1 at top of key with ball. -->

### Phase 3: Three Scoring Options for 1
1. **Option 1**: Jump shot for **1** off the PnR if the defense misplays
2. **Option 2**: Pass to **2** cutting off **5** for a jump shot, or **post feed to 5** (after 5 down-screened)
3. **Option 3**: Pass to **3** cutting off **4** for a jump shot, or **post feed to 4**

## Key Coaching Points
- This play uses the PnR as a decoy — Hardaway is dangerous enough to force defensive attention, which opens the baseline cutters
- **5**'s sequence** — roll → set down screen — is the creative element that makes this play unique; the defender on 5 must defend the roll AND the screen
- Both post players (4 and 5) are secondary post-up options after they set the baseline screens
- The triple stack alignment disguises which players will use which screens until the last moment

## Counters
- If defense loads up to stop **1**'s drive, **2** and **3** come open off the screens
- If the screener's defender sags to help, **4** or **5** can pop to the elbow after screening
- If X2 chases **2** hard, **2** can fake the curl and backdoor

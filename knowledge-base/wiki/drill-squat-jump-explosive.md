# Squat Jump — Explosive (Power Jump Chain — Step 2)

## Objective
Build total lower-body jumping power by integrating the hips and knees into the jump pattern, progressing from the ankle-only straight hop to a full-body explosive jump.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface — no equipment required
- Minimal space needed

## Execution
1. Stand feet slightly wider than shoulder-width, body flexed for action
2. Dip down into a squat — approximately 1/3 of the way down (not quite a half squat) for most athletes; advanced athletes can go to thighs parallel
3. Explode upward using full lower-body power plus total-body spring
4. Keep arms reasonably neutral at first (add arm swing in Step 3)
5. After take-off, allow legs to move naturally — do not pull them up or thrust them down
6. Land softly on balls of feet
7. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- "The cornerstone of all jumping techniques" [S9, p.14] — every beginner should master this before progressing
- Optimal knee bend is ~1/3 squat for most players — "bending the knees too acutely will reduce your explosive potential" [S9, p.14]
- "Lead with the hips as if sitting down" — prevents the common forward-trunk-hinge error
- Watch for asymmetrical take-off (one foot pushing harder) — corrects by cueing hip symmetry
- More knee bend = more work on knees at take-off and landing; appropriate for advanced athletes only

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Quarter-squat jumps — halfway between straight hop and full squat jump
2. **Intermediate**: Standard squat jump at 1/3 depth; elastic rebound continuous reps
3. **Advanced**: Below-parallel depth (strengthens knees and glutes, but reduces explosive height); closer stance increases difficulty

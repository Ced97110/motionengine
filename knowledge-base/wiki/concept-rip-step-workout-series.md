# Rip Step Workout Series

## Summary
The Rip Step Workout Series (Workouts #2-5 through #2-18) is a complete progressive training system for developing the rip step move and all four finishing options off it: stride stop jump shot, stride stop step-through layup/floater, big hop two-foot finish, hop back three-pointer, and hop back with a roll-out counter dribble.

Each finish type is trained in three volume tiers (80, 120, and 240 total shots) with both 1-dribble and 2-dribble variations, practiced going left (L) and right (R) in every workout. Free throws are interspersed between every set to simulate game pressure and build mental reset capacity. Each workout has quantified shooting targets for FG% and FT% to drive accountability.

## When to Use
- As a dedicated individual skill development workout (pre-practice, post-practice, or off-day)
- When building a player's ability to create shots off the dribble from the wing or elbow
- When a player needs to develop a specific finishing move to complement their existing game
- Progressively across a season: start at 80 shots, build to 120, then 240 as conditioning and skill improve

## Key Principles
1. **Bilateral development**: Every finish is practiced going left AND right — equal reps each side every workout
2. **1-dribble before 2-dribble**: Shorter dribble variations come first to build decisiveness; 2-dribble extends the range
3. **Free throws between every set**: Integrates FT practice into the skill workout; builds composure and total-body volume
4. **Quantified targets**: Each workout specifies FG% targets (typically 60%) and FT% targets (80–85%) to hold players accountable
5. **Progressive volume**: Three tiers (80 → 120 → 240 shots) allow scaling to player fitness and available practice time
6. **Shot charting**: Every session is logged with Date, FGA, FGM, %, FTA, FTM, % (and 3PTA/3PTM for hop-back workouts) to track improvement over time

## Common Mistakes
1. **Skipping bilateral practice** → always alternate L and R — asymmetric players are easily scouted and stopped
2. **Rushing through free throws** → FT sets between reps are mandatory; they build real game FT muscle memory
3. **Not tracking numbers** → the built-in shot charts are essential for identifying improvement and accountability
4. **Jumping volume too quickly** → start at 80-shot workouts and only advance to 120+ when hitting the 60% FG target consistently
5. **Treating finishes as interchangeable** → each finish (stride stop, step-through, big hop, hop back) is a distinct read with different footwork; train them separately before combining

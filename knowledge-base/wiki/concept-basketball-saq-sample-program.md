# Basketball SAQ Sample Program (6-Week Mesocycle)

## Summary
This is the sample sport-specific 6-week SAQ (Speed, Agility, Quickness) mesocycle for basketball (and netball) from *Training for Speed, Agility, and Quickness* (S8). It targets the key physical qualities for both ends of the floor: **defensive** jumping/lateral mobility and **offensive** first-step explosiveness and vertical jump. The program is built on a skill-mastery-then-complexity framework — weeks 1–3 develop fundamental movement patterns; weeks 4–6 combine drills and add sport-specific actions. [S8, p.233]

## Program Goals
- **Defense**: Improve jumping ability for rebounding and blocking; improve lateral mobility for coverage and change of direction
- **Offense**: Improve jumping ability for shooting; improve first-step quickness and acceleration for breakaways

## 6-Week Program Schedule

### Weeks 1–3: Skill Mastery Phase

| Need | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Speed** | A-March Walk (#6) | A-Skips for Distance (v) (#7) | A-Skips for Height (v) (#7) |
| **Speed** | A-Form Runs (v) (#7) | Partner-Resisted Starts (#35) | Bullet Belt (#39) |
| **Agility** | 20-Yard Shuttle (#53) | Lateral 20-Yard Shuttle (v) (#53) | 30-Yard T-Drill (#54) |
| **Agility** | Medicine-Ball Wall Chest Passes (#124) | Medicine-Ball Overhead Throw (#127) | Medicine-Ball Wall Scoop Toss (#128) |
| **Quickness** | Repeated Vertical Jumps (v) (#139) | Standing Long Jump (#140) | Power Skips (#142) |

### Weeks 4–6: Complexity Phase

| Need | Week 4 | Week 5 | Week 6 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Speed, Agility, Quickness** | Squirm (#55) | X-Pattern Multiskill (#65) | Z-Pattern Cuts (#69) |
| **Speed, Agility, Quickness** | Hexagon Drill (#104) | Hexagon Drill (#104) | Drop and Get Up (#103) |
| **Speed and Quickness** | Ladder Speed Runs (#10) | Hop-Scotch Drill to catch a pass (v) (#150) | One-Leg Hop to dribble and lay-up (v) (#151) |
| **Speed and Quickness** | Repeated Vertical Jumps (#139) | Vertical Jump to Sprint (#139) | Sprint to Vertical Jump (#139) |
| **Agility and Quickness** | Backward Icky Shuffle (#192) | Medicine-Ball Lateral Shuffle/Pass (#114) | Medicine-Ball Lateral Shuffle/Pass with one partner leading (v) (#114) |
| **Agility and Quickness** | Four-Point Pop-Up to 20-Yard Shuttle (#172) | Sit-to-Stand Pop-Up to Z-Pattern Run (#173, 167) | Lying-to-Stand Pop-Up to 30-Yard T-Drill (#174, 54) |

## Key Coaching Points
- The program embeds **basketball-specific tasks** in the complexity phase (catch a pass, dribble, lay-up) to bridge SAQ training and on-court performance [S8, p.233]
- **Vertical jump** appears across multiple weeks — it is a primary physical quality for both ends of the floor in basketball
- **Lateral work** increases in complexity from basic shuttles to medicine-ball lateral passes to backward ladder patterns
- **Pop-up drills** in weeks 4–6 simulate getting up from loose ball situations and going immediately into court speed patterns
- Adjust volume and intensity per individual athlete — this is a template, not a prescription [S8, p.223]

# Orlando Magic Angled Wing Pick-and-Roll

## Overview
Developed by Chuck Daly and continued by Doc Rivers, the Orlando "angled" PnR runs the ball screen at a 45-degree angle rather than the standard side or high alignment. This angle defeats teams that try to force the ball toward the sideline or trap, because the ball handler has a large open area to attack if overplayed. Darrell Armstrong (1) drove off Horace Grant (4). Weak-side staggered screens occupy the defense and eliminate quick rotations to the roll man. [S14, pp.33-35]

## Formation
- **1** brings the ball up the sideline, positioned higher than normal
- **4** steps off the elbow and sets the screen **midway between the elbow and three-point line** — establishing a 45-degree angle with the ball handler
- **5** and **3** align inside the three-point line on the weak side
- **2** works off the right block
- Stagger: **5** and **3** set staggered screens for **2** as **1** crosses **4**

## Phases

### Phase 1: Angled Pick-and-Roll
- **1** dribbles up the sideline, keeping the ball higher than normal to establish the 45-degree angle
- **4** sets the screen midway between elbow and three-point line
- **1** crosses **4** to the middle off the pick-and-roll
- Simultaneously, **5** and **3** begin the staggered screens for **2** on the weak side

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.7 — 1 dribbles sideline, 4 screens at 45-degree angle midway elbow-to-3PT. On weak side, 5 and 3 stagger-screen for 2 off right block as 1 crosses 4. -->

### Phase 2: Four Options for 1
1. **Option 1**: **1** shoots the **jump shot** if the defender goes under on the PnR
2. **Option 2**: **1** passes to **4**, who **pops** after setting the screen — jump shot
3. **Option 3**: **1** passes to **5**, who **slips into the lane** after setting the screen for 2
4. **Option 4**: **1** passes to **2**, who has **cut off the double screen** on the weak side

### Phase 3: Secondary Off 2's Catch
- If **2** catches and the jump shot is unavailable:
  - **2** swings to **3**, who pops to the corner
  - **5** punches into the low post for an inside feed

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.8 — 2 has caught off weak-side double screen. 3 pops to corner. 5 establishes low post on ball side. 2 reads feed-or-swing. -->

### Phase 4: Forced Sideline Counter
- If the defense forces **1** to the sideline or cheats toward the screen:
  - **1** drives to the **outside** (the defense has committed inside)
  - Only **4**'s defender can help → **4 pops back and spots up** for pass-and-jump-shot
  - If pass reaches **4**: **5** and **3** double-screen for **2** (who has reset)
  - **4** looks for shot or pass to **2** off the double screen

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 3.9 — 1 drives sideline. 4 pops for kick-out. 5 and 3 set double screen for 2. 4 passes to 2 off double screen. -->

## Key Coaching Points
- The 45-degree angle is the key differentiator: if the defense forces sideline, the ball handler has a wide-open lane; if they trap, **4**'s pop is automatic
- **The double-screen action on the weak side occupies the defense** and eliminates quick rotations to the roll man [S14, p.35]
- The stagger occurs as **1** crosses **4** to the middle — timing is essential
- Need a PG who can drive if overplayed, paired with a screener who can make the perimeter jump shot

## Counters
- If defense traps: **4** pops → **5** slips → **2** off double screen (three safety valves)
- If defense goes under: **1** shoots the pull-up off the PnR
- If X4 sags to protect against the roll: **4** pops wide open

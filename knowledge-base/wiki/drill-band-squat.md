# Band Squat (ACL Ligament Dominance)

## Objective
Strengthen the gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, and quadriceps to prevent dynamic knee valgus (knock-knee collapse) during cutting and changing direction.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface (gym floor, weight room)
- Equipment: elastic resistance band

## Execution
1. Place an elastic band just below both knees. Choose resistance that allows you to maintain both legs shoulder-width apart and knees in proper alignment throughout the movement.
2. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out.
3. Perform a squat until the thighs are parallel to the floor.
4. While in the bottom position, **continue to apply outward tension to the band**, actively pushing the knees out against the resistance — this is the key activation cue for the gluteus medius and gluteus minimus.
5. Return to standing and repeat.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- Cue "push your knees out" continuously throughout the movement, especially at the bottom
- "Knees should track over the second toe — not cave inward"
- Watch for the knees caving toward each other (dynamic valgus) — this is the exact pattern you are training the player to eliminate
- Keep the torso upright; avoid excessive forward lean
- Beginners: start with the lightest resistance band available and increase resistance as strength develops [S2, p.166]
- Maintain proper foot position: attention to knee placement is as important as the band itself

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Lightest resistance band, bodyweight only, focus on alignment
2. **Intermediate**: Medium resistance band; add a brief pause at the bottom (2 seconds) while driving knees out
3. **Advanced**: Heavy resistance band; add a loaded barbell or dumbells; band stays on as external feedback

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius), gluteus medius, gluteus minimus
- **Secondary**: Tensor fasciae latae, hamstrings (semitendinosus, biceps femoris, semimembranosus)

# Triangle Offense: Line of Deployment

## Summary
The "line of deployment" is a foundational concept within the triangle offense. It describes an imaginary line drawn from the ball-side forward (with the ball) through the low-post center (the target), the center's defender, and the basket. Mastering this concept is the primary mechanism by which the triangle offense successfully delivers the ball to the post despite concentrated defensive efforts to prevent it. [S7, pp.92-93]

## When to Use
- Any time the low-post center is the primary target for a triangle offense entry
- When the post defender plays standard (between center and basket) or overplays (high or baseline side)
- As the read framework for the ball-side forward deciding where to deliver the entry pass

## Key Principles
1. **The center shapes up on the line.** The center must position himself on the line of deployment — maintaining the ball-side forward, himself, his defender, and the basket in a straight line.
2. **Standard defense = direct pass.** When the center's defender plays standard (between the center and the basket), the forward can pass directly to the center without difficulty.
3. **Overplay = open side pass.** If the defender overplays to prevent the direct pass (either on the baseline side or the high side), the defender loses his alignment with the center and the basket. The forward can then make a quick pass to the **open side** of the center.
4. **Center reads and reacts.** After receiving the pass on the open side, the center can either shoot immediately or pass out to a teammate in a position to do something constructive with the ball.
5. **Systematic results.** "By thoroughly indoctrinating the players on the line of deployment theory, we feel we have been successful in getting the ball to our center, and this has been true despite a concentrated effort by opponents to prevent the pass to the post."

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF (F with ball):** Reads the defender's position relative to the line; decides to pass to the standard side or the open side based on defensive overplay.
- **C (low post):** Maintains position on the line of deployment; seals the defender away from the passing lane; receives the pass on the open side when the defender overplays.
- **Defender (X-C):** Must choose between standard position (easy pass allowed) or overplay (open side exposed).

## Variations
### Baseline Overplay
If X-C overplays the baseline side, the forward passes high to the open high side of the center. The center pivots to receive and face up.

### High Overplay
If X-C overplays the high side (attempting to front), the forward throws a lob or bounce pass to the baseline side of the center.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Center drifts off the line** → Center must discipline himself to stay on the line of deployment; drifting makes the passing lane less clear and the overplay read harder.
2. **Forward passes to the guarded side** → The pass must go to the open side created by the defender's overplay — this is the fundamental read.
3. **Center catches and holds** → After receiving the pass, the center must immediately make a decision: shoot or pass out.

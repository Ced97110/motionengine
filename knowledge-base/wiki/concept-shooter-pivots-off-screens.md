# Shooter's Pivots Off Screens

## Summary
Every off-ball screen situation requires the cutter/shooter to apply the correct pivot footwork in order to (1) set up the defender with a convincing V-cut, (2) scrape off the screen tightly, and (3) catch and instantly square up to the basket for a quick, low-pressure shot. The inside-foot heel pivot principle governs all of these situations, but the specific foot used varies by the player's court position, body orientation (back to basket vs. facing basket), and the type of screen involved.

Both the cutter *and* the screener must use appropriate pivots — the screener's pivot after the screen determines whether they become a secondary scoring threat (slip to the lane, pop to the perimeter, or seal the defender). [S6, pp.145-160]

## When to Use
- Cutter is coming off any off-ball screen (down, back, flare, stagger, double) and intends to shoot
- Screener is slipping a screen to become a scoring threat
- Ball-screener is executing pick-and-pop action
- Any situation where a player catches with their back or side to the basket after a cut

## Key Principles
1. **V-cut before the screen**: Every cutter must first sell the defender on going the wrong direction with a realistic multi-step V-cut (up to 3 steps) before using the screen. A V-cut is not just a single jab step. [S6, p.146]
2. **Inside foot plants for the V-cut direction change**: The inside foot is planted as the cutter changes direction to scrape off the screen.
3. **Scrape shoulder-to-shoulder off the screen**: Cut close to the screener's body to prevent the defender from getting through.
4. **Inside heel pivot on the catch**: Upon receiving the pass, pivot off the inside heel to stop momentum, square up, and get legs under the body for a vertical shot.
5. **Get feet and hands ready while still cutting**: By the time the ball arrives, feet should already be preparing the pivot and hands should be in the shooting pocket.
6. **Screener's pivot choice depends on scoring intent**: If the screener expects to be an immediate shot threat after slipping → front pivot off inside foot. If not an immediate shot threat → reverse/inside pivot off outside foot to enter triple-threat facing the ball.

## Screen-by-Screen Application

### Down Screen (Cutter with Back to Basket)
- Cutter on the left side of court: **left foot is inside foot** (back to basket) → pivot foot for the V-cut plant; **left (inside) heel** is the pivot point on the catch to square up
- After scraping off the screen, catch, pivot off the inside heel, swing the free foot around to face the basket
- Screener (after setting the down screen) can slip and duck into the lane; uses **reverse pivot off inside foot** to seal defender for post-up, or **front (crossover) pivot off inside foot** to face the ball [S6, pp.145-146]

### Back Screen (Lob Cutter)
- The offensive player cutting to the rim off a back screen plants the **inside foot** before receiving the screen, then scrapes off and cuts to the basket
- The back screener (after setting the screen), if slipping to the perimeter as a shooter, uses a **front pivot off the inside foot** (right foot on right side of court, back to basket) to become an immediate jump-shot threat
- If not an immediate shooter, the screener uses a **reverse (inside) pivot off the outside foot** to enter triple-threat position with the new inside foot as the pivot foot and the outside foot as the free foot for a baseline drive [S6, pp.147-148]

### Flare Screen (Cutter Facing Basket)
- Cutter on the left side, facing basket: V-cut by planting **right (inside) foot**, step out with outside foot, scrape off flare screen toward the perimeter
- On the catch: **slight front pivot off the inside (right) heel**, swing free (left) foot around to fully square up to the basket
- Screener (after setting flare screen), if slipping to the ball: **front pivot off inside foot** (left foot on left side, back to basket) to square up for immediate elbow jump shot [S6, pp.148-149]

### Stagger Screen (Cutter with Back to Basket)
- Cutter uses inside (left) foot plant for V-cut, then steps with right foot to receive stagger screens
- After scraping off the outside shoulder of the first screener: **pivot off inside (left) heel**, peek around screen, receive ball from passer
- Allows cutter to see all four key elements: passer, both screeners, and defender
- **Outside screener** (01): reverse pivots off foot farthest from the ball → drifts out of the lane while maintaining sight of the ball
- **Inside screener** (05): reverse pivots off foot closest to the ball → opens up to the ball and steps back toward it, picking up sight of the ball earlier [S6, pp.149-151]

### Ball Screen / Pick-and-Pop
- The ball screener who pops out to the three-point line instead of rolling: **reverse pivot off the inside foot** to maintain sight of the ball, basket, and defender while waiting for the throwback pass [S6, p.151]

### Double Screen / Baseline OOB
- Cutter approaching from left side: V-cut begins with step of **inside foot (right)**, then front crossover step as player scrapes off screens
- On the catch: **front pivot off inside (right) heel**, swing outside (left) foot around to square up instantly for the shot [S6, pp.151-152]

### Pick-the-Picker / Flare Screen After Ball Screen
- The original ball screener who receives a back/flare screen on the opposite side: if on the right side of court and facing basket, uses **inside (left) heel pivot** and front pivot with outside (right) foot to square up for immediate triple threat [S6, p.153]

## Screener Slip Reads
When a defender switches or hedges toward the cutter, the screener can slip the screen and step toward the ball/lane:
- If **facing the cutter (toward outside)**: must **reverse pivot with the contact foot** (foot closest to defender) to seal the defender and create a post-up opportunity with hands up
- Specific example: defender on the top side → reverse pivot using top foot as contact/pivot foot, swing other foot to seal
- Defender on the low side → reverse pivot off the low foot, swing the other foot to seal [S6, p.153]

## Common Mistakes
1. **V-cut is only a single jab step** → Defender isn't sold; stays attached through the screen → Teach multi-step V-cuts of 2-3 steps that genuinely sell a direction
2. **Not scraping tightly off the screener's shoulder** → Defender slips through → "Shoulder to shoulder" through every screen
3. **Pivoting off ball of foot instead of heel** → Momentum not stopped; shot drifts → Emphasize planting and pivoting off the inside heel
4. **Screener doesn't pivot after setting the screen** → Screener stands still and becomes a non-threat → Every screen ends with a pivot decision and an active scoring move
5. **Shooter catches with hands low** → Pass arrives before hands are in shooting pocket; slower shot release → Hands in shooting pocket before the catch
6. **Shooter doesn't see the basket on the cut** → Ball caught with no vision of the target → Train players to pick up the rim on the cut, before the catch

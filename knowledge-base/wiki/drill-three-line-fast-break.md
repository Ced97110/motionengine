# Three-Line Fast Break Drill

## Objective
Train wings to maintain sideline width, teach the PG to push the ball immediately on the dribble, and develop full-court fast-break execution without defensive pressure.

## Setup
- Full court
- Three lines at one baseline (left wing, middle, right wing)
- Each group of 3 has a ball
- No defense

## Execution
1. First player in each of the three lines steps up — one player in the middle (PG), one on each wing
2. Middle player (PG) tosses the ball off the backboard to initiate the drill (simulates a defensive rebound)
3. PG receives the ball and immediately begins dribbling up the middle
4. **Wings START RUNNING only when the PG receives the ball and begins the dribble** — do not leave early
5. Wings sprint as close to the sidelines as possible — "touch" the sideline to stay in their precise lanes
6. Players are free to:
   - Cut at the free-throw line extended
   - Make a give-and-go with the PG
   - Try any variation as long as **proper spacing is maintained**
7. Attack the basket and finish
8. Next group of three goes; run continuously for 10–15 minutes [S7, p.159]

## Coaching Points
- Wings must *wait* for the PG to catch before sprinting — removes the habit of leaving before possession is secured
- Wings must stay as wide as possible — "tell them to touch the sideline" [S7, p.159]
- PG should push the ball with purpose immediately; no hesitation dribbling
- Proper spacing prevents a single defensive player from covering both offensive options
- Cut at the free-throw line extended — the most dangerous read on the 3-on-0 break

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No options — PG pushes, wings run to corner, PG decides (layup or pass)
2. **Intermediate**: Add cut-at-FT-line-extended option and give-and-go
3. **Advanced**: Add a single retreating defender to simulate a 3-on-1 or 2-on-1 read

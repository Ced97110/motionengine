# Swing Kip (Muscle-Up Chain — Step 1)

## Objective
Build rhythmic forward-backward body momentum on a horizontal bar using coordinated chest-thrust and hollow-body positions, laying the foundation for all explosive bar movements including the muscle-up.

## Setup
- 1 player
- A horizontal overhead bar at full hang height
- No additional equipment needed
- Prerequisite: athlete should be comfortable performing regular pull-ups before attempting this drill [S9, p.13]

## Execution
1. Hang from an overhead horizontal bar with a shoulder-width grip. Keep shoulders pulled down tight into the armpits.
2. **Forward swing**: Thrust hips and chest forward while pushing arms back and bringing feet behind you. The body should curve into a concave (arch) position. Knees may bend slightly.
3. **Rebound**: Don't hold the back-curve — allow the body to spring out of it, assisting with an abdominal contraction.
4. **Backswing**: As glutes and hips shoot back, pull down with hands and lats (keeping arms *straight*). Allow the body to curl slightly into the *hollow body position* — body shaped like the letter "C", feet raised, trunk curved (not just bent at hips).
5. Continue the back-and-forth motion, building momentum with each cycle.
6. Goal: get as high as possible on each backswing. Eventually, the head should be level with the bar at the peak of the backswing — this position is what makes a true, power-based muscle-up possible [S9, p.7].
7. Stop when form deteriorates — reps are less important than quality of movement [S9, p.14].

## Coaching Points
- "Shoulders down into the armpits" — this protects the shoulder socket throughout
- The concave (forward) position and the hollow/convex (backswing) position are both full-body shapes — not just hip flexion or extension
- "Pull the lats down, not the arms" — lats engage on the backswing with straight arms, not bent
- This is NOT a swing on a playground — it's a controlled kinetic chain using the whole spine and posterior chain
- "Build momentum each rep" — the goal is accumulating height on the backswing, not hitting a number of reps
- Watch for: athletes who only use hip flexion instead of whole-body curling in the hollow position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Start with simple pendulum swings — forward and back without any kipping form. Just get comfortable hanging and moving [S9, p.13].
2. **Intermediate**: Add the arch-to-hollow transition, building rhythm and beginning to use the lat pull-down on the backswing.
3. **Advanced**: Build maximum height on the backswing — goal is head level with the bar. Progress to the full kip (advanced variation where athlete swings all the way up to the bar level) [S9, p.14].

# Line Slide With Change of Direction

## Objective
Train lateral defensive sliding footwork with smooth direction reversal at angled lines, building both straight-plane and diagonal movement competency.

## Setup
- Taped lines on the court: straight lines and 45-degree angled lines
- Both feet must go in each space between lines
- Court baseline area or any flat surface

## Execution
1. Player starts to one side of the line pattern.
2. **Lateral slide** through the straight lines, placing **both feet in each space**.
3. At the **45-degree angled line**, player **pivots to turn around** and face the opposite direction.
4. Continue through the remaining lines **without stopping**.
5. **Pick up the front foot as quickly as possible** at each direction change.
6. Return to start and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- The 45-degree line is the pivot point — practice the direction reversal as quickly as possible, like a defender reading a dribbler's crossover.
- "Don't stop at the line — pivot and go!"
- Maintain low, athletic base throughout; don't pop up on the direction change.
- Eyes forward, not down at the feet.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the pattern to learn pivot direction; slow down at each 45-degree line.
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed; add a competition for who completes the most reps in 30 seconds.
3. **Advanced**: Coach calls out direction at each angle (forward or backward) so player must react rather than anticipate.

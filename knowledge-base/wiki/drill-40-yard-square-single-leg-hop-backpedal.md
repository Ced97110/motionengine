# 40-Yard Square Drill — Sprint, Single-Leg Hop, Backpedal

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction, body position, and transitions between movement skills using single-leg lateral hops for unilateral explosive training.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4 cones positioned in a 10-yard (9-meter) square:
  - Cone 1: start line
  - Cone 2: 10 yards straight ahead
  - Cone 3: 10 yards to the left of cone 2
  - Cone 4: 10 yards to the left of cone 1

## Execution
1. Start in a ready position at cone 1.
2. **Sprint** forward from cone 1 to cone 2.
3. **Single-leg hop on the left leg** laterally to the left from cone 2 to cone 3.
4. **Backpedal** from cone 3 to cone 4.
5. **Single-leg hop on the right leg** laterally to the right from cone 4 back to cone 1.
6. Upon reaching the start, immediately repeat going in the **opposite direction**.
7. Two consecutive trips (counterclockwise then clockwise) = one full set.

## Coaching Points
- Land softly on each single-leg hop — absorb with the ankle, knee, and hip
- Maintain balance between hops; do not let the body sway excessively
- Keep the non-hopping leg slightly elevated — do not drag the foot
- "Stick and go" — land, stabilize for a split second, then explode to next hop

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Use double-leg hops instead of single-leg to learn the pattern.
2. **Intermediate**: Single-leg as described; time each full circuit.
3. **Advanced**: Increase hop distance (wider square); add a reactive stimulus (coach calls direction change).

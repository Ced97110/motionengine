# Lateral Plank (Side Plank) — Core Lateral Flexion

## Objective
Develop lateral trunk stability and hip strength through an isometric side-plank hold — building the pillar strength needed to withstand physical contact, generate jump power, and maintain balance against bigger defenders.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (gym, court, or anywhere)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Lie on your left side with feet, knees, hips, and shoulders in a straight line.
2. Prop the upper body on the left elbow and forearm; elbow should be positioned directly under the shoulder.
3. Tighten abdominal muscles as if bracing for a punch to the stomach.
4. Raise the hips until they are in a straight line with the knees and shoulders.
5. Hold for the required time.
6. Slowly lower the hips to starting position.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions, then repeat on the **opposite side**. [S2, pp.46-47]

## Coaching Points
- **"Straight as a board"** — hips, knees, and shoulders must form one rigid line
- Elbow directly under shoulder — if it drifts, the shoulder will fatigue and form breaks
- Bracing cue: "tighten up like someone is about to punch your stomach" — this activates transversus abdominis
- "Power comes from the floor up" — a strong lateral pillar translates to a higher jump and faster lateral movements [S2, p.47]
- Watch for hips sagging or rotating — these are signs of insufficient core lateral stability

## Progressions
1. **Beginner — Modified Lateral Plank**: If unable to maintain a straight line, bend both knees and raise the hips from the bent-knee position; build up to straight-leg version [S2, p.47]
2. **Intermediate**: Full straight-leg hold for prescribed duration (e.g., 20-30 seconds each side); 2-3 sets
3. **Advanced**: Increase hold time (45-60+ seconds), add hip dips (lower and raise hips rhythmically), or elevate feet on a bench

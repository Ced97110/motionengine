# Skater Hop

## Objective
Build explosive lateral quickness, improve cut-and-change-of-direction ability, and strengthen lateral hip muscles to reduce knee injury risk during basketball-specific movements.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space; no equipment required
- Mark lateral distance targets on the floor if desired to track progression

## Execution
1. Face forward and stand erect on the right leg. Position the left leg behind with only the tip of the left foot touching the floor.
2. Flex the knee and hip of the right (standing) leg, then jump laterally to the left, landing softly on the left leg by flexing the knee and hip upon contact.
3. Immediately after landing on the left leg, bring the right leg behind the left leg with the tip of the right foot touching the floor.
4. Immediately jump to the right, landing on the right leg with the same soft-landing mechanics.
5. The upper body faces forward throughout — do not rotate the trunk.
6. **Begin with small lateral jumps; progressively increase lateral distance** as strength and technique improve.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

> **Upper body stays square forward** — rotating the trunk is a form break. [S2, p.116-117]

## Coaching Points
- **"Stick the landing"** — single-leg landings should be stable, controlled, and immediately reactive
- Knee tracks over toes — no valgus collapse on the landing leg
- **"Push off the outside edge"** — the lateral force comes from the hip abductors and gluteus medius
- Progress distance gradually — form breaks under fatigue lead to injury
- Watch for the trailing foot touching the floor prematurely; it should touch lightly as a support marker only

## Primary Muscles
- **At takeoff:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medius), tensor fasciae latae
- **At landing:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, quadriceps, tensor fasciae latae, biceps femoris, semimembranosus, semitendinosus [S2, p.117]

## Basketball Application
Strong lateral hip muscles (gluteus medius, gluteus maximus, vastus lateralis, tensor fasciae latae) generate force for quick direction changes and jab steps, and absorb force during cutting and rebounding landings — directly reducing knee injury risk. [S2, p.117]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Small lateral hops (1–2 feet/30–60 cm), slow tempo, focus on stick-landing
2. **Intermediate:** Wider lateral hops (3–4 feet/90–120 cm), increase repetitions
3. **Advanced:** Maximum lateral distance, reactive speed, add sport-specific cues (react to coach signal)

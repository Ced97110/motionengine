# Shot-Ready Position

## Summary
Being shot ready means a player is prepared to shoot before they even receive the pass. Rather than catching the ball and then setting the shooting hand and elbow, a shot-ready player shows the palm of the shooting hand to the passer, with the elbow already in the L-position and the wrist already cocked (wrinkled). On the catch, the player simply lifts into the shot — the elbow was never out of alignment. This eliminates wasted motion, speeds up the release, and prevents the elbow from flaring during the catch. [S5, p.16]

## When to Use
- Every time a player is about to receive a pass and may need to shoot immediately
- Especially for catch-and-shoot opportunities off screens, kick-outs, and defensive rotations
- Trained as a habit in all catch-and-shoot drills

## Key Principles
1. **Show the palm to the passer** — the shooting hand faces the passer with palm open; this signals readiness and pre-sets the shooting hand position [S5, p.16]
2. **Elbow is already in L-position** — the elbow is tucked and pre-positioned above the shooting-side foot before the ball arrives [S5, p.16]
3. **Wrist is already wrinkled** — the wrist is pre-cocked before the catch [S5, p.16]
4. **Catch and lift** — the only movement on the catch is lifting the arm from L-position upward into the release; no repositioning needed [S5, p.16]
5. **Elbow stays aligned through the catch-and-lift** — pre-setting prevents the elbow from flaring during the catch, which is the most common cause of misalignment [S5, p.16]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG, SG, SF**: Especially critical for perimeter players who receive kick-out passes or come off screens with defenders closing out; being shot ready means the ball is off before the closeout arrives.
- **PF, C**: Applied when receiving perimeter passes on step-out situations or high-post catch-and-shoot opportunities.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Catching with two hands first, then setting the shooting hand** → Two extra movements; elbow often flares during the transition → Pre-set the shooting hand before the catch [S5, p.16]
2. **Not being mentally ready to shoot** → Hesitation leads to defender recovery; shot-ready is both physical and mental → Decide to shoot before catching when open [S5, p.16]
3. **Elbow dropping out of L on the catch** → Ball catches the body in an awkward position → Actively hold the elbow up in L-position as the ball arrives [S5, p.16]

# Handstand Roll (Front Flip Chain — Step 4)

## Objective
Develop explosive leg power to kick up to a momentary handstand position, and build the arm strength required to bear full bodyweight before rolling through — the final roll in the front flip chain.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Soft/padded surface recommended
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Stand in a split stance with the strongest foot forward. Place palms on the floor and raise the back leg behind you.
2. Push up through the strongest foot, swinging the other foot back and up. Allow the torso to become more vertical.
3. Let leg momentum carry the legs above the torso; straighten them until the entire body is aligned in a **handstand**.
4. **Do NOT hold the handstand.** As soon as you begin to fall forward, allow arms to bend and the trunk to curl beneath you.
5. Braced arms guide the roll — head tucked, no weight on the head.
6. Roll over the back without veering sideways.
7. Finish the roll all the way to the standing position.

## Coaching Points
- **"You don't need to hold the handstand"** — The handstand is momentary; it is a position passed through, not sustained [S9, p.12]
- **Explosive leg kick** — The legs must be driven aggressively upward; this is what builds the power capacity needed for front handsprings
- **Arms hold full bodyweight** — This is the key conditioning difference from Steps 1–3; the upper limbs now must briefly support the entire body [S9, p.12]
- **Head protection** — Same cue as prior steps; once past the handstand, head tucks and arms guide the roll
- **Regression**: Keep arms bent throughout to avoid requiring a full handstand — reduces demand on balance and arm strength
- **Progression**: Begin with hands off the floor and kick up with legs a split-second *before* palms contact the ground — this increases the plyometric demand

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Arms bent throughout; no need to achieve handstand; focus on leg swing and arm weight-bearing
2. **Intermediate**: Arms straight at handstand; momentary handstand before curling into roll
3. **Advanced**: Hands start off floor; kick up before palms contact — maximum plyometric arm-catch demand

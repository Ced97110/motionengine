# Handstand Roll (Front Flip Chain — Step 4)

## Objective
Develop the explosive leg-kick power and arm strength required for front handsprings — the final roll-based step before transitioning to true handspring mechanics.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (solo)
- **Equipment**: Mat or soft surface recommended
- **Space**: Any open floor area

## Execution
1. Take a split stance with the strongest foot forward; place palms on the floor and raise the opposite leg behind you.
2. Push through the strongest foot, simultaneously swinging the rear leg back and upward. Allow the torso to become more vertical as momentum builds.
3. Let the leg push/swing momentum carry the legs above the torso — as they whip upward, straighten them until the entire body is in a handstand alignment.
4. You do not need to hold the handstand. Allow the arms to bend and the trunk to curl up beneath you as soon as forward fall begins.
5. Let the braced arms guide the movement as you roll over — head tucked, taking virtually no weight.
6. Roll over the upper back without veering.
7. Finish all the way to standing position.

**Key biomechanical point**: This is the final true roll of the front flip chain. Momentarily bearing full body weight on the hands (even for a fraction of a second) begins developing the arm strength required for front handsprings. [S9, pp.152-153]

## Coaching Points
- "Kick the legs hard — you need explosive hip extension to reach the handstand position."
- Arms only need to hold the handstand position briefly — immediately allow forward fall and roll.
- Head must stay tucked and clear of the floor throughout.
- If dizziness occurs, revisit earlier steps before returning to this drill.
- The split-stance kick (stronger leg pushing, weaker leg swinging) is the most efficient entry.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Keep the arms bent throughout — this eliminates the need to achieve a true handstand position, reducing the strength and coordination demands.
2. **Intermediate**: Standard execution — full kick to handstand alignment before rolling through.
3. **Advanced**: Begin with hands off the floor and kick the legs up a split-second *before* the palms make contact with the ground — increasing plyometric and coordination demands significantly.

# Ankling

## Objective
Increase foot speed and elastic ankle strength by emphasizing rapid plantar flexion at ground contact and minimizing ground contact time.

## Setup
- 1 player, no equipment needed
- Any flat surface, typically performed over 20-40 yards
- Can be done as a warm-up drill before sprint work

## Execution
1. Begin jogging forward with very short steps
2. Emphasize the **plantar flexion phase** of ground contact — push off the ball of the foot actively
3. Keep foot recovery **low** — minimal heel lift
4. Feet should be **quiet but fast** — rapid, light contacts
5. Land and push off the **ball of the foot** at all times
6. Minimize time that the foot is in contact with the ground
7. Maximize the number of foot contacts per unit of time [S8, p.24]

## Coaching Points
- "Quiet but fast feet" — loud landings indicate too much ground contact time
- Focus on the **push-off**, not just the landing — active plantar flexion accelerates turnover
- This drill directly trains the ankle's elastic (reactive) strength used in rapid court movements
- Keep upper body relaxed; arms should still move in a coordinated sprinting pattern

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk with exaggerated ball-of-foot push-off, emphasizing plantar flexion
2. **Intermediate**: Ankling at jogging pace as described — steady rhythm, consistent contacts
3. **Advanced**: Increase speed progressively; transition directly from ankling into a sprint acceleration to feel the carry-over [S8, p.24]

# Sprint, Catch, and Shoot Conditioning Drill

## Objective
Develop the ability to execute offensive skills (catch-and-shoot, pull-up jumper, drive-to-layup) while physically fatigued, building mental toughness and skill under pressure.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 passer/rebounder (or coach)
- Full court
- Basketball

## Execution
**Sequence 1 — Catch and Shoot:**
1. Player starts at baseline.
2. Sprints down the wing to a designated spot.
3. Receives a pass and **catches and shoots** immediately.
4. Turns and sprints to the other end. Repeat on the other side.

**Sequence 2 — Dribble and Pull-Up:**
1. Player sprints from baseline down the court to the wing.
2. Receives a pass, dribbles **once or twice**, then **pulls up and shoots**.
3. Immediately sprints to the other end. Repeat.

**Sequence 3 — Sprint, Catch, Drive:**
1. Player sprints the full court.
2. Receives a pass on the wing.
3. **Drives to the basket** and either **dunks or shoots a layup**.
4. Repeat on the other end.

Total: 4-6 complete sequences (one sequence = all three rounds, end to end). [S7, p.328]

## Coaching Points
- The goal is skill execution under fatigue — "When you're tired, that's when your technique must be perfect."
- Don't slow down before the catch — sprint to the spot, square up, and shoot on rhythm.
- Passer must time the pass so the player doesn't have to wait or adjust their stride.
- Track makes-to-attempts: players can see improvement day to day, which is itself motivating.
- "Mental toughness is built when skill meets fatigue."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk or jog; focus on clean shooting mechanics at each spot.
2. **Intermediate**: Sprint the full court; all three sequences at game speed.
3. **Advanced**: Add a specific live-ball move (rip step, shot fake) before the pull-up in Sequence 2; use game-clock pressure (player must attempt 12 shots in 3 minutes).

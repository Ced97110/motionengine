# Dribble, Stop, Pivot, Pass, Cut

## Objective
Integrate dribbling, stopping, pivoting, passing, and V-cutting into one continuous team drill, developing the complete foundational skill chain of half-court basketball.

## Setup
- Players line up in pairs of two, spaced all over the court
- Each pair has one basketball
- No cones or equipment needed
- Duration: 3 minutes

## Execution

### Intermediate
1. Players line up in pairs spaced all over the court (for clarity, the diagram shows one pair)
2. Player 1 dribbles using speed or control dribble (coach's choice)
3. After 3–5 dribbles, Player 1 stops (jump stop or stride stop: coach's choice)
4. After stopping, Player 1 pivots (front or reverse: coach's choice)
5. After pivoting, Player 1 passes to Player 2 using the appropriate pass type:
   - Far away: overhead pass
   - Near: bounce pass or chest pass
6. Meanwhile, Player 2 has V-cut to get open; Player 2 calls out to Player 1 so Player 1 knows where 2 is; Player 2 may V-cut anywhere on the court
7. Watch execution of the dribble, stop, pivot, and pass by Player 1, and Player 2's V-cut; correct immediately

### Advanced
- Continuous drill: Player 2 receives the pass, assumes triple-threat position, then begins to dribble in any direction
- Player 2 now dribbles, stops, pivots, and passes while Player 1 runs a V-cut
- Roles continuously alternate: the passer becomes the cutter and vice versa

## Coaching Points
- Enforce all components simultaneously: the dribble, the stop, the pivot, AND the pass
- Player 2 must call out (verbal communication) to Player 1 while cutting — this develops game-realistic court communication
- V-cuts can go anywhere — do not restrict to specific spots; build habit of reading open space
- In the advanced version, Player 2 should assume triple-threat position immediately on reception before dribbling
- Break into parts if needed: drill only dribble/stop/pass without cuts, then add cuts

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: Prescribed stop type and pivot type by coach; 3–5 dribbles then pass to V-cutter
2. **Advanced**: Continuous alternating roles — passer becomes cutter, cutter becomes passer; full triple-threat position required on each reception

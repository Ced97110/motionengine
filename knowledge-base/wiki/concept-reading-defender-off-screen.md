# Reading the Defender Off a Screen

## Summary
When a player uses an off-ball screen, the defender's position determines which scoring action to take. There are three primary reads: fade (baseline), fill (wing), and curl (middle/paint). The ball-handler must establish a dominant pivot foot immediately upon receiving the pass and then attack the open space. Combining these reads with dribble attacks creates a complete off-screen scoring system.

This reading system is the foundation of every shooting-off-screens workout in the S3 curriculum. The defender's choice — slip the screen high, trail/body the cutter, or stay level — dictates which option the cutter selects.

## When to Use
- Any time a player uses a down screen or wing screen in the half-court
- When a defender overplays the catch, opening the baseline (fade/dribble fade)
- When a defender trails or bodies the cutter on the low side, opening the middle (fill/dribble fill)
- When a defender is directly on the cutter's back through the screen, the cutter should curl tighter toward the basket

## Key Principles
1. **Set up the screen properly** — use a V-cut underneath the basket before using the screen; don't drift directly to your spot
2. **Read the defender before the catch** — know where help is before you receive the ball
3. **Establish a dominant pivot foot immediately** — on the catch, plant the inside foot and pivot into the best action
4. **Attack, don't just catch and stand** — every catch off a screen should threaten the basket with a drive, shot, or dribble attack
5. **Three reads, three actions** — defender high → fade; defender low/trailing → fill; defender directly behind → curl
6. **Any scoring opportunity is valid** once you attack the open lane — shot, floater, layup, or pass out

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF (cutter)**: Read defender position, set up screen, establish pivot foot on catch, execute correct action (fade, fill, curl, or dribble variation)
- **Screener**: Set a legal, stationary screen at the right angle; hold the screen until the cutter clears
- **Passer**: Deliver the ball to the cutter's outside hand in rhythm with the cut

## Variations
### Fade / Dribble Fade
Defender slips the screen and closes out at a high angle, leaving the baseline open. Cutter catches on the wing, establishes pivot foot, and attacks the baseline with a dribble fade. Any scoring option is available once the baseline is attacked. [S3, pp.141-143]

### Fill / Dribble Fill
Defender trails or bodies the cutter and closes out on the baseline or low side, leaving the middle open. Cutter catches on the wing, establishes pivot foot, and attacks the middle of the paint with a dribble fill. Any scoring option is available once the middle is attacked. [S3, pp.144-151]

### Curl / Dribble Curl
Defender trails the screen and is directly behind the cutter. The cutter curls tighter around the screen toward the elbow area (10–15 feet) for a catch-and-shoot curl shot. If the defender remains directly behind, the cutter continues to dribble curl to the middle for a dribble curl scoring play. The dribble curl is similar to the dribble fill but the cutter is already attacking with a curl cut trajectory. [S3, pp.152-159]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Catching flat-footed** → establish the dominant pivot foot the instant the ball arrives
2. **Not reading the defender** → look before the catch; the read determines the entire play
3. **Drifting to the same spot every time** → vary the cut angle based on the defender's position
4. **Attacking a closed lane** → if the defender beats you to the spot, counter with the opposite action
5. **Hesitating after the catch** → attack immediately; hesitation gives the defender time to recover

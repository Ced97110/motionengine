# Wing to Top Rip Step Live-Ball Move Shooting (Workout #11-18)

## Objective
Develop the rip-step move at the three-point line — catching on a two-foot stop, ripping the ball through low to protect it and create separation, then shooting off the live-ball action.

## Setup
- 1 shooter, 1 rebounder/passer (or solo with self-pass spinout)
- Half court; three-point arc
- Shot chart tracking sheet (FGA, FGM, FTA, FTM columns)
- Both sides of the floor used

## Execution
1. Shooter starts on the wing and moves toward the top of the key (or back to the wing) at game speed.
2. Player receives the ball on a **two-foot stop** beyond the three-point line.
3. After the catch, the player performs a **rip step live-ball move** — sweeping the ball low and through ("ripping") past the imaginary or live defender, initiating the dribble and stepping past for a pull-up jumper or driving finish.
4. The move is completed with a shot out past the three-point line or just inside, depending on defender position.
5. Repeat until **10 shots** are taken from each side.
6. Intersperse **5 free throws** between each set of 10 field-goal attempts.

**Full workout structure (each side):**
- Wing to Top Rip Step Shots: 10
- FTs: 5
- Wing to Top Rip Step Shots: 10
- FTs: 5
- Wing to Top Rip Step Shots: 10

**Total shots per workout: 80 field-goal attempts + 20 free throws** (both sides combined).

**Performance targets:**
- FG: 36-60 made (60%)
- FT: 17-20 made (85%)

## Coaching Points
- The rip must be **low and quick** — ball goes from chest height down through the hip to protect it from the defender's reach
- Keep the ball in a "safe" path; ripping high exposes it to steals
- "Rip, step, and go" — one fluid motion, not three separate actions
- The two-foot stop allows selection of either pivot foot; use this to create optimal angle for the rip direction
- Practice ripping to both the strong side and weak side
- Track all attempts on the shot chart

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stand still, practice the rip motion without movement; add the step and shot once the rip path is clean
2. **Intermediate**: Add the wing-to-top movement pattern at moderate speed before performing the rip-step
3. **Advanced**: Add a live or dummy defender closing out; shooter reads the closeout and rips to the open side

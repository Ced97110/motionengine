# Dribble Fill Shot

## Summary
The dribble fill is the counterpart to the [[concept-dribble-fade-shot]]. When a defender trails or "bodies" the cutter off a screen and closes out on the baseline or low side (leaving the middle open), the cutter establishes a dominant pivot foot and attacks the middle of the paint with a dribble fill. Any scoring opportunity can be used once the middle has been attacked — pull-up jumper, floater, layup, or drawing a foul. [S3, pp.155-161]

This read is essential for any off-ball cutter: if the defender overplays the baseline, the middle is the reward.

## When to Use
- Defender trails or bodies the cutter through the screen
- Defender closes out on the baseline or low side of the cutter
- Middle of the paint is left unprotected after the screen
- Anytime a low-side closeout creates a middle gap on a wing catch

## Key Principles
1. **Read the defender's position** — trailing or low-side = attack the middle
2. **Establish the dominant pivot foot on the catch** — instantaneous, not delayed
3. **Attack the middle of the paint** with a controlled dribble fill drive
4. **Use any scoring opportunity** inside: finish at the rim, floater, pull-up mid-range, or draw the foul
5. **Keep the ball protected** as you enter the paint against potential help defenders

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF (ball receiver/cutter)**: Use the screen, read trailing/low-side defender, establish pivot foot, attack middle of paint decisively
- **Screener**: Set a legal screen that forces the defender to trail or go low
- **Passer**: Time the pass to the wing catch so the cutter can read and react without an extra dribble before attacking

## Variations
### Wing Dribble Fill
Standard version — catch on the wing, defender trails low, attack the middle/paint area directly. [S3, pp.155-161]

### Dribble Curl (Extended Fill)
When the cutter is already in a curl cut trajectory toward the basket and the defender is trailing directly behind, the cutter continues the curl momentum into the middle attack — see [[concept-dribble-curl-shot]]. [S3, pp.163-170]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Not reading the defender** → attacking the wrong direction; identify trailing/low-side coverage before moving
2. **Pivot foot indecision** → hesitating to establish a pivot foot after the catch; correct by landing and setting the foot in one motion
3. **Attacking into a crowded paint** → not scanning for help defense; keep head up to read help and adjust finish
4. **Fading away from the middle** → reverting to a fade when the middle is open; commit to the read
5. **Over-dribbling** → giving help defense time to recover; one or two decisive dribbles to scoring range

# BLOB Flip

## Overview
An simple but highly effective BLOB play from a box formation. Guards flash to the corners to spread the defense, then a cross-screen frees two post players to attack the rim simultaneously from opposite sides, creating a layup for one of them. [S4, pp.23-24]

## Formation
Box formation with two bigs (4, 5) on the elbows and two guards (2, 3) on the low blocks. 1 (best passer) inbounds from the sideline. [S4, p.23]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Guards Clear, Cross Screen Initiated
- 1 slaps the basketball to start the play.
- 2 and 3 immediately flash out to the corners, calling for the ball loudly to drag their defenders away from the paint.
- Ball-side post player (5) sets a cross screen on 4's defender.

### Phase 2: Dual Rim Attack
- 4 immediately flashes toward the ball-side low block, calling for the basketball.
- After setting the cross screen, 5 seals off 4's defender and then flashes to the opposite side of the rim, calling for the basketball.
- 1 reads the defense and makes the pass to whichever post player is open for the layup.

### Phase 3: Safety Outlet
- If neither pass to 4 nor 5 is available, 3 cuts to the top of the key to receive a lob pass over the top. [S4, p.24]

## Key Coaching Points
- 5 must set the cross screen at the **correct angle on the back hip** of 4's defender, preventing the defender from slipping under and beating 4 to the rim. [S4, p.24]
- Post players must show **target hands** (where they want the ball) as they flash toward the rim. [S4, p.24]
- 2 and 3 **must call loudly for the basketball** in the corners even though they likely won't receive it — this is essential for dragging defenders out of the paint. [S4, p.23]
- 4 and 5 should **stay on their own sides** of the lane when cutting to the rim. If they get too close, one defender can deflect passes to both. [S4, p.24]

## Counters
- 3 cuts to the top of the key for the lob pass over the top if both post options are covered. [S4, p.24]

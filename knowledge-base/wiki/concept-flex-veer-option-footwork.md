# Flex Veer Option Footwork

## Summary
The veer option is a counter within the flex offense that attacks defenders who over-help on the baseline cutter (03) and neglect the back screener (05). When 03 scrapes off 05's back screen on the low side, 05 ducks into the free throw lane on the high side using a reverse pivot, exploiting X5's over-commitment to defending 03's cut. The result is a position advantage for 05 near the middle of the free throw lane — a high-percentage interior scoring location. [S6, pp.252-256]

This option is especially valuable when 05 is a quality interior scorer, or when the offense wants to isolate X5 in a mismatch situation.

## When to Use
- When the defense is over-committed to stopping 03's baseline cut off 05's screen
- When 05 is a superior interior scoring threat (post mismatch)
- When the offense wants to isolate X5 one-on-one in the lane
- As an automatic counter whenever X5 helps hard on the cutter's baseline cut

## Key Principles
1. **Cutter goes low to give screener high side**: 03 cuts off 05's back screen on the low (left) shoulder, pulling X5 lower. This creates spacing for 05 to duck into the free throw lane on the high side [S6, p.252].
2. **Reverse pivot with contact foot**: 05 uses a reverse pivot with the foot closest to his defender (left foot) into the lane — a devastating move to a defense that does not respect the screener as a scorer [S6, p.253].
3. **Footwork sequence for the duck-in**: After 03 cuts off the screen, 05 steps into the center of the lane first with the top foot (right, in this scenario). The movement resembles a controlled defensive slide pace: top foot → lower foot → top foot again [S6, p.255].
4. **Position above the notch and in the middle**: 05's first steps go into the middle of the free throw lane AND higher up the lane. This centers him and creates a better passing angle for both 01 and 02, and opens a lob option if X5 fronts [S6, p.253].
5. **Spacing by 02**: After reversing the ball to 01, 02 makes a shallow flare cut to clear congestion, improve his own passing angle to 05, and allow 01 the spacing to center the ball via dribble [S6, p.253].
6. **01 in triple-threat position**: 01 receives the ball reversal and must be immediately in passing, driving, and shooting (triple-threat) position, pivoting off the inside (left) heel to square up [S6, p.255].
7. **Seal and lob option**: If X5 fronts 05 in the lane, 05 seals the defender and looks for the lob from 01 or 02 [S6, p.255].

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG (01 — Weak-side guard receiving reversal)**: Receive reversal pass from 02; be immediately in triple-threat position (pivot off inside heel); look first to pass to 05 in the lane; secondary = quick perimeter shot or shot fake and drive.
- **SG (02 — Original ball handler)**: Reverse the ball to 01; immediately make a shallow flare cut to improve spacing and provide a return pass option if 01 cannot deliver to 05.
- **SF (03 — Baseline cutter)**: Scrape off 05's back screen on the LOW side deliberately — this is what pulls X5 lower and creates the veer opening for 05.
- **C (05 — Screener/veer cutter)**: Set the flex back screen; after 03 scrapes low, reverse pivot with the contact foot (left) and step into the middle of the free throw lane; top foot → lower foot → top foot footwork sequence; seal defender if fronted; receive pass and use interior post moves to score.

## Variations
### Triple-Threat Counter with Drive
If 01 receives the ball in triple-threat position and 05 is fronted, 01 attacks with a drive to create a passing angle or draw a foul, with a skip back to 02 as a safety outlet [S6, p.255].

### Return Pass to 02
If 01 cannot deliver to 05, 01 passes back to 02 (who has flare cut to an open angle). 02 receives in triple-threat and looks immediately to pass inside to 05, shoot, or shot fake and drive [S6, p.255].

## Common Mistakes
1. **03 cuts on the high side of 05's screen** → This does NOT pull X5 lower; the cutter must deliberately go low to execute the veer properly.
2. **05 ducks in too early** → Must wait for 03 to complete the cut before making the reverse pivot and duck-in, or the action is telegraphed.
3. **05 positions on the block (too low)** → 05's first step must go toward the middle AND higher up the lane, not just toward the low block, to maintain the passing angle and lob option.
4. **01 not in triple-threat immediately** → Delayed triple-threat eliminates the passing window to 05 before X5 can recover.
5. **02 does not flare cut** → Without the shallow flare, lane congestion makes it harder for 01 to deliver the ball to 05.

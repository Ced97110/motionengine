# One-on-One-on-One-on-One (#107)

## Objective
Develop competitive 1-on-1 scoring ability and defensive stops in a king-of-the-court format where winners stay and face a fresh defender.

## Setup
- **Players**: 4+ in a line at the baseline; 1 attacker, 1 defender at top of key
- **Court area**: Half court; attacker must race back to the head of the key after each possession change
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball
- **Scoring**: Track individual attacker points; first to 10 wins

## Execution
1. Players line up at the baseline (or end of court); player 1 is the attacker, X1 is the defender.
2. Player 1 uses **any move** to attempt to score against X1.
3. **If 1 scores**: X1 goes to the end of the line; player 2 races out as the new defender on 1.
4. **If X1 stops 1** (turnover or defensive rebound): 1 goes to end of the line; X1 takes the ball and **races back to the head of the key** where X1 reverse pivots or front pivots to establish a pivot foot. X1 becomes the new attacker; player 2 races out to defend.
5. The new attacker may immediately attempt a shot upon reaching the head of the key and pivoting **if the defender has not yet arrived**.
6. Score is kept for each attacker. First player to 10 wins.

## Coaching Points
- New attacker must race back to the head of the key and establish a pivot foot before any move — this reinforces catch-and-pivot fundamentals
- "If you pivot and the defender isn't there yet — shoot it"
- Emphasize transition speed; slow transitions allow the defender to recover
- Defenders must sprint out hard — passive defense will give up easy catch-and-shoot opportunities

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Any move allowed; basic pivot foot establishment
2. **Intermediate**: Add designated pivot foot (right one rep, left next)
3. **Advanced**: Restrict to 2-3 specific moves per session to develop depth on fewer skills

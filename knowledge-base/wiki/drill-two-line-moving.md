# Two-Line Moving Drill

## Objective
Develop passing on the move by having two diagonal lines continuously exchange passes while players rotate lines after each delivery — building passing rhythm, movement, and court awareness simultaneously.

## Setup
- Divide team into two squads
- One squad lines up above the free-throw line
- The other squad lines up about 15 feet (4.5 m) away at a diagonal
- One ball per pair of squads
- Half court

## Execution

### Beginner
1. The squad at the top begins with a basketball; the first player passes to the first player in the other line
2. The first passer moves to the end of the lower line after making the pass
3. The lower-line player who received the pass immediately passes to the second player in the upper line, then moves to the end of the upper line
4. The drill continues until stopped by the coach
5. Pass types: chest passes for 1 minute → bounce passes for 1 minute → overhead (flip) passes for 1 minute

### Intermediate
- Same as beginner, but add one-handed chest pass for 1 minute and one-handed bounce pass for 1 minute

### Advanced
- Same as intermediate, but add overhead outlet pass for 1 minute and baseball pass for 1 minute
- Spread squads to 30 feet for the baseball pass segment

## Coaching Points
- Players must move immediately after passing — the pass-and-move habit is critical for game application
- Receiver steps to the ball on every pass
- Passer must watch the receiver's hands, not just throw to a general area
- The diagonal line angle simulates game-like passing angles — players rarely pass in straight horizontal lines in a game
- The rotation system eliminates dead time; every player is active
- "Barely touch the reception before zipping off another pass" — develop a quick-release habit [S15, p.93]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two diagonal lines; chest → bounce → overhead (1 min each); 15 ft spacing
2. **Intermediate**: Add one-handed chest and one-handed bounce
3. **Advanced**: Add overhead outlet and baseball passes; spread to 30 ft

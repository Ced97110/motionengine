# Jump Roll (Front Flip Chain — Step 3)

## Objective
Introduce upward explosive power into the forward roll by jumping UP (not forward) before hands catch the floor, building the plyometric arm strength and rotational tolerance needed for handsprings.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Soft surface preferred (grass, gymnastics mat)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Stand with feet symmetrical. Bend forward, aiming palms toward the floor.
2. **Jump up** as you descend, allowing the torso to fall as hands drop toward the floor.
3. Land on the palms with arms fairly straight — just as feet leave the floor. "You are basically catching yourself with your palms."
4. Braced arms bend to guide the movement as you roll over — head tucked, no weight on head.
5. Roll over the back without veering sideways.
6. Finish by rolling all the way to the **standing position**.

## Coaching Points
- **Jump UP, not forward** — This is the key distinction from the gymnast's *dive roll* which emphasizes forward projection; here, the jump emphasizes vertical height, building power for the front flip [S9, p.10]
- **Arms catch plyometrically** — The higher the hands are from the floor when the feet leave the ground, the more plyometric demand is placed on the upper limbs
- **"Catching with palms"** — Cue athletes to think of the hand contact as a controlled catch, not a crash
- **Head tucked at all times** — Same as Steps 1-2; no weight on the cervical spine
- **Regression**: Begin with palms already braced on the floor (removing the need to catch) to reduce upper-body power demands

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Palms pre-braced on floor; small jump to practice the turn — finish standing
2. **Intermediate**: Hands start off the floor; jump up and catch with palms at a low height
3. **Advanced**: Higher starting hand position creates longer fall distance — more plyometric demand on the arms; closer to the back-drop handspring mechanics

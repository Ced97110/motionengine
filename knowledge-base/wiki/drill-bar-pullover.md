# Bar Pullover (Muscle-Up Chain Step 9)

## Objective
Master the full unassisted bar pullover — pulling the body completely over the horizontal bar from a hanging position — building the explosive pulling strength and technique that is the foundation of the muscle-up.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Overhead horizontal bar
- **False grip required**: thumbs over the bar [S9, p.229]
- No leg assistance (unlike Step 8)

## Execution
1. Grab an overhead horizontal bar with shoulder-width grip, **shoulders pulled down tight**.
2. Use a **false grip** — thumbs over the bar.
3. Build a **powerful swing kip** — the backswing should carry you higher than in earlier steps.
4. As soon as you approach the peak of the backswing, **snap backwards with your arms**.
5. Simultaneously **clench glutes and posterior chain** to thrust your upper abs into the bar.
6. Keep **pulling back hard with the elbows** as you hurl your torso forward over the bar. **Keep legs tilted** to assist momentum.
7. **Rotate your wrists** as you reach the final pull-over position.
8. Finish in the over-bar position: **bar under upper abs, head and chest over the bar, legs raised, forearms as vertical struts**.
9. **Pause** in this position, then finish the repetition by rolling forwards over the bar.

## Coaching Points
- **"Calisthenic pullovers are not only an incredible exercise for harnessing nuclear-level explosive strength, they are also functional as hell — you are learning to pull your bodyweight up and over."** [S9, p.229]
- **False grip + wrist relaxation**: Need powerful grip on the way up, but must relax at the top to allow forearm/wrist rotation. Without relaxing, the forearms cannot rotate and the torso stalls.
- **Pause requirement**: Initially just completing the movement is sufficient. The pause over the bar will develop over time.
- **One step from the muscle-up**: Mastering the bar pullover means the athlete is just the concentric dip-press away from completing a full muscle-up [S9, p.229].
- **Legs tilted forward**: Keeping the legs tilted (not hanging straight down) creates counter-leverage that helps hurl the torso over the bar.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Complete the movement without pausing at the top — just get over the bar.
2. **Intermediate**: Hold a 1–2 second pause at the top (forearms vertical, bar under upper abs).
3. **Advanced**: Roll forwards over the bar as a full repetition finish [S9, p.230].

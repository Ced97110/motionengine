# Forward Step Lunge

## Objective
Build bilateral lower-extremity strength (quads, glutes, hamstrings, hip flexors) through a stepping lunge pattern that transfers directly to basketball acceleration, change of direction, and jump height.

## Setup
- 1 athlete, 1 spotter recommended
- Barbell across the upper back (high bar position), feet approximately shoulder-width apart
- **Dumbbell variation**: hold a dumbbell in each hand at the sides (neutral grip) if space is limited

## Execution
1. Take **one large step forward** with either leg (b).
2. Flex the knee of the lead leg until the front thigh **approaches parallel** to the floor.
3. Keep pressure on the front foot; use the rear foot for balance.
4. Keep the **torso erect** — do not lean forward.
5. As the front knee flexes, the heel of the rear foot lifts and the rear knee flexes.
6. Lunge to a position where the **front knee is over the front foot** and the **rear knee is 1-2 inches (3-5 cm) off the floor** (c).
7. Push the front foot off the floor to **return it back next to the rear foot** (d).
8. Keep torso erect on the return — do not lean backward.
9. Pause and repeat with the other leg.

## Coaching Points
- **Torso stays erect** throughout — forward or backward lean shifts load improperly
- Front knee stays **over the front foot** at the bottom — not caving inward or driving past the toes
- Back knee points at the floor and stays in line with the body
- Adjust step width to find the right balance — too short or too long disrupts mechanics
- **Inhale** on the downward/forward phase; **exhale** after the sticking point of the upward/backward return
- Spotter: stand 12-18 in. behind, shadow the step, hands near hips/waist

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight forward step lunge — master balance and alignment
2. **Intermediate**: Dumbbell forward step lunge (neutral grip, arms hang perpendicular to floor)
3. **Advanced**: Barbell forward step lunge with progressive load

## Common Mistakes
1. **Leaning forward** → shifts excess load to the knee → cue "chest tall, eyes forward"
2. **Front knee caving inward (valgus)** → ACL risk → cue "knee over second toe"
3. **Step too short or too long** → loss of balance and depth → adjust step width until depth and posture are achieved together
4. **Rear knee smashing the floor** → controlled descent needed → cue "hover 1-2 inches above the floor"

# Medicine-Ball Release Push-Ups With Partner

## Objective
Improve quickness in the upper-body pushing musculature by combining an explosive kneeling medicine ball throw with an immediate push-up — training rapid alternation between explosive and reactive contractions.

## Setup
- 2 players facing each other
- 1 medicine ball
- 5–10 feet of separation between partners

## Execution
1. Both partners start in a **kneeling position** facing each other.
2. Player A **throws the medicine ball** explosively to Player B, then immediately **falls forward into a push-up**.
3. Player A **pushes back up** to the kneeling start position while Player B returns the medicine ball.
4. Player A catches the ball and immediately repeats.
5. **Repeat as quickly as possible** for a set number of reps or time.

## Coaching Points
- The key is the **speed of the transition** — the push-up should begin the moment the ball leaves the hands.
- Keep the body straight during the fall and push-up; hips do not sag.
- Partner should return the ball with a smooth lob so the thrower can catch it in rhythm.
- Works the same muscles as passing — triceps, pectorals, anterior deltoid — in a fatigue-resistant, reactive pattern.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Perform with a lighter ball and slower throw; full push-up not required (just the lower eccentric).
2. **Intermediate**: Full kneeling throw → full push-up → reset at game speed.
3. **Advanced**: Perform from a standing position, falling into a full push-up then springing back up to catch.

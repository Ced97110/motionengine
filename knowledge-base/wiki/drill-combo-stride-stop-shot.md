# Combo Stride Stop Shot Drill

## Objective
Train the stride stop jump shot off 1 and 2 live dribbles, bilaterally, to build a reliable pull-up scoring move from both directions.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court — elbow to elbow range (12–18 ft)
- No additional equipment required
- Shot tracking table (FGA, FGM %, FTA, FTM %)

## Execution
1. Player starts with the ball at the perimeter in triple-threat position.
2. **1-Dribble Left**: take one hard left-hand dribble driving toward the lane, gather, land on both feet simultaneously in a stride stop, rise into a balanced jump shot.
3. Shoot 20 reps (Beginner: 5 reps; Intermediate: 10 reps) going left.
4. Step to the free-throw line. Shoot 5–10 FTs with full routine; track makes.
5. **1-Dribble Right**: repeat step 2 going right.
6. Shoot 20 reps going right; follow with 5–10 FTs.
7. Repeat the L/R/FT cycle a second time for 1-dribble (totaling 80 combo shots + 40 FTs for Workout #4-6 at full volume).
8. **2-Dribble Left/Right**: repeat the same structure but take 2 hard dribbles before the stride stop gather.
9. Total structure for the 240-shot workout: 8 sets × 20 combo shots + 8 clusters × 10 FTs.

**Volume Progressions:**
- **Workout #4-6 (240 shots)**: 8 sets × 20 combos + 8 × 10 FTs | FG goal: 97–160 (60%) | FT goal: 68–80 (85%)
- **Intermediate (120 shots)**: 8 sets × 10 combos + 8 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 48–80 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (80%)
- **Beginner (80 shots)**: 8 sets × 5 combos + 8 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 24–40 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (85%)

## Coaching Points
- **Stride stop mechanics**: both feet must land at the same time — a sequential (one-two) landing is not a stride stop and forfeits the pivot-foot option
- **Stay low on gather**: keep hips down during the gather so the shooting power comes from the legs, not the arms
- **Shot line**: elbow under the ball, aligned with the shooting foot, straight to the basket on every rep
- Cue: **"Gather, land, load, lift"** — four distinct beats between the dribble and the shot release
- Track every FGA and FTM — if FG% drops below 55%, slow down and focus on technique before continuing volume
- Both directions must reach 60% before adding volume; address the weaker side explicitly

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (80 shots)**: 5 reps per set, 5 FTs between sets — emphasize perfect landing mechanics over speed
2. **Intermediate (120 shots)**: 10 reps per set, 5 FTs — increase pace while maintaining 60% FG threshold
3. **Advanced (240 shots)**: 20 reps per set, 10 FTs — game-speed execution; add a chair/cone as a simulated defender at the elbow to make decisions more realistic

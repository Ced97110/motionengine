# Closeout Contest — Verticality

## Summary
Verticality is the defender's right to occupy vertical space above them when contesting a shot. Applied correctly during a closeout, the defender decelerates under control, maintains a straight vertical jump with arms extended overhead, and does not lean into or undercut the shooter. The goal is to change the shooter's release arc without drawing a foul. Done wrong — leaning forward, closing out long, or jumping laterally — it becomes a shooting-foul magnet.

## When to Use
- Any closeout where the shooter has squared up and is about to release
- Late-possession contests where a foul resets the shot clock
- Pick-and-roll big-on-guard switches where verticality replaces lateral containment

## Key Principles
1. Decelerate early — two or three short choppy steps. A long, flat-footed closeout is a foul waiting to happen.
2. Arrive low, hands high — body weight over the hips, arms already above the head.
3. Jump straight up from the landed position. Do NOT jump toward the shooter.
4. Keep both arms extended overhead on the way up and on the way down.
5. Land on the same spot of floor. No drift forward or sideways.

## Player Responsibilities
- **All defenders**: same fundamentals; perimeter players contest more per possession

## Common Mistakes
- Flying at the shooter with arms up — body momentum creates contact, official calls the foul
- Poking at the ball instead of contesting vertically — release is uncontested and the hand drops on the follow-through
- Leaving the feet laterally to redirect the shooter — opens a straight drive

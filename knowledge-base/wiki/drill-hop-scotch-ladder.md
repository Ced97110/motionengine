# Hop-Scotch Drill (Agility Ladder)

## Objective
Enhance elastic strength in the ankle complex and develop multi-directional foot coordination using a hop-scotch pattern on a half agility ladder. [S8, p.182]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half agility ladder
- Open space

## Execution
1. Start with one foot on each **side** of the ladder (feet outside, straddling the first rung).
2. Jump with **both feet into the first square** of the ladder (feet inside together).
3. Jump again, spreading feet so **each foot lands on the outside of the ladder** on the sides of the next square.
4. Jump back in with **both feet into the next square**.
5. Continue repeating this in/out/in/out pattern down the ladder.
6. Look straight ahead, not at the ground. [S8, p.182]

## Coaching Points
- Minimize ground contact time at each landing
- Land softly to control the movement — don't let the knees collapse inward
- Eyes up — use peripheral vision to track the ladder
- Rhythm is key: "in-out-in-out" as a verbal cue helps establish timing

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow walk-through of the in/out pattern to learn foot positioning
2. **Intermediate**: Maximum speed with both feet landing simultaneously
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**: (a) Land on **one foot** when landing inside the ladder squares (single-leg hop-scotch); (b) Add a sport-specific skill to the drill

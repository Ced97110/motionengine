# Sequence Jumping Jacks

## Objective
Improve lower-body power and quickness through jumping jacks that vary foot landing patterns, developing multi-planar leg elasticity. [S8, p.179]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open space
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Start with feet hip-width apart and perform a **standard jumping jack** (feet spread wide, arms overhead).
2. On the next jump, land in a **split-foot position** (one foot forward, one foot back), arms coordinating overhead.
3. Jump again and return to the standard starting position (feet together or hip-width).
4. Repeat the sequence continuously. [S8, p.179]

## Coaching Points
- Each landing should be quick and reactive — minimize sinking into the floor
- Keep the trunk upright throughout all positions
- Arm coordination should mirror the leg movement naturally
- Increase tempo once the movement pattern is established

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard jumping jacks only to establish rhythm
2. **Intermediate**: Add the split-foot variation as described
3. **Advanced (Complex Variation)**: Add a third jump in which you land in a **crossover foot position** — adds a rotational element and increases coordination demand

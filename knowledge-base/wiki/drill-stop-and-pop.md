# Stop and Pop

## Objective
Develop the pull-up jump shot off a live dribble by forcing players to stop quickly and shoot a midrange jumper at game speed.

## Setup
- 1 player (can be used with full team)
- Half court or full court
- 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Player starts at half court (or full court baseline) and uses a **speed dribble** toward the basket.
2. Player stops decisively ("on a dime") and pulls up to shoot a **12–15 foot (3.6–4.5 m) jump shot**.
3. Perform **10 repetitions with each hand.**
4. **Full-court version:** Go down using the right hand, come back using the left hand.

## Coaching Points
- **The stop must be decisive.** Dribbling at full speed and stopping sharply simulates game situations where the ball handler reads a defender and pulls up.
- **Do not dribble the ball straight down** when stopping — push the ball forward slightly into the stop so it doesn't hit the foot.
- **Maintain consistent shooting mechanics** regardless of speed: shot pocket catch, no dip, one-two step into the stop, elbow above eyebrow on the follow-through.
- **Both hands equally.** Players who only practice right-hand pull-ups have a major exploit for defenders who force them left.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Half court at reduced speed; focus on stopping in balance and maintaining proper shooting form.
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed from full court; track makes with each hand; require equal makes before finishing.
3. **Advanced**: Add a **crossover dribble** or **inside-out dribble** before stopping and popping. Adds a ball-handling deception element before the pull-up.

# Three-Player Continuous Screening

## Objective
Train all five players to apply every motion offense screen type (down, back, screen-the-screener, on-ball) continuously and read defensive adjustments in real time — without shooting.

## Setup
- 5 players on a half court (no defense initially; defenders optional at advanced stage)
- 3 players on one side of the court (weak side), 2 players on the ball side
- No shooting allowed; layup-only exception when adding defenders
- Coach-controlled time: divide 8 minutes into four 2-minute segments or space them throughout practice

## Execution
1. Line players up: 3 on the weak side, 2 on the ball side.
2. **On every pass, every player must move** — this is the core motion offense rule.
3. When 1 passes to 2, Player 1 immediately screens away (for Player 3). Player 3 sets up the screen with a V-cut before using it.
4. Player 1 may alternatively cut instead of screening, or dip and come off a screen set by Player 3.
5. On the weak side, Players 3, 4, and 5 cycle continuously through: down screens, back screens, screen-the-screener actions, V-cut replacements.
6. When Player 2 passes to 4 (figure 2), a new series activates on both sides — e.g., Player 3 screens down for 5, Player 1 sets a back screen for 3 (screen-the-screener).
7. Player 4 uses 2's down screen to dribble; Player 2 fades after screening.
8. Figure 3 illustrates 4 passing to 5, with the former weak-side 3 joining 2 and 4 while 1 back-screens for 5 (on-ball screen and roll).
9. **Key rule**: When a player cuts off a back screen to the ball side, the ball-side player **must pass to the weak side** — maintaining 2 on ball side, 3 on weak side.
10. Optional: Add 5 defenders. No shots except layups — effectively teaching a stall/motion offense simultaneously.

## Coaching Points
- Always maintain **2 players ball side, 3 players weak side**.
- Screeners must **call out the name** of the player they are screening for.
- The player receiving the screen must always **dip (V-cut) first** to set up their defender for the screening angle.
- Screener and cutter must work in tandem — the screener sets the screen where the cutter will be *after* the V-cut, not where they are now.
- Screening on the ball: screener sets the pick **half-body on the high side** of the dribbler's defender, forcing the defender to go below.
- Dribblers must **fake away** from intended direction (ball fake, direct-drive fake, or crossover fake from rocker step series).
- Down screens free jump shooters — set the screen where the teammate will be post-V-cut.
- Back screens: screener sets at least a few feet from the defender to avoid an illegal screen call; stays **motionless** while cutter sets up the defender.
- Cutter off back screen scrapes shoulder-to-shoulder with screener, forcing a defensive switch or easy layup.
- After setting the back screen, screener **fades outside**.
- Screen-the-screener: read whether defense is switching all screens (screen 5's defender) or fighting through (screen 3's defender).

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: 3 players weak side, 2 ball side — no defense, no shooting. Focus on V-cut timing and calling names.
2. **Intermediate**: Restrict which screen types are allowed on each side to reduce confusion. Name specific cuts allowed.
3. **Advanced**: Add 5 defenders. Still no shooting except layups — teaches motion offense under full defensive pressure and develops stall-game capability.

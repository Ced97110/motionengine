# Swinger

## Overview
A man-to-man set play designed to create a blindside pick-and-roll for the point guard at the top of the key. After shifting the defense with a wing pass, the point guard receives a sudden screen and attacks the rim with three read-based scoring options depending on how the defense rotates. [S4, pp.49-50]

## Formation
1-3-1 set. 1 (best decision-maker) at the top. 2 on the strong-side wing. 3 on the weak-side wing. 5 at the high post. 4 in the corner/baseline area.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Defense Shift
- 1 passes to 2 on the wing to shift the defense.
- 2 passes back to 1 at the top of the key.
- As this pass is made, **5 sets a blindside screen on D1** (1's defender, who has their back turned after chasing the pass). [S4, p.49]

### Phase 2: Pick-and-Roll Attack
- 1 immediately uses the screen and attacks towards the rim.
- As 1 drives, **3 slides down to the corner** and **4 slides along the baseline** to find a hole in the defense. [S4, p.49]
- 5 rolls after setting the screen.

### Phase 3: Read and Score
Three options based on defensive rotation:

- **Option 1 — No help:** If no defender steps up to stop the ball, 1 pulls up for a jump shot. [S4, p.50]
- **Option 2 — D4 steps out:** If D4 steps out to defend 1's drive, 1 passes to 3 in the **corner for the three-point shot**. [S4, p.50]
- **Option 3 — D5 steps out:** If D5 steps out to defend, 1 bounce passes to 4 for the **baseline layup**. [S4, p.50]

## Key Coaching Points
- 5 must set the screen at a **good angle** so 1 can attack the rim directly. "Youth players often set the screen on the side of the defender and they easily slip under it" — the screen must be set in front of D1. [S4, p.50]
- 3 and 4 must be in an **athletic stance, ready to catch and shoot** the moment they receive 1's pass. [S4, p.50]
- 1 must be a great decision-maker — read the defense immediately off the screen and commit to the right option.
- Key personnel: 1 (great decision-maker), 5 (strong screener), 3 (three-point shooter). [S4, p.49]

## Counters
- If D1 anticipates the blindside screen, 5 can slip to the basket instead of setting the screen.
- If the defense switches, 5 rolling to the rim after the switch creates a size mismatch.

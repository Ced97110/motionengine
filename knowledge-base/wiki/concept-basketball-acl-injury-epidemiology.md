# ACL Injury in Basketball — Epidemiology and Risk Factors

## Summary
ACL disruption is a serious basketball injury with significant loss of playing time and a long post-operative rehabilitation program. In basketball specifically, the female-to-male ACL injury ratio is 3.5:1 — the highest of any sport (compared to 2.67:1 in soccer, 1:1 in alpine skiing) [S10, p.209]. In the NBA, 22% of players who underwent ACL surgery failed to return to competition, and nearly half (44%) who did return experienced lower player efficiency ratings [S10, p.208].

## When to Use
- Designing ACL prevention programs for basketball teams at all levels
- Counseling female athletes on ACL risk factors
- Screening high-risk athletes for dynamic valgus and proprioceptive deficits
- Return-to-play decision-making after ACL reconstruction

## Key Principles

### Mechanism of Injury
1. **Noncontact dominant** — most ACL injuries in basketball involve deceleration and sudden direction change causing abnormal tibial rotation [S10, p.209]
2. **Valgus knee collapse** — contributes to ACL injury; occurs more frequently in women [S10, p.209]
3. **Increased knee abduction moment** — high sensitivity and specificity for ACL injury risk [S10, p.209]
4. **Female athletes** — predisposed to land with increased knee abduction [S10, p.209]

### Gender Disparity — Contributing Factors
- Female landing mechanics with increased knee abduction (correctable with training) [S10, p.209]
- Women experienced ACL injuries at 2–4× the rate of men (University of Connecticut data) [S10, p.209]
- Increased knee abduction is correctable with dynamic proprioceptive training [S10, p.209]

### Skeletally Immature Athletes
- Common ACL injury pattern: avulsion fracture of the tibia (less commonly femur) [S10, p.209]
- Chondro-osseous junction is the weakest part of the ACL complex in growing athletes [S10, p.209]

### Return-to-Play After ACL Surgery (NBA Data)
- 22% of NBA players failed to return to competition after ACL surgery [S10, p.208]
- 44% of those who returned experienced lower player efficiency rating [S10, p.208]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Participate in neuromuscular ACL prevention programs (jump training, proprioceptive training)
- **Female athletes**: Prioritize dynamic proprioceptive training to correct valgus landing mechanics

## Common Mistakes
1. **Assuming ACL injuries are contact injuries** → Most basketball ACL injuries are noncontact — deceleration and direction change are the primary mechanisms
2. **Neglecting landing mechanics training for female athletes** → Dynamic proprioceptive training has been shown to correct the valgus landing pattern that predisposes to ACL injury
3. **Expecting full NBA-level return to play** → 22% of NBA players never return; coaches should have realistic expectations for recovery
4. **Missing avulsion fractures in young athletes** → Chondro-osseous junction is weakest link in growing athletes; suspect tibial avulsion rather than midsubstance tear

# One-on-Two Pivoting Drill

## Objective
Develop ball protection and escape footwork under extreme pressure by forcing one offensive player to pivot away from two simultaneous defenders.

## Setup
- Divide team into squads of 3 players
- Each squad has one basketball
- Court area: any open space; no specific court area required
- Duration: 90 seconds total (30 seconds per role)

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players form groups of 3; one player is offense, two are defenders
2. The two defenders try to steal the ball — they may move side to side in any manner; their mission is to at least deflect the basketball
3. The offensive player tries to pivot away from the two defenders constantly, using a low swing-through of the ball to keep defenders from deflecting it
4. **Critical rule**: Players must keep their pivot foot on the floor — no traveling; use pivots to keep the body between defenders and the basketball
5. After 30 seconds, one defender becomes the offensive player; the offensive player becomes a defender
6. After another 30 seconds, the remaining defender who has not yet been on offense becomes the new offensive player

### Intermediate
- The offensive player may use the **step-through move** and dribble the ball twice before reestablishing a pivot foot (or pivot feet) via the jump stop or stride stop

## Coaching Points
1. **Step-through technique**: To execute the step-through, the attacker fakes in one direction and then comes back to split the two defenders
2. **Ball swing after split**: After splitting the two defenders, swing the ball through — no higher than the middle of the lower leg; the swing should be hard and quick
3. **Dribble after split**: If the player uses the dribble after the split, it should begin with a **toss of the ball out a few feet (1–1.5 m)**; run to catch up with the basketball and begin to dribble; the ball must be out of the dribbler's hands before the pivot foot is lifted — otherwise it is a walk; tossing prevents defenders from flicking the ball from behind
4. Coaching cue: "Keep your body between your defenders and the basketball at all times"
5. Watch for traveling — this drill naturally produces many travel violations; correct immediately

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary pivoting only — no dribble; two defenders try to deflect
2. **Intermediate**: Offensive player can step-through and dribble twice before reestablishing pivot foot
3. **Advanced**: Add a third defender to create a 1-on-3 scenario

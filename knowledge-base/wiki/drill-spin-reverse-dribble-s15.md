# Spin (Reverse) Dribble Maneuver (Drill #30)

## Objective
Develop the spin (reverse) dribble — a 180° reverse spin that protects the ball through the turn while changing direction away from a defender.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- 4 lines at one baseline
- **Tape 2×2 foot (.6×.6 m) boxes** at midcourt and ~20 feet (6 m) from each baseline
- 1 basketball per player
- Duration: 1 minute

<!-- DIAGRAM: Four columns of players. Three taped boxes per column. Players dribble to each box, execute a spin/reverse at the box, continue in new direction with a speed dribble. [S15, p.67] -->

## Execution
1. Players line up in four columns at the baseline.
2. Dribble to each box and execute the **spin (reverse) dribble**:
   - **Control the ball with the right hand throughout the spin** — do NOT switch to the left hand until the spin is complete. Switching too early leaves the ball exposed for a steal.
   - After completing the spin, **switch ball to the left hand**.
   - The **right side should be close to the ball** after the spin, protecting it.
   - **Explode in an almost straight line** down the floor after the spin.
3. Dribble **down and back with the right hand** first (learning both spin directions), then **down and back left-handed**.
4. At the far end, execute a **reverse (or front) pivot** and return, using the spin at each box.
5. **Jump-stop** about 10 feet (3 m) from the next teammate and deliver a **chest or bounce pass**.
6. All players repeat the sequence.

## Options
- **Race format**: Guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Advanced Progression
1. **Alternating dual-ball**: Alternate bouncing each ball while driving.
2. **Simultaneous dual-ball**: Both balls touch hands and bounce at the same time.

## Coaching Points
- **Timing of the hand switch**: The **critical error** is switching ball from right to left too early — this exposes the ball to a defender stepping through to steal it. **Wait until the spin is complete**.
- **Eyes downcourt**: Eyes stay on the downcourt area throughout the spin — **not** on the ball.
- **Post-spin explosion**: Explode when the ball touches the left hand. The explosion should be in a near straight line down the floor.
- **Ball protection**: Keep the body (right side) close to the ball after the spin.
- **Fingerpads**: Always dribble with the first pads of the fingers.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary spin — practice the 180° turn with hand-switch timing before adding movement. Use a wall to feel the correct body position.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-court bilateral drill with box stops, pivot, and pass at end.
3. **Advanced**: Dual-ball versions; add a live defender chasing through the spin.

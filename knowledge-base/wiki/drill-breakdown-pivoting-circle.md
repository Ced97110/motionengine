# Breakdown Pivoting Circle Drill for Shooters

## Objective
Train the inside-heel pivot and free-foot square-up footwork for shooting off a cut or off the dribble — in isolation, without the distraction of actually shooting, allowing players to focus exclusively on foot and hand mechanics.

## Setup
- Use the **free throw circles and center jump circle** in a gymnasium — up to 7 circles available
- **3–4 players** per circle (groups of 4 preferred)
- A ball, coach, or manager is placed in the **exact center** of each circle to represent the passer
- Players hold a basketball (for self-toss) OR dribble around the circle perimeter
- Players jog **slightly outside** the circle line in a controlled, clockwise direction to start [S6, pp.164-165]

## Execution

### Phase 1 — Cutter/Pass-Receiver Footwork (No Shooting)
1. On the coach's first command, all players at each circle begin jogging clockwise, slightly outside the circle line.
2. On the second command (whistle), each player **stops the jog**, tosses the ball slightly ahead of themselves, and catches it (simulating a pass).
3. As they catch, they **drive the inside heel (right foot when going clockwise) straight down** into the floor to stop lateral momentum.
4. They **swing the free foot (left) around** to square their shoulders to the simulated basket (the coach/ball in the center of the circle).
5. They hold the ball in the shooting pocket — **no shot is taken** — to keep full focus on footwork.
6. On the next command, players resume jogging until the next whistle.
7. After several repetitions, the group reverses to **counterclockwise** — now the left foot is the inside pivot foot. [S6, pp.164-165]

### Phase 2 — Dribbler/Shooter Footwork (No Shooting)
1. Same format, but players **dribble with the outside hand** (left hand when going clockwise, right hand going counterclockwise) rather than self-tossing.
2. On the whistle, each dribbler **kills the dribble** by driving the inside heel down simultaneously with the final dribble hitting the floor.
3. Free foot swings around to square up to the imaginary basket at the circle's center.
4. Ball is pulled into the shooting pocket — **no shot taken**.
5. On the next command, players resume dribbling.
6. Reverse direction after several reps. [S6, pp.165-166]

## Coaching Points
- Stand in the **center of the circle** to observe all players' footwork simultaneously — this is the best vantage point for correction.
- Watch that the inside heel **drives straight down** — not forward or sideways — at the moment of the catch or last dribble.
- Watch that the free foot **fully swings around** — partial swings leave shoulders angled offline.
- "Inside heel stops you. Free foot squares you." — core teaching phrase.
- Players who float or drift are using the wrong pivot foot or not driving the heel down hard enough.
- "Be quick, but not in a hurry" (John Wooden) — players must execute at game speed but with body control. [S6, p.161]
- **Motivate players to work on their weak direction.** Players will gravitate toward their strong side — force reps going both directions. [S6, p.166]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary — players stand at a spot on the circle and practice only the heel-drive and free-foot swing without jogging, multiple reps.
2. **Intermediate**: Jog + self-toss (Phase 1 above) — add movement but remove dribble to keep footwork focus pure.
3. **Advanced**: Dribble version (Phase 2) — add the dribble at jogging speed in both directions; then increase to a controlled run.
4. **Game-speed**: After footwork is established, players actually **shoot** at a basket to integrate footwork with a real shot.

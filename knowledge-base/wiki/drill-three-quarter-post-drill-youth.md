# Three-Quarter Drill — Post Defense and Offense (Drill 101)

## Objective
Teach **three-quarter defensive post coverage** and the offensive counter-moves — how defender and post attacker battle for the dominant foot position, and how the attacker exploits any defensive mistake.

## Setup
- Individual or team — intermediate level
- Low block / lane area
- **3 roles**: Player 5 (post attacker), X5 (three-quarter defender), Player 3 (corner passer) and a coach
- **Rotation**: 5 → X5 → 3 → end of line

## Execution

### The Battle:
1. **Player 5 flash pivots** toward the coach. **X5 defends from the three-quarter defensive position** (between full fronting and playing behind).
2. **Player 5 tries to get the right foot ahead of X5's right foot.** X5 tries to keep their right foot in front of Player 5's. Both use **slide steps** to compete for this position.
3. After several small steps, **Player 5 will be pushed too far outside** — X5 immediately tries to get **behind** Player 5. Player 5 counters by sliding back toward the lane.
4. Allow this foot battle to continue until **a defensive mistake is made**.

### Offensive Reads:
- **If X5 gets behind Player 5**: Player 5 moves into **sitting position** (low, buttocks out), receives the pass from the coach, and makes an offensive move.
- **If no mistake is made in a reasonable time**: The coach passes to **Player 3** in the corner. Player 5 **releases toward the goal**, trying to keep X5 on their back. Player 3 passes to Player 5, who tries to score — using pump fakes and slide-step dribble as needed.

## Options
1. Use **Player 1** in place of the coach — make it a live one-on-one post drill.
2. Play **defenders X1 and X3** to prevent rapid ball movement outside while still drilling 1-on-1 in the post.

## Coaching Points
- The three-quarter position means the defender is **neither fully fronting nor fully behind** — diagonal to the post player, cutting off the direct entry pass.
- "It's a battle of feet" — no hands or arms in the pushing contest.
- When the ball moves to the corner (Player 3), the post player's **release angle changes** — Player 3 has a much better entry pass angle.
- Post player may need pump fakes + slide-step dribble to maintain balance after catching.

## Progressions
1. **Intermediate**: As described — coach as passer, no perimeter defense.
2. **Advanced**: Add perimeter defenders (X1, X3) to force timed entry passes.

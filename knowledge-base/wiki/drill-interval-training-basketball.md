# Interval Training — Basketball Preseason Conditioning

## Objective
Develop basketball-specific anaerobic conditioning using position-graded interval runs that build fast-twitch muscle capacity, lung efficiency, and recovery speed for the demands of competitive basketball.

## Setup
- **Players**: Entire team, split into three position groups (guards, forwards, centers)
- **Court area**: Full court (94 feet / 28.7 m) or indoor track with 94-foot (200m equivalent) distance marked
- **Equipment**: Wall clock visible to all players (set to 24:00), stopwatch per assistant coach, record sheet
- **Personnel**: Head coach + assistant coaches as timers and recorders; statistician recommended

## Execution

### Eighths Day (Monday, Wednesday, Friday)
1. Set the clock to 24:00. Clock runs continuously until all groups complete the full mile (8 intervals of 200m each).
2. **Guards** step to the starting line and begin at 24:00. They run one-eighth mile (200m). Assistant coaches record each player's time as they cross the finish line.
3. **Forwards** begin at the 23:00 mark on the clock.
4. **Centers** begin at the 22:00 mark.
5. When the centers finish, the guards begin their second interval. Continue in rotation until every player has completed 8 intervals (1 full mile).
6. Players must touch the end lines with both hands on every turn. Failure to do so = one additional interval.
7. **False start**: One extra eighth-mile interval penalty.

### Quarters Day (Tuesday, Thursday)
1. Set the clock to 24:00.
2. **Guards** begin at 24:00 and run one-quarter mile (400m).
3. **Forwards** begin at 22:00.
4. **Centers** begin at 20:00.
5. Two-minute intervals separate the start of each group.
6. Each group runs 4 quarter-mile intervals for a total of 1 mile.

### Time Standards
**Week 1:**
- Guards: :44 (Mon 200m) → 1:38 (Tue 400m) → :43 (Wed) → 1:35 (Thu) → :42 (Fri)
- Forwards: :44 → 1:38 → :43 → 1:35 → :43
- Centers: :45 → 1:42 → :44 → 1:41 → :44

**Week 2:**
- Guards: :41 → 1:33 → :40 → 1:33 → :39
- Forwards: :42 → 1:33 → :41 → 1:33 → :40
- Centers: :44 → 1:40 → :43 → 1:40 → :42

### Makeup Penalties
- **Missed time**: Player runs one additional eighth. Add 5 seconds to the time requirement.
- **Missed makeup (second attempt)**: Add 10 seconds for the third attempt.
- **Missed court turn**: Two additional eighths.
- **Quarters miss (first)**: 15 extra seconds on the makeup; thereafter set individually.
- All makeups completed before Phase 2 (agility stations) begins.

## Coaching Points
- Use the clock as the motivator — players race the clock, not each other. This removes the coach as the disciplinarian.
- "The objective is to get players in sufficient condition to complete a two-hour practice without injury — not boot-camp mentality" [S13, p.5].
- Record all times daily. The goal is for every player to reduce their time each day.
- Guards and forwards should make Week 1 times without major difficulty. "Everyone struggles with breathing in the quarters."
- Big man times should be established in consultation with the players; modify for age, weight, and speed.
- Maintain proper hydration; players must have water available throughout.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner/High School**: Extend time allowances by 3-5 seconds per interval; allow a 3-day ramp before reducing times.
2. **Intermediate/College**: Use the standards as written; enforce makeup rules strictly.
3. **Advanced/Pro**: Reduce starting times further; add a 3rd week with additional quarter-mile intervals; consider GPS tracking for distance accuracy.

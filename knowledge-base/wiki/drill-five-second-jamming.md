# Five-Second Jamming Drill

## Objective
Develop the wrist-crossed ball-jamming technique — preventing a pass after the dribble is picked up — in a short, intense two-player format.

## Setup
- 2 players (1 offense, 1 defense) per pair
- Half-court; use both half-court sidelines, the baseline, and the half-court line as the out-of-bounds boundaries
- Can use any two lines on the court
- No additional equipment

## Execution
1. Defender passes to the offensive player to start.
2. Offensive player takes **two hard dribbles**, picks up the ball, and raises it over his head.
3. Defender slides to guard the two dribbles and, the instant the ball is picked up, **jams the ball**: raises both hands with wrists crossed to mirror and prevent any pass.
4. The offensive player picks up and raises the ball 3–4 times during the possession.
5. Defender jams every time.
6. Ball handler dribbles back and forth from the starting point.
7. Players exchange roles.
8. Repeat.

## Coaching Points
- **Wrists crossed, thumbs pointing at shoulders** — this is the quickest hand position for deflecting a pass.
- Hands no lower than shoulders at all times.
- Defender must believe he can deflect the next pass on every repetition.
- "This is a very short but intense drill; we emphasize the technique of jamming the dribbler." [S7, p.237]
- The defender guarding the receiver also believes he can intercept every pass — applies mental intensity to the drill.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary — offensive player holds ball above head, defender practices jam position repeatedly.
2. **Intermediate**: Two dribbles + jam as described above.
3. **Advanced**: Live two dribbles; defender must mirror every pump fake after the pick-up without fouling.

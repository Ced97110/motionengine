# Cooldown — Breath and Heart Rate Down-Regulation

## Objective
Bring the heart rate down and reset the nervous system after a hard practice or a tight game.

## Setup
- 1 player (works for a full team in a quiet circle on the baseline)
- Any flat surface — court, bench, or floor in the locker room
- No equipment

## Execution
1. **Stand or sit tall, eyes soft — 15 seconds.** Settle the feet flat, drop the shoulders, let the jaw relax. Cue: "Long spine, soft eyes."
2. **Box breathing — 8 cycles, about 90 seconds.** Breathe in through the nose for a count of four. Hold for four. Out through the mouth for four. Hold empty for four. Repeat eight times. Cue: "In four, hold four, out four, hold four — even sides on the box."
3. **Belly breathing — 6 cycles, about 60 seconds.** Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly. Breathe so only the bottom hand moves. Slow it down — five seconds in, seven seconds out. Cue: "Lower hand rises, upper hand stays still."
4. **Quiet sit — 30 seconds.** Drop the count, breathe normally, notice that the heart is slower than when you started. Cue: "Notice the difference — this is the state you want before tip-off, not after."

## Coaching Points
- The longer exhale (seven counts) is the move that drops the heart rate. If players rush it, they get nothing.
- Eyes can be open or closed — closed often works better, but a shy player will not close them in front of teammates. Do not force it.
- Use this segment to send the team out of the gym calm. Players who walk out wired sleep worse and recover slower.
- Pair with [[drill-cooldown-hip-flow]] for a longer parasympathetic block.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Drop the box-breath holds; just inhale four, exhale four for eight cycles.
2. **Advanced**: Extend the exhale to a count of eight in the belly-breathing block.

# Interception Stance

## Objective
Develop the interception stance — a fence-slide denial position with pressure on the front foot — enabling defenders to front-pivot into passing lanes for steals and to deny backdoor cuts.

## Setup
- Half court
- 3 players: player 1 (passer/ball handler at guard spot), player 2 (offensive wing), X2 (defender on 2)
- Line of additional players outside
- Rotation: 1 → X2 → 2 → end of line
- Duration: 3 minutes

<!-- DIAGRAM: ① at guard spot (right side), ② at left wing, X2 between them in denial fence-slide position, line of players on the right side. Ball is with ①. -->

## Execution
**Beginner:**
1. Player 2 uses V-cuts to make X2 use fence slides to stay in denial.
2. Player 1 passes toward player 2.
3. X2 **pivots on the front foot** and intercepts the pass.

**Intermediate:**
1. Allow player 2 to cut backdoor.
2. X2 uses the **other** advantage of the interception stance: pick up the back foot and slide-step with 2 to deny the backdoor pass.

## Coaching Points
1. **Front pivot for interception**: fence-slide pressure on the front foot → simple front pivot → defender is in the passing lane for the steal. "A simple front pivot puts the defender in the passing lane for the interception" [S15, p.271]
2. **Backdoor denial**: pressure on the front foot → pick up back foot → slide-step with 2 to prevent receiving the backdoor pass
3. **Blocking the middle cut**: being in fence-slide position prevents 2 from stepping toward X2 to gain the advantage of a middle cut
4. The interception stance is the **bridge between denial and help** — same physical position enables both actions
5. "By being in fence-slide position, 2 cannot step toward X2 and gain the advantage of the middle cut" [S15, p.271]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Passive V-cuts, player 1 passes predictably, X2 practices front pivot
2. **Intermediate**: Player 2 adds backdoor option; X2 reads and reacts
3. **Advanced**: Player 1 can pass at any time; player 2 can V-cut, middle cut, or backdoor; X2 plays full denial live

# Midrange Jump Shot

## Summary
The midrange jump shot (approximately 12–19 feet / 3.6–5.8 m) is the cornerstone of offensive scoring. There has never been a truly great scorer in the history of the game who could not consistently hit shots from the midrange area. While the three-point shot gets more attention, the midrange is a critical weapon that separates shooters from scorers.

The foundational approach is the one-two step catch-and-shoot: use momentum stepping into the ball, catch in the shot pocket, and flow directly into the jump shot without pausing. The pull-up off the dribble extends these same mechanics to live-dribble situations.

## When to Use
- Catch-and-shoot off a pass (inside-out, skip pass, pass off a screen)
- Pull-up off one or two dribbles when the defense overplays the drive
- Off shot fakes and jab steps to create space (intermediate/advanced)
- Mid-post and elbow jumpers for bigs attacking from the high post

## Key Principles
1. **Use the one-two step — be the aggressor.** Step into the ball; do not catch and pause. The one-two step generates momentum and brings the legs into the shot efficiently. "Don't catch and pause — step, catch, rise."
2. **Prefer one-two step over a jump stop.** A jump stop kills all momentum, forcing a reload; the defender becomes the aggressor and can close out easily. The one-two step keeps the shooter on the front foot.
3. **Catch in the shot pocket.** Ball arrives directly into the L-shaped shooting position. No dipping, no raising the ball above the waist before bringing it down (high-low-high is a defensive gift).
4. **Shoot low to high.** A dipping motion slows the shot and gives the defender time to contest.
5. **Shooting foot stays in a straight line to the target.** The body should not turn or twist during the jump.
6. **Release just before the top of the jump.** Waiting until the peak creates downward momentum and the ball falls short.
7. **Elbow finishes above the eyebrow, in front of the face.** Freeze the follow-through.
8. **Shoot and stay from 15+ feet.** The longer the shot, the longer the rebound off the rim; stay in place to track the ball or be an outlet.

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Core skill for all players. Bigs use the elbow jumper; guards and wings use the wing/corner midrange.

## Variations
### Pull-Up Jump Shot
Using a live dribble as a "pass to yourself":
- Push the ball out in front — never dribble outside the body.
- Long stride with the live (non-pivot) foot, enabling the standard one-two step into the shot.
- Going to your **strong hand**: pick up the ball with two hands into the shot pocket.
- Going to your **weak hand**: let the ball come up into the weak hand, then bring it across to the shooting hand — "loading the gun." This keeps the elbow tucked on the shot line and maintains balance.
- Practice with one-dribble and two-dribble pull-up drills, going both directions with each foot as the pivot.

### Midrange With Fakes
Add shot fakes, jab steps, and jab-then-fake combinations to the same catch-and-shoot reps. Rotate through variations across practice days to build a multi-layered midrange arsenal.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Jump stop instead of one-two step** → Kills momentum; defender becomes the aggressor. Use one-two step to stay aggressive.
2. **Catching high and going low (high-low-high)** → Negative motion; defender gets a hand on the ball and more time to close out. Stay low and come up continuously.
3. **Dribbling outside the body on pull-up** → Leads to dribbling off the foot. Push the ball out in front to lead into the shot.
4. **Reaching across the body with the shooting hand going weak-side** → Pulls shot line out of alignment. Load the gun: catch weak-hand, bring across to shooting hand.
5. **Turning or twisting the body in the air** → Shot line goes offline. Body must remain straight and square through the jump.
6. **Following shot inside 15 feet** → Acceptable here, but at 15+ feet, shoot and stay; longer rebounds travel farther off the rim.

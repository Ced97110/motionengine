# Press Roll (Front Flip Chain — Step 2)

## Objective
Develop the use of hands as levers to control the body through forward rotation — building the upper-limb conditioning required for eventual handspring and front flip mastery.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (solo)
- **Equipment**: Soft surface (grass, carpet) recommended for beginners
- **Space**: Any open floor area

## Execution
1. Stand with feet symmetrical (no offset); squat toward the ground.
2. Place both palms flat on the floor in front of you as you dip down.
3. Continue lowering slowly forward by squatting and bringing the torso down.
4. Straighten the legs until you overbalance forward. Brace the arms to absorb most of your weight.
5. Let the braced arms guide the movement as you roll over — head tucked tightly, taking virtually no weight.
6. Roll over the upper back, trying not to veer to either side.
7. Finish naturally in a seated position.

**Key biomechanical point**: This drill begins making use of the hands as levers to control the body's forward rotation — a foundational skill for handsprings. More advanced drills progressively increase the forces passing through the upper limbs. [S9, p.148]

## Coaching Points
- "Both palms flat — symmetrical base — let the arms do the work."
- Head takes virtually no weight — it is tucked and protected throughout.
- Keep abdominals braced (trunk tension) through the entire roll.
- Movement should be smooth and controlled; no crashing or sudden drops.
- The hands and upper-back both take weight at the beginning; as skill improves, shift more load to the hands.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Use soft surfaces (grass, carpet); control the head descent carefully; brace the trunk hard with abdominal tension.
2. **Intermediate (Standard)**: Both hands and upper back share weight at the initiation of the roll; head fully tucked.
3. **Advanced**: Take *more* force through the hands so that the upper back only contacts the floor at the last moment — increasing the functional demand on the upper limbs and preparing them for handspring forces.

# Flex Veer Option

## Summary
The veer option is a counter within the flex offense used when the defense is overplaying the baseline cutter and neglecting the back screener. The back screener (05) sets what appears to be a normal flex back screen for 03, but instead of following out to the perimeter, 05 ducks in on the high side of his own screen into the free-throw lane, exploiting the position advantage that X5's attention to 03 has created. Combined with proper perimeter spacing and footwork by the guard (02), this action isolates X5 on the interior and creates a high-percentage post-up opportunity. [S6, pp.252-256]

## When to Use
- Defense is very concerned with the flex baseline cutter (03) and not respecting the screener
- 05 is a good interior scoring threat who can capitalize on a position advantage
- The offense wants to attack/isolate X5 specifically on the interior
- As a constraint play that punishes aggressive flexed-out back-screen defense

## Key Principles
1. **Low-side cut by 03 creates the space**: 03 cuts off 05's back screen on the low side — this causes X5 to step over/hedge to help on 03, positioning X5 lower than 05. [S6, pp.252-253]
2. **05 ducks in high**: After 03 cuts low, 05 reverse pivots (contact/left foot as pivot) and steps into the middle of the free-throw lane on the high side of his own screen. [S6, pp.253-254]
3. **05's footwork into the lane**: 05 steps into the center of the lane first with his top foot (right foot), then lower foot, then top foot again — similar to a controlled defensive-slide pace — to reach the ideal high percentage location. [S6, p.256]
4. **Stay above the notch**: 05 must position himself above the block and in the middle of the lane so that passing teammates have a clear entry angle and can lob the ball if needed. [S6, pp.255-256]
5. **Guard spacing (02)**: After reversing to 01, 02 makes a shallow flare cut to reduce defensive congestion and give 01 a better passing angle to 05 in the lane; also creates a return-pass option. [S6, pp.253-254]
6. **Lob option**: If X5 three-quarters or fronts 05, 05's high-middle lane position gives 01 or 02 a lob angle from the top or wing. [S6, p.255]
7. **Triple-threat on reversal**: 01 receives the reversal pass and immediately gets in triple-threat position (pivot off inside left heel to square up); looks first to 05 in the lane, then perimeter shot, then shot fake and drive. [S6, p.256]

## Player Responsibilities
- **C (05 — Back Screener / Veer Cutter)**: Set flex back screen for 03; as 03 cuts low, duck in high on the high side with a reverse pivot (left foot pivot, right foot free); slide into the middle of the lane with top-foot-first footwork; seal if fronted; use post moves on catch.
- **SF (03 — Baseline Cutter)**: Cut off 05's screen on the low side to draw X5 toward the low block and open the high side for 05.
- **SG (02 — Ballside Guard / Reversal)**: Reverse ball to 01; then make a shallow flare cut to spread the defense, reduce congestion, and create a passing angle to 05 if 01 cannot deliver.
- **PG (01 — Weak-side Guard)**: Receive reversal pass; pivot off inside heel to triple-threat position; look first for 05 in the lane; option to center ball via dribble to improve passing angle.

## Variations
### Veer with Full Reversal to 02
If 01 reverses back to 02 after the veer starts, 02 is in triple-threat to attack X2 — with option to pass inside to the veer-cutting 05, shoot, or shot fake and drive. [S6, p.256]

### Standard Veer (No Reversal Back)
01 holds the ball and looks directly to 05 in the lane after receiving 02's reversal pass. [S6, p.254]

## Common Mistakes
1. **03 cuts high instead of low** → High cut doesn't force X5 low; X5 can stay high and contest 05's duck-in; 03 must commit to a low cut.
2. **05 ducks in low instead of high** → Eliminates the whole concept; 05 must go high-side where X5's attention has moved away.
3. **05 steps across with the wrong foot first** → Top foot (right) must lead; wrong foot sequence disrupts rhythm and allows X5 to recover.
4. **02 stays in place after the reversal** → Without 02's shallow flare cut, lane congestion is high and 01 has no outlet for return pass if needed.
5. **01 doesn't get into triple threat** → Not squaring up on the catch means a slower pass delivery to 05, giving X5 time to recover.

# Deadlift (NSCA Basketball)

## Objective
Build total posterior chain strength (glutes, hamstrings, erector spinae, quadriceps) to power jumping, running, and physical contact on the court — and reduce lower-body injury risk.

## Setup
- Barbell on the floor
- No spotter required (bar can be dropped if needed)
- Primary muscles: gluteus maximus, semimembranosus, semitendinosus, biceps femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, erector spinae [S11, Ch.6]

## Execution

### Beginning Position
1. Stand with feet **hip-width apart** (narrower than squat stance)
2. Set the bar over the **middle of the feet**, toes slightly pointing out
3. Grip bar with **closed pronated or alternating grip**, hands shoulder-width apart
4. Arms vertical to the floor, elbows fully extended
5. Lower hips by flexing hips and knees until **shins are near the bar**
6. Keep bar over midfoot; **raise the chest**; maintain **neutral spine**

### Movement Phases
1. With chest out, shoulders back, and elbows fully extended — **extend hips and knees simultaneously** to stand up with the weight
2. Keep the bar **in contact with the front of the thighs** as it rises
3. Fully extend hips and knees at lockout
4. **Lower the bar under control** to the floor in a straight line over the midfoot
5. Return bar to floor at the start of each repetition [S11, Ch.6]

**Breathing:** Inhale during the downward (lowering) phase; exhale after the sticking point of the upward movement

## Coaching Points
- "The barbell should be on the floor, over the midfoot, at the start of each rep" — do not drift the bar away from the body
- "Feet should be hip-width apart" — narrower than the squat
- **"Keep the chest up to avoid rounding the back"** — the most critical safety cue
- Head in neutral position — do not crane the neck upward or tuck the chin
- **"Do not shrug or lean back at the top"** — lockout = full hip extension only, not hyperextension
- Use alternating grip or hook grip for heavier loads to prevent the bar from rolling

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Romanian deadlift (hip hinge from standing) to learn the hinge pattern before pulling from the floor; or trap bar deadlift (more upright torso, easier to learn)
2. **Intermediate**: Conventional deadlift from the floor — controlled tempo, full ROM
3. **Advanced**: Heavy conventional deadlift; add clean pull or snatch pull derivatives for explosive carryover; single-leg Romanian deadlift for unilateral work

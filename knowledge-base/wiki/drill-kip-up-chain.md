# Kip-Up Chain (10-Step Progressive System)

## Objective
Progressively build the hip-snap, core strength, shoulder-push power, and kinesthetic awareness required to execute a full kip-up — getting from flat on the back to standing in one explosive movement.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface with adequate padding for beginners (grass, mat, or rubberized surface)
- No equipment required
- Work each step until it is mastered before progressing

## Execution

### Step 1: Rolling Sit-Up
1. Sit on the floor, knees bent, heels on the ground.
2. Roll onto back, bringing legs above body (keep knees bent).
3. As you roll, place hands palms-down alongside ears, fingers pointing toward feet.
4. Continue swinging legs over and back, rolling onto upper-back and shoulders.
5. Bring knees close to head.
6. Roll back to starting position and repeat.
- **Regression**: Swing legs up above head only (smaller range).
- **Progression**: Touch toes to ground behind head at the top.

### Step 2: Rolling Squat
1. Same beginning as Rolling Sit-Up.
2. After rolling back, allow forward momentum to push hips off the ground.
3. Arms reach forward; finish in a low squat position.
4. Extend into a full standing position.
5. Squat back down, roll back, repeat.
- **Regression**: Hands push from floor when glutes touch down.
- **Progression**: Arms folded across chest throughout.

### Step 3: Shoulder Pop
1. Same beginning as Rolling Sit-Up.
2. When feet are above head, explosively kick legs straight up (vertical).
3. Simultaneously push down through palms, briefly "popping" shoulders 1–2 inches off the floor.
4. Cushion the descent using muscular tension in arms and shoulders.
5. Roll back to start and repeat.
- **Regression**: Focus on just getting legs above head and kicking hard vertically.
- **Progression**: Push shoulders higher; advanced athletes can push into a handstand.

### Steps 4-10 (overview)
- **Step 4 — Bridge Kip**: Explode legs and land on feet in a bridge position (not standing).
- **Step 5 — Butt Kip**: Hip-snap throws torso forward; land on glutes not feet.
- **Step 6 — Half Kip**: Partial completion; build momentum toward full kip-up.
- **Step 7 — Classic Kip-Up**: Full movement from back to feet.
- **Step 8 — Straight-Leg Kip-Up**: Legs kept straight throughout; hips/spine must open at maximum speed.
- **Step 9 — Wushu Kip-Up**: Hands placed out to sides rather than alongside ears — reduced hand assistance.
- **Step 10 — No-Hands Kip-Up**: Master step; pure hip snap with zero hand support.

## Coaching Points
- Master each step completely before advancing — do not rush the chain
- For Steps 1-3, the "coil and explode" timing is critical — kick immediately out of the roll
- Rolling Sit-Ups can be trained daily; they are low-impact and high-frequency friendly
- The kick in Step 3+ must go UP, not outward — height generates the rotation, not outward momentum
- Hip-snap is the single most important quality; think "super-fast sit-up" in the rotation phase
- Eye focus: look at a point above the horizon beyond your feet throughout the movement
- Prerequisite conditioning: leg raises (hip flexors) + bridge push-ups (shoulder/wrist prep)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Steps 1-3 (Rolling Sit-Up, Rolling Squat, Shoulder Pop)
2. **Intermediate**: Steps 4-7 (Bridge Kip through Classic Kip-Up)
3. **Advanced**: Steps 8-10 (Straight-Leg, Wushu, No-Hands Kip-Up)

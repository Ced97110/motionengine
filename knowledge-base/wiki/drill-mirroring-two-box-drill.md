# Mirroring Two-Box Drill

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction and react to an opponent's movements using two adjacent cone boxes where one athlete sprints to random cones and the other mirrors every movement. [S8, p.190]

## Setup
- 2 players
- **Two boxes** each marked with 4 cones, with **5 yards (4.6 meters) between each cone**
- The two boxes are positioned side by side
- Each athlete starts in the center of their own box

<!-- DIAGRAM: p.190 shows two adjacent 4-cone boxes side by side. Each box has a 'Start' position marked at the center-front cone. Arrows show the commander sprinting from center to a random cone and back. -->

## Execution
1. One athlete is the **commander**, the other is the **mirror**.
2. Both start in the **center of their respective box**.
3. The commander sprints to **any given cone** in their box, touches it, and returns to the center.
4. The mirror athlete must **react and mirror** the commander's movement in their own box.
5. Continue for the designated time or number of reps, then switch roles. [S8, p.190]

## Coaching Points
- The mirror athlete must watch the commander's hips — the hips reveal direction before the feet do
- "First step explosive, return to center every time" — no drifting between cones
- The commander should vary cone selection unpredictably to prevent anticipation
- This drill mimics on-ball defensive positioning and reaction

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Commander moves slowly between cones; mirror focuses on reading direction cues
2. **Intermediate**: Game-speed cone sprints with immediate mirror response
3. **Advanced**: Add a basketball: commander catches a thrown ball at each cone, mirror must still react

## Concepts Taught
- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — stop-and-start reactive cone sprints train deceleration and re-acceleration
- [[rate-of-force-development-basketball]] — instantaneous reaction to a random stimulus demands maximal RFD
- [[defensive-transition-principles]] — on-ball defenders use the same hip-reading skills in game situations

# Sleeper Stretch

## Objective
Restore posterior shoulder flexibility and rotator cuff range of motion to support shooting and defending mechanics.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: none (floor or mat)

## Execution
1. Lie on the affected shoulder as if sleeping on your side.
2. Raise the lower arm to a 90-degree angle in front of you. Bend the elbow 90 degrees so the hand points toward the ceiling.
3. Using the upper hand, gently press the lower hand toward the floor until you feel a stretch on the posterior (back) aspect of the shoulder.
4. Hold this position for 10 seconds.
5. Perform 10 repetitions; repeat on each shoulder.

## Coaching Points
- "Gentle press" — this is a passive stretch; avoid forcing the hand down aggressively
- The stretch should be felt in the back of the shoulder, not the front
- Keep the body from rolling backward during the press; use core stability to maintain side-lying position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Light gentle press, 10-second hold × 10 reps
2. **Intermediate:** Hold up to 20 seconds per repetition for deeper stretch

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Rotator cuff (infraspinatus, supraspinatus, subscapularis, teres minor), posterior deltoid

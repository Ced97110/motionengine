# Dribble Closeout Drill (#149)

## Objective
Train defenders to close out on a breakaway dribbler and keep them under control using overplay and slide steps — applicable both to on-ball defense and help-side rotation.

## Setup
- Half court or full court
- 1 offensive player dribbling, 1 defender closing out
- Multiple pairs can go simultaneously

## Execution
1. Offensive player dribbles under control (beginner) or at speed dribble (intermediate).
2. Defender closes out: **slide-steps a few steps toward the dribbler**, overplaying **half their body** to force the dribbler to change direction via crossover or spin move.
3. When the dribbler changes direction: defender uses a **swing step** and resumes slide steps into a new overplay position — always keeping the dribbler in front. [S15, p.311]
4. Attacker dribbles a few steps before starting the drill again (beginner); or continues live (advanced).

## Coaching Points
- The defender overplays **half their body** — not head-on. This forces the dribbler to use a crossover or spin (both slower than a direct drive) toward the pivot foot.
- After the swing step to catch up, immediately re-establish the **overplay** on the new side.
- This defense applies to: (1) the defender's own assignment, AND (2) a help-rotation situation where the defender closes out a teammate's breakaway dribbler. [S15, p.311]
- "Always keeping the dribbler in front of her." [S15, p.311]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Offense dribbles under control. Defender picks up and keeps under control. Drill restarts after a few steps.
2. **Intermediate**: Offensive player uses a **speed dribble**. Defender must still close out and control.
3. **Advanced**: Start the drill at half court; defender is near the free-throw line. When the dribbler is closed out, the two players play **1-on-1 at the basket** — live until a score or stop. [S15, p.310]

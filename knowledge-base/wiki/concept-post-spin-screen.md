# Post Spin Screen (Lane Exchange Back Screen)

## Summary
The spin screen is a two-post offensive action designed to free a primary post scorer (05) via a blindside back screen set by the weakside post player (02) after a ball reversal. When 05 initiates a duck-in cut and is jammed by X5, 02 crosses the lane and blindsides X5 with a small-on-big back screen. The result is either an open post catch for 05 or a size-mismatch switch forcing X2 to guard 05 — both are high-probability outcomes [S6, pp.181-182].

The play is triggered by a perimeter ball reversal (e.g., 01 → 03 at the top of the key). With the ball centered, there is no true helpside or ballside defense, isolating both post defenders from each other and from perimeter help.

## When to Use
- After a ball reversal to the top of the key when the defense denies the ballside post
- When the primary post player (05) is being fronted or three-quartered and cannot receive the ball directly
- When a size mismatch can be exploited by forcing the defense to switch a small-on-big screen
- When the offensive team wants to attack with two simultaneous post threats

## Key Principles
1. **02 starts lower than 05** — 02's lower vertical position places him out of X5's line of sight, creating an almost indefensible blindside angle [S6, p.182].
2. **Duck-in first** — 05 must genuinely initiate the duck-in cut to freeze X5 and draw his full defensive attention before the spin screen arrives.
3. **Reverse pivot off the screen** — When X5 jams 05's duck-in cut, 05 immediately reverse pivots and spins a complete 180° off the baseline (low) shoulder of 02's screen, cutting to the opposite side of the lane [S6, p.182].
4. **Contact foot into the defender** — 05 places his contact foot (the foot closest to his defender) between the defender's two feet and uses it as the pivot foot; the free foot swings around to seal [S6, p.183].
5. **Free elbow high and locked** — As the reverse pivot is made, the elbow on the free-foot side is held high and locked (like a defensive box-out) to hold off the defender [S6, p.183].
6. **Sit on the defender's leg** — After sealing, 05 should "sit down" on the defender's leg to maintain position and not allow being pushed below the new ballside block [S6, p.183].
7. **Target: notch above the block** — 05's ideal post-up location after the spin is the notch above the block on the new ballside for maximum scoring angles [S6, p.183].
8. **Screener (02) is also a scoring threat** — If the defense switches, 02 is positioned on the inside half of X5 in the middle of the lane and should seal his new defender to become the primary scoring option [S6, p.182].

## Player Responsibilities
- **PF (05 — primary cutter)**: Initiate a genuine duck-in cut; if jammed, reverse pivot off 02's baseline shoulder; place contact foot between defender's feet as pivot; use free elbow to seal; post up at notch above new ballside block.
- **C (02 — spin screener)**: Start lower than 05 on weakside; time the cross-lane cut to arrive as X5 commits to jamming 05; set a blindside back screen on X5's baseline side; if defense switches, execute a reverse pivot (or front crossover pivot / swim move) to seal X5 in the middle of the lane.
- **PG (03 — ball reversal)**: Reverse the ball to trigger the action; read which post player is more open after the screen and deliver the pass.

## Footwork Detail — Cutter (05)
1. **Step 1 (left foot)**: Inside contact foot initiates the duck-in cut toward the center of the lane.
2. **Step 2 (right foot — if not denied)**: Normal continuation step.
3. **When denied — Step 2 (right foot — reverse pivot)**: 05 places the right foot next to the defender's feet and pivots 180° off the baseline shoulder of 02's screen, swinging the left free foot around to seal off the defender. [S6, pp.182-183]

## Footwork Detail — Screener (02) Counter When Switched
**Option A — Reverse Pivot Seal**: 02 starts with back toward basket on the right side; reverse pivots off the top (left) foot, swings the right foot around to box-out X5 and seal in the middle of the lane [S6, pp.184-185].

**Option B — Front Crossover Pivot / Swim Move**: 02 sets the spin screen, then makes a front crossover pivot off the top (left) foot and uses the swim technique with the contact (right) arm to seal X5 in the same high-percentage scoring location [S6, p.186].

## Why the Switch Backfires
- **X2 guarding 05**: Likely a significant height/strength mismatch; no helpside or ballside defense exists with ball centered at top [S6, p.182].
- **X5 guarding 02**: 02 is positioned in the middle of the lane — a high-percentage scoring area — and may be a quicker offensive player, negating X5's size advantage [S6, p.185].
- **Unpredictability**: The primary scoring threat can vary each possession — 05, 02, or whoever is more open — making the action extremely difficult to scout [S6, p.184].

## Common Mistakes
1. **02 starts too high** → X5 sees the screen coming; correction: 02 must start below 05's level, out of X5's line of sight.
2. **05 does not commit to the duck-in** → X5 is not committed to the denial; the screen has no value; correction: 05 must sell the duck-in cut aggressively before reversing.
3. **05 uses the wrong foot as pivot** → Contact foot is determined by which side the defender is on; use the foot closest to the defender as the pivot foot.
4. **05 settles below the new block** → Losing the battle of the feet; "Sit down on the defender's leg" to hold position at the notch above the block.
5. **02 does not act after the switch** → Ball handler has no secondary target; correction: 02 must immediately seal his new defender after the screen.

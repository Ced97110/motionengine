# SLOB X

## Overview
A sideline out-of-bounds play from a box formation that deploys two sequential waves of staggered screens to free two different shooters — first on one wing, then the other — for open mid-range or three-point shots. [S4, pp.99-100]

## Formation
Box formation with one shooter (2) on the ball-side elbow and the other shooter (3) as the inbounder on the sideline. 1 starts at the weak-side elbow area. 4 is at the weak-side low block. 5 is at the ball-side low block. [S4, p.99]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Entry — Free the Ball Handler at the Top
- **2** sets a **diagonal down-screen** for **1**, who pops to the top of the key to receive the inbound pass from **3**.
- **3** (the inbounder) inbounds to **1** and then immediately steps onto the court. [S4, p.99]

### Phase 2: First Shooter — 2 off Staggered Screens to the Wing
- **2** cuts off a **staggered screen** set by **5** and **3**, looking to receive the basketball on the wing for an open shot.
- **1** looks to hit **2** first — this is the primary read and usually the best look. [S4, pp.99-100]
- Simultaneously, **4** begins walking down to the weak-side low block to set up the next action.

### Phase 3: Second Shooter — 3 off Staggered Screens to the Opposite Wing
- **5** turns around after screening for 2 and sets another screen on **3's defender**.
- **3 sprints the baseline**, cutting off sequential screens from **5** and **4**, and pops out to the wing to receive the pass from **1** for the shot.
- **1** delivers the pass to the inside shoulder of the shooter so they can curl into the shot. [S4, pp.99-100]

## Key Coaching Points
- **Practice shooting off screens**: players must be able to get their feet set and shoot on-balance coming off staggered screens. [S4, p.100]
- **Primary read is 2** off the first staggered screen — always make this pass if it's open. Don't bypass it to go to the secondary action. [S4, p.100]
- **Pass to the inside shoulder**: 1 should aim for the shooter's inside shoulder so they can curl naturally into the shot rather than fading away. [S4, p.100]
- Screens must be set with proper timing — screeners should be stationary before the cutter makes their move.

## Key Personnel
- **2 (SG)**: Must be one of the team's best three-point shooters; primary shot option off first staggered screen.
- **3 (SF)**: Must be one of the team's best three-point shooters; secondary shot option; starts as the inbounder.
- **1 (PG)**: The team's point guard or best playmaker; receives entry pass and delivers the kick-out to either shooter.
- **4 (PF)**: Sets the second screen in the staggered sequence for 3 along the baseline.
- **5 (C)**: Sets the first screen for 2 off the staggered action, then pivots to set the first screen for 3.

## Counters
- If the defense fights over both screens and neither shooter is open, **1** can attack downhill off a ball screen from **5** (who is already in position after the sequential screening actions).
- If defenders switch, the screeners (4 and 5) may have a size mismatch advantage posting up smaller defenders.

# Flyspring / Bounder (Front Flip Chain — Step 7)

## Objective
Master the symmetrical, bilateral version of the front handspring where both feet block simultaneously and both feet land simultaneously — developing maximum total-body explosive power as the immediate predecessor to the true front flip.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Clear runway (20–30 feet)
- Soft/padded surface initially for back-drop flyspring regression

## Execution
1. Take a run-up to build momentum.
2. Perform a **block jump with BOTH feet together**, simultaneously throwing hands down.
3. Kick up hard through both legs equally; torso falls as hands drop to the floor.
4. Land on palms (arms nearly straight) as feet leave the floor — bilateral palm-catch.
5. Let momentum carry legs above the torso.
6. Allow forward momentum to turn you over. **Extend through arms and hands for extra height.**
7. Land on **both feet symmetrically.** Step forward to dissipate excess momentum if needed.

## Coaching Points
- **Two-foot block is the key difference** — Unlike the front handspring (Step 6) which blocks off one foot, the flyspring uses both feet equally; "this requires much more total body power" [S9, p.18]
- **Symmetrical landing** — Both feet must hit the ground at the same time and the same distance from the hands; asymmetry indicates uneven leg power
- **Arms extend for height** — Same cue as Step 6; arm push creates extra height and rotation
- **Also called a "bounder"** — Multiple names in gymnastics/calisthenics communities [S9, p.18]
- **Regression**: If the jump from Step 6 to Step 7 is too large, perform a **back-drop flyspring** — two-foot block setup but finish landing on feet/glutes (back-drop position of Step 5) with padding
- **Progression**: Gradually reduce run-up steps; the perfect flyspring can be performed from standing — though "this requires huge power and is not necessary to approach the front flip" [S9, p.19]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Back-drop flyspring on thick padding — two-foot block, back-drop landing
2. **Intermediate**: Full flyspring with run-up; land with forward step to dissipate momentum
3. **Advanced**: Reduce run-up to 2–3 steps; land without adjustment steps; increase height and extension of landing position

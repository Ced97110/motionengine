# Bank Shot Drills

## Objective
Develop reliable bank shot mechanics and touch from the 45-degree angle at distances from close range out to 15 feet (4.5 m), using both one-handed form, two-handed form, spinouts, and pull-up off the dribble.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball, 1 basket
- Half-court area — primarily the 45-degree wing zones on both sides of the basket (landmark: just above the low-post block, extended to 15 feet)
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution

### Drill 1: Bank Shot Form Shooting
1. Start at midcourt, tight to the sideline.
2. Dribble down the sideline until you reach the 45-degree bank shot angle.
3. Shoot one-handed form shots from that angle.
4. Make 5 shots, then move out half a step.
5. Continue stepping out in half-step increments until you reach 15 feet (4.5 m) or form breaks down.
6. Repeat the entire sequence with two-handed form.
7. Perform on both sides of the basket.

This is the ideal warm-up before practicing bank shots at game speed. [S5, p.87]

### Drill 2: Close-to-Midrange Spinouts
1. Begin close to the basket at the 45-degree angle.
2. Perform spinouts and catch in a one-two-step catch-and-shoot motion.
3. Shoot the bank shot using proper target (top corner of the backboard square).
4. Work both sides of the basket.
5. Progress outward to 15 feet (4.5 m).
6. Goal: 10 makes from each spot, or shoot "strings" style (set a number of consecutive makes required). [S5, p.87]

### Drill 3: Under-the-Basket Spinouts (Turnaround Bank Shot)
1. Start on the low block.
2. Perform a spinout toward the wing at a 45-degree angle. Take short choppy steps, then a longer stride with the opposite foot (left foot for a right-handed shooter).
3. Make an inside pivot (shooting foot opens to the middle of the floor) to square to the basket and shoot the turnaround bank shot. Catch the ball low off the spinout — shoot from low to high.
4. Make 10 using left foot as pivot + inside pivot.
5. Make 10 using left foot as pivot + front pivot (turning toward the baseline).
6. Make 10 using right foot as pivot + inside pivot.
7. Make 10 using right foot as pivot + front pivot.
8. That is 40 makes from one side. Repeat from the opposite side.
9. Variation: From under the basket, spinout and jump stop with your back to the basket. Practice all four pivots: outside right, outside left, inside right, inside left. [S5, pp.87-88]

### Drill 4: Pull-Up Bank Shot
1. Begin at midcourt, tight to the sideline, with a live dribble.
2. Speed-dribble down the sideline to the 45-degree angle.
3. Attack the lane until 5–10 feet from the basket, then pull up and shoot the bank shot.
4. Keep the dribble out in front of the body (not to the side) so you can use a clean one-two step into the shot.
5. Goal: 10 makes with your strong hand.
6. Repeat using the weak hand, loading the gun on the pull-up.
7. Repeat on the opposite sideline.
8. Can also incorporate a shot-fake-and-go move or jab step before the drive. [S5, pp.89-90]

## Coaching Points
- Target the **top corner of the backboard square closest to the shooter** on every rep — reinforce this on every make and miss
- "Don't look at the ball — eyes on the square."
- The shot fake should look exactly like a real shot — same speed, same lift
- Catch the ball **low** on spinouts so you shoot from low to high
- Use the low-post block as the court landmark for the 45-degree angle
- "Don't shoot higher or harder — same mechanics, same touch as your jump shot"
- Hesitation kills bank shots; the ball comes up short — commit and shoot

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Form shooting one-handed, both sides, close range only
2. **Intermediate**: Spinouts with one-two step, both sides, out to 15 feet; add turnaround bank shot off one pivot foot
3. **Advanced**: Pull-up bank shot off the dribble with both hands; incorporate shot fakes and jab steps before driving; turnaround bank shot off all four pivot combinations from both sides of the floor

# Atlanta Hawks Zipper to Cross Screen

## Overview
Run by Lenny Wilkens in Atlanta (and used by other NBA coaches), this combination play begins with a 5-3 zipper screen while the PG dribbles to the wing. It evolves quickly into a back-screen lob for the 4, then a cross-screen post feed for Dikembe Mutombo (5). The play is designed to move so quickly that it becomes a pick-the-picker action, stressing the defense with two big men both needing to be guarded simultaneously. [S14, pp.61]

## Formation
- **1 (PG)** on the right wing with the ball
- **2 (SG, Jim Jackson)** positioned to receive zipper
- **3 (SF)** at the top ready to receive
- **4 (PF, Alan Henderson)** positioned for the back screen/cut
- **5 (C, Dikembe Mutombo)** at the elbow (zipper screener)

## Phases

### Phase 1: Zipper Screen and Back Screen
- 1 dribbles the ball to the right-wing area
- 5 sets a zipper screen (up screen from elbow) for 3 bringing 3 to the top
- 1 passes to 3 at the top
- As 3 catches the ball, **2 sets a back screen for 4**
- 4 cuts toward the rim for a possible lob pass (Henderson lob)
- 2 pops out after setting the back screen — option for a jump shot (Jim Jackson)

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.9 — 5-3 zipper; 1 passes to 3; 2 sets back screen for 4 cutting to rim; 2 pops for shot. -->

### Phase 2: Cross Screen for 5 — Pick-the-Picker
- If the pass to 2 (after 2 pops) is made but no shot is available, the play continues:
- 4 continues his cut off the rim to set a **cross screen for 5**
- Simultaneously, 5 sets a down screen for 3 (pick-the-picker action)
- 5 then receives the cross screen from 4
- 2 reads:
  - **5 cutting to the post** (primary: Mutombo post feed)
  - **4 rolling back** if the switch on the big-big screen occurs and 4 can seal
- If NBA teams switch the big-on-big screen, 4 must attempt to seal and roll back to the ball

<!-- DIAGRAM: Fig. 6.10 — 4 cross-screens for 5; 5 sets down for 3 (pick-the-picker); 2 feeds 5 in post or 4 rolling. -->

## Key Coaching Points
- Speed is essential — the action must flow quickly to become a pick-the-picker before the defense sets
- The primary target is Mutombo (5) in the post — every other action is designed to stress defenders enough to free him
- When NBA teams switch the big-big cross screen, the screener (4) must immediately seal and seal back to the ball
- 2 must recognize immediately whether the switch occurred — the correct pass changes completely
- "The primary focus of this action is Dikembe Mutombo (5) in the post" [S14, p.61]

## Counters
- If X4 switches to 5 on the cross screen → 4 seals X5 who has switched to him and rolls back to ball
- If lob to 4 is available immediately off the back screen → take it before the cross screen develops

# Figure 8 Dribble (Drill #20)

## Objective
Develop fingertip dribbling control and bilateral coordination by dribbling a figure-8 pattern through and around both legs, progressing to full-court movement.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1 per ball (individual or full-team lines)
- **Court area:** Stationary position or full court
- **Equipment:** 1 basketball per player
- **Duration:** 2 minutes

## Execution

### Beginner (Single-Leg Circles)
1. Dribble the basketball very low (no higher than the middle of the lower legs) around the right leg using only the right hand — fingertips only.
2. Repeat, dribbling around the left leg with only the left hand.
3. Team format: players ~15 feet apart.

### Intermediate (Through-the-Legs Figure 8 Stationary)
1. Dribble in front of the right leg with the right hand.
2. Dribble through the legs — pick up with the left hand behind the left leg.
3. Dribble in front of the left leg, then through the legs to behind the right leg — right hand picks up.
4. Continue the figure-8 pattern for 5–15 seconds.
5. Reverse direction for another 5–15 seconds.

**Options (Intermediate):**
- Spread around half-court and dribble figure-8 in position.
- Add a "dance" motion — left foot forward when taking the ball through from right hand; right foot forward when taking from left hand — continue throughout.

### Advanced (Full-Court Race)
1. Players race down the floor while dribbling figure-8 through the legs — begin as a walk until coordination is achieved, then at maximum controlled speed.
2. Divide into teams for a dribbling race: guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Coaching Points
- Use only the **first pads of the fingers** when dribbling — not the palm
- Keep the ball **low** and dribble **rapidly**
- **Do not watch the dribble** — head up, eyes forward
- The racing/team competition format significantly increases intensity and motivation

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Single-leg circles with each hand separately
2. **Intermediate:** Through-the-legs figure-8 stationary with dance variation
3. **Advanced:** Full-court race maintaining technique — guards vs. post players adds competitive element

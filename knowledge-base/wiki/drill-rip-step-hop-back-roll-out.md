# Rip Step Hop Back / Roll Out

## Objective
Develop the hop-back counter with a roll-out dribble — teaching players to use the hop back to freeze or redirect the defender, then "roll out" (re-attack with a live dribble) to create a new scoring angle.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half court; wing, elbow, or top of key
- Ball only
- Choose volume level (40, 80, or 120 total shots)

## Execution
1. Start in triple threat on the wing or elbow.
2. Execute the **rip step** and take **1 dribble** (or **2 dribbles**) toward the defender.
3. Perform the **hop back**: gather and hop backward to create space (as in the Hop Back drill).
4. Read the defender's reaction:
   - If the defender stops or retreats → shoot the jump shot from the hop-back position
   - If the defender over-pursues → **roll out**: immediately push a live dribble to the side (L or R) to re-attack a new angle, driving baseline or toward the key
5. The roll-out shot or finish completes the rep.
6. Alternate **left (L)** and **right (R)** with free throws between each set.

**Set Order (Workout #2-16 as example):**
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Hop Back to Roll Out Dribble (L) × 5
- FTs × 5
- Rip Step 1 Dribble Hop Back to Roll Out Dribble (R) × 5
- FTs × 5
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Hop Back to Roll Out Dribble (L) × 5
- FTs × 5
- Rip Step 2 Dribble Hop Back to Roll Out Dribble (R) × 5
- FTs × 5

## Coaching Points
- The roll-out dribble must be to the outside hand (away from the defender) to protect the ball
- "Hop back to make them commit, roll out to punish them" — this is a two-read combination move
- After the roll out, be ready to shoot, drive, or pass immediately — don't waste the advantage
- The hop-back position can become the shot if the defender stops — don't always roll out automatically
- Practice rolling out in the direction the drill specifies (L = roll out left, R = roll out right) to build ambidexterity
- Track FGA, FGM, FTA, FTM in the workout log [S3]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #2-16, 40 shots)**: 5 reps per set — work footwork pattern slowly; always roll out for the first weeks
2. **Intermediate (Workout #2-17, 80 shots)**: 10 reps per set — add the decision component: shoot or roll out based on imagined defender
3. **Advanced (Workout #2-18, 120 shots)**: 20 reps per set — full volume; add a partner to provide live roll-out reads

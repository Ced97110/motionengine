# Incline Barbell Press (Basketball Strength Exercise)

## Objective
Develop upper-chest, anterior deltoid, and triceps strength using an incline angle that places greater emphasis on the upper pectoralis and shoulder than the flat bench press — directly transferring to chest-pass force production and maintaining rebounding position.

## Setup
- 2 players (1 lifter + 1 spotter — **mandatory**)
- Incline bench set to **45 degrees** (some athletes may prefer 60 degrees)
- Barbell with appropriate weight
- **Safety Tip**: Always have a spotter present

## Execution
1. Set the incline bench to 45 degrees. Sit back so that your back, shoulders, and head are all in contact with the bench.
2. Place both feet firmly on the floor. Grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder width with thumbs fully wrapped around the bar.
3. Retract the scapula to establish a stable pressing platform.
4. Lift the bar by extending the arms until the bar is at approximately eye level.
5. Slowly lower the barbell under control by bending the elbows. Maintain upper arms at a **45-degree angle** to the body as the bar touches the upper chest. **Do not bounce the bar off the chest.**
6. With a controlled exhalation, press the bar away from the upper chest, extending the arms back to eye level. Keep the lower back flat on the bench.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Bar touches upper chest" — not mid-chest as in flat bench; the angle shifts the target
- 45-degree upper-arm angle throughout — same rule as flat bench press
- Scapula retracted before pressing — shoulder protection is critical
- Lower back flat on the bench — avoid arching to generate extra force
- Standard incline is 45°; 60° increases anterior deltoid demand further
- "Do not bounce" — controlled touch and press

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master flat bench press form first; reduce weight when moving to incline
2. **Intermediate**: Barbell incline press at 45° with proper form
3. **Advanced (Incline Dumbbell Press)**: Use dumbbells to increase range of motion and address bilateral imbalances

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Pectoralis major, pectoralis minor, anterior deltoid
- **Secondary**: Lateral deltoid, triceps brachii

## Basketball Transfer
The incline angle targets the upper chest and places more emphasis on the anterior deltoid and triceps than the flat bench press does. This translates to greater force production in chest passes (arms extend from upper chest level) and improved ability to hold position under the boards against physical contact. [S2, pp.82-83]

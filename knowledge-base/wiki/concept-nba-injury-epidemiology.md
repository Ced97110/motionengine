# NBA Injury Epidemiology

## Summary
A comprehensive 17-year analysis of NBA injuries (Drakos et al.) identified the ten most significant causes of games missed and the most frequent injuries by incidence. Patellofemoral inflammation and lateral ankle sprain top both lists. The majority of high-incidence injuries have short recovery periods (days to weeks), but meniscal tears, foot fractures, lumbar disc degeneration, and ACL tears involve surgical intervention and longer recovery [S10, pp.149-151].

Since 2012, the NBA's centralized EMR system has substantially improved data quality, and future research will better characterize contemporary injury burdens.

## When to Use
- Prioritizing injury prevention programs (e.g., ankle stability, patellofemoral care)
- Counseling athletes on realistic return-to-play timelines
- Allocating medical resources pre-season
- Understanding which injuries require the most conservative management

## Key Principles

### Top 10 Causes of GAMES MISSED (% of total games missed)
1. Patellofemoral inflammation — 17.5%
2. Lateral ankle sprain — 8.8%
3. Knee sprain — 7.4%
4. Lumbar sprain/strain — 6.6%
5. Meniscal tear — 4.1%
6. Foot fracture — 3.7%
7. Lumbar disc degeneration — 3.6%
8. Triceps surae strain — 3.5%
9. Foot inflammation — 3.5%
10. Hamstring strain — 3.1% [S10, p.149]

> Note: Meniscal tears, foot fractures, and lumbar disc degeneration frequently require surgery and result in disproportionately longer absences.

> Note: ACL tears were not in this dataset but ACL reconstructions account for 18.7% of all surgical procedures in the NBA literature (Minhas et al.) [S10, p.149]

### Top 10 Most FREQUENT INJURIES (% of total injury occurrences)
1. Lateral ankle sprain — 13.2%
2. Patellofemoral inflammation — 11.9%
3. Lumbar sprain/strain — 7.9%
4. Hamstring strain — 3.3%
5. Adductor strain — 3.1%
6. Knee sprain — 3.1%
7. Foot inflammation — 2.9%
8. Quadriceps contusion — 2.7%
9. Knee/patella contusion — 2.5%
10. Finger sprain — 2.4% [S10, pp.149-150]

### Non-Musculoskeletal Injuries and Illnesses
- **Upper respiratory infection (URI)** — 16.7% of ALL reported injuries/illnesses; the single most common documented illness; responsible for 2.4% of games/practices missed [S10, p.150]
- **Concussion** — 4.3–14.9 concussions per season (0.6%–2.1% of all injuries); after 2011 NBA concussion protocol, average games missed per concussion rose from 1.6 to 5 [S10, p.150]
- **Gastrointestinal distress** — 3.5% of injuries/illnesses; only 0.5% of games missed; a 2010 norovirus outbreak affected 24 players/staff from 13 teams [S10, p.150-151]
- **Head/face lacerations** — 2.5% of all injuries; only 0.1% of games/practices missed [S10, p.151]
- **Other** — dehydration, eye injuries, vitamin D deficiency, high rates of inadequate immunity to vaccine-preventable illnesses [S10, p.151]

## Common Mistakes
1. Over-emphasizing ACL prevention while neglecting patellofemoral and ankle programs → data shows patellofemoral and ankle injuries cause far more total missed games
2. Treating URI as minor → URIs are the single most common documented illness, accounting for nearly as many missed games as hamstring strains
3. Underestimating vitamin D deficiency → documented problem in NBA athletes with fracture risk and performance implications

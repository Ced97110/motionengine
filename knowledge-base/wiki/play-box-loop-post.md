# Box Loop Post

## Overview
A simple SLOB (sideline out of bounds) play from a box formation designed to get the team's best post player isolated at the high post in a 1-on-1 situation. Sequential down screens clear defenders and create the isolation. [S4, pp.81-82]

## Formation
Box set: 1 and 2 at the high-post elbows, 4 and 5 at the low blocks. 3 inbounds from the sideline. The post player targeted for the high-post isolation (4) sets up on the **weak-side high post**. [S4, p.81]

## Phases

### Phase 1: First Down Screen — Get the Ball In
- 5 sets a down screen for 1, who cuts to the top of the key.
- 1 receives the inbound pass from 3.
- After screening, 5 stays below the block.
- 3 steps onto the court after making the inbound pass. [S4, p.81]

### Phase 2: Second Down Screen — Clear the Helper
- A second or two after 5's down screen, 4 sets a down screen for 2.
- 2 curls off the down screen and clears through the key to the **weak-side corner**.
- This removes 2's defender from any help position near the high post. [S4, p.81]

### Phase 3: High-Post Isolation
- After screening, 4 **seals their defender** in the high post and holds position.
- 1 takes a couple of dribbles across the top to improve the passing angle.
- 1 passes to 4 at the high post.
- 4 is now in a 1-on-1 situation where defensive help is difficult. [S4, p.81]

## Key Coaching Points
- "The timing of the second screen is crucial. 4 must not set it too early." [S4, p.82]
- 4 must seal their defender immediately after the screen — don't drift, establish contact and hold position.
- 1 must read the passing angle and take those extra dribbles before throwing; a straight pass is easier to intercept.
- 5 staying below the block keeps the weak-side lane clear for 4's 1-on-1 moves. [S4, p.82]

## Counters
- If 2's defender cheats under the screen (anticipating the play being used repeatedly), 2 can **flare to the wing** instead of clearing corner and receive the pass from 1 for an open shot. [S4, p.82]

# Make 'Em to Move

## Objective
Develop three-point shooting accuracy across all five spots by requiring every player on the team to make one shot before the team advances to the next spot — building individual accountability within a team competition [S13, p.125].

## Setup
- **Players**: Full squad divided into 3 equal teams
- **Court area**: Half court
- **Equipment**: One ball per team
- **Five key spots**: Both corners, both wings, and the top of the key

## Execution
1. Each team selects one of the five three-point shooting spots to begin
2. **Individual accountability**: Every player on the team must make one three-point shot from the current spot before the team can rotate
3. One ball is used per team — making the shot, following the shot for the rebound, and delivering a quality pass to the next shooter are all equally important
4. Players are NOT allowed to touch the opponent's basketball
5. When all players on a team have made their shot at the current spot, the team immediately rotates to an open spot and begins again
6. **First team to complete all five spots wins**

## Coaching Points
- Unlike the standard competitive three-point drill, every individual must make their shot — no one can hide behind teammates who shoot well
- Identify players who stall their team at a spot; they need targeted three-point shooting work outside of practice
- Emphasize the full sequence: shoot → follow the rebound → make a good pass → next shooter gets ready quickly
- Teams must not touch the opponent's ball — this rule maintains court separation and teaches focus
- Consider rotating which spot each team starts at across practices so no team develops a "home spot"
- Add a time limit per spot for more advanced groups to increase pressure

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Each player must make one three-pointer; no time limit; coach can allow mid-range misses to count from wing spots
2. **Intermediate**: Standard rules as above; team must also complete a lay-in drill before beginning each spot (adds movement)
3. **Advanced**: Time limit per spot (e.g., 2 minutes); if any player misses twice in a row, the team must run a sprint before continuing

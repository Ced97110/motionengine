# 15-Yard Turn Drill

## Objective
Improve ability to change direction, hip flexibility, and footwork through sharp cuts around two cones arranged in a triangular pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 3 cones: start cone at bottom-left, cone 1 five yards directly forward, cone 2 five yards to the right and diagonally forward from cone 1 (forming a right triangle with a 5-yard base)
- Total distance: ~15 yards

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Sprint forward 5 yards (4.6 m) to cone 1 and make a sharp right turn around it.
3. Sprint diagonally to cone 2 (approximately 7 yards), located 5 yards to the right of the start and diagonal from cone 1, and make a sharp left turn around it.
4. Sprint approximately 5 yards (4.6 m) back through the finish.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Triangle layout — start at bottom, sprint to top-left cone (right turn), sprint diagonally to bottom-right cone (left turn), sprint back to finish. [S8, p.89] -->

## Coaching Points
- Plant on the inside foot to make sharp turns (not the outside foot)
- Drop the hips and low-step through each turn — do not run around the outside of the cone
- Head swings around to find the new visual focus point to initiate each turn
- Explosive arm drive out of each turn

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk-through with exaggerated inside-foot plant
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed, timed
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Put the inside hand on the ground when making turns [S8, p.89]
   - Change the distance to the cones
   - Make turns on coach's command rather than at the cone

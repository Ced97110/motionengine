# In-Out Shuffle Agility Ladder Drill

## Objective
Improve agility, balance, coordination, and quickness through a lateral in-out stepping pattern with both feet visiting every ladder square.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder laid flat
- Player starts in two-point stance, standing sideways to the ladder with the ladder directly in front

## Execution
1. Start in two-point stance, sideways to the ladder.
2. Step **left foot** straight ahead into the first square.
3. Follow with **right foot** into the first square (both feet in).
4. Step **left foot** back and diagonally until it is in front of the second square.
5. Follow with **right foot** until it is in front of the second square (both feet out, advanced).
6. Repeat: step both feet into the next square, then back out diagonally in front of the following square.
7. Each foot hits every box throughout the ladder.

## Coaching Points
- Maintain two-point stance — do not stand up straight between steps
- Look up; do not watch feet [S8, p.108]
- Quick touches — minimize time inside and outside the ladder
- "In-in, out-out" rhythm — verbalize the pattern as players learn it

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Complex Variation 1)**: Perform the same pattern with each foot in a separate box (wider lateral step).
2. **Intermediate**: Use every other box to increase lateral step length.
3. **Advanced**: Perform drill **backward** (start with ladder behind you).

- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — rapid multi-directional foot placement trains ankle stability

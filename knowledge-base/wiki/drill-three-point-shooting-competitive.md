# Three-Point Shooting (Competitive Team Drill)

## Objective
Improve three-point shooting accuracy and shot selection from all five key spots through competitive team pressure and continuous repetition [S13, p.124].

## Setup
- **Players**: Full squad divided into 3 groups of 4-5 players
- **Court area**: Half court (two teams) + opposite half (warm-up team)
- **Equipment**: One ball per team
- **Five key spots**: Both corners, both wings, top of the key

## Execution
1. Two teams begin on one half of the court; the third team warms up on the other half
2. Each team selects one of the five three-point shooting spots
3. **Goal**: First team to make 5 (or 10) shots at their spot wins
4. One ball per team — players shoot in order; after a make or miss, the next player in line shoots
5. Teams call out their makes aloud as they score
6. Emphasis is on: making the shot, rebounding quickly, and making a good pass back to the next shooter
7. **Winner stays** on the court; the team that loses rotates out and the third team rotates in from the warm-up half
8. Teams may change spots between rounds to practice all five locations

## Coaching Points
- One ball per team creates pressure — the rebound and pass back are just as important as the shot itself
- Emphasize game-speed shooting mechanics: players should not get extra time to set up
- Rotate which spots each team practices — all five spots must be mastered, not just the favorite wing
- Use this drill to identify which players can execute under competitive pressure vs. who struggles
- Call out makes loudly — the competitive atmosphere mirrors late-game pressure

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Goal of 3 makes; stationary catch-and-shoot from a designated spot only
2. **Intermediate**: Goal of 5 makes; catch from various angles within the spot
3. **Advanced**: Goal of 10 makes; shooter must V-cut to receive the pass before shooting; shot clock added

# Straight Hop (Power Jump Chain Step 1)

## Objective
Build elastic rebound in the ankles and lower legs, and teach proper take-off and landing patterns as the foundation for all progressive jump training.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any small flat surface (no equipment needed)
- Shoulder-width stance starting position

## Execution
1. Stand with feet approximately shoulder-width apart, body flexed and ready for action
2. Dip down slightly — less than a quarter-squat; minimal knee bend
3. Without pausing, explode upward using lower leg power and total-body spring
4. "Pull" up with the shoulders; keep arms reasonably neutral (not swinging)
5. Land softly, catching most weight on the balls of the feet
6. Immediately use the elastic rebound to repeat — do NOT pause between hops
7. Perform rhythmically for target reps or time

## Coaching Points
- **Minimal knee bend** — This is an ankle-dominant drill; if knees bend too much, the athlete is performing a squat jump (Step 2)
- **Balls of feet landing** — "Catch on the balls of your feet" — heels should not slam the floor
- **No pause** — The elastic rebound is the training stimulus; pausing kills it
- **Body coiled** — The body should feel "coiled like a spring," not loose
- Watch for: athletes who land stiff-legged or on their heels — correct immediately
- Watch for: athletes who pause and rest between reps — cue rhythmic continuous hopping

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hop with minimal intensity; keep body "loose" rather than maximally coiled to reduce difficulty
2. **Intermediate**: Add arm swing — swing arms up with each hop, adding total-body momentum
3. **Advanced**: Bend slightly more at the knee (approaching but not reaching squat jump depth) to transition toward Step 2

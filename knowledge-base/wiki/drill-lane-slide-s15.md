# Lane Slide

## Objective
Develop proper defensive slide-step technique, lateral quickness, conditioning, and change of direction — measured by number of lane-line touches in a timed set.

## Setup
- **Players**: 3 per group (run multiple groups simultaneously)
- **Court area**: Foul lane (the paint), players line up at the foul lane with each player about 3 feet behind the one in front
- **Equipment**: None
- **Duration**: 30–60 seconds per group (30 seconds early season, extend to 60 seconds by mid-season)
- All players face out of bounds; start in the middle or on one lane line

## Execution

**Beginner:**
1. Players slide from side to side using a proper defensive slide step.
2. Touch the foul-lane line with the outside foot before sliding back in the other direction.
3. Count the number of touches per 30 seconds — each player should get more touches each day the drill is run. The goal is quicker slides = more touches.

**Intermediate:**
- As the group slides, each player executes an in-and-out defensive fake with each step taken (step in with front foot and strike hand forward like a snake, then step back).

**Advanced:**
1. Reduce sliding distance to only two steps in either direction from the center.
2. Further reduce to only one step in either direction from the center — maximum quickness and foot control at very short distance.

**Option (all levels):** Two players face each other. The first player slides in either direction (player's choice), while the second player mirrors every movement.

## Coaching Points
- **Do not cross the legs** — this is the single most important technical rule of the slide step [S15, p.11]
- Begin with a long step in the direction of travel; trail foot comes up to the first foot — almost touching but never crossing
- "Stay low" — lower legs perpendicular to the floor, upper legs at 135-degree angle to lower legs, torso straight and perpendicular to the floor
- Hands out, palms pointing upward — arms must not drop
- Toes grab at the floor; feet rise only minimally from the floor to promote quickness
- Track touches per session as objective quickness improvement data

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Full lane width, 30 seconds, count touches
2. **Intermediate**: Add in-and-out defensive fake with every step
3. **Advanced**: Reduce distance to 2 steps then 1 step each direction — maximum quickness in a small window

# Shot Fake Pull-Up Three-Point Workout

## Objective
Develop the shot-fake pull-up three-pointer — using a shot fake to freeze a closing defender, then rising into a three-point pull-up shot — through high-volume, self-documented progressive workouts.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Beyond the three-point arc — multiple spots (wings, corners, top of key)
- Three volume tiers: 25 shots (Workout #3-22), 50 shots (Workout #3-23), 100 shots (Workout #3-24)
- Shot chart log recommended for tracking

## Execution

### The Core Move
1. Start with the ball in triple-threat position beyond the arc.
2. Execute a **shot fake** — ball rises to shooting pocket, eyes at the rim, feet remain grounded.
3. The defender (or imagined defender) rises to contest.
4. As the defender's momentum carries him upward or forward, **pull up immediately** into the three-point shot.
5. Land balanced. Freeze the follow-through.

### Workout #3-22 (25 Total 3PT Shots)
- SF pull-up 3s: 5 sets × 5 shots = 25 total 3PT shots
- Free Throws: 5 between each set = 25 total FTs
- **Targets:** 3PT 15-25 (60%), FT 21-25 (85%)

### Workout #3-23 (50 Total 3PT Shots)
- SF pull-up 3s: 5 sets × 10 shots = 50 total 3PT shots
- Free Throws: 10 between each set = 50 total FTs
- **Targets:** 3PT 15-25 (60%), FT 21-25 (85%)

### Workout #3-24 (100 Total 3PT Shots)
- SF pull-up 3s: 10 sets × 10 shots = 100 total 3PT shots
- Free Throws: 10 between each set = 100 total FTs
- **Targets:** 3PT 30-50 (60%), FT 43-50 (85%) [Note: Workout #3-24 total 200 shots including FTs, targets 60-100 3PT/85-100 FTs]

## Coaching Points
- **The fake must draw a reaction** — practice with a manager or coach who will actually bite on the fake so the player learns to read the over-committed defender.
- **Don't dip the ball** — after the fake, rise directly into the shot without dropping the ball. Dipping resets the defender.
- **Feet must be set before rising** — if the player drifts on the pull-up, the shot is long. Land in the shot pocket, then rise.
- **Free throws are about maintenance under fatigue** — take the full routine on every FT attempt.
- **Log every session** — track 3PTA, 3PTM, FTA, FTM. Progress toward 60% from three is a realistic standard; below 40% signals a mechanical issue.
- Coaching cue: *"Fake → freeze → rise. Three moves, one breath."*

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #3-22, 25 shots):** 5 shots per set from one or two preferred spots; focus on shot-fake quality and not dipping.
2. **Intermediate (Workout #3-23, 50 shots):** 10 shots per set, work multiple spots along the arc; add a passive defender who contests on the rise.
3. **Advanced (Workout #3-24, 100 shots):** 10 sets of 10 at game speed; use a live defender who randomly chooses to contest or not — player reads and either fakes or shoots straight.

# Posterior Capsule Stretch

## Objective
Restore flexibility in the posterior shoulder to increase range of motion during shooting and defending.

## Setup
- Individual exercise; no equipment needed (optional: a pole or stationary object for the advanced variation)
- Can be done anywhere — court, locker room, weight room

## Execution
1. Stand erect and pull one elbow across your body until you feel a stretch in the back of the shoulder.
2. Hold this position for 30 seconds.
3. Repeat 3 to 5 times per day on each shoulder.
4. **Advanced variation:** Hold on to a pole or other stationary object and lean toward your arm to deepen the stretch.

## Coaching Points
- Keep the stretching arm parallel to the floor — do not let the elbow drop
- Feel the stretch specifically in the posterior (back) of the shoulder, not the neck
- Breathe steadily; do not hold your breath
- Progress to the pole-assisted version only after the basic version feels comfortable

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Standing cross-body pull, 30-second hold × 3–5 reps
2. **Advanced:** Pole-assisted lean to increase stretch depth

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Rotator cuff (infraspinatus, supraspinatus, subscapularis, teres minor), posterior deltoid
- **Secondary:** Serratus anterior, latissimus dorsi

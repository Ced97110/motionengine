# Back Flip (Master Step)

## Objective
Execute a complete backward airborne rotation landing on both feet with no hand contact — the master step demonstrating integrated explosive strength: fast jump, hip snap, powerful agile waist/spine, and reactive upper-body force generation.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat surface; soft surface (grass, mat) recommended until landing is reliable
- No equipment required
- **Prerequisite**: four-point back flip landing on feet before hands (Step 9) [S9, p.16]

## Execution
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hands held high.
2. Dip down at knees and hips, swinging arms down by your sides. **Keep looking forward** as you descend.
3. **Jump UP hard** — swinging hands above you. You can look up as you jump. **Critical cue: Do NOT jump backward. Jump vertically!** Too many athletes fail here [S9, p.16].
4. When your body is fully extended at maximum height, **explode your knees up into your chest as hard as you can** — this is the *tuck*. The momentum of the tuck creates the rotation.
5. Bring arms to your sides or your thighs as you spin over.
6. When you begin to see the ground below you, **whip your legs out straight** to make contact with the floor.
7. When feet are planted, catch your balance and extend your body again.

## Coaching Points
- "The back flip displays integrated mastery of some of the most fundamental traits required for total explosive strength: a super quick jump, massive hip snap, a powerful, agile waist and spine, and an upper-body that can generate high-levels of responsive force like lightning." [S9, p.16]
- **Jump vertical, not backward** — this is the single most common error
- Tuck explosiveness is everything: a lazy tuck = insufficient rotation
- Arms pulling to thighs (not flailing) helps speed the tuck
- "Everyone can get this far, if they put the time and effort into the previous nine steps" [S9, p.16]
- Practice on soft surfaces until landing is consistent and balanced

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Four-point back flip (Step 9) with feet landing before hands — essentially a back flip with safety hands
2. **Intermediate**: Full back flip; may land in a deep squat
3. **Advanced**: Tight tuck, feet together, land in an upright extended stance

## Variations
- **Flashkick**: One leg extended, one tucked during rotation [S9, p.19]
- **Gainer**: Land forward of take-off point [S9, p.19]
- **Loser**: Land behind take-off point [S9, p.19]
- **Switchflip**: Flip off one leg, land on opposite leg [S9, p.19]
- **Partner/Pitch Tuck**: Partner holds foot to assist — self-replicated by placing foot on knee-height object [S9, p.18]
- **Wall flip**: One foot on a low wall assists the rotation — a basic parkour move [S9, p.18]

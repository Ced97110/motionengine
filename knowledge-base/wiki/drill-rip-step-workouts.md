# Rip Step Workout Series (Workouts #2-1 to #2-5)

## Objective
Develop proficiency and confidence in all rip step live-ball scoring variations — stride stop, step-through, big hop, hop back, hop back to roll out, and hop back to crossover — through structured, high-volume, game-speed individual workouts with built-in free throws and progress tracking.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (individual workout; can be done solo)
- **Court area**: Half court — player works from multiple spots including mid-range and three-point range
- **Equipment**: Basketball, optional rebounder/partner, shot chart log
- **Tracking**: Each workout has a progress chart tracking Date, FGA, FGM, %, 3PTA, 3PTM, %, FTA, FTM, %

## Execution

## Coaching Points
- **Game speed non-negotiable**: Every rip must be executed at full intensity — slow reps build slow habits. [S3, p.9]
- **Both directions every time**: Always work left AND right in every session. The off-hand/off-direction is where games are won.
- **True pivot foot establishment**: Watch for players who forget to step with the rip; this creates travel risk and breaks the footwork chain.
- **Realistic rip fake**: The ball must move convincingly across the body — a limp rip does not move a defender.
- **FT mental discipline**: Free throws between physical sets simulate game free-throw pressure; do not rush them.
- **Track everything**: Record FGA, FGM, 3PTA, 3PTM, FTA, FTM after every workout and compare against target percentages.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Workout #2-3, #2-4)**: Start with attack lay-ups and 1-dribble stride stops only — fewer variables, closer range, higher success rate builds confidence.
2. **Intermediate (Workout #2-1)**: Full 130-shot sequence covering all rip step variations at 5 reps each with game-speed threes.
3. **Advanced (Workout #2-2)**: Double-volume 260+ shot sequence at 10 reps per set; add a live defender on select sets.

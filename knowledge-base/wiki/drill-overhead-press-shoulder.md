# Overhead Press (Shoulder Strengthening)

## Objective
Strengthen the rotator cuff and deltoid muscles to increase general shoulder strength, leading to improvement in shooting, defending, and blocking.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: barbell or dumbbells
- Standing position; enough overhead clearance

## Execution
1. Stand holding a barbell or dumbbells with elbows bent at approximately 30-degree angles.
2. Hold weights at shoulder level.
3. Lift the weights by raising the arms overhead to a fully extended position. The path of the weights should go directly overhead, concluding with both arms in line with the ears.
4. Do not arch the back or look up during the press.
5. Slowly lower weights to the starting position.
6. Perform 3 sets of 10 repetitions.
7. **Advanced:** Increase the weight progressively.

## Coaching Points
- "Arms finish in line with the ears" — full overhead extension without excessive forward lean
- Do NOT arch the lower back — brace the core throughout
- Keep the movement smooth and controlled on both the press and the return
- Do not look up during the press — neutral neck position

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Light dumbbells, emphasize technique
2. **Intermediate:** Increase weight, maintain strict form
3. **Advanced:** Progressively load; may use barbell

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Rotator cuff (supraspinatus)
- **Secondary:** Anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid, middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids, rectus abdominis

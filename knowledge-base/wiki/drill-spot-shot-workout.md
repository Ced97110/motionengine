# Spot Shot Workout (Individual Full-Speed)

## Objective
Build catch-and-shoot consistency, conditioning, and game-speed shot-preparation habits through solo full-court spot shooting with free throws interspersed between sets.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Full court (minimum half court)
- No shots inside 5 feet are allowed
- Three-point shots are permitted

## Execution
1. Shooter starts directly underneath the basket.
2. Throw the ball out toward the center of the court (self-pass spinout or chest toss).
3. Sprint to catch up to the ball — **do not walk after rebounds**.
4. Square up to the basket and shoot.
5. Sprint after the rebound immediately, throw the ball back out, and repeat.
6. After each set of spot shots, step to the free-throw line and shoot the prescribed number of free throws.
7. Repeat the spot shot / free-throw cycle for the full workout.

## Coaching Points
- **Sprint every time.** Walking after rebounds trains bad habits. The drill only works if it simulates game-speed recovery.
- **No shots inside 5 feet.** This is an *outside* shooting workout — every rep should be a legitimate perimeter look.
- **Square up every shot.** The throw-out forces the shooter to gather and set feet under game-like conditions.
- **Free throws are mandatory rest and mental training.** They should not be skipped — stepping to the line after conditioning trains clutch free-throw habits.
- Track FGA, FGM, %, 3PTA, 3PTM, FTA, FTM by date to measure progress.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Workout #11-2 (50 spot shots, 5 per set)
2. **Intermediate**: Workout #11-3 (100 spot shots, 20 per set)
3. **Advanced**: Combine with live-ball move workouts (see [[drill-follow-the-leader-shooting-workouts]])

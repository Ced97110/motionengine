# Drop Step Squat

## Objective
Develop hip abductor strength and lateral-plane lower-body power by combining an active hip abduction step with a deep squat, directly transferring to defensive stance, defensive slides, and explosive lateral movement.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor space (no equipment required for bodyweight version)
- Can be loaded: hold a dumbbell, kettlebell, or medicine ball at the chest

## Execution
1. Stand in the ready/athletic position with feet hip-width apart and toes pointing slightly outward.
2. Flex the left hip to lift the left leg, then **open** (abduct) the left hip to move the left leg horizontally away from the body — this turns the body approximately 90° to the left.
3. Place the left foot on the floor roughly shoulder-width from the right foot; **keep the right foot in its original position**. Both feet should be externally rotated approximately 45° (sumo squat stance).
4. Descend in a squat until the thighs are parallel to the floor. Maintain a neutral spine, high elbows, and chest up and out.
5. Extend the hips and knees at the **same rate** to return to standing, keeping the torso-to-floor angle constant.
6. Flex the left hip to lift the left leg, then **close** (adduct) the left hip to move the left leg back to the beginning position.
7. At the end of the set, repeat with the right leg stepping out.

**Breathing:** Inhale during the lowering phase; exhale during the upward phase.

## Coaching Points
- "Tall and erect" — keep the torso upright throughout; no forward lean
- "Grip the floor" — the supporting foot grips the floor to prevent pronation or collapse during flexion/extension
- "Sit the hips back" — during the descent, sit as if sitting onto a chair, not forward-lunging
- **Do not let the knees sway in** (valgus collapse)
- Keep the foot of the supporting leg completely stationary as the stepping leg moves out, contacts, lifts, and returns

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight drop step squat — focus on foot position, depth, and torso uprightness
2. **Intermediate**: Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell at the chest; add controlled tempo
3. **Advanced**: Use the floating-leg variation — at the end of the rep, keep the stepping-leg hip flexed (thigh parallel to floor, foot off the floor) as the leg moves back, pauses, and begins the next rep — demands greater hip flexor endurance

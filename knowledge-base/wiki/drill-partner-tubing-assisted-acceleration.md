# Partner Tubing-Assisted Acceleration Drill

## Objective
Improve quick leg recovery in the first few steps and enhance stride frequency during acceleration using elastic tubing assistance from a partner.

## Setup
- 2 players: the assisted runner and the pulling partner
- A 10- to 20-yard (9- to 18-meter) piece of rubber/elastic tubing connected at both athletes' waists
- Open flat surface (field, track, or court)
- Partner lines up 15 to 25 yards (14 to 23 meters) ahead of the runner before the drill begins

## Execution
1. Both athletes attach the rubber tubing around their waists.
2. The partner moves forward to 15–25 yards away, stretching the tubing to create tension.
3. On signal, the runner gets into their chosen start position.
4. Both athletes explode forward simultaneously — the partner's running speed pulls the tubing forward, assisting the runner's acceleration.
5. The runner accelerates for 10 to 20 yards with the aid of the rubber tubing pull.
6. For **longer acceleration runs**: the partner also runs at signal to maintain tension and provide extended assistance.
7. Rest and repeat, switching roles.

## Coaching Points
- The elastic pull forces the runner's legs to cycle faster than under normal conditions — "feel the speed, then remember it."
- Maintain good acceleration posture despite being pulled — do not allow the hips to shoot up too early.
- Arm drive must match the accelerated leg frequency — arms cannot be passive.
- Use this drill to teach athletes what a faster stride frequency feels like, building neural patterning for competition.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Shorter tubing (10 yards), partner stationary, 10-yard acceleration burst
2. **Intermediate**: 15-yard tubing, partner walks/jogs to maintain tension, 15-yard burst
3. **Advanced**: 20-yard tubing, partner sprints at signal, 20-yard full-speed burst

# Unilateral Jump

## Objective
Develop single-leg explosive power, coordination, landing balance, and ankle strength through a one-leg squat into a vertical jump — the small-space single-leg plyometric.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Minimal space required
- No equipment
- **Prerequisite**: Must have mastered the one-leg squat (pistol squat) before attempting the jump variation

## Execution
1. Stand on one leg with the opposite leg **chambered** (raised, bent at the knee).
2. Squat down on the loaded leg — can go all the way to a full **pistol squat** position.
3. **If going to full depth**: Pause at the bottom before driving upward.
4. Spring back up explosively, extending fully through the hip, knee, and ankle (triple extension).
5. Land softly on the same foot, absorbing through bent knee.
6. Perform all reps on one side before switching.

## Coaching Points
- "Plenty of benefits for this move, ranging from increased coordination, landing balance, stronger ankles, etc." [S9, p.169]
- If going to full pistol depth, **pause at the bottom** — do not use a bounce/rebound to cheat the concentric
- The non-loaded leg stays chambered throughout — do not use it for balance assistance
- Land on the ball of the foot with a soft bent knee — absorb the impact
- This is suitable only for athletes who have already mastered the one-leg squat

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Single-leg partial squat (45°) with controlled lowering and slow rise
2. **Intermediate**: Single-leg squat to parallel with a small hop at the top
3. **Advanced (Unilateral Jump)**: Full pistol depth with pause and maximum explosive jump, both sides

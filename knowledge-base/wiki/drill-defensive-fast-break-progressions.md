# Defensive Fast-Break Progressions Drill

## Objective
Train transition defense in every numeric disadvantage scenario — from 2-on-1 through 5-on-5 — in one continuous full-court drill that builds communication, recognition, and ball containment habits.

## Setup
- Full court
- Divide the squad into groups of five
- Players begin on one end; groups rotate through all five scenarios in sequence
- No stopping between sequences; the drill is continuous until all five stages are complete

## Execution
1. **2-vs-1**: Two offensive players attack one defender. Defender must stop the ball and protect the rim — no layup allowed.
2. **3-vs-2**: Three offensive players attack two defenders (tandem: one at the rim, one stopping the ball). Third offensive player fills the wing; defenders communicate and rotate.
3. **4-vs-3**: Four offensive players attack three defenders. Defenders must level back, match up by proximity (not assignment), stop the ball, and cover the nearest offensive threats inside.
4. **5-vs-4**: Four defenders against five offensive players. A defensive trailer is added once the ball crosses half-court. The four defenders must protect the goal, stop the ball, and hold until the trailer recovers and picks up the free offensive player.
5. **5-vs-5**: Full transition. If offense scores, teams immediately switch roles — offense becomes defense and vice versa. Defense looks to fast break or run secondary offense after steals/misses. [S1, pp.172-173]

## Coaching Points
- Emphasize **talking on defense** — every switch, every pick-up must be communicated verbally
- "Stop the ball" — the closest defender must contain the dribbler to buy recovery time
- **Level with the ball** — no defender should trail behind the line of the ball
- First big back covers the rim; the next man back must locate and stop the ball
- The man farthest from the ball on the perimeter is the last man picked up if someone must go free
- Remind players to watch for **opponents leaking out** when their team shoots jump shots or a point guard penetrates
- The nearest perimeter teammate is responsible for stopping penetrators

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through each scenario (2v1, 3v2) with no defense allowed to move until the ball is passed — teaches positioning and communication before intensity.
2. **Intermediate**: Live drill at half-speed, emphasizing verbal communication and matching up by proximity.
3. **Advanced**: Full speed, continuous, competitive — offense scores = immediate role reversal with no rest; defense scores = same. Emphasize no uncontested layups at any stage.

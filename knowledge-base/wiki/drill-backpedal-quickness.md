# Backpedal (Assorted Biomotor Skills)

## Objective
Improve quickness and flexibility in the hip flexors — critical for defensive transition and closing out on shooters. [S8, p.144]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open space or full court
- Optional: cones to designate sprint target direction

## Execution
1. Assume an **athletic stance**: feet shoulder-width, slight knee bend, center of gravity over the base of support.
2. Maintain center of gravity over the base of support throughout — do not lean excessively backward.
3. Run backward with good form, increasing stride length progressively.
4. Keep the head up and arms pumping for balance and momentum.

## Coaching Points
- "Stay low — don't stand up when you backpedal"
- Drive the knees up during the backward stride to recruit hip flexors
- Increase stride length gradually — do not sacrifice posture for distance
- Eyes always forward (toward the simulated offensive player)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Backpedal straight-line 10–15 yards at moderate tempo; focus on posture
2. **Intermediate**: Increase stride length and speed; add head-turning (looking left/right) to simulate court vision during a backpedal
3. **Advanced**: On command from a coach or partner, execute an **"open step"** (drop-step pivot) and sprint at full speed to a designated cone [S8, p.144] — simulates the close-out-and-sprint transition requirement of defensive help

- [[defensive-transition-principles]] — backpedaling into transition is a core defensive footwork pattern

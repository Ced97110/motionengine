# One-on-One Baseline Touch Drill

## Objective
Develop first-step quickness, competitive one-on-one instincts, and conditioning by racing to the baseline and immediately playing live 1-on-1.

## Setup
**Perimeter version**:
- Players 1 and 2 set up at the middle of the free-throw line
- Coach stands behind them with the ball

**Bigs version**:
- Chair set up in the middle of the lane with a ball on it
- Two big men stand at the free-throw line

## Execution
**Perimeter version**:
1. Coach yells "Go!"
2. Both players sprint to the baseline, touch it with one foot
3. Both cut back out to the wing area
4. The **first player** to reach the wing receives the ball from the coach
5. They play 1-on-1 until a basket is scored or the defender gets the ball

**Bigs version**:
- Same rules, but the race and 1-on-1 are run **inside the lane**
- Chair with ball simulates a low-post catch point

## Coaching Points
- Sprint all the way to the baseline — no short-cutting the touch
- First step out of the baseline touch is critical — this is where the race is won
- Offensive player attacks immediately on receiving the ball — no hesitation
- Defensive player must get into a defensive stance quickly after losing the race
- "It's a competition — sprint or give up the advantage"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Players jog to baseline and walk through 1-on-1 so they understand the setup
2. **Intermediate**: Full competition as described
3. **Advanced**: Add a second defender who stays on the floor — make it 1-on-2 to practice playing through help

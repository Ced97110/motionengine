# Run and Trap (Defensive Stunt)

## Objective
Trap the ball-handler with two defenders who hold the trap as long as the dribble is alive, while three teammates cover all passing lanes using safety or all-out interception techniques to force a turnover.

## Setup
- **Players**: 5 offense, 5 defense
- **Court area**: Full court
- **Formation**: 1-3-1 (odd front) — mirrors drill 155's 2-1-2 so coaches see the stunt from both common formations
- Rotate defenders: X1 → X2 → X3 → X4 → X5 → X1; then rotate offense to defense

## Execution

### Core Mechanics
1. All run-and-jump techniques are the same as in drill 155 (Run-and-Jump) with one critical difference:
2. **X2 stays with X1 and traps player 1** instead of recovering. Both X1 and X2 remain in the trap as long as player 1 keeps the dribble alive.
3. Adjustments are made by the three teammates trying to intercept.

### Safety Trap (Figure 1)
1. Once X1 and X2 set the trap:
 - **X3**: Moves away from denial and plays **in the gap between players 3 and 5** for a possible interception. X3 reads player 1's eyes — shades in the direction 1 is looking.
 - **X5**: Plays in the gap between players 2 and 5, using the same eye-reading technique as X3.
 - **X4**: Covers the basket (safety). X4 can pursue any lob pass out of the trap if an interception opportunity presents itself.
2. Two defenders shade between three nearest receivers, reading the passer's eyes.
3. The receiver the passer turns their back on is left alone.
4. The basket defender covers the basket area.

### All-Out Interception (Figure 2)
1. The three defenders off the trap **cover the three attackers nearest the ball** tightly — no possible passes to those three.
2. The **attacker farthest from the ball (player 4)** is left completely open.
3. You are hoping player 1 cannot find player 4. In 5 seconds: turnover.
4. **Decision**: Safety tactics can run the entire game. Go all-out only at chosen moments — time and score dictate the gamble.

### Player Rotation
- Rotate X1 → X2 → X3 → X4 → X5 → X1 so all defenders practice the trap.
- Then rotate offense to defense.

## Coaching Points
- "Make sure the trap is properly set and arms are active" [S15, p.327]
- "Make sure passing lanes are covered properly whether it is a safety or an all-out interception technique" [S15, p.327]
- X3 and X5 must **read the passer's eyes** — the eyes will telegraph the pass direction
- Safety trap: shade toward where the passer is looking; leave the receiver whose back the passer turns
- All-out: trust that the far player cannot be found in 5 seconds under trap pressure

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Safety trap only — two trappers, two gap defenders, one safety
2. **Intermediate**: Add all-out interception — three tight denials, one player left open
3. **Advanced**: Coach calls safety or all-out mid-drill based on time and score context

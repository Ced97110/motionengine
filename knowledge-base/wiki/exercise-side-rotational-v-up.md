# Side Rotational V-Up

## Objective
Develop rotational core strength (rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, internal/external obliques, iliopsoas) through a side-rotated simultaneous arm-and-leg lift — training the trunk rotation needed for pivoting, screening, and absorbing off-angle contact.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor or exercise mat
- Optional: dumbbell or kettlebell for advanced variation

## Execution
1. Lie face up on the floor with arms outstretched to the sides in a "T" position, legs and feet held together. Rotate the torso and hips to one side so that half of the buttocks (one glute) and the lateral surface of the leg and foot on that side are in contact with the floor. Begin with a deep inhale [S11, Ch.8].
2. Initiate movement with the anatomical core musculature by lifting the legs and the **contralateral arm** simultaneously toward each other, moving into hip and spinal flexion plus partial torso rotation.
3. Keep the opposite (lower) arm flat on the floor, palm facing down, for stability. Maintain full extension of the knees and elbows during the upward movement.
4. As the contralateral arm and feet travel toward each other, lift the shoulders and head off the floor to maximally "crunch" the torso. Hold the final position for approximately 1 second.
5. Return to the beginning position by lowering the arm and legs and gradually relaxing the torso back.
6. Repeat the movement starting with the torso and hips rotated to the **other side**, reaching with the opposite arm [S11, Ch.8].

## Coaching Points
- "Keep the arms and legs extended through the full range of motion" [S11, Ch.8]
- "The legs and arms should not touch the floor" between reps — keeps the anatomical core engaged during the entire exercise [S11, Ch.8]
- Exhale during the crunch, inhale during the eccentric lowering
- Watch for the lower arm pushing off the floor to assist — the floor arm should be passive (palm down, no push)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard V-up (no rotation) — [[exercise-v-up]]
2. **Intermediate**: Side Rotational V-Up with bodyweight only
3. **Advanced**: Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell in the contralateral arm to increase the resistance and rotational demand [S11, Ch.8]

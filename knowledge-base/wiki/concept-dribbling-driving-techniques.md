# Dribbling and Driving Techniques

## Summary
Dribble penetration is a cornerstone of any offensive system — it creates high-percentage shots for the driver and open teammates by forcing help defenders to leave perimeter players. When a ball handler kills his dribble or attacks from triple-threat position, he must use precise footwork to defeat the on-ball defender. The two primary attacks are the **blast move** (straight-line drive) and the **front crossover move** (cross-step drive), both built around the same rule: *drivers step north, not east or west.* [S6, pp.129–133]

The drive-and-dump (penetrate and pass inside) and penetrate-and-pitch (penetrate and kick out to perimeter) are the two end goals of successful dribble penetration. [S6, p.129]

## When to Use
- After a shot fake freezes the defender (shot fake must be realistic — same speed and look as the actual shot) [S6, p.130]
- When the defender's lead foot creates a predictable gap to attack
- Any time the ball handler has a live dribble and can attack the basket
- After identifying defender's stance: inside foot lead vs. outside foot lead

## Key Principles

### Shot Fake Before Driving
1. Shot fake must replicate the actual shot — bring the ball up through the face and look at the rim. [S6, p.130]
2. Fake should be slow enough for the defender to react — not too quick. [S6, p.130]
3. Once the defender lunges at the fake, immediately initiate the drive with proper footwork.

### Ball Protection at Drive Initiation
- Place the ball beside the knee of the free (outside) leg before the first dribble — the knee shields the ball from the defender. [S6, p.130]
- First dribble is placed forward/ahead as the free foot completes the first attacking step.

### Passers vs. Drivers: Direction Rules
- **Passers step east or west** (laterally) to outflank the defender.
- **Drivers step north** (toward/past the defender) to eliminate recovery angles. [S6, p.131]
- Coaching cue: *"Attack the lead foot — step north."*

## The Two Drive Moves

### Blast Move (Straight-Line Drive)
**When to use:** The defender's outside foot (the foot on the same side as the dribbler's free foot) is the lead foot.

1. Free foot is on the same side as the defender's lead foot.
2. Step almost directly at the defender — step north toward the lead foot. [S6, p.131]
3. Free foot scrapes off the defender's shoulder, cutting off the defender's angle of pursuit. [S6, p.131]
4. Ball is placed beside the free knee; as the free foot completes the first step, push the ball forward into the first dribble.
5. Coaching cues: *"Attack the lead foot."* / *"Scrape off the defender's shoulder to cut off the angle of pursuit."* [S6, p.131]

**Why step north (not laterally)?** Stepping laterally creates space for the defender to recover and cut off the drive. Stepping toward the defender eliminates that recovery space. [S6, p.132]

### Front Crossover Move (Cross-Step Drive)
**When to use:** The defender's inside foot (the foot on the opposite side from the dribbler's free foot) is the lead foot.

1. Free foot must cross over to attack the defender's inside lead foot. [S6, pp.132–133]
2. Rip the ball low and hard across the shoe tops, with the free leg following the ball path — front pivot toward the defender's inside shoulder. [S6, p.133]
3. Free foot steps north (scraping past the defender's inside shoulder), cutting off pursuit angle from that side.
4. First dribble is made with the hand away from the defense (the hand on the opposite side from the crossover). [S6, p.133]
5. The defender may attempt a swing step (front pivot) to recover; driver must complete the crossover before the defender can adjust. [S6, p.133]

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Primary user of both moves; must read defender's lead foot before initiating the drive.
- **SG/SF**: Apply on wing isolation drives; shot-fake entry into blast or crossover move.
- **PF/C**: Apply in high-post and elbow catches when facing up with a live dribble.

## Variations

### Drive and Dump
Driver penetrates and passes inside to a teammate cutting to the basket or pinning in the lane. [S6, p.129]

### Penetrate and Pitch
Driver penetrates and passes out to a perimeter teammate left open by the collapsing defense. [S6, p.129]

### Dribble Drive Offense Integration
The dribble drive offense (referenced as a contemporary system) relies entirely on these blast and crossover footwork principles — proper footwork is the prerequisite for this system to work. [S6, p.129]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Stepping laterally to avoid the defender** → creates recovery room; step north directly at the defender instead.
2. **Shot fake too fast** → defender doesn't react; slow the fake down to match actual shot timing.
3. **Ball not protected beside the knee** → ball gets stolen before the first dribble; place ball beside the free knee immediately.
4. **First dribble pushed to the same side as the crossover** → defender can deflect it; dribble with the hand away from the defense after a front crossover.
5. **Over-striding on the crossover** → loss of balance; first crossover step should be near the defender to cut off pursuit, not an exaggerated lunge.

## Teaching Cues
- *"Attack the lead foot of the defender."*
- *"Scrape off the defender's shoulder to cut off the defender's angle of pursuit."*
- *"Step north — not east or west."*
- *"Ball beside the knee — first dribble north."*

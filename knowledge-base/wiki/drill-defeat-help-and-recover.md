# Defeat Help and Recover (Drill 60)

## Objective
Teach the weak-side offensive player the three correct reads when a help defender slides over to stop dribble penetration: stay for the kick-out, cut backdoor, or cut behind the dribbler for a handoff.

## Setup
- Coach at the wing or elbow with the ball
- Player 1: ball handler / driver at the wing
- Player 4: weak-side offensive player at the short corner / low block
- X1: defender on Player 1
- X4: help-side defender on Player 4
- Multiple groups rotate: offense → defense → end of opposite line
- Equipment: 1 basketball, full half court

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Coach passes to Player 1. X1 plays token defense, allowing Player 1 to drive to the middle.
2. X4 must stop Player 1's drive or 1 will score a layup. X4 is positioned on the help side.
3. As X4 slides over to close the gap, Player 4 reads X4's movement and chooses one of three options:
   - **Option A**: Stay put — receive a pass from 1 for an open jump shot (defender has left)
   - **Option B**: Cut backdoor — X4's movement toward the ball vacates 4's side; cut to the basket for a layup pass
   - **Option C**: Cut behind the dribbling Player 1 — trail 1 through the lane for a handoff and jump shot
4. At the end of the drill, players rotate: offense → defense → end of the opposite line.

### Intermediate
- X1 plays real defense on Player 1. Player 1 must use the **rocker-step sequence** to create the drive.
- Add a second offensive player and defender on the strong side so Player 1 can drive right or left, forcing X1 to play straight up rather than overplaying.

### Advanced
1. X1 can deny Player 1 receiving the coach's pass. Player 1 must use a V-cut, backdoor cut, or middle cut to get free.
2. If Player 1 cannot get free from X1, Player 4 should flash pivot cut or V-cut outside to receive the coach's pass.
3. Now Player 4 becomes the dribbling driver and Player 1 takes on Player 4's duties — read X1's help movement and choose from Options A, B, or C.

## Coaching Points
- "This drill runs the entire sequence of offensive and defensive maneuvers" — it can be broken down into one part at a time initially [S15, p.123]
- In Option A: the kick-out three-pointer is a legal result — teach 4 to be ready to shoot immediately
- In Option B: 4 must read X4's hip positioning, not just wait to see where X4 ends up
- In Option C: 4 must be trailing close enough to take the handoff without hesitation
- Stop play whenever a **bad decision** is made — turnovers don't need correction, but wrong reads do [S15, p.123]
- X4 should draw the charge if possible when Player 1 drives (advanced)
- Option: add a ball-handler trap when two players cross (if part of your defensive scheme), which eliminates Option C

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: X1 token defense, Player 1 drives easily, Player 4 reads one option at a time
2. **Intermediate**: X1 plays real defense; drive must be created via rocker step; all three options available
3. **Advanced**: X1 denies the catch; Player 1 must get open; role reversal if denial is successful

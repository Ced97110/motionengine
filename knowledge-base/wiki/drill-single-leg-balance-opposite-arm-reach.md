# Single-Leg Balance With Opposite-Arm Reach

## Objective
Increase single-leg balance by using the arms as drivers through multiple reach vectors at varying heights, progressively challenging neuromuscular control. [S8, p.132]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open floor area
- No equipment required (optional: cones or targets at reach vectors)

## Execution
Perform all drills on the left leg; repeat with right-side stance. Attempt to complete all reps without the opposite foot contacting the ground. Reaches can be performed to foot, waist, or shoulder height. [S8, p.132]

1. **Both arms anterior reach at waist height**: Standing on left leg, reach both arms at waist height as far forward as possible without losing balance. Return to starting position.

2. **Both arms anterior/medial reach at waist height**: Standing on left leg, reach both arms at waist height along an anterior/medial vector as far as possible without losing balance.

3. **Both arms anterior/lateral reach at waist height**: Standing on left leg, reach both arms at waist height along an anterior/lateral vector as far as possible without losing balance.

4. **Both arms posterior overhead reach**: Standing on left leg, reach both arms back over your head as far as possible without losing balance.

5. **Opposite-arm multivector reach at foot height**: Standing on left leg, reach the opposite arm toward a given vector as far forward as possible toward foot height.

6. **Same-side-arm multivector reach at foot height**: Standing on left leg, reach the same-side arm toward a given vector as far forward as possible toward foot height. [S8, p.132]

## Coaching Points
- Arms are the active drivers in these drills — the upper body creates the perturbation the stance leg must resist
- Keep a slight bend in the stance knee throughout
- Hips square — do not rotate the pelvis when reaching laterally
- "Reach long, stand strong" — the coaching cue for maximizing reach distance while maintaining posture
- The overhead reach is the most destabilizing — progress to it after mastering waist-height variations

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Waist-height bilateral reaches with foot lightly contacting the ground for assistance
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill as described with no opposite-foot ground contact
3. **Advanced**: All vectors on an unstable surface; add a basketball pass or catch at the end of each reach

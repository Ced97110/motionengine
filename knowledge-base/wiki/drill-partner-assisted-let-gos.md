# Partner-Assisted Let-Gos

## Objective
Teach quick transitions in speed and enhance stride frequency during acceleration by building lean against partner resistance then exploding free.

## Setup
- 2 players: runner and hold partner
- Open flat surface — court, field, or track
- A towel or rope may be used; or the partner's hands
- No additional equipment required

## Execution
1. The runner leans forward at approximately a **45-degree angle** while the partner holds them in place — from the front (hands on shoulders) or from behind (hands/towel at waist).
2. The runner starts pumping the legs and arms **explosively**, building against the held position.
3. After approximately **five strides** of resisted pumping in place, the partner releases the runner.
4. As the runner is let go, they explode out of the falling position using fast leg and arm movements to recover from the forward lean sensation and immediately accelerate.
5. Sprint for 15–20 yards after release.
6. Rest fully; repeat 4–6 times.

## Coaching Points
- The fall angle at 45 degrees maximizes the gravity-assisted forward lean — greater lean = greater initial acceleration force.
- "Feel the fall and sprint out of it" — the release sensation should trigger an aggressive survival response of fast leg turnover.
- Arm pumping during the held phase must be match-speed — arms feed into the legs.
- Partner must release cleanly — no gradual letting go; a crisp release produces the best contrast effect.
- Contrast effect: the immediate shift from resisted/held → free movement teaches the nervous system to produce rapid force transitions.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Shallow lean (30°), 3 strides held, short sprint
2. **Intermediate**: 45° lean, 5 strides held, 15-yard sprint after release
3. **Advanced**: Full 45° lean, 5 strides held, partner pops the release at different times (unpredictable) to build reaction

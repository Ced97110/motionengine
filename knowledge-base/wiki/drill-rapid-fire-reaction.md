# Rapid Fire (Whole-Body Reaction)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, and total-body quickness. [S8, p.214]

## Setup
- 1 player per rep, any number of players total
- Any open court area
- Coach/partner provides visual or audible cues

## Execution
1. Assume a ready position (athletic stance: slight knee bend, weight on balls of feet, hands up).
2. Wait for the start signal from the coach.
3. On the signal, begin **tapping feet alternately as fast as possible** (rapid fire foot taps) until the next command is given.
4. When the next command comes (visual or audible), react **as quickly as possible** to execute the prescribed movement:
   - Lateral shuffle
   - Forward sprint
   - Backward sprint
   - Jump
   - Sprawl (drop to ground)
   - Any other coach-designated action
5. Return to rapid fire foot taps after each movement and await the next command.

## Coaching Points
- The rapid foot tapping keeps the CNS primed for fast response — do not slow down between commands
- React to the **cue itself**, not the anticipation of it
- Commands can be visual (hand signal, finger count) or audible (word, whistle)
- "Fire your feet first — if your feet are moving, your body is ready"
- Short, quick contacts with the ground — do not stomp

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Simple commands (sprint left/right only), longer intervals between cues
2. **Intermediate**: Add jump and sprawl commands, faster command sequence
3. **Advanced**: Random command sequence at high speed; add sport-specific actions (e.g., defensive slide, shot-block jump)

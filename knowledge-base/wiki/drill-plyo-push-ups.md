# Plyo Push-Ups

## Objective
Improve quickness in the upper-body pushing musculature by training explosive ground-contact reactivity. [S8, p.156]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat floor surface
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Begin in the up (top) position of a push-up with the body straight.
2. Perform an explosive push-up: push hard enough that both hands leave the ground, achieving as much air time as possible. Finish with arms extended.
3. When landing, keep arms stiff but not locked.
4. Spend as little time on the ground as possible — explode back up immediately upon contact.
5. Repeat for prescribed reps or time.

## Coaching Points
- "Get your hands off the ground" — height of hand clearance is a measurable improvement indicator.
- Landing with stiff (not locked) elbows protects the joint while preserving the reactive demand.
- Minimal ground contact is the key to developing quickness (amortization phase reduction).
- Athletes who are too slow off the floor are losing the plyometric benefit — prompt them verbally.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard push-ups focusing on explosive upward phase before adding hand clearance.
2. **Intermediate**: Achieve consistent hand lift-off; maintain straight body.
3. **Advanced**: Add a clap between reps; move to medicine ball or box variations.

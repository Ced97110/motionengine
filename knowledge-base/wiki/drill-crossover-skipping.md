# Crossover Skipping

## Objective
Develop explosive crossover mechanics for direction changes and enhance explosive contralateral hip flexion and extension.

## Setup
- 1 player (can run in lines)
- Open court space, sideline to sideline or along the baseline
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point athletic stance.
2. Begin skipping laterally to the left, crossing the right leg over the left in an exaggerated skipping motion.
3. Emphasize left-hip extension (pushing off) and right-hip flexion (driving the knee up and across).
4. Rotate the hips to the left as the right leg goes over and in front of the left leg.
5. Keep the shoulders square to the front — do not rotate the upper body.
6. Continue for the desired distance, then reverse direction.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Single photo showing athlete in mid-crossover skip — right knee driven up and across, hips rotated left, shoulders square forward. [S8, p.78] -->

## Coaching Points
- The key difference from a regular skip is the exaggerated crossover of the lead leg
- "Hips left, shoulders square" — isolate hip rotation from trunk rotation
- Drive the crossing knee aggressively upward and across — do not just drag the foot
- Keep a tall posture; avoid leaning sideways
- Arm action: opposite arm drives forward with the crossing leg for power
- Progress from controlled rhythm to explosive power

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk-through crossover step pattern, exaggerating hip rotation
2. **Intermediate**: Skip rhythm at moderate speed; emphasize hip rotation cue
3. **Advanced**: Maximum-effort explosive crossover skipping; add a sprint out of the last rep to simulate first-step attack off a crossover dribble

# Quick Feet (in All Directions)

## Objective
Enhance stride frequency on the first step by running through a half agility ladder with one foot down per rung, then sprinting to a reaction target. [S8, p.180]

## Setup
- 1–2 players (athlete + coach for reaction command)
- Half agility ladder (half the rungs of a standard ladder to prevent neural fatigue)
- 2 cones placed 5–10 yards (4.6–9 meters) to each side of the ladder end

## Execution
1. Run through the agility ladder using a "one foot down between each rung" pattern.
2. Concentrate on foot speed, not linear running speed — rapid ground contacts are the goal.
3. On completion of the ladder, react to a visual or auditory command from the coach.
4. Sprint to the designated cone (left or right).
5. Every drill can also be performed laterally or backward for increased difficulty.

## Coaching Points
- Cue: "Fast feet, not big steps — pop off the ground."
- Athlete keeps eyes on the coach during the ladder run to receive the reaction command.
- Using only half a ladder prevents neural fatigue from becoming a limiting factor — keep the quality high.
- Watch for: excessive forward lean that slows foot cycling, looking down at the ladder, slow reaction to the command.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: One foot per rung forward, no reaction command.
2. **Intermediate**: One foot per rung + reaction sprint to a designated cone.
3. **Advanced**: Lateral or backward ladder run + reaction command sprint.

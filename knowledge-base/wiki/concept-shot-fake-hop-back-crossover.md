# Shot Fake → Hop Back → Crossover Dribble

## Summary
The shot fake hop back to crossover is the most advanced entry in the hop-back dribble-attack series. After using the shot fake to drive the defender, hopping back to create space, and seeing the defender over-recover to the hop-back side, the ball handler immediately crosses the ball over to the opposite hand and attacks in the new direction. This creates a full directional reversal that is extremely difficult to guard if the preceding moves have been established.

The crossover after the hop back is the penultimate piece of the shot-fake combination chain: fake → drive → hop back → defender recovers → crossover → new attacking angle. [S3]

## When to Use
- When the defender has been successfully baited by the hop-back on previous possessions and closes hard to the hop-back side
- Against faster defenders who can recover to the hop back but cannot handle the crossover change of direction
- In pick-and-roll situations where the ball handler uses the fake series to pull the covering defender
- As the final move in an advanced one-on-one dribble series

## Key Principles
1. **All prior moves must be credible** — this only works if the defender is reacting to the hop-back; without establishing it first, the crossover gains no advantage.
2. **Crossover must be low and quick** — the ball crosses at knee height or below immediately after the hop-back landing.
3. **Attack downhill on the crossover** — do not hesitate; the crossover creates the lane, attack it immediately.
4. **Protect the ball on the cross** — extend non-ball arm slightly to shield during the crossover.
5. **Finish with a shot or draw contact** — the crossover attack typically ends at the basket or in a mid-range pull-up.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG**: Primary user — most advanced ball-handler technique.
- **SG/SF**: Secondary users where applicable.

## Variations
### 1-Dribble Hop Back → Crossover (Left)
Fake, 1 left dribble (attack right), hop back, crossover dribble to right hand, attack left. [S3, Workout #3-19]

### 1-Dribble Hop Back → Crossover (Right)
Fake, 1 right dribble (attack left), hop back, crossover dribble to left hand, attack right.

### 2-Dribble Hop Back → Crossover
Same pattern with two attack dribbles, covering more ground before the hop back.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Slow crossover** → The window between hop-back and defender recovery is milliseconds; the crossover must be instantaneous.
2. **High dribble on the cross** → Invites a steal; keep the ball low throughout.
3. **Crossing without attacking** → The crossover is only valuable if followed immediately by an explosive attack dribble toward the basket.
4. **Skipping foundational moves** → Do not practice this move until the hop-back and roll-out are solid; it relies entirely on those setups.

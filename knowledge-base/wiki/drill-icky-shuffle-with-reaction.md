# Icky Shuffle With Reaction (Quick Feet)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, court sense, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, total-body agility, and general athleticism. [S8, p.218]

## Setup
- 1 player, agility ladder
- Start on the **left side** of the ladder
- Coach positioned at the side or end of the ladder

## Execution
1. Start on the **left side** of the ladder, facing the ladder's length.
2. Step **laterally with the right foot** into the first square.
3. Bring the **left foot** into the same square.
4. Step **laterally with the right foot** to the right side of the ladder (outside).
5. Bring the **left foot** forward to its next square.
6. Bring the **right foot** into the same square as the left.
7. Step **laterally with the left foot** to the left side of the ladder (outside), advancing the right foot to the next square.
8. Repeat this pattern (2-in-left, out-right / 2-in-right, out-left) to the end of the ladder.
9. **Simultaneously**, react to the coach's visual commands throughout the drill.

## Coaching Points
- This is the classic "Icky Shuffle" — the footwork pattern must become automatic so cognitive resources can focus on the reaction task
- High knee lift, fast ground contact
- Stay light on the balls of the feet throughout
- "Quick in, quick out — keep your eyes on the coach, not your feet!"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master Icky Shuffle footwork pattern without reaction cues
2. **Intermediate**: Add coach finger-count visual reaction
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Odd/even number filtering
   - Coach semicircle for peripheral vision development
   - Multiple coaches at periphery
   - Add 2–3 step run-in before the ladder [S8, p.218]
   - Add a sport-specific skill at the end of the ladder

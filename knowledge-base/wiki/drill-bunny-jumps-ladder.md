# Bunny Jumps (Agility Ladder)

## Objective
Enhance elastic strength in the ankle complex by performing rapid bilateral jumps into every square of a half agility ladder with minimal ground contact. [S8, p.181]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Half agility ladder
- Open space

## Execution
1. Stand at the start of the ladder.
2. Perform **fast multiple jumps** into every square, landing on both feet simultaneously.
3. Use a **quick ankling motion** — the power comes from the ankle/calf, not from a deep knee bend.
4. **Minimize ground contact** — pop off the floor immediately after each landing.
5. Look straight ahead, not at the ground.
6. To increase difficulty, a **sport-specific skill** (e.g., catching/passing a ball) can be added while performing the jumps. [S8, p.181]

## Coaching Points
- "Think ankles, not legs" — avoid deep knee bends that slow ground contact time
- Eyes forward at all times — builds spatial awareness under fatigue
- The sound of landing should be soft, not heavy — a loud thud means too much ground contact
- Adding a basketball skill (e.g., dribbling) while doing bunny jumps is a high-level challenge that bridges conditioning and sport skill

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow bilateral hops into each square with focus on two-foot landings
2. **Intermediate**: Maximize speed with minimal ground contact
3. **Advanced**: Add a reaction command at exit (sprint to designated cone) or layer in a sport-specific skill

## Concepts Taught
- [[stretch-shortening-cycle-plyometrics]] — quick ankling motion exploits elastic energy storage in the Achilles tendon
- [[ankle-proprioception-basketball]] — repetitive fast landings at varying points build proprioceptive map of the foot
- [[ankle-strengthening]] — repeated calf/ankle push-off builds plantar flexion strength endurance

# Deny the Wing Drill (#147)

## Objective
Teach ball-side wing denial using the fence slide — preventing a direct pass to a wing player through active positioning and physical contact, while allowing a backdoor option when overplayed.

## Setup
- Half court
- 3 players: player 1 (passer at top of key), player 3 (wing cutter), X3 (defender)
- Line of waiting players behind the baseline/corner
- Rotation: 1 → 3 → X3 → end of line

<!-- DIAGRAM: Player 1 at top of key with ball. Player 3 on the wing. X3 between player 3 and the ball, one step toward the ball in the passing lane. X3's outside arm extended into the passing lane with thumb down. -->

## Execution
1. Player 3 runs a V-cut. X3 denies the pass using the fence slide.
2. Player 3 has the option of running a backdoor cut if X3 overplays and makes that available.
3. Once player 3 receives the ball (or goes backdoor), player 3 front-pivots (or reverse-pivots) into triple-threat position; passes back to player 1; players rotate.

## Coaching Points (Fence Slide Technique)
- X3 stays **one full step toward the ball** from player 3, in a straight line between 3 and the ball — "keeps an ear on the chest" of player 3. [S15, p.308]
- X3 keeps arm and hand in the **passing lane** between player 1 and player 3.
- Turn the palm toward player 1 with **thumb pointed down**.
- The **off-arm** is straight out into the running lane of player 3 — when player 3 makes contact, X3 stiffens to prevent full-speed movement.
- X3 must **not initiate contact** — only stiffen when the offensive player runs into X3's extended arm.
- If X3 is over-positioned, player 3 cuts **backdoor** — the open-court layup is the price of being too aggressive. [S15, p.308]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Base drill as described — V-cut, fence slide, pass/rotate.
2. **Intermediate**: When player 3 receives the pass, player 3 front-pivots into triple-threat and the two players play **1-on-1** live.

# Full-Court Cutting (Drill 61)

## Objective
Develop passing accuracy and cutting timing by running a continuous full-court passing-and-cutting sequence where every passer immediately replaces the player they passed to, building conditioning through constant movement.

## Setup
- 8+ players, one line at each baseline
- Full court
- 1 basketball per line
- Equipment: 2 basketballs (one at each end for both directions)

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Players line up at the baseline. Player 1 begins with the basketball.
2. Player 2 cuts into the court. Player 1 passes to Player 2. Player 1 immediately runs to **replace Player 2's vacated spot**.
3. Player 3 cuts in from the opposite side, timing the cut to be ready to receive Player 2's pass immediately. Player 2 replaces Player 3's spot.
4. Player 3 passes to Player 4. Player 3 replaces Player 4.
5. Player 5 begins a V-cut to the perimeter. When Player 4 receives the pass, Player 5 cuts **backdoor or middle** for a pass from 4 and a layup.
6. Player 6 rebounds the layup and initiates the same sequence back down the court: Player 6 passes to Player 3; Player 3 to Player 2; Player 2 to Player 1; Player 1 to Player 7 for a layup.
7. Player 8 rebounds and continues. Player 7 rotates to the end of the line.
8. **Teaching key**: tell players to always replace the player they pass to — this removes all confusion about positioning.

### Intermediate
- When the passer passes to the cutter, the passer runs a **V-cut to replace the receiver** (same mechanic, now named as a V-cut).
- Add front and reverse pivots: when Player 2 receives the pass from 1, Player 2 executes a front pivot before passing to 3. Continue down the line.

## Coaching Points
- "Players must concentrate on making their cuts and passes sharply. You want the drill to be constant motion so that the action also develops conditioning" [S15, p.125]
- Focus on whichever pass type needs the most work: bounce, chest, or overhead
- Focus on whichever cut needs the most work: middle, backdoor, flash pivot, or V-cut
- Timing is everything: each new cutter must time their cut to be at the correct spot **exactly when** the previous receiver is ready to pass — no waiting
- The final pass-and-layup sequence (Player 4 → Player 5) should use a proper reading cut — backdoor if the defender gives it, middle cut if open

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Continuous passing-and-cutting without defenders; passers replace receivers
2. **Intermediate**: Add V-cut verbalization; add pivot-before-pass requirement
3. **Advanced**: Add a half-court defender on the final cutter (Player 5) who must read and choose backdoor or middle cut

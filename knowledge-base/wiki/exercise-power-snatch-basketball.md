# Power Snatch (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop maximum total-body explosive power and overhead shoulder stability through a single continuous pulling movement from the floor to arms-fully-extended overhead — the most technically demanding Olympic lift in the basketball S&C toolkit.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Barbell with appropriate load (lighter than power clean due to overhead catch requirement)
- No spotter required

## Primary Muscles Trained
Gluteus maximus, biceps femoris, semitendinosus, semimembranosus, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, soleus, gastrocnemius, deltoids, trapezius [S11, p.13]

## Execution
1. **Beginning position**: Stand with feet hip- or shoulder-width apart, bar near the shins, toes turned out slightly. Grip the bar with a closed pronated grip, elbows fully extended. **Hands should be 6–8 inches (15–20 cm) wider than the power clean grip** — this wide grip allows the bar to be received overhead at arm's length.
2. **First pull**: Under control, lift the bar from the floor keeping shoulders over the bar and back flat (neutral spine). Keep bar close to the body and hips down. As the bar rises, raise the hips slightly to prepare for the second pull.
3. **Second pull**: Explosively extend the hips, legs, and ankles while keeping elbows fully extended. Thrust shoulders up and slightly back; drive hips forward and up. Rise onto toes before arms begin pulling. Keep bar close to the body.
4. **Overhead catch**: As the lower body becomes fully extended, rapidly shrug the shoulders and start the arm pull. Pull the body under the bar and rotate the hands under the bar while dropping to a quarter squat. **Catch the bar overhead with elbows fully extended.** Stand up to an upright position.
5. **Lowering**: Under control, lower the bar to the thighs keeping it close to the body. Lower to the floor by flexing hips and knees, keeping shoulders over the bar and back flat.

## Breathing Guidelines
- Take a deep breath before initiating the first pull off the floor
- Exhale during the aggressive, explosive jumping action of the second pull [S11, p.14]

## Coaching Points
- **"The exercise should be one fluid movement from the floor to overhead"** [S11, p.15]
- **"After the initial pull, be explosive"** [S11, p.15]
- The wide grip requires greater shoulder flexibility and stability than the clean — ensure adequate shoulder mobility before loading
- Can catch in a deep squat position or a taller position; taller catch is safer for beginners
- No spotter — athletes can drop the weight safely if the catch fails

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hang snatch (bar starts mid-thigh or above knees) — reduces first-pull technical demand
2. **Intermediate**: Power snatch from floor with light-to-moderate load, quarter-squat catch
3. **Advanced**: Power snatch with heavier loads; deep-squat catch variation

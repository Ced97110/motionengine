# Boston Celtics X Action (1-4 High)

## Overview
Rick Pitino's Boston Celtics ran constant cutting motion out of a 1-4 high alignment. The "X Action" creates a running start for wing players into baseline screening, sets up a two-man game between the small forward (Pierce) and power forward (Walker), and provides a pick-and-roll alternative when the primary actions are denied. The set is demanding — players must be in elite condition to execute it at both ends. [S14, pp.47-50]

## Formation
- **1 (PG)** at top of the key
- **2 (SG)** on the right wing
- **3 (SF)** on the left wing
- **4 (PF)** at the right elbow
- **5 (C)** at the left elbow

## Phases

### Phase 1: Initial Cut and Two-Man Game
- 1 passes to 4 at the right elbow and cuts to the right wing
- 2 cuts off 5 down to the left block
- 5 pops out to receive a pass from 4
- 4 moves to set a down screen for 2
- 3 cuts off 4's back as 4 screens down the lane
- 5 passes to 2 for the shot OR post-feeds 4 if the pass to 3 slicing through the lane is denied
- This creates a two-man game on the left side between 2 (Pierce) and 4 (Walker)

### Phase 2: PnR Alternative (if 5 cannot pass to 2)
- 5 passes to 1 on the right wing, then follows 1 for a pick-and-roll
- 3 cuts back to set a back screen on 5's defender
- 1 drives off the 1-5 PnR looking for:
  - 5 on the lob
  - 4 punching into the lane
  - 2 spotting up on the weak side
- This is the Kenny Anderson–Vitaly Potapenko screen-and-roll creating penetration and draw-and-kick opportunities

## Key Coaching Points
- 2 and 3 must get a running start into baseline screening — the 1-4 alignment facilitates this
- 4 sets wide screens so that 1 (Stockton analogy: small guard) can use either the inside or outside path
- Constant movement — no passing and standing; every player is active on every possession
- In great condition: the system wears opponents down on both ends
- "The 4 cannot move, so it is the job of the 1 to run his defender off the wide screen" [S14, p.50]

## Counters
- If the interior post feed from 4 to 3 is denied → 4 reverses to 2 (who cuts off 5's screen) → **Down-Screen Option**: 4 sets down screen for 3 cutting to the elbow for a jump shot [S14, p.49-50]
- **Back-Screen Option**: Instead of the down screen, 4 reverses to 2; 2 has cut off 5's screen; 3 sets a back screen for 4; 2 looks for a lob to 4; if X3 helps on the lob, 3 pops to the top for a jump shot [S14, p.50]

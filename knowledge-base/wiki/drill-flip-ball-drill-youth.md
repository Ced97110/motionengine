# Flip-Ball Drill

## Objective
Use the ball's natural tendency to roll off a misaligned hand as self-correcting feedback — if the player can hold and flip the ball one-handed, alignment is correct; if it rolls off, a specific fault is revealed.

## Setup
- Players: entire team, individual, or pairs
- Court area: open floor — no basket needed
- Equipment: one basketball per player
- Players spread ~15 feet (4.5 m) apart

## Execution
1. Each player holds the ball in the **shooting hand only**, using only the pads and fingertips — NOT the palm
2. Lower arm is perpendicular to the upper arm; upper arm is parallel to the floor (L-position)
3. Wrist is cocked; ball is in front of the face or slightly to the shooting-hand side
4. Players flip the ball very slightly (2-3 feet) into the air and catch it with only the shooting hand when it returns
5. Continue for 1 minute

**Optional aid**: wrap a piece of tape around the center of the ball — any side-to-side spin (tape whirling) means the ball was not released with perfect backspin; pure backspin = correct wrist action [S15, p.183]

## Coaching Points
- **"If the ball rolls off, something is crooked"** — the ball cannot stay in the hand unless all parts of the arm are correctly aligned
- If the ball rolls to one side, the arm is slanted or crooked toward that side
- **"Elbow in"** — a flared elbow makes the arm crooked; the ball will roll off the outside of the hand
- **"Wrinkle behind the wrist"** — if the wrist is not cocked, the ball rolls out of the fingertips immediately; check for the wrinkle
- The tape test: watch the tape ring after release — it should spin end-over-end (pure backspin), not wobble or spin sideways

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: flip 2-3 feet into the air and catch with both hands; focus on keeping the ball balanced
2. **Intermediate**: flip and catch with only the shooting hand; requires better balance and alignment
3. **Advanced**: increase flip height to 5-6 feet; add the wrist cock → flip sequence without a pause between cock and flip

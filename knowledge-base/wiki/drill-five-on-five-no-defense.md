# Five-on-Five With No Defense

## Objective
Develop fluid motion offense movement and decision-making by allowing five players to run the offense freely from multiple formations without defensive pressure, then add progressive defense.

## Setup
- Half court, 5 offensive players in any formation
- Pairs of teams rotate: one team plays while the other waits
- Duration: 6 minutes (1 minute per team per repetition)
- No equipment beyond a basketball

<!-- DIAGRAM: 1-3-1 formation shown — player ①  at top, ②  left wing, ③ right wing, ④ left low post, ⑤ right low post near the block. Ball starts with ①. -->

## Execution
1. Five players begin in **any formation** (1-3-1, 2-1-2, 1-2-2, 1-4, etc.) and run the motion offense for **1 full minute without scoring**.
2. Players mix techniques and tactics, creating new sequences at all times.
3. After 1 minute, the team finishes with a score. The next team steps on.
4. Each successive team starts from a **different formation** than the previous team.

**Sample sequence** (illustrating the variety expected):
- 1 passes to 2 and screens away for 3, then fades to the corner
- 4 screens for 5 and rolls back to basket
- 2 passes to 5 (not shown) and runs the blast with 5
- Meanwhile, 4 V-cuts to the corner; 3 V-cuts to the wing
- 1 executes a backdoor or middle cut

**Advanced:** Put five defenders on the five offensive players. Start with token defense, then make it **live 5v5**. When live, defense must stop the offense **two consecutive times** before rotating to offense.

## Coaching Points
- Demand **constant movement** — no standing still waiting for the ball
- "Mix up their techniques and tactics, creating new sequences at all times" [S15, p.258]
- Rotate through different starting formations to prevent pattern dependency
- During the live 5v5 version, the two-consecutive-stops rule creates urgency for defense and rewards offensive execution
- Watch for players over-relying on the same two or three reads — the drill should expose all motion rules

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No defense — 1 full minute of free motion, finish with a score
2. **Intermediate**: Token defense — passive defenders allow reads but challenge timing
3. **Advanced**: Live 5v5 — defense earns rotation by stopping offense twice in a row

# Z-Pattern Cuts Cone Drill

## Objective
Improve cutting ability and sharp change of direction by sprinting through a Z-shaped cone pattern, planting on the outside leg at each turn.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open field or court space
- 4 cones arranged in a Z-pattern:
  - Cone 1: start (top left)
  - Cone 2: 10 yards right of cone 1 (top right)
  - Cone 3: 5 yards down and 5 yards left of cone 2 (middle)
  - Cone 4: 10 yards right of cone 3 (bottom right)

<!-- DIAGRAM: Z-shape — two rows offset horizontally, connected diagonally. Top row 10 yards wide, 5 yards forward to middle, then 10 yards right to finish. See [S8, p.95] -->

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance at cone 1.
2. Sprint diagonally to cone 2.
3. Plant on the outside leg at cone 2 and cut sharply toward cone 3.
4. Sprint diagonally to cone 3, plant, and cut toward cone 4.
5. Sprint to cone 4 to finish.
6. Perform drill in both directions for one full repetition.

## Coaching Points
- Drive hard into the plant foot at each cone — "load and explode"
- Plant foot should be on the outside of the cone for maximum cut angle
- Keep hips low during the cut; don't stand up at the change point
- Sprint between cones — do not jog

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Start from lying or four-point stance to train reactive acceleration.
2. **Intermediate**: Change cone distances to match court dimensions (e.g., elbow to elbow).
3. **Advanced**: Cut with inside leg only; put inside hand to the ground during turns; add ball-handling with a basketball during the sprint phases.

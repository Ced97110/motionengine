# Round-the-Clock Push-Ups

## Objective
Build explosive upper-body endurance by performing a continuous lateral-traveling push-up sequence that rotates 360° around a pivot point.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface with adequate space (approximately 3–4 feet diameter)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Assume the push-up position.
2. Dip down and explode up.
3. On the way up, land with hands approximately one foot to the side (not directly back in the same position).
4. Use feet as a pivot point — feet stay approximately in place while hands rotate around them.
5. Continue rhythmically with no pausing between reps.
6. Keep rotating until a full 360° has been completed.
7. Can be performed clockwise, counter-clockwise, or alternating directions.

## Coaching Points
- "No stopping — the rhythm is the drill"
- Hands move about a foot to the side each rep — too small a step slows rotation, too large risks losing balance
- Feet act as the pivot point; they should not move significantly
- A full 360° = approximately 12 push-ups depending on step width
- Train both clockwise and counterclockwise equally

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Half-clock (180°) in one direction before resting
2. **Intermediate**: Full 360° in one direction
3. **Advanced**: Alternating directions without stopping; add clap at peak of each push-up

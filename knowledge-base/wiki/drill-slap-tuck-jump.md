# Slap Tuck Jump

## Objective
Develop explosive lower-body power and learn to tuck the knees toward the chest during a vertical jump, as a progression step between butt-kick jumps and full tuck jumps.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any open space (no equipment required)
- Can be performed indoors or outdoors

## Execution
1. Stand shoulder-width apart, body flexed and ready for action. Hands can be placed slightly in front of the chest (palms down) or at the sides. [S9]
2. Dip down into a squat (same depth as a squat jump).
3. Explode upward using maximum lower-body and total-body spring.
4. At the peak of the jump, explosively draw the knees up to slap the hands. Knees should reach approximately hip-level at the top of the movement.
5. If starting with hands at sides, move them even faster than the legs to meet the knees.
6. After the tuck, throw the legs back down for landing.
7. Land softly, catching most weight on the balls of the feet.
8. Use the elastic rebound immediately to repeat.

## Coaching Points
- "Knees to hands, not hands to knees" — the knees must actively drive up
- At the peak, knees should be level with the hips — that is the target height for this step
- Land softly — feet, ankles, knees absorb force; do not crash to the floor
- Use the elastic rebound immediately; do not pause between reps
- Do not allow the hips to drop or the back to round excessively during the tuck

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: If you cannot raise knees to hip level, drop hands lower and slap knees at whatever height is currently achievable — knee height will improve with practice [S9]
2. **Intermediate**: Standard — slap hands at hip level
3. **Advanced**: Slap the side of the calves, the insteps, or the feet — requires more explosive knee drive and greater hand/arm speed [S9]

# Trap the Pass

## Objective
Force a turnover by deliberately inviting a pass to the wing and immediately trapping the receiver before they can re-initiate the offense.

## Setup
- **Players**: 5 offense (1 at top with ball, 2–5 spread), 5 defense
- **Court area**: Full court — trap occurs when the wing pass is caught
- **Formation**: 1-3-1 (odd front) as shown in the figure — formations changed so coaches see traps work against any offensive set
- Rotate defenders: all play all positions before rotating offense/defense

## Execution
1. **One change from Trap the Dribble**: X3 does **not** deny the wing. Instead, X3 allows — even encourages — a free passing lane to player 3, luring player 1 to pass there. X3 can even use a hedge-and-recover from the dribbler (without being in the passing lane to 3) to make the wing look even more open.
2. **Once the pass is made**: X1 and X3 immediately trap the pass receiver (player 3). The trap is set before player 3 can establish a dribble.
3. **Safety interception** (shown in figure):
   - X2 covers both players 1 and 2 in the gap between them, reading the passer's eyes. (A cross-court lob to player 2 is a very slow, easily intercepted pass — X2 shades harder toward player 1.)
   - X5 covers player 5 and shades to help on player 1.
   - X4 (safety) covers player 4 and the basket.
4. **All-out interception option**: Three defenders off the trap cover the three attackers nearest the trapped player tightly. Player 4 (farthest from the ball) is left open — you are counting on the trapped player being unable to find the open player under pressure within 5 seconds.
5. **Decision**: Coach decides safety or all-out based on time and score.

## Coaching Points
- "Make sure the trap is properly set and arms are active" [S15, p.331]
- X3 must appear passive in the passing lane — the goal is to **invite** the pass, not deny it
- Once the pass leaves player 1's hands, X3 **explodes** to trap immediately
- X2 shades harder toward player 1 because a cross-court lob is too slow to complete safely
- This drill is beginner-accessible: "the trap-the-dribbler and trap-the-pass are so simple even beginners can use them" [S15, p.323]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Passive X3, safety trap only — two trappers plus X2/X5 in gaps, X4 safety
2. **Intermediate**: Add all-out interception — deny all three closest receivers, leave player 4 open
3. **Advanced**: X3 varies between deny-and-deny (trap the dribble) and open-lane (trap the pass) in the same session so player 1 cannot predict which is coming

# Overplay Step

## Objective
Train defenders to use the overplay slide — nose on the basketball — to force a live dribbler to change direction at least three times per half-court while staying fundamentally sound.

## Setup
- Three lanes: left lane (sideline to free-throw lane line), center lane (between free-throw lane lines), right lane
- Best dribblers in the center lane
- 3 attacker-defender pairs, one per lane
- Full court, down and back

<!-- DIAGRAM: Three full-court lanes with attacker-defender pairs running zigzag overplay patterns simultaneously. p.289 -->

## Execution
1. Each attacker dribbles down the court and back in their lane; offense and defense switch roles after each trip.
2. Attackers stay in their lane the entire way.
3. Defenders must turn (force a direction change on) their opponent at least **three times per half-court**.
4. Begin with dribblers moving slowly; progressively allow them to try to drive by the defender.
5. At the end of the dribble the attacker must put both hands on the ball. When the defender sees two hands on the ball, they immediately move in tight.
6. **Option**: Make defenders put arms behind their back — feet only. This emphasizes foot movement.

## Coaching Points
1. **Nose on the basketball**: the defender's nose is exactly level with where the basketball is during the dribble — overplay is approximately one half of a body
2. **Head steady**: below the dribbler's armpits; move as though balancing a bucket of water — no bouncing up and down
3. **Eyes on the belt buckle**: this part moves least during fakes
4. **Slide steps**: front foot slightly behind parallel to trail foot
5. **Snake-strike hands**: arms and hands out to the side, constantly striking in toward the dribble — quick in and back out
6. **Draw the charge**: if attacker dribbles into the overplayed position, take the charge
7. **Trail hand low**: allows defender to deflect the crossover dribble

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Restrict attacker to only one or two moves (crossover + spin)
2. **Intermediate**: Allow all four dribbling moves (in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin)
3. **Advanced**: Attacker goes full speed; arms-behind-back variation for defenders

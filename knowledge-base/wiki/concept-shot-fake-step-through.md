# Shot Fake → Stride Stop / Step-Through

## Summary
The stride stop/step-through is the natural counter to the shot fake stride stop. After showing a convincing shot fake, if the defender leaves their feet (jumps to block the shot), the offensive player does NOT pull up and shoot — instead, they drive past the frozen/airborne defender into a stride stop and then step through underneath or around the defender for a close-range finish or drawn foul.

This is a two-read move: (1) if the defender stays down → shoot the pull-up; (2) if the defender goes up → step through for the easier shot or foul. Having both options in the same action makes it nearly unguardable. [S3]

## When to Use
- Against aggressive shot-blockers who have been conditioned to jump fakes
- In the mid-post and elbow areas where shot-blocking help is close
- When the defense is protecting the shooter in late-game situations
- After establishing the pull-up — defenders will start jumping fakes, opening the step-through

## Key Principles
1. **Same setup as the stride stop** — perform an identical shot fake and attack dribble; the defender cannot know which option you will take.
2. **Read the defender's feet** — if feet leave the floor, continue your drive momentum and step around them.
3. **Step through below the defender** — take a long, low step under and past the airborne defender's landing zone.
4. **Protect the ball with the body** — use a strong inside arm to shield the ball as you step through; do not expose it.
5. **Finish softly** — a step-through finish is typically a layup, baby hook, or floater at close range.
6. **Both directions, 1 and 2 dribbles** — practice going left and right with both attack-dribble lengths.

## Player Responsibilities
- **PG/SG/SF**: Primary users — all perimeter-to-midpost scorers.
- **PF**: High-value skill in face-up and elbow iso situations.

## Variations
### 1-Dribble Step-Through (Left and Right)
Minimal ground covered — used when defender is directly in front.

### 2-Dribble Step-Through (Left and Right)
More lateral or forward movement before the fake; step-through opens a wider lane.

### Stride Stop → Shoot vs. Step-Through (Decision Drill)
Player reads live defender in practice — if defender goes up, step through; if defender stays, shoot. [S3, Workout #3-7/3-8/3-9]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Not fully committing to the fake** → The step-through only works if the fake is convincing enough to get the defender airborne.
2. **Jumping into the defender** → Step through, not into them; avoid charging fouls.
3. **Holding the ball too high during the step** → Keep the ball low and protected as you step through — high ball invites a strip.
4. **Losing balance on the step-through** → Stride stop first establishes balance, then step through in a controlled manner.
5. **Only practicing one direction** → Alternate L/R every set; defenders will exploit one-directional players.

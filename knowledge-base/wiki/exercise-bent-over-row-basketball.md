# Bent-Over Row (Basketball Strength Training)

## Objective
Develop latissimus dorsi, upper back, and posterior deltoid strength through a loaded horizontal pull, supporting athletic posture, rebounding physicality, and driving through contact.

## Setup
- **Equipment**: Barbell (or dumbbells)
- **Players**: 1 athlete
- **Location**: Weight room, barbell floor space

## Execution
1. **Beginning Position**: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointed straight ahead, knees slightly flexed, shins close to the bar. Grip the bar with a **pronated, closed grip wider than shoulder-width**. Hinge at the hips until the torso is **almost parallel to the floor** with a flat back and neutral spine. Eyes look toward the floor; chin tucked for a neutral neck.
   - *Alternative*: Perform a deadlift first to lift the bar from the floor, then hinge to the beginning position.
2. Pull the bar toward the torso — **touch bar to the lower chest or upper abdomen**.
3. Keep the torso rigid, back neutral, and knees slightly flexed throughout the pull.
4. Lower the bar back toward the floor in a controlled manner, maintaining neutral spine and body position.

## Coaching Points
- **"Hinge at the hips"** — allows the torso to move forward while protecting the back
- Flex the knees more if the hamstrings are tight
- **Maintain a stationary, rigid torso** — no jerking or rowing with the body
- Do not pull the bar below the nipple line
- Do not round the back when trying to touch the bar to the chest
- Do not use torso momentum to jerk the weight up

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two-dumbbell bent-over row with lighter load — allows each side to work independently and is easier to balance
2. **Intermediate**: Barbell bent-over row with moderate load at full range
3. **Advanced**: Single-leg dumbbell row (standing on one leg) — adds a proprioception/balance challenge; supinated grip variation for greater biceps involvement

## Variations
- **Dumbbell variation**: One or two dumbbells instead of a barbell
- **Supinated grip**: Reverse the grip to increase biceps contribution
- **Bench-supported**: Lie on a tall bench with bar under — eliminates lower-back loading, isolates the lats
- **Single-leg dumbbell row**: Stand on one leg for added proprioception challenge

# SAQ Drill Finder — Complete Index

## Summary
The Drill Finder from *Training for Speed, Agility, and Quickness* (S8) catalogs all 195 drills in the book organized by five physical quality categories: Speed, Agility, Balance, Quickness, and Reaction Time. Each entry lists the drill number, page number, primary emphases, difficulty level (Basic/Intermediate/Advanced), and equipment needed. This index serves as a program-design reference — coaches can select drills by quality, level, or available equipment and plug them into the 6-week program template. [S8, pp.241-251]

## When to Use
- Building a 6-week SAQ program for a team or individual
- Selecting drills by level when working with mixed-ability groups
- Identifying equipment-free alternatives for limited facility settings
- Progressing athletes from Basic → Intermediate → Advanced in any physical quality

## Key Principles
1. **Five physical qualities**: Speed, Agility, Balance, Quickness, Reaction Time — each requires dedicated training, not just general conditioning
2. **Level progression**: Basic → Intermediate → Advanced; never skip levels with unprepared athletes
3. **Emphasis clarity**: Each drill has a primary mechanical emphasis (e.g., "hip extension," "stride frequency," "first-step quickness") — choose drills that match your players' needs
4. **Equipment matching**: Many advanced drills require specific equipment (sleds, parachutes, agility ladders, angle boards); plan accordingly
5. **Sport specificity**: Not all drills are equally applicable to basketball — prioritize lateral movement, reaction, first-step, and cutting drills

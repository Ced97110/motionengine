# Shooting-Hand Form Shooting Drill

## Objective
Isolate and perfect shooting arm alignment (especially elbow position) by removing the balance hand from the equation, building a consistent and straight release from close range outward.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any basket, starting directly in front of the rim
- 1 basketball
- Begin 2 feet (0.6 m) from the basket

## Execution
1. Assume proper shooting stance: feet shoulder-width apart, shooting-side foot forward, knees bent, hips bent forward
2. Grip the ball in the shooting hand only — no balance hand. Fingertips and fingerpads only; palm off the ball
3. Set the elbow in the L-shaped position, directly under the ball and above the shooting foot
4. Wrist is cocked and locked (bent back until skin wrinkles)
5. Seam the ball: valve between index and middle finger
6. Shoot until you make **5 consecutive shots** from 2 feet
7. Take a half-step back and shoot until you make 5 more
8. Continue stepping back half a step at a time, making 5 at each spot
9. Stop when your form breaks down — when you're throwing rather than lifting. That is your current maximum shooting range
10. Goal: complete **10 spots** for a total of **50 makes**

## Coaching Points
- The balance hand is intentionally excluded because it is often the culprit for causing the elbow to pop out (chicken wing). Removing it forces proper shooting arm mechanics [S5, p.16]
- Watch for the elbow: it must stay directly under the ball and above the shooting foot — not flaring out to the side
- The ball should arc cleanly off the fingertips with backspin; if it rolls off the palm, hand position is wrong
- "Be a **lifter**, not a **slinger** or a **pusher**" — slingers over-bend the elbow; pushers under-bend [S5, p.15]
- Finish with elbow fully extended and **above the eyebrow**
- Do NOT rush to move back; earn each distance by making 5 clean shots
- When form breaks down (ball is being thrown), stop — do not practice poor mechanics

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Shoot straight on (directly in front of rim), making 5 at each half-step spot
2. **Intermediate — Around the World**: Start in front, then step to the side along the first lane marker above the block, make 5, go to the other side, continue up the lane to the free-throw line; work back a half step at a time from each new angle
3. **Intermediate — Angled**: Instead of straight on, work one side out toward the wing or along the baseline
4. **Advanced — Strings**: Rather than making 5 before advancing, shoot for a consecutive streak (e.g., 10 in a row) or until you miss. Requires greater concentration and prevents the drill from becoming monotonous [S5, p.17]

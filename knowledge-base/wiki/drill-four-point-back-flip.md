# Four-Point Back Flip

## Objective
Bridge the back handspring and the back flip by developing the jump height and leg-whip speed to delay arm contact until it is simultaneous with foot contact — the direct precursor to the no-hands back flip (Step 9).

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat or cushioned surface
- No equipment required
- **Prerequisite**: solid one-arm back handspring (Step 8) [S9, p.14]

## Execution
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hands held high.
2. Dip down at knees and hips, swinging arms down and behind. Keep looking forward.
3. Explode **up and backwards diagonally** — goal: **jump higher than a regular back handspring** [S9, p.14].
4. As you jump, look behind you; slight back arch. Hands follow line of vision.
5. Once legs reach vertical, whip feet down to ground rapidly.
6. **Delay putting arms down as long as possible** — wait until the very last moment. For this drill, **hands and feet should make contact with the floor at approximately the same time** [S9, p.14].
7. When feet and hands are supporting you, stand up straight.

## Coaching Points
- "This is a transitional drill. At first, your hands will land before your legs—that's fine." Keep trying to jump higher and whip legs sooner [S9, p.14]
- The objective is to progressively sequence the landing so **feet touch first** — at that point, the athlete is essentially performing a back flip
- One-arm handspring training should have built the height and leg independence needed for this step [S9, p.14]
- "Eventually you will be landing on your feet before your hands — in a deep squat. This, essentially, is a back flip." [S9, p.14]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hands land clearly before feet — acceptable starting point
2. **Intermediate**: Hands and feet land simultaneously — true "four-point" landing
3. **Advanced**: Feet land before hands in a deep squat → move to Master Step (back flip) and add tuck [S9, p.14]

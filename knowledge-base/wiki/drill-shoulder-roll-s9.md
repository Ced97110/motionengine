# Shoulder Roll (Front Flip Chain — Step 1)

## Objective
Teach the athlete to roll safely forward by using the leading shoulder and upper-back to absorb force — conditioning the body to forward rotation without loading the wrists, elbows, or cervical spine.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Soft surface preferred (grass, carpet, gymnastics mat)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Squat toward the ground with the strongest foot slightly forward for comfort.
2. Place the *edge* of the palm heel (not the full palm) on the ground on the dominant side. Free hand may be placed on the floor as an additional stabilizer.
3. Continue lowering slowly forward until the body overbalances. Brace the leading arm to take most of the body's weight.
4. As the legs swing, simultaneously bring both hands up and toward the head.
5. Let the braced arm and shoulder guide the roll — **keep the head tucked in and out of the way; the head should take zero weight.**
6. Roll over the back without veering to either side.
7. Finish the roll in a seated position.
8. **Progression**: Use momentum to roll all the way to standing. Some martial artists add a spin to face the opposite direction upon standing.

## Coaching Points
- **"Head tucked — always"** — The neck must never be loaded; leading shoulder and upper-back do all the work
- **Smooth, not explosive** — This drill is performed gently; there is no impact; "keep the movement smooth and impact-free"
- **Strongest side first** — Begin on the dominant side to learn the movement pattern, then practice both sides
- **Nausea is normal early** — If rolling makes the athlete dizzy, lower the head close to the floor before initiating to slow the spin perception
- **Regression**: Head down first (near floor) before initiating the roll reduces perceived spin speed and builds comfort

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow and controlled to a seated finish; prioritize head protection and shoulder pathway
2. **Intermediate**: Increase speed while maintaining smooth, impact-free form; roll all the way to standing
3. **Advanced**: Add a half-turn spin to stand facing the opposite direction — useful for sport-specific agility

# Side Right-In Agility Ladder Drill

## Objective
Improve agility, balance, coordination, and quickness through a side-stepping pattern where one foot leads inside the ladder and the other crosses over.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder laid flat
- Player starts in two-point stance, standing sideways to the ladder

## Execution
1. Start in two-point stance, sideways to the ladder (ladder to your right).
2. Step **right foot** into the first square.
3. Step **left foot forward** across the first square to the other side of the ladder.
4. Step **laterally** with the right foot to the second square.
5. Step **backward** with the left foot, landing in front of the second square.
6. Step **laterally** with the right foot to the third square.
7. Repeat this sequence — right foot leads inside, left foot alternates front-to-back — the full length of the ladder.

## Coaching Points
- Stay low in an athletic stance throughout; do not stand tall between steps
- Look up — not at feet [S8, p.108]
- Keep quick, light foot contacts in each square
- **Complex Variation — Side Left-In**: Perform with left foot leading inside; use opposite foot instructions [S8, p.110]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through slowly to establish the rhythm of the pattern.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed execution, both directions (Side Right-In and Side Left-In).
3. **Advanced**: Increase lateral step pace; compete for time.

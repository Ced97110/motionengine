# Post-Up Mechanics (Drill 96)

## Objective
Teach all foundational post-scoring techniques — posting position, drop step, pump fake, face-up moves, spin, and half-spin — with correct footwork, making each player dangerous near the basket.

## Setup
- Individual or team
- Paint area / low block
- **Figure 1** (basic): Single line of post players, coach passes from the wing
- **Figure 2** (advanced): Perimeter player at free-throw line extended, player in corner; rotate: 1 → 2 → 5 → X5 → end of line
- 5 minutes per technique

## Execution

Coach instructs players to execute one post technique at a time (or combine 2–3 per session). Work one per day or combine, e.g., post-up position + drop step + pump fake.

### a. Posting Up
- Coach announces defensive coverage (upper side, low side, fronted, directly behind).
- Player uses **body** to push defender to create space — upper side defender: push them a step up from the block; low side: push lower; fronted: push slightly outside.
- If defended directly **behind**: get low (sitting in chair position), hold hands out front as target, keep buttocks into defender's body.

### b. Drop Step
1. Place **front foot in front of defender's front foot**.
2. Put **arm in defender's torso**, elbow bent 90°, to block off the defender.
3. When coach passes inside, **move the back foot toward the basket** while still holding off with the front foot and arm.
4. Receive the pass and shoot the layup (use slide-step dribble if needed — see Drill 97).

### c. Pump Fake
1. Post player must be near the basket — square **shoulders to the basket**.
2. Using both hands, **violently throw both arms up** toward the basket (must look like a real shot).
3. May pump-fake up to **3 times** — shoot anytime the defender leaves the floor.
4. **3+ pump fakes = 3-second violation risk**.
5. Can be used at the end of a dribble, end of a post move, or after an offensive rebound.

### d. Pump-Fake Crossover (Up and Under)
1. Execute pump fake — defender comes up on toes.
2. **Step by the defender** with a crossover step for the layup.
3. Always end the post move or dribble with a **jump stop** to preserve two pivot feet (enabling crossover in either direction).
4. Alternative: **pump fake direct step** = "up and under."
5. Use whichever maneuver the defender gives.

### e. Face-Up Moves
1. Post player executes a **front pivot or reverse pivot** to face the basket.
2. Options: jump shoot immediately, pump fake then shoot, or use a **rocker step** to drive directly or crossover drive.

### f. Spin Move
- Same mechanics as the dribbling spin move, executed by post player with **back to the basket**.

### g. Half-Spin Move
- Same as the dribbling half-spin, executed by post player with **back to the basket**.

## Options / Progressions
1. **One defender**: Put one defender on the post player; post player reads and reacts to coverage.
2. **Full perimeter** (Figure 2): Perimeter player at FT line extended, another in corner; they pass back and forth until post player gets free for an entry; X5 can front, one-step, two-step, or play behind — post player reads and reacts.

## Coaching Points
- "Make sure all mechanics, including **footwork**, are executed properly."
- Pump fake must look exactly like the shot — if it doesn't, defenders won't bite.
- Jump stop at the end of dribbles to preserve pivot options.
- Post players should **not** dribble outside their legs — see slide-step dribble (Drill 97).

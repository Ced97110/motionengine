# Dribble Intos Drill

## Objective
Train the pull-up jump shot by forcing players to decelerate quickly and execute a controlled two-count stop inside a triangle of chairs at the free-throw line.

## Setup
- Half-court
- 3 chairs set up in a tight triangle at the free-throw line
- 1 rebounder in the lane
- Player starts with the ball outside the 3PT arc

## Execution
1. Player dribbles fast from outside the 3PT arc toward the triangle of chairs
2. Player makes a **two-count stop** under full control inside the chair triangle
3. Player pulls up and shoots a jump shot
4. Rebounder retrieves and returns the ball
5. Run on either side of the half-court for set reps or time

## Coaching Points
- Dribble with maximum speed toward the chairs — simulate a real game drive
- The two-count stop must be **under control** — no stumbling or drifting
- Stay low through the stop; rise into the shot naturally
- Chairs provide a visual target for the deceleration zone — respect them
- "Pound the dribble" approaching the chairs — hard, quick dribble keeps the ball in hand

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow approach, emphasize the two-count stop mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Game-speed approach as described
3. **Advanced**: Add a closeout defender who enters from the baseline after the stop

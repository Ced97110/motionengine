# Lat Pull-Down (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop upper-back pulling strength — particularly the latissimus dorsi — using adjustable machine resistance, building the back muscles essential for running, rebounding, injury prevention, and physicality in the paint.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Lat pull-down machine with thigh support pads
- Seat adjusted so legs fit comfortably under support pads

## Execution
1. Sit facing forward, legs secured comfortably under the support pads.
2. Extend arms overhead and grip the bar in an **overhand grip** (palms facing out), hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
3. Lean back slightly. Initiate each rep by pulling the bar toward the **upper chest** in a controlled motion until the bar touches the top of the chest. Keep elbows close to the sides of the body.
4. Slowly return the bar to the starting position with arms fully extended.
5. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Lean back slightly — just enough to clear the face, not to turn this into a back lean."
- Keep elbows tracking toward the hips — do not flare wide.
- Controlled return — do not let the weight stack crash; feel the lats stretch at the top.
- This exercise is preferred over pull-ups for athletes of **varying training experience levels** because resistance is adjustable.
- "Chest tall, shoulder blades pulling down and together."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard overhand (pronated) wide-grip pull-down.
2. **Intermediate**: **Close-Grip Lat Pull-Down** — bring grip toward the center of the bar; emphasizes triceps brachii, rhomboid major, and rhomboid minor.
3. **Advanced**: **Reverse-Grip Lat Pull-Down** — close grip with palms facing the body (supinated); further incorporates biceps brachii.

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Latissimus dorsi, biceps brachii, brachialis, brachioradialis, posterior deltoid
- **Secondary**: Rhomboid major, rhomboid minor, teres major, teres minor, infraspinatus, external oblique, trapezius

## Basketball Focus
The lat pull-down offers the same muscle development focus as pull-ups with the advantage of **variable resistance**, making it accessible to athletes at all training levels. Key basketball benefits:
- Develops pulling muscles of the upper back for **running and rebounding**
- Provides **stabilization and injury prevention** — especially important as basketball contact under the basket increases
- A well-developed back helps **fend off opponents** attempting to steal the ball
- Allows athletes to become physically strong while still maintaining the **flexibility needed to shoot**

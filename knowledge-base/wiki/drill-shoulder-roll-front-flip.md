# Shoulder Roll (Front Flip Chain — Step 1)

## Objective
Build forward rotation tolerance, breakfall mechanics, and kinesthetic comfort with rolling — the foundational movement prerequisite for all front flip progressions.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (solo)
- **Equipment**: None required; soft surface (grass, carpet, mat) recommended for beginners
- **Space**: Any open floor area

## Execution
1. Squat toward the ground; placing the foot of your strongest side slightly forward may feel more comfortable.
2. Place the *edge of your palm heel* (not the full palm) on the ground as you dip down — use your dominant/strongest side.
3. Continue lowering slowly forward and downward until you begin to overbalance.
4. Brace your arm to absorb most of your body weight. As your legs swing, simultaneously bring your hands up and toward your head.
5. Let your braced arm and shoulder *guide* the movement as you roll over — keep the head tucked in tightly and out of the way.
6. Roll over your upper back, trying not to veer to either side.
7. Finish naturally in a seated position.

**Key biomechanical point**: The leading upper arm directs body weight directly onto the upper back — wrists, elbows, and shoulders experience virtually no strain during this exercise. [S9, p.146]

## Coaching Points
- "Head stays tucked — never let the head take weight."
- Keep the roll smooth and impact-free — this should feel like a gentle glide, not a crash.
- Do not veer left or right; aim for a straight line along the upper back.
- Athletes new to rolling may feel nauseous — this is normal and passes with repetition.
- Perform gently at first; this is not a drill to rush.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Carefully lower the head close to the floor *before* initiating the rolling motion to reduce the sensation of spinning.
2. **Intermediate**: Once slow execution is clean, pick up speed while keeping the movement smooth.
3. **Advanced**: Use the momentum of the roll to rise all the way to standing at the finish. Some martial artists add a spin during the roll, finishing facing the opposite direction.

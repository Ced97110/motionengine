# Band Lateral Walk

## Objective
Increase lateral hip and thigh strength (gluteus medius/minimus, TFL) to improve lateral direction changes when dribbling, guarding an opponent, or driving to the basket.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1
- **Location:** gym floor, court, or open space
- **Equipment:** miniband (looped around each ankle); start with lightest resistance, progress as able
- Minimum 25–50 yards of open space [S2, p.26]

## Execution
1. Stand in a quarter-squat position, feet shoulder-width apart. Loop a miniband of appropriate resistance around each ankle.
2. Step to the left by **pushing off the right foot** — do NOT initiate movement with the left leg moving laterally.
3. On completion of the step left, step left again, again initiating by pushing from the right foot. Repeat for the prescribed distance.
4. Return to the starting position by performing the exercise to the right, initiating each step by pushing the left foot.
5. Begin with **25-yard lateral walks**; progress to **50-yard lateral walks** for up to five sets. [S2, pp.26-27]

## Coaching Points
- **"Push, don't pull"** — the trailing leg drives the movement, not the lead leg. This is the most common error.
- Maintain a consistent quarter-squat position — do not stand up between steps.
- Feet should not come together at any point during the walk.
- Progress band resistance level (lighter → heavier) only after the required distance can be completed with proper form.
- This movement directly mirrors lateral defensive slide mechanics.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** 25-yard lateral walks × 3 sets, lightest band.
2. **Intermediate:** 50-yard lateral walks × 5 sets, medium band.
3. **Advanced:** 50-yard lateral walks × 5 sets, heavy band; add upper-body defensive hand position for full defensive-stance integration.

## Basketball Application
Increases lateral hip and thigh strength to assist in rapidly changing direction when dribbling. This ability helps beat the defender whether establishing court position or driving to the basket for a layup. [S2, pp.26-27]

**Primary muscles:** gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, tensor fasciae latae

**Secondary muscles:** gluteus maximus, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius)

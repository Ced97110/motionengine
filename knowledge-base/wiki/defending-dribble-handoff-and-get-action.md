# Defending Dribble Handoffs and Get Action

## Summary
Dribble handoffs and get action (pass-and-return) are quick ball-movement concepts that can create open layups or pull-up shots if defenders are slow to communicate. Herb Brown's system prioritizes aggressive switches and traps to take away these advantages, and emphasizes that defenders must never get screened in these situations. [S1, pp.143-144]

## When to Use
- When scouting reveals an opponent's heavy use of DHO (dribble handoff) or get action
- Any time a ball handler dribbles toward a teammate and executes a handoff
- Short or late clock situations where switching is preferred

## Key Principles
1. **Dribble Handoff**: Go through or inside your teammate whose man passes the ball, OR aggressively trap or switch and send the dribbler back to his defender
2. **May overplay the receiver** to force him backdoor on the handoff
3. **Get Action**: Jump switch and trap, OR step back to let the pass-defender go through; never let anyone get screened
4. **Pass and Screen Away**: Switch (back man + nearest-to-ball man call the switch), or body up and ride over the screen [S1, pp.143-144]

## Player Responsibilities
- **Ball-handler's defender**: Steps back to allow his teammate to go through on a DHO; or initiates the trap with his teammate
- **Receiver's defender**: Goes through/inside on DHO; or calls the switch and presses the new ball handler on get action
- **Third defender**: Covers the cutter on get action and is ready to pick up any back-cut

## Variations
### Trap the Handoff
Aggressively trap the DHO — especially when the handoff is anticipated. Two defenders converge on the ball handler as the exchange happens. [S1, p.143]

### Go Through / Inside
The receiver's defender goes through the inside gap between the two offensive players rather than going around. Requires the ball-handler's defender to step back and create the lane. [S1, p.143]

### Backdoor Overplay
Overplay the receiver on the handoff to force him backdoor — useful when the receiver is a great shooter who thrives off of handoff catch-and-shoots. [S1, p.143]

### Jump Switch Get Action
On pass-and-return handback, jump switch and trap immediately — X1 and X3 trap the handback; X2 is ready to pick up the cutter. [S1, pp.143-144]

### Pistol Action / Get Hand-Back and Flare
Switch all screens in Pistol action whenever possible (especially short/late clock). If not switching the second screen: body up and ride the screener over it while applying tough ball pressure. On the get hand-back and flare: switch as in Pistol, or body up and fight over the jam/flare; may trap the hand-back if anticipated. [S1, p.145]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Getting screened on the DHO** → receiver gets a free catch-and-shoot; correction: communicate early and go through inside
2. **Both defenders following the ball on the switch** → backdoor cut goes uncovered; correction: third defender stays alert to the cutter
3. **Defender retreating too much on get action** → gives the passer a running start on the return cut; correction: step back only enough to allow the through-go

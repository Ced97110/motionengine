# Left-to-Right V-Cut Shooting Drill

## Objective
Develop catch-and-shoot footwork off a V-cut while simultaneously training passers to deliver the ball in rhythm and rebounders to pursue the ball aggressively.

## Setup
- **Players**: Minimum 7; for a team of 15, use two baskets and split into groups of 8 and 7
- **Court area**: Half court, free-shot-line extended, outside the three-point line on both wings
- **Equipment**: Multiple balls (one per passer)
- **Positions**:
  - **R** (Rebounder): 1 player positioned near the basket — keeps the ball from hitting the floor
  - **P** (Passing line): 3–4 players with balls on the **right** side of the floor, free-shot-line extended, outside the three-point line
  - **S** (Shooting line): Remaining players on the **left** side of the floor, free-shot-line extended, facing the passing line

## Execution
1. The first shooter (S) takes an imaginary defender **down toward the basket** for several steps.
2. Shooter pushes off the **left (inside) foot** and V-cuts back toward the passing line, calling for the ball to signal readiness.
3. Passer (P) delivers the ball to the shooter's shot pocket, timed so the shooter catches in rhythm.
4. Shooter **plants the left (inside) pivot foot**, catches on balance, squares to the basket, shoots on balance, and **lands on balance**.
5. After shooting, the shooter **freezes the follow-through** and holds position ("shoot and stay") to track long rebounds.
6. **Rebounder** (R) retrieves the ball and speed dribbles (preferably with the weak hand) to the **end of the passing line**.
7. **Passer** sprints to the **end of the shooting line**.
8. **Shooter** becomes the new **rebounder**.
9. After mastering left-to-right, repeat the drill **right-to-left**: shooter now pushes off the **right foot** on the V-cut and pivots on the right foot on the catch.

## Coaching Points
- Passers must work on delivering the ball **to the shot pocket, in rhythm** — "If players can't make good passes in practice, how are they going to make good passes in a game with defenders harassing them?" [S5, p.2]
- Rebounders: develop the "get after it" mentality — **hands up, hustle, run down the ball**.
- Insist on the "shoot and stay" principle: shooter does not follow the shot; staying allows tracking of long rebounds and positioning for outlet passes or defensive transition.
- Right-handed and left-handed players align themselves differently; watch each player's dominant hand alignment on the rise.
- **Progression for advanced groups**: Add a screener. The shooter comes off the screen and dumps the ball to the screener instead of shooting — teaching the screener to "think screen and score." Screeners often find themselves open after a defender is rubbed off by a good shooter. [S5, p.3]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the V-cut footwork without shooting; focus on inside-foot push and plant.
2. **Intermediate**: Full drill at moderate speed — shooting from left-to-right first, then right-to-left.
3. **Advanced**: Add a screener; shooter reads whether to shoot or dump to the screener based on defensive pressure.

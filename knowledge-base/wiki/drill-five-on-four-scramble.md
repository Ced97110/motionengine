# Five-on-Four Scramble

## Objective
Train the four-man defense to challenge shots, rotate to open men, and protect the three-second lane under the pressure of a numerical disadvantage, while simultaneously training 5 offensive players to get a good shot quickly.

## Setup
- 5 offensive players: 3 out (perimeter), 2 in (post/elbow) — a three-players-out, two-players-in formation
- 4 defenders in a diamond formation at the primary end
- 1 additional defender at the **opposite end of the floor**
- Full court

## Execution
1. Offense runs its set with a **maximum of 5 passes and 3 dribbles** before shooting — they have the advantage and should find a good shot quickly.
2. Defense (4 players) works on:
   - Challenging shots
   - Rotating to open men
   - Protecting the three-second lane
3. **As soon as** the offense scores OR the defense gets the ball, the **fifth defender (at the opposite end) is now in play** and can receive a pass.
4. This forces 1 and 2 (offensive guards) to **get back on defense immediately**.
5. Now play **5-on-5** at that end.
6. Then come back and play **5-on-5 at the original end**.
7. Stop after that second 5v5 possession. **"Tell the defense to keep coming."**

## Coaching Points
- **Offense**: "You have the advantage — find a good shot in 5 passes or 3 dribbles."
- **Defense**: Communicate early; the diamond calls who takes the ball, who takes the nearest corner, who sinks to the paint.
- **Transition key**: 1 and 2 must read the moment the defense gets the ball or a score happens and sprint to half court immediately.
- The fifth defender entering creates a full 5v5 scramble — no one is safe, everyone must transition.
- This drill is primarily a **transition defense drill** disguised as an offensive drill.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the initial 4v5; identify who takes what spot in the diamond.
2. **Intermediate**: Live 5v4; fifth defender sprints full court when triggered.
3. **Advanced**: Full sequence as described — 5v4 → 5v5 at second end → 5v5 back at first end.

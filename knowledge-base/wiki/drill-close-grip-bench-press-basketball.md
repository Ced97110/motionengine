# Close-Grip Bench Press (Basketball Strength Exercise)

## Objective
Develop triceps brachii and pectoralis major strength by pressing with a narrower grip, building the explosive upper-body power needed for shot-blocking, ball protection against defenders, and chest-pass force.

## Setup
- 2 players (1 lifter + 1 spotter — **mandatory**)
- Flat weight bench with barbell rack
- Appropriate weight plates
- **Safety Tip**: A spotter must be present

## Execution
1. Lie flat on the weight bench with knees bent at 90 degrees, feet flat on the floor.
2. Place hands **less than shoulder-width apart** (closer to the midchest region) and wrap thumbs fully around the bar.
3. Retract the scapula to create a stable pressing platform. Extend the arms to lift the barbell to approximately eye level.
4. Slowly lower the barbell under control by bending the elbows, maintaining upper arms at a **45-degree angle** to the body. The bar descends to touch at midchest (nipple) level. **Do not bounce the bar off the chest.**
5. With a slow, controlled exhalation, press the bar away from the chest. Keep the lower back flat on the bench.
6. Extend the arms while maintaining the 45-degree upper-arm angle as you return to the starting position.
7. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- Grip is inside shoulder width — this is the key distinguisher from flat bench press
- "45-degree upper arm" rule still applies — same as flat bench
- Greater triceps activation than standard bench press — feel the difference in the back of the arm
- "No bouncing" — the closer grip creates additional wrist stress if the bar is bounced
- Spotter hands ready throughout — closer grip can be more unstable for newer lifters
- Lower back flat; no arch

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master standard bench press form before switching to close grip
2. **Intermediate**: Close-grip bench press with moderate load (8–12 reps)
3. **Advanced**: Increase load progressively; can combine with triceps-focused pressing superset

## Muscles Worked
- **Primary**: Triceps brachii, pectoralis major
- **Secondary**: Pectoralis minor, anterior deltoid, anconeus

## Basketball Transfer
The close-grip press isolates the triceps more than standard bench, developing the explosive extension strength needed to block shots, protect the ball from defenders reaching for steals, and deliver powerful chest passes. Strong triceps are the "finisher" of every overhead arm extension — shooting, passing, and finishing at the rim all depend on them. [S2, pp.86-87]

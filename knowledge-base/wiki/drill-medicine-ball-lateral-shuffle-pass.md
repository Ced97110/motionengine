# Medicine-Ball Lateral Shuffle/Pass

## Objective
Improve quickness and elastic strength through lateral shuffling combined with continuous medicine-ball chest passing — directly transfers to defensive lateral quickness and ball-handling under pressure. [S8, p.147]

## Setup
- 2 players facing each other
- Any flat surface or court
- 1 medicine ball (lighter ball = more lateral distance traveled)

## Execution
1. Two partners face each other in an athletic stance.
2. Both begin **shuffling laterally** in the same direction (one player goes right, the other mirrors going left).
3. While shuffling, continuously **chest pass** the medicine ball back and forth along the predetermined route.
4. Upon reaching the target distance, **reverse direction** and return — continue passing throughout.
5. Lighter ball = farther lateral travel; adjust ball weight to match the intended lateral distance.

## Coaching Points
- Stay in a **defensive stance** throughout — feet shoulder-width, knees bent, hips low
- "No crossover steps" — true shuffle steps only; feet should not cross
- Pass on the shuffle — don't stop to pass; maintain movement throughout
- Keep elbows in and wrists snapping through each pass for force transfer

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Predictable direction (set distance and return); medium ball weight; moderate pace
2. **Intermediate**: Increase pace and ball weight; extend lateral distance
3. **Advanced**: One partner becomes the **leader** — free to change direction at any moment; the other reacts and mirrors. [S8, p.147] This converts the drill from a predictable to a reactive (choice reaction) format directly mimicking on-court defensive footwork.

# Backward Roll Over Shoulder (Tumbling Drill)

## Objective
Develop total-body agility and kinesthetic awareness by performing a controlled backward roll over the shoulder and returning to a standing position. [S8, p.122]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Soft grass or gymnastics mat
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Bend the legs and start to sit on the ground behind you.
3. As you are about to make contact with the ground, roll back over the left shoulder.
4. Continue to roll and come back up to your feet.
5. Perform backward rolls over **both shoulders**. [S8, p.122]

## Coaching Points
- Protect the neck by rolling over the shoulder, not straight back onto the spine
- Tuck the chin to the chest as you initiate the backward sit
- Keep the body curled tightly — a loose body slows the roll and increases injury risk
- Practice both sides equally to prevent asymmetrical movement patterns
- Land in a balanced two-point stance — immediately ready to react

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow controlled seated roll on a mat
2. **Intermediate**: Full standing backward roll as described
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Add a sprint in any direction before or after
   - React to a visual cue (e.g., run to a cone) immediately after rolling
   - Add a sport-specific basketball skill (e.g., catch and shoot) after tumbling [S8, p.122]

# Half-Spin Dribble Maneuver (Drill #31)

## Objective
Develop the half-spin dribble — a partial reverse spin that sends the ball back in the original direction, catching defenders who over-commit to the spin.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- 4 lines at one baseline
- **Tape 2×2 foot (.6×.6 m) boxes** at midcourt and ~20 feet (6 m) from each baseline
- 1 basketball per player
- Duration: 1 minute

## Execution
1. Players line up in four columns at the baseline.
2. Dribble to each box and execute the **half-spin dribble**:
   - **Control the ball with the right hand** through the half-spin by leaving the right hand **cupped over the top of the ball**.
   - As they plant the **right foot** and raise the **left foot**, move the hand **beside** the ball (not on top) and **push the dribble back in the same direction they came from**.
   - The left side should be close to the ball, protecting it.
   - **Explode back in the original direction** after the half-spin.
3. Dribble **down and back with the right hand** first, then **down and back with the left hand**.
4. At the far end, execute a **reverse (or front) pivot** and return, using the half-spin at each box.
5. **Jump-stop** about 10 feet (3 m) from the next teammate and deliver a **chest or bounce pass**.
6. All players repeat the sequence.

## Options
- **Race format**: Guards vs. post players, or first team vs. second team.

## Advanced Progression
1. **Alternating dual-ball**: Alternate bouncing each ball while driving.
2. **Simultaneous dual-ball**: Both balls touch hands and bounce at the same time.

## Coaching Points
- **The key distinction from the full spin**: In the half-spin, the dribbler does NOT complete the 180° turn — instead the ball is redirected back the original way. Defenders who read a full spin are fooled.
- **Hand position cue**: Right hand goes **over the top** of the ball during the turn; as the foot plants, **slide hand beside** the ball to redirect the dribble back.
- **Eyes downcourt**: Eyes stay on the downcourt area — **not** on the ball.
- **Post-spin explosion**: Explode back in the original direction immediately after the half-spin.
- **Head/shoulder fakes**: Use fakes before the move to maximize deception.
- **Fingerpads**: Always dribble with the first pads of the fingers.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary — practice the half-turn and ball redirect before adding forward motion.
2. **Intermediate**: Full-court bilateral with box stops, pivot, and pass at end.
3. **Advanced**: Dual-ball; combine half-spin and full spin at alternating boxes to teach decision-making.

# Bag Weave (Bag Drill)

## Objective
Improve flexibility, high-knee action, and quickness of foot movements using a multi-directional sprint/shuffle/backpedal pattern through a row of bags. [S8, p.115]

## Setup
- 1 player
- 4 agility bags (or equivalent obstacles) set in a straight line, each spaced approximately 3-4 feet apart
- Open grass or court area

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance outside the first bag.
2. Sprint forward until you are in front of the first bag.
3. Shuffle your feet to the right (laterally) until you reach the space between the bags — **do not cross your feet** when moving sideways.
4. Backpedal quickly until you are one step past the bag.
5. Repeat this sprint → shuffle → backpedal pattern through all four bags.
6. Finish when you reach the outside of the last bag. [S8, p.115]

## Coaching Points
- Keep shoulders square to the front at all times — "chest always faces forward"
- Stay in a two-point (athletic) stance throughout the entire drill
- Keep the head up, use good running form even when moving sideways or backward
- No crossing of feet during the lateral shuffle — this is the most common error
- Move as fast as possible; this is a speed drill, not a form drill at half speed

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the pattern slowly to learn the footwork sequence before adding speed
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed execution as described
3. **Advanced**: Add a defensive closeout or catch-and-shoot action at the end to make it basketball-specific

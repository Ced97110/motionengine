# Running Front Flip (Step 9)

## Objective
Develop explosive rotational power, total-body agility, and kinesthetic awareness through a forward aerial somersault with a running start, building toward the standing front flip.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Flat open surface (grass preferred for safety)
- No equipment required; padding (mattress, cushions) strongly recommended for beginners
- Sufficient run-up space (5–10 yards)

## Execution
1. Take a moderate-speed run-up of several steps to build forward momentum — **do not run too far or too fast**, as this will sacrifice height for the rotation.
2. Perform a **block jump** (two-foot simultaneous plant/jump from a running step) on the floor with both feet to convert horizontal momentum into vertical lift.
3. Jump up hard, simultaneously **throwing your hands downward** and **pulling your curled upper body down at the hips** to initiate rotation.
4. As feet leave the floor, **snap your knees up tightly to your chest**. The tighter the tuck, the faster you rotate.
5. As you rotate over, **quickly whip your legs out beneath you** in preparation for landing.
6. Land on the balls of your feet with **slightly bent knees** to absorb impact shock.
7. Take a few steps forward (or backward if under-rotated) to steady yourself if needed.

## Coaching Points
- "Keep run-up moderate speed — you need height, not horizontal distance."
- The block jump is the engine of the flip; a poor block kills the rotation before it starts.
- Tight tuck = fast rotation. Loose tuck = under-rotation and dangerous landing.
- Land on the balls of the feet, never heels — knees absorb, don't lock.
- Fear is normal; use padding to build psychological confidence before removing it.
- Practice on grass before attempting on hard surfaces.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Use thick padding (mattress) beneath you — partly safety, mostly psychological to counter the primal fear of crashing. Progress to cushions, then pillows, then bare grass.
2. **Intermediate**: Perform the flip with a 3–4 step run-up on grass without padding.
3. **Advanced**: Reduce the number of run-up steps progressively — fewer steps forces the body to produce the explosive power required. This bridges toward the standing front flip (Master Step).

## Common Mistakes
1. **Running too fast** → sacrifices height; keep run-up at moderate pace
2. **Loose tuck** → under-rotation; snap knees tight to chest immediately at takeoff
3. **Landing with straight knees** → injury risk; always absorb with bent knees on balls of feet
4. **Looking down during rotation** → disrupts spatial orientation; trust the rotation

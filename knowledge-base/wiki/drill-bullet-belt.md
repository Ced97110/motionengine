# Bullet Belt

## Objective
Teach quick transitions in speed and enhance stride frequency of acceleration by using a Velcro-release belt that breaks free once the athlete generates sufficient force.

## Setup
- 2 players: runner and hold partner
- A commercially available "bullet belt" — a waist belt attached to the partner via a Velcro strip that breaks when enough tension is applied
- Open flat surface

## Execution
1. The runner puts on the bullet belt; the partner holds the connected end.
2. The runner begins accelerating while the partner resists with the belt.
3. As the runner continues to accelerate and applies increasing force, the **Velcro strip breaks**, releasing the runner instantly.
4. The athlete explodes out of the release and continues to sprint.
5. Release techniques used by the partner include:
   - **Rip method**: Partner actively rips the belt at a chosen moment
   - **Pop method**: Partner applies a sudden pop/jerk to break the Velcro at peak resistance
6. Rest and repeat 4–6 reps.

## Coaching Points
- This drill automates the contrast release — the belt breaks only when the athlete produces enough force, rewarding effort with freedom.
- The unpredictability of the release moment (especially in the pop method) trains reactive acceleration.
- Athlete must continue accelerating through and past the release — do not anticipate and relax.
- Works on the same principle as [[drill-partner-assisted-let-gos]] — contrast from held to free.
- Safety note: ensure the Velcro strength matches the athlete's level — too heavy a Velcro strip can cause the athlete to strain.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light Velcro tension, rip release (partner-controlled)
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate Velcro, pop release at peak effort
3. **Advanced**: Variable Velcro tension, athlete does not know when release occurs — maximum reactive acceleration training

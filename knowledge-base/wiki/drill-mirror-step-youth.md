# Mirror Step

## Objective
Consolidate all individual defensive footwork (advance, retreat, swing, overplay/slide) into one reactive drill — defender mirrors every offensive move with the correct defensive counter.

## Setup
- 5 attacker-defender pairs lined up along the baseline, each pair in their own lane
- Attackers move parallel to the baseline
- Defenders face them and mirror using proper defensive steps
- 1 basketball per pair (or no ball for footwork-only version)

## Execution
1. Line players up across the half-court baseline: attackers on one side, defenders directly in front.
2. Defenders begin in front-foot-to-pivot-foot stance or front-foot-to-free-foot stance.
3. Call out the attacking move; defenders react with the correct defensive step:
   - Jab step → **Retreat step**
   - Any dribble → **Overplay slide**; direction change → slide back into overplay
4. Begin with one move at a time — analyze and discuss before continuing.
5. Progress to continuous combinations with one move flowing into the next.
6. **Option**: Allow attackers to choose their own moves freely — defenders must read and react without being told.

## Coaching Points
- "Match the move with the correct step — no guessing"
- Jab step = retreat step (never lean in to the jab)
- Dribble = overplay slide (nose on the ball)
- Spin move = swing step (step wide, build a new stance)
- Eyes always on belt buckle, head steady
- This drill is the diagnostic: if a defender is confused here, go back to individual step drills first

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach calls each move; one move at a time; no dribble
2. **Intermediate**: Continuous sequence of two or three moves; defender must transition smoothly
3. **Advanced**: Attacker chooses freely at game speed; defender reacts without cueing

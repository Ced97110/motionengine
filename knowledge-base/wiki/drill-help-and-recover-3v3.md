# 3-on-3 Help-and-Recover Drill (Fratello)

## Objective
Train three defenders to execute the full baseline rotation AND two versions of middle-penetration coverage: help-and-recover (stunt-and-return) and full rotation (trap-and-rotate).

## Setup
- 3 offensive players: both wings (1 and 3) and top of the key (2)
- 3 defenders: X1 guards 1 (right wing), X2 guards 2 (top), X3 guards 3 (left wing)
- Ball can start at any position; shown starting on right wing (1)
- Half-court; play is LIVE

## Execution — Baseline Rotation
1. X1 guards 1 and forces baseline drive
2. **X3 rotates off 3 and cuts off 1's baseline drive** (baseline help)
3. **X2 rotates off 2 and picks up 3** on the weak side
4. **X1 rotates off the baseline back to 2** at the top of the key
5. Play continues live until defense secures possession or offense scores

## Execution — Middle Help-and-Recover
1. Ball penetrates to the middle
2. **X2 stunts** — steps into the lane to stop penetration, then **recovers to 2**
3. X1 stays with 1; X3 is in position to help X2 on 2's penetration, then recovers to 3
- Key principle: closest help defender stunts and returns; no one fully leaves their man

## Execution — Full Rotation
1. Ball penetrates to the middle
2. **X2 rotates off 2 and fully traps** the ball
3. **X3 rotates to cover 2** (X2's abandoned man)
4. **X1 rotates to 3** to cover X3's man
5. The only uncovered pass is backward — all defenders stay below the ball line

## Coaching Points
- "Stunts help — rotations trap. Know which one we're in."
- Defense goes off at end of possession; new defense comes on (do NOT send next group in on offense)
- Can make competitive by keeping score
- Always sub fresh defenders — defense chooses the matchup
- "Every rotation is a communication: call the screen, call the drive, call the help"

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Teach baseline rotation alone before adding middle coverage options
2. **Intermediate:** Baseline + help-and-recover on middle penetration
3. **Advanced:** All three reads live; coach can call "stunt" or "rotate" to force specific coverage

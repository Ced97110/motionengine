# Forward Roll–Backward Roll Combination (Tumbling Drill)

## Objective
Develop total-body agility and kinesthetic awareness by chaining a forward roll directly into a backward hand push-off roll in one continuous movement. [S8, p.124]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Soft grass or gymnastics mat
- No additional equipment needed

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Perform a forward roll to your feet (see [[drill-forward-roll-over-shoulder]]).
3. Immediately upon landing, go into a backward roll with a hands push-off (see [[drill-backward-roll-hand-push-off]]).
4. Note: You may start and end the drill on your knees to reduce amplitude and difficulty for beginners. [S8, p.124]

## Coaching Points
- The key is the seamless transition — no stopping between the forward and backward roll
- The forward roll's landing position feeds directly into the backward roll's sit-back — stay tucked
- "Roll, plant, roll back" — one fluid motion, not two separate drills
- Knees-start modification is acceptable for beginners to reduce height and injury risk
- All complex variation rules apply: add sprint, visual cue, or sport-specific skill after completion

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Start and end on knees, or practice each roll separately before combining
2. **Intermediate**: Full standing combination as described
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Add a sprint in any direction before or after
   - React to a visual cue to a cone or ball after the final roll
   - Add a basketball skill (catch a pass, catch and shoot) after standing [S8, p.124]

- [[concept-ankle-proprioception]] — multi-phase body awareness and balance

# Upper-Body Shuffles

## Objective
Improve quickness in the upper-body pushing musculature through rapid hand-position changes in the push-up position. [S8, p.162]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor
- No equipment

## Execution
1. Begin in the up position of a push-up, hands placed directly underneath each shoulder.
2. Shuffle one hand up and one hand down as you lower into the push-up.
3. Explosively push yourself up while exchanging hand positions in the air.
4. Upon landing, the hand positions have reversed — repeat.

## Coaching Points
- Focus on minimal ground-contact time — "get your hands off the floor."
- Core stays rigid throughout; no hip drop.
- The up-down shuffle (one hand forward, one back) trains asymmetrical upper-body reactivity specific to reaching and passing.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Practice the up-down shuffle slowly before adding explosive push-off.
2. **Intermediate**: Full execution at speed — explode off the floor and shuffle in the air.
3. **Advanced (Wide Shuffle)**: Start with hands shoulder-width apart. Shuffle the hands out wider than the shoulders as you drop into the push-up. Explosively push up and shuffle the hands back underneath the shoulders while in the air.

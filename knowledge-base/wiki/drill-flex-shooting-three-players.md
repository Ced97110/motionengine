# Flex Offense Shooting Drill — Three Players

## Objective
Practice the flex offense's multiple perimeter-screen reads (vertical screen pop or back screen) and the post roll, with three offensive players and live passing options.

## Setup
- 3 offensive players: 5 (post), 2 (guard/wing), 3 (wing)
- 2 coaches as passers (at the elbows or wing areas), each with a ball
- Half court; players start in flex positions

## Execution
1. **5 and 2** each have a ball and pass to the coaches to initiate the drill
2. **2 and 3** move together for a specific cut based on the chosen read:
   - **Option A (Vertical Screen Read)**: 3 exploits 5's screen and pops high to the wing; 2 cuts to the baseline and fills the opposite corner
   - **Option B (Back Screen Read)**: 3 goes around 2 and back-screens for 5; 2 cuts across the baseline to the opposite corner
3. Coaches can pass to any of the three shooters based on who is open:
   - Pass to 5 using the back screen (Option B)
   - Pass to 2 in the corner (who has advantage on the defender)
   - Pass to 3 who pops out after screening
   - Pass to 5 who **rolls to the basket** after the screen for 3
4. All players shoot when they receive the ball; grab their own rebound; rotate to the next position

## Coaching Points
- "3, decide your cut based on what the defense gives you — commit and go"
- 5's screen must be set with a wide base before 3 uses it — no moving screens
- 2's baseline cut should be sharp and wide, filling the far corner properly
- The post roll is a live option — coaches must be willing to throw it to 5 cutting
- All three shooters must be in shooting stance before receiving the pass
- Rotate the coach passers to simulate different angles and speeds

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach calls which option (A or B) before the action; no defender
2. **Intermediate**: Player reads and chooses the cut; coach passes to the best look
3. **Advanced**: Add a defender on 3 to create a live read; roll option stays open for 5

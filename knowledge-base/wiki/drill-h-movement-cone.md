# H-Movement Cone Drill

## Objective
Improve multi-directional agility, body-position transitions, and footwork by moving through a 6-cone H-shaped pattern combining sprint, backpedal, and shuffle.

## Setup
- 1 player
- 6 cones in an H-shape:
  - Cone 1: starting line (left side)
  - Cone 2: 5 yards in front of cone 1 (left column, center)
  - Cone 3: 5 yards to the right of cone 2 (right column, center — crossbar of H)
  - Cone 4: 5 yards below cone 2 (left column, bottom)
  - Cone 5: 5 yards above cone 2 (left column, top)
  - Cone 6: 5 yards to the right of cone 1 (right column, bottom)

## Execution
1. Start at cone 2.
2. **Sprint** from cone 2 to cone 4.
3. **Backpedal** from cone 4 to cone 2.
4. **Shuffle** from cone 2 to cone 3.
5. **Sprint** from cone 3 to cone 5.
6. **Backpedal** from cone 5 to cone 6.
7. **Sprint** from cone 6 to cone 3.
8. **Shuffle** from cone 3 to cone 2.
9. **Backpedal** from cone 2 to cone 1.

## Coaching Points
- Drive hips forward at each transition from backpedal to sprint
- Shuffle crossbar: low athletic stance, no foot crossover
- Backpedal to start: this final segment is a fatigue test — maintain proper form
- Call out each movement phase during practice to build pattern recognition

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk all 9 segments, then jog.
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed; time each rep.
3. **Advanced**: Three consecutive reps with 30-second rest; compete against teammates.

- [[basketball-acceleration-and-deceleration]] — sprint/backpedal transitions with lateral shuffles mirror on-court movement
- [[defensive-transition-principles]] — backpedal-to-sprint transitions are the core of defensive recovery

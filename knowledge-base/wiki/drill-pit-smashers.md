# Pit Smashers

## Objective
Develop the habit of driving out of the starting position aggressively and powerfully at a low angle, promoting full hip, knee, and ankle extension (triple extension) through the start.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Starting blocks (or a marked starting line)
- A high-jump pit placed approximately **4 feet (1.2 meters)** from the starting line
- Alternative: a sand/long-jump pit

## Execution
1. Assume your starting position in the blocks (or crouch start).
2. On signal, **drive explosively forward** at a low angle, projecting the body into the high-jump pit.
3. The upper body should **slide onto the top of the pit** while the lead knee strikes the side of the pit.
4. The drill forces a **low drive angle** — athletes who pop up too early will overshoot or miss the pit entirely.
5. Reset and repeat 4–6 reps.

### Complex Variation
- Perform the drill with a **sand/long-jump pit** instead of a high-jump pit for the same mechanical benefit with slightly different feel.

## Coaching Points
- The pit acts as instant feedback — proper mechanics (low angle, body projection) produce a correct landing.
- "Drive into the pit, not over it" — the objective is to project the torso forward and low.
- Full triple extension (hip, knee, ankle) must occur before the body leaves the ground — lazy extension = short start.
- This drill is excellent for athletes who habitually pop upright too quickly on their first step.
- Works best early in a speed session after a thorough warm-up and before fatigue affects mechanics.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standing start (no blocks), slow-moderate drive, 4 reps
2. **Intermediate**: Crouch start, full-effort drive, 4–6 reps
3. **Advanced**: Full block start, maximum effort, competition angle assessment

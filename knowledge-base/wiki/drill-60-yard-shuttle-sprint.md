# 60-Yard Shuttle Sprint

## Objective
Improve agility and conditioning through progressively longer sprint-touch-return intervals covering 60 total yards.

## Setup
- 1+ players
- Four parallel lines: starting line, lines at 5 yards, 10 yards, and 15 yards
- Use court markings or cones/tape

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance.
2. Sprint forward 5 yards (4.6 m) to the first line, touch it with either hand, turn and return to the start line.
3. Sprint forward 10 yards (9 m) to the second line, touch it with either hand, turn and return to the start line.
4. Sprint forward 15 yards (14 m) to the third line, touch it with either hand, turn and sprint back through the start line.

<!-- DIAGRAM: Three parallel lines at 5, 10, and 15 yards from start; athlete sprints out-and-back to each in sequence. [S8, p.83] -->

## Coaching Points
- Maintain maximum effort on each leg — this is a conditioning drill as well as an agility drill
- Stay low on direction changes — "sit into the turn"
- Touch with either hand but always make clean contact with the line
- Time the drill and track progress weekly

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Rest 2:1 work-to-rest ratio between reps
2. **Intermediate**: 1:1 work-to-rest ratio
3. **Advanced**: Reduce rest; add biomotor skill variations on the return legs

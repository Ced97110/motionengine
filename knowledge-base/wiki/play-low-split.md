# Low Split

## Overview
A 2-3 zone play from a 1-3-1 set that relies on spacing and smart passing in tight space. The play passes into the heart of the zone by flashing two post players to ball-side simultaneously, forcing X5 to choose between guarding a mid-post and a baseline player — resulting in an open layup, close shot, or drawn foul. [S4, pp.42-43]

## Formation
1-3-1 set. 1 at top, wings (2 and 3) positioned between the wing and the corner (low wings), 4 at the free-throw line (high post), 5 in the short corner.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Wing Entry and Post Flash
- 1 dribbles to one side and passes to the low wing on that side (e.g., to 2).
- On the catch, 4 flashes from the free-throw line to the **ball-side mid-post**.
- Simultaneously, 5 flashes across from the short corner to the **ball-side baseline**.
- X3 (low zone defender) is forced to play on-ball defense on 2 (wing), making it very difficult for X5 to guard both 4 and 5. [S4, p.42]

### Phase 2: Interior Pass and Finish
- 2 passes to either 4 (mid-post) or 5 (baseline) — whichever is open.
- On catching the interior pass, the post player quickly evaluates options:
  - Pass to the other post player inside
  - Turn and shoot
  - Kick out to the perimeter
  - Take one dribble and finish at the rim [S4, p.42]

### Phase 3: Reset if Denied
- If the interior pass is not available, 2 rotates back to 1, who dribbles across and passes to 3 on the opposite wing.
- The two post players reset on the **opposite side** of the key and the action repeats.
- This continues until the offense gets a good shot. [S4, p.43]

## Key Coaching Points
- **Fake passes are crucial.** Players must use fake passes to force defenders to move and create passing lanes in tight space. "Create small pockets of space to pass to." [S4, p.43]
- This play creates great opportunities to draw fouls in the paint — practice free throws, as players will earn many. [S4, p.43]
- Wings must **not force the interior pass**. If it's not open, rotate and try the other side. [S4, p.43]
- The wing player must be positioned **low enough** to force the low zone defender (X3) to play on-ball, preventing the high zone defender from simply sliding down. If the wing is too high, X3 can sag and help. [S4, p.43]
- Post players (4 and 5) need good hands and the ability to make smart passes under pressure. [S4, p.42]

## Counters
- If the zone sags to prevent interior passes, the wing should kick back to 1 for a perimeter shot.
- If X5 commits fully to the baseline player, the mid-post catch leads directly to a high-percentage shot.

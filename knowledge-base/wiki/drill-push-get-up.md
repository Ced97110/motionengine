# Push Get-Up

## Objective
Build total-body explosive power and coordination by transitioning from a pushup position to standing in one explosive movement, integrating upper-body push with lower-body knee drive.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Small flat surface (no equipment needed)
- Can be performed as a warm-up, finisher, or between sets of any chain drill

## Execution
1. Begin in a standard pushup position — palms flat, body aligned, legs extended.
2. Dip down into the bottom of a pushup.
3. Explode up off the floor, simultaneously bringing your knees in and under your body.
4. Use the upward momentum to land in a standing position (finish upright).
5. Squat back down, place hands on the floor, and return to pushup position to repeat.

## Coaching Points
- "Explode, don't crawl" — the transition from floor to standing must be one fluid, powerful movement
- The knee drive must be simultaneous with the hand push — not sequential
- Can be performed rhythmically for higher reps — do not pause between reps
- Works well as a preliminary drill for Aztec pushups — builds the hip and abdominal power needed to get the hips high [S9, p.106]
- Also useful as a recovery movement — "the coolest possible way to get back on your feet after performing your pushups" [S9, p.106]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Step one leg at a time under the body to stand (walking burpee)
2. **Intermediate**: Both legs come in simultaneously — jump-to-stand
3. **Advanced**: Perform rhythmically with a clap pushup before each get-up

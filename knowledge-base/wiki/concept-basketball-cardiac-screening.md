# Cardiac Screening for Basketball Athletes

## Summary
Cardiovascular disorders are the leading cause of sudden death in young athletes during sports and exercise. Basketball players — particularly male athletes of African descent — face a higher risk of certain cardiac anomalies that can cause sudden cardiac death, likely related to the extended high-level aerobic demands of the sport. A thorough pre-participation cardiac screening that goes beyond the minimum Bruce Protocol stress test is strongly recommended for all competitive basketball programs [S10, pp.140-142].

## When to Use
- Pre-participation evaluation (PPE) for all new team members
- Any time a player presents with exercise intolerance, chest pain, inappropriate shortness of breath, syncope, or palpitations
- When screening taller players for connective tissue disorders (Marfan's)
- Following a near-miss sudden cardiac event

## Key Principles
1. **Cardiovascular disorders are the #1 cause of sudden death in young athletes** — this is not a low-probability event in basketball [S10, p.140]
2. **Male athletes of African descent have higher risk** for certain cardiac anomalies causing sudden cardiac death [S10, p.140]
3. **The Bruce Protocol alone is insufficient** for elite program evaluation — add cardiac echo (ideally stress echo) at minimum
4. **HCM can develop slowly** — a normal initial screening does not guarantee permanent clearance; rescan when new symptoms appear
5. **Screening benefit requires follow-up capacity** — cardiac screening is only valuable if equivocal findings can be interpreted and additional testing accessed [S10, p.142]

## Cardiac Conditions Affecting Basketball Players

### Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)
- **Prevalence**: ~1:500 in general population; most common cause of sport-associated sudden cardiac death in USA (~50% of cases)
- **Pathology**: Abnormal LV/septal wall thickening → pathologic outflow obstruction OR electrical instability/arrhythmia from myofibrillar disarray
- **Genetics**: Autosomal dominant; varying penetrance — many gene carriers never develop pathologic condition
- **Physical exam**: Harsh systolic murmur at lower left sternal border, increases with Valsalva or squat-to-stand; does NOT radiate to carotids; NOTE: some patients have no murmur
- **EKG**: Left axis deviation or LV hypertrophy
- **Definitive**: Echocardiogram or cardiac MRI
- **Challenge**: Develops slowly over years, often after initial PPE screening [S10, p.140]

### Marfan's Syndrome
- **Prevalence**: Connective tissue genetic disorder; associated with tall stature → screen all taller basketball players
- **Risk**: Progressive aortic root widening → rupture
- **Diagnosis**: Revised Ghent criteria (Box 3) — systemic score ≥7 points indicates systemic involvement; includes wrist/thumb sign, pectus deformity, hindfoot deformity, dural ectasia, scoliosis, skin striae, etc. [S10, p.141]
- **Management**: May play with close cardiology monitoring for aortic root dilation status

### Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy (ARVC)
- **Geography**: Most common cause of sudden cardiac death in athletes in Italy
- **Pathology**: Desmosome unlinking → focal myocardial weakness + fibrofatty deposition → arrhythmogenic source
- **Basketball participation**: Generally **not compatible** with high-level basketball [S10, p.141]

### Other Cardiac Conditions
- Anomalous coronary arteries
- Myocarditis
- Various cardiomyopathies
- **Congenital long QT syndrome**: Inherited rhythm disorder; may present as syncope or sudden death with exercise
- **Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) syndrome**: Accessory conduction pathway; exercise-triggered tachyarrhythmia risk

**Common presentations of all the above**: Exercise intolerance, chest pain, inappropriate shortness of breath, syncope, palpitations, or sudden death [S10, p.141]

## NBA Cardiac Screening Protocol
Cardiac screening at the NBA level is performed by a cardiologist and includes:
- Standard vital signs
- Auscultation of heart sounds
- Resting electrocardiogram (ECG)
- Treadmill stress transthoracic echocardiogram [S10, p.146]

## European Elite Recommendation
For European elite basketball programs, beyond the Bruce Protocol stress test:
- Cardiac echo (stress echo preferred)
- CBC, full biochemistry, vitamin levels
- EBV, CMV, HIV tests
- Urine analysis [S10, pp.126-127]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Bruce Protocol only** → This is a minimum standard for competition eligibility, not adequate for elite-level cardiac safety evaluation
2. **One-time screening = permanent clearance** → HCM develops slowly; maintain surveillance when players develop new symptoms regardless of prior normal screens
3. **Skipping the cardiologist** → Echo and stress echo interpretation requires specialist expertise; team physicians should not interpret these studies alone
4. **Missing Marfan's in tall players** → Apply Ghent criteria systematically to all players of unusually tall stature; this is a basketball-specific population risk

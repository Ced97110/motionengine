# Butt-Kick Jump (Power Jump Chain Step 5)

## Objective
Develop hip flexor conditioning, leg speed, and the foundational tuck mechanics needed for advanced tuck jumps — the first drill in the jump chain with objective mid-air performance criteria.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface (no equipment needed)
- Shoulder-width stance

## Execution
1. Stand with feet approximately shoulder-width apart, body flexed for action
2. Dip down into a squat (as for squat jump, Step 2)
3. Explode upward using full lower-body power and total-body spring
4. At the peak of the jump, explosively bend both legs, driving heels up and back to kick your own glutes
5. After the heel-kick, throw legs back down for landing preparation
6. Land softly on the balls of both feet
7. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- **"This is the first drill with objective performance criteria"** — If you don't jump high enough or move your legs fast enough, you physically cannot kick your own butt [S9, p.20]
- **Knees pull slightly forward** — Even though the goal is heel-to-glute, the knees lift slightly forward due to hip flexor activation; this is intentional conditioning for harder tucks ahead
- **Leg speed is critical** — Heels must be driven up AND back down fast enough to land safely; slow athletes will struggle at first
- **Arms stay involved** — Arm swing helps generate momentum; removing arms (hands behind head) makes this significantly harder
- Watch for: athletes who jump high but can't get heels to glutes — diagnosis: insufficient hamstring contraction speed; prescription: unilateral variation first
- Watch for: landing with legs still bent — legs must be thrown back down before ground contact

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (regression)**: Unilateral butt-kick jumps — jump and kick with only ONE leg; reduces speed requirement
2. **Intermediate**: Bilateral butt-kick jump with arms assisting
3. **Advanced**: Place hands behind head (NOT pulling neck); arms removed from momentum generation forces higher jump and faster leg action

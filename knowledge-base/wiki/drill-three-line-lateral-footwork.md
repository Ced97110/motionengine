# Three-Line Lateral Footwork Drill

## Objective
Build foot-mind coordination and lateral quickness by moving both feet rapidly through spaces between three taped lines without crossing over.

## Setup
- Three parallel lines on the court, spaced roughly 12-18 inches apart (made with tape)
- Any number of players working simultaneously in lines
- Court area: baseline to elbow, or any flat surface

## Execution
1. Players begin with both feet to **one side** of the three lines.
2. Step the **inside foot** into the first space between lines, then bring the second foot into the same space.
3. Both feet pass between each set of lines.
4. Drill is completed when both feet are **outside the third line** on the far side.
5. Player immediately reverses direction and repeats going the other way.
6. Players **must not crossover** — stay laterally; feet should be as quick as possible.
7. Perform at **maximum speed for 6-10 seconds** before fatigue slows movement.

## Coaching Points
- "Pick up your feet — don't drag them!"
- No crossover steps — this is lateral movement, mimicking defensive slides.
- Work at maximum speed for 6-10 seconds; the neuromuscular training benefit is in the speed, not the duration. [S7, p.325]
- Keep an athletic base: slight bend in knees, shoulders back.
- Do these early in practice when the body is fresh to ensure maximum speed of movement.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow walk-through to learn the foot pattern; focus on no crossovers.
2. **Intermediate**: Full speed; add a competition (first to complete 5 round-trips).
3. **Advanced**: Narrow the spacing between lines (increases demand on foot speed); add a reaction command — coach says "go" and player must freeze then restart.

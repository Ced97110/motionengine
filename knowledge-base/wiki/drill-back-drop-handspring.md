# Back-Drop Handspring (Front Flip Chain — Step 5)

## Objective
Teach the explosive entry into a handspring and the first portion of the forward spin-out — the critical transitional step between rolling drills and the full front handspring.

## Setup
- **Players**: 1 (solo)
- **Equipment**: Padding (pillows, cushions, mattress) is strongly recommended, especially for beginners
- **Space**: Open floor area with sufficient padding

## Execution
1. Take a single step to build momentum, hands raised overhead. Kick off with the strongest leg.
2. Allow the torso to fall forward while dropping the hands to the floor. As the hands approach the ground, jump hard while simultaneously swinging the highest leg upward.
3. Keep arms fairly straight as the feet leave the floor — "you are catching yourself with your palms."
4. Let the momentum from the jump carry the legs above the torso.
5. Allow forward momentum to turn the body over. As you spin, tuck the head in, curl the trunk, and whip the hands up off the floor.
6. The bottoms of the feet should be the *first* body part to contact the ground after the hands lift off.
7. The feet act as shock absorbers — glutes, arms, and back then contact the ground as a soft landing. [S9, pp.154-155]

**Key point**: This drill teaches athletes the first portion of the handspring spin-out of the handstand. Very little upper-body explosiveness is required — even relatively weak athletes can perform this drill successfully.

## Coaching Points
- "Catch yourself with the palms — arms slightly bent to absorb the impact."
- Tuck the head in tight as soon as the legs begin to carry over.
- Feet land first, even in the back-drop finish — never land flat on the back.
- "Think of the fall-back handspring as a hidden step between the back-drop handspring and the running front handspring" — drill this in-between stage until you transcend it. [S9, p.155]
- Always use padding when beginning this step.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Land on glutes or back before the feet can act as shock absorbers — use maximum padding (thick mattress) and continue drilling. This is a normal and expected part of the process.
2. **Intermediate**: Feet begin to contact first before glutes; padding still recommended.
3. **Advanced**: Consistent foot-first landing; transition toward the "fall-back handspring" (landing on feet then falling back) as a hidden intermediate step before Step 6.

**Safety Warning**: Beginners should always use whatever padding is available. Do not attempt on bare hard surfaces until foot-first landing is consistent. [S9, p.155]

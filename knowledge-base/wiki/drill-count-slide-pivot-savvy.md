# Count-Slide-Pivot-Savvy

## Objective
Teach players to integrate defensive boxout techniques, offensive rebound escape moves, and primary rebounding position (savvy) through a systematic 7-spot shooting drill.

## Setup
- 1 offensive player (O), 1 defensive player (X), both near the basket
- Coach (or a third player) shoots from 7 designated spots (A through G) around the half court:
  - **A, B, C**: Strong side (right of basket)
  - **D**: Straight on (top/center)
  - **E, F, G**: Weak side (left of basket)
- Duration: 4 minutes; players rotate after coach completes all 7 spots

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Line up one offensive and one defensive player near the basket.
2. Coach shoots from Spot A. Both players begin from the **same starting position** for each shot.
3. **Defensive rebounder** uses the technique the coach calls (immediate boxout, slide-step boxout, or a mix).
4. **Offensive player** uses jab-step-and-go or jab-step-and-roll with the swim technique.
5. If the **defensive player** gets the rebound → **overhead outlet pass** to the coach.
6. If the **offensive player** gets the rebound → **pump fake**, then power layup.
7. Coach moves A → B → C → D → E → F → G. Both players begin in the same spot every shot, exposing them to weak-side (A/B/C) and strong-side (E/F/G) rebounding, plus straight-on (D).
8. After all 7 spots, the two players rotate roles.

### Options (Progressions)
1. Alter the **arc of the shot** — alternate flat shots and high-arcing shots to teach savvy.
2. Alter the **distance** — coach can move to the 3-point line or come into the lane from any spot.
3. Alter **starting positions** — move offensive player out to the wing for spots A/B/C; keep defender near the basket. This replicates the actual game situation of a weak-side defender.
4. Coach can **pass to the offensive player** at any time (instead of shooting). Offensive player goes 1-on-1. Prevents the defender from cheating for better rebound position.
5. Remove the defensive phase entirely: state that "passes cannot be made to the offensive player as long as the defender is in reasonable defensive position" — pure rebound battle.

### Intermediate
- Place two passers on the court (areas A and C, both strong side). Offensive and defensive players must constantly adjust to prevent the entry pass *and* get primary rebounding position simultaneously.

### Advanced
- Two shooters: one weak side (e.g., area F), one strong side (e.g., area B). They pass back and forth until one shoots. They may pass inside to the offensive post player if the defender gets out of position.

## Coaching Points
- Defensive body position must always be **correct for rebounding** (see [[drill-bull-in-ring-rebounding]] body positioning section).
- **Near the basket**: use **immediate boxout** (reverse pivot) — the carom arrives quickly; the front pivot takes too long.
- **Perimeter**: use **slide-step + front pivot** — allows the defender to watch the assignment longer before turning.
- Develop a **count system**: "one thousand one, one thousand two" for long shots. Eventually this becomes automatic.
- Offensive players: use **jab-step-and-go or jab-step-and-roll** with ample use of the swim technique.
- **Primary rebounding position savvy rules**:
  - Higher arc → shorter rebound
  - Longer shot → longer rebound
  - Shot angle caroms to the mirror angle on the opposite side
  - Livelier/more-inflated ball → further and higher carom

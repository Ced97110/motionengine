# Dumbbell Bench Press

## Objective
Develop pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, and triceps brachii strength to improve passing power, contact resistance in post play, boxing-out physicality, and overall upper-body pressing ability.

## Setup
- 1 player + 1 spotter
- Flat bench; dumbbells of appropriate weight
- Feet flat on the floor

## Execution
1. Lie supine on the bench, feet flat on the floor, shoulders flat on the bench.
2. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with a closed (pronated) grip; arms extended above the nipple line. Shoulders relaxed (not elevated); wrists above elbows — this is the beginning position.
3. **Lowering:** Lower the dumbbells from over the chest to nipple-line level. Dumbbells naturally separate on the way down, ending slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
4. **Pressing:** Brace the abdominals and push the dumbbells away from the body back to the beginning position. Hands should trace the shape of a triangle — wide at the bottom, close together at the top.

**Breathing:** Inhale during the lowering phase; exhale during the press.

**Spotting:** Spotter stands at the head of the athlete in an athletic stance, hands ready to support the **wrists** (not elbows). Spotter follows the path of the wrists, ready to brace and take weight away at any point.

## Coaching Points
- "Wrists over elbows" — wrists must stay directly above the elbows throughout; do not let wrists stray
- "Tight core" — abdominal muscles held firm throughout the press
- Do not let arms come too close to the body (narrows the movement path) or wrists drift away from vertical alignment
- Control the dumbbells at all times — no dropping or bouncing

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light dumbbells on a flat bench; focus on alignment and control
2. **Intermediate**: Standard barbell bench press variation; or alternate arms (one dumbbell at the top while the other lowers)
3. **Advanced**: Perform on an incline or decline bench; single dumbbell; floor press (limits range of motion — shoulder-friendly); alternating-arm press

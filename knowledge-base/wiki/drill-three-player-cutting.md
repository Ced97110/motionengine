# Three-Player Cutting (Drill 59)

## Objective
Introduce the third and fourth rules of the motion offense — "every player must move on every pass" and "when a player dribbles toward you, cut or fade" — while reinforcing the first two rules through continuous 3-player cutting and verbalization.

## Setup
- 3 players per group; multiple groups rotate (3 minutes active, then switch)
- Half court
- 5 cones at various spots (change daily)
- No defenders in the beginner version
- Equipment: 1 basketball, 5 cones

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Three players spread across half court, each approximately 15 feet from the others.
2. Player 2 passes to Player 3 and calls out "middle cut" with the reason: "The defender didn't jump to the ball when I passed."
3. Simultaneously, Player 1 V-cuts and calls out "V-cut" with the reason: "Every player must move on every pass" or "To keep 15-foot spacing."
   - **This is Rule 3**: when the ball moves, ALL players without the ball must make a cut.
4. Player 3 dribbles toward Player 1. Player 1 immediately calls out "fade" with the reason: "On any dribble toward a properly spaced player, that player must cut or fade."
   - **This is Rule 4**: when a dribbler moves toward you, cut or fade.
5. Player 3 passes and verbally states what move was made and why.
6. The drill continues, using all four motion offense rules plus any rocker step or dribbling moves players choose.
7. Groups rotate every 3 minutes; three groups means all players work twice in 9 minutes.

## Coaching Points
- **All four rules must be articulated**: verbalization ensures players understand not just execution but decision-making.
- Change cone locations daily so players must genuinely read rather than memorize patterns.
- Watch Rule 3 carefully: all three players must move every time the ball moves — two movers and one static player is a rule violation.
- Rule 4 is often forgotten: the player being dribbled toward should react **immediately**, not wait to see if they'll receive a handoff.
- Players may use rocker steps and dribbling moves to drive to the basket and kick back outside.
- "You are teaching player savvy and want to make corrections while the movement is still fresh" [S15, p.123]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: No defenders; 5 cones; verbalization required for all cuts
2. **Intermediate**: Add one live defender on the ball handler; the other two players still use cones
3. **Advanced**: All three players have live defenders; no verbalization required — read and react in real time

# Straight Hop (Power Jump Chain — Step 1)

## Objective
Develop elastic ankle rebound, basic take-off and landing patterns, and ankle/knee conditioning as the foundation for all subsequent jump training.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface — no equipment required
- Minimal space needed (small space / cell training compatible)

## Execution
1. Stand with feet around shoulder-width, body "flexed for action" (coiled like a spring, not loose)
2. Dip down slightly — less than a quarter squat, minimal knee bend
3. Without pausing, explode upward using lower leg power plus total-body spring
4. "Pull" up with the shoulders, keeping the arms reasonably neutral
5. Land softly, catching most weight on the balls of the feet
6. Immediately use the elastic rebound to repeat without pausing — "bounce" rhythmically

## Coaching Points
- "Stay flexed, not loose" — a coiled-spring body posture amplifies elastic rebound [S9, p.12]
- Minimal knee bend separates this from squat jumps — the power comes from ankle plantar flexion and Achilles tendon elasticity
- "Don't pause between reps" — the myotatic reflex is only active when reps are continuous
- Watch for athletes landing flat-footed → correct to ball-of-foot landing immediately
- This is a quiet exercise — loud landings indicate poor soft-tissue absorption technique

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Jump less intensely, keep body loose rather than coiled; focus purely on landing softly
2. **Intermediate**: Full-effort hops with maximum elastic rebound; aim for 30 consecutive reps
3. **Advanced**: Add total-body arm swing and increased knee bend → transitions into Squat Jump (Step 2). Target 100 continuous reps or 60-second timed sets

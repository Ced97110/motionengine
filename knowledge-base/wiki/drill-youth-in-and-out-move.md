# In-and-Out Move (Drill 12)

## Objective
Teach the in-and-out dribble move — a head-shoulder-ball fake to one side while continuing to dribble on the same side — to create a straight lane past a defender who is playing straight up or has stepped toward the fake.

## Setup
- Individual drill (teaching): one coach, one player
- Drilling: players line up as shown; three lines at half-court with cones/marks at free-throw line extended
- Mark a **2-foot × 2-foot (.6m × .6m) square** on the floor near a line (e.g., free-throw line)
- Dribbler starts ~10 feet (3m) away and dribbles toward the coach
- Begin with a **control dribble**; progress to a speed dribble in subsequent reps

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Dribbler starts ~10 feet away and dribbles toward the coach (starts with control dribble).
2. At the square, dribbler executes the in-and-out move.
3. Coach moves in the direction of the step-in fake — teaching the *when* while teaching the *how*.
4. Note: This move is the **complement of the crossover**.

## Coaching Points

### When to Use
- When the defender plays them **straight up** (no overplay)
- To get the defender moving **away from the intended direction of attack**
- When the dribbler's fakes have gotten the defender to put a foot forward on the **same side as the dribble**

### How to Execute (Right-Hand Dribble Example)
1. Dribbler **fakes in** with the head and shoulders, bringing the ball in as they step in (left foot steps left).
2. To maintain control: **cup the dribbling hand at the side of the ball**, knowing you'll place the hand on the other side of the ball as soon as it bounces back up.
3. The right (dribbling) hand cups from the right side of the ball and swings through moderately low, then quickly **pulls the ball back to the right**.
4. May actually switch hands while dribbling in if the defender is far enough away to prevent a deflection.
5. While faking with the ball and head/shoulders, **plant the left foot on the toes** and swing the **right foot forward with a long, hard step** — get the right foot at least forward of the defender's left foot.
6. If the fake is good enough, the defender will have stepped right — opening a **straight lane to the basket**.

### Progression
- After mastering with the dominant hand, practice with the **weaker hand**.

## Common Mistakes
1. Fake is too small → exaggerate head, shoulder, and ball movement to sell the fake
2. Losing control of the ball during the cup swing → practice the hand position slowly first
3. Jab-step foot is flat, not on toes → land on toes only to enable rapid re-direction
4. Right foot doesn't get past the defender's left foot → must get depth on the attack step

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Control dribble into the in-and-out move with a passive coach-defender
2. **Intermediate**: Speed dribble into the move
3. **Advanced**: Combine with the crossover as a counter (see Drill 16 Combination Dribbling)

# Balance-Hand Form Shooting

## Objective
Maintain perfect shooting-arm alignment when the balance hand is added, preventing the balance hand from disrupting elbow position or creating a two-hand shooting motion.

## Setup
- 1 shooter + 1 partner/coach (optional but recommended for position checks)
- Directly in front of the basket, starting 2 feet (.6 m) from the rim
- 1 basketball

## Execution
1. Assume the proper shooting stance.
2. Grip the ball in the shooting hand: L-position, wrist cocked, elbow under the ball above the shooting foot.
3. Add the balance hand to the SIDE of the ball — fingertips and fingerpads only (no palm contact). Check T-thumb alignment (thumbs together form a T).
4. **Before shooting**, have a partner or coach verify the positioning of both arms: shooting elbow tucked and under ball, balance hand on the side (not front or top), shooting hand still centered under the ball.
5. Shoot, releasing only from the shooting hand. The balance hand falls away naturally at the release point.
6. Make 5 shots from this distance.
7. Take a half-step back and make 5 more shots.
8. Continue until form breaks down.
9. Goal: 5 makes from 10 spots (50 total). Work straight-on, angled, and around-the-world patterns. [S5, p.19]

## Coaching Points
- **The most common error**: balance hand is placed in front of the ball (blocks target) or on top of the ball (adds motion and slows release) [S5, p.18]
- **Watch for the shooting hand moving to the side** when the balance hand arrives — the shooting hand must stay under and centered [S5, p.18]
- **T-thumb check**: if thumbs came together, they form a T; balance hand thumb is the top of the T [S5, p.18]
- Remind shooters: "The balance hand does NOT shoot the ball" — it only balances and lifts
- After the release, the balance hand should not have influenced the shot direction; if the ball consistently veers toward the balance hand side, that hand is interfering
- Progress to strings (consecutive makes or shoot-till-miss) once the make-five routine feels routine [S5, p.19]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Coach-verified positioning check before every single shot. Straight-on only.
2. **Intermediate**: Self-check (no coach needed). Add angle variety — side, baseline, around-the-world.
3. **Advanced — Strings**: Consecutive makes or shoot-till-miss. Eliminate all unnecessary motion from the balance hand; target a quick, compact release.

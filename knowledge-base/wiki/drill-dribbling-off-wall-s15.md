# Dribbling Off the Wall

## Objective
Develop fingertip ball control, hand quickness, wrist action, and the fundamental dribbling skill of using only the finger pads (not the palm) at multiple heights and with each individual finger.

## Setup
- **Players**: Individual (can run simultaneously with entire team at a wall)
- **Court area**: Any wall surface
- **Equipment**: One basketball per player
- **Duration**: 1 minute

## Execution

**Beginner:**
1. Player stands facing a wall and dribbles the ball off the wall with the right hand.
2. Begin dribbling at approximately head height (elbow at 90 degrees).
3. Progress to elbow flexed at 135 degrees, then to full elbow extension.
4. Repeat steps 2-3 with the left hand.

**Intermediate:**
1. Repeat the beginner drill but use only the index finger of the right hand to dribble the basketball.
2. Progress through: only the middle finger → only the ring finger → only the little finger.
3. Repeat all steps with the left hand.

**Advanced:**
1. Repeat all beginner steps (two-hand dribbling at all heights) while walking/running the full length of the wall.
2. Repeat all intermediate steps (individual fingers at all heights) while walking/running the full length of the wall.

## Coaching Points
- **Finger pads only**: Players dribble, shoot, and pass using only the upper finger pads — never the palm. The palm may only support and lift the ball [S15, p.9]
- **Wrist action**: Wrist should follow an up-and-down motion as if waving goodbye. Watch for a wrinkle created behind the wrist and released with each stroke
- **Consistency**: Once dribbling at a set height, maintain that height throughout that phase — don't let it drift
- **Eyes up**: Players must not look at the ball; they should look straight ahead
- The finger-isolation variations are especially powerful for developing individual finger strength and control that transfers to all ball-handling skills

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Full hand (finger pads) at three heights — stationary
2. **Intermediate**: Individual fingers at three heights — stationary
3. **Advanced**: Both beginner and intermediate patterns while moving along the wall

# Side-Lying Shoulder External Rotation

## Objective
Strengthen rotator cuff muscles (specifically the external rotators) to support shooting, defending, and blocking.

## Setup
- Individual exercise
- Equipment: rolled towel; optional hand weights for advanced version
- Floor or mat space

## Execution
1. Lie on your side with a rolled towel placed under your upper arm and your elbow bent to 90 degrees.
2. Pinch your shoulder blades together, then lift your forearm toward the ceiling. Keep the elbow bent and rotate through the shoulder (external rotation only — the upper arm stays against the towel).
3. Complete 3 sets of 10 repetitions per side.
4. **Advanced:** Add hand weights (start with 2–3 lb).

## Coaching Points
- "Elbow stays glued to the towel" — the only motion is shoulder external rotation; the humerus should not swing away from the body
- "Pinch shoulder blades first, then lift" — activate scapular stabilizers before rotating
- Slow and controlled on both the up and down phases; do not drop the arm

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Bodyweight only, 3 × 10
2. **Intermediate:** Add 2–3 lb hand weights
3. **Advanced:** Increase weight progressively

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary:** Rotator cuff (infraspinatus, teres minor)
- **Secondary:** Middle trapezius, lower trapezius, rhomboids

## Concepts Taught
- [[acl-and-shoulder-injury-prevention]] — rotator cuff strength protects the shoulder during repetitive overhead basketball motions

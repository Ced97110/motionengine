# Band Defensive Slide (ACL Ligament Dominance)

## Objective
Build hip and gluteal strength in the defensive slide pattern to prevent dynamic knee valgus during lateral basketball movements, specifically cutting and pivoting.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface (gym floor, court)
- Equipment: elastic resistance band
- Space: 10-15 feet of lateral room

## Execution
1. Place an elastic band around both knees.
2. Begin in a good defensive stance (knees bent, hips down, chest up, weight on balls of feet). Maintain enough tension in the band so there is no slack.
3. Take a defensive slide with one leg, increasing tension in the band as the lead leg reaches maximum width.
4. Once maximum tension is reached (based on individual strength), bring the trail leg over to re-establish proper defensive position.
5. Continue sliding in the same direction for the required distance or repetitions.
6. Repeat the procedure moving in the opposite direction.
7. Optional: hold arms out as if guarding an opponent to increase realism.

## Coaching Points
- "Stay low — defensive position throughout, not just at the start"
- The band provides feedback: if the knees cave in, the band goes slack; maintain tension by pushing knees outward
- Do not cross the feet during the slide; step-and-recover pattern only
- Hip and gluteal strength is especially important for female players stabilizing the lower leg during pivoting and cutting [S2, p.167]
- This exercise mimics an actual basketball movement; hold your arms as if defending an opponent

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary defensive stance — isometric knee-out hold against band before adding lateral movement
2. **Intermediate**: Lateral slide for 5-6 steps each direction, lightweight band
3. **Advanced**: Longer distance (full lane width), heavier band; add on-ball defensive reaction cues

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius), gluteus medius, gluteus minimus
- **Secondary**: Tensor fasciae latae, hamstrings (semitendinosus, biceps femoris, semimembranosus)

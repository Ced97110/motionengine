# Philadelphia 76ers Baseline Screening Plays

## Overview
Larry Brown's system for the 1998-99 76ers used baseline staggered screens to free Allen Iverson (2) as the primary scorer after repositioning him at shooting guard. Tony Kukoc (3) or Eric Snow handled the ball to initiate the offense. The series includes multiple variations: a staggered-screen lob option, a handoff drive, a flare-and-isolation play, and a flare-to-PnR conversion. [S14, pp.44-46]

## Formation
**Play A (2 cuts off screens)**:
- **3** handles at the top/wing area
- **2** (Iverson) starts below the screens
- **1** and **5** set staggered screens
- **4** positioned on the right low block

**Play B (Flare for 2, 1 cuts)**:
- **2** handles at the top
- **1** cuts off staggered screens set by **3** and **5**
- **4** sets the back-screen flare for **2**

## Phases

### Play A: Staggered Screen + Lob/Handoff

#### Phase 1: Staggered Screen Cut
- **3** handles and initiates
- **2** cuts off **staggered screens set by 1 and 5**
- **1** pops back to the left corner after screening
- **5** holds on the left block
- **3** hits **2** and **cuts off the back screen set by 4**

#### Phase 2: Two Options
- **Option 1**: **2** throws a **lob pass to 3** cutting off 4's back screen to the basket
- **Option 2 (Lob denied)**: **4** flashes to the ball; **2** hits **4** → sets up a **handoff action**
  - **2** (Iverson) makes a quick cut off **4** (Tyrone Hill) to get the ball back
  - **2** drives to the basket
  - **3** clears to the right corner
  - **2** can pass to **Kukoc** in the corner if his defender drops to help

### Play B: Flare-and-Isolation

#### Phase 1: Initiate with 2 Handling
- **2** handles at the top
- **1** cuts off **staggered screens set by 3 and 5**
- **3** pops back to the left corner after screening
- **5** holds on the left block
- **2** hits **1**

#### Phase 2: Back-Screen Flare
- **2** flares off the **back screen set by 4**
- **1** skips the pass over **4** to **2** for the **shot or isolation and drive**
- If **4**'s defender steps out to help on the flare: **4 slips to the rim** with **1** making the inside pass

### Play C: Flare to Pick-and-Roll

#### When Flare Doesn't Produce a Shot
- If **2** catches the skip pass but cannot shoot or isolate:
  - **4** follows the pass to **2** and sets a **wing pick-and-roll**
  - **1** and **3** occupy **spot-up positions**
  - **4** rolls after setting the screen
  - **5** punches into the lane
  - **2** reads roll (4), slip (5), or spot-up (1 or 3)

## Key Coaching Points
- The **lob option** after the stagger is dangerous because **3** is cutting off a back screen — defenders focused on **2**'s stagger cut often lose **3**
- The **handoff** requires perfect timing — **2**'s cut off **4** must be quick and sharp to maintain momentum for the drive
- The **flare-and-iso** is designed specifically for a guard who can isolate and create — Iverson was uniquely suited for this
- **4's slip counter**: if X4 steps out aggressively to help on the flare, the slip to the rim is wide open — coaches must teach **4** to read X4's feet
- The PnR conversion (Play C) is a built-in answer if the defense takes away the isolation

## Counters
- Lob denied → handoff drive chain
- Flare shot denied → isolation and drive
- Isolation path closed → wing PnR with 4
- X4 steps to flare → 4 slips to rim

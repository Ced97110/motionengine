# Baby Hook Shot

## Objective
Teach youth post players the proper mechanics of a left-hand baby hook shot from the left side of the basket.

## Setup
- Individual or small group
- Paint area — left side of the basket (facing midcourt)
- One ball per player

## Execution
1. Player begins on the **left side of the goal**, facing midcourt, back to the basket.
2. Turn slightly until back is **parallel to the backboard** and the right shoulder points to the opposite side of the court.
3. Jump, landing on the **right leg** while raising the **left leg as high as possible** while remaining balanced.
4. Transfer the ball from both hands to the **left hand only** as the jump begins.
5. Extend the **left arm until straight**.
6. **Flip the ball gently** over the rim — soft touch, not a hard shot.

## Coaching Points
- Cue: "Back to board, right foot land, left arm rise, flip."
- The left leg kick provides balance and prevents the defender from blocking the shot.
- The arm extension must be fully straight — bent elbows cause the ball to fall short or off-line.
- "Flip gently" — power comes from extension, not a forced throw.
- Do not bring the ball down; catch and immediately extend upward.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary form work — no jump, just arm extension practice with a soft toss.
2. **Intermediate**: Add full jump mechanics from a standing start.
3. **Advanced**: Add a flash pivot cut to receive a pass, then execute the baby hook in one motion.

# Fun Cone Dribbling Drill (Drill #32)

## Objective
Integrate multiple dribble moves (in-and-out, crossover, spin, half-spin, hesitation, retreating) in a gamelike, unpredictable cone-maze format that develops ball control, agility, and change of pace.

## Setup
- Individual or full team
- Full court
- **Cones placed haphazardly** around the court (no set pattern — random arrangement creates variety)
- 1 basketball per player (Intermediate: 2 balls per player)
- Duration: 2 minutes

<!-- DIAGRAM: Full court with cones scattered in a non-uniform pattern. Players follow a winding path through the cones, executing moves at each cone. [S15, p.70] -->

## Execution
### Beginner
1. Place cones around the court in a haphazard arrangement.
2. Using one ball, each player dribbles through the court following the cones.
3. Coach **calls out different moves** for players to execute at each cone — or designates one move to be used consistently throughout.
4. A second player may begin dribbling when the first player passes the first cone.
5. **Variation**: Instead of using a move to go past a cone, players **circle the cone completely** before moving to the next one (especially fun as a team race).

### Intermediate
- Players use **two balls** instead of one through the same cone maze.

## Options
1. **Position race**: Divide into post players vs. guards; create two equal cone mazes and race.
2. **Team race**: First team vs. second team through identical cone mazes.

## Coaching Points
- **Eyes up**: Peripheral vision is essential — players must keep eyes up to see the next cone and their teammates.
- **Ball under control**: Ball stays to the side, 2–3 feet from the knee — never directly in front where it can be stripped.
- **Agility and body balance**: The unpredictable path demands balance transitions that straight-line drills don't provide.
- **Change of pace**: Encourage players to slow into moves and explode out — mimicking actual game dribbling rhythm.
- **Fingerpads**: Dribble with the first pads of the fingers.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: One ball, coach calls a single move to use throughout.
2. **Intermediate**: One ball, coach calls different moves at each cone.
3. **Advanced**: Two balls through the full cone maze.

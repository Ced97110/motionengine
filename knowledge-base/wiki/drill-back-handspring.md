# Back Handspring

## Objective
Execute a two-arm explosive backward flip with hand contact on the floor — the first true back-flip pattern that breaks fully from the floor-hand-starting macaco drills.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Open flat floor space (6+ feet clearance behind)
- No equipment required; beginners should use a soft surface (grass, gymnastics mat, padded floor)
- **Prerequisite**: Comfortable monkey flip (Step 6)

## Execution
1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hands out in front of your head or slightly higher.
2. Dip down at knees and hips simultaneously, swinging arms down and behind you. Keep looking forward during the descent. ("Sit back as if into a chair." [S9, p.191])
3. Explode up and **diagonally backward** — approximately 45° — swinging hands above you as you jump.
4. As you become airborne, look behind you. A slight back arch helps initiate rotation. Your hands should follow your line of vision.
5. Make contact with the floor with arms **locked** (not bent), at approximately shoulder-width.
6. Once your legs reach the vertical point, **whip your feet down to the ground rapidly**.
7. When your feet are planted, step or hop to catch your balance if needed, then extend your body again.

## Coaching Points
- "The main barrier is fear. Athletes who've mastered the monkey flip should not find this challenging — the pattern is the same." [S9, p.190]
- "Practice on soft surfaces (grass, pillows) if anxiety is present — this is a psychological step as much as a physical one."
- Watch for: arm contact with bent elbows → cue arm lockout before contact; and for landing in a deep crouch → cue extended-body landing
- "Land with your body extended, not crouched — overcome the crunch instinct."
- Hands should be shoulder-width at contact — too wide = unstable; too narrow = collapse risk

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regression)**: Practice on grass or heavily padded floor; use a spotter at the lower back for the first attempts; land on a mat behind you.
2. **Intermediate**: Consistent arm-locked contact with a moderate crouch landing; no spotter needed.
3. **Advanced**: Land with extended body, feet close together — improved height leads directly to the four-point back flip (Step 9) [S9, p.190]

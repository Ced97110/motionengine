# Butt Kip (Step 5 of Kip-Up Chain)

## Objective
Build enough explosive momentum to lift palms off the floor and project the torso forward, landing on butt and feet with an upright torso — the bridge between bridge kip and the half kip.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat open floor or mat
- No equipment needed

## Execution
1. Sit on the floor with knees bent and heels on the ground.
2. Roll back and swing legs up while placing hands in the **bridge position** (by ears, fingers pointing toward feet).
3. Roll onto upper-back and shoulders. Squeeze into a tight ball with bent knees close to the head.
4. **"Unravel"** by exploding legs in an arc UP then DOWN while pushing DOWN through palms **hard enough to lift palms off the ground**.
5. As you kick off, **throw torso and hands forward** — like an explosive sit-up.
6. **Do NOT try to land on feet at this stage.** Aim to land on both butt AND feet with torso upright.
7. Lower body back down and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- The key difference from Step 4: palms must leave the floor during the motion
- "Throw the torso and hands forward" is an active cue — not passive momentum, an intentional forward snap
- Landing on the butt (not full standing) is the correct goal — master this before Step 6
- As momentum increases, the athlete may briefly touch feet before sitting back — that is essentially Step 6

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Kick in arc, land on butt, bring head/back only slightly off the floor
2. **Intermediate**: Land on butt with torso fully upright ("sit-up" finish position)
3. **Advanced**: Generate enough momentum to briefly touch feet before sitting back — transition to [[drill-half-kip]] [S9, p.4]

# Shot Fake Workout Series — Progressive Skill Development System

## Summary
The shot-fake workout series from *Basketball Coaches' Guide to Advanced Offensive Skill Development* represents one of the most systematic individual scoring development programs in the coaching literature. It organizes shot-fake moves into a progressive hierarchy — from basic stride-stop pull-ups to advanced hop-back crossovers — and trains each move at three volume levels (introductory, intermediate, advanced) with built-in free throw intervals and quantitative shot-charting targets.

The overarching philosophy is: **master the foundational move before adding the counter.** Each level of the series (stride stop → step-through → big hop → hop back → roll-out → crossover) builds directly on the previous one. A defender who has seen the stride stop pull-up now respects the drive, making the hop back possible. A defender who closes on the hop back becomes vulnerable to the roll-out or crossover. [S3]

## When to Use
- Individual pre-practice or off-day skill development sessions
- Summer skill-building programs for serious players
- Position-specific individual workout plans (all perimeter positions)
- When a player needs to develop a reliable mid-range pull-up game
- When building a complete "shot-fake package" that makes a player difficult to guard

## Key Principles
1. **Progressive volume** — each move is practiced at three levels: ~80 shots (introductory), ~120 shots (intermediate), ~240 shots (advanced).
2. **Four mandatory combinations** — Left × 1-dribble, Left × 2-dribble, Right × 1-dribble, Right × 2-dribble. Skipping any combination creates a scoutable weakness.
3. **FT interleaving** — free throws are shot between every field goal set. This replicates game conditions (elevated heart rate, composure under pressure, transition from dribble moves to static shooting).
4. **Quantitative targets** — each workout has explicit make goals: 60% FG and 80-85% FT. If a player consistently falls below these, they step back to the prior volume level.
5. **Shot charting is non-negotiable** — every workout is tracked (Date, FGA, FGM, %, FTA, FTM, %). Progress is measured, not assumed.
6. **Move hierarchy must be respected** — do not introduce the hop-back before the stride stop is automated; do not introduce the crossover before the roll-out is mastered.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Rushing through the hierarchy** → Each move must be mastered (60% FG consistently) before adding the next layer.
2. **Skipping free throw intervals** → These are not optional — they build clutch free throw shooting and simulate game fatigue.
3. **Not tracking shots** → Without the chart, there is no accountability and no progress measurement.
4. **Training only one direction** → Defenders will force the weak side; all four combinations must be equally trained.
5. **Low-effort fakes** → In solo workouts, players tend to execute lazy fakes. Hold the standard: every fake must be game-quality.

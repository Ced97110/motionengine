# Flip the Ball Off the Wall

## Objective
Use a wall to physically lock the player into perfect straight-stick alignment so they can feel the correct position before executing the shot — the wall acts as an external alignment guide.

## Setup
- Players: individual or entire team simultaneously (each player at a wall)
- Court area: any wall surface
- Equipment: one basketball per player
- No basket needed

## Execution
**Beginner — Establish alignment at the wall:**
1. Player stands in front of a wall (instructions for right-handed shooters; mirror for left-handed)
2. Place **right toe against the wall**; left foot about 18 inches (45 cm) behind the right foot
3. Place the **right knee against the wall** and the **entire lower right arm against the wall**
4. **Cock the right wrist** with the end of the wrist touching the wall
5. Hold a ball in the right hand; the wrist should be slightly to the right of the face with the thumb almost over the right eye
6. Confirm: a straight line from wrist → lower arm → elbow → knee → toe — this is perfect straight-stick alignment
7. Player steps back a few steps from the wall and **shoots the ball about 10 feet (3 m) into the air**, letting it hit off the wall
8. Catch the return with both hands
9. Step back to the wall, confirm alignment, and repeat — focusing on reproducing the wall-contact alignment on every shot

**Intermediate:**
- Execute the beginner drill but catch the return with only the shooting hand

## Coaching Points
- **"A crooked shooting elbow is the worst fault in all of basketball"** [S15, p.187]
- Check positions at the wall before every rep — do not skip this step; the wall verification IS the drill
- **Straight-stick checkpoints** (illustrated in source figures):
  - Wrist cocked, ball on fingertips (not palm)
  - Elbow forms ~90° angle with forearm perpendicular to floor, upper arm parallel to floor
  - Wrist, elbow, knee, and toes in a straight line
  - Knee slightly bent; toes pointing toward the target
- **Jump shot coordination points** (from source Figure 2):
  - Front edge of ball is even with the elbow
  - Target sighted with the shooting eye (right eye for right-handed shooters)
  - Shoulders squared to the basket
  - Body stays erect — do not arch the back
  - Let legs hang loose; toes point to the floor; return to the same spot
- **Front view** (from source Figure 3):
  - Wrist cocked with ball on fingertips
  - Elbow on line to the target (slight lateral shift allowed if more comfortable)
  - Balance hand lightly on the ball — comfortable but not guiding
  - Eyes focused on the target, not the flight of the ball

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: wall contact to verify alignment → step back → flip against wall → catch with both hands
2. **Intermediate**: same but catch with shooting hand only
3. **Advanced**: eliminate wall contact verification; reproduce the alignment from memory and check with a partner or video

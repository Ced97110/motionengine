# Shoulder Pop (Kip-Up Step 3)

## Objective
Begin conditioning the wrists and shoulders for the explosive hand-push required in a full kip-up, while developing vertical leg-kick momentum.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat surface (mat recommended for beginners)
- No equipment

## Execution
1. Sit on floor with knees bent, heels on the ground.
2. Roll onto back, bringing legs above body (knees bent).
3. Place hands palms-down alongside ears, fingers pointing toward feet.
4. When feet are above head, explosively kick legs straight up to gain vertical momentum.
5. Simultaneously push down through palms, briefly "popping" shoulders and head 1–2 inches off the ground.
6. Arms and shoulders act as springs — cushion the descent as much as possible using muscular tension.
7. Roll back to starting position and repeat.

## Coaching Points
- The kick must go STRAIGHT UP — this is vertical momentum, not outward kick
- Shoulders and hands should be thought of as "springs compressing" on landing — absorb, don't crash
- Steps 1 and 2 trained leg-waist momentum; this step introduces the explosive hand-push that will be critical in Steps 7-10
- Wrist and shoulder conditioning here prevents injury when attempting the full kip-up
- Bridge push-up experience (Convict Conditioning background) makes this step significantly easier

## Progressions
1. **Regression**: Focus only on kicking hard vertically — don't worry about lifting shoulders yet
2. **Standard**: 1–2 inch shoulder lift with palm push
3. **Progression**: Push shoulders progressively higher — advanced hand-balancers can push all the way into a handstand from this position

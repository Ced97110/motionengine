# Closeout Shooting Drill Series — Off the Dribble

## Objective
Develop game-realistic shot creation off closeout pressure: catch, shot fake, drive past the defender, and finish with a pull-up jump shot, power layup, or penetrate-and-pitch to an open teammate.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2–4 depending on variation
- **Court area**: Half court — passer inside the lane; shooter restricted to a designated zone
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball (2 for advanced variation)
- **Zones**: Outside the lane (midrange), inside the lane (paint), behind the three-point line

## Drill Variations

### Variation 1: Shot Fake, Drive, and Pull-Up Jump Shot
*Diagram 1-17 from source*
1. Passer (P) passes to Shooter (S) and closes out aggressively.
2. S catches, makes a **realistic shot fake**, then drives past the closeout defender (scraping off the defender).
3. S attacks the basket directly with a **predesignated number of dribbles**, then pulls up for an outside jump shot.
4. S alternates the side of the drive on each rep (left and right of defender).
5. S rebounds → outlet passes → closes out on new shooter. P becomes next shooter.
6. **Targets (3 min, both shooters)**: 33–35 attempts; 60% = ~20 made; 50% = ~16–18 made.

### Variation 2: Shot Fake, Drive, and Power Layup
*Diagram 1-18 from source*
1. Same sequence as Variation 1, but S drives all the way to the basket for a **power layup** rather than a pull-up.
2. S changes drive direction and number of dribbles each rep.
3. **Targets (3 min, both shooters)**: 30–32 attempts; 70% = ~21–22 made; 60% = ~18–19 made.

### Variation 3: Shot Fake, Drive, Power Lay-Up vs. Second Defender
*Diagram 1-19 from source — 3 players: P, S, D*
1. S catches, shot fakes, scrapes past closeout defender (P).
2. A **second token defender (D)** waits near the basket.
3. S attacks D with a power move using 1–3 dribbles and shoots.
4. Rotation: P→S→D→P.
5. **Targets (3 min per shooter)**: ~20 attempts; 60% = ~12 made; 50% = ~10 made.

### Variation 4: Shot Fake, Dribble Drive, and Dish
*Diagram 1-20 from source — 4 players: P, S, D, O*
1. S catches, shot fakes, drives past P (1–3 dribbles toward basket).
2. S **dishes** to a second offensive player (O) who attacks a second defender (D) for an inside shot.
3. Rotation: P→S→D→O→P.
4. **Targets (4 min per shooter)**: ~14–16 inside shots; 70% = ~9–11 made; 60% = ~8–10 made.

### Variation 5: Shot Fake, Penetrate, and Pitch
*Diagram 1-21 from source — 4 players: P, S, D, O*
1. S catches, shot fakes, drives 1–3 dribbles toward basket.
2. S **pitches out** to second offensive player (O) beyond the three-point line.
3. O catches and shoots a three-pointer vs. second defender (D) closing out from the lane.
4. Rotation: P→S→D→O→P.
5. **Targets (4 min per shooter)**: ~10–12 three-point attempts; 50% = ~5 made; 40% = ~4 made.

### Variation 6: Shot Fake, Dribble Drive, Pull-Up — Dual Ball (Advanced)
*Diagram 1-22 from source — 3 players, 2 balls*
1. P1 passes and closes out; S shot fakes, scrapes off, drives, and pulls up for an outside jump shot.
2. P1 becomes next shooter; P2 passes the second ball to P1.
3. All three players rotate continuously (P1→S→P2→P1).
4. **Targets (3 min per shooter)**: ~24 attempts; 60% = ~14 made; 50% = ~12 made.

## Coaching Points
- **"Make a realistic shot fake"** — a convincing freeze of the defender, not a head bob
- **"Scrape off the defender"** — drive shoulder-to-shoulder past the closeout to eliminate recovery angle
- A second shot fake vs. a second interior defender may be required before the final shot
- Alternate drive directions and dribble counts every rep — prevent defenders from anticipating
- Token defenders should apply enough pressure to be realistic without blocking shots
- Record makes and attempts from every zone for each player

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Variation 1 only — shot fake + pull-up, no second defender
2. **Intermediate**: Add Variation 3 — second defender near basket
3. **Advanced**: Variations 4 and 5 — dish and pitch with 4 players; Variation 6 — dual ball

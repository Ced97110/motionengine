# Freeze Follow-Through Drill

## Objective
Train shooters to hold their follow-through position until the ball hits the floor, building proper muscle memory and creating a diagnostic window for coach feedback.

## Setup
- 1 player per basket (can be run simultaneously across multiple baskets)
- Any court area within the player's shooting range
- No special equipment required
- Incorporate into every shooting warm-up session

## Execution
1. Player assumes a proper shooting stance within their comfortable shooting range.
2. Player shoots a standard shot using correct form.
3. **After release, the player freezes the follow-through position** — both hands held high, shooting arm fully extended, elbow above the eyebrow, shooting hand in the "hand-in-the-hoop" position.
4. Player holds this position **until the ball hits the floor** (not just until it hits the rim or goes through the net).
5. Coach checks: Is the elbow above the eyebrow? Is the hand in the hoop? Is the finish straight on the shot line? Is the balance hand still up?
6. Player collects the ball, returns to position, and repeats.

## Coaching Points
- *"Freeze your finish until the ball bounces off the floor"* — this overemphasizes the hold and combats the natural tendency to drop arms early
- Check both hands: the balance hand must remain up, not drop to the side
- Verify the shooting arm did not drift across the face or outside the shoulder
- "If you drop your hands too quickly in practice, you will drop them even more quickly in a game" [S5, p.42]
- This drill belongs in **every shooting warm-up** alongside one-handed form shooting and two-handed form shooting [S5, p.42]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary shots close to the basket (6-8 feet); focus entirely on holding the position.
2. **Intermediate**: Extend to mid-range distances; player calls out self-check ("elbow up, hand in hoop") before lowering arms.
3. **Advanced**: Catch-and-shoot off a pass or spinout; freeze finish while moving slightly laterally — simulates game conditions while maintaining the feedback habit.

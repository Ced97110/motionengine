# Defending Sideline Pick-and-Rolls Drill

## Objective
Develop execution, communication, and technique for defending the sideline pick-and-roll with a predetermined defensive strategy at the point of the screen, including trap and rotation rules.

## Setup
- 10 players, 5 vs 5 on the half court
- O1 starts with ball at the top; O3 on the wing/corner; O5 at the high post; O2 and O4 on opposite perimeter spots
- Defense in man-to-man; technique (trap, hedge, fight over, switch, go under) must be predetermined before action begins

## Execution
1. O1 dribbles to the free-throw line extended, pushing O3 to the corner.
2. O5 relocates from the high post to set a sideline pick-and-roll screen for O1.
3. **Decision point**: The defense must have its technique predetermined at the moment O5's screen is set:
   - Stay with the opponent
   - Go under
   - Fight over
   - Switch
   - Trap (create a team rotation)
4. **If O1 turns the corner into the middle**, the interior defense scrambles. O1's options are:
   - a. Jump shot
   - b. Pass to O5 rolling to the basket
   - c. Pass to O2 for a jump shot or drive-and-dish to O4
5. **Sideline trap execution** (primary scenario): X1 and X5 apply a double team, forcing O1 to pick up the dribble or pass. Both trappers **stay in the double team** until O1 passes — this is non-negotiable.
6. **Rotation on the kick-out pass**:
   - **O1 passes to O3**: X4 rotates to cover O5 rolling to the basket; X2 covers the middle area.
   - **O1 passes to O4**: X3 rotates to cover O5.
   - **X5's responsibility**: Rotate back toward the basket and pick up whichever of O4 or O3 is open.
   - **If the trap works**: O2 must sink and find O4 should X4 rotate to cover O5.

## Coaching Points
- **Know the guard's tendencies first**: Going over the screen is correct against a good jump shooter; going under is correct against a basket attacker [S13, p.211].
- **"Communicate before the screen"** — any confusion at the point of the screen gives the offense the advantage.
- **"Don't split" rule** — X1 must not let O1 split X1 and X5 on initial contact; this is Rule #2 for all PnR defense.
- **"Stay in the trap"** — X1 and X5 must stay in the double team until O1 passes; abandoning it early defeats the purpose.
- **Rotation is assignment-based**, not reactive — know who covers the roll and who covers the kick-out BEFORE the trap is executed.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through rotation rules without O5 rolling; identify coverage assignments by verbal drill ("If O1 passes to O3, who covers O5?").
2. **Intermediate**: Live 5v5 with only trap/rotation as the allowed technique; practice communication and rotation.
3. **Advanced**: O1 chooses between sideline PnR and middle PnR based on defensive alignment; defense must identify and adjust.

# Hip-Strike Pushup (Power Pushup Step 6)

## Objective
Develop the explosive power and shoulder speed needed to move hands behind the body during a pushup, bridging between front-body strikes and the behind-the-back convict pushup.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor
- No equipment

## Execution
1. Squat down, place palms on floor, stretch legs out behind
2. Palms below shoulders at shoulder-width or wider
3. Feet at comfortable width
4. Keep legs, hips, torso aligned and horizontal
5. Dip ~1/3 depth
6. Immediately push explosively off floor
7. In mid-air, whip hands **back** to slap both hips
8. Return hands in front to catch floor
9. Use elastic rebound to immediately repeat

## Coaching Points
- Even powerful clap-pushup athletes can be humbled by behind-body clapping variations; hip-strikes are the essential bridge [S9, p.94]
- Regression: if hips are unreachable, strike the belly — split the difference between chest and hips [S9, p.94]
- Progression sequence toward convict pushup (Step 7): hips → side of thighs → throw hands behind hips (like a straight-arm kickback) → get hands behind back → bring into contact (convict) [S9, p.95]
- Key: "Inch your hands back a little farther over time" — small progressive steps make the impossible possible [S9, p.95]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Strike the belly; work toward hips
2. **Intermediate**: Fully slap both hips each rep
3. **Advanced**: Inch hands progressively farther back — sides of thighs → behind-hip kickback position → advance to Step 7 (convict pushup)

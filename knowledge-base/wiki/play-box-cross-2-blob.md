# Box, Cross 2 (BLOB)

## Overview
A flexible box-set baseline out-of-bounds system using simple directional calls ("Cross," "Diagonal," "Up") to indicate the screening angle. Players can occupy any of the four box spots and execute based on the call. "Cross 2" designates O2 as the primary cutter off a cross screen; "Cross 3" targets O3; "Diagonal 4" targets O4. The system attacks weak defenders and seeks layups or fouls.

## Formation
- **O1**: Inbounder on the baseline (top of the box)
- **O5**: Left-side elbow (decoy — fakes inside then moves to corner)
- **O3**: Left-side block (calling "Back screen" — moves toward O2's defender)
- **O2**: Right-side block (primary cutter off the cross screen)
- **O4**: Right-side elbow (screener for O2)

## Nomenclature System
| Call | Meaning | Hot Spot |
|------|---------|----------|
| "Cross 2" | Play for O2 off a cross screen | Opposite elbow from inbounder |
| "Cross 3" | Play for O3 off a cross screen | Opposite elbow |
| "Diagonal 4" | Play for O4 who lines up diagonally | Post-up position |
| "Up" | Up screen action | Weak-side high |

Players line up at any of the four box spots and execute based on the call — highly versatile and hard to scout.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Decoy Action
- O1 inbounds and calls "Cross 2"
- **O5 fakes inside** (as if receiving the pass) then moves to the corner — pretending to be the primary target
- O3 calls out "Back screen" and moves toward X2 (O2's defender)

### Phase 2: Cross Screen for O2
- **O2 jab-steps to the sideline** (setup move to freeze the defender)
- **O4 sets a cross screen** on X2 (O2's defender)
- **O2 uses the screen and cuts down the middle** looking for a lob or layup

### Phase 3: Secondary and Safety Options
- **O4 holds the screen** on X2, then **opens to the ball** and goes to the basket — second option
- **O1 fakes to O5** (reinforcing the decoy) and looks for O2, then O4
- **O3 relocates high** as the safety valve

## Key Coaching Points
- The "Cross 2" nomenclature means the play belongs to O2 — everyone else's job is to set up O2's cut
- O5's fake inside is critical — it draws at least one defender's attention
- O2's jab-step to the sideline creates the separation needed to use O4's screen effectively
- O4 holds the screen long enough that X2 is caught, then immediately opens to the ball as option 2
- Pick on the weakest defender — the system allows you to call plays for whichever matchup is favorable
- "Attacking the basket" is the priority — seek layups and fouls, not perimeter shots

## Counters
- If X2 anticipates the cross screen, O4 can slip the screen and cut to the rim
- O3 high is always available as a safety for O1 to inbound and reset
- Change the call ("Cross 3," "Diagonal 4") to attack a different matchup in the next possession

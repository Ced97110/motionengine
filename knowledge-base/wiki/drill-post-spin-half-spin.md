# Post Spin and Half-Spin Drill (#115)

## Objective
Teach the post spin move and half-spin move as mutual counters off the drop-step middle, creating separation from defenders who anticipate either direction.

## Setup
- **Players**: 2-5 per group (1 active, rest in line; coach calls moves)
- **Court area**: Low-post area (same format as Drill 114)
- **Equipment**: 1 basketball per group

## Execution
1. Players line up in the same format as Drill #114 (single line, coach as passer).
2. Player 1 flash-pivot cuts or V-cuts toward the coach.
3. **Coach calls out the move combination**: e.g., "drop-step middle," "drop-step middle spin move," or "drop-step middle half-spin move."
4. Player 1 executes the called sequence.
5. After successful completion, 1 goes to end of line; Player 2 cuts toward coach. Drill continues until all players have learned both moves.

### Teaching Sequence
- Teach **drop-step middle spin move first**
- Then teach **drop-step middle half-spin move**
- Do NOT teach drop-step baseline spin or half-spin (player goes out of bounds)
- After mastering the middle versions, teach **drop-step baseline spin and half-spin toward the middle of the court** (always spin/half-spin back toward the middle regardless of original drop-step direction)

### Spin Move (off Drop-Step Middle)
1. Execute drop-step middle
2. The attacker spins fully (360° reverse pivot) away from the defender to create separation
3. The spin generates a jump shot, power layup, or baby hook opportunity
4. **Teach one step at a time, walking pace first**: "First do just step 1 (the drop-step). Then add step 2. Go very slowly at first. Then add quicker movement — but never faster movement. Quickness is the key, not speed." [S15, p.236]

### Half-Spin Move (Counter to the Spin)
1. Execute drop-step middle
2. Begin the spin motion (attacker shows they are spinning)
3. Stop halfway through the spin — "half-spin" — and shoot or attack from that position
4. Used when the defender anticipates the full spin and overcommits; the half-spin leaves the defender moving in the wrong direction
5. **The spin and half-spin are counters to each other**: "They are used to create separation from the defender... to get the defender off balance so the attacker can shoot a jump shot, power layup, or baby hook." [S15, p.236]

## Coaching Points
- Spin and half-spin always go **back toward the middle of the floor** — never toward the baseline (out of bounds risk)
- "Quickness is the key, not speed" — master the mechanics at slow pace before adding quickness
- Drill the spin move **several days on both sides of the court** before progressing to the half-spin
- The half-spin is the more difficult move — go one step at a time through the teaching sequence
- These moves can be used off either a drop-step middle or a drop-step baseline (spinning back toward the middle in both cases)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Drop-step only (from Drill #114); no spin/half-spin yet
2. **Intermediate**: Drop-step middle → spin move; teach at walking pace, both sides of court over multiple days
3. **Advanced**: Drop-step middle → spin move OR drop-step middle → half-spin (counters); drop-step baseline → spin toward middle; full sequences with slide-step dribble and pump fakes

# Side Blaze

## Overview
A simple, quick half-court play from a horns set using a dribble hand-off on the wing that flows directly into a pick-and-roll. The guard attacking the PnR penetrates the middle of the lane with corner and roll options. A simultaneous weak-side down screen keeps defensive help out of the key. [S4, pp.70-71]

## Formation
Horns set: 4 and 5 at the elbows (high post), 1 at the top with the ball, 2 in the strong-side corner, 3 in the weak-side corner. [S4, p.70]

## Phases

### Phase 1: Wing Entry
- 4 steps out of the horn position and receives the pass from 1.
- 1 cuts to the opposite wing. [S4, p.70]

### Phase 2: Dribble Hand-Off & Weak-Side Action
- 4 turns and dribbles down the wing toward 2.
- 2 takes a step toward the basket (jab) then **explodes** to the wing to receive the dribble hand-off from 4.
- Simultaneously on the opposite side, 1 sets a down screen for 3 — they exchange places, keeping those defenders moving and removing help from the key. [S4, p.70]

### Phase 3: Pick-and-Roll & Roll Man
- As soon as the hand-off is completed, 5 sprints across and sets an on-ball screen for 2.
- 4 **rolls to the rim** off the hand-off looking for the pass for an easy layup.
- 2 dribbles into the middle of the key and makes the best play: layup, pass to 4 rolling, or pass to 1 (who has cleared to the opposite wing). [S4, pp.70-71]

## Key Coaching Points
- Players must **not telegraph** their next move. Stay in a low stance and explode into the action. [S4, p.71]
- After running this play multiple times, x2 will try to cheat and get between the hand-off. This is the signal for **2 to go backdoor**. [S4, p.71]
- The weak-side screen exchange (1 down-screening for 3) is critical to remove potential help defenders from the paint. [S4, p.71]
- 4 must remember to roll hard to the rim immediately after the hand-off — 4 is often open because x4's attention shifts to guarding the hand-off.

## Key Personnel
- **1 (PG)**: Initiates entry pass to 4; cuts to opposite wing; sets weak-side down screen for 3; is a secondary outlet after 2 attacks.
- **2 (SG)**: Receives the dribble hand-off and attacks the PnR; must make the right read (layup, pass to 4 on roll, pass to 1).
- **3 (SF)**: Weak-side; uses 1's down screen to exchange and occupy their defender.
- **4 (PF)**: Receives entry pass, executes DHO with 2, rolls hard to the rim as the roll man.
- **5 (C)**: Sprints to set the on-ball screen for 2 immediately after the hand-off.

## Counters
- If x2 cheats on the hand-off, 2 goes backdoor for the layup.
- If the defense switches the PnR, 5 seals and posts up for a quick dump-off.
- 4 rolling to the rim is a primary counter if 2's defender drops to protect the paint.

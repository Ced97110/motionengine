# Hopping (Proprioception)

## Objective
Develop lower-extremity deceleration ability, balance, and proprioception through progressive hopping, building the reactive floor power needed for rebounding, sprinting, and direction changes.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open court or flat surface
- Optional equipment: hurdles or low boxes for advanced variation

## Execution
1. Assume a standing position with a slight bend in the knees.
2. Sling the arms back and hop forward as far as possible. Land with the knees bent and hips back — a soft, controlled landing position.
3. **Progression path**:
   - Hop off two legs, land on two legs
   - Hop off two legs, land on one leg
   - Hop off one leg, land on one leg
4. Once the basic progressions are mastered, hop forward and backward, and side to side, using hurdles or boxes for added challenge [S2, pp.134-135].

## Coaching Points
- "Jump far, land soft" — distance without control is incomplete
- Land with knees bent and hips back; do not let knees cave inward (valgus)
- "React off the floor" — ground contact time should be minimized as the drill advances
- Reacting quickly off the floor improves rebounding: "the ability to quickly react off the floor can make the difference between getting a rebound and being beat to the ball" [S2, p.135]
- Hurdles and boxes can increase the demand when basic hops are mastered

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Two-leg hop forward and land on two legs; focus on landing mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Two-leg hop, land on one leg; introduce side-to-side hops
3. **Advanced**: One-leg to one-leg hops; forward/backward/side-to-side over hurdles or boxes [S2, pp.134-135]

# Skiers With Reaction (Whole-Body Reaction)

## Objective
Improve mental processing speed, court sense, total-body reaction time to visual and audible stimuli, total-body agility, and general athleticism. [S8, p.216]

## Setup
- 1 player, agility ladder laid flat
- Coach positioned at the side or end of the ladder
- No additional equipment required

## Execution
1. Start in a two-point stance with the **right foot inside the first square** of the ladder and the **left foot outside** the first square.
2. Jump forward and diagonally to the **right** — land with the **left foot inside the second square** and the **right foot on the outside** of the second square.
3. Immediately upon landing, jump forward and diagonally to the **left** — land with the **right foot inside the third square** and the **left foot outside** the third square.
4. Repeat this alternating skier pattern through the entire ladder.
5. **Simultaneously**, react to the coach's visual commands throughout the drill (e.g., call out the number of fingers the coach holds up) without interrupting the footwork pattern.

## Coaching Points
- Maintain rhythm through the ladder while dividing attention toward the coach's visual cues
- Quick ground contact — do not linger on each landing
- Keep shoulders relatively square throughout the skier jumps
- Peripheral vision is key: "See the ladder with your feet, see the coach with your eyes"
- Note: stimuli picked up by **cones** (central vision) register faster than those picked up by **rods** (peripheral vision) — practicing both improves overall reaction time [S8, p.216]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Master skier footwork without reaction cues
2. **Intermediate**: Add coach finger-count visual reaction
3. **Advanced (Complex Variations)**:
   - Call out only odd or even numbers (coach shows both) — requires processing inhibition
   - Coach works a semicircle at the end of the ladder to stimulate peripheral vision
   - Two or three coaches flash numbers from different positions around the periphery
   - Add a 2–3 step "run in" before the ladder to increase speed and quickness demands [S8, p.216]
   - Add a sport-specific skill at the end of the ladder

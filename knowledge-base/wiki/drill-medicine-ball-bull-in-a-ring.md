# Medicine-Ball Bull in a Ring

## Objective
Improve quickness and elastic (reactive) strength through continuous medicine-ball chest passing while moving in a circle — simulates the upper-body reactive demands of passing through traffic and contesting shots. [S8, p.146]

## Setup
- 2 players
- Any flat surface
- 1 medicine ball (weight adjusted to skill level)

## Execution
1. Two partners stand facing each other.
2. Both players begin moving **in a circle** (clockwise or counterclockwise), maintaining their face-to-face orientation.
3. While circling, continuously **chest pass** the medicine ball back and forth.
4. Passes should be firm and reactive — catch and immediately release.
5. Continue for a set time or distance, then reverse direction.

## Coaching Points
- Catch with "soft hands" — absorb the ball quickly and immediately redirect force
- Stay in a low athletic stance while circling — do not stand upright
- "React, don't wait" — the moment the ball is caught, it should already be on its way back
- Arms remain bent until the catch, then extend explosively through the pass (stretch-shortening cycle)

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary chest passing at a moderate pace with a light ball
2. **Intermediate**: Add circular movement; increase ball weight slightly
3. **Advanced**: One partner calls the direction change; add an overhead pass variation or bounce pass to require rapid adjustment [S8, p.146]

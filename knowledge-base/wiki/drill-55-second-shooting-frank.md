# 55-Second Shooting Drill (the head coach)

## Objective
Develop game-speed catch-and-shoot mechanics (wing and corner) with inside-foot pivot and one-two step, using a high-rep timed format.

## Setup
- 3 players: shooter (1), rebounder (R), passer (P) with 2 balls
- Half court — wing and corner area
- Timer for 55-second work intervals, 5-second rotations

## Execution
1. **Passer** holds 2 balls at the passer spot (top/elbow area).
2. **Shooter (1)** comes hard to the **wing** for a catch-and-shoot jumper:
   - Plants on the **inside pivot foot**
   - Takes a **one-two step** into the catch
   - Shoots a catch-and-shoot jump shot
3. After the shot, 1 **fades hard to the corner** for the **second shot** (from the passer's second ball).
4. Repeat: wing → corner → wing → corner for **55 seconds**.
5. At the 55-second mark, **5 seconds** to rotate:
   - Shooter → Rebounder
   - Rebounder → Passer
   - Passer → Shooter

## Coaching Points
- **"Come hard to the wing"** — the cut must simulate game-speed movement, not a jog.
- **Inside pivot foot plant** — the shooter's inside foot (closer to the basket) lands first to allow proper square-up.
- **One-two step** — the catch footwork must be a rhythmic one-two step, not a jump stop, to maintain momentum into the shot.
- **Fade to corner** — the fade must be active and explosive, not a casual drift.
- The passer must deliver the ball to the shooter's catch spot **before** they stop — the pass leads the cut.
- This drill integrates seamlessly into the [[drill-shell-shooting-frank]] rotation (shooting end of the court).

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary catch-and-shoot from wing only; no corner fade.
2. **Intermediate**: Wing then corner sequence as described at game speed.
3. **Advanced**: Add a defensive closeout on the wing catch — shooter reads high/low and adjusts (shot, shot fake, drive).

# Shooting Elbow Alignment

## Summary
The elbow is the single most important component in a player's ability to make shots consistently. When the elbow is correctly positioned — directly under the ball, above the shooting foot, in an L-shape — the shot is automatically aimed at the target. When the elbow flares out (the "chicken wing" problem), the shooter is misaligned and will miss left or right regardless of other mechanics. Proper elbow alignment makes the shooter a "lifter" rather than a "slinger" or "pusher." [S5, pp.15-16]

## When to Use
- Checked on every single shot and during every form shooting drill
- Priority correction for any player who misses shots consistently to one side
- Critical teaching point when introducing shooting mechanics to beginners

## Key Principles
1. **Elbow directly under the ball** — the elbow must be positioned beneath the center of the ball, not to the side [S5, p.15]
2. **Elbow above the shooting foot** — the elbow, when in the L-position, should be directly above the lead (shooting-side) foot to maintain the shot line [S5, p.15]
3. **L-shape is non-negotiable** — too much bend = slinging; too little bend = pushing; the true L-shape = lifting; "Be a lifter, not a slinger or a pusher" [S5, p.15]
4. **Elbow faces the target** — when the elbow is in alignment, it naturally points directly at the basket [S5, p.15]
5. **Finish with elbow above the eyebrow** — the follow-through completes with the elbow fully extended and above the eyebrow level [S5, p.16]
6. **Be shot ready before the catch** — show the palm of the shooting hand to the passer with elbow already in L-position; the elbow is in alignment before the ball arrives [S5, p.16]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Consciously check elbow position on every shot in practice. Players must internalize the "elbow under the ball, above the shooting foot" feel before it becomes automatic.

## Variations
### Shot-Ready Elbow
Before receiving a pass, the shooting hand palm faces the passer and the elbow is already in the L-position. The player simply catches and lifts — the elbow never moves out of alignment during the catch-and-lift motion. [S5, p.16]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Chicken wing elbow (elbow flares out)** → Ball misses left or right of target, poor arch → Tuck elbow under the ball; use one-handed form shooting to ingrain the correct position [S5, pp.15-16]
2. **Young players shooting at full 10-foot baskets too early** → Forced to throw the ball, elbows fly out, bad habits form → Lower the basket or practice close-range form shooting first [S5, p.16]
3. **Balance hand pulling the shooting arm out of alignment** → Shooting elbow pops out when balance hand is added → Practice shooting-hand-only form shooting before adding the balance hand [S5, p.16]
4. **Not being shot ready** → Elbow out of position when receiving a pass → Pre-set elbow in L-position before the pass arrives [S5, p.16]
5. **Elbow not finishing above eyebrow** → Incomplete follow-through, flat shot, reduced accuracy → Drive the elbow up through the release and hold finish [S5, p.16]

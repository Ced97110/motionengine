# Thrusters (Burpees)

## Objective
Train explosive hip flexion and extension in the prone position while building cardiovascular conditioning — a small-space drill requiring zero equipment.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Minimal space (push-up position footprint)
- No equipment required
- Can be performed rhythmically for high reps as a cardio finisher

## Execution
1. Start in a **push-up (plank) position** with arms extended.
2. Explosively **drive both knees up into your chest** (hip flexion tuck).
3. Immediately **thrust both legs back straight** to the starting push-up position.
4. Repeat continuously at maximum speed for the desired rep count or time.

## Coaching Points
- Maintain neutral spine throughout — do not let hips sag when legs extend
- The "explosion" comes from the hip flexors driving knees upward — not a slow crawl
- This drill can be performed for high repetitions for a significant cardio benefit [S9, p.169]
- "Essentially this is a kind of prone tucking exercise." [S9, p.169]
- Excellent warm-up or conditioning finisher in any explosives or basketball session

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow step-in, step-out with control to learn the movement pattern
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate-speed simultaneous knee drive; 3 sets × 15 reps
3. **Advanced**: Maximum speed for 30–60 seconds; perform as a conditioning circuit with Kojaks and Unilateral Jumps

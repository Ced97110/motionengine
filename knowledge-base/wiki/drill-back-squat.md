# Back Squat

## Objective
Build bilateral lower-extremity strength in the quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, and core to support basketball force production, deceleration, and injury resilience.

## Setup
- 1 player (or athlete)
- Squat rack with barbell
- Weight room
- Begin with light/empty bar to learn technique before adding load

## Execution
1. Stand erect and place the barbell behind the neck on the upper trapezius, with weight evenly distributed across both shoulders.
2. Set feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, toes outwardly rotated approximately 15 degrees.
3. Keeping the back as straight as possible, slowly descend by bending at the hips and knees simultaneously until the thighs reach slightly *below* parallel to the floor.
4. During the descent, keep elbows pointed down and in line with the trunk. Allow some trunk flexion to maintain the barbell over the base of support — the trunk hinges at the pelvis while the spine stays neutral.
5. At the bottom position, do NOT bounce. Pause briefly and change direction by extending the hips and knees to return to the starting position.
6. Complete the designated number of repetitions, then rack the bar safely.

## Coaching Points
- **"Back straight, hinge at the hip"** — The spine stays neutral throughout. Any rounding of the lumbar spine under load is a red flag; reduce weight.
- **"Knees track over toes"** — The knees should not cave inward (valgus collapse) during descent or ascent. This is a critical ACL injury risk factor.
- **"Elbows down"** — Keeping elbows in line with the trunk helps maintain the barbell position and trunk alignment.
- **"No bounce at the bottom"** — Bouncing out of the bottom position shifts load to passive structures (ligaments) rather than muscles. Control the reversal.
- **"Weight on whole foot"** — Heels should not rise. If they do, the athlete lacks ankle mobility — address before adding load.
- Start with light weights. Movement quality precedes load intensity [S2, p.9].

## Primary Muscles
- **Primary**: Quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis), gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, biceps femoris
- **Secondary**: Gastrocnemius, adductor longus, rectus abdominis, internal/external obliques [S2, p.10]

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Bodyweight squat to a box (box squat) — learn depth and posture without load. Progress to goblet squat with a light dumbbell.
2. **Intermediate**: Barbell back squat to parallel with controlled load. Focus on 3-5 sets of 5-8 reps with progressive weight increase.
3. **Advanced**: Full below-parallel back squat with periodized loading. Incorporate pause squats (2-3 second hold at bottom) to eliminate bounce and build positional strength.

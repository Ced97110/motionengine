# Barbell Bent-Over Row (Basketball)

## Objective
Develop upper-back strength, low-back stability, and grip strength through a standing free-weight pulling movement — building the total back power needed for basketball's athletic demands.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Barbell with appropriate weight plates
- Open floor space
- Optional: adjustable bench at 45-degree incline for dumbbell variation

## Execution
1. Stand and hold a barbell in an **overhand grip** (palms facing the body), arms extended, feet **shoulder-width apart**.
2. Hand position on the bar: in line with or **slightly wider than the shoulders**.
3. Keep the back **flat**. Slightly bend the knees and lower the bar to just below knee height by **shifting the hips back** (hip hinge).
4. Pull the bar up in a straight line toward the **lower chest**, keeping elbows close to the body. **Squeeze the shoulder blades together** at the top.
5. Slowly lower the bar to the starting position while maintaining a flat back and bent knees.
6. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions.

## Coaching Points
- "Flat back from start to finish — a rounded back under load is an injury."
- Hip hinge to lower — not a squat. Knees bend slightly but the movement is driven by the hips shifting backward.
- "Drive elbows up and back — bar path is straight, not swinging."
- Squeeze shoulder blades at the top of every rep.
- This is a **more advanced exercise than the seated row** because it demands strong low back and grip strength in a standing position.
- Grip strength developed here transfers directly to ball control.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: **Dumbbell Bent-Over Row on incline bench** — adjust bench to 45-degree incline, straddle the bench with chest on the pad, both feet flat on floor; row dumbbells from hanging to chest height in overhand grip. Removes low-back stability demand.
2. **Intermediate**: Standard barbell bent-over row.
3. **Advanced**: **Supinated Grip (palms facing forward)** — makes it easier to keep elbows close to the body and shifts emphasis; or increase load.

## Muscles Involved
- **Primary**: Latissimus dorsi
- **Secondary**: Trapezius, rhomboid major, rhomboid minor, teres major, posterior deltoid, biceps brachii

## Basketball Focus
The barbell bent-over row is essential for developing the **proper athletic position** used in all basketball movements. You need strength in **both the upper and lower back** to handle:
- Extensive **running on the court**, including quick bursts and sudden stops
- **Grip strength** for controlling the basketball during dribbling, passing, and rebounding
- The **physical battles** under the basket — holding position, creating openings, scoring through contact

The standing position requirement makes this exercise particularly sport-specific to basketball's upright, loaded athletic stances.

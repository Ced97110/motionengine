# Power Down Attack

## Overview
A low-post featured offensive set starting from a 1-4 box formation, designed to deliver the ball to a dominant center in a one-on-one situation while keeping all other players actively occupied to counter double teams. The ball does not go directly to the post — player movement creates the safer indirect entry route [S13, pp.114-116].

## Formation
- 1-4 box set: O4 and O5 at free-throw line elbows (high), O2 and O3 on low blocks, O1 above the circle in the middle
- O1 determines side, then the play begins

## Phases

### Phase 1: Guard-to-Post Down Screen Entry
- O1 dribbles to the same side as O5, free-throw line extended, keeping the dribble alive
- O5 turns strong side and sets a down screen for O2 on the low block
- O2 uses the screen and moves to the top of the key
- After O2 clears, O5 executes a **reverse pivot and posts up** — spreads feet wide for balance, arms and elbows up as a big target

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.9 — O1 dribbles to right wing, O5 down-screens for O2 at block, O2 pops to top of key, O5 reverse pivots to post -->

### Phase 2: Pass to Post or Pass to Top
- O1 decides: **pass inside to O5** OR **pass to O2 at the top of the key**
- **If O1 passes to O5**:
  - O1 sets a split-action screen for O2, who becomes an outlet in case of a double-team on O5
  - O5 makes a move; if no shot, O5 passes to O2

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.10 — O1 passes to O5 post-up, O1 immediately screens for O2 (split action), O2 as double-team outlet -->

### Phase 3: Ball Reversal — Quick Passes Around the Horn
- After O5 outlets to O2, O2 quickly passes to O1 (relocated to top of key off split-action screen)
- O1 rotates ball to O4 at free-throw line extended
- O3 on the left block pops toward the corner as O4 delivers the ball
- O4 screens away for O1, who seeks the ball

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.11 — Ball reversal: O5→O2→O1→O4; O3 pops corner; O4 screens away for O1 -->

### Phase 4: Follow the Ball — O5 Re-Posts
- When O5 sees O2 pass to O1, O5 follows the ball across the lane and establishes a **strong post-up position on the new ball side**
- After the floor is properly spaced and balanced, O3 (now with the ball in the corner) should be able to get the ball into O5 for a move

<!-- DIAGRAM: Figure 5.12 — O3 in left corner with ball, O5 has followed ball across lane and re-posted on left block -->

### Phase 5: Continuity Option
- If O3 cannot feed O5, O3 passes to O1 coming off O4's screen
- O1 reads: dribble drive or pull-up jump shot

## Key Coaching Points
- Do NOT start O5 on the block and attempt a direct entry pass — defense will immediately double-team
- Player movement must occur first inside and out; the center should move to where the pass is less dangerous
- O5's reverse pivot post-up fundamentals: feet wider than shoulders, knees bent/crouched, arms and elbows up, eyes on passer
- After catching, O5 **immediately identifies where defensive help is coming from** before making a decision
- O5's post scoring reads: move to score, pass to feeder/cutter, kick to spot-up shooter whose defender doubled, pass to weak-side cutter
- "Follow the ball" — O5 must read ball direction and relocate to the new ball side quickly
- Peripheral players must stay active and alert to create kick-out opportunities when double teams occur

## Counters
- If O5 is fronted: use the ball reversal (Phase 3) to move the defense and feed from a new angle
- If double team comes early: O1's split-action screen for O2 is the designed pressure release
- If no post entry is available: O1 coming off O4's screen creates a dribble drive/pull-up option

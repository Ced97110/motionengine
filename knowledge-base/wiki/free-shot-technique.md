# Free-Shot Technique

## Summary
The free shot (also called "free throw" or "foul shot," though the author prefers the positive term "free shot") is the highest-leverage uncontested shot in basketball — and the most neglected in practice. The physical mechanics are identical to standard shooting form; the critical differentiator is the **mental and routine component**. Consistency is the foundation: the same stance, same grip, same pre-shot routine, every single time. The standard of excellence is **80% or above** — anything less is unacceptable at any level. [S5, pp.46-55]

## When to Use
- Anytime a player steps to the free-throw line in practice or a game
- End-of-practice routine requiring **makes** (not just attempts) to build quality reps
- Players should aim to make **50-100 free shots per practice session** [S5, p.48]

## Key Principles

### Stance
1. **Locate the tack/nail** in the center of the free-throw line — it marks the alignment point with the center of the basket. [S5, p.49]
2. **Shooting foot on the tack**: Right foot for right-handed shooters, left for left-handed shooters.
3. **Staggered stance**: Place the non-shooting foot next to the shooting foot, slide it back until toes are level with the arch of the shooting foot, then spread to shoulder-width. [S5, p.49]
4. **Bend at hips AND knees**: Bring shoulders forward so they are in front of the feet, head in front of the shoulders. Heels come off the floor. Flat-footed shooting creates "negative motion" — body weight moves backward on the release, causing the ball to fall short. [S5, pp.49-50]
5. **Young players**: May need to step into the shot (bring shooting foot behind non-shooting foot, then step to the tack) to generate enough power; be cautious not to step over the line. [S5, p.50]

### Grip and Arms
6. **Shooting hand under and centered** on the seams; ball rests on fingertips and fingerpads — **no palm contact**. [S5, p.52]
7. **Balance hand on the side** of the ball; thumbs form the letter **T**. Balance hand steadies the ball; palm does NOT touch the ball. [S5, p.52]
8. **Wrist wrinkled, cocked, and locked**; elbow tucked in, aligned directly above the shooting foot — shooting arm forms the letter **L**. [S5, p.52]
9. This establishes the **full shot line**: toe → knee → hip → elbow → shoulder → wrist → ball → center of basket. [S5, p.52]

### Release and Follow-Through
10. **Bend and extend** rhythm: heels come off the floor as knees and hips extend upward into the shot. [S5, p.52]
11. Shooting arm fully extends, **elbow finishes above the eyebrow**, hand in the hoop. [S5, p.53]
12. **Both hands stay up** until the ball swishes through the net. [S5, p.53]
13. **Stay on toes/balls of feet** until the ball clears the net. [S5, p.53]

### Pre-Shot Routine
14. The **specific details of the routine are inconsequential** — what matters is absolute consistency between practice and games. [S5, p.50]
15. Choose a **fixed number of bounces** and never vary it.
16. **Clear negative thoughts** before stepping to the line — focus only on the next shot.
17. **Visualize the swish**: See the ball going through the net before you shoot (Steve Nash example). [S5, p.50]
18. **Take a breath and relax** — tension disrupts mechanics. [S5, p.50]
19. **Own your routine** with personal rituals (e.g., Jason Kidd's kiss to the basket, Karl Malone's self-talk). Consistency is the only requirement. [S5, p.51]

## Player Responsibilities
- **All positions**: Every player on the roster must develop a consistent free-shot routine and meet the 80%+ standard. Centers and power forwards with poor free-shot shooting are exploited via intentional-foul strategies ("Hack-a" tactics).

## Variations
### Stepping Into the Free Shot (Youth)
For younger or smaller players who cannot reach the basket with standard form, establish normal stance then bring the shooting foot behind the non-shooting foot. Step forward to the tack when ready to shoot. This generates extra momentum. Must avoid crossing the line. [S5, p.50]

## Common Mistakes
1. **Inconsistent routine** (different bounce count, different alignment) → Destroys muscle memory. Correction: drill the exact same routine every single rep.
2. **Flat-footed stance (no hip bend)** → "Negative motion" — body weight drifts backward, shot falls short. Correction: *"bend at the hips AND the knees — shoulders in front of feet."* [S5, p.52]
3. **Shooting for attempts, not makes** ("50 and go home") → Players rush, no quality focus. Correction: require **makes** as the goal, not attempts. [S5, p.48]
4. **Bringing negative thoughts to the line** → Mental clutter disrupts routine. Correction: one deep breath, reset, focus only on the shot in front of you.
5. **Dropping the balance hand** → Creates body twist, shot drifts. Correction: hold both hands high until the net swishes.
6. **Grip not on the seams** → Inconsistent release direction. Correction: always find the seams before settling into the grip.

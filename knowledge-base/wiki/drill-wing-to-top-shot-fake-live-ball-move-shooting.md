# Wing to Top Shot Fake (SF) Live-Ball Move Shooting (Workout #11-19)

## Objective
Reinforce the **shot fake as a live-ball move** from beyond the three-point line — teaching players to sell the fake convincingly and then attack off the dribble or rise into a pull-up shot, within the wing-to-top movement pattern.

## Setup
- 1 player (rebounder/passer recommended)
- Half court, three-point arc
- Same 3-spot wing-to-top movement pattern
- Shot chart tracking FGA, FGM, %, FTA, FTM, %
- **Total shots**: 80 per full session (60 field goal attempts + 20 free throws)

## Execution
1. Player moves through the **wing → top → wing** arc.
2. At each spot, the player **catches on a two-foot stop** past the three-point line.
3. Player immediately performs a **shot fake (SF)** — showing the ball upward as if shooting — to draw the defender into the air or into a close-out lean.
4. After the fake, the player attacks off the dribble (show-and-go) or resets and rises for the jumper.
5. Repeat until **10 shots** have been taken per set.
6. Full session structure:
   - Right Side: SF Shots (10) → FTs (5) → SF Shots (10) → FTs (5) → SF Shots (10)
   - Left Side: same structure
7. All moves and shots at **game speed**.

## Coaching Points
- The shot fake must be **convincing** — lift the ball up to the shot release point, bend the knees slightly, and make eye contact with the basket. "If you don't believe it, the defender won't either."
- After the fake, **protect the ball** — rip to hip or attack immediately before the defender recovers.
- Players should practice the shot fake resolving into *both* a drive *and* a shot — the threat of both makes each option better.
- Do not over-dribble after the fake — one hard dribble into the pull-up is the standard action.
- Benchmark: FG 36-60 (60%), FT 17-20 (85%).
- Train both sides of the floor equally.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Stationary shot fake with no defender — rehearse the mechanics of a convincing fake.
2. **Intermediate**: Add the wing-to-top movement; player chooses to shoot immediately or fake-and-go based on their feel.
3. **Advanced**: Live closeout defender — defender sprints at shooter and reads the fake; shooter must win the play.

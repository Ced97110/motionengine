# Deadlift

## Objective
Build posterior-chain strength (low back, glutes, hamstrings) to enhance force output for jumping, acceleration, deceleration, and stability for boxing out and rebounding.

## Setup
- **Players:** 1
- **Location:** weight room
- **Equipment:** barbell, weight plates, lifting straps or chalk optional
- Feet slightly narrower than shoulder-width to allow room for arms [S2, p.14]

## Execution
1. Stand over a barbell with the bar positioned above the midfoot, feet slightly narrower than shoulder-width.
2. Grip the bar with arms straight and perpendicular to the floor, shoulders slightly over the bar, hands outside the legs. Use a **reverse grip** (one palm down, one palm up) for grip security.
3. Slowly bend the hips and knees, keeping the back straight, until the shins touch the bar. The bar remains at the midfoot. Raise shoulders to straighten the arms; head aligns with a neutrally positioned spine.
4. Pull the bar from the floor by slowly extending the hips and knees simultaneously; shoulders rise with the hips. Keep arms and back straight — **do not let the back round.** Maintain close bar-to-body contact throughout the pull.
5. Return the bar to the floor by slowly flexing the hips then the knees, controlling the bar until it touches the floor. [S2, pp.14-15]

## Coaching Points
- **"Back straight, chest up"** — rounding of the lumbar spine under load is the primary injury risk.
- The bar should travel in a vertical line as close to the body as possible.
- Hips and shoulders should rise at the same rate — "don't shoot the hips up first."
- Reverse grip prevents the barbell from rolling out of the hands at heavier loads.
- Control the eccentric (lowering) — don't drop the bar.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner:** Romanian deadlift (RDL) to build hamstring flexibility and hinge pattern before loading from the floor.
2. **Intermediate:** Conventional deadlift from the floor at moderate load, full range.
3. **Advanced:** Increase load; incorporate trap-bar deadlift variation for more upright torso position.

## Basketball Application
As a multijoint exercise the deadlift enhances strength in the low back, hip, and lower extremity — muscle groups important for force output, acceleration, and jumping (e.g., layups). It also enhances deceleration/cutting and improves base of support for boxing out, receiving the basketball, rebounding, and playing under the basket. [S2, pp.14-15]

**Primary muscles:** gluteus maximus, erector spinae (iliocostalis, longissimus, spinalis), hamstrings (semitendinosus, semimembranosus, biceps femoris)

**Secondary muscles:** rectus abdominis, transversus abdominis, quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius), iliopsoas

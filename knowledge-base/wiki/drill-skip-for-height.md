# Skip for Height

## Objective
Increase hip extension and flexion strength, improve ankle-muscle stiffness, and enhance leg power and stride length by maximizing vertical height on each skip.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Open flat surface — court, field, or track
- Distance: typically 20–30 meters per rep

## Execution
1. Begin skipping forward.
2. Drive the free knee **upward as aggressively as possible** on each skip cycle.
3. Coordinate aggressive arm action — drive the opposite arm upward with each knee drive.
4. Focus on maximum height on each skip rather than covering horizontal distance.
5. Land softly but quickly, minimizing ground contact time.

## Coaching Points
- "Drive the knee to the sky" — the higher the knee drive, the greater the hip flexor and hip extensor loading.
- Arm action must be equally aggressive — passive arms reduce skip height and undermine the purpose.
- Land on the ball of the foot, not the heel, to preserve ankle stiffness.
- Keep the torso tall — do not collapse forward.
- Works as an excellent warm-up and speed development drill before sprint work.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Slow-paced skip for height emphasis, short distance (15m), focus on mechanics
2. **Intermediate**: Full-speed aggressive skip, 25–30m per rep, 3–4 reps
3. **Advanced**: Weighted vest skip for height, 20m per rep — increases hip power demands

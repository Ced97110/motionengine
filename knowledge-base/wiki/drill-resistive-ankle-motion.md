# Resistive Ankle Motion

## Objective
Strengthen the ankle through full range of motion (dorsiflexion, plantar flexion, inversion, eversion) using a resistance band, building the foundational ankle strength needed to prevent sprains during basketball activity.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Bench, table, or mat
- Equipment: resistance band, rolled towel

## Execution
1. Assume a long-sitting position (legs extended) with a towel roll placed under the ankle to hold it in a neutral position.
2. Attach a resistance band around the foot; hold the other end of the band in your hands (or anchor it to a fixed point).
3. Moving against the resistance of the band, complete the full ankle range of motion:
   - **Dorsiflexion**: pull toes toward shin
   - **Plantar flexion**: push toes away (like pressing a gas pedal)
   - **Inversion**: turn sole inward
   - **Eversion**: turn sole outward
4. Perform the prescribed number of repetitions in each direction.
5. **Progression**: Increase the resistance of the band [S2, p.137].

## Coaching Points
- Ankle strength is often overlooked in basketball training — this is foundational injury prevention work [S2, p.137]
- Move slowly and with control through full range in each direction
- The towel roll ensures the ankle starts from a true neutral, maximizing range worked
- "Ankle sprains happen fast — strength through full range is your insurance policy"
- Can be performed bilaterally or focus on the injured/weaker side

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Light resistance band, slow tempo, each direction separately
2. **Intermediate**: Moderate resistance band, all four directions in sequence
3. **Advanced**: Heavier band, increase repetitions and speed of motion [S2, p.137]

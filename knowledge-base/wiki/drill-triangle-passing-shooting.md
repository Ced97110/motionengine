# Triangle Passing Shooting Drill

## Objective
Generate high-volume midrange catch-and-shoot repetitions with a game-realistic passing angle, while simultaneously training passers and rebounders.

## Setup
- Minimum 7 players: 1 rebounder (R), 3+ passers (P) with balls, 3+ shooters (S)
- Half court
- **Triangle formation:** Rebounder under the hoop; passers stacked at an elbow; shooters stacked along the baseline
- Can be run from both sides and reversed (pass from baseline to elbow) for different shooting angles

## Execution
1. First passer in line delivers the ball to the shooter's shot pocket.
2. First shooter in line is in a **shot-ready position**, calls for the ball, takes a one-two step into the pass, catches, sights the target, and shoots — freezing the follow-through.
3. Rebounder secures the ball out of the net (trying not to let it hit the floor) and immediately **speed dribbles** with the weak hand to the end of the passing line.
4. Passer (who just threw) moves to the end of the shooting line.
5. Shooter (who just shot) becomes the next rebounder.
6. Cycle continues without stopping.

## Coaching Points
- **The passing angle is more gamelike than spinouts or partner shooting.** In games, passes come from angles — this drill simulates that while still practicing one-two-step mechanics.
- **Shooter must call for the ball every rep.** The passer delivers when the shooter calls.
- **Rebounder speed dribbles with the weak hand** — builds weak-hand ball handling while keeping the drill moving.
- **Freeze the follow-through every rep.** Coach watches and corrects immediately.
- **Competition options:**
  - Team total makes in 2 minutes
  - Individual make tracking with losers running sprints
  - 2-minute clock from four spots: both corners and both elbows
- **Run from both sides of the court** to train shots from all angles.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Focus on form only; slow the rotation if needed; 1 minute per spot.
2. **Intermediate**: Add competitive element (team makes goal); run from both corners and elbows.
3. **Advanced**: Reverse line rotation (pass from baseline to elbow) to practice elbow catch-and-shoot; add closeout from the passer before transitioning to the shooting line.

# Ladder Speed Run

## Objective
Enhance stride frequency timing and teach quick turnover by running through an agility ladder with both feet contacting inside each rung.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Agility ladder (sticks 18 inches / 46 cm apart)
- Open court or field

## Execution
1. Set up agility ladder flat on the ground with rungs 18 inches (46 cm) apart
2. Run through the ladder **as fast as possible**, touching **both feet down between each rung**
3. Emphasize **lifting the knee high** on each step
4. **Quickly contact the ground** after each knee lift — minimize ground contact time [S8, p.32]

## Coaching Points
- "High knees, quick contacts" — the pairing of these two cues produces optimal stride frequency mechanics
- Athletes tend to slow down to be accurate — push them to maintain high speed while being accurate
- This drill directly trains the rapid foot turnover that produces higher stride frequency on the court
- Proper arm mechanics should be maintained throughout
- Use the ladder as a tempo setter, not a limiter — the goal is maximum speed within the pattern

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Walk through the ladder touching both feet in each rung, focusing on high knee
2. **Intermediate**: Ladder Speed Run as described at near-maximum pace
3. **Advanced**: [[drill-ladder-stride-run]] — one foot per every other rung with upright posture to simulate actual sprint stride patterns [S8, pp.32-33]

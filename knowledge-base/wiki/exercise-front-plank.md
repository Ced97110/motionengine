# Front Plank

## Objective
Build isometric core stability (transversus abdominis, rectus abdominis, multifidus, erector spinae) for holding athletic position, absorbing contact, and transferring force from legs to upper body.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor or exercise mat
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Lie face down on the floor. Position feet hip-width apart with toes pointed down. Fully extend legs and hips.
2. Flex the elbows to 90 degrees and position them directly under the shoulders with forearms in contact with the floor. Make fists with palms facing each other, or place palms flat on the floor.
3. Begin by digging the toes into the floor and engage the muscles of the legs and hips.
4. While keeping the lower body engaged, drive the elbows into the floor and lift the hips off the floor to a **neutral spine position**.
5. Hold the plank position for the prescribed amount of time.
6. Breathe with controlled inhales matched with controlled exhales throughout the hold [S11, Ch.8].

## Coaching Points
- "Maintain proper alignment with the ankle, knees, hips, torso, and head all in line" — no sagging hips or piking [S11, Ch.8]
- "Dig the toes and elbows into the floor" — active engagement, not passive hanging
- If the hips sag, regress to the knee-supported variation first
- Watch for the head dropping below the spine — cue athlete to look at the floor 12 inches in front of the hands

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (Regressed)**: Lift the feet off the floor and flex the knees to 90 degrees — lower body is supported at the knees; shorter lever reduces demand [S11, Ch.8]
2. **Intermediate**: Full front plank on toes for timed holds (20–60 seconds)
3. **Advanced**: Birddog plank — lift contralateral arm and leg from the plank position (see [[exercise-birddog-plank]])

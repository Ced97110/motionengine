# V-Up

## Objective
Develop dynamic core flexion strength (rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, iliopsoas) through a full-range simultaneous arm-and-leg lift — building the trunk power needed for jumping, cutting, and contact finishes.

## Setup
- 1 player
- Flat floor or exercise mat
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Lie face up on the floor with arms extended overhead, legs together, and toes pointed up — this is the beginning position. Begin with a deep inhale [S11, Ch.8].
2. Initiate movement with hip and spinal flexion by lifting the **legs and arms simultaneously** toward each other. Maintain full extension of the knees and elbows as the hips flex.
3. Exhale slowly and with control as feet and hands move toward each other.
4. As the hands and feet travel toward each other, lift the shoulders and head off the floor (chin tucked) to allow hands and feet to nearly meet over the hips. Hold this final position for approximately 1 second.
5. Return to the beginning position with complete control of the arms and legs as the hips and spine extend back. Elbows and knees remain extended throughout.
6. Begin the next inhale as the arms and legs travel back toward the floor [S11, Ch.8].

## Coaching Points
- "Keep the knees and elbows remain fully extended through the full range of motion" [S11, Ch.8]
- Regression: If athletes cannot reach hands to feet, have them contact somewhere between the knees and ankles [S11, Ch.8]
- Watch for bent knees — the most common compensation when core strength is insufficient
- The movement should be slow and controlled, not a momentum-driven swinging motion

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Touch hands to lower leg (between knee and ankle) instead of feet — shorter lever
2. **Intermediate**: Full V-up with hands meeting feet at full hip flexion
3. **Advanced**: Hold a light weight plate overhead to increase the arm-lever resistance demand

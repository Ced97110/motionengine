# Defensive Pivot Breakdown Drills (Push-Push, Push-and-Talk, Pride)

## Summary
Three interrelated breakdown drills from S6 Chapter 7 teach and reinforce the four basic on-ball defensive foot movements — advance step, drop-and-swing step, push-off step, and slide-step. These drills progress from mass (coach-led, no ball), to directional movement (structured route with 6 direction changes), to full game-realistic application (live dribbler, full court). All three drills emphasize the reverse pivot drop-step and the push-push method as the foundation of effective man-to-man defense. [S6, pp.273-280]

## When to Use
- Daily pre-practice warm-up or early-practice defensive segment
- Whenever on-ball defensive footwork breaks down in games or scrimmages
- Early season when establishing the team's defensive identity
- As a conditioning tool combined with technical development

## Key Principles
1. **All three drills emphasize the reverse pivot as the core action** — quickness comes from the push-off after the drop-step, not from athleticism alone [S6, p.273]
2. **Progress from mass → directional → competitive** — the whole-part-whole method applied to defensive footwork [S6, p.273]
3. **Eyes up and communication** — especially in the push-and-talk drill, defenders must keep their head up and communicate to avoid collisions while still executing proper footwork [S6, p.276]
4. **Never reach for the ball** — hands-behind-back variation of the pride drill eliminates the reaching habit and forces foot movement [S6, p.278]
5. **Constant variety** — change start/finish points of the pride drill to prevent boredom and increase game realism [S6, pp.278-279]

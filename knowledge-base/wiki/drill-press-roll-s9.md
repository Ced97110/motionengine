# Press Roll (Front Flip Chain — Step 2)

## Objective
Develop the ability to use both hands symmetrically as load-bearing levers to control a forward roll, progressively conditioning the wrists, elbows, and shoulders for the greater forces they will absorb in later handspring steps.

## Setup
- 1 athlete
- Soft surface preferred (grass, carpet, gymnastics mat)
- No equipment required

## Execution
1. Stand with feet symmetrical (shoulder-width). Squat toward the ground.
2. Place **both palms** on the floor in front as you dip down.
3. Lower slowly forward by squatting and bringing the torso down. Straighten the legs until the body overbalances forward.
4. Braced arms guide the movement as you roll over — **head tucked in and protected; head takes virtually no weight.**
5. Roll over the back without veering sideways.
6. Finish in a seated position.

## Coaching Points
- **Symmetry** — Both hands and both sides of the upper-back should load evenly; avoid rotating to a single shoulder
- **Abdominal tension** — A braced (curled) trunk stabilizes the roll and protects the spine; cue: "hollow body through the roll"
- **Head takes zero weight** — Tucking the chin protects the cervical spine; the upper-back, not the head, contacts the floor first
- **Intimidation is real** — "A controlled descent of the head, abdominal tension, and soft surfaces are tricks to make rolls seem easier" [S9, p.8]
- **Progression load shift**: Easiest version — hands AND upper-back take weight from the start. Harder version — maximize force through hands so upper-back only loads at the last moment

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Hands and upper-back contact simultaneously; head fully tucked; finish seated
2. **Intermediate**: Increase hand loading — upper-back contacts only at the last moment; roll to standing
3. **Advanced**: Increase speed toward a smooth, flowing, standing-finish roll — bridging toward the jump roll

# Three-Against-Three / Four-Against-Four Continuous Full-Court Drill

## Objective
Develop full- and half-court defensive organization, transition, and competitive toughness in a multi-team format that rewards defensive stops. [S1, p.211]

## Setup
- Four teams in different colored jerseys
- Two teams at each basket (so two full-court games can run simultaneously if needed)
- Begin with 4-on-4 (or 3-on-3) at one basket
- Win condition: first team to a **predetermined number of stops** (5 or 7 recommended)
- Losers play a 3-team round-robin to a smaller stop count (3 or 5)

## Execution
1. Play 4-on-4 (or 3-on-3) at one basket. One team is on offense, the other on defense.
2. **On a stop** (turnover, steal, or defensive rebound): the defensive team earns 1 point. The losing offensive team's opponent gets the ball and attacks the next basket, where a fresh team waits at mid-court to defend.
3. The fresh team defends the advancing team — the cycle continues full-court, back and forth.
4. First team to accumulate the preset number of stops wins; winners rest and shoot free throws while losers compete in a round-robin consolation bracket.
5. Coach sets the specific defense to practice (e.g., trapping the first wing pass, run-and-jump, doubling the post) and may also dictate the offensive set to run, creating deliberate practice scenarios.

## Coaching Points
- Emphasize **full- and half-court organization** on every possession — no freelancing
- Assign a penalty to losers and a reward to winners to drive competitive intensity
- Rotate players through all positions so everyone practices both transition offense and defense
- Use the drill to install and test specific defensive coverages against specific offensive sets
- "Stop the ball, protect the paint, make them shoot from outside."

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (3v3):** Fewer players per team reduces spacing complexity; focus on individual matchup defense and transition.
2. **Intermediate (4v4):** Introduce help defense and rotations on the drive.
3. **Advanced:** Coach calls a defensive scheme after each stop (e.g., switch to zone on the next possession) forcing teams to communicate and adjust.

# In-Place Tuck Jumps

## Objective
Improve power in the lower body through explosive bilateral jumps with knee-to-chest tuck, developing vertical leap and reactive strength. [S8, p.170]

## Setup
- 1 player
- Any flat surface
- No equipment

## Execution
1. Stand in the power position; load the lower body by swinging both arms back while flexing the hips and knees.
2. Begin the triple extension sequence: hips → knees → ankles as the arms swing forward (but close to the body).
3. Jump straight up, tucking both knees to the chest at the peak of the jump.
4. Upon landing, immediately repeat with the same technique.
5. For quickness: perform as rapidly as possible for time while counting reps, or execute a fixed number of reps as rapidly as possible.

## Coaching Points
- Arms drive close to the body — wide arm swing dissipates upward energy.
- "Triple extension then tuck" — the sequence matters: ankles are last to fire, then knees come up immediately.
- Land softly but rebound immediately; the goal is minimal ground contact time.
- Can be performed on a single leg to increase difficulty.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Standard bilateral tuck jump; moderate speed.
2. **Intermediate**: Increase speed; count reps in 10-second sets.
3. **Advanced (Pike Jump)**: Keep legs straight while tucking (pike position) — increases hamstring demand.
4. **Advanced (Single Leg)**: Perform on one leg.

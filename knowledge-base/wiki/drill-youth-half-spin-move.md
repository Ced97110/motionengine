# Half-Spin Move (Drill 15)

## Objective
Teach the half-spin move — a partial (~90-degree) spin that fakes the defender into reacting to a full spin, then reverses direction past the defender — used as a counter to the spin move.

## Setup
- Individual drill (teaching): one coach, one player
- Drilling: players line up in three lines at mid-court; coach at free-throw line area
- Mark a **2-foot × 2-foot (.6m × .6m) square** using tape near a line (e.g., free-throw line)
- Dribbler starts ~10 feet (3m) away, dribbles toward the coach
- Begin with a control dribble; progress to a speed dribble
- This move is the **counter to the spin move**

## Execution

### Beginner
1. Dribbler starts ~10 feet away and dribbles toward the coach (control dribble).
2. At the square, dribbler executes the half-spin move.
3. Coach moves in the direction of the step-out fake — teaching the *when* while teaching the *how*. Coach may also begin in an overplay position.

## Coaching Points

### When to Use
- When the defender has adjusted and is **anticipating the full spin move**
- After using the spin move successfully (sets up the half-spin)
- Against overplaying defenders who are shifting to one side

### How to Execute (Right-Hand Dribble Example)
1. Get the defender to **overplay**: fake with the right foot toward the defender's left foot and use head/shoulder fakes. Once the defender overplays, position the **left foot in the middle of the defender**.
2. **Fake the spin**: swing the right foot so the body is **sideways to the defender's body** — making it look exactly like the full spin.
3. **Execute only ~90 degrees** of the spin. As the dribbler is executing the half-spin, the defender is **shifting to the right** in anticipation of the full spin.
4. **Immediately explode** with the right foot **back** in the original direction of attack. Swing the right foot back to the right — get the right foot **outside the defender's left foot**.
5. Bring the **left foot around the defender's left foot** and explode in a **straight line toward the basket**.

### Progression
- After mastering with the dominant hand, practice with the **weaker hand**.

## Common Mistakes
1. Half-spin is too small — defender doesn't commit → make the first 90 degrees look identical to the full spin
2. Right foot doesn't get past the defender's left foot on the reversal → no positional advantage gained
3. Hesitation after the reversal → must explode immediately the moment the right foot reverses direction
4. Using the half-spin without setting it up with the full spin first → defenders won't commit to the right if they haven't been burned by the full spin

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Control dribble into half-spin with passive coach-defender
2. **Intermediate**: Speed dribble into half-spin
3. **Advanced**: Chain spin and half-spin alternately in combination (Drill 16)

# Closeout 1 and Closeout 2 (the head coach)

## Objective
Build defensive closeout explosiveness, lateral slide endurance, and the discipline to never give up the middle of the court on a closeout.

## Setup
- **Closeout 1**: Half court, cones at left block, right corner of free-throw lane, right block, left corner of free-throw lane.
- **Closeout 2**: Half court, cones at left corner, first cone just outside left corner of free-throw lane, directly under the rim, right corner of free-throw lane, right corner.
- Medicine balls needed for Closeout 2 (2 coaches retrieve and return balls).
- Each player goes 3 trips.

## Execution

### Closeout 1
1. X1 starts on the **left block**.
2. Sprint to the **right corner of the free-throw lane** — close out, not giving up the middle.
3. Defensive slide down the lane to the **right block**.
4. Sprint to the **left corner of the free-throw lane** — close out again, no middle.
5. Defensive slide back to the starting position (left block).
6. That equals one trip. Do 3 trips per player.

### Closeout 2
1. X1 starts in the **left corner**, back to the basket, holding a **medicine ball**.
2. Defensive slide to the first cone just outside the left corner of the free-throw lane; drop the medicine ball.
3. Slide to the cone **directly under the rim**; pick up a medicine ball there.
4. Slide to the cone outside the **right corner of the free-throw lane**; drop the medicine ball.
5. Slide to the cone in the **right corner**.
6. That equals one trip. Do 3 trips per player.
7. Two coaches pick up dropped balls and return them to the baseline for the next cycle.

## Coaching Points
- **"Stay in a stance"** — do not stand upright between movements.
- **"Feet never come together"** — slides must be disciplined lateral steps, not crossovers.
- **"Short, quick, and low"** — slides are not big strides; they are short, rapid, and low.
- **"Not giving up the middle"** — every closeout is angled to force baseline or sideline, never the center of the court.
- Medicine ball adds resistance and trains body awareness during slides.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Closeout 1 without time pressure; focus on stance and closeout angle.
2. **Intermediate**: Closeout 1 at full speed, add medicine ball (Closeout 2).
3. **Advanced**: Timed competition — fastest clean trip wins.

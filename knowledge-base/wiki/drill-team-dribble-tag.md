# Team Dribble Tag (Drill #34)

## Objective
Develop quick footwork while handling a basketball, peripheral vision, agility, and ball security in a competitive full-squad tag game.

## Setup
- Full team (5–15 players)
- **Half court** (or any confined area — in the source, bordered by half-court line, sidelines, and baseline)
- 1 basketball per player (options vary the count)
- Duration: 1 minute (or until all players are tagged)

## Execution
### Beginner (Base Game)
1. All players line up within the designated space (half-court).
2. All players begin dribbling one basketball simultaneously.
3. Designate **one player as "It."** This player must **tag** another player; the tagged player becomes the new It.
4. Tagging counts only if both players maintain their dribble throughout.

## Options (All Skill Levels)
1. **Asymmetric ball count — tag to transfer**: It dribbles **one ball**; all others dribble **two balls**. When It tags a player, that player gives up one of their balls to It and becomes the new It.
2. **Asymmetric ball count — elimination**: It dribbles **one ball**; all others dribble **two balls**. When tagged, the player is eliminated and goes to the other end to practice shooting.
3. **Team tag**: Divide into **two teams** (all players dribble one or two balls). Team A tries to tag all members of Team B. Teammates may double-team to get the tag. Coach times how long it takes. Then Team B tags Team A. Fastest team wins.
4. **Reversed advantage**: It dribbles **two balls**; all others dribble **one ball**.

## Coaching Points
- **Eyes up**: The most important cue — players must see other players to avoid being tagged and to pursue their target. Never look at the ball.
- **Fingerpads**: Dribble with only the first joints of the fingers.
- **Correct dribbling stance**: All players must remain in either a **control-dribbling stance** OR a **speed-dribbling stance** — they should choose one appropriate to the situation, not mix both arbitrarily.
- **Change of pace**: Vary speed to escape or pursue — slow down to set up the acceleration past a tagger.
- **Peripheral vision**: This game naturally trains peripheral vision — players must see the entire space, not just their own ball.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: One ball each, large half-court space, no elimination.
2. **Intermediate**: Two-ball options; smaller space (quarter court).
3. **Advanced**: Team elimination format with time tracking and competition between groups.

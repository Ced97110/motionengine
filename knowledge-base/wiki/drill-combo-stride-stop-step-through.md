# Combo Stride Stop / Step-Through Drill

## Objective
Train the step-through finish as a secondary read off the stride stop — when the defender challenges the jump shot, step through for a layup or floater, bilaterally.

## Setup
- 1 player, 1 ball
- Half court — starting at elbow or wing
- Optional: a chair or cone at the finish spot to represent a defender
- Shot tracking table (FGA, FGM %, FTA, FTM %)

## Execution
1. Player starts in triple-threat at the perimeter.
2. **1-Dribble Left**: take 1 hard left-hand dribble, gather into a stride stop.
3. Show the ball (shot fake), then step through — lead foot crosses past the imagined defender — and finish at the rim with a layup or floater.
4. Shoot 5 reps (Beginner) or 10 reps (Intermediate) or 20 reps (Advanced) going left.
5. Step to the free-throw line. Shoot 5–10 FTs with full routine.
6. **1-Dribble Right**: mirror the same sequence going right.
7. Repeat the L/R/FT cycle a second time for 1-dribble step-throughs.
8. **2-Dribble series**: repeat the full L/R/FT structure taking 2 dribbles before the gather.
9. Total: 8 sets of combos + 8 FT clusters across all four volume levels.

**Volume Progressions:**
- **Workout #4-7 (80 shots)**: 8 sets × 5 combos + 8 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 24–40 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (85%)
- **Workout #4-8 (120 shots)**: 8 sets × 10 combos + 8 × 5 FTs | FG goal: 48–80 (60%) | FT goal: 34–40 (80%)
- **Workout #4-9 (240 shots)**: 8 sets × 20 combos + 8 × 10 FTs | FG goal: 97–160 (60%) | FT goal: 68–80 (85%)

## Coaching Points
- **Sell the shot fake first**: the step-through is only effective when the defender has committed to the shot — if there is no fake, this is just a drive, not a read
- **Lead foot direction**: the step-through foot should step past the defender's near hip, not directly into them — contact before the step is a charge
- **Two-foot stride stop first**: the stride stop must be completed before the step begins; cue: **"Stop. Show. Step."**
- **Protect the ball low**: on the step-through, hold the ball below shoulder height and to the side of the body away from the defender
- Track FGA and FTM every session; if step-through layups are below 70% make rate, slow down and work closer to the basket

## Progressions
1. **Beginner (80 shots)**: 5 reps per set; walk through the stride stop → fake → step-through footwork without a ball before adding the ball
2. **Intermediate (120 shots)**: 10 reps per set; increase pace and vary the step-through finish (layup, floater, reverse)
3. **Advanced (240 shots)**: 20 reps per set at game speed; use a live passive defender who raises a hand on the fake to train the read

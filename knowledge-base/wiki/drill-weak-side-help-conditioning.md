# Weak-Side Help Conditioning Drill

## Objective
Condition the defender to execute a full weak-side help rotation, closeout, and penetration stop — combining defensive movement pattern training with aerobic conditioning.

## Setup
- 1 defender, 1 offensive player at the far baseline (just inside the three-point line), 1 coach/player at the free-throw line
- Half court
- 1 basketball

## Execution
1. **Defender** starts at the weak side of the basket.
2. **Offensive player** (with ball) starts at the other baseline, just inside the three-point line.
3. When the offensive player begins to dribble toward the basket, the **defender sprints to close out** and forces a decision.
4. The offensive player **passes to a coach or player at the free-throw line** (cannot score).
5. When the ball swings to the top, the **defender pivots and sprints to close out on the coach/player at the top**.
6. The player at the top **drives left or right**; the defender **cuts off penetration**.
7. Rotate roles after each rep.

## Coaching Points
- First closeout must be explosive — "Sprint, then chop your feet to brake!"
- Don't overcommit on the first closeout; force the pass but stay under control.
- After the pass to the top, pivot immediately — "Ball moves, you move!"
- On the second closeout, arrive in a balanced defensive stance before the offensive player decides which direction to drive.
- This drill simulates the exact weak-side help rotation seen dozens of times per game.

## Progressions
1. **Beginner**: Offensive player is passive — just pass, don't drive. Defender learns the movement pattern.
2. **Intermediate**: Full execution as described; offensive player at the top drives live.
3. **Advanced**: Add a second offensive player on the weak side — defender must decide whether to stay with the driver or rotate to cover the skip pass.
